# Bidding strategies for Sponsored Products

Learn about bidding strategies and options to optimize sponsored ad campaigns.

Updated on Dec 11, 2025.

In Sponsored Products campaigns, you choose between a dynamic, fixed, or rule-based bidding strategy. If you have custom audiences in Amazon Marketing Cloud, you can also adjust bids by audience.

## Dynamic bids - down only

When you choose dynamic bids - down only, Amazon will reduce bids for clicks that may be less likely to convert to a sale.

If Amazon considers that your ad may be less likely to convert to a sale, it may lower your bid for that auction. Bids may decrease by up to 100% for all placements based on performance. This can happen on a less relevant search query or on a placement that does not perform well.

This strategy is ideal for:

- Optimizing for conversion while setting the maximum bid amount
- New advertisers
- Strict budgets

## Dynamic bids - up and down

Amazon will increase bids for clicks that may be more likely to convert to a sale. With this strategy, Amazon also reduces bids for clicks that are less likely to convert to a sale.

Bids may increase or decrease by up to 100% for all placements based on performance. Since this strategy adjusts bids up and down in proportion to likelihood of conversion, it may deliver more conversions for the ad spend compared to other strategies.

If Amazon considers that an ad is more likely to convert to a sale, it may increase the bid for that auction. This can happen if the ad appears for a highly relevant search query, for a shopper highly likely to purchase, or on a placement that performs well.

If another opportunity looks less likely to convert to a sale, Amazon may lower the bid for that auction.

This strategy is ideal for:

- Advertising a product with good historical performance data
- Campaigns targeting highly sought-after placements
- Flexible budgets with room for higher bids

Example: Amazon can adjust a bid of `$1.00` up to `$2.00` for all placements.

## Fixed bids

Amazon will use your exact bid for all opportunities and will not adjust bids.

Compared to dynamic bidding strategies, fixed bids may get more impressions but fewer conversions for the ad spend.

You can manually adjust base bids to optimize campaign performance. You can also use placement bid adjustments with fixed bidding campaigns to increase bids for specific placements.

This strategy is ideal for:

- Campaigns with historical data and a specific target ACOS
- Testing new products

## Rule-based bidding

Rule-based bidding automatically adjusts advertising bids to match campaign goals.

For any Sponsored Products campaign that meets the eligibility criteria, you can apply a rule with a guardrail of return on ad spend (ROAS). Amazon may then adjust base bids up and down to increase conversions while maintaining guardrails.

This strategy is ideal for:

- Large catalogs requiring automated management
- Experienced sellers with established performance history
- Campaigns with clear ROAS objectives

## Bids by audience

In addition to selecting a bidding strategy, you can adjust bids by audience. This enables you to reach and engage custom-designed audiences.

To get started, create custom audiences in Amazon Marketing Cloud. These audiences will automatically appear in the sponsored ads account when creating new campaigns, allowing specific bid modifications for each selected audience to enhance engagement.

You can track how these audiences perform through the Audiences tab in the all campaigns page.
