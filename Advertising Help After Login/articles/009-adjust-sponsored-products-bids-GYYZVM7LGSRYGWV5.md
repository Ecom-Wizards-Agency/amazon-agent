# Adjust Sponsored Products bids

Learn how to set bids to increase when an ad is eligible to show in certain locations or to specific groups of shoppers.

Updated on Dec 01, 2025.

You can adjust bids by placement and/or audience to increase the bid when the ad is eligible to appear in certain locations and/or to certain groups of advertisers. This helps advertisers be more strategic with where budget is spent.

## Adjust bids by placement

Enter a percentage amount to increase your bid when the ad is eligible to compete for one or all of these placements:

- **Top of search (first page):** the top row of first-page search results.
- **Rest of search:** the middle or bottom of shopping results and the second page of shopping results and beyond.
- **Product pages:** Sponsored Products ads on product detail pages and all non-shopping placements such as the add-to-cart page.
- **Amazon business placements:** all Sponsored Products placements that appear on Amazon Business including Top of Search, Rest of Search, and Amazon Business product pages.

The specified increase works in addition to the selected bidding strategy, dynamic or fixed.

Adjusting bids by placement is best for advertisers who have observed that certain placements led to conversions, sales, or other desired activities. Placement metrics are visible at the campaign level on the bid adjustment tab in campaign manager.

Examples:

- Fixed bid: You set a fixed bid of `$1` for the keyword "camera" and set a `50%` bid adjustment for top of search. When eligible to appear in top of search against "camera," the bid increases to `$1.50`.
- Dynamic bid: You set a dynamic bid of `$1` for the keyword "camera" and set a `50%` bid adjustment for top of search. When eligible to appear in top of search, the bid increases to `$1.50`. Dynamic bidding up and down can further increase the bid up to `$3`.

In Sponsored Products across retailers campaigns, you cannot adjust bids for Amazon Business placements, but have two additional placement options:

- **Top of browse:** top of browse pages, capturing shopper attention as they navigate categories and subcategories.
- **Rest of browse:** browse pages below top placements, providing additional visibility as shoppers explore the catalog.

## Adjust bids by audience

You can adjust bids by audience to help drive discovery and sales for specific groups of shoppers. You choose an audience and a percentage to increase bids by when a customer from that audience searches for products.

Audience types:

- **Amazon built audiences:** informed by first-party shopping and streaming signals, including in-market, lifestyle, interests, and life events.
- **Amazon Marketing Cloud custom audiences:** AMC users can apply bid adjustments to AMC custom audiences in Sponsored Product campaigns. Use data like impressions, clicks, and conversions across Amazon Ads investments to capture high-intent audiences during the shopping journey.

## Adjust bids by video

**Note:** Sponsored Products video is only available in the US and is currently not available for Books.

You can adjust bids for ads with Sponsored Product Videos, which are videos within the ad that highlight product benefits. Adjusting bids here increases the likelihood of the ad appearing compared to other video ads.

You can enter up to a `900%` increase, or `10x`, to the base bid in Sponsored Products. If a Sponsored Product Video bid is applied with other bid modifiers such as Audiences or other Placements, these adjustments apply together when conditions are met.

Example:

Bid: `$1.00` for keyword "camera"

Adjustments:

- 50% rest of search bid adjustment
- 50% custom audience bid adjustment
- 100% bid adjustment for Sponsored Products Video

Final bid for video ads appearing on rest of search for the custom audience: `$4.50`.

Calculation:

- a: `$1` original bid + `50%` rest of search adjustment = `$1.50`
- b: `$1.50` + `50%` custom audience adjustment = `$2.25`
- c: `$2.25` + `100%` Sponsored Products Video adjustment = `$4.50`

## Applying placement and audience adjustments together

You can apply placement and audience adjustments in the same campaign. If both placement and audience bid percentages are applied and both conditions are met, these bid adjustments are applied together to determine final bid.

For all adjustment types, you can enter up to a combined `900%` increase, or `10x`, to the base bid.

Example:

You bid `$1` for keyword "camera" and set:

- 50% adjustment for Top of search
- 100% adjustment for Amazon Business
- 50% adjustment for an audience

Final bid: `$4.50` for an opportunity in the top of search placement on Amazon Business for a customer in that audience.

Calculation:

- a: `$1` original bid + `50%` top of search adjustment = `$1.50`
- b: `$1.50` + `100%` Amazon Business adjustment = `$3.00`
- c: `$3.00` + `50%` audience adjustment = `$4.50`
