# Learn about Amazon Marketing Cloud

Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC) is a secure, privacy-safe clean room solution for analytics and custom audiences.

In AMC's cloud-based clean room, advertisers can query across inputs such as Amazon Ads campaign events. Inputs can include impressions, clicks, conversions from Amazon DSP and sponsored ads campaigns, and advertiser-provided inputs to build aggregated and anonymized analyses.

## AMC analytics

AMC analyses can support campaign measurement, audience refinement, supply optimization, and cross-channel marketing decisions.

Common AMC analytics use cases include:

- **Performance deep dive:** deeper findings about a specific campaign.
- **Audience insights:** connections between engagement actions and audience attributes.
- **Shopping journey analysis:** common paths to conversion by media exposure, frequency, timing, and other attributes.
- **Media mix analysis:** collective impact of Amazon Ads media channels and incremental impact of specific media usage.
- **Omnichannel impact:** how Amazon media contributes to sales across Amazon store types and direct-to-consumer channels.
- **Custom attribution:** assign conversion credit using custom logic for engagement type, lookback windows, and other criteria.

## AMC audiences

AMC audiences can create custom audiences for activation in Amazon DSP and sponsored ads.

Custom audiences can use signals in the AMC instance, including Amazon DSP and sponsored ads traffic events, ad-relevant conversion events, first-party tables, and select paid-features tables.

Common AMC audience use cases:

- **Ad engagement:** audience engaged with ads but not yet purchased.
- **Cart abandoners:** audience added to cart but has not purchased.
- **High-value lookalikes:** audience similar to high-volume customers.

Audiences created in AMC are sent to corresponding Amazon DSP and sponsored ads accounts. They can be selected for relevant ad groups, then monitored through Amazon DSP, sponsored ads, or AMC.
