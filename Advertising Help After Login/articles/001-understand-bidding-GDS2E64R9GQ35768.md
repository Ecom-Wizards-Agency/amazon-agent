# Understand bidding

Learn how Amazon's auction-based bidding model works, the difference between CPC and vCPM bidding, and how to use suggested bids to make your ads more competitive.

This article explains bidding in Sponsored ads. To learn about billing on Amazon DSP, see the related Amazon DSP bidding article.

## What is bidding?

Amazon uses an auction-based inventory buying model that allows you to place bids for your ads to display to shoppers. When you set a bid, you're setting the maximum amount you're willing to pay for a click or impression, depending on the bidding strategy of your campaign.

Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Display campaigns use either **cost-per-click** or **cost per 1,000 viewable impressions** bids.

| Bid Type | Supported Campaigns |
| --- | --- |
| cost-per-click (CPC) bids | Sponsored Products; Sponsored Brands > drive page visits; Display > page visits; Display > conversions |
| cost per 1,000 viewable impressions (vCPM) bids | Sponsored Brands > grow brand impression share; Display |

## Cost-per-click (CPC) bidding

A bid is the amount you're willing to pay when someone clicks your ad. Your daily budget is how much you're willing to spend on clicks each day.

The standard process is:

1. Your ad appears to shoppers. This is an impression.
2. You pay only when shoppers click your ad.
3. Each click deducts your bid amount from your daily budget.

**Note:** Never bid more than you're willing to pay for a click.

## Cost per 1,000 viewable impressions (vCPM)

Cost per 1,000 viewable impressions is available only for on- and off-Amazon recognized traffic, including Twitch, Amazon Fresh, Whole Foods Market, and off-site.

When a sponsored ad gets 1,000 viewable impressions, the bid amount is deducted from the daily or lifetime budget. For advertisers that sell products on Amazon, this gives visibility into how many sales the views of the ad generated after customers later bought the brand's ASINs on Amazon.

Example:

Daily budget ($20) / winning bid ($1) = number of impressions per day (20,000)

Amazon Ads follows the MRC definition for an ad view:

- At least 50% of the ad should be in the customer's viewport for at least 1 second for it to count as a viewed impression.
- For videos, at least 50% of the ad should be in the customer's viewport with 2-second playback completed.

There is a minimum bid requirement for vCPM. It varies by ad type and country. Using the minimum bids may not lead to viewable impressions.

## Suggested bid and bid range

The suggested bid and bid range are calculated from bids for ads similar to yours that won impressions over the past 7 days. They are updated daily based on competing bids and ads in each auction.

You see bid suggestions when creating a campaign and can access them after campaign creation on the Targeting tab in campaign manager.

Amazon suggests using the suggested bid if you're new to Amazon Ads. To make bids more competitive and improve the chance that an ad displays, start with the suggested bid or higher within the range.

Example:

If the suggested bid is $0.70 ($0.40 - $1.40), the suggested bid is $0.70 and the bid range is $0.40 to $1.40.

### Bidding above the suggested range

If each click or view is worth more than the suggested bid, for example $1.60, you can set the bid at $1.60. Since it is higher than the suggested bid and above the high end of the bid range, the ads are more competitive in auctions. This may increase chances of winning impressions, clicks, and sales.

### Bidding below the suggested bid

If each click or view is worth less than the suggested bid, Amazon recommends staying within the suggested range. For example, $0.50. Even though it is lower than the suggested bid, it can still win impressions, but a higher bid may win more impressions.

Performance Estimate beta can show estimated weekly impressions a target may receive from suggested bids and from a range of bids.
