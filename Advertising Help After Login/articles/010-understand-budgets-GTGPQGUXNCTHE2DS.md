# Understand budgets

Understand how budgets function and the difference between daily and lifetime budgets.

## What is a budget on Amazon Ads?

A bid is how much you spend per click, and budget is how much you are willing to spend on clicks per day.

Each time a shopper clicks an ad, the bid amount is deducted from the daily budget. Budgets are spent as quickly as possible and are not paced throughout the duration of the campaign.

## Daily budget

A daily budget is the maximum amount that you are willing to spend, on average, each day over a calendar month.

You will not necessarily spend all of the daily budget each day. If you reach the daily budget within a day, the campaign pauses until the following day when the daily budget resets.

Formula:

`Daily Budget ($10) x Days in the month (30) = Monthly budget ($300)`

To help capitalize on days when more potential shoppers view ads, Amazon uses leftover funds from low-traffic days to increase budget by up to 100%.

At the end of the month, spend will not exceed the daily budget multiplied by the number of days in that month.

Example:

Daily budget is `$100`. You may only spend `$90` on Monday, but `$110` on Tuesday, which is the daily budget plus the `$10` left over from Monday. Total spend over two days is `$200`, averaging `$100` per day.

By default, Amazon may increase budget up to 100% on high-traffic days. If you want Amazon to only increase daily budget by 25%, change this in campaign settings:

`Sponsored ads > Campaigns > Settings`

## Suggested daily budget

The suggested daily budget is the minimum daily budget required for the campaign to remain active through the day.

With a budget lower than the suggested one, the campaign risks going out of budget early and not surfacing products to shoppers. This can cause missed clicks or sales.

Amazon calculates suggested daily budget using:

- Campaigns with ASINs similar to yours, based on category and price.
- Machine-learning models of historical campaigns with similar ASINs, bids, bidding strategies, and targeting types.
- Data from the moment when the campaign is created.
- For seasonal events, product and date relevance based on similar campaigns that increased budget for that special event.

## Lifetime budget

In Sponsored Brands campaigns, you can choose between a daily budget and a lifetime budget.

A lifetime budget is the total amount you are willing to spend on a single campaign for as long as it runs. It requires an end date and a minimum `$100` budget.
