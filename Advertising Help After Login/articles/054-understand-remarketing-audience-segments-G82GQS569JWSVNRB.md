# Understand remarketing audience segments

Purchases and views remarketing strategies target audiences based on purchase and browsing behaviors.

Advertisers must sell products or services on Amazon to use remarketing audiences in Display campaigns.

## What is a remarketing audience segment?

Remarketing audience segments help engage Amazon customers based on historical behaviors during a chosen lookback window. Advertisers can use purchase behavior, views behavior, or both.

- **Purchases remarketing:** targets audiences based on past purchase behaviors on Amazon. It can drive repeat purchases, build customer loyalty, cross-sell to existing customers, and reach new audiences.
- **Views remarketing:** targets audiences based on product detail pages viewed on Amazon. It can support awareness during launches, encourage consideration, and re-engage customers who have not purchased.

Example: reach an audience that purchased household supplies under `$50` in the past 14 days or viewed children's running shoes under `$250` in the past 30 days.

## How remarketing segments work

To add a remarketing audience:

1. Choose a strategy: purchases or views.
2. Choose a lookback window.
3. Add audience categories and/or dynamic segments.

## Categories

Categories include customers who purchased or viewed products within a specific category on Amazon during the selected lookback window. Categories can be refined by brand, price band, Prime eligibility, or star rating.

If the audience is too narrow, Amazon displays a message. To broaden the audience, choose a different category, remove refinements, or increase the lookback window.

## Dynamic segments

Dynamic segments include advertised products or similar/related products.

## Lookback windows

Lookback windows reach audiences based on behaviors during a specific time window.

- Purchases remarketing supports lookback windows up to 365 days.
- Views remarketing supports lookback windows up to 90 days.

> Note: All lookback windows include consumers currently browsing content related to the specified categories.
