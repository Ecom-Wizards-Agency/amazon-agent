# Set bids in a Sponsored Products campaign

Learn how to set bids, choose a bidding strategy, and apply bid adjustments to optimize Sponsored Products campaign performance.

You select your bidding strategy and set bids during campaign setup. You can change bids or bid strategy once the campaign is live in campaign manager:

1. In the navigation side menu, click **Campaigns**.
2. Click the campaign you want to edit to open it.
3. In the left menu, click **Campaign settings**.

## Step 1: Choose your bid type

The process to set a bid differs based on the targeting type in the campaign:

- Automatic targeting
- Manual targeting - Keyword
- Manual targeting - Product

On the automatic targeting card:

1. Choose to set one default bid, or unique bids for each targeting group.
2. Enter your bid in the text box.

Notes:

- The **suggested bid for regular days** is calculated from a group of recent winning bids similar to yours.
- The **suggested bid range** is displayed. Amazon recommends choosing a bid in this range.
- If a special event such as Prime Day or a major holiday is coming up in the next 45 days, Amazon may also provide a suggested bid and bid range to use leading up to that event.
- During high-volume shopping events, bids may increase because of increased advertising activity.
- Higher CPCs typically occur in the weeks leading up to and during major shopping events.

**Note:** Cost-per-click bids set at the keyword level take precedence over the default cost-per-click bid set at the ad group or campaign level.

## Step 2: Choose a campaign bidding strategy

In Sponsored Products campaigns, choose between a dynamic, fixed, or rule-based bidding strategy:

- **Dynamic bids - down only:** Amazon reduces bids for clicks that may be less likely to convert to a sale.
- **Dynamic bids - up and down:** Amazon increases bids for clicks that may be more likely to convert to a sale, and reduces bids for clicks less likely to convert.
- **Fixed bids:** Amazon uses the exact bid for all opportunities and does not adjust bids. This may get more impressions but fewer conversions for ad spend.

## Step 3: Set bid adjustments

You can adjust bids by placement, audience, or video to increase bids when an ad is eligible to appear in certain locations or to certain groups of advertisers. This helps advertisers be more strategic with budget.

Amazon recommends this strategy for experienced advertisers who have insights about where they want ads to serve.

Adjustment types:

- Adjust bids by placement
- Adjust bids by audience
- Adjust bids by video

Enter a percentage amount to increase the bid when the ad is eligible to compete for one or more placements:

- **Top of search (first page):** the top row of first-page search results.
- **Rest of search:** the middle or bottom of shopping results and the second page of shopping results and beyond.
- **Product pages:** ads on product detail pages and all non-shopping placements such as the add-to-cart page.
- **Amazon Business placements:** all Sponsored Products placements that appear on Amazon Business, including Top of Search, Rest of Search, and Amazon Business product pages.

You can enter placement bid adjustments during new campaign setup or add them to an existing campaign:

1. In the navigation side menu, click **Campaigns**.
2. Open the campaign where you want to add a placement adjustment.
3. In the left menu, click **Bid adjustments**.
4. Next to the desired placement type and bid strategy, enter the value by which you want to adjust bids by placement.

## Step 4: Set bid rules

Once the campaign is set up, you can implement bid rules:

- **Rule-based bidding:** set a rule with a performance goal, for example ROAS. Amazon then adjusts base bids for each ad impression, increasing or decreasing them as needed to try to achieve the performance target.
- **Schedule bid rules:** schedule bid increases for specific times of day, days of week, date ranges, or high-traffic events such as Black Friday.

To apply a bid rule:

1. In the navigation side menu, click **Campaigns**.
2. Open the campaign where you want to add a bid rule.
3. Next to the campaign name, click **More**, then select **Edit settings**.
