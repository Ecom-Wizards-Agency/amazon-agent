# Understand bid adjustments in Sponsored ads

Learn how bid adjustments help you strategically increase bids when your ad is eligible for specific placements, audiences, or video formats.

## What are bid adjustments?

Bid adjustments let you increase bids when an ad is eligible to appear in specific placements, to specific audiences, or with Sponsored Product Videos.

You set a percentage increase that applies on top of the base bid when certain conditions are met. This gives more control over where advertising budget is spent and helps compete more effectively in high-value opportunities.

There are three types of bid adjustments:

- **Placement adjustments** help compete in high-visibility locations like top of search.
- **Audience adjustments** help reach specific shopper segments more effectively.
- **Video adjustments** increase the likelihood that Sponsored Product Videos appear compared to other video ads.

For setup instructions, Amazon points to:

- Set up bid adjustments in Sponsored Products
- Set up bid adjustments in Sponsored Brands

## How bid adjustments work

Each bid adjustment type functions differently.

For example, if ads at the top of search results convert better than other placements, you can increase the bid specifically for that placement. Similarly, if certain audiences are more likely to purchase, you can bid higher when those shoppers search for relevant keywords.

In Sponsored Products campaigns, the methodology also changes depending on whether fixed or dynamic bids are set.

Adjustment categories:

- Placement
- Audience
- Sponsored Products video

Adjusting bids by placement helps compete in high-visibility locations. In Sponsored Brands campaigns, bids can only be adjusted for top of search and rest of search placements.

Enter a percentage amount to increase a bid when an ad is eligible to compete for one or all of these placements:

- **Top of search (first page):** the top row of first-page search results.
- **Rest of search:** the middle or bottom of shopping results and the second page of shopping results and beyond.
- **Product pages:** Sponsored Products ads on product detail pages and all non-shopping placements such as the add-to-cart page.
- **Amazon Business placements:** all Sponsored Products placements that appear on Amazon Business, including Top of Search, Rest of Search, and Amazon Business product pages.

The specified increase works in addition to the selected bidding strategy, dynamic or fixed.

Adjusting bids by placement is best for advertisers who have observed that certain placements led to conversions, sales, or other desired actions in the past. Placement metrics are available at the campaign level on the bid adjustment tab in campaign manager.

Examples:

- Fixed bid: You set a fixed bid of $1 for the keyword "camera" and set a 50% bid adjustment for top of search. When eligible to appear in top of search against "camera," the bid increases to $1.50.
- Dynamic bid: You set a dynamic bid of $1 for the keyword "camera" and set a 50% bid adjustment for top of search. When eligible to appear in top of search, the bid increases to $1.50. Dynamic bidding up and down can further increase the bid up to $3.

## How bid adjustments work when combined

You can apply placement and audience adjustments in the same campaign. If both placement and audience bid percentages are applied and both conditions are met, these bid adjustments are applied together to determine the final bid.

For all adjustment types, you can enter up to a combined 900% increase, or 10x, to the base bid.

Example:

You bid $1 for the keyword "camera" and set:

- 50% adjustment for Top of search
- 100% adjustment for Amazon Business
- 50% adjustment for an audience

The final bid will be adjusted to $4.50 for an opportunity in the top of search placement on Amazon Business for a customer in that audience.

Calculation:

- a: $1 original bid + 50% top of search adjustment = $1.50
- b: $1.50 + 100% Amazon Business adjustment = $3.00
- c: $3.00 + 50% audience adjustment = $4.50
