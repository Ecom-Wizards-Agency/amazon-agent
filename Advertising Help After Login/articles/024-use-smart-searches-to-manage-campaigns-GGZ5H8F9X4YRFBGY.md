# Use smart searches to manage campaigns

Smart searches help find and analyze campaign performance data using natural language queries in Campaign manager.

> Note: This feature is currently in beta and is not available to everyone.

Use smart searches to filter, sort, and edit campaigns with natural language. Type a request into the search bar to pull up campaign metrics, targets, conversion insights, and more.

Example: "campaigns that ran out of budget last week" updates the data based on that search.

Smart searches can switch between campaign and Sponsored Products targeting views by including "campaign" or "target" in the prompt:

- "campaigns with orders in last 7 days"
- "targets with impressions in the last 3 days"

Tip: the five most recent searches appear when clicking the search bar.

## Search prompts and tips

> Note: The search bar uses the currently selected date range if the prompt does not specify one. It also defaults to active campaigns or targets unless otherwise specified.

Smart searches can combine filters, sorting, column additions, and date-range changes into one prompt.

Example: "increase bids by 3% for SP targets with ROAS>3" filters the table to Sponsored Products targets with ROAS greater than 3 and prompts to increase bids by 3%. Amazon allows review before applying the bid increase.

The search bar can filter and sort by any metric or setting in the campaigns or targeting data table, including date range.

## Natural language search examples

- Keywords getting an order this week.
- Targets with impressions and no orders.

## Specific search examples

- Clicks > 15, orders = 0, last 14 days sorted by Clicks descending.
- Sponsored Brands, % of Orders NTB > 5%, sorted by % of Orders NTB descending.
- Targets, conversion rate > 2%, impressions > 100, last 3 days.
