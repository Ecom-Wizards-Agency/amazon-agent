# Understand targeting in Display campaigns

Use audiences and contextual targeting to determine who sees Display ads.

- Advertisers that sell on the Amazon store can use remarketing, in-market, lifestyle, custom audiences, and/or contextual targeting.
- Advertisers that do not sell on the Amazon store can target interest/lifestyle and in-market audiences.

## What is targeting?

Display targeting uses first-party shopping, streaming, and browsing signals and insights from Amazon, Prime Video, Twitch, and IMDb to reach relevant consumers.

Ads may serve on and off Amazon, including Amazon detail and shopping result pages, Amazon Fresh, Whole Foods Market, IMDb, Twitch, and third-party websites and apps.

## How targeting works

Audiences can target shoppers based on interests and shopping behavior. Advertisers that sell on Amazon can use remarketing segments to target shoppers who viewed or purchased the advertiser's product or something similar.

## Targeting types

- **Contextual:** show ads to customers visiting selected products, categories, and similar products. Ads may serve on and off Amazon. Available only to advertisers that sell products and services on Amazon.
- **Remarketing:** target shoppers based on views or purchases.
- **In-market:** target shoppers based on market signals.
- **Interest and lifestyle:** target shoppers by broader interests/lifestyles.
- **Custom:** use custom audience options where available.

Dynamic segments are collections of products to target. Individual products change based on performance and relevance criteria to reach customers likely to engage with ads.

> Note: Display campaigns that use Audiences always exclude past customers who purchased advertised products or similar products in the past 30-day window.

## Optimized targeting

Use optimized targeting when:

- New to Display advertising.
- Unsure which audiences to target.
- You want Amazon to match ads to relevant customers, including prospecting for new customers who viewed detail pages or purchased products similar to yours.

Use manual targeting when:

- Past campaign data indicates which audiences produce the most impressions, clicks, and sales.
- Specific targeting needs exist.
