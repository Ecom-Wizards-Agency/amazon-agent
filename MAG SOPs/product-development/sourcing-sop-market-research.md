# Sourcing SOP: Market Research

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/product-development/page/sourcing-sop-market-research

## **Market Research**

**Who is this for?**

- This SOP is intended for Amazon sellers, especially those new to the platform or looking to expand their product offerings.
- It is also useful for experienced sellers seeking to refine their product sourcing strategies.

**Objective:**

- The objective of this standard operating procedure (SOP) is to guide Amazon sellers in conducting effective market research to source products for sale on the platform, using Helium 10 as the recommended software tool.
- By following this SOP, sellers will be able to identify profitable product opportunities, understand market trends, and make informed decisions about product selection and sourcing strategies.

**Market Research and its Importance in Product Sourcing**

Market research involves gathering and analyzing information about the market, customers, competitors, and trends to make informed decisions about product sourcing.

It is crucial for understanding customer demand, identifying niche markets, analyzing market saturation, evaluating pricing, profitability, and differentiating products.

**Recommended Market Research Tool or How to Choose a Tool**

Helium 10 is recommended for market research due to its powerful keyword and product research features.

Other tools like Jungle Scout are also available, so it's important to research and choose based on specific needs and preferences.

Choosing the best market research tool for sourcing products on Amazon depends on several factors, including your budget, experience level, and desired features. Here are some key points to consider:

**Your Budget:**

- Free options: Tools like Google Keyword Planner and Amazon Seller Central tools offer basic data, but may lack advanced features.
- Paid options: Tools like Jungle Scout, Helium 10, AMZScout, and SellerApp offer more in-depth data analysis, competitor insights, and product research features, but come with monthly subscription fees.

**Your Experience Level:**

- Beginner: Start with free or basic paid options like SellerApp Lite or Helium 10's free plan to learn the ropes.
- Intermediate: Consider tools like AMZScout Pro or Jungle Scout Pro for their comprehensive features and data analysis.
- Advanced: Opt for advanced tools like Helium 10 Diamond or SellerApp Enterprise with complex features and supplier-sourcing functionalities.

**Desired Features:**

- Product research: Look for tools that analyze sales data, estimated profits, competition levels, and identify profitable niches.
- Keyword research: Tools with keyword search volume, competition, and PPC insights can help target profitable keywords.
- Supplier sourcing: Advanced tools offer supplier directories, product listings, and negotiation tools.
- Inventory management: Some tools help manage inventory levels, track supplier performance, and predict demand.

**Here are some additional tips for choosing the best market research tool:**

- Read reviews and compare features: Look for user reviews on websites like G2 Crowd, TrustRadius, and Capterra.
- Try free trials: Most tools offer limited free trials to test their features before committing.
- Focus on specific needs: Prioritize features that align with your specific sourcing goals and budget.
- Consider customer support: Choose a tool with good customer support in case you encounter any issues.

**Look-Alike Products and Demand Generation Products**

Look-alike products are similar to existing products in the market and aim to capitalize on existing demand.

Demand generation products create new demand by offering unique solutions or addressing unmet needs.

Understanding Look-Alike Products and Demand Generation Products can be crucial for your Amazon product sourcing research, even though they seem like different areas. Here's why:

**Look-Alike Products:**

- Identifying Potential Demand: By analyzing existing successful products (often called "best sellers" or "top sellers"), you can identify similar products with potential demand. These "look-alike" products share similar features, target audience, or solve similar problems.
- Idea Generation & Refinement: Analyze successful look-alike products to understand their strengths and weaknesses. This can inspire you to create new variations, improve existing product ideas, or adapt features to better fit the market.
- Competitive Analysis: Studying look-alike products helps you understand the competitive landscape, pricing strategies, and customer reviews. This allows you to position your product strategically for better success.

**Demand Generation Products:**

- Understanding Market Trends: These tools analyze search trends, social media buzz, and product popularity to identify emerging trends and predict future demand. This can help you source products that align with growing demand and avoid saturated markets.
- Finding Untapped Niches: By identifying trending products or features, you can explore niches with high potential but low competition. This gives you an edge and avoids saturated categories.
- Product Validation & Refinement: Testing your product ideas against trending products and demand data can help you validate your initial concept and refine it to better match customer needs and preferences.

**Combining Both Approaches:**

Using both look-alike product analysis and demand generation tools helps you:

- Source products with proven demand: Look-alikes provide evidence of existing demand, while demand-generation tools help identify emerging trends.
- Reduce risk and increase success: By understanding market trends and competition, you can make informed sourcing decisions and minimize the risk of choosing products with low demand.
- Find profitable niches: Combining both approaches helps you discover untapped niches with high potential and avoid saturated categories.

**Customer Demand**

Understanding customer demand involves gaining insights into customer preferences, needs, and buying patterns.

1. Dig into reviews: Uncover pain points, desired features, and dissatisfaction for product improvement ideas.

2. Track search trends: Discover popular searches and emerging trends to identify untapped demand.

3. Explore other marketplaces: See what's hot and identify gaps in Amazon's offerings.

4. Listen to social media: Join relevant communities and monitor trending discussions for product inspiration.

5. Leverage product research tools: Analyze data, competition, and profitability to find hidden gems.

6. Combine & Validate: Use multiple methods and confirm demand before investing.

**Identifying Niche Market and Why it Matters to Product Sourcing**

Niche markets are specialized segments of the market with less competition. They offer opportunities for sellers to target specific customer segments and stand out from competitors.

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you discover your niche:

1. **Leverage your interests and expertise**:

- Start with what you know and love: What are you passionate about? What hobbies or skills do you have? Consider these areas as potential starting points for your niche.
- Identify your unique selling proposition (USP): What makes you stand out from other sellers? Do you have access to unique products or resources? Can you offer exceptional customer service or product knowledge?

2. **Research market trends and opportunities:**

- Use market research tools: Tools like Jungle Scout, Helium 10, and AMZScout can help you analyze product demand, competition, and profitability.
- Explore online communities and forums: Join groups related to your interests to see what products people are discussing and what problems they face.
- Look for trending topics and emerging markets: Stay updated on industry trends and identify new niches with potential for growth.

3**. Validate your niche idea:**

- Estimate demand: Use keyword research tools to gauge search volume and identify profitable keywords related to your niche.
- Analyze competition: Assess the level of competition in your chosen niche and identify opportunities to differentiate yourself.
- Consider profitability: Calculate potential profit margins based on product costs, selling price, and Amazon fees.

4.**Refine your niche and develop a product strategy:**

- Focus on a specific sub-niche: Don't try to be too broad. Instead, target a specific audience with unique needs and preferences.
- Develop a product line: Offer a variety of related products within your niche to cater to different customer segments.
- Create a competitive advantage: What makes your products unique and better than what's already available?

**Additional tips:**

- Start small and scale gradually: Don't try to launch too many products at once. Test the waters with a few products and scale up based on your success.
- Stay passionate and focused: Building a successful Amazon business takes time and effort. Stay motivated by your passion for your niche and focus on delivering value to your customers.

**Analyzing Market Saturation**

Market saturation refers to the level of competition and the number of sellers in a particular market segment.

It's important to assess market saturation to determine the viability of entering a market.

**Market Saturation Check: Key Steps**

1. Define your market: Be specific about category, audience, price.
2. Market size & growth: Use tools to estimate size and growth trends.
3. Competition: Analyze number, share, offerings, and pricing.
4. Sales trends: Track sales volume and growth for similar products.
5. Customer reviews: Look for both positive and negative feedback.
6. External factors: Consider regulations, technology, and consumer trends.
7. Industry reports: Stay updated on market insights and potential disruptions.

**Remember:**

- Saturation can vary within categories, look for sub-niches with potential.
- Differentiation is key, even in saturated markets.
- Target untapped markets for growth opportunities.
- Monitor continuously as market conditions change.

**Evaluating Pricing and Profitability**

Sellers should consider factors such as FBA fees, margins, and the need for advertising when evaluating pricing and profitability.

Aiming for a margin of at least 15% is advisable to ensure profitability.

**Importance of Differentiation and Strategies to Overcome Competition**

Differentiation is key to standing out in a competitive market.

Strategies such as offering unique features, branding, and targeting underserved niches can help overcome competition.

FAQs

#### 1. What are the different Amazon selling models, and how do they impact market research?

Amazon sellers use different models that influence product research:

- Private Label – You create your own branded product (most research-intensive).
- Wholesale – You buy bulk products from brands and resell them (less research needed).
- Retail/Online Arbitrage – You find discounted products in retail stores or online to resell (quick research).
- Dropshipping – You list products from a supplier without holding inventory (focus on demand validation).

#### 2. How can I identify weaknesses in my competitors’ listings?

Use Helium 10’s Review Insights tool to analyze competitor reviews. Look for:

- Common customer complaints.
- Missing features customers request.
- Low-quality images or weak product descriptions.
  By improving on these areas, you can differentiate your product.

#### 3. How do I validate that a product has real demand before investing?

- Check search volume trends using Helium 10’s Cerebro or Magnet.
- Look at monthly sales estimates in Black Box.
- Review customer engagement on social media and forums.
- Test demand with a small batch order before committing to a large inventory.

#### 4. How do I check if a product is seasonal or trending short-term?

- Use Google Trends and Helium 10 Trendster to analyze historical search data.
- Look at past sales spikes in Helium 10 Black Box—if demand fluctuates heavily, it may be seasonal.
- Check Amazon Best Sellers and see if similar products disappear after certain months.

#### 5. How can I use Helium 10 for in-depth market research?

- Black Box – Find high-demand, low-competition products.
- Cerebro – Reverse-search ASINs to discover competitor keywords.
- Magnet – Generate keyword ideas for product listings.
- Trendster – Identify seasonal patterns.
- Xray – Analyze live product sales and demand on Amazon.

#### 6. What should I do if I enter a highly saturated market?

- Niche down – Instead of “yoga mats,” target “eco-friendly travel yoga mats.”
- Differentiate with features – Add a unique selling point (e.g., extra thickness, premium material).
- Focus on branding – A strong logo, packaging, and brand story can set you apart.
- Use PPC advertising strategically – Target long-tail, less competitive keywords.

#### 7. What pricing strategies should I use for profitability?

- Competitive pricing – Match or slightly undercut top competitors.
- Value-based pricing – Price higher if offering superior quality or features.
- Dynamic pricing – Adjust based on demand trends and inventory levels.
- Loss-leader strategy – Sell one product at a lower margin to increase sales of a higher-margin product.

#### 8. What are common mistakes new sellers make in market research?

- Choosing products with low demand due to incomplete research.
- Ignoring Amazon fees, leading to unprofitable margins.
- Picking trendy products without long-term demand.
- Failing to check for restricted or gated categories before sourcing.
- Underestimating advertising costs needed to rank new products.

#### 9. How can I position my product effectively in a competitive market?

- Develop a strong brand identity with a unique logo, packaging, and brand story.
- Use high-quality images with lifestyle shots to enhance product appeal.
- Optimize your Amazon listing with engaging bullet points, a detailed description, and A+ Content.
- Offer bundles or complementary products to add more value.

#### 10. How do I ensure my product complies with Amazon policies and legal regulations?

- Check Amazon’s restricted product list before choosing a product.
- Verify if your product requires FDA approval, safety certifications, or import restrictions.
- Conduct a trademark search to avoid legal disputes.
- If selling in multiple countries, ensure you comply with VAT and customs laws.
