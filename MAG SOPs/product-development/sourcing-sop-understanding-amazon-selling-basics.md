## **Understanding Amazon Selling Basics**

**Who is this for?**

- This Standard Operating Procedure is designed for individuals who are new to selling on Amazon or want to refresh their knowledge on the basics of Amazon selling.
- It provides a comprehensive overview of key concepts, strategies, and considerations for successful selling on the platform.

**Objective:**

- This aims to educate individuals on the fundamental aspects of selling on Amazon, including product listing, fulfillment options, fee structures, and category considerations.
- By the end of this SOP, readers should have a clear understanding of the basic principles of Amazon selling and be better equipped to start their own Amazon selling journey.

**Instructions:**

In this SOP, we will discuss some essential introductory aspects of selling on the Amazon platform. This includes understanding the difference between FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) and FBM (Fulfillment by Merchant), exploring Amazon's product categories and restrictions, and analyzing Amazon's fees and pricing structure.

  **Understanding FBA, FBM, and Vendor Central**

  - Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA):

    - Products are shipped from Amazon's fulfillment centers.
    - Sellers can gain a "Prime" badge and benefit from Amazon's shipping and customer service.
  - Fulfillment by Merchant (FBM):

    - Sellers fulfill orders themselves, shipping directly to customers.
    - Useful as a backup, especially during peak periods like Prime Day and holidays.
  - Vendor Central:

    - Primarily for large companies selling to Amazon in a wholesale model (1P).
    - Amazon handles the selling process after purchasing the items from the vendor.

1. **Selecting Product Categories**

Shoppers on Amazon find products by searching or browsing categories. Browsing lets them zoom in on what they want by picking specific categories and subcategories. To get your products seen in these browsing results, they need to be categorized correctly.

For bulk editing, use a [Browse Tree Guide (BTG)](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/G1661?mons_sel_mkid=amzn1.mp.o.ATVPDKIKX0DER&mons_sel_mcid=amzn1.merchant.o.A14CUC1LRMCT6S&mons_sel_persist=true). It guides you with unique numbers and keywords linked to specific browsing paths on Amazon.

For smaller batches, try "Add Products via Upload" (Product Classifier) for up to 30 products.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/jJ9image.png)

Alternatively, hand-pick a category for each product.

**Want to use a BTG? Here's how:**

1. Grab the right BTG from the [Inventory File Templates and BTG page](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/1641).
2. Use the guide to choose the best categories and subcategories for your products.
3. Upload your inventory template with the corresponding browse node IDs to ensure proper classification.

Here are some tips to help you make the right choice:

- **Research Popular Keywords:** Check search query performance on Amazon to identify top keywords that are searched frequently. While these keywords may not directly relate to your product, they can give you an idea of trending categories and products.
- **Consider Market Saturation:**Some popular categories, like iPad cases, may be heavily saturated with competition. While these categories may have high search volumes, it can be challenging to stand out among competitors.
- **Evaluate Your Interests and Expertise:** Choose a product category that aligns with your interests and expertise. Selling products you are passionate about can make the process more enjoyable and increase your chances of success.
- **Assess Profitability:** Research the profitability of different product categories. Consider factors like manufacturing costs, Amazon fees, and potential profit margins.
- **Look for Niche Opportunities:** Consider entering a niche market with less competition. While niche markets may have lower search volumes, they can offer higher profit margins and a more targeted audience.
- **Check for Seasonality:** Be aware of seasonal trends in different product categories. Selling seasonal products can be profitable but may require additional planning and inventory management.
- **Use Tools and Resources**: Utilize Amazon's tools and resources to help you choose the right product category. Tools like the Amazon FBA fee calculator and market research tools can provide valuable insights.
- **Stay Flexible:**Be willing to adapt your product category based on market trends and customer demand. Continuously monitor and evaluate your product selection to ensure long-term success.

FAQs

1. What is the difference between an Individual and a Professional Amazon seller account?

- Individual sellers pay a fee per item sold and have limited access to advanced tools.
- Professional sellers pay a monthly subscription fee but gain access to bulk listing tools, advertising, and eligibility for the Buy Box.

2. How do I create an Amazon seller account?

To register as a seller:

1. Go to Amazon Seller Central.
2. Click Sign Up and choose either an Individual or Professional account.
3. Provide your business information, bank account details, tax information, and verification documents.

3. How can I optimize my product listings for better visibility?

To improve your product listing:

- Use high-quality images (Amazon recommends 1000x1000 pixels or higher).
- Write compelling titles with relevant keywords.
- Create detailed bullet points highlighting product benefits.
- Write a clear and persuasive description with engaging formatting.

4. Are there restricted or gated categories on Amazon?

Yes. Some product categories require Amazon’s approval before you can sell in them. Common restricted categories include:

- Beauty & Personal Care
- Health & Household
- Grocery & Gourmet Food
- Watches & Jewelry

You can check category requirements in Seller Central > Add a Product > Check Restrictions.

5. How should I manage my inventory and shipments?

For FBA sellers, use the Inventory Performance Index (IPI) to track storage limits and avoid long-term storage fees.
For FBM sellers, ensure on-time shipping and use Amazon's Buy Shipping Services for reliable tracking.

6. How do I handle customer service and returns?

- FBA sellers: Amazon handles customer service and returns.
- FBM sellers: You must respond to customer inquiries within 24 hours and process returns according to Amazon’s policies.

7. What are Amazon’s key seller performance metrics?

Amazon monitors seller performance through:

- Order Defect Rate (ODR) – Must be below 1%.
- Late Shipment Rate – Must be below 4%.
- Pre-Fulfillment Cancel Rate – Must be below 2.5%.

8. How can I advertise my products on Amazon?

Amazon offers Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Sponsored Display Ads to help sellers increase visibility. You can set up ads in Seller Central > Advertising > Campaign Manager.

9. What should I do if my listing is suppressed or my account is suspended?

- For suppressed listings: Check Seller Central > Inventory > Fix Suppressed Listings and update missing information.
- For account suspensions: Submit an appeal with a Plan of Action (POA) addressing the issue Amazon flagged.

10. Do I need to collect sales tax or VAT when selling on Amazon?

Yes. Depending on where you sell, you may need to register for:

- Sales tax (U.S.) – Amazon collects and remits sales tax in certain states.
- VAT (Europe & UK) – Required if selling in European marketplaces.

Check Amazon’s Tax Settings in Seller Central to ensure compliance.
