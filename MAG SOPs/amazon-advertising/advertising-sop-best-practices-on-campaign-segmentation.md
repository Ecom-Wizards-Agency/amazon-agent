# Advertising SOP: Best Practices on Campaign Segmentation

## **Best Practices on Campaign Segmentation**

**Who is this for?** This SOP is intended for small business owners, entrepreneurs, and marketing professionals looking to enhance their advertising strategies through effective campaign segmentation.

**Objective:** The aim of this SOP is to present a concise set of best practices for segmenting advertising campaigns, helping users to deliver targeted and personalized ads that improve campaign performance and ROI. The guide will cover key segmentation aspects, such as demographic, geographic, behavioral, and psychographic factors, along with practical tips for leveraging data and analytics.

**Best Practices**

1. Determine How Many Campaigns Are Needed

  - Utilize [THIS SOP](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/amazon-advertising/page/advertising-sop-campaigns-in-every-account) to determine which campaigns exist, and which will need to be created.
2. Organize your Campaigns.
3. Suggested Keyword targets per campaign

  - Broad : 5 - 10 targets that are 1 - 2 words in Length that are HIGHLY RELEVANT
  - Phrase : 5 - 10 targets
  - Exact : 10 - 15 targets that are 3 - 5 words in Length that are needed to RANK organically
4. Never Segment by ASINS to start
5. Category Targeting

  - Always refine

    - New Product with Low reviews = 3 Stars and Under no price limit
    - Long-Standing Product = 4 Stars and Below with Min Price set to $2 under what the product price is.
6. Campaign Allocation

  - Sponsored Products: **60%** of your campaigns
  - Sponsored Brands: **25%** of your campaigns
  - Sponsored Display: **15%** of your campaigns

**PPC Golden Rule:** Optimize your PPC Campaigns by focusing on 1-5 keywords each, ensuring a targeted and efficient approach to boost performance *(Refer to the official Amazon PPC Guide 2025 for best practices)*

  FAQs

  - **Q1: How do I determine the number of campaigns needed?**

    - **A1:**Use the provided SOP to identify existing campaigns and determine which ones need to be created.
    - Organize campaigns for clarity and efficiency.
  - **Q2: What are the suggested keyword targets for each campaign type?**

    - **A1: Broad Match Campaigns:**

      - **Target Range:** 5–10 keywords.
      - **Keyword Length:** 1–2 words.
      - **Relevance:** Ensure they are highly relevant to the product.
    - **Phrase Match Campaigns:**

      - **Target Range:** 5–10 keywords.
    - **Exact Match Campaigns:**

      - **Target Range:** 10–15 keywords.
      - **Keyword Length:** 3–5 words.
      - **Goal:** Focus on keywords needed to rank organically.
  - **Q3: Should I segment campaigns by ASINs at the start?**

    - **A3: No.** Avoid segmenting campaigns by ASINs initially. Focus on broader targeting and refine over time based on performance data.
  - **Q4: What are the best practices for refining category targeting?**

    - **A4: For New Products with Low Reviews:**

      - Target products with 3 stars and under.
      - Do not set a price limit.
    - **For Long-Standing Products:**

      - Target products with 4 stars and below.
      - Set a minimum price at $2 under the product price to ensure competitiveness.
    - **For New Products with Low Reviews:**

      - Target products with 3 stars and under.
      - Do not set a price limit.
    - **For Long-Standing Products:**

      - Target products with 4 stars and below.
      - Set a minimum price at $2 under the product price to ensure competitiveness.
  - **Q5: How should I allocate campaigns across ad types?**

    - **A5: Sponsored Products (SP):**

      - Allocate 60% of your campaigns to SP ads.
    - **Sponsored Brands (SB):**

      - Allocate 25% of your campaigns to SB ads.
    - **Sponsored Display (SD):**

      - Allocate 15% of your campaigns to SD ads.
  - **Q6: Is there a template to review existing campaigns?**

    - **A6:**Yes. You can use the **Campaign Checklist Template** to identify opportunities and gaps across different ad types:

      - **Sponsored Products Campaign Checklist.**
      - **Sponsored Brands Campaign Checklist.**
      - **Sponsored Display Campaign Checklist.**
  - **Q7: Why is segmentation important in campaign management?**

    - **A7:**It helps in delivering targeted ads to the right audience.
    - Increases ad relevance, improving click-through rates (CTR) and conversions.
    - Optimizes campaign spend by focusing on high-performing segments.
  - **Q8: How can I refine and improve campaign segmentation over time?**

    - Continuously analyze performance data to adjust targeting.
    - Refine keyword lists and negative keywords.
    - Adjust campaign allocation as needed based on ROI and market trends.
