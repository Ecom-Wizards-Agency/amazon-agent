At this point, we should rely on our human judgment to make the final call on the keywords we choose. However, if we need some guidance, we can follow these general guidelines:

- Broad keywords typically consist of shorter terms that encompass the fundamental features of the product, such as "sage sticks," "smudge sticks," or "incense."
- Exact keywords are usually more specific, longer-tail phrases that capture the product's precise qualities.

It's critical to ensure that the chosen keywords are relevant to the product being sold. Take the time to understand the product thoroughly before commencing the keyword research process.

**Broad Keywords**

- To find Broad match keywords, it's crucial to filter the Word Count boxes to 2 and 3, as well as the Organic Rank boxes to 1 through 20. By doing so, we can identify short-tail, highly relevant keywords by filtering through the top organic keywords of our competitors. It's important to ensure that all keywords are sorted by search volume, starting with the highest and working our way down

  See the example below:

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-04-02%20at%201.01.32%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/vqAimage.png)

- Another important consideration when searching for broad match keywords is to avoid selecting keywords that already contain the root keyword more than once. This is because we want to avoid duplicate broad keywords. For example, consider the following set of keywords:

  - sage smudge
  - sage smudge sticks
  - white sage smudge sticks
- In this example, since the root phrase "sage smudge" already encompasses "sage smudge sticks" and "white sage smudge sticks," it's redundant to target all of them in a broad match. However, we can still consider targeting them separately in an exact match.

**Exact Keywords**

- When searching for Exact match keywords, it's possible (but not necessary) to filter the Word Count, to begin with, 3-4, and the Organic Rank boxes from 1 to 50. It's crucial to sort all keywords by search volume, starting from the highest to the lowest.

  It's important to note that there isn't a one-size-fits-all approach to finding the right keywords. It's essential to experiment with different filters while keeping in mind the fundamental rules we discussed earlier.

**2. Keyword Research Using Search Query Performance Report**

- Suppose we intend to advertise ASIN B09C2L41QN under the Age of Sage account.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-04-02%20at%201.04.48%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/49vimage.png)

- Here are the steps to conduct Keyword Research using the Search Query Performance Report:

  - Click on Brand Analytics.
  - Click on Search Query Performance.
  - Click on ASIN View because it provides us with specific keywords related to a specific ASIN. The Brand View, on the other hand, provides us with only keywords related to the entire brand.
  - Input the ASIN you want to conduct the Keyword Research for in the ASIN Box.
  - Select a reporting range. Usually, for comprehensive KW research, we use a Monthly or Quarterly reporting range. A weekly reporting range is often used for weekly check-ins to compare the data between specific weeks.
  - Select the year. Typically, we use the current year, but you can also use the past year to obtain more information about a particular product.
  - Choose the month if you chose Monthly Reporting Range or select a Quarter if you selected Quarterly Reporting Range.
  - Sort the keywords by Search Query Volume.
  - You can generate a CSV file by clicking on the "Generate Download" button on the right.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-04-02%20at%201.04.57%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/sgkimage.png)

- Delete the other columns retain the following - Search Query (column A), Clicks: ASIN Count (column I), Purchases: ASIN Count (column AA). Add new columns for conversion rate and word count.

  - On the conversion rate column divide the purchase over clicks and use filter to get the ones with =>10%
  - On the word count column use this formula **=IF(LEN(TRIM(A2))=0,0,LEN(TRIM(A2))-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(A2," ",""))+1)**
- It is crucial to concentrate on the most pertinent keywords and use two to three-word keywords in Phrase Match campaigns and keywords with four or more words in long-tail Exact Match campaigns.
- Here are some good and bad keyword examples for sage sticks product:

  - **Good KW** **Examples**

    - smudge kit - Broad Match
    - sage smudge - Broad Match
    - sage kit - Broad Match
    - white sage smudge kit - Exact Match
    - white sage smudge sticks - Exact Match
    - sage house cleansing kit - Exact Match
  - **Bad KW****Examples**

    - peppermint incense
    - fake sage
    - sage for death
    - sahumerio salvia blanc**a**

FAQs

- **What are broad match and exact match keywords?**

  - Answer:

    - Broad Match Keywords: Short, general terms (e.g., "sage sticks," "smudge sticks").
    - Exact Match Keywords: Specific, longer-tail terms that detail the product (e.g., "white sage smudge kit").

- **How should I filter keywords to find broad and exact matches?**

  - Answer:

    - For Broad Match: Filter word count to 2-3 and organic rank to 1-20.
    - For Exact Match: Filter word count to 3-4 and organic rank to 1-50.

- **Why is manual keyword selection recommended after sorting by search volume in Cerebro?**

  - Answer : Automated filters often group keywords based on average rankings. Manual selection allows for nuanced choices based on specific product relevance and strategy.

- **Can I conduct multi-ASIN searches in Helium 10 more efficiently?**

  - Answer : Yes, focus on high-volume, relevant keywords that most competitors rank for. This reduces the need for individual ASIN searches.

- **Is it necessary to target all broad match keywords that contain a root term?**

  - Answer: No, avoid redundant keywords. For example, if "sage smudge" already targets the root, do not include "sage smudge sticks" in broad match unless specifically needed for exact match.
