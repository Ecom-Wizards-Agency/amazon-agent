## **Campaign in Every Account**

**Who is this for?**This is intended for campaign managers who are responsible for creating and organizing campaigns for sellers. It is particularly useful for those who are new to the role and need a clear structure to follow.

**Objective:**To provide a clear and structured outline for campaign managers to follow when creating campaigns for sellers. It outlines the criteria for including ASINs in campaigns and provides a list of campaigns that should be created for each ASIN.

Here we will be outlining what campaigns**must** exist in every sellers campaign manager. If campaigns are missing.

**The following criteria are how you will decide what ASINS to include in each campaign and what campaigns you need for each ASIN.**

- If Parent listings exist, identify top child listing sellers (Top 5) and make sure that they’re included in all campaigns together;

Or

- If listings are un-parented - each ASIN and similar products (product type/sub-category) are included in all campaigns together;

Or

- If ASINs are stand-alone products, and ALSO top sellers, then ensure they’re segmented in **THEIR OWN CAMPAIGNS**

FAQs

- **Q1: Why is segmentation important for standalone ASINs?**

  - **A1:**Segmenting standalone top-selling ASINs into their own campaigns ensures better tracking, optimization, and budget allocation.
- **Q2: What should be prioritized when setting up campaigns for parent listings?**

  - **A2:**Focus on the top 5 child listings and ensure they are consistently included across all campaigns.
- **Q3: How should broad and exact match keywords be managed?**

  - **A3:**Broad Match Keywords should have dedicated campaigns, while Exact Match Keywords should be used in separate, tightly focused campaigns for precision targeting.
- **Q4: When should Sponsored Display Campaigns include Audience Targeting?**

  - **A4:**Use Audience Targeting if the HERO Product receives significant traffic but has low conversion rates.
