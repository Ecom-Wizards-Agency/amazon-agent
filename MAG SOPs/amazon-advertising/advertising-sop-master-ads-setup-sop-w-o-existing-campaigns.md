## **Master Ads SOP (With No Existing Campaigns)**

**Who is this for:**

- This is for the team responsible for setting up master ad campaigns.
- It is suitable for those who have a basic understanding of Amazon advertising and can use Seller Central and other relevant tools.
- It is also suitable for those who need to gather advertising goals, select ASINs, optimize campaigns, and analyze data to make informed decisions.

**Objective:**To provide a step-by-step guide for setting up an effective Amazon Advertising campaign for a client/seller with no existing campaigns.

d. Build the Segmentation Sheet

In the image below we have the segmentation sheet with 7 important columns

1. ASIN COMP - We target Competitor ASIN
2. KW BROAD - We target Short-tail Keywords - 2 max 3 words
3. SV - Search Volume
4. KW EXACT - We target Long-tail Keywords - 4 or more words
5. KW SELF - We target our own brand-related keywords
6. ASIN SELF - We target our own brand-related ASIN

Gold Panning - We target one-word keywords regardless of relevancy in Broad Campaigns with very low bids

Campaigns Creation and Bidding and Budgets

NOTE 1: Strategy and Ads Creation should be done according to the most up-to-date goals.

NOTE 2: If you see that there are some ASINs that we are advertising but their Listings are not optimized (in terms of Images, EBC, Bullets, Title, etc) please be aware that if we start ads of those ASINs, they may not perform very well and that could lead to the Bleeding Money.

NOTE 4: BUDGETING: Please know that our provided Budgeting Ladder is just an estimation of how many Campaigns you should create, it does not have to be the exact same every time, Budgeting and No. of new campaigns depends on your Avg CPC as well

If the Avg CPC is low in the niche then you can exceed the number of campaigns mentioned in the Budgeting Ladder Below

a. Ads Creation Strategy Based on the Budget

The most important thing that we have to have when we start a strategy it’s the budget. To have a better view of how many campaigns we should create on an account we need to convert this monthly or weekly budget into a daily one

- If the monthly budget it’s $3000 our daily budget it’s 3000/30 = $100 / day
- If the weekly budget it’s $700 our daily budget it’s 700/7 = $100 / day

Here are some mathematical calculations to find out how many campaigns we can create with how much budget.

- For example, with a $100 budget/day, we can create 10 new campaigns with $10 for each one. $10 it’s the recommended budget to start a campaign with
- As a recommendation, because the budget will not spread equally each day we can have 20% more on the current budget (This can only be applied to the new ad setups)
- For example, if our budget it’s $100 a day we can consider that the budget it’s $120 and we can create a total of 12 new campaigns with $10 each.

NOTE: We should only create campaigns that are missing for a specific ASIN or Segment. We shouldn’t create duplicate campaigns as we already checked this in part

**PPC Golden Rule:** Optimize your PPC Campaigns by focusing on 1-5 keywords each, ensuring a targeted and efficient approach to boost performance *(Refer to the official Amazon PPC Guide 2025 for best practices)*

c. Bidding

Bidding can vary differently depending on:

- Advertising Goals
- Product Price
- Both Client Goals and Product Price
- Avg CPC
- Niche (i.e. Supplements)

Always go with lower-than-suggested bids on the high-volume keywords and drop your comments on the keyword to put low bids while creating the campaign

**Bidding Rules**

- If the product price is less than $15 we should start with a bid <0.8$ – If the suggested bid is higher, than $1 we should limit it to $1 maximum
- If the Product Price is greater than $15 we should start with a bid >0.25$ but <suggested bids – If the suggested bid is higher than $1.5 - $2 we should limit it to $2 maximum

**Budget Allocation**

The budget is flexible and can be changed based on research and/or results.

- 70% Sponsored Products Ads
- 20% Sponsored Display Ads
- 10% Sponsored Brand Ads

**NOTE 1**

- If the monthly budget is very low ($500 / month) we can focus first on 100% Sponsored Products Ads.
- If the monthly budget is medium ($1000 - $3000 / month) we can spread 85% Sponsored Products, 10% Sponsored Display,s and 5% Sponsored Brands
- If the monthly budget is high (>$5000 / month) we can use our main rule 70% 20% 10%

**NOTE 2**

This budget allocation can also depend on the number of products.

- EX: If we have an account $3000 / month budget and 5 different ASINs to be advertised and we need to create Auto campaigns and KW campaigns for each of the ASINs so we can allocate 100% of the budget on Sponsored Products.

**NOTE 3**

All the above stats are only examples. We need to understand the account and make a plan based on what we find.

- EX: If the account has a lot of SB - Video campaigns that are working great we can expand our budget here and test more SB - Videos even if we can reach 30% of our budget.

**NOTE 4**

SB headline is not the best option for newly launched products, so try to stick with SP, SD, and SB video if any.

FAQs

- **Q1: How do I begin the setup process?**

  - **A1: Gather Goals:**

    - Understand the seller’s advertising goals.
    - Make a copy of the **Initial QA Sheet** and start filling it out.
  - **Select ASINs to Advertise:**

    - Use Business Reports to identify top-performing ASINs (e.g., Best Sellers by Child Variation).
    - Sort by **Order by Product Sales** and select the top 10 best-selling ASINs.
    - Combine prioritized ASINs with Best Sellers if applicable.
- **Q2: What types of campaigns should I prioritize?**

  - **A2: Sponsored Products (SP):** Best for boosting product rankings.
  - **Sponsored Display (SD):** Good for retargeting.
  - **Sponsored Brands (SB):** Useful for brand awareness but less effective for new products.
  - Focus on **SP campaigns** if basic campaigns are lacking.
- **Q3: How do I determine the budget and number of campaigns?**

  - **A3: Calculate the Daily Budget:**

    - Example: For a $3000 monthly budget, the daily budget is $3000/30 = $100/day.
  - **Estimate Campaign Numbers:**

    - Example: With a $100 daily budget, create 10 campaigns ($10 each).
    - Add 20% flexibility to account for uneven daily spend.
  - **Allocate the Budget:**

    - **Low Budget ($500/month):** Focus 100% on SP campaigns.
    - **Medium Budget ($1000–$3000/month):** Allocate 85% to SP, 10% to SD, and 5% to SB.
    - **High Budget (>$5000/month):** Use a 70%-20%-10% split across SP, SD, and SB.
- **Q4: How do I handle bidding strategies?**

  - **A4:**For **high-volume keywords:** Start with lower-than-suggested bids.
  - **Bidding Rules:**

    - **Product Price < $15:** Start bids at <$0.8, limit to $1 max if suggested bid is higher.
    - **Product Price > $15:** Start bids >$0.25 but <$2 max if suggested bid is higher.
  - Consider advertising goals, product price, and niche when setting bids.
- **Q5: What should I do before launching campaigns?**

  - **A5: Complete Keyword Research:**

    - Use tools like Helium10 or Search Query Reports.
    - Remove duplicate keywords or campaigns.
  - **Check Listing Optimization:**

    - Ensure ASINs have optimized titles, images, EBC (Enhanced Brand Content), and bullet points.
    - Avoid advertising unoptimized listings to prevent wasted ad spend.
  - **Align Strategy with Client Goals:**

    - Ensure campaigns reflect the client’s goals and product potential.
- **Q6: What are some important notes to remember?**

  - **A6: Do not duplicate campaigns.**
  - For new products, focus on SP, SD, and SB video campaigns instead of SB headline ads.
  - Adjust budget allocation based on account needs and performance.
- **Q7: What if the account has existing campaigns?**

  - **A7:**Rename or adjust old campaigns and portfolios before starting new ones.
  - Use existing campaign data for keyword and ASIN research.
