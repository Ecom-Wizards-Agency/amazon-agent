# Advertising SOP: Amazon Marketing Cloud

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/amazon-advertising/page/advertising-sop-amazon-marketing-cloud

## **Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC)**

- **Who is this for:** Marketing and advertising teams who want to leverage Amazon’s data to analyze, segment, and optimize campaigns.
- **Objective:** To provide advanced, privacy-safe analytics and audience creation tools that improve targeting, measurement, and advertising performance.

Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC) – Overview and Usage

#### **Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC) – Overview and Usage**

**What Is Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC)**

Amazon Marketing Cloud is a **data clean room environment** provided by Amazon that allows advertisers to access, analyze, and visualize cross-channel marketing data across **Sponsored Ads**, **DSP**, and other Amazon advertising solutions.

Previously limited to enterprise-level sellers through DSP access, AMC is now **available to all Amazon sellers**, enabling advanced analytics and audience targeting without requiring third-party tools or API knowledge.

[**Video Reference**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QEKnfCRT9EQ)

**Key Features and Functions**

**AMC Dashboard Overview**

Accessible from Seller Central, the AMC dashboard includes:

- **Home:** Central hub for account insights and reports.
- **Use Cases:** Pre-built analysis templates for common marketing questions.
- **Query Editor:** Advanced data query environment (optional for technical users).
- **Projects:** Space for saving and organizing analysis workflows.
- **Audiences:** Area for building and managing custom audience segments.
- **Paid Features:** Additional tools for enhanced analytics (optional upgrades).

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-10/scaled-1680-/TlIimage.png)

**Core Components and Use Cases**

**Insights**

- Found on the homepage; provides **instant analytics** visualizations.
- Examples:

  - **Sponsored Ads and DSP Overlap:** Shows how campaigns overlap across ad types.
  - **Time to Conversion:** Measures average duration from ad exposure to purchase.
  - **Audience Overlap Analysis:** Identifies shared audiences across campaigns.

**Purpose:** Helps visualize how campaigns interact and identifies areas for optimization in targeting and frequency.

**Use Cases and No-Code Features**

- The **Use Cases** section provides ready-made analytical models.
- The **No-Code** section enables users to execute prebuilt data solutions without technical expertise.

**Common No-Code Use Cases:**

1. **High-Value Audience Solution:** Identifies top-spending or high-engagement users.
2. **Purchase Overlap for Upselling:** Reveals opportunities to cross-sell between ASINs.
3. **Sponsored Ads and DSP Overlap:** Measures ad duplication or synergy.
4. **Audience Overlap Analysis:** Compares segments to identify shared or unique users.
5. **Path to Conversion by Campaign Group:** Maps out the customer journey from first exposure to purchase.
6. **Reach & Impression Frequency Report:** Evaluates ad exposure levels and engagement quality.

**Recommended For:** Sellers spending **$1,000–$5,000+ per month** on advertising.

**Building and Using Audience Groups**

**What Are Audience Groups**

Audience groups are **custom segments** built within AMC that can be used for **targeted advertising** in Sponsored Ads or DSP campaigns.

These allow advertisers to:

- Create tailored campaigns for specific customer behaviors.
- Apply granular targeting without external data.
- Use similar strategies as seen in Meta (Facebook) advertising platforms.

**Recommended Audience Segments**

| Audience Type | Purpose | Best Use Case |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Added to Cart but Not Purchased | Retarget users who showed intent but didn’t convert. | Brand Tailored Promotions and remarketing campaigns. |
| High-Value, New-to-Brand Customers | Capture high-spending new customers within a category. | Category targeting and customer acquisition campaigns. |
| Frequently Exposed Audiences | Maintain brand visibility among users repeatedly exposed to your ads. | Defensive or awareness campaigns. |
| High-Value Customer Segments | Target users who regularly purchase high-value items on Amazon. | Auto campaigns using Close Match or Lookalike types. |

**Applying Audience Groups in Sponsored Ads**

Once audience groups are built in AMC, they can be linked to **Sponsored Ads campaigns**:

- Go to **Campaign Manager** in Seller Central.
- Select **Create Campaign** under Sponsored Ads.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-10/scaled-1680-/4MVimage.png)

- Scroll to **Bid Adjustments → Audiences.**

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-10/scaled-1680-/dBtimage.png)

- You’ll see:

  - **Built by Amazon** audiences:

    - Purchased Brand’s Product
    - Clicked or Added Brand’s Product to Cart
    - High Interest (based on shopping history)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-10/scaled-1680-/96Jimage.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-10/scaled-1680-/eupimage.png)

- - **AMC-built custom audiences** (if created).
- Choose your AMC audience and adjust bids based on your strategy.

**Tip:** Use higher bid adjustments for retargeting segments (e.g., cart abandoners) and moderate increases for lookalike audiences.

**Update as of 12/3/2025:**

When the Auto-Adjust Date option is selected as "Yes", it is not necessary to resubmit AMC Audiences. By default, AMC audiences in DSP are refreshed daily. Amazon offers the option to set the “frequency of audience refresh,” meaning you can configure it to update regularly so that the audience stays timely and reflects new user behavior.

This configuration should be set with careful consideration. (For instance, if you are creating a brand cart abandoner campaign specific to BF/CM audience targeting, you want it at a stationary)

**Practical Strategies**

- **Upselling & Cross-Selling:** Use “Purchase Overlap” data to recommend related ASINs.
- **Customer Retention:** Retarget “Added to Cart but Not Purchased” audiences with special offers.
- **New Customer Acquisition:** Focus on “New-to-Brand” audiences to expand market share.
- **Performance Optimization:** Use “Path to Conversion” to identify and strengthen underperforming campaign stages.

**Summary**

| AMC Feature | Primary Benefit |
| --- | --- |
| Insights | Understand cross-campaign performance and audience overlap. |
| Use Cases | Access prebuilt, no-code analytics solutions. |
| Audience Groups | Create and apply detailed segmentation for ad targeting. |
| Sponsored Ad Integration | Use AMC audiences to optimize bids and campaign performance. |

Getting Started with Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC)

#### **Getting Started with Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC)**

**Procedure**

**Step 1: Register and Enable AMC**

- Sign up for an **AWS Account** (if not already created)
- Navigate to the **AWS Management Console**
- Enable **AMC**

**Step 2: Integrate Data Sources**

- Connect existing platforms such as:

  - CRM systems
  - Email marketing platforms
  - Amazon Ads solutions (Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, DSP)
  - Other first-party insight sources
- Verify that all integrated data is:

  - Accurate
  - Clean
  - Up-to-date

**Step 3: Set Up User Permissions**

- Assign user roles to team members (e.g., Admin, Analyst, Campaign Manager)
- Control access based on responsibility and security needs

**Step 4: Explore AMC Features**

- Review tools for:

  - Campaign management
  - Audience segmentation
  - Personalization
  - Analytics and reporting

**Step 5: Import and Organize Customer Insights**

- Import customer data by:

  - Manual uploads
  - Automated integrations
- Consolidate interactions from multiple channels into one view

**Step 6: Create Audience Segments**

- Segment audiences based on:

  - Demographics
  - Purchase history
  - Behaviors and interactions
- Save and label segments clearly for campaign use

**Step 7: Design and Launch Campaigns**

- Build campaigns using AMC campaign management tools
- Deploy campaigns across channels such as:

  - Amazon Ads (SP, SB, DSP)
  - Social media
  - Email
  - Display networks
- Schedule campaigns with:

  - Lifecycle triggers
  - Automated workflows

**Step 8: Monitor and Analyze Performance**

- Track performance metrics such as:

  - Click-through rate (CTR)
  - Conversion rate
  - Return on investment (ROI)
  - Open rate (for email campaigns)
- Use **Amazon Marketing Stream** to receive:

  - Hourly campaign metrics
  - Near real-time performance updates

**Step 9: Optimize Campaigns**

- Apply continuous improvements through:

  - **A/B testing** (subject lines, CTAs, creatives)
  - **Personalization** for specific audience segments
  - **Automation** to deliver timely and relevant messages
  - **Channel performance reviews** to allocate budget effectively

**Best Practices for Optimization**

- Maintain clean and updated customer data
- Refine audience segments regularly to match changing behaviors
- Personalize campaigns to increase engagement and conversions
- Leverage automation to reduce manual workload
- Continuously test and iterate campaign elements

**Notes**

- AMC does not serve ads directly—it integrates with:

  - Amazon Ads (SP, SB, DSP)
  - Social media platforms
  - Search engines
  - Display networks and ad exchanges
  - Email services
  - Mobile ad networks
- Ads appear on their respective channels, not within AMC itself
- Amazon Ads support is available on request (budget minimums apply)

Creating Lookalike and Rule-Based Audiences in Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC)

#### **Creating Lookalike and Rule-Based Audiences in Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC)**

**Procedure**

**Step 1: Navigate to Audience Filters**

- In your campaign manager, access **Amazon Marketing Cloud** under Measurement & Reporting.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/WBgimage.png)

- Select **Audiences**> **Create audience** > **Create with instructional query**

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/hO9image.png)

- Select **Audience Filter**.
- Choose your base audience type, such as:

  - Cart abandoners (added to cart but did not purchase).
  - Wishlist or registry adders.
  - DSP campaign viewers (excluding other exposures).

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/ikaimage.png)

**Step 2: Define Audience Type**

- Once an audience category is filtered, select your **Audience Type**:

  - Rule-Based Audience → built from defined behaviors (e.g., cart abandoners).
  - Lookalike Audience → modeled from an existing audience.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/Sjuimage.png)

- If using a lookalike audience, choose similarity tier:

  - Most Similar - Audience size will be between 300K – 1M depending on relevance and audience geography.
  - Similar - Audience size will be between 450K – 3M depending on relevance and audience geography and will include Most similar lookalike population.
  - Balanced - Audience size will be between 600K – 6M depending on relevance and audience geography and will include Similar lookalike population.
  - Broad - Audience size will be between 750K – 8M depending on relevance and audience geography and will include Balanced lookalike population.
  - Most Broad - Audience size will be between 900K – 10M depending on relevance and audience geography and will include Broad lookalike population.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/WeLimage.png)

**Step 3: Name and Describe Audience**

- Enter a unique audience name using the format: [BrandName]_[AudienceType]_[DateRange].

  - Example: Lego_CartAbandoners_Last30Days.
- Add a **description** that includes:

  - Specific ASINs or portfolios included.
  - Purpose of audience (e.g., retargeting, awareness).
  - Campaign alignment notes.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/lOOimage.png)

**Step 4: Configure Audience Parameters**

- **Date Range**:

  - Default is last 30 days.
  - Extend up to 365 days if the audience size is too small.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/WFBimage.png)

- **ASIN to include**:

  - Include ASINs to limit audience to specific ASINs or product categories.
  - Keep relevance high (avoid mixing unrelated categories).
- **Auto-Adjust Date**:

  - Leave as is unless dynamic date adjustment is required.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/klpimage.png)

**Step 5: Create the Audience**

- Review setup and click **Create Audience**.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/46Ximage.png)

- Audience creation process:

  - Amazon validates size and eligibility.
  - Processing time is typically 30–60 minutes.
  - Audience must contain at least **2,000 people** to be used in Sponsored Ads.

**Step 6: Troubleshoot Audience Size Limitations**

- If the audience is too small:

  - Expand the **date range** (up to 365 days).
  - Broaden ASIN or product filters.
- If the audience still fails:

  - Use **lookalike modeling** to increase reach.

**Step 7: Audience Availability**

- Audiences typically appear in **Campaign Manager** within 48 hours.
- To find them:

  - Go to **Campaigns** → **Bid Adjustments** → **Audiences**.
  - Options will display **All Shoppers** plus any custom AMC audiences.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-09/scaled-1680-/n9kimage.png)

**Notes & Limitations**

- Minimum audience size: **2,000 users** for Sponsored Ads.
- Processing time: 30–60 minutes to validate, up to 48 hours to appear in Campaign Manager.
- Use descriptive naming conventions when managing multiple accounts or brands.
- Audience applicability depends on product relevance — do not mix unrelated product categories.

**Best Practices**

- Always include **brand name + date range** in the audience name.
- Use the **description field** to capture details on scope, ASINs, or product restrictions.
- Start with **rule-based audiences**, then expand with **lookalike audiences** to scale reach.
- Revisit and adjust **date ranges** and **filters** if audiences are consistently too small.
