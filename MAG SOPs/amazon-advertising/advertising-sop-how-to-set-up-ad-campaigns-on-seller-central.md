## **How to Set Up Ad Campaigns on Seller Central**

**Who this is for :** This SOP is intended for Amazon sellers who have registered on the Seller Central platform and are looking to set up ad campaigns to promote their products. The audience should have a basic understanding of how the Seller Central platform works and should be familiar with the product they want to advertise. Additionally, the audience should have an understanding of basic digital marketing concepts, such as keywords and targeting.

**Objective:** The objective of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to provide step-by-step instructions for setting up ad campaigns on Seller Central, a platform for Amazon sellers to manage their online business. The purpose of this SOP is to help Amazon sellers effectively promote their products and increase their sales by using the advertising features available on Seller Central.

**Step-by-Step Instructions**

1. Log in to Seller Central. Go to the marketplace switcher and choose the account where you will set up the ad campaign.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1721672652460.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/oSRimage.png)

2. Go to Manage All Inventory to see what products they currently have. This will help you create ad groups depending on the product.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-M5BTQWO6.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wNeimage.png)

3. Create a Google Sheet where you can separate and sort products

**Note: Segment the products if they have the same category but different kinds (example below). A product with multiple sizes is the same product (e.g. packs, size) and a bunch of products that have the same traits such as sauce or jam could all have a generic ad group with generic keywords. It is also recommended that you create different tabs for different ad campaigns you will have to create (e.g, Manual, Competitor, Brand)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712252038549.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Vhcimage.png)

4. Next, go to Advertising > Campaign Manager

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712252046461.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/yGAimage.png)

5. Select Create Campaign

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1721672753754.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/eSNimage.png)

6. Choose Sponsored Products

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1721673003374.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/CdKimage.png)

Important note: The placement of each items in the "Create Campaign" page will be different depending on updates from Amazon, but, the steps will essentially be the same.

7. Create Campaign name, set Daily budget to how much the seller budget is and choose Manual Targeting

**Note: You will usually have to create 4 different campaigns (Manual, Brand, Competitor, and Automatic -- for Automatic campaigns, instead of choosing Manual Targeting, you will choose Automatic ) always put (MAG) for us to identify who did it (MAG - My Amazon Guy). For the Daily budget, if the seller hasn’t provided it yet, put $10)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/t8jimage.png)

4. Choose Keyword Targeting

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/3Oximage.png)

5. Select the Top 5 Suggested Keywords. Use the lowest suggested bid or if it exceeds $1.5 set the bid to $1. Launch Broad and Phrase match campaigns separately at the beginning especially if there are no existing campaigns.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-11-22%20at%204.56.31%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Y6Wimage.png)

6. Go to Amazon and search for the same kind of product you are creating the keywords for

**Note: Make sure you enter the same type of material they use (e.g., band rings gold plated)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/advertisement-setup-on-amazon-image-vwi4rkxb(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/PmDimage.png)

7. Choose at least 2-3 products that are related to the product we are going to advertise

**Note: Choose only the ones ranking organic and not sponsored

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1682008993776.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/p3pimage.png)

8. Go to Helium10, enter the ASIN of the products you checked (one at a time) and click on Get Keywords

**Note: It will show you the keywords that this product is being ranked for and then we can just go through the keywords and take the ones that we think will do well on the product we are working on

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-3B1YYL4H.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/m00image.png)

9. Scroll down to the keywords result, filter it by Search Volume

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-0GLKM7XR.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/lxnimage.png)

10. Find Keywords that are relevant to the product, have a high volume, and have a high organic ranking. High organic rankings tell us that this particular keyword converts well for this product.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-BHE6G1Y4.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/QrUimage.png)

11. Copy and paste the Top 5 keywords into the “Enter List” section back in the campaign creator. Launch Broad and Phrase Match Campaigns separately especially if there are no existing campaigns. Use the lowest suggested bid or if it exceeds $1.5 set it at $1.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/0.53%20(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Rwsimage.png)

**Note: Browse through the keywords and make sure that there’s nothing too high on the bids (e.g., higher than $1.5)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-11-22%20at%204.56.31%E2%80%AFAM(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Chmimage.png)

**PPC Golden Rule:** Optimize your PPC Campaigns by focusing on 1-5 keywords each, ensuring a targeted and efficient approach to boost performance *(Refer to the official Amazon PPC Guide 2025 for best practices)*

12. Put the brand name on the Negative Keywords for Automatic and Manual campaigns, add it and click on Launch Campaign/Create Ad Group

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/advertisement-setup-on-amazon-image-0yjjqtx5(1).png)

FAQs

- **Q1: Why is it recommended to use a Google Sheet for organizing products?**

  - **A1:**A Google Sheet helps to separate and sort products by category and type, making it easier to create targeted ad groups and track campaigns.
- **Q2: How should products be grouped for campaigns?**

  - **A2:**Group products with similar traits (e.g., same category or product type). For products with variations (e.g., sizes or packs), they can be grouped under one generic ad group with appropriate keywords.
- **Q3: What is the purpose of Competitor Campaigns?**

  - **A3:**Competitor Campaigns target similar products by using competitor brand names and keywords to increase visibility among buyers searching for related items.
- **Q4: How should keyword research be conducted?**

  - **A4:**Use tools like Helium10 or Junglescout to find high-volume, high-ranking keywords relevant to the product.
  - Filter results by search volume and organic ranking to identify the most effective keywords.
- **Q5: Should negative keywords be used in campaigns?**

  - **A5:**Yes, add the brand name as a negative keyword in both Automatic and Manual Campaigns to optimize spending.
- **Q6: Why is it important to launch Broad and Phrase Match Campaigns separately?**

  - **A6:**Separating Broad and Phrase Match campaigns ensures more precise targeting and allows better tracking of keyword performance.
- **Q7: How do you decide which products to target in a Competitor Campaign?**

  - **A7:**Focus on organic, high-ranking products similar to the seller’s items and use keyword tools to extract relevant data.
- **Q8: What is the purpose of creating Defensive Campaigns?**

  - **A8:**Defensive Campaigns protect a seller’s traffic by targeting their own product listings, reducing the chance of losing customers to competitors.
