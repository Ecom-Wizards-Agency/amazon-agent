# Advertising SOP: Negation Guidance

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/amazon-advertising/page/advertising-sop-negation-guidance

## **Negation Guidance**

**Who this is for:** This negation guidance is intended for the advertising and marketing team, as well as any other relevant stakeholders responsible for managing and optimizing Amazon advertising campaigns. This could include team members responsible for setting and monitoring advertising budgets, analyzing performance metrics, and optimizing campaigns for maximum ROI.

**Objective:** The objective of this negation guidance is to provide a standardized and data-driven process for Amazon advertising budget planning, ensuring that negations are efficiently allocated to maximize ROI and meet business objectives. This guidance includes specific criteria for determining when to negate ASINs and keywords, as well as recommendations for monitoring and optimizing negations over time.

**Timing:** Negations must be performed at least once a week on ALL active campaign groups, per account. (Where applicable).

- Keyword and ASIN Negation is an essential part of optimizing Amazon advertising campaigns.

**Variable for consideration**

- Whether or not the product is new or established.

  - If the product is new and has little to no reviews and we’re trying to gain traction, we may allow for additional clicks/spend. However, if the product is established, we may negate the target earlier.

**Negation Criteria for Negative Exact:**

1. ACOS =>90%
2. With spend and 0 order

  - Spend: 60% of the product price
  - Clicks 20 and above

Advance Negations:

- If a product revolves around models, you can negate the most searched model or size in advance so that your campaign doesn’t spend on irrelevant searches.
- You can use your common sense and analyze most searched behaviors from keyword research and can add negations in advance which you believe will spend in the future and you will not convert as well.

  - Ex. If you are selling an iPhone X protector then you should negate iPhone Xs and iPhone XS Max so that your ads don’t show on these searches
  - Ex. You are selling curtain rod size 12 to 30 Inches and upon checking the most searched keywords you found out curtain rod 50 inches has a high search volume then you can add it as negative in advance.
- Similarly, If you are new in the market and launching a new product with less reviews and ratings then you can negate your top competitor's products and brand name in advance so that your ad doesn’t show up against their searches, use your senses If you are adding value in terms of price or product quality/functionality then you can leave it but if the top competitors are better in all areas then you should negate them in your new Campaigns so that you can avoid waste spending.

  - Ex: You are selling a resistance band and your top competitors(who are selling the exact product) in the market have >5k reviews, ratings above 4.3, well Known famous brands with a price closer to or less than your product then add their ASIN’s and brand name in advance so that you don’t compete with them in your starting phase.

**Note:** If a keyword phrase is critical to a campaign's organic rankings, we should always try to avoid negating the phrase. If it doesn’t perform well, choose to lower the bid to get as close to profitability as possible.

- Ex. We would not want to negate the phrase “tweezers” if we were advertising tweezers.
- Ex. We would not want to negate the phrase “watercolor paint set,” if we were advertising a watercolor paint set.

FAQs

- **Q1: How often should negations be performed?**

  - **A1:**Negations should be conducted at least **once a week** on all active campaign groups per account, wherever applicable.
- **Q2: What factors should be considered before implementing negations?**

  - **A2:**Whether the product is **new** or **established**:

    - **New Products**: Allow additional clicks/spend to gain traction.
    - **Established Products**: Negate targets sooner to avoid wasteful spending.
- **Q3: What are the criteria for negating keywords or ASINs?**

  - **A3:**Negate keywords or ASINs based on the following criteria:

    - **Negative Exact Match**:

      - **ACOS** ≥ 90% with spend and no orders.
      - **Spend** exceeds 60% of the product price.
      - **Clicks** ≥ 20 without any orders.
    - **Unprofitable Customer Search Terms**:

      - Zero orders over the past 30–65 days but high spend (above 60% of the product price).
      - Keywords or ASINs with ACOS ≥ 90%.
- **Q4: How do I retrieve the data needed for negations?**

  - **A4:**Steps

    - Go to **Advertising > Campaign Manager > Reports**.
    - Create a report:

      - Select **Sponsored Products** for Report Category.
      - Select **Search Term** for the Report Type.
      - Choose the date range (30D or 65D) and run the report.
    - Download the CSV file and analyze metrics such as Customer Search Terms, Spend, ACOS, and Clicks.
- **Q5: How do I exclude unprofitable keywords and ASINs?**

  - **A5:**Filter search terms with:

    - 7 Day Total Orders = 0.
    - Spend exceeding 60% of the product price.
    - ACOS ≥ 90%.
  - Apply filters:

    - Match Type = Exact.
    - Text Filters = "Does Not Contain" specific ASINs or keywords.
  - Negate these terms in your campaigns.
- **Q6: What is the role of advanced negations?**

  - **A7:**Advanced negations are proactive measures to exclude irrelevant or unprofitable search terms before they incur costs. Examples include:

    - Negating irrelevant product models, sizes, or competitors.
    - Excluding terms unlikely to convert based on keyword research.
    - Filtering searches for products with overwhelmingly strong competitors.
- **Q7: Can you provide examples of advanced negations?**

  - **A7: Model Negations**: If selling an iPhone X protector, negate searches for iPhone XS or XS Max.
  - **Size Negations**: If selling curtain rods for 12–30 inches, negate searches for 50-inch curtain rods.
  - **Competitor Negations**: If launching a resistance band with fewer reviews than competitors, negate their ASINs and brand names.
- **Q8: Should critical keywords be negated if they are unprofitable?**

  - **A8:**No, avoid negating critical keywords that impact organic rankings. Instead:

    - Lower the bid to improve profitability.
    - Example: For a “watercolor paint set” campaign, do not negate this keyword even if it performs poorly.
- **Q9: How do logical negations improve campaign efficiency?**

  - **A9:**Logical negations exclude irrelevant gender or product-specific terms immediately. Examples:

    - For a **women’s product**, negate terms like "men," "guy," or "male."
    - For a **men’s product**, negate terms like "women," "lady," or "female."
- **Q10: What should I do if my campaign data suggests specific patterns?**

  - **A10:**Use insights from keyword research and performance data to refine negations dynamically. For instance:

    - Focus on search behaviors to identify trends.
    - Expand negations on terms proven unprofitable over time.
- **Q11: Can negations be adjusted after implementation?**

  - **A11:**Yes, negations should be revisited regularly to ensure they align with campaign performance and objectives. Adjust as necessary based on new data or shifts in market conditions.
