## **How to Setup and Manage a New Ads Account**

**Who This is For:**This is designed for Walmart Marketplace sellers, digital marketing professionals, and e-commerce managers who are new to the Walmart advertising ecosystem and seek a comprehensive guide to establish and maintain a successful advertising account for their products or services.

**Objective:** To provide clear and concise instructions on how to set up and manage a new advertising account for Walmart sellers, ensuring that they can effectively create, launch, and monitor advertising campaigns to increase sales and brand visibility on the platform.

**Step 1: Understand the Account and Client Goals**

- Before getting started you will need to understand the client's accounts, their product line, and their budget goals.
- Understanding the product category will help you get an idea of what the budget will look like to successfully set up a campaign. Feel free to do a quick research on their product and see where ASIN's rank is.
- The following questions should always be asked:

  - What is the overall budget?
  - Does the client want all items advertised?
  - What are their growth goals?
- While some of these items can be found on our onboarding survey, some information will need to be gathered from the client themselves, or the account manager.
- Always review the onboarding survey before you start work.

**Step 2: Evaluate the Current Structure and Understanding of the Product we are Advertising**

- No matter if an account has hundreds of products or just a couple, and no matter if a client has hundreds of campaigns or just two or three, there typically is a structure involved on every account that MAG will be managing.
- Many times the client will have their own legacy campaigns active from either their self-management or a previous agency or company.
- For my example, I will be using the client AJA Legacy Group. This client sells products in the following categories:

**Categories:**

- Dryer Sheet Dispensers

- Collapsible Laundry Baskets

**KPIs:**

ACOS: Under 30%

FAQs

1. How do I select the most effective keywords for my Walmart campaigns?

To select effective keywords for your Walmart campaigns:

- Analyze Competitor Keywords: Use tools like Helium 10’s Cerebro to identify high-volume keywords competitors in your category are ranking for.
- Use Long-Tail Keywords: Focus on long-tail keywords, such as “artificial roses for decoration,” to capture niche searches with less competition.
- Refine Search Term Reports: Review the Search Term Summary from previous campaigns to find high-converting keywords.
- Leverage Amazon Autocomplete: Use Amazon’s search suggestions and auto-fill feature to discover keywords that customers are frequently searching.

2. How do I measure and analyze campaign performance on Walmart?

To measure campaign performance, use the following metrics:

- CTR (Click-Through Rate): Indicates how effective your ads are at attracting clicks. A low CTR suggests your ads may need better targeting or creative adjustments.
- ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sale): This metric helps you understand the cost of generating sales through your ads. Aim for an ACOS under 30% for profitable campaigns.
- CPC (Cost Per Click): Track this to ensure you are bidding competitively for your keywords.
- Conversion Rate: Measures how many clicks resulted in purchases. A low conversion rate could mean that your landing page or product description needs optimization. You can use Walmart’s Reporting Tools to monitor these metrics regularly and adjust your campaign strategy as needed.

3. What should I do if I notice underperformance or errors during my campaign launch?

If you encounter issues or underperformance:

- Address Keyword Issues: If a keyword is underperforming, adjust the bid or replace it with better-performing ones.
- Review Error Reports: If there are errors during setup, download the error report from the Activity Feed and fix the identified issues (e.g., incorrect CPC, missing product IDs).
- Pause Low-Performing Ads: If certain ads or keywords are consistently underperforming, consider pausing them or adjusting your targeting strategy.
- Reallocate Budget: Shift your budget toward higher-performing campaigns or keywords to maximize ROI.

4. How do I optimize my Walmart campaigns over time?

Campaign optimization is an ongoing process:

- Adjust Bids Regularly: Use performance data to adjust your bids for high-performing keywords to ensure you are winning impressions without overspending.
- Use A/B Testing: Experiment with different ad creatives, headlines, and keywords to find the most effective combinations.
- Refine Keyword Selection: Based on your performance reports, eliminate underperforming keywords and add new ones that show promise.
- Reallocate Budget: Adjust your budget allocation to favor the campaigns that drive the highest ROI.

5. What are the best practices for organizing and managing Ad Groups?

When managing Ad Groups, follow these best practices:

- Group Similar Products: Organize products with similar attributes or categories into the same Ad Group for better targeting and reporting.
- Naming Conventions: Use consistent naming conventions for Ad Groups to make it easier to track performance (e.g., “SP_Keyword - Manual - Exact - Roses”).
- Limit the Number of Products: For better manageability, keep the number of products in an Ad Group to 50-100 items. This ensures each product gets adequate visibility without cluttering the group.
- Use Separate Ad Groups for Different Match Types: For manual campaigns, create separate Ad Groups for Exact, Phrase, and Broad match keywords to ensure better tracking and optimization.

6. How should I allocate my budget across multiple campaigns?

Budget allocation should be strategic:

- Identify High-Performing Campaigns: Allocate more budget to campaigns that are generating the most traffic and conversions.
- Use a Daily Budget Strategy: Start with a daily budget and adjust based on performance. For example, if certain keywords or campaigns are underperforming, reduce their budget.
- Adjust for Match Types: Allocate more budget to high-converting Exact Match campaigns and less to Broad Match or Phrase Match campaigns.
- Campaign Goals: Tailor budget allocation based on your growth goals. If brand awareness is the goal, consider prioritizing automatic campaigns and broad match keywords.

7. What should I do if I have too many duplicate or redundant campaigns?

If you have duplicate or redundant campaigns:

- Consolidate Campaigns: Avoid creating new campaigns if existing ones are already performing well. Instead, restructure or consolidate redundant campaigns to use the existing data.
- Pause Low-Performing Campaigns: If a campaign is underperforming or overlapping with others, pause it and focus on campaigns that are delivering better results.
- Use Data to Restructure: Take advantage of data from existing campaigns, including search term reports, to optimize targeting and bid settings.

8. How do I target competitor item pages with my Walmart ads?

To target competitor item pages:

- Use Item Titles and Brands: Use the Exact Match for targeting competitor item pages based on their title or brand.
- Phrase Match for Broader Targeting: For broader targeting, use Phrase Match keywords that include competitor product names or categories.
- Leverage Product Taxonomy: If you don’t know the competitor’s exact item, use broad or phrase match keywords related to their product category or taxonomy.

9. Can I add keywords to my Walmart campaign after it is live?

Yes, you can add keywords after your campaign is live:

- Edit Keywords in Ad Groups: Go to the Bidded Keywords tab, click edit next to the ad group, and add new keywords as necessary.
- Add Additional Keywords: You can also use the Additional Keywords tab to add more keywords to improve targeting.
- Adjust Bid Strategy: For the newly added keywords, adjust your bids according to their relevance and expected performance.
