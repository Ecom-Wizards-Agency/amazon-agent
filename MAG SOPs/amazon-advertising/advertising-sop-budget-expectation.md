# Advertising SOP: Budget Expectation

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/amazon-advertising/page/advertising-sop-budget-expectation

## **Budget Expectation**

**Who is this for?**

- This is intended for all marketing team members, advertising managers, digital marketing specialists, and other personnel involved in the Amazon advertising budgeting process within the organization.
- This includes staff responsible for campaign planning, execution, monitoring, and reporting, as well as any decision-makers involved in setting and approving advertising budgets and strategies for Amazon advertising campaigns.

**Objective:**

- To establish a comprehensive and consistent framework for managing Amazon's advertising budget expectations within the organization.
- To ensure that all relevant departments and personnel involved in the Amazon advertising budgeting process are aligned in their understanding of goals, priorities, and constraints.

**Steps**:

1. Know the budget.

  - This should be updated on the Asana task or indicated in the onboarding survey. (Ask Account Manager if there are questions)
2. Verify Budget is in Asana

  - You should do a budget check three times a week for ALL Managed accounts. How do I do a budget check?

**Budget Check:**

1. Verify Weekly Budget
2. Login to the Amazon Seller Central Console and select the date range. Let’s say you are checking on a Wednesday.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/advertising-sop-budget-expectation-image1.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Z5cimage.png)

1. In our example we have a $4,800 weekly budget. To calculate what average daily spend should be to maintain the budget, simply divide the budget by 7, and then times that number by the days in the select period.

  - $4,800 / 7 = $685 per day and then * 4 = 2742. In our case we had spent $2,665.95 in this period, so we are right on budget. However, if we had not been on budget, and instead had spent $3,700 by wednesday, you would need to take the following action steps.
  - Or check 7 day spend, overall and then check to see how each day is doing on a daily spend, to just double check to see that there’s any evening out of spend.

**Action Steps for Going Out of Budget:**

1. Communicate directly with your Account Manager and tag the weekly Asana task. “We have spent $3,700 of our $4,000 budget, which means we need to reduce ad spend for the remainder of the week by xx number in order to stay on budget. Please reach out to the client and let them know that our budget has spent significantly more than expected and ask them if they would like us to:

- Keep the current spending (if we have a valid explained reason)
- Turn off ads (to stop spending) - warn them that this is a bad idea overall
- Significantly decrease bids for this week, find cause for increase, and correct, so it will not be an issue going forward.

1. **Make Adjustments:**

  - Sort ad spend by top spending campaigns for the period in question as shown below.

    ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/advertising-sop-budget-expectation-image2.png)

    ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/1Q5image.png)

1. Check ACOS. If ACOS is high and spend is also high, this shows “wasted spend” and that optimization is needed. See example below.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/B2Ximage.png)

**Steps to Reduction of Wasted spend:**

[https://www.loom.com/share/b5d566cf1cad4163a83cfb3a285985a5](https://www.loom.com/share/b5d566cf1cad4163a83cfb3a285985a5)

- Use negations for heavy spending search terms based on STR. - [Negation Guidance SOP](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/amazon-advertising/page/advertising-sop-negation-guidance) - Negations are key on Auto, Broad, and Phrase Match Type Campaigns.
- Utilize CPC to see the value of your bid. If your CPC is below the current bid, lowering bids will have less of an impact, than if the CPC was at or higher than the current bid. Do keep in mind that there are sometimes dynamic bidding, so this can have some influence here as well.
- Change Bidding Strategy from Up/Down or Fixed bids to Down Only
- Significantly lower bids by using ***max bid formula***. *avg conv rate x avg selling price x acos target %*

  - [*http://www.marketingmo.com/campaigns-execution/how-to-calculate-your-max-bid-price/*](http://www.marketingmo.com/campaigns-execution/how-to-calculate-your-max-bid-price/)
- Heavily check relevancy. (conversion rates/CTR for proposed keywords and bids)
- Pausing campaigns should be a last resort (if there are relevant sales). Pause campaigns with high spend with less than 10 orders in the past 30 days as outlined in the screenshot in 3 above.

1. In other words use the following method:

  - Check to see the overall conversion and CTR of a keyword (even if it does not meet the guidelines for negation)
  - Ensure well converting keywords are included in “exact match Amazon campaigns”
  - You want to ensure that higher bids are on exact match keywords as you do not want to compete with yourself, and you can have lower bids on broad or phrase match where Amazon will give priority to. This is exactly where you can focus for conversions. You always want to ensure that your keywords are properly split into exact, phrase, broad, etc and do not combine them together. See this SOP for more guidance on organization [How to Setup and Manage a New Ads Account](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/amazon-advertising/page/advertising-sop-how-to-setup-and-manage-a-new-ads-account)
  - This methodology can help you make smart bidding decisions, when lowering bids to ensure budgets do not go out.
2. Other Methods:

  - Ensure there is decent organization on the account and naming conventions.
  - If absolutely needed, create a budget cap. However, note budget caps should only be used in short term situations, and do not cover for sponsored display or sponsored brand ads. Please see SOP - for detailed information on how to properly utilize budget caps on Amazon. - [Budget Cap SOP](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/amazon-advertising/page/advertising-sop-setting-up-a-budget-cap)

**Summary**:

1. Regular check-ins are key. This only takes 4 minutes and can save an account.
2. Communication of issues with clients is key.
3. Methods above will not work in every situation, but will give you all a great starting place for how to deal with these types of issues.

FAQs

##### **FAQs**

**Budget Management**

- Q1:

  How can the weekly budget for an Amazon advertising campaign be calculated?

  - A1:

    Divide the weekly budget by 7 to determine the average daily spend. Multiply the daily spend by the number of days in the review period. For example, with a $4,800 budget, the daily spend is $685. For a 4-day period, the target spend would be $2,742.
- Q2:

  What should be done if a campaign is over budget?

  - A2:

    Communicate with the Account Manager and tag the weekly Asana task. Provide options to the client:

  - Keep the current spending with an explanation.
  - Turn off ads (not recommended).
  - Decrease bids significantly, identify the cause of overspending, and correct it to prevent future issues.
- Q3:

  How often should budget checks be performed?

  - A3:

    Budget checks should be performed three times a week for all managed accounts.

**Reducing Wasted Spend**

- Q4:

  What steps can be taken to reduce wasted spend on Amazon campaigns?

  - A4:

1. Use negations for high-spending search terms.
2. Lower bids based on CPC comparisons.
3. Change the bidding strategy to "Down Only."
4. Apply the max bid formula:

  avg conversion rate x avg selling price x ACOS target %

  .
5. Review keyword relevancy and adjust accordingly.

- Q5: When should campaigns be paused?

  - A5:

    Campaigns should only be paused as a last resort, such as when high spend results in fewer than 10 orders over the past 30 days.

**Keyword Management**

- Q6: Why is keyword organization important?

  - A6:

    Properly organizing keywords into exact, phrase, and broad match types prevents competition within the account, ensuring higher bids are applied to exact match keywords for better conversions.
- Q7:

  How should well-converting keywords be handled?

  - A7:

    Ensure they are included in exact match campaigns, as this prioritization leads to better performance.

**Bidding Strategies**

- Q8: How do CPC and bid value influence bidding adjustments?

  - A8:

    If CPC is below the current bid, lowering bids will have less impact. When CPC is equal to or higher than the bid, adjustments are more effective, keeping in mind potential dynamic bidding effects.

**Budget Caps**

- Q9: When should budget caps be used?

  - A9:

    Budget caps should only be used for short-term situations and do not cover sponsored display or sponsored brand ads.

**Best Practices**

- Q10: What are key practices to manage Amazon advertising budgets effectively?

  - A10:

    - Perform regular check-ins (takes about 4 minutes).
    - Communicate issues with clients promptly.
    - Follow the outlined methods to address budget concerns, adapting them to specific situations as needed.
