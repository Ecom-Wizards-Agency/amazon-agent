## **Master Ads SOP (With Existing Campaigns)**

**Who is this for :**

- This is for the team responsible for setting up and optimizing master ad campaigns with existing campaigns.
- It is suitable for those who have a basic understanding of Amazon advertising and can use Seller Central and other relevant tools.
- It is also suitable for those who need to gather advertising goals, select ASINs, optimize campaigns, and analyze data to make informed decisions.

**Objective :**To provide a step-by-step guide for setting up a master ad campaign with existing campaigns. This aims to ensure that the team gathers the seller's goals, selects ASINs to be advertised, and understands the current campaign structure.

We might already have active campaigns on the account and we want to keep all the campaigns that are converting well active and pause all the unnecessary ones. Our final goal is to only create campaigns that are missing from the account. To do that, we need to complete the important steps.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/advertising-sop-steps-to-reduction-of-wasted-spend-image6.png)

Export all the campaigns in the same Actions tab. After that, you can select all campaigns and pause them if you think it is worth doing so. We do that because we want to pause all the campaigns with slow sales and high ACOS and create campaigns according to our own strategy. We don’t want to pause campaigns with more than 5 sales in the last 60 days ( even the ACOS it’s more than 150% ) because we can optimize them.

**NOTE 1 :** Never pause a campaign with a high amount of orders >5 in the last 60 days even if the ACOS it’s high. We need to protect the account and we don’t want to lose traction on the ads.

**NOTE 2 :**Some accounts might have more than 150% ACOS goal. In that case, check the goals first before pausing anything and ask.

**NOTE 3 :** If it works don’t touch it! That means you might come across some weird campaigns which sometimes do not make any sense at all, if their Performance is good in Lifetime, Last 30 days, or Last 60 Days then don’t make any changes to that campaign

**NOTE 4 :** Always check the “Budgets Tab” in Campaign Manager, this way you’ll know which campaigns are getting OOB (Out of Budget) and if some good performing campaigns are getting out of budget then we can adjust their budget accordingly

**a. Ads Creation Strategy Based on the Budget**

The most important thing that we have to have when we start a strategy it’s the budget. To have a better view of how many campaigns we should create on an account we need to convert this monthly or weekly budget into a daily one

- If the monthly budget it’s $3000 our daily budget it’s 3000/30 = $100 / day
- If the weekly budget it’s $700 our daily budget it’s 700/7 = $100 / day

Here are some mathematical calculations to find out how many campaigns we can create with how much budget.

- For example, with a $100 budget/day, we can create 10 new campaigns with $10 for each one. $10 it’s the recommended budget to start a campaign with
- As a recommendation, because the budget will not spread equally each day we can have 20% more on the current budget (This can only be applied to the new ad setups)
- For example, if our budget it’s $100 a day we can consider that the budget it’s $120 and we can create a total of 12 new campaigns with $10 each.

**NOTE:** We should only create campaigns that are missing for a specific ASIN or Segment. We shouldn’t create duplicate campaigns as we already checked this in part

**c. Bidding**

Bidding can vary differently depending on:

- Advertising Goals
- Product Price
- Both seller Goals and Product Price
- Avg CPC
- Niche (i.e. Supplements)

Always go with lower-than-suggested bids on the high-volume keywords and drop your comments on the keyword to put low bids while creating the campaign

**Bidding Rules**

- If the product price is less than $15 we should start with a bid <0.8$ – If the suggested bid is higher, than $1 we should limit it to $1 maximum
- If the Product Price is greater than $15 we should start with a bid >0.25$ but <suggested bids – If the suggested bid is higher than $1.5 - $2 we should limit it to $2 maximum

**Budget Allocation**

The budget is flexible and can be changed based on research and/or results.

- 70% Sponsored Products Ads
- 20% Sponsored Display Ads
- 10% Sponsored Brand Ads

**NOTE 1**

- If the monthly budget is very low ($500 / month) we can focus first on 100% Sponsored Products Ads.
- If the monthly budget is medium ($1000 - $3000 / month) we can spread 85% Sponsored Products, 10% Sponsored Displays, and 5% Sponsored Brands
- If the monthly budget is high (>$5000 / month) we can use our main rule 70% 20% 10%

**NOTE 2 :** This budget allocation can also depend on the number of products.

- EX: If we have an account $3000 / month budget and 5 different ASINs to be advertised and we need to create Auto campaigns and KW campaigns for each of the ASINs so we can allocate 100% of the budget on Sponsored Products.

**NOTE 3 :**All the above stats are only examples. We need to understand the account and make a plan based on what we find.

- EX: If the account has a lot of SB - Video campaigns that are working great we can expand our budget here and test more SB - Videos even if we can reach 30% of our budget.

**NOTE 4 :** SB headline is not the best option for newly launched products, so try to stick with SP, SD, and SB video if any.

FAQs

- **Q1: How do I evaluate the current campaign structure?**

  - **A1:**Analyze Performance:

    - Determine monthly ad spend and compare it to the authorized budget.
    - Review campaigns by performance (Best and Worst).
  - Segregate Campaigns into Tiers:

    - Identify campaigns that need optimization or pausing.
    - Use filters to evaluate performance:

      - First Filter:

        - Active Campaigns | 0 Orders | Data Range: Last 60 Days.
        - Pause campaigns with no orders to clean the account and focus resources.
      - Second Filter:

        - Active Campaigns | ACOS > 150% | Orders > 0 | Orders < 5 | Data Range: Last 60 Days.
        - Pause low-performing campaigns unless they had significant orders (>5).
  - Check Niche Dependency:

    - Review Sponsored Products (SP), Sponsored Brands (SB), and Sponsored Display (SD) performance.
    - Analyze competitor ads and past campaign results to determine ad type focus.

- **Q2: What are the rules for pausing or optimizing campaigns?**

  - **A2:** Do not pause campaigns with more than 5 orders in the last 60 days, even if ACOS is high. Instead, optimize.
  - Always check client goals before pausing campaigns.
  - If a campaign works (good lifetime performance), don’t make unnecessary changes.
  - Check the Budgets Tab in Campaign Manager to identify high-performing campaigns going Out of Budget (OOB) and adjust their budgets.

- **Q3: How do I prepare a Segmentation Sheet?**

  - **A3:** Create the Sheet:

    - Use the template from the folder "NEW - Template KW Research and Segmentation."
    - Add prioritized ASINs and follow segmentation guidelines.
  - Find Keywords and ASINs to Target:

    - Conduct thorough keyword and ASIN research using Helium10 or Search Query Reports.
    - Add keywords and ASINs under appropriate columns, such as:

      - ASIN COMP: Competitor ASINs.
      - KW BROAD: Short-tail keywords (2–3 words).
      - KW EXACT: Long-tail keywords (4+ words).
      - KW SELF: Brand-related keywords.
      - ASIN SELF: Brand-related ASINs.
  - Check for Duplicates:

    - Ensure keywords are not repeated across different campaigns.
  - Build the Segmentation Sheet:

    - Organize keywords and ASINs into tiers for precise targeting.

- **Q4: How do I handle existing campaigns?**

  - **A4 :** Rename and Organize Campaigns:

    - Rename active campaigns for clarity.
    - Pause and repurpose old campaigns based on performance.
  - Create or Rename Portfolios:

    - Rename old portfolios if necessary.
    - Align new portfolios with campaigns for better organization.
  - Check Budget Caps:

    - Ensure budget caps align with client goals and adjust if needed.

- **Q5: How should I allocate the budget across campaigns?**

  - **A5:**Budget Allocation:

    - Low Budget ($500/month): 100% Sponsored Products (SP).
    - Medium Budget ($1000–$3000/month): 85% SP, 10% Sponsored Display (SD), 5% Sponsored Brands (SB).
    - High Budget (>$5000/month): 70% SP, 20% SD, 10% SB.
  - Campaign Creation Based on Budget:

    - For a $3000/month budget:

      - Daily budget = $3000/30 = $100/day.
      - Create 10 campaigns with $10/day each.
    - Adjust budget by 20% for flexibility if needed.
  - Focus on Missing Campaigns:

    - Create campaigns only for missing ASINs or segments. Avoid duplicates.

- **Q6: What are the recommended bidding strategies?**

  - **A6:** Initial Bidding Rules:

    - Product Price <$15: Start bids below $0.80; cap at $1 if suggested bids are higher.
    - Product Price >$15: Start bids at $0.25–$2, depending on suggested bids.
  - Adjust Bids Based On:

    - Advertising goals.
    - Product price.
    - Average CPC in the niche.

- **Q7: What should I keep in mind for campaign success?**

  - **A7:** Optimize Listings:

    - Ensure advertised ASINs have optimized titles, images, EBC, and bullet points.
    - Avoid advertising unoptimized listings to prevent wasted spend.
  - Understand the Niche:

    - Check if competitors are running aggressive SB/SD ads.
    - Focus on SP campaigns if basic ones are missing.
  - Budget Flexibility:

    - Adjust campaign structure and budget based on results and account performance.
  - Performance Monitoring:

    - Regularly analyze campaign data for optimization opportunities.
