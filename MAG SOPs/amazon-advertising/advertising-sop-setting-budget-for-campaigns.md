## **Setting Budget for Campaigns**

**Who is this for :** The SOP on Amazon advertising budget planning is intended for individuals managing and optimizing Amazon advertising campaigns. This could include team members responsible for setting and monitoring advertising budgets, analyzing performance metrics, and optimizing campaigns for maximum ROI.

**Objective :** To provide a comprehensive guide on setting up budgets for Amazon advertising campaigns, empowering marketers and e-commerce businesses to make informed decisions that maximize return on investment and drive sustainable growth.

**How many campaigns can we create with a specific budget?**

Formula:

- To estimate the total number of campaigns we plan to create, we can utilize the following formulas

  - Daily Budget for all campaigns = (Monthly budget÷4)÷7
  - Number of Campaigns = Daily budget for all campaigns ÷ 10 OR 5 to 8

| Budget / Month | Estimated Campaigns ($10 each) |
| --- | --- |
| $500 | 2 - 4 campaigns |
| $1000 | 4 - 6 campaigns |
| $2000 | 6 - 10 campaigns |
| $3000 | 10 - 14 campaigns |
| $4000 | 14 - 18 campaigns |
| $5000 | 18 - 25 campaigns |

- For more than $5000 a month, please calculate based on the table above.

**EXAMPLE 1**

- $6,000 a month budget we can create $5000+$1000 = 21-31 campaigns
- $10,000 a month budget we can create $5000+$5000 = 36-50 campaigns

  - NOTE

    - The number of campaigns is practically the total number that we should create based on the budget.

**EXAMPLE 2**

- If we have a monthly budget of $3000 a month and 3 ASINs to be advertised, we should create 4 campaigns for each ASIN in a total of 12 campaigns.

- The budget is flexible and can be changed based on research and/or results.

  - 70% Sponsored Products Ads

    - NOTE 1

      - If the monthly budget is very low ($500 / month) we can focus first on 100% Sponsored Products Ads.

    - NOTE 2

FAQs

##### **FAQs**

- **Q1: How do I calculate the daily budget for all campaigns?**

  - **A1:**Use the formula:

    - Daily Budget for All Campaigns = Monthly Budget / (4×7)

- **Q2:****How many campaigns can I create based on my budget?**

  - **A2:** The estimated number of campaigns depends on your budget and the average cost per campaign:

    - **Formula:**

      - Number of Campaigns = Daily Budget for All Campaigns / 10. (Or 5 to 8 campaigns based on actual costs and objectives.)
    - **Examples:**

      - $500/month: 2–4 campaigns
      - $1000/month: 4–6 campaigns
      - $5000/month: 18–25 campaigns
      - For budgets above $5000, calculate using increments based on the table.

- **Q3: How should I allocate my advertising budget across different ad types?**

  - **A3:** Budget allocation depends on the monthly budget:

    - **High Budget (> $5000/month):**70% Sponsored Products, 20% Sponsored Display, 10% Sponsored Brands
    - **Medium Budget ($1000–$3000/month):**85% Sponsored Products, 10% Sponsored Display, 5% Sponsored Brands
    - **Low Budget (< $500/month):**100% Sponsored Products Ads

- **Q4: Can the budget allocation vary based on specific requirements?**

  - **A4:** Yes, budget allocation is flexible and can be adjusted based on:

    - Number of ASINs being advertised
    - Campaign performance insights
    - Ad type success (e.g., Sponsored Brand Video campaigns performing well might receive up to 30% of the budget).

- **Q5: How should I allocate budgets if I have multiple ASINs?**

  - **A5:**If advertising multiple ASINs, allocate your budget proportionally. For example:

    - For a $3000 monthly budget with 5 ASINs, focus on Auto and KW campaigns for Sponsored Products, which may require allocating 100% of the budget to Sponsored Products.

- **Q6: Are there specific recommendations for new product launches?**

  - **A6:**For newly launched products:

    - Avoid using Sponsored Brand headlines initially.
    - Focus on Sponsored Products (Auto and KW campaigns) and Sponsored Display Ads.
    - Sponsored Brand Video Ads can be used if applicable.

- **Q7: Are the provided statistics and allocations fixed?**

  - **A7:**No, these statistics are orientative. A detailed account analysis is necessary to create a custom plan. For example:

    - If SB Video Ads perform exceptionally well, allocate more budget there, even up to 30%.

- **Q8: What is the best approach if my monthly budget is very limited?**

  - **A8:**For low budgets (e.g., $500/month), prioritize:

    - Sponsored Products Ads (100% of the budget).
    - Gradually expand to other ad types as the budget increases and performance insights are gathered.

- **Q9: What should I do if existing campaigns show strong performance in specific ad types?**

  - **A9:**Increase budgets for well-performing campaigns.
  - Expand to similar campaign types (e.g., testing more Sponsored Brand Video campaigns if they show strong results).

- **Q10: Can I change the budget mid-campaign?**

  - **A10:**Yes, budgets are flexible and can be adjusted based on research, performance analysis, and specific account goals.
