# Advertising SOP: Setting Up a Budget Cap

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/amazon-advertising/page/advertising-sop-setting-up-a-budget-cap

## **Budget Cap**

**Who is this for :** For Individuals who will create a hard spending limit across their campaigns.

**Objective :** Utilize this budget SOP when clients request hard spending limits across accounts, or for specific products/campaigns.

**Instructions:**

1. Sponsored Products: Use the Account Setting to set the hard budget cap.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/drximage.png)

1. Sponsored Brands: For sponsored brands, the account settings will not keep them from overspending (offer hard budget caps). You have to adjust the sponsored brands through their INDIVIDUAL daily budget.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Nacimage.png)

1. Sponsored Display: For sponsored display, the account settings will not keep them from overspending (does not offer hard budget caps). You have to adjust the sponsored display through their INDIVIDUAL daily budget.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/gIeimage.png)

**Consideration:**

We don’t recommend using a budget cap as you can not control the budget on a Campaign level, your best campaigns with good ACOS and sales might not deliver all day long and the campaigns which don't really perform best can take up the budget which may result in high ACOS and less profitable sales.

FAQs

##### **FAQs**

- **Q1: Can I set a hard budget cap for Sponsored Brands?**

  - **A1:** No, the account settings do not support hard budget caps for Sponsored Brands. Instead, you must manage the budget by adjusting the daily budget for each campaign manually.

- **Q2: Can I set a hard budget cap for Sponsored Display?**

  - **A2:** No, Sponsored Display does not support hard budget caps through account settings. Like Sponsored Brands, you need to manage spending by adjusting the daily budgets of individual campaigns.

- **Q3: Why is a budget cap not recommended?**

  - **A3:** A budget cap is not recommended because it restricts spending across campaigns without prioritizing performance. This can lead to:

    - High-performing campaigns (with good ACOS and profitable sales) running out of budget and stopping prematurely.
    - Poor-performing campaigns consuming the available budget, resulting in higher ACOS and reduced profitability.

- **Q4: What are the alternatives to using a budget cap?**

  - **A4:**Instead of a budget cap, consider:

    - Regularly monitoring and optimizing your campaigns.
    - Allocating higher budgets to high-performing campaigns to maximize profitability.
    - Setting individual daily budgets tailored to the performance of each campaign.

- **Q5: How can I ensure that I stay within my overall advertising budget without using a budget cap?**

  - **A5:**You can achieve this by:

    - Keeping track of daily budgets and total spending across campaigns.
    - Using tools or reports from Amazon Advertising Console to monitor and adjust spending as needed.
    - Regularly reviewing and pausing underperforming campaigns.

- **Q6: Are there any tools to help manage budgets effectively across campaigns?**

  - **A6:** Yes, tools like Amazon’s Campaign Manager, third-party advertising management platforms, and budget tracking spreadsheets can help manage and optimize budgets effectively.

- **Q7: What should I do if a client insists on a hard budget cap?**

  - **A7:** If a client requests a hard budget cap, ensure they understand the potential trade-offs. Provide them with a clear explanation of how this might impact campaign performance and offer alternative strategies to achieve their goals.
