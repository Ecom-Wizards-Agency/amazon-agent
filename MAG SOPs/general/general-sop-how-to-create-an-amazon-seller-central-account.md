# General SOP: How to Create an Amazon Seller Central Account

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/general/page/general-sop-how-to-create-an-amazon-seller-central-account

## **How to Create an Amazon Seller Central Account**

**Who is this for?**

This SOP is for individuals or businesses looking to create an Amazon Seller account to start selling on Amazon.

**Objective:**

This SOP provides a step-by-step guide on how to successfully create an Amazon Seller account, enabling users to start selling on Amazon.

**Before you create your selling account, make sure you have the following:**

- Government-issued ID
- Email address
- Internationally chargeable credit card
- Bank account and routing number
- Business license or registration
- Proof of residential address from the last 180 days, like a bank or credit card statement
- Tax information

**Step 1: Visit Amazon Seller Central and Click ‘Sign Up’**

- [Go to Amazon Seller Central.](https://sellercentral.amazon.com)
- Click on the "**Sign Up**" button to begin the registration process.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/general-sop-how-to-create-an-amazon-seller-central-account-image-1.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Wv4image.png)

**Step 2: Select ‘Create Your Amazon Account’**

- Choose the option to “**Create Your Amazon Account**.”
- Follow the on-screen prompts to proceed with setting up your account.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/fdfimage.png)

**Step 3: Verify Your Email Address**

- Input your email address and click “**Continue**.”
- Amazon will send a verification code to the email provided.
- Enter the code to verify your email address.
- Once verified, you'll be taken to a page informing you of the required documents:

  - A valid government-issued ID or passport.
  - A recent bank account or credit card statement.
  - A chargeable credit card.
  - A mobile phone for verification.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/general-sop-how-to-create-an-amazon-seller-central-account-image-3.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/KyXimage.png)

**Step 4: Select Your Business Location and Business Type**

- Choose your business location (the country where your business is based).
- Select your business type from the following options:

  - State-owned business
  - Publicly-owned business
  - Privately-owned business
  - Charity
  - None, I am an individual

    *(Note: Most sellers will select “Privately-owned business.” If you’re not a registered business, select “None, I am an individual.”)*

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Hq4image.png)

**Step 5: Enter Seller and Billing Information**

- Provide your personal contact information for verification.

  - Use a government-issued ID like a passport to enter the following information:

    - Full legal name (including middle name)
    - Country of citizenship
    - Country of birth
    - Date of birth
    - Residential address
- Verify your phone number by receiving a PIN code via text message or call.
- Enter your valid bank account number for transactions and provide a copy of a recent bank statement.
- Enter your credit card details for payment of selling plan fees.
- Ensure the bank account name matches the primary contact or business name you provided earlier.
- Finally, indicate whether you are a beneficial owner of the business, a legal representative of the business, or both.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/qbximage.png)

**Step 6: Enter Your Products & Store Information**

- Choose a name for your Amazon store (this can be changed later).
- Enter details about the products you plan to sell, including whether you have UPC codes, product certifications, or are the manufacturer/brand owner.

**Step 7: Verify Your Identity**

- Upload a copy of your government-issued ID (license or passport) and a recent bank statement for identity verification.
- You will be required to complete an identity verification via video call with an Amazon associate.
- During the video call, the associate will verify your identity and documents.
- Once verified, your Amazon Seller account will be active and ready to use.

**Note:**

Be sure to keep your verification documents ready and double-check the information you provide to avoid delays in the registration process.

FAQs

**Q1: Do I need an LLC to sell with Amazon?**

No, you do not need to be an LLC or a registered business to sell on Amazon. You can select "None, I am an individual" as your business type during registration.

**Q2: What if I want to sell with Amazon in other countries?**

You can use your selling account to sell in the US, Canada, and Mexico. Additionally, you can create separate selling accounts for regions like Europe, Asia-Pacific, and the Middle East and North Africa.

**Q3: How do I know if I’m a beneficial owner?**

A beneficial owner is someone who owns more than 25% of the business or has significant voting rights. If no one qualifies, any senior manager can be considered a beneficial owner.

**Q4: How do I know if I’m a legal representative?**

A legal representative is authorized by your business to manage and act on its behalf. If you are not a legal representative, someone else from your business may need to provide an authorization letter.

**Q5: What bank account holder name should I use?**

Use the name under which your bank account is held, which could either be your name or your business name.

**Q6: Do I need a US bank account?**

No, you can use the Amazon Currency Converter to receive proceeds in a bank account outside of the US and convert them to the relevant local currency.

**Q7: What credit card types can I use?**

Amazon accepts American Express, Diners Club, Discover, JCB, MasterCard, and Visa. The credit card does not need to be in your name or your business's name.

**Q8: What if my store name is already being used by another seller?**

Your store name needs to be unique. If your preferred name is taken, select an alternative or variation.

**Q9: What are product IDs—and how do I find or obtain them?**

Product IDs (like UPC, ISBN, EAN, or JAN) are used to identify your products. They can be found on product packaging or you may need to obtain them from Global Standards 1 or apply for a GTIN exemption.

**Q10: What are the requirements for uploading documents?**

Documents must be high-quality color scans or photos showing all four corners and legible text. Avoid blurry images, screenshots, and ensure that documents are signed.
