Merchandising SOP: Seller Central Terms and Definitions

### Seller Central Terms and Definitions

**Who this is for?** This SOP is designed for marketing and sales teams, customer service representatives, and any other individuals responsible for managing and maintaining Amazon Seller Central accounts. This document is also useful for individuals who are new to Amazon Seller Central and seeking to understand the platform's terminology.

**Objective:** The objective of this SOP is to provide clear explanations of common terms and definitions used in Amazon Seller Central to ensure a comprehensive understanding of the platform's features, functions, and requirements. By familiarizing users with these terms, the SOP aims to facilitate efficient account management, streamline communication between team members, and reduce potential errors or misunderstandings.

**Consignment**

- Consignment sales can be conducted on both Vendor Central and Seller Central for Sellers who sell to Amazon via FBA or Fulfillment by Amazon. Consignment involves shipping products to Amazon as a supplier, with payment only made by Amazon upon consumer purchase from the inventory holding on their website. Amazon receives a commission fee from this payment. Sales, shipments, and replenishment reports enable Vendors and Sellers to monitor their FBA Consignment Stock on their Amazon Storefront.

**Consolidation and Inventory Placement Service**

- When creating a shipping plan as a Seller on Seller Central shipping via FBA, your shipment may be divided into multiple shipments, each directed to different receive centers or fulfillment centers by default, which is referred to as distributed inventory placement. The selection of the fulfillment centers is based on your products and shipping location. Distributing your inventory across multiple fulfillment centers across the country enables faster delivery of products to customers who buy your product, which is made possible through Amazon routing the shipment through a receiving center that receives units and then reships them to other fulfillment centers in their network. Once Amazon has successfully received your products at any receive center or fulfillment center, they become available for sale, but customers may be shown a future ship date if no other units are available for immediate fulfillment. Amazon consolidates all your shipments into one shipment going to one fulfillment center before separating them out. Signing up for the Inventory Placement Service enables you to send all eligible inventory to a single receive center or fulfillment center, which is determined by Amazon. The shipment is then split and sent to different fulfillment centers for you, but you cannot choose the receiving center or fulfillment center to which you send your shipment as it may vary from shipment to shipment.

**PAN EU**

- PAN EU is a type of Fulfilled By Amazon (FBA) service that allows Sellers to send their products to a single Fulfilment Centre, and Amazon will distribute the products to various Fulfilment Centres across Europe based on anticipated sales. To use this service, Sellers must be registered for VAT in the different countries in Europe.

**European Fulfillment Network**

- Amazon's European Fulfilment Network (EFN) enables Sellers to sell their products on other EU marketplaces but fulfill all orders from Fulfilment Centres in their home country.

**Multi-Country Inventory (MCI)**

- Multi-Country Inventory (MCI) is a service offered by Fulfilled by Amazon (FBA) that allows Sellers to choose specific countries to send their stock to and fulfill orders for that marketplace using the local inventory.

**Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP)**

- Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP) is an option available for Sellers to display the prime badge on their listings while retaining complete control over inventory, as the stock is stored and shipped from their own warehouses.

**Amazon Business Advisory**

- Amazon Business Advisory (ABA) is a fee-based account management service that offers sellers strategic insights driven by business intelligence to help them increase their revenue and succeed on the platform by providing essential inputs. This service provides sellers with a dedicated account manager who has expert knowledge in their relevant category.

**"Damaged" Terms**

- Vendor Damaged refers to products that are damaged before reaching Amazon due to handling by the vendor or the vendor’s carrier.
- Warehouse Damaged refers to products that are damaged after delivery to Amazon's fulfillment center but before leaving for delivery to an Amazon customer, including damage caused by handling in the fulfillment center.
- Undamaged / Undamaged Overstock refers to products that are not damaged or defective, but for which Amazon does not have customer demand, resulting in overstock.
- Carrier Damaged refers to products that are damaged during transit or delivery to an Amazon customer or during return transit or delivery to an Amazon fulfillment center after a customer return.
- Customer Damaged refers to products returned by a customer that is not in new condition due to damage, wear, or signs of use, including broken seals, opened boxes, and product testing.
- Expired products are products that have passed their expiration date or will expire in the next 90 days.
- Defective products are those that Amazon has determined to be defective, including missing parts, manuals, instructions, or warranty information, among other things.

**Fulfilled by Amazon (FBA)**

- Amazon's Fulfilled by Amazon (FBA) service allows sellers to send their products to Amazon's fulfillment centers, where Amazon takes care of the picking, packing, and shipping process once a customer places an order.

**Fulfilled by Merchant (FBM)**

- When a seller uses Fulfilled by Merchant (FBM), it means that they are responsible for shipping products from their own inventory and handling any customer inquiries or feedback.

**Fulfillment by Amazon Small and Light**

- Fulfillment by Amazon Small and Light is an option that provides lower fulfillment costs on qualified items, allowing sellers to pass on the savings to customers. By signing up for Small and Light, sellers can benefit from free shipping for Prime customers, reduced fulfillment costs, and instant customer trust. Eligible products for this option include ASINs that are 10 oz or less, 16 x 9 x 4 inches or less, $7 or less, and fast-moving ASINs.

**Multi-Channel Fulfillment**

- Amazon's Multi-Channel Fulfillment (MCF) service provides access to Amazon's fulfillment network, expertise, and shipping options for all your orders, regardless of where they are placed. MCF enhances the customer experience by providing faster delivery and simplifying operations. You can store your inventory in Amazon's warehouses, which can help you lower your fulfillment costs with competitive rates and scalable capacity. With MCF, you can choose to send some or all of your inventory directly to an Amazon fulfillment center, and Amazon will pick, pack, and ship the products to your customers when they make a purchase on your website or other third-party sales channels. MCF is available with or without Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA), and if you use FBA, your existing inventory can serve both Amazon customers and customers from your other sales channels.

**Amazon Storage and Replenishment**

- Amazon's Storage and Replenishment service enables its sellers to store inventory near Amazon's delivery operation, allowing for quick replenishment of products. This service is designed to provide a temporary warehouse solution to facilitate faster holiday deliveries.

**Fulfillment by Amazon Onsite**

- Amazon's Fulfillment by Amazon Onsite is an exclusive program that combines the features of both FBA and SFP. It involves Amazon's intervention to enhance the efficiency of seller warehouses to match Amazon's fulfillment center standards. The service includes the provision of Amazon's Warehouse Management System (WMS), label printing system, and shipping managed by Amazon.

**Seller Flex**

- Amazon's Seller Flex program is an invitation-only program aimed at providing more space for other sellers in conventional FCs. The criteria for invitation are as follows: sellers should have their own warehouse, should currently occupy space in a conventional FC, wants to sell more ASINs through FBA but are unable to add them due to space/cost constraints, or wants to sell in new categories and add more products to FBA. If invited, Seller Flex sellers will be required to carry out various inventory and order processing operations at their specified premises, also known as the 'Flex Site,' recorded in Amazon systems.

**Amazon Buy Shipping and Print Connect**

- Amazon offers sellers the option to purchase shipping labels for non-FBA orders through the Merchant Fulfillment API or a Seller Central account. Additionally, you can configure your Buy Shipping preferences within Seller Central to expedite the process of purchasing shipping labels. By setting these preferences, you can reduce the amount of time it takes to purchase each label.

**Amazon Global Selling**

- Amazon Global Selling is a program designed to assist sellers in registering their products for sale across Amazon's global markets. By leveraging the power of Amazon's trusted brand, sellers can accelerate their international sales and gain access to millions of potential customers who visit Amazon's international marketplaces. Additionally, taking advantage of peak sales seasons in other countries can help sellers reduce low sales periods.

**FBA Export**

- FBA Export is a service that enables customers from different countries to purchase export-eligible items. This service utilizes the FBA inventory stored in US-based fulfillment centers to make the products available to customers from all over the world on amazon.com. Amazon takes care of the shipping process for eligible products at no additional cost to the seller, making it easy for international customers to purchase items from your store.

**European Fulfillment Network**

- Sellers registered for Fulfillment by Amazon on Amazon Europe Marketplaces can utilize the European Fulfillment Network (EFN) to store their inventory in their local fulfillment center. This allows them to fulfill orders from other European marketplaces using the same local inventory pool, providing them with greater control and flexibility over their inventory. Additionally, by storing inventory in one fulfillment center and registering for taxes in one country, sellers can streamline their operations. Amazon lists the seller's products in all European country stores, and the seller pays Amazon's cross-border fulfillment fees for using the EFN service.

**Pan-European FBA**

- Pan-European FBA provides a way for sellers to expand their reach across Europe by storing their inventory closer to their customers and fulfilling orders at a lower cost and with faster delivery times. By using this service, sellers can make their products eligible for Prime and visible to millions of customers across Amazon's European marketplaces.
- To use Pan-European FBA, sellers must send their inventory to one fulfillment center, and Amazon will move the inventory across Europe based on demand. Sellers need to register for taxes in each country where their inventory is stored, which can require additional paperwork. However, by utilizing this service, sellers can avoid paying Amazon cross-border fees, resulting in a better customer experience.

**North America Unified Account**

- The North America Unified Account allows for unified inventory management across the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

**European Unified Account**

- The European Unified Account allows for unified inventory management across the various countries in Europe.

**Repricing Software**

- Repricing software is a type of software that assists in managing a product's price to increase the likelihood of winning the buy box.

**Best Seller Rank**

- The Best Seller Rank on Amazon is the ranking of a product at a particular point in time. This rank provides insight into how well or poorly the product is currently performing in terms of sales.

**Seller Feedback**

- Seller Feedback or Seller Rating is a system that allows customers to leave feedback on the quality of service provided by an Amazon merchant within three months of the purchase date. Ratings are based on a star system, ranging from 1 star (poor) to 5 stars (best), and can be submitted through the Amazon feedback page or the "your orders" section of the customer's Amazon account.

**Amazon A-Z Guarantee**

- The Amazon A-to-Z Guarantee provides customers protection when they purchase items from a third-party seller that are both sold and fulfilled by them. This guarantee encompasses timely delivery and the condition of the items, and is often referred to as "Amazon's amazing return policy."

**Cube**

- In the wholesale purchasing and shipping industry, a "cube" refers to the volume of space that a shipment occupies on a trailer. Specifically, a "case cube" is a measurement that denotes how much space a case of any given item will take up in a warehouse. This measurement is typically given in cubic feet or cubic meters. To determine the case cube, one can measure the length, width, and height of a single unit in either inches or meters and multiply them together. Then, divide the result by 1728 if the measurements were in inches, or by 100,000 if the measurements were in centimeters. This calculation is necessary to ensure that the correct cubic feet measurement is provided in Routing Requests and Advanced Shipment Notifications.

**Pallet**

- A Pallet is a portable platform that is used for stacking cases or boxes to make handling easier. These are typically made of wood or plastic.
- A PECO Pallet is a pallet that is rented from the PECO pallet rental service.
- A CHEP Pallet is a pallet that is provided by the CHEP Pallet Pooling service of the Commonwealth Handling Equipment Pool.
- GMA Pallets refer to pallet specifications that are provided by the Grocery Manufacturers Association (GMA) Pallet Recycling Corporation.

**ORM-D**

- ORM-D is an acronym that stands for "Other Regulated Material - D." It is a marking used in the United States for consumer commodities that contain hazardous material in a limited quantity, which presents a limited hazard during domestic transportation. ORM-D is a type of Hazard Class, similar to Class 3 (Flammable and Combustible Liquid) and Class 8 (Corrosive Material). Despite popular belief, the "D" in ORM-D does not stand for Domestic; it only distinguishes it from other ORM classifications. Hazardous materials that may be classified as ORM-D are restricted to Consumer commodities, Cartridges (Small Arms), and Cartridges (Power Devices). You can identify ORM-D packages by the ORM-D label on the package.

**Amazon Payments Marketplace**

- Amazon Payments Marketplace provides payment processing tools for third-party sellers.
- The feature enables shopping cart providers, e-commerce platforms, and other service providers to:

1. Submit transactions through the Amazon Payments Marketplace service on behalf of their clients.
2. Access the transaction data of their clients.

**Certificate of Analysis**

- A document verifying the quality and safety of a product, especially supplements or consumables. Often required for compliance.

**Subscribe & Save**

- A program allowing customers to schedule recurring deliveries of products at a discount.

**Prime Exclusive Discounts**

- Time-limited discounts available only to Amazon Prime members, commonly used during deal events.

**Solution Provider Portal**

- Amazon's platform where service providers (like agencies and software tools) manage their service offerings to sellers.

**Ships in Product Packaging**

- A fulfillment designation where the item is shipped in its own branded packaging, no Amazon box.

**Letter of Authorization**

- A document from the brand owner authorizing someone else to act on their behalf (e.g., for Brand Registry or appeals).

**Amazon Warehouse and Distribution**

- A logistics program for upstream inventory storage and automatic replenishment to FBA; part of Amazon’s supply chain network

Vendor Central SOP: Vendor Central Terms and Definitions

### Vendor Central Terms and Definitions

**Who is this for?** Knowing the Amazon Vendor Central Terms and Definitions is essential for businesses that operate as vendors on Amazon's platform.

**Objective:** The objective of understanding these terms and definitions is to gain a comprehensive understanding of the rules, processes, and requirements associated with selling products through Amazon Vendor Central.

**Amazon Retail Analytics (ARA Basic)**

- Amazon Retail Analytics offers a range of powerful tools for Vendor Central account sellers. Once a vendor sets up an account, they are given default access to the ARA Basic version, which includes essential reports on sales, inventory, and operations. This tool is without a doubt the most powerful for understanding metrics and reports.
- The ARA Basic version offers reports on Sales Diagnostics, Traffic Diagnostics, Operational Diagnostics, Forecast and Inventory Planning, and Inventory Health. Vendors can filter these reports based on various factors like brand, category, subcategory, product group, and replenishment code to get more specific details.
- However, the reports on Sales and Operations are basic and have limited data history, despite being free. By using the data provided by ARA reports, vendors can refine their marketing efforts within Amazon and other channels to drive traffic back to Amazon. Analyzing the data can help sellers create actionable strategies.

**Amazon Brand Analytics (Previously ARA Premium)**

- Brand Analytics, previously known as ARA Premium, is now accessible under Reports -> Analytics. Previously, there was a cost of 30K per year subscription for this service, but it is now free for Vendors registered with the Brand Registry.
- Brand Analytics is more extensive than the basic package, providing a broad range of reports and data. The service provides access to reports on Sales & Operations, Traffic, Consumer Behavior, Customer Reviews, Scheduled Email Reports, Real-time sales, and more.
- Brand Analytics also has different metrics than the basic package. For instance, the Sales Diagnostics report includes an additional metric called “Ship Revenue,” whereas the only metric available in ARA Basic is “Ship Cost.” Another feature in the Premium package is Search Terms, which grants access to Amazon's top 100 search terms.
- In conclusion, Brand Analytics provides vendors with an in-depth understanding of revenue and insights into where revenue is generated, competition, customer behavior, and ASIN level performance metrics. This service offers more actionable data and sorting options for detailed analysis, providing a huge advantage to be Brand Registered.

**Manufacturing Views**

- Only manufacturers have access to Manufacturing View on Amazon Reporting, which displays aggregate sales and inventory data for products associated with their brand, regardless of whether Amazon procures the items directly from them as a vendor or through a third-party distributor. The reporting can comprise sales from the manufacturer, distributor, or wholesaler, as well as sales from outside the particular territory.

**Sourcing Views**

- The Sourcing view shows the sales and inventory for the products under that specific vendor. In the EU, this includes all goods sold in the same country as well as goods purchased to be sold in another EU country, also known as the European Fulfilment Network (EFN).

**Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)**

- Amazon's Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) refers to the amount a seller charges Amazon to purchase their products or the price Amazon pays to procure the products directly from the seller. Determining the COGS can be a complicated process as it does not solely rely on the landed or ex-warehouse cost of the product.
- In addition to these costs, the COGS also includes other factors such as co-op agreements that a seller must agree to when starting a business with Amazon, any program fees such as Subscribe and Save or Prime, and buffers to minimize losses from chargebacks and penalties fees.

**Contra COGS**

- The Contra Cost of Goods Sold (CCOGS) refers to the funding that vendors provide to Amazon, which excludes advertising and sales promotions. This funding includes backend items like Market Development Funds, Co-Op Agreement Fees, Vine, and ARA Premium. These items serve as buffers that vendors must add to their landed or ex-warehouse costs to determine the overall price they will sell their product to Amazon for. Essentially, CCOGS represents the total cost to Amazon for purchasing a vendor's product.

**Consignment**

- Selling inventory through consignment is possible on both Vendor Central and Seller Central. If the seller on Seller Central uses FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon), they can also sell to Amazon using consignment. Consignment involves the supplier shipping the product to Amazon, but Amazon only pays for the inventory when a consumer purchases it from the Amazon website and from the inventory holding. Amazon takes a commission fee from this payment. With FBA Consignment Stock, vendors or sellers can keep track of their inventory on their Amazon Storefront through their sales, shipments, and replenishment reports.

**Cube**

- The measurement of space or volume that a shipment occupies on a trailer is called a cube. In the wholesale industry, a case cube refers to the measurement of space a case of a particular item will occupy in your warehouse, usually in cubic feet or cubic meters. To accurately calculate the case cube, you need to know the dimensions of the case, which can be measured in inches or meters. Multiply the length, width, and height of the case and divide the result by 1728 if your measurements are in inches or by 100,000 if your measurements are in centimeters. Having the correct case cube measurement is important when submitting Routing Requests and Advanced Shipment Notifications.

**Pallet**

- Pallets are movable platforms used for stacking cases or boxes for ease of handling and transportation. There are several types of pallets available in the market. A PECO Pallet is a pallet provided by the PECO pallet rental service. A CHEP Pallet, on the other hand, is a Commonwealth Handling Equipment Pool Pallet provided by the CHEP Pallet Pooling service. Another type is the GMA Pallet, which refers to pallet specifications provided by Grocery Manufacturers Association (GMA) Pallet Recycling Corporation.

**Inbound Cross Dock (IXD)**

- An IXD, or Inbound Cross Dock, is an Amazon warehouse that receives freight from vendors and sellers and redistributes products to various fulfillment centers. The primary function of an IXD is to streamline the flow of goods into Amazon's fulfillment center network. In the past, these facilities were referred to as redistribution centers. They serve as inventory receiving, break-bulk, and storage buffers for import containers from foreign vendors, similar to import distribution centers. Once the goods arrive at the port, they are received at the IXD facility and held until the fulfillment center requires replenishment of its inventory. The products are then consolidated into truckloads and shipped to the fulfillment center. These facilities are strategically located near major ports to reduce inbound ground transportation costs. Additionally, they may also be used to store and replenish domestic goods.

**Vendor Lead Time**

- The lead time is the delay that is considered for inventory control purposes and is calculated by adding the supply delay and reordering delay. The supply delay refers to the time it takes for a supplier to deliver the goods once an order is placed, while the reordering delay is the time until an ordering opportunity arises again. In the case of Amazon Vendors, lead time is the number of days required for the Vendor to supply Amazon with the product after Amazon places a Purchase Order.

**Accepted Confirmation Rate (ACR)**

- The Accepted Confirmation Rate is a percentage that is calculated by dividing the Accepted Confirmation Quantity by the Quantity submitted, based on the units. To illustrate, suppose a Purchase Order (PO) A1234567 is submitted for 1000 units of line item ASIN ABCDEFGHIJ, and 800 units are confirmed as accepted, then the Accepted Confirmation Rate % would be 80%.

**Unsellable On Hand Units**

- Unsellable On Hand Units are units that are held in Amazon's fulfillment center(s) and have been deemed unsuitable for sale due to significant damage incurred during transportation or while in storage.

**Unsellable On Hand Cost**

- Unsellable On Hand Cost is the cost of the inventory that is classified as unsellable and is still on hand at the end of the specified period.

**Collect and Prepaid Freight**

- Amazon provides two options for sending Purchase Orders: Collect and Prepaid. With Collect, Amazon pays for the freight using their preferred carriers and highly favorable rates. However, it requires agreeing to a Freight Allowance which is updated annually and becomes part of the standard agreements with Amazon. On the other hand, Prepaid requires the supplier to pay for the freight of their Purchase Orders to Amazon's Fulfillment Centers at non-negotiated shipping rates and make all necessary shipping arrangements themselves, but they can use their preferred carriers.

**Line Item Statuses**

- When you receive a Purchase Order from Amazon, each individual line or ASIN is referred to as a Line Item. You will need to confirm the status of each specific line item or ASIN on the Purchase Order. You have two Yes options, which are "Accepted: In Stock" if you have the product on hand and ready to ship, or "Backordered: External Confirmation" if you are expecting the product within the next few days or weeks. There are also three No options (Temporarily Unavailable), which include "Cancelled: Out of Stock" if the product is out of stock for longer than a week after the shipping window closes, "Cancelled: Not Yet Available" if the product is new and not yet available to ship on the Purchase Order, or "Cancelled: Invalid Product Information" if your system doesn't recognize the ASIN or product Amazon is ordering. Finally, there are two No options (Permanently Unavailable), which include "Discontinued: Obsolete" if the product has been discontinued from the Amazon catalog completely, or "Cancelled: Suspend Orders" if the product is being reviewed or restructured and will not be available for an indefinite period of time.

**Routing Request**

- A Routing Request is a mandatory web-based application that Amazon suppliers must use to facilitate the movement of inventory from their distribution center to an Amazon FC. This is required for all collect shipments, regardless of weight. Amazon expects suppliers to request a carrier assignment for their shipments and provide details such as the pickup date, time, number of cartons, weight, and volume of the purchase order. This is known as the routing request in Vendor Central. After confirming the purchase order, suppliers must create a routing request in Vendor Central, and Amazon will assign a reference number called the Amazon Reference Number (ARN) and send a routing confirmation email to the supplier.

**Amazon Reference Number (ARN)**

- When Amazon has assigned a carrier to make a pick-up for a shipment and the routing request has been completed, Amazon generates a reference number called the Amazon Reference Number (ARN). This reference number is only generated for collect shipments. After the carrier has been assigned, the routing request status changes to "shipments with assigned carrier and pending ASN."

**Advance Shipment Notification (ASN)**

- An Advanced Shipment Notification (ASN) is a virtual packing list that must be sent electronically before the physical shipment arrives at Amazon's fulfillment centers. The purpose of this notification is to allow for proper labor resource planning. Suppliers are required to inform Amazon of the ASN within 24 hours after handing over the Purchase Order to carriers or 4 hours before the arrival, whichever comes first. The ASN can be created manually in Amazon Vendor Central or EDI suppliers can send an EDI 856 (ASN) to Amazon. The ASN should include all the information from the Routing Request and the carrier's tracking number, which can be a PRO number for LTL or FTL shipments or a carton tracking number for small parcel shipments.

**PRO Number**

- A PRO number is a reference number consisting of a series of 7 to 10 digits used by carriers for tracking freight movement. The term "PRO number" is derived from "progressive number" and serves as a tracking tool for carriers.

**BOL**

- A bill of lading (BL or BOL) is a legal document issued by a carrier to a shipper that outlines the shipment details, including the type, quantity, and destination of the goods being carried, and serves as a shipment receipt upon delivery. It includes information such as the consignor's and consignee's name, departure, and arrival ports, vessel name, an itemized list of goods with packaging information, weight/volume, and freight rate. It can be issued in negotiable or non-negotiable form and serves as proof of ownership of the cargo. A bill of lading is required for compensation claims and dispute resolution. The rights and responsibilities of the carrier and shipper are governed by either the Hague or Hague-Visby rules. The Bill of Lading Number is a unique identifier on the bill of lading at the time of shipment acceptance.

**Packing List**

- A Packing List is a document prepared by the vendor that itemizes the contents and quantities of a shipment. It must accurately match the information provided in the Advanced Shipment Notification, including the products packed in each carton and the number of cartons. The Packing List essentially provides a detailed breakdown of the contents of each carton and how many of each product is in it. This document is required for all shipments to and from any given address, but especially for shipments from the vendor's warehouse to an Amazon Fulfillment Center.

**Purchase Order (PO)**

- The Purchase Order is a contractual agreement between Amazon and its vendors for the shipment of specific quantities of particular ASINs to Amazon's Fulfillment Centers. This order is created through Vendor Central, where Amazon places the request.

**Fulfillment Center (FC)**

- An Amazon Fulfillment Center, also known as FC, is a warehouse where goods are stored for extended periods and shipped directly to customers.

**Commercial Invoice**

- A commercial invoice is a crucial customs document used in foreign trade. It is essentially a customs declaration, used when exporting an item across international borders, such as a US Supplier shipping to an Amazon Fulfillment Center in Canada or Mexico. The document must contain specific information, including the parties involved in the shipping transaction, the goods being transported, the country of manufacture, and the Harmonized System codes for those goods. Although there is no standard format, a commercial invoice must include a statement certifying its accuracy and a signature. Amazon has its own specific format for creating commercial invoices through Vendor Central. The document is used to calculate tariffs, international commercial terms, and customs purposes. In European countries, commercial invoices are not typically used for payment, and the invoice for payment usually includes only the word "invoice," although it can also serve as a commercial invoice if additional information is disclosed.

**Standard Carrier Alpha Code (SCAC)**

- The Standard Carrier Alpha Code, also known as SCAC, is a four-letter code that serves as a unique identifier for Amazon's carriers. The list of SCAC codes can be found in Amazon's Vendor Central Resource Center, where you can determine which code applies to which carrier. This information will also appear in the Advanced Shipment Notifications, making it a convenient way to track carriers.

**Labels (BPS, 2D Label, SSCC, and AMZNCC)**

- Amazon has a labeling policy that requires each carton in a shipment to be labeled with either an SSCC label, AMZNCC barcode label, or a 2D barcode shipping label. The SSCC label is a preferred option for Amazon EDI vendors and the AMZNCC barcode label for manual vendors. The 2D barcode shipping label can be in the form of a BPS label meeting GS1-128 standards or other formats such as PDF417, Data Matrix, QR Code, or Maxi. These labels provide Amazon with the necessary information, including SKU barcodes, to facilitate the auto-receive process and inventory management. The SSCC label and AMZNCC barcode label contain an 18-digit number and a 14-digit number respectively. The 2D barcode shipping label has no character limit when using PDF417 and is ranked in order of preference, with Maxi being the least preferred due to its 93-character limit.

**Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA)**

- When a consumer returns a product to an Amazon Fulfillment Center, vendors who have agreed to accept returns on their account can authorize those returns through the Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) process on the Vendor Central or Advantage website. In other words, the RMA process allows vendors to query and approve their own returns, giving them more control over the return process. Once a return is received at the Fulfillment Center, Amazon will request authorization from the vendor before processing the return.

**ORM-D**

- ORM-D is a marking used for consumer commodities in the US that contain hazardous material in a limited quantity that presents a limited hazard during transportation due to its form, quantity, and packaging. ORM-D is not an abbreviation for Domestic but stands for "Other Regulated Material - D". It is a type of Hazard Class, similar to Class 3 - Flammable and Combustible Liquid and Class 8 - Corrosive Material. The "D" simply distinguishes it from other ORM's that previously existed. The Hazardous Materials that can be re-classed as ORM-D are limited to consumer commodities, cartridges, small arms, and cartridges for power devices.

**Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)**

- Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) is a standardized electronic format for exchanging business information. Trading partners use EDI to electronically send and receive information, eliminating the need for paper-based communication. With Amazon, vendors, carriers, and drop shippers can transmit data in formatted messages using the EDI standards X12 (for US and JP) or EDIFACT (for EU). EDI enables automatic updating across systems, so any changes made in one system are reflected in the others. This speeds up the process of managing purchase orders and shipments to Amazon FCs and reduces the risk of errors.

**International Safe Transit Association (ISTA)**

- The International Safe Transit Association (ISTA) is a nonprofit organization that is dedicated to transport packaging. It is the most trusted and respected authority in predictive package performance testing worldwide. ISTA sets the standards for how packages should perform to ensure the protection of their contents during the constantly changing risks of the global distribution environment. By creating and publishing packaged-product test procedures, certifying packaging laboratories, packaged products, and professionals, and providing education, training, and support, ISTA helps its members develop more effective packaging. Its aim is to optimize the resources in packages that are designed to be survivable, sustainable, and successful, and to help members control costs, damage, and resources during the distribution of packaged products.

**FFP and Packaging Certifications**

- Amazon.com has a program called Certified Frustration-Free Packaging, which works with manufacturers to reduce the amount of packing materials used in packaging products. - LTL stands for "Less than Truckload" and is used to describe a shipment that takes up less than 50% of a 53-foot trailer. The carrier consolidates freight from multiple shippers before transporting it to the destination.
- FTL stands for "Full Truckload" and is used to describe a shipment that takes up more than 50% of a 53-foot trailer. The carrier consolidates freight from a single shipper before transporting it to the destination.

Vendors can access their Operational Performance Dashboard, which displays the bulk of their chargebacks, from the scrolling bar under the Top Menu of their Vendor Central Dashboard or by selecting “Operational Performance” from the Drop Down Menu under the Reports Tab on the Top Menu. **The Born to Run Program**

- Amazon's Born to Run Program was designed to simplify the traditionally slow process of launching products on the platform. Through the program, vendors can choose their initial ten-week inventory positions for new product launches, allowing for a faster sales process. This program is particularly beneficial for newer vendors who may gain a lot of leverage if it works as expected. Born to Run offers vendors the opportunity to take advantage of their personal knowledge of their product line, the benefits of a fully-in-stock launch, and AMS support. Vendors who take part in Born to Run will have the freedom to select their beginning ten-week inventory position for new launches in exchange for full return rights on any unsold products from the ten-week inventory position and investment in Amazon Marketing Services (AMS) support worth 10% of the Launch Buy Quantity’s total cost on the launch ASIN. Currently, the Born to Run program requires vendors to follow three key requirements: the total product cost of the launch buy quantity (LBQ) cannot exceed $50,000; the ASIN must meet certain criteria such as having a product cost of at least $5/unit and not being heavy or bulky; and vendors must utilize AMS for at least ninety days prior to signup. Participating in the program is straightforward once requirements are met, and Amazon claims that participation should take no more than twenty minutes of a vendor’s time. To participate in the program, vendors must source their ASIN and fill out a Born to Run Intake Form, receive approval from their Vendor Manager, deal with the policies outlined in a contract, purchase the agreed-upon amount of products, and sell through their inventory within ten weeks. Any unsold inventory will be returned to the vendor at full cost after the ten-week time frame.

Finance Definitions SOP

### Finance Definitions SOP

The objective of this SOP is to provide comprehensive definitions of key financial terms within the finance department, equipping IT specialists with a clear and concise reference guide. By establishing a standardized terminology framework, this SOP aims to enhance collaboration and communication between IT professionals and finance teams, ensuring accuracy and coherence in the creation of SOPs and other documents related to the finance department.

**Key Financial Terms**

1. **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)**

- What Is It: Retainer income that reoccurs more than one month.
- How is it Calculated: By including commission and base retainer fee.
- Source of Truth: IN
- Audit Frequency: Monthly
- Replicated to: Burn report, MRR summary, HubSpot
- Self Audit: By checking the latest invoice from Invoice Ninja, or latest contract signed.

1. **Monthly Recurring Revenue Gained (MRR Gained)**

- What Is It: New MRR brought in, will be the base retainer fee only per finances MRR summary report.
- How is it Calculated: Base retainer fee
- Source of Truth: Contract
- Audit Frequency: Once

1. **Monthly Recurring Revenue Retained (MRR Retained)**

- What is It: Upsells to existing MRR, or MRR counted as lost which is retained (maintained or downgrade)
- How is it Calculated: Gained MRR to existing deal or saved MRR
- Source of Truth: MRR Summary Report
- Audit Frequency: Once

1. **Monthly Recurring Revenue Lost (MRR Lost)**

- What is It: When a client gives their cancel notice, quit paying, or are otherwise terminated the entire MRR amount is counted as lost
- How is it Calculated: The entire MRR amount from the last invoice when they put in notice
- Source of Truth: Ninja and MRR Summary
- Audit Frequency: Once

1. **Net Monthly Recurring Revenue (Net MRR)**

- What is It: MRR remaining when taken into effect of gains , lost, and retained
- How is it Calculated: MRR gained - MRR lost + MRR retained
- Source of Truth: MRR summary
- Audit Frequency: Monthly

1. **Churn**

- How is it Calculated: Total MRR at start of month - MRR lost + MRR retained (MRR gained is not included in the formula).
