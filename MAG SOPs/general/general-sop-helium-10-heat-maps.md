# General SOP: Helium 10 Heat Maps

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/general/page/general-sop-helium-10-heat-maps

## **Helium 10 Heat Maps**

**Who is this for :**

- Sellers that have a Helium 10 subscription (at least a Platinum level subscription required)
- Sellers that would like a visual of where exactly their inventory is being stored
- Sellers who want to launch or relaunch a product

**Objective:**This SOP (standard operating procedure) will help you understand the latest tool within the Profit’s tool suite on Helium 10. Please note that this tool is still in Beta as of 03/16/2022.

**What are Helium 10 Heat Maps**

- Heat Maps, or Heat Map Analytics, gives you a map illustrating all the different warehouse locations where your Amazon FBA inventory is currently stored. The map will look something like this:

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-10-16%20at%2011.15.07%E2%80%AFPM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/9zUimage.png)

**How do I read the Map?**

- To understand the map, simply look at the circles - the bigger the circle, the more inventory is stored at that location. For example: on the map above, there is more inventory stored in Arkansas (AR) than in Florida (FL).

**How is this important for sellers who want to launch or relaunch a product?**

- When launching or relaunching a product, it’s best to keep the product listing closed or suppressed until your inventory is widely distributed across the country.
- The reasoning behind this is as follows: imagine you’ve sent in several new units for an upcoming launch, but the units are only available in California. Someone in New York may click on your ad and view your listing, but they’re not likely to convert to a sale if they see the “few days to ship” notification.
- Heat Maps will provide a visual of exactly where the inventory is being stored without having to download an inventory report.

**Where can I find Sales Distribution Maps in Helium 10?**

- Sales Distribution Maps can be found in the Profits dashboard within its own tab labeled “Heat Maps”, as shown below:

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/helium-10-heat-maps-image-aq7ua1as.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/SCsimage.png)
- Heat Maps can also be found while viewing any product in your main Profits dashboard. Simply navigate to the far-right column and you’ll see a folded map icon. Clicking on this icon will take you to your inventory Heat Map.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/helium-10-heat-maps-image-km190o46.png)

**Frequently Asked Questions:**

Is there a way to save my current Heat Map?

- Yes! You can export your report directly to Excel, which can also be helpful for sellers who would like a text-based record of where their inventory is currently stored.

**Can I search for ASINs individually?**

- Yes! To review an individual product, type the ASIN or SKU at101010025 - on the top of the Heat Maps tool and you’ll instantly get a map of the country where your inventory is currently being stored! As of this writing (03/16/2022), Heat Maps is only available in the United States.

**How often are Heat Maps updated?**

- Heat Maps is updated once every day and includes the following information breakdown (which can be further broken down based on the city your inventory is stored):

  - Number of sellable items
  - Number of customers' damaged items
  - Defective items
  - Total items

FAQs

General Information

- Q1: What are Helium 10 Heat Maps?

  - A1: Heat Maps, or Heat Map Analytics, is a feature in Helium 10’s Profits tool suite that provides a visual representation of all Amazon FBA warehouse locations where your inventory is currently stored.
- Q2: Where can I find Sales Distribution Maps in Helium 10?

  - A2: Sales Distribution Maps are located in the Profits dashboard under a tab labeled “Heat Maps.” You can also access Heat Maps for specific products via the folded map icon in the far-right column of the main Profits dashboard.

Functionality

- Q3: How do I interpret the circles on the Heat Map?

  - A3: The circles on the map represent warehouse locations. Larger circles indicate more inventory stored at that location.
- Q4: Can I save my current Heat Map?

  - A4: Yes, you can export your Heat Map report to Excel for a text-based record of your inventory storage locations.
- Q5: Can I search for individual ASINs or SKUs?

  - A5: Yes, you can search for a specific product by typing its ASIN or SKU in the search bar at the top of the Heat Maps tool. This will display a map of where that product’s inventory is stored.

Updates and Data

- Q6: How frequently are Heat Maps updated?

  - A6: Heat Maps are updated once daily. They provide a breakdown of sellable items, customers' damaged items, defective items, and total items, which can also be further analyzed by city.

Practical Use

- Q7: Why are Heat Maps important for launching or relaunching a product?

  - A7: Heat Maps help sellers ensure their inventory is widely distributed across the country before launching or relaunching a product. This reduces the likelihood of customers seeing delays like “few days to ship,” which can negatively impact conversions.
- Q8: Do Heat Maps work outside of the United States?

  - A8: Currently, as of 03/16/2022, Heat Maps is only available in the United States.
