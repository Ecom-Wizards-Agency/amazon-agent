# Supplemental Article: Reviving Sales on Amazon (Strategies for Overcoming Common Seller Challenges)

## **Supplemental Article: Reviving Sales on Amazon (Strategies for Overcoming Common Seller Challenges)**

**Purpose:**

This article aims to help Amazon sellers identify and resolve common issues that may be affecting their sales performance. By understanding the root causes of sales drops, sellers can apply practical solutions to restore product visibility, improve listing quality, and drive more sales. This guide provides a detailed checklist to address problems such as stockouts, keyword ranking drops, image suppressions, advertising issues, and more.

I.

Identifying Stockouts

Identifying potential stockouts is a critical step in understanding sharp drops in sales.

**What is a Stockout?**A stockout occurs when an ASIN runs out of inventory and cannot be sold via Fulfilled by Merchant (FBM) or Fulfilled by Amazon (FBA), resulting in a "stocked out" product.

**How Can I Locate a Stockout?**To identify stockouts, navigate to your Amazon inventory page, sort your ASINs, and look for "inactive" items. If your ASIN is marked as active, then it's not stocked out. Keep track of your inventory levels and sales velocity.

**Methods to Monitor Stockouts:**

1. **BSR Reports:** Locate your Best Seller Rank (BSR) for ASINs and track sales over the last 30 days. Ensure you always have a 60-day supply by comparing sales data with current inventory levels and lead times.
2. **Inventory Page:** Sort ASINs by availability and check if any are inactive.

**Notes:**

- Proactively monitor stockouts.
- Keep a 60-day supply to prevent issues.
- Prioritize fast restocks when necessary.

**Why Do Stockouts Matter?**

Stockouts matter because they result in a loss of ranking, velocity, and placement on Amazon, which can be difficult to regain.

**Mitigation Strategies for Stockouts:**

- Know your sell-through rate and always maintain a 60-day inventory buffer.
- Cultivate strong relationships with reliable suppliers.
- Prepare for seasonal events (e.g., Black Friday, Cyber Monday) to ensure you're adequately stocked.
- If stockouts can't be avoided, consider reducing advertising spend or adjusting prices to slow sales and avoid full depletion.
- Utilize Fulfilled by Merchant (FBM) as a fallback.

**Notes:**

- Stockouts can damage long-term sales performance.
- Prevent them with proactive inventory management and supplier relations.

II. Identifying Hidden Search Suppressions

Hidden suppressions occur when you cannot find an ASIN by its number, affecting search rankings and indexing.

**How to Detect Hidden Suppressions:**

1. **Search for ASIN:** Go to the homepage on Amazon and type in the ASIN. If it doesn’t show up, there may be a hidden suppression.
2. **Action Required:** If suppression is found, contact Amazon and request that the correct category and keyword information be applied to fix the issue.

**Notes:**

- Search for hidden suppressions as part of routine account auditing.
- Take immediate action to resolve any discovered suppressions.

**Solving Hidden Search Suppressions**

**Steps to Resolve Hidden Suppressions:**

1. **Determine the GL Product Type and Browse Node:** Check your category in Amazon's [Browse Tree Guides (BTG)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/merchandising-sop-classify-your-products-using-browse-tree-guides).
2. **Ticket Amazon:** Submit a request to fix the ASIN’s category and keyword, including necessary documentation like screenshots.

**Template for Fixing Suppression:**
*Hey Amazon,*

*We’ve identified an issue with our ASIN (enter ASIN) not appearing in search. The correct GL product type is (Product Category), and the correct browse node is (enter browse node).*

*Please assist us in resolving this issue as soon as possible.*

*Thanks, [Your Name]*

**Notes:**

- Hidden suppressions can impede sales—solve them quickly by contacting Amazon support.

III. Identifying and Solving Inventory Location Issues

Inventory location issues can cause extended handling times, leading to a slowdown in sales.

**How to Identify Inventory Location Problems:**

1. **Inventory Page:** Check where your inventory is located (inbound, FC transfer, reserved).
2. **Prime Badge Issues:** Products in "FC transfer" may lose their Prime badge, leading to a drop in visibility and sales.

**Notes:**

- Review inventory locations to ensure timely shipments and avoid the loss of the Prime Badge.

V. Image Suppressions

Proper product images are essential for Amazon compliance and sales conversion. Amazon has specific guidelines for product images, and failure to follow them can lead to listing deletions, suppressed images, or loss of visibility.

**Amazon's Image Guidelines:**

1. **Main Image:** Must have a white background and show only the product being sold. No props, logos, or text allowed.
2. **Additional Images:** Can show different angles and usage but must not include any promotional content or logos.
3. **Size Requirements:** Images should be at least 1000x1000 pixels, with 2000x2000 pixels recommended for zoom functionality.
4. **File Format and Naming:** Use .jpg, .gif, or .png formats. Follow Amazon's naming conventions for files.

**Notes:**

- Ensure your images meet Amazon’s technical and visual requirements to avoid listing issues.
- Provide clear, high-quality images to enhance the customer experience.

VI. Banned SKUs - How to Identify and Solve

An ASIN can be banned from Amazon ads for policy violations, leading to a major drop in sales.

**Common Reasons for Banning:**

- Violations like drug references, weight loss claims, or inappropriate content in ads or A+ content.
- Issues with SKU listings being linked to inappropriate content, such as misleading labels.

**How to Identify and Fix Banned SKUs:**

1. **Advertising Dashboard:** If campaigns show no impressions or a sudden drop, the SKU may be banned.
2. **Resolution:** Remove prohibited content, update labels, and file a ticket with Amazon.

**Notes:**

- Ensure compliance with Amazon’s TOS to prevent ads bans.
- Routinely monitor ad performance to catch issues early.

VII. Losing the Buybox

Losing the Buy Box is a major issue, as it can lead to reduced sales and less visibility for your product.

**Causes for Losing Buybox:**

- Pricing issues.
- Fulfillment problems.
- Poor performance metrics.

**Solution:**

1. Regularly check if you are winning the Buy Box and fix any issues that might be causing you to lose it.
2. Use Amazon’s Buybox Cheat Sheet for detailed guidance.

**Notes:**

- Always ensure you are winning the Buy Box to maximize sales and visibility.
- Losing the Buy Box can directly impact your product's performance and conversion rates.

VIII. Going Out of Budget in Amazon PPC

When an Amazon PPC campaign goes out of budget, it stops running until the next day, causing potential revenue loss.

**Solution:**

- Keep track of your PPC budgets to ensure they’re sufficient.
- Regularly optimize campaigns to maintain continuous advertising.

**Notes:**

- Keep a close eye on your campaigns’ budgets to avoid extended periods without ads.
- Optimize your campaigns to prevent unnecessary budget issues.

**Pricing Strategy**

**Summary:**Pricing plays a significant role in sales performance on Amazon. Pricing too high or too low can negatively affect visibility and sales.

**Solution:**

- Monitor competitor prices regularly.
- Consider using dynamic pricing tools to adjust pricing according to market conditions.

**Notes:**

- Competitive pricing is essential to securing a strong market position.
- Use automated tools to adjust pricing based on competitor activity and market trends.

IX. Customer Experience (NCX) Ratings and Suppressions

Negative NCX ratings can lead to suppressions and affect listing visibility.

**How to Manage NCX Ratings:**

- Regularly check your NCX ratings through Amazon’s Voice of the Customer portal.
- Address any issues that cause poor ratings.

**Notes:**

- Keep a close eye on NCX ratings.
- Address negative ratings quickly to prevent suppressions.

X. Combatting Hijackers

Hijackers can seriously harm your Amazon business. They can undercut your pricing, cause you to lose the Buybox, and damage your brand image.

**How to Combat Hijackers:**

1. Enroll in Amazon Brand Registry for added protection.
2. Monitor your listings and report any unauthorized sellers through Brand Registry.

**Note:** Protect your listings from hijackers and ensure your brand’s reputation stays intact.
