## **How to Design a CTR Hack Main Image**

**Who is this for?**

This SOP is for teams assigned to design a product's main image using the CTR Hack.

**Objectives**

Update a main image using MAG’s CTR Hack playbook. The process will help customers gain interest on the product by attracting them to click on it more often which will potentially increase sales and improve a listing's SEO and PPC traffic.

**What:**Update a main image using MAG’s CTR Hack playbook

**Why:** 2x sales, improves PPC and SEO traffic (CTR)
**How:** Helps customers gain interest on the search page for the product and click on it more often (CTR)

**Training:** [Video](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1Gr3wVkJFjPv7F8p8qtIsowl0S_16pPPi/view?usp=sharing)and [Script](https://docs.google.com/document/u/1/d/1VyYd56Ki8_EY5DGhoiCU98EbJfMlbORswormVwmpLmo/copy)

Estimated time to complete:

- 10 minutes to look at the ASIN detail page and come up with a plan
- 35 minutes to design the image
- 5 minutes to deliver the image and get QA

f. More often than not, put the ingredients in the back of the main package shot/hero (or the side)

l. Never say “produced from” just say the material example Glass vs Made from or Produced from Glass

**Mobile Optimization & Image Design Guidelines**

What Designers Should Do (with Mobile Optimization in Mind)

Keep in mind that many Amazon customers browse using mobile devices. As designers, here’s what we should do to ensure the best viewing experience across both desktop and mobile:

**General Best Practices**

- minimize/avoid tiny text, specially if it's important to be read
- make proper adjustments for Premium A+ content mobile view
- try to view what you made on a mobile device

**Main Image Tips**

- Crop out empty areas on the left and right sides of the image:

  - Makes it display the same as a square if the layout calls for a square
  - It will display bigger on mobile compared to a square
- Maximize vertical space within the canvas:

  - Wide/flat products won't be able to take advantage of the vertical layout*
  - Options for wide and/or flat (horizontal) products: show it diagonally, but only if it doesn't look weird or awkward
  - On mobile, there is no way to make use of the full vertical space in search results

**Additional Images**

- Use 1600 x 2000 px instead of square images when possible:
- If thumbnails are on the left side and your image is rectangular, it MIGHT display (slightly) on desktop compared to a square image.

**Suppression guidance:**

- Do not include ™ or R symbols
- Tags usually don’t get suppressed.
- Circular stickers cause the most suppression but often look the best
- Fixing a suppression is often easy. We've zoomed in 0.5% on a rejected image, saved it, and re-submitted and it worked.

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What is the main goal of the CTR Hack for product images?**

  - **A1:** The goal is to update a product's main image to attract more customer clicks, thereby doubling sales, improving PPC, and enhancing SEO traffic.
- **Q2: What are the estimated time requirements for completing the CTR Hack process?**

  - **A2:**

    - 10 minutes to review the ASIN detail page and plan.
    - 35 minutes to design the image.
    - 5 minutes to deliver the image and perform QA.
- **Q3: What is the first step in implementing the CTR Hack for a main image?**

  - **A3:** The first step is to go to the ASIN, review all the images on the listing, and compare the product against competitors by searching it on Amazon.

Identifying Improvements

- **Q4: How can I identify what is missing from the main image?**

  - **A4:** Consider the following:

    - Missing keywords (e.g., from Brand Analytics or Cerebro).
    - Packaging details.
    - Context like users (e.g., child, animal, person).
    - Product details like ingredients, color, accessories, or quantity.
    - Relevant starbursts for specific audiences (e.g., age group).
    - American-made flag (if applicable).
- **Q5: What should I add to the image to improve its CTR?**

  - **A5:** Add 2-4 missing elements such as keywords, packaging details, user context, and features like starbursts or product ingredients. Ensure these additions add value and improve the shopping experience.

Best Practices

- **Q6: What are the best practices for adding text to main images?**

  - **A6:**

    - Text should be vertical, large, and easy to read.
    - Avoid unnecessary fillers like "with."
    - Keep descriptions simple, e.g., "Age 3-6" instead of "for kids ages between 3-6."
- **Q7: What are the requirements for image design?**

  - **A7:**

    - Use a pure white background with the product occupying at least 85% of the image.
    - Ensure all additions are accurate and visually appealing.
    - Avoid floating icons, reflections, or showing excluded accessories.
- **Q8: Can I use models or illustrations in the main image?**

  - **A8:**

    - Models can be used for clothing or mobility aids but must face the camera.
    - Kids and baby clothing must be photographed flat, and shoes should be displayed at a 45-degree angle.

Troubleshooting

- **Q9: What should I do if the client dislikes the updated image?**

  - **A9:** Ask for:

    - Additional assets.
    - Specific feedback on what they want to see.
    - Examples of listing images they prefer.
- **Q10: What if I cannot improve an already well-optimized image?**

  - **A10:** Make modest improvements, post in Slack for suggestions, and if necessary, offer promo codes or another ASIN for free to satisfy the client.

Suppression and Compliance

- **Q11: What are common suppression risks for updated images?**

  - **A11:**

    - Avoid ™ or ® symbols, which can lead to suppression.
    - Circular stickers, while often look the best, cause the most suppression
    - Suppression fixes may involve small adjustments, like zooming in slightly and resubmitting.
- **Q12: Are there restrictions on using flags or origin labels?**

  - **A12:** Yes, only include an American-made flag if the product listing explicitly states "Made in the USA." Do not use it if the country of origin is listed as China or another location.

Case Examples

- **Q13: Can you provide examples of successful CTR Hack implementations?**

  - **A13:**

    - **Cookie Baking Sheets:** Added cookies and a keyword sticker to evoke sensory appeal.
    - **HappyMe Journal:** Highlighted age demographic (6-12), added keywords, and showed the book's interior, tripling sales.
    - **Poochie Powder:** Replaced unattractive powder visuals with fresh veggies and emphasized USDA certification.
