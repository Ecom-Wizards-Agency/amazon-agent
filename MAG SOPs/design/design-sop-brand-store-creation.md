## **Brand Store Creation**

**Who is this for?**For those who need to set up a Brand Store on Amazon.

**Objective:**The purpose of this document is to present a comprehensive, sequential procedure for creating brand stores as well as choosing the best modules for a solid layout.

The ultimate goal is to enhance product visibility, engagement, and sales performance on Amazon by adding images, infographics, crawlable text, and links to products and subpages.

**Page Manager**

Within Store Builder, the page manager area is where you will create, name, and organize your pages. These pages appear in the Navigation Bar beneath the header image.

**Webpage Preview Window**

The Webpage Preview Window offers a current view of your web page design. It can also be used to select and move tiles for designing a good visual flow of the products.

**Also Important:**Mobile and Desktop Views

The Mobile and Desktop view options are in the top center of the webpage preview. This allows you to easily toggle between “how the Storefront will display and navigate” in both viewing methods. Keep your styles consistent but be sure to use tiles that will make navigation in the viewing method simple and easy.

**Page Sections: Tile Manager**

The Tile Manager is where you select various tiles. You can add, delete, move, and edit your tiles in this section. There are also helpful tips from Amazon on how to start writing about your brand.

**Store: Versions**

The Versions drop-down allows you to select between the different visions of the store you’ve already made. You can edit the Live version or perhaps you’ve made other templates to match the season!

**Selecting Tiles Types**

There are a total of **14 Tile Type Options** available for your storefront build. These include options for product grids, individual tiles, galleries, best sellers or recommended buys, graphic images, video and text blocks, and more to choose from.

**Each tile has its own unique benefits.**

For example, similar to the Carousel Brand Story Module on the PDP - the **Gallery Tile** allows you to display images or videos in an interactive scrolling carousel. While you can use this for showing product variations you can also use it to feature your best sellers.

**Pro TIP:** It’s good practice to utilize unique image graphics on the home page! Keep them consistent with your Brand's style, but having an engaging video or product graphic advertising multiple products in a visually appealing way increases the chance of your customer scrolling to see what else your store has to offer.

**Brand Store Design - Best Practices**

1. Start with a **Brand Header**(featured above the navigation bar)

  - This should be eye-catching.
  - Consistent with Brand Style.
2. A **Minimum of 3 Pages**: Home / Product Type / Product Type

  - If it's possible to create attention pages sorted by category or feature differences this is also a good practice.
3. All Product Images should Link to their PDP

  - Every image should be **clickable!**
  - Changing up the method of clicks also keeps things engaging. (ie. allow your template to flow consistently but try not to be too repetitive.)
4. Avoid Long Product Lists: **Graphic Images** can break this up and provide information on product features and usage without denying the customer an opportunity to purchase.
5. Customize your Storefront to **Match the Season!**

  - Whether it’s nearing Christmas, Halloween, New Year's, or any other popular holiday - it’s a great practice to update your banner or graphic images to include a call out to the time of year.
6. Do not forget to save the creative works and resources to shared client’s creative folder.

FAQs

Preparation

- **Q1: What initial steps should I take before starting the Brand Store creation?**

  - **A1:**

    - Review assets provided in the task by the Account POC.
    - Gather color and font information from the task description and client survey.
    - Check linked social media or websites to understand the brand’s style.
    - Read product bullet points on the PDP to identify key features.
    - Review ASINs for inclusion in a product grid or comparison chart.
- **Q2: Can I proceed if the client is not Brand Registered?**

  - **A2:** No, the client must be Brand Registered to create a Brand Store. If not, refer the task back to the Brand Manager for assistance with the registration process.

Store Builder Features

- **Q3: What are the main sections in the Store Builder?**

  - **A3:**

    - **Page Manager:** Create, name, and organize pages.
    - **Webpage Preview Window:** Visualize the design and toggle between mobile and desktop views.
    - **Tile Manager:** Add, move, and edit tiles for customizing the store.
    - **Store Versions:** Manage and edit different store versions, such as seasonal templates.
- **Q4: How can I ensure consistency between mobile and desktop views?**

  - **A4:** Use the Mobile and Desktop view toggle in the Webpage Preview Window to confirm consistent navigation and design across both viewing methods.

Designing the Store

- **Q5: What are the recommended tile types for a Brand Store?**

  - **A5:**
    Amazon offers 14 tile types, including:

    - Product grids
    - Galleries
    - Graphic images
    - Videos
    - Text blocks
  - Each tile serves a unique purpose, such as the Gallery Tile, which displays interactive carousels for product variations or best sellers.
- **Q6: What are the available tile template layouts?**

  - **A6:** Amazon provides four pre-made layouts: Marquee, Product Highlight, Product Collection, and Blank. These templates can be customized to fit the brand’s needs.
- **Q7: What are some design best practices for Brand Stores?**

  - **A7:**

    - Use an eye-catching header consistent with the brand style.
    - Include a minimum of three pages (e.g., Home, Product Type 1, Product Type 2).
    - Link all product images to their PDPs.
    - Break up long product lists with graphics or images.
    - Customize banners and graphics for seasons or holidays.

Troubleshooting

- **Q8: What should I do if I encounter a rejection error?**

  - **A8:** Carefully read the error message for clues on resolving the issue. Amazon’s error messages usually specify the problem and provide guidance for correction.

Examples and Inspiration

- **Q9: What are some examples of excellent Amazon storefronts?**

  - **A9:**

    - **Example #1:** Features a prominent video and product grids for a wide product range (e.g., swimsuits and accessories).
    - **Example #2:** Uses bold colors and a showroom-style layout with clickable buttons for product remedies.
    - **Nutrivein:** Incorporates a high-quality homepage with videos emphasizing product benefits and adventure.
    - **Age of Sage:** Known for exceptional seasonal designs, promoting loyalty through fun and timely updates.
