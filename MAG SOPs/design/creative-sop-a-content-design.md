# Creative SOP: A+ Content Design

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/design-XyS/page/creative-sop-a-content-design

## **A+ Content Design**

**Who is this for :**

- For those who need to create an A+ Content design for Amazon Listings
- For those who want to improve the A+ Content's layout & structure

**Objective :**

The purpose of this document is to present a sequential procedure for creating A+ content for product pages as well as choosing the best modules for a solid layout. The ultimate goal is to enhance product visibility, engagement, and sales performance on Amazon by adding images, infographics, crawlable text, and product comparison charts.

**Summary of Process:**

Review the assets provided on the task by the Account POC

- Gather color and/or font information from the task description or Design Brief
- View social media (if linked) or websites to obtain an idea of the style
- Use Lorem Ipsum to represent copy for key product features
- Review what ASINs will be included for a product grid (client provides details)

**A+ Creation Breakdown:**

- Seller Central - A+ Content Manager
- Create New A+ Design - EBC Title
- Selecting Modules
- Approved Module Template Layout
- Saving As Draft - Screenshot
- Submitting for QA - Asana Task
- Copywriting Stage
- Client Edits
- Uploading Stage - Pending Amazon Approval
- Approved
- Past A+ Design Highlights

(Crawlable text **must** be incorporated in each A+ design)

Crawlable Text is the highlightable text separate from the photos. While Amazon can detect the words in infographic images they don’t have any backend value. Crawlable text is highly valuable as it is utilized in SEO/Indexing. For best practices, there should be 500 words of crawlable text incorporated in each EBC design.

**Approved Modules Template Layout**

While as the designer you have free creative liberty - Here is an excellent set of modules our clients have reacted very positively towards. Each client will not be the same but this is a safe layout to start with!

**Approved MAG Module Template Layout**

- Standard Image & Dark Text Overlay
- Standard Image Header With Text
- Standard Single Image & Sidebar
- Standard Image Header With Text
- Standard Four Image & Text
- Standard Image Header With Text
- Standard Comparison Chart

The **Standard Image & Dark Text Overlay** module works great as a thinner/narrower header image. This Module is great for Logos, Brand Banners, and Graphic Headers (If you leave the text area blank, it won’t block your image when previewed)

The **Standard Image Header With Text** module works great as large graphic images call out. Promote the product right away with a large clear graphic! This Module also has room for text directly beneath the image.

The **Standard Single Image & Sidebar** module works great when used for logo placement or smaller graphic images accompanied by informative text. Good practice for this module is Brand Information on the far right and product details and bullets in the center.

The **Standard Four Image & Text** module is the perfect solution for conveying product benefits, selling points, ingredients, uses, instructions, and much more! Create relevant graphic images or icons to relate to the text below.

The **Standard Comparison Chart**module should always be located at the bottom of the A+ design as the 7th module. The Comparison cart will consist of a maximum of 6 other products (from the client's catalog) with the comparative metrics listed on the left. A maximum of 10 listed metrics can be featured. The columns can be highlighted, check-marked, or can have written text.

**Submitting for QA - Asana Task**

When ready to submit for QA - Return to the task and in the Comment area assign it to your designated QA Pod Leader. Your QA Pod Leader will review the draft, note any edits then forward the deliverable to the client for approval.
**IMPORTANT:** Always take a screenshot/proof or link the fireflies call for every client’s approval/disapproval and comment it in the task.

**Copywriting Stage**

There will be a brief copywriting stage that takes place AFTER the client approves of the A+ Design. A copywriter will use high-ranking keywords, rewrite the content and return a document that has all the text to be “Copy and Pasted” into the A+.

The task, once QA’d for design, is then tasked to a copywriter within your pod. If you feel that your copywriter is exceeding their task counts, please reach out for assistance. Do not assign copywriting tasks to project managers. Copywriters need to get to know their clients, too!

Place the copy text into the appropriate module areas indicated in the document

**Seller Edits - Procedure**

A+ Content Edits should have a turnaround time of 1-2 days maximum depending on the scope of the edit. Type-o’s and minor graphic edits need to be addressed quickly - send these to an intern for a fast fix. Full redesigns are considered acceptable in 3 days.

Repeat the Submitting for QA step when edits are complete.

Edits (even minor ones) must be delivered as a full screenshot.

FAQs

Preparation

- **Q1: What steps should I take before starting the A+ Content design?**

  - **A1:**

    - Review assets provided by the Account POC.
    - Gather design elements like color palettes and fonts from the task description or Design Brief.
    - Research brand style via linked social media or websites.
    - Use placeholder text (Lorem Ipsum) for key product features.
    - Confirm ASIN details for the product grid, as provided by the client.

Creating A+ Content

- **Q2: Where do I start in Seller Central?**

  - **A2:**

    - Log in to Seller Central.
    - Navigate to **Advertising > A+ Content Manager**.
    - Select “Start creating A+ content” and choose **Create Basic** for A+ (EBC).
    - Name your design using the format:

      - **Brand - ASIN - A+**
      - **Brand - Product Type - A+**
- **Q3: How many modules can be included in an A+ Content design?**

  - **A3:** A+ Content allows a maximum of **7 modules per design**. Ensure crawlable text is included in each module for SEO benefits, aiming for 500 words in total.
- **Q4: What is crawlable text, and why is it important?**

  - **A4:** Crawlable text is highlightable and separate from images. While Amazon can "see" text in images, it lacks SEO value. Crawlable text improves product indexing and discoverability.

Module Selection and Layout

- **Q5: What modules are recommended for A+ Content?**

  - **A5:** Use the following MAG-approved template:

    - **Standard Image & Dark Text Overlay**: Ideal for logos, brand banners, and graphic headers.
    - **Standard Image Header With Text**: Showcases large graphics with a text section underneath.
    - **Standard Single Image & Sidebar**: Combines small images with text for brand or product info.
    - **Standard Four Image & Text**: Displays product benefits, instructions, or features.
    - **Standard Comparison Chart**: Best placed as the 7th module to highlight product comparisons.
- **Q6: What is the function of the Comparison Chart module?**

  - **A6:** The Comparison Chart allows a side-by-side view of up to 6 products, listing key metrics (maximum of 10). It should be placed at the bottom of the design for easy navigation.

Drafting and QA

- **Q7: What should I do after completing the design?**

  - **A7:**

    - Save the design as a draft.
    - Screenshot the full draft preview using tools like **GoFullPage** for Chrome.
    - Submit the screenshot to the QA Pod Leader through Asana for review.
- **Q8: What is the procedure for QA and client edits?**

  - **A8:**

    - QA checks the design and notes any required edits.
    - For minor edits, interns can assist; full redesigns should be completed within 3 days.
    - Resubmit the updated design with a full screenshot to QA.

Copywriting

- **Q9: What happens during the copywriting stage?**

  - **A9:** Once the client approves the design:

    - A copywriter adds high-ranking keywords and provides text for the modules.
    - Designers copy and paste the text into the appropriate areas.
    - Copywriting tasks should be assigned to the designated copywriter, not project managers.

Uploading and Approval

- **Q10: What steps are involved in uploading the approved design?**

  - **A10:**

    - Confirm client approval via screenshot or Fireflies call link.
    - Open the A+ Content Manager and apply ASINs to the design.
    - Click **Next: Review & Submit**.
    - Monitor the status, which may take up to 7 days for Amazon’s approval.
- **Q11: How will I know the design is approved?**

  - **A11:** Check the content status in the **Content Preview** or **A+ Content Manager**. Notify the QA Team Lead once approved.
