## **Utilizing a Balanced Mix of Organic SEO and Sponsored Content for Maximum Visibility**

**Who is this for :** This is for business owners and marketers who are looking to increase their online visibility by utilizing the balanced mix of 2 parts organic SEO and 1 part sponsored content to maximize the benefits of PPC and organic SEO.

**Objectives:**

- Maximize the benefits of PPC and organic SEO by utilizing both strategies together.
- Increase online visibility by pushing traffic to targeted keywords and optimizing for SEO.

**Amazon PPC and Amazon SEO**

Amazon PPC and SEO are a happily married couple, they should work in correlation with one another to enhance organic rankings and overall, sales.

Over the course of about six months, for every PPC sale you make, you have the potential to make three SEO sales. Therefore, by investing in PPC on the keywords that you convert well for, you're increasing organic rankings throughout many different keywords which in turn, will increase your overall sales.

Although you can boost traffic to your listings through PPC, you need to also ensure you’re optimizing your listings correctly in order to convert those clicks into sales. Sending paid traffic to listings that are not fully optimized will not only waste money but also, have a negative effect on organic rankings.

**Organic to Sponsored Ratio (The 2:1 Ratio)**

As stated above, keeping your SEO and PPC efforts working together is crucial. This allows you to reap the benefits of higher organic rankings over time.

You should, however, keep in mind the ratio between organic and sponsored keywords. You should have 2x the amount of organically indexed SEO keywords as you do PPC keywords. Maintaining this ratio enables you to gain organic traffic for more keywords to maintain profitability, while also pushing paid traffic to supplement the sales and continuously improve overall organic ranking. You can find this ratio of organic to paid keywords on almost any Amazon keyword research tool on the market.

**Take Away:**

1. PPC and organic SEO should work hand in hand with one another in order to gain the most benefits.
2. Pushing traffic to keywords is only one-half of the equation. You must also ensure your SEO efforts are in line.
3. You should maintain a 2:1 organic to sponsored-ratio.

FAQs

General Strategy

- **Q1:** **Why is a 2:1 ratio of organic to sponsored keywords important?**

  - **A1:** Maintaining a 2:1 ratio ensures you benefit from organic traffic while supplementing it with PPC to drive immediate sales. This balance helps improve organic rankings over time without becoming overly reliant on paid traffic.
- **Q2: How do organic SEO and Amazon PPC complement each other?**

  - **A2:** Amazon PPC boosts visibility and drives immediate sales, which can positively impact organic rankings for the targeted keywords. Simultaneously, optimizing for organic SEO ensures your listings convert effectively, leveraging both strategies for long-term success.

Implementation

- **Q3: What happens if I only focus on PPC without optimizing my listings for SEO?**

  - **A3:** Focusing solely on PPC without proper SEO optimization can lead to wasted ad spend and lower organic rankings, as poorly optimized listings may not convert clicks into sales efficiently.
- **Q4: How can I identify the right keywords for the 2:1 organic-to-sponsored ratio?**

  - **A4:** Use Amazon keyword research tools to identify high-converting keywords. Prioritize organically indexed keywords and supplement with PPC campaigns for the most relevant ones.

Measuring Success

- **Q5: How long does it typically take to see results from the combined SEO and PPC strategy?**

  - **A5:** Results can vary, but a combined approach often shows significant improvement in organic rankings and sales over six months, as PPC sales contribute to organic ranking growth.
- **Q6: How can I measure the effectiveness of the 2:1 ratio strategy?**

  - **A6:** Monitor organic traffic growth, keyword rankings, and conversion rates for organically indexed keywords alongside PPC performance metrics. Tools like Ad Badger can help track progress effectively.

Best Practices

- **Q7: What are the risks of not maintaining the recommended 2:1 ratio?**

  - **A7:** A skewed ratio can lead to over-reliance on PPC, higher ad spend, and reduced profitability. Conversely, neglecting PPC can slow down organic growth and limit your reach.
- **Q8: Should the organic-to-sponsored ratio differ for new product launches?**

  - **A8:** For new product launches, the ratio may temporarily skew towards PPC to build initial visibility. However, transitioning to the 2:1 ratio over time ensures sustainable growth.

Tools and Resources

  - **A9:** Amazon keyword research tools like Helium 10, Jungle Scout, or Ad Badger can help track and analyze your organic and PPC keyword performance to maintain the recommended ratio.
