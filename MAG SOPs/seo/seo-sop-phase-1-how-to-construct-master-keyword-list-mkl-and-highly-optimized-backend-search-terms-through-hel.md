# SEO SOP: Phase 1 - How To Construct Master Keyword List (MKL) and Highly-Optimized Backend Search Terms Through Helium10 Cerebro Reverse ASIN Lookup

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/seo/page/seo-sop-phase-1-how-to-construct-master-keyword-list-mkl-and-highly-optimized-backend-search-terms-through-helium10-cerebro-reverse-asin-lookup

## **SEO PHASE 1: Creating a Master Keyword List and Optimizing the Backend Search Terms**

**WHO IS THIS FOR?**

This SOP is designed for individuals seeking to enhance the visibility and revenue of their Amazon listings through strategic backend search terms optimization. This SOP will walk you through the process of building a Master Keyword List (MKL) using Helium 10 Cerebro's Reverse ASIN Lookup and constructing highly optimized backend search terms.

**OBJECTIVES:**

- Identify the product’s top 10 competitors
- Build the Master Keyword List
- Construct highly optimized backend search terms and other search attributes, including subject matter, intended use, and target audience
- Implement the optimized backend search terms to maximize the product listing's visibility and revenue on Amazon

*Note : You may skip SEO Phase 1 and proceed to* [***SEO Phase 3***](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/seo/page/seo-sop-phase-3-seo-high-level-keyword-strategy-for-advanced-keyword-striking-distance-rankings)*if :*

- *The listing is more than 6 months old*
- *Organic KW (Keyword) Count is 800+*
- *Must have 50 words in ranks 20 -50*

[**How to QA Loom Video**](https://www.loom.com/share/60b4e56fd2274aa28415c1ea54aff097)

**Tools Needed :**

- Scribbles (H10)
- [MKL and SEO Worksheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/<google-sheet-id>/export?format=xlsx)

**BEFORE STARTING:**

- Always check the product’s organic keyword count to determine the appropriate SEO phase to be conducted. SEO Phase 1 applies to ASINs indexed in less than 800 organic keywords. For ASINs indexed in 800+ organic keywords, it is advisable to skip to SEO Phase 3 for strike zone optimization opportunities.

- If the client has provided their own top 10 competitors, ensure they meet the criteria for keyword extraction (high BSRs, sales, and revenue). Extracting and using keywords from low-performing products may not yield the desired benefits for the client’s product listing.

**STEPS:**

**I. Understanding the Product and Identifying the Seed Keyword**

- Fundamental to this process is a comprehensive understanding of the product. Whether it's supplements, mattresses, mats, carpets, or any other item, selecting keywords that accurately describe the client's product is essential. For instance, if the product is in the supplement category, focus on keywords related to supplements or vitamins when conducting searches on Amazon.
- For experienced specialists dealing with common listings (e.g., vitamin C supplements), a straightforward approach involves searching for competitors using the seed keyword "Vitamin C Supplements."

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/seo-phase-1-creating-a-master-keyword-list-and-optimizing-backend-search-terms-image2.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/s4zimage.png)

- However, if you prefer a more systematic approach, **H10 Xray Keywords** can be a valuable tool. This feature furnishes a list of high-volume search keywords that aids in efficiently identifying competitors, especially in scenarios where identifying the seed keyword proves challenging, particularly for new-to-market or super-niche products with limited competition.
- To use this method, utilize either the client's own product or a high-performing competitor product. Run **H10 Xray Keywords** on the product detail page to extract the top-performing keywords associated with this listing. Subsequently, you may use these keywords to conduct a targeted search for other pertinent competitors within the same category.

**II. Searching for Relevant Products and Identifying the Top 10 Competitors**

- After pinpointing the seed keyword, proceed to identify competitors using the Amazon search page.
- Confirm that you are in the appropriate Amazon marketplace region relevant to your product. Input the correct delivery postal code associated with your intended market.
- Type the identified seed keyword into the Amazon search bar. Initiate the Search.

[2] Subsequently, **select** all ASINs, and then

Because normally duplicate brands may mean they are in the same variation and using the same set of keywords so we want to maximize the chance of getting more KWs by using listings with different brands*

[3] proceed to **deselect** the top 10 ASINs that closely align with or are highly relevant to the client's product. Once this is done,

[4] remove all the selected ASINs that fall outside the top 10 or are deemed non-relevant products.

[a] Search Volume: **Min 100**

[b] Word Count: **Min 1**

[c] Match Type: **Organic**

[2] Click **Apply Filters.**

[3] On the worksheet, don’t forget to **Filter the Search volume to >=300**

**NOTE : As much as possible, we use keywords with greater than 300 search volume, that is why on the worksheet, we filter the SV to greater than or equal to 300.**

Scrolling down to the filtered keywords list,

[1] **Export** the list

[2] as an**XLSX file**.

**Important Notes:**

- The MAG score indicates the count of ASINs within the top 10 products that rank 30 or better for the corresponding keyword phrase.
- To establish a benchmark for acceptable search volume, we set the minimum threshold at 300 searches per month, equivalent to an average of 10 searches per day. This can be adjusted accordingly (lowered to 200 then 100 if there are not enough keywords), especially if the product belongs to a super-niche category with an expected search volume of less than 300 per month.

**Dos and Don’ts When Optimizing the Search Terms:**

- Stay within the ideal length limit.
- Avoid duplicate keywords within the search terms.
- Include spelling variations, misspelled words, & Spanish terms.
- Include abbreviations and alternate names.
- Exclude words with punctuation.
- Separate words with single spaces.
- Exclude brand names.
- Exclude ASINs.
- Exclude common / stop words such as a, an, and, by, for, of, the, with, etc.
- Use the singular OR plural form of the word - no need for both.
- Exclude temporary statements such as new, on sale now, etc.
- Exclude subjective claims such as best, cheapest, amazing, etc.
- Exclude abusive or offensive terms.
- Exclude health claims / medical terms.
- Exclude brand names. (Our own branded is also to be excluded)

- Subject Matter – 50 characters
- Intended Use – 100 characters
- Target Audience – 50 characters
- Other Attributes – 50 characters

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What should I do if I cannot identify the seed keyword for the product?**

  - **A1:** Use Helium 10's Xray Keywords feature on the product detail page of the client’s product or a high-performing competitor's product. This will generate a list of high-volume keywords to help identify potential competitors and seed keywords.
- **Q2: How do I handle niche products with limited competition?**

  - **A2:** For niche products, focus on high-performing competitor products or similar items. Adjust the minimum search volume filter in Helium 10 Cerebro to as low as 100 or 200 if the product category has naturally low search volumes.

Competitor Selection

- **Q3: Can I include duplicate brands in the top 10 competitor list?**

  - **A3:** No, avoid duplicate brands in the top 10 competitor list to maximize the diversity of keywords extracted. Duplicate brands may use similar keyword sets, limiting the scope of your keyword research.
- **Q4: What should I do if a product lacks sales or revenue data but has a BSR?**

  - **A4:** You can still include the product in the top 10 competitor list as long as it has a BSR. Prioritize products with available BSR data over those lacking it.

Keyword Research

- **Q5: Why is a minimum search volume of 300 used as the benchmark?**

  - **A5:** A minimum search volume of 300 ensures the keywords have sufficient visibility. For niche products, you can lower this threshold to 200 or even 100 to accommodate low-volume markets.
- **Q6: What is the MAG Score, and why is it important?**

  - **A6:** The MAG Score represents the number of top 10 competitors ranking in the top 30 positions for a given keyword. It helps identify high-value keywords relevant to your product's niche.

Optimizing Backend Search Terms

- **Q7: What should I do if my search terms exceed the character limit?**

  - **A7:** Prioritize high-MAG-score and high-volume keywords. Ensure the final list excludes duplicate, irrelevant, or low-value terms, and fit within the character count limit by removing stop words and unnecessary phrases.
- **Q8: Can I include misspelled words and synonyms in the backend search terms?**

  - **A8:** Yes, include spelling variations, misspelled words, synonyms, and Spanish terms to capture a broader range of searches.
- **Q9: Why should I exclude brand names and ASINs from the search terms?**

  - **A9:** Including brand names or ASINs violates Amazon’s policies and does not contribute to improving organic visibility.

Implementation and QA

- **Q10: What should I do if the client does not approve the optimized search terms?**

  - **A10:** Revise the search terms based on client feedback and resubmit for approval. Document the client’s feedback or Fireflies call link in Asana for reference.
- **Q11: How should I upload optimized search terms for multiple ASINs?**

  - **A11:** For multiple ASINs, use a bulk flat file upload to save time and ensure consistency. Follow Amazon’s flat file guidelines for proper formatting and submission.

Troubleshooting

- **Q12: What if Helium 10 Xray cannot extract BSR data?**

  - **A12:** Use revenue data as a fallback. If neither BSR nor revenue data is available, prioritize competitors based on review count.
- **Q13: How do I handle prohibited keywords in Helium 10 Keyword Processor?**

  - **A13:** Remove any underlined keywords marked as prohibited or branded. Ensure only relevant, policy-compliant terms remain in the final search term list.

Other Search Attributes

- **Q14: Are other search attributes, like subject matter and intended use, as important as backend search terms?**

  - **A14:** No, these attributes have less impact on SEO but should still be filled in for reinforcement. Ensure compliance with the character count limits for each field.

Tools and Resources

- **Q15: Can I use alternative tools instead of Helium 10 for keyword research?**

  - **A15:** While Helium 10 is recommended due to its integration with Amazon, you can use other tools like Jungle Scout or Viral Launch. Ensure the alternative tools provide similar keyword filtering and competitor analysis capabilities.
