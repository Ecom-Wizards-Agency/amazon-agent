# SEO SOP: QA Checklist for Copywriting

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/seo/page/seo-sop-qa-checklist-for-copywriting

## **QA Checklist for Copywriting**

**Who is this for :** This document is for individuals who are involved in copywriting and quality assurance of product listing optimization

**Objective :** This SOP provides guidelines and best practices for optimizing the title, bullet points, & description for Amazon listings while adhering to Amazon's terms and conditions.

**General QA Checklist**

- Check the product listing page to make sure that the optimizations written are about the product.
- Does the listing read in flowing English while applying top SEO terms?
- Are the grammar and spelling free of any mistakes? (Utilize Grammarly as a tool, but don’t rely on it for all the answers)
- Is the brand name spelled correctly and follows the same capitalization?
- Are the listings optimized for SEO based on our r[ecommended best practices](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D3imvLFOV04&t=511s)?
- - Titles utilizing 150-200 characters with high-volume & relevant keyword phrases
  - Unique bullet points utilizing SEO with most high ranking in the first 2-3 bullets
  - The description is at least 500 characters (even if there is A+ content)
  - Redundancy and keyword repetition reduced or removed
- Does the copy speak to the brand demographic that the client has portrayed?
- Does the copy use the client’s preferred language (funny, persuasive, emotional, etc.)?
- There are no prohibited copy terms (see this [SOP](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/seo/page/seo-sop-prohibited-amazon-terms-phrases-with-creative))

  - Putting down competitor listings (including indirectly, such as “unlike others”)
  - Sexual terms
  - Competitor brand names
  - Prohibited gifting language
  - Warranties or guarantees
  - External references (websites)
  - Medical claims (not using support language) or mention of Covid, cancer, disease, etc
  - Any mention of drugs, alcohol, tobacco, CBD, etc
  - Copyrighted terms

**Below is an example of a well-optimized listing:**

**Title**

✓ Starts with the brand name for brand awareness and is followed by specific identification of the type of product

✓ Contains specific product-identifying information

✓ Ends with the variation, such as the size or color of that ASIN.

✓ The first letters of each word are capitalized (except for the prepositions, conjunctions, & articles)

✓ Contains highly relevant keywords and phrases that are in compliance with Amazon's writing style and policies

✓ Does not contain promotional phrases and subjective commentaries

✓ Has 150-200 characters

**Additional QA Checklist for Medical and Nutritional Listings**

Confirm that there are no prohibited claims, health terms, or identifying of diseases:

- There are no names, terms, or phrases of any medical conditions
- Everything that is in the listing is a fact and can be backed with government documentation
- There is no reference to anything sexual, including symptoms
- There is no mention of CBD, Nicotine, or Cannabis
- There is no use of anti-symptoms, including antibacterial, antifungal, or anti-inflammatory
- There is no body shaming (lose weight, get rid of it)
- There are only commonly required certifications, such as GMP Certified
- It is not listed as Vegan if there are animal bi-products or contains Gelatin (all supplement listings are required to have an ingredient list)
- There is no direct mention of pain areas, such as joint pain or back pain
- There is no mention of anxiety, insomnia, emotions, or moods (aside from happy)
- There is no mention of relief, cure, or prevention
- There are no claims of medical or professional backing/recommendations
- There are no trademarked terms such as Keto and Paleo unless it is “Keto Friendly”

FAQs

General Overview

- **Q1: What is the purpose of the QA checklist for copywriting?**

  - **A1:** The checklist provides guidelines and best practices for optimizing titles, bullet points, and descriptions of Amazon listings while adhering to Amazon’s terms and conditions.

General QA Checklist

- **Q2: What should you verify to ensure the listing focuses on the product?**

  - **A2:** Check the product detail page (PDP) to confirm that all optimizations are relevant to the product.
- **Q3: How can you ensure the listing reads well in English?**

  - **A3:** Verify that the listing flows naturally in English while incorporating top SEO terms, and ensure there are no grammar or spelling mistakes (use Grammarly as a tool but don’t rely on it completely).
- **Q4: What are the key SEO requirements for titles, bullet points, and descriptions?**

  - **A4:**

    - **Titles:**

      - Use 150-200 characters.
      - Include high-volume, relevant keyword phrases.
    - **Bullet Points:**

      - Place high-ranking keywords in the first 2-3 bullets.
      - Keep each bullet point between 200-300 characters.
    - **Descriptions:**

      - Ensure at least 500 characters, even with Enhanced Brand Content (EBC).
      - Avoid redundancy and unnecessary keyword repetition.
- **Q5: What content should be avoided to comply with Amazon’s terms?**

  - **A5:** Prohibited content includes:

    - Competitor listings (directly or indirectly, e.g., “unlike others”).
    - Sexual terms, competitor brand names, prohibited gifting language, warranties or guarantees.
    - External references, medical claims, or mentions of drugs, alcohol, tobacco, CBD, and copyrighted terms.

Title Optimization

- **Q6: What are the best practices for writing a product title?**

  - **A6:**

    - Start with the brand name for brand awareness.
    - Follow with specific product information and end with variations (e.g., size or color).
    - Capitalize the first letters of each word except prepositions, conjunctions, and articles.
    - Avoid promotional phrases or subjective commentary.

Bullet Point Optimization

- **Q7: How should bullet points be structured?**

  - **A7:**

    - Start each bullet with a headline.
    - Include supporting information with clear, concise sentences.
    - Incorporate highly relevant keywords without sales pitches or subjective commentary.

Product Description and A+ Content

- **Q8: What are the key components of an optimized product description?**

  - **A8:**

    - Provide a summary of the product.
    - Ensure infographic texts align with images.
    - Include highly relevant, crawlable text that supports accompanying images.
    - Use clear, short sentences and avoid promotional language or subjective commentary.

Additional QA Checklist for Medical and Nutritional Listings

- **Q9: What medical claims or terms should be avoided in listings?**

  - **A9:** Prohibited terms and claims include:

    - Names or phrases of medical conditions.
    - Sexual references or symptoms.
    - Mentions of CBD, nicotine, cannabis, or anti-symptoms (e.g., antibacterial, anti-inflammatory).
    - Terms like “lose weight” or body shaming language.
    - Claims of relief, cure, prevention, or professional recommendations.
- **Q10: What requirements must nutritional listings meet?**

  - **A10:**

    - Ensure certifications like “GMP Certified” are accurate.
    - Avoid labeling products as “Vegan” if they contain animal by-products or gelatin.
    - Confirm ingredient lists are present and accurate.
    - Avoid claims of medical or professional backing unless documented.
