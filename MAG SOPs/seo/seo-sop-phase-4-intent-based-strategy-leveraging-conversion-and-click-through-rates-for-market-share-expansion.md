## **Intent-Based SEO Strategy - Leveraging Conversion and Click-through Rates for Market Share Expansion**

**Who is this for :** For individuals who want to further their backend search terms using MAG’s 4th SEO strategy.

**Objectives :** Analyze top search queries from Amazon's Search Query Performance tool to optimize product listings, increase market share, and improve rankings based on intent, leveraging conversion and click-through rates.

**IMPORTANT : Phase 4 SEO is not applicable for Vendor Central accounts due to the absence of SQP report, in that case, we just repeat Phase 3**

Before any implementation to back end or uploading, have the POC communicate, and get client’s approval first. Include the proof of approval (screenshot or fireflies call link) as Task Details or as a Comment.

**When** : Do a new market share analysis on a monthly basis

**Scope:** Rotate keywords every 90 days; must align with Phase 4 SEO goals

- Phase 3R1 > Phase 4 Round 1A > Phase 4 Round 1B
- Phase 3R2 > Phase 4 Round 2A > Phase 4 Round 2B
- Phase 3R3 > Phase 4 Round 3A > Phase 4 Round 3B
- (The phase cycle will repeat until further decisions are made)

**Eligibility:** Eligible ASINs are ones who meet the criteria set for our Phase 3 process.

**Note: This report must be done on an ASIN level.** Amazon rankings can change from time to time. Doing the market share analysis on a monthly basis can help maintain and improve the overall brand share, and mitigate areas that could potentially hurt the brand.

**Broad Overview :** SEO Phase 4 takes our strike zone keyword strategy (phase 3) to the next level, with Amazon’s new search query tool. Our strike zone strategy currently reviews well-indexed products with 800+ organically ranked keywords currently reflecting, in which we take organically ranked keywords from 20-50 and utilize them in order to get these specific keywords to rank 1-19. The Phase 4 SEO Query phase also is for mature listings with 800+ indexed keywords, but in this phase, we will be utilizing Amazon’s real-time data in order to make determinations of top keywords for each ASIN.

**Do’s & Don’ts:**

**Search Terms Optimization**

- Stay within the ideal length limit (246-250 characters).
- Avoid duplicate keywords within the search terms.
- Include spelling variations, misspelled words, & Spanish terms.
- Include abbreviations and alternate names.
- Exclude words with punctuations.
- Separate words with single spaces.
- Exclude client’s brand or other brand names.
- Exclude ASINs.
- Exclude common / stop words such as a, an, and, by, for, of, the, with, etc.
- Use the singular OR plural form of the word - no need for both.
- Exclude temporary statements such as new, on sale now, etc.
- Exclude subjective claims such as best, cheapest, amazing, etc.
- Exclude abusive or offensive terms.
- Exclude health claims / medical terms.
- Exclude brand names. (Our own branded is also to be excluded)

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What should I do if the ASIN does not have 800+ organic keyword counts?**

  - **A1:** If the ASIN does not meet the 800+ organic keyword count threshold, it is not eligible for Phase 4 SEO. Consider optimizing it further using Phase 3 SEO strategies and rechecking after 30 days.
- **Q2: How do I handle ASINs that fail to meet the eligibility criteria for Phase 4 SEO?**

  - **A2:** If an ASIN does not meet eligibility, document the reason in the Phase 4 worksheet and continue monitoring it monthly until it qualifies for Phase 4.

Report Preparation

- **Q3: What should I do if the Search Query Performance report does not generate?**

  - **A3:** Verify that the ASIN is part of Brand Registry and ensure you are in the correct Brand Analytics section. If issues persist, contact Amazon Seller Support for assistance.
- **Q4: Can I use prohibited or brand name keywords from the SQP report?**

  - **A4:** No, you must exclude prohibited, brand name, or irrelevant keywords when filtering SQP results. Use the Prohibited Keyword Tool to simplify this process.

Keyword Optimization

- **Q5: What happens if the search terms exceed the 250/500-character limit?**

  - **A5:** If the search terms exceed the character limit, prioritize relevant, high-performing keywords and remove duplicates or low-priority terms from the Phase 3 search terms.
- **Q6: How should I handle duplicate keywords in the backend search terms?**

  - **A6:** Remove duplicates using Helium 10’s Keyword Processor tool before finalizing the search terms. Ensure all keywords are unique within the allotted character limit.

Approval Process

- **Q7: What is the protocol for obtaining client approval before implementation?**

  - **A7:** Communicate the proposed changes to the POC, and secure client approval by sharing evidence (e.g., screenshot or Fireflies call link). Upload proof of approval in Task Details or as a comment in Asana.
- **Q8: What should I do if the client denies the proposed search terms?**

  - **A8:** Document the reason for denial, adjust the proposed search terms based on client feedback, and resubmit for approval.

Post-Implementation

- **Q9: How can I verify that search terms are reflecting on the backend after uploading?**

  - **A9:** Wait 24 hours after uploading, then check the backend to confirm the changes. If the search terms do not reflect, create a case with Amazon Seller Support.
- **Q10: What should I do if the updated search terms are not sticking?**

  - **A10:** Open a support case with Amazon Seller Support to investigate why the search terms are not sticking. Document the case details in the Phase 4 worksheet.

Monitoring and Tracking

- **Q11: How do I track the performance of the updated search terms?**

  - **A11:** Add the chosen keywords to Helium 10’s keyword tracker tool. Monitor their rankings monthly to identify improvements or areas requiring further optimization.
- **Q12: What actions should be taken if rankings for targeted keywords do not improve?**

  - **A12:** Reevaluate the selected keywords for relevance and competition. Adjust the backend search terms, PDP, and ad campaigns, and re-monitor rankings.

Documentation and Workflow

- **Q13: How should I handle ASINs within parent-child relationships during optimization?**

  - **A13:** Apply updates to all child SKUs within the parentage, ensuring the search terms are relevant to each SKU. Track and document changes in the worksheet.
- **Q14: When should the due date of the Asana task be updated?**

  - **A14:** Update the Asana task due date to 30 days from the completion date of Phase 4 SEO to allow for follow-up analysis and tracking.
