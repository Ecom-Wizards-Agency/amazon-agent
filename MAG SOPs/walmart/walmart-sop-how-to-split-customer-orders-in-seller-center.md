# Walmart SOP: How to Split Customer Orders in Seller Center

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/walmart/page/walmart-sop-how-to-split-customer-orders-in-seller-center

## **How to Split Customer Orders in Seller Center**

**Who is this for?**

This SOP is intended for amazon specialists who are responsible for managing and processing customer orders in Seller Center. It is also relevant for teams involved in inventory management, shipping, and order tracking to ensure that all orders are fulfilled accurately and promptly.

**Objective:**

The objective of this SOP is to show how to split customer orders in Seller Center when items cannot be shipped together due to size limitations, stock availability, or other reasons. By following these steps, sellers can ensure efficient processing and shipping of orders in a way that meets customer expectations while maintaining proper tracking and documentation for each shipment.

**Procedure:**

**Step 1: Find the Order**

1. Navigate to the **Orders** page within Seller Center.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/JvLimage.png)

1. Use the **Order #** drop-down menu or search using the **Purchase Order #** column to locate the specific customer order you need to process.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/6Jeimage.png)

1. Select the **Purchase Order Number** link to open the order details.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wMcimage.png)

**Step 2: Split the Order**

1. In the order details view, select the **Ship items** button.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Ym7image.png)

1. Use the drop-down menus to select the items you wish to ship immediately. Choose **Ship** and specify the quantity of the items to ship.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Fvpimage.png)

1. For the remaining items that will be shipped later, select **New** from the drop-down menu.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/du4image.png)

1. In the **Shipping Details** section, choose your **Shipping label**and **Shipping Address** for the items being shipped.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/dhximage.png)

1. Mark if the product you are shipping us considered as **dangerous goods.**

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/KEYimage.png)

1. Select the appropriate **Package Type and Weight** then click **Estimate rate**.

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/1SZimage.png)
2. Select your **Carrier**and click **Confirm and Continue**.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-how-to-split-customer-orders-in-seller-center-image-gi9mopkz.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/exfimage.png)

1. Click on **Mark as Shipped** to finalize the split for the first shipment.
2. Repeat the same process for the remaining items in the order, using the same **Purchase Order Number** for consistency.

FAQs

**1.Why do I have to repeat this process to ship the other items in the order?**

Each package must have its own unique tracking information. Therefore, it’s necessary to record each split shipment separately, marking as shipped only the items that will be included in the respective package.

**2. What should I do if a product being shipped is considered dangerous goods?**

When splitting an order, if any product being shipped is classified as dangerous goods, make sure to mark it as such in the Shipping Details section. This ensures compliance with shipping regulations for hazardous materials.

**3. Do I need to select the same Purchase Order Number for each shipment?**

Yes, when splitting an order, you should always use the same Purchase Order Number for each separate shipment. This maintains consistency and ensures that all items in the original order are properly tracked, regardless of how many shipments are made.
