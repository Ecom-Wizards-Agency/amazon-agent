Who is this for : This is for the Account Specialists who are in charge of creating a parentage.

Objective : To provide customers with a more comprehensive view of the available options for a product.

Note:

**How do I edit or remove an existing variation family?**

**To edit:**

- Download the Maintenance Spec Sheet, update the necessary fields, and re-upload.

**To remove a child from the parent:**

- Look for the column **“Remove from Variant Group?”** and choose **Yes** for the applicable SKU.
- Alternatively, you may also **leave the Variant Attribute Names field blank** for that SKU and re-upload the file.

**To delete parentage completely:**

- Remove all child SKUs and resubmit the file with the **Is Primary Variant** field set to **"No"**.

FAQs

1. What is parentage on Walmart, and why is it important?

Parentage (or a variation family) groups similar products under one listing, making it easier for customers to compare options (e.g., different sizes or colors). This improves the shopping experience and can increase conversions.

2. What are Walmart’s parentage rules?

- All child items must belong to the same Product Type.
- Parent SKUs do not hold inventory and must not have a price.
- Allowed variation attributes include Size, Color, Count Per Pack, Flavor, Scent, etc.
- Each child listing must have a unique value for the selected variant attribute.

3. What are the supported variation attributes on Walmart?

Common variation attributes include:

- Size (e.g., Small, Medium, Large)
- Color (e.g., Red, Blue, Black)
- Count Per Pack (e.g., 1 Pack, 2 Pack, 4 Pack)
- Flavor (e.g., Vanilla, Chocolate, Strawberry)
- Scent (e.g., Lavender, Citrus, Fresh Linen)

You must select the correct attribute that applies to all child products.

4. How do I choose the primary variant?

The primary variant is the default option displayed on the product page. Choose the most popular variation, often based on sales history or customer preference.

5. What should I do if the Maintenance Spec Sheet upload fails?

Check for these common issues:

- Incorrect SKU or Product ID format – Ensure SKU and ID match existing listings.
- Missing required attributes – All variation attributes must be filled in.
- Parent SKU contains inventory – Parent listings must not hold stock.
- Conflicting variant attributes – Ensure all child products share the same variation type.

6. How long does it take for Walmart to process parentage updates?

Parentage updates typically take 15 minutes to a few hours, but larger files may take up to 24 hours. You can check the status in the Activity Feed under Product Catalog.

7. How do I edit or remove an existing variation family?

To edit:

- Download the Maintenance Spec Sheet, update the necessary fields, and re-upload.

To remove a child from the parent:

- Leave the Variant Attribute Names field blank for that SKU and re-upload the file.

To delete parentage completely:

- Remove all child SKUs and resubmit the file with the Is Primary Variant field set to "No".

8. How do I verify that the parentage update was successful?

- In Items > Manage Variants, check if the “Manage Variants” option appears.
- Click View on Walmart.com to confirm the variation appears on the product page.
- Download a full item report and check if the Variant Group ID is assigned correctly.

9. What are Walmart’s best practices for parentage?

- Keep variations consistent – Don’t mix product types (e.g., don’t group a T-shirt with a hat).
- Use clear variant attributes – Avoid vague names like "Option 1" or "Style A".
- Follow proper SKU naming conventions – Ensure each child SKU is unique and matches your internal records.

10. What happens if I incorrectly set up parentage?

- Variations may not display properly on the Walmart product page.
- Walmart may reject the update or suppress listings.
- Customers may be confused, leading to lower sales and increased returns.
