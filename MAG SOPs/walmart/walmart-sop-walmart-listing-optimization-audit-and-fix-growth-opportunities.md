## **Walmart Listing Optimization Audit and Fix (Growth Opportunities)**

**Who is this for?** Can be done by the seller themselves or by a third-party service provider who specializes in Walmart marketplace optimization.

**Objective:** To evaluate and improve the visibility and performance of a product listing on Walmart. To identify any areas for improvement and implement changes if necessary.

Before any changes, have the POC communicate, and get client’s approval first for the proposed changes. Include the proof of approval (screenshot or fireflies call link) as Task Details or as a Comment.

FAQs

1. What is Walmart’s Listing Quality Score, and why does it matter?

The Listing Quality Score is Walmart’s internal rating of a product listing’s content, discoverability, pricing, and fulfillment reliability.

- A higher score (closer to 100%) improves your product’s search ranking, visibility, and conversions.
- Low scores can cause reduced traffic, suppressed listings, or lost sales opportunities.

2. What does a fully optimized Walmart listing look like?

A well-optimized listing includes:

- Title: Includes brand, product type, key attributes, and size/color if applicable (max 75 characters).
- Images: At least 1000x1000 pixels, multiple angles, lifestyle images, and no watermarks/logos.
- Key Features (Bullet Points): At least 4-5 bullet points highlighting product benefits.
- Description: SEO-optimized but natural, covering product benefits, specs, and usage.
- Attributes: Complete and accurate details (e.g., weight, material, color, size).
- Competitive Price & Offer: Matches or beats competitors while ensuring profitability.

3. How do I find and add relevant keywords to improve discoverability?

1. Check Walmart's search bar suggestions for commonly searched terms.
2. Analyze top-ranking competitors’ listings for keyword patterns.
3. Use tools like Helium 10, Jungle Scout, or Walmart’s Search Insights to find high-traffic keywords.
4. Add keywords naturally in the title, bullet points, and description without keyword stuffing.

4. How should I optimize listings with multiple variants (e.g., different sizes or colors)?

- Ensure each variation has a unique SKU and attributes while maintaining consistent descriptions.
- Use the correct Walmart variation attributes (e.g., Size, Color, Flavor) to group them properly.
- Optimize images to clearly show differences between variations.
- Avoid keyword repetition across all variants—focus on unique selling points for each.

5. What should I do if some listing attributes are locked and cannot be updated?

- First, check if the locked attribute can be changed via a full item spec sheet upload.
- If not, submit a support ticket to Walmart Partner Support with proof of the correct details.
- Follow the relevant SOPs:

  - Single Listing: Ticketing Walmart Support for Content and Image Update
  - Multiple Listings: Ticketing Walmart Support for Content and Image Update

6. How long does Walmart take to process listing updates and unlock attributes?

- Content updates typically take 24-72 hours.
- Locked attribute support requests can take 5-7 business days.
- Bulk updates for multiple SKUs may take up to 10 business days.

7. What content restrictions does Walmart enforce?

- No promotional language (e.g., "Best Price," "Limited Time Offer").
- No ALL CAPS or emojis in titles or descriptions.
- No misleading claims (e.g., "Guaranteed to Cure" for supplements).
- No unapproved comparisons to competitors (e.g., "Better than [Brand Name]").
- No duplicate listings for the same product.

8. How can I track whether my listing optimizations are working?

- Monitor Listing Quality Score changes in the Growth Dashboard.
- Track organic traffic, conversion rate, and sales before and after changes.
- Compare performance against competitors’ ranking and pricing.
- Use Walmart Sponsored Ads to test new keywords and improve visibility.

9. What if my listing never reaches a 100% score?

- Some product categories cannot achieve a perfect score due to missing attributes beyond your control.
- Focus on high-impact elements (Title, Images, Bullet Points, Price) rather than small percentage differences.
- If the score is above 85-90% and sales are strong, further adjustments may not be necessary.

10. Should I update my listings seasonally?

Yes! Seasonal optimization can boost sales:

- Add keywords related to holidays or trends (e.g., "Winter Boots" in December).
- Adjust images and descriptions to match seasonal demand.
- Run limited-time promotions during high-traffic periods (e.g., Black Friday).
- Ensure inventory levels are sufficient for seasonal peaks.
