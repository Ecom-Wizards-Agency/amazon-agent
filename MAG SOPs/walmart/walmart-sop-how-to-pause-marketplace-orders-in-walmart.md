# Walmart SOP: How to Pause Marketplace Orders in Walmart

## **How to Pause Marketplace Orders in Walmart Seller Center**

**Who is this for?**

This guide is for **Amazon Specialists** working with **Walmart accounts**. It shows you how to pause or adjust your sales and order fulfillment on Walmart Marketplace, especially during breaks, vacations, or operational issues.

**Objective:**

This SOP helps **sellers** temporarily pause sales and order processing in Walmart Seller Center. It’s useful when you need a break, have an operational outage, or are unavailable for a while but want to manage orders efficiently.

**How to Pause Sales & Order Operations (3 Methods)**

**I. Set Inventory to Zero**

Setting your inventory to zero will stop customers from placing new orders.

**Steps:**

1. Go to the **Catalog** page in [**Seller Center**](https://seller.walmart.com/home).

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wEKimage.png)

1. Update the inventory to **zero** for all your products.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/jR0image.png)

1. Click **Update** to save the changes.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-how-to-pause-marketplace-orders-in-walmart-image-2w5fxwvl.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wwhMABimage.png)

**Why this option?**

- It’s the fastest way to stop new orders without canceling existing ones or frustrating customers.
- You can quickly restart sales whenever you're ready.

**Important:**Even if inventory is set to zero, you’re still responsible for fulfilling any existing orders.

**II. Adjust Shipping for Planned Breaks**

If you’re taking a break or on vacation, you can adjust the shipping settings so orders are still placed but fulfillments are delayed.

**Steps:**

1. Click on the **Account Settings gear** and go to **Shipping info**.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-how-to-pause-marketplace-orders-in-walmart-image-0bhc9pdb.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/3ZVimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-how-to-pause-marketplace-orders-in-walmart-image-kfj6g3n9.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/xlcimage.png)

1. Find the **Additional Days Off** section and select the dates you won’t process orders.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-how-to-pause-marketplace-orders-in-walmart-image-v36st931.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/8koimage.png)

1. Orders will be placed but their fulfillment dates will be pushed back.

**Why this option?**

- Perfect for planned breaks where you want to keep receiving orders but can’t fulfill them right away.
- Not ideal for unplanned or long-term disruptions.

**III. Temporary Account Deactivation**

If you need a full break and want to stop all operations temporarily, you can deactivate your account.

**Steps:**

1. Click the **Help** button in the **Seller Center** menu.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-how-to-pause-marketplace-orders-in-walmart-image-kkvrecb9.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/SuBimage.png)

1. Contact **Walmart Support** and ask for a **temporary deactivation**.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-how-to-pause-marketplace-orders-in-walmart-image-2gbgdkbs.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/FaWimage.png)

**Why this option?**

- This is for extreme cases when you need a complete pause.
- You’ll need to contact Support to reactivate your account when you're ready to return.

FAQs

**1. Will My Seller Performance Metrics Be Affected?**

- If you cancel orders during a disruption, it may impact your performance metrics.
- To prevent penalties, contact **Walmart Support** via the **Help** button in **Seller Center**. If approved, any canceled orders won’t affect your metrics.

**2. Can I Reactivate My Account After Temporary Deactivation?**

- Yes, after you’ve temporarily deactivated your account, you can contact **Walmart Support** again to reactivate it once you’re ready to resume operations.

**3. Can I Use These Methods for Long-Term Breaks or Operational Issues?**

- Setting inventory to zero or adjusting shipping options is good for short-term pauses (like a vacation or brief operational pause).
- For longer-term pauses, consider requesting a temporary account deactivation.

**4. Will My Customers Know That I’ve Paused Operations?**

- If you adjust shipping settings, your customers can still place orders, but fulfillment times will be delayed. They won’t be notified of your break, but they will see adjusted delivery dates.
- If you set inventory to zero, customers won’t be able to place new orders, but existing orders will still need to be fulfilled.

**5. How Quickly Will My Changes Take Effect?**

- Changes to your inventory (setting it to zero) take effect as soon as the feed is processed.
- Adjusting shipping dates for planned breaks will take effect once the settings are updated in **Seller Center**.
