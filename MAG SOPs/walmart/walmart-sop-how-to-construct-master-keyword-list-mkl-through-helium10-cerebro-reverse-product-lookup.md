## **How To Construct Master Keyword List (MKL) Through Helium10 Cerebro Reverse Product Lookup**

**Who is this for:**

Individuals who want to construct titles, key features, and descriptions with high-ranking keywords for their Walmart items.

**Objectives:**

- To identify the top 10 competitors on Walmart *(only applies in scenarios when the full list of competitors is not provided by the client; the specialists will verify the top competitors if they have been provided)*;
- To help build product listings by constructing the Master Keyword List (MKL) that is generated through Helium10 Cerebro and;
- To ultimately increase the product listing’s visibility and revenue on Walmart.

FAQs

1. How do I ensure I select the most relevant competitors for my product?

- To select relevant competitors, ensure that the products share similar features, pricing, and target audience. For example, if you're selling stemless wine glasses, competitors should sell similar drinkware or stemless products. Consider product size, material, price range, and brand when filtering out irrelevant competitors. You can also use Walmart's product categories to narrow down competitors with similar products.

2. Why is the MAG Score formula applied, and how does it help?

- The MAG Score formula helps identify high-performing keywords by counting how many of the competitors' listings rank within the top 30 positions for a particular keyword. This is important because it helps prioritize keywords with strong visibility and relevance. Higher MAG scores indicate that a keyword is performing well across many competitors, making it a good candidate for optimization.

3. What criteria should I use to select the top 10 competitors?

- To select the top 10 competitors, focus on products that closely match your item in terms of:

  - Category: The product must belong to the same or similar category (e.g., drinkware, home decor).
  - Revenue: Competitors with good sales and a strong revenue history should be prioritized.
  - Customer Reviews: Competitors with high customer ratings and reviews indicate trust and sales success.
  - Brand Reputation: Look for well-established brands that are likely to have a strong presence in search results.

4. How do I filter irrelevant products or competitors?

- Use filters like Walmart Revenue (sort from highest to lowest) and Review Count (focus on competitors with a high number of reviews) to ensure that the competitors you are reviewing have a successful track record. Additionally, use Walmart’s category filters to exclude items that are not similar to your product. Removing sponsored listings and low-performing competitors ensures your list remains relevant.

5. What should I do with duplicate keywords in the Master Keyword List (MKL)?

- When working with the MKL, remove duplicate keywords from the list, especially when they appear multiple times across different competitor listings. The goal is to keep the most effective keyword variations. You can use tools like Google Sheets or Excel to highlight duplicates automatically using functions like COUNTIF to make the process easier.

6. How should I manage multiple versions of the same keyword (e.g., singular vs. plural)?

- Group variations of the same keyword together (e.g., "stemless wine glass" and "stemless wine glasses"). In your MKL, include both the singular and plural forms if they have significant search volume, as well as long-tail variations. This helps cover all potential search queries while avoiding redundancy.

7. How do I store and organize the final Master Keyword List (MKL)?

- After compiling the MKL, store it in a cloud-based document (e.g., Google Sheets or Excel) for easy access and updates. Use separate sheets to organize keywords by type (e.g., exact match, broad match, and phrase match). You can also create separate columns for search volume and MAG scores to prioritize which keywords to use first.

8. How can I integrate the MKL into my Walmart product listings?

- Once you have your final MKL, use the relevant keywords in your product titles, key features, descriptions, and backend search terms to optimize your listings for search visibility. Make sure that the keywords used in your product copy are naturally integrated and do not result in keyword stuffing.

9. How do I handle keyword competition during optimization?

- When optimizing, focus on targeting low-competition keywords that still have substantial search volume. Use long-tail keywords for niche targeting and consider exact match for highly relevant phrases. Use broad match keywords to capture a wider audience and test performance over time.
