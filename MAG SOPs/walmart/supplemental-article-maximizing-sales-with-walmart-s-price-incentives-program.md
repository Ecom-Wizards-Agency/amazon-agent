## **Maximizing Sales with Walmart’s Price Incentives Program**

The **Price Incentives Program** is designed to help you improve sales by ensuring that items are priced competitively on Walmart Marketplace. It provides two types of incentives: **Walmart-funded adjustments** and **Reduced Referral Fees**. These incentives help make products more appealing to buyers, ultimately increasing chances of higher sales and visibility.

**Walmart-Funded Incentive**

With the Walmart-funded incentive, Walmart automatically lowers the price of eligible items on [Walmart.com](http://walmart.com) to make them more competitive. Walmart covers the difference between the original price and the adjusted price, so you won’t lose out financially. This price adjustment ensures that your product remains competitive in the marketplace, and the incentive is applied automatically to your orders.

Here’s a quick breakdown:

1. Walmart adjusts your item’s price to be more competitive.
2. Walmart covers the price difference between your original price and the new, adjusted price.
3. The incentive is automatically applied to your orders.

  1. ***Example****:*

    1. If your item is priced at $25, but Walmart reduces it to $22 for competitiveness, Walmart will pay the $3 difference, so your product is now being offered at the lower price of $22.

**Program Enrollment**

Eligible items are automatically enrolled in the program, but you can choose to opt out if you prefer. You can decide to exclude specific items or even turn off Walmart-funded incentives entirely. For more details on opting in or out, refer to the [**Walmart SOP: How to Enroll in Walmart-funded Incentives Program**](https://myamazonguy.document360.io/docs/walmart-sop-how-to-enroll-in-walmart-funded-incentives-program-2).

**Item Enrollment**

You have the option to manage which of your items are enrolled in the program. Whether you want to include all items or only specific ones, you can enroll products individually or in bulk, refer to [**Walmart SOP: Managing Item Enrollment in Walmart-fulfilled Incentives Program**](https://myamazonguy.document360.io/docs/walmart-sop-managing-item-enrollment-in-walmart-fulfilled-incentives-program-2)**.**

***Note:*** The incentive amount can vary with each sale and is subject to a maximum cap. If the incentive reaches that cap, the item price will return to your last submitted price, and no further incentive will be applied. You can view the incentive amounts in your **Payment Report**.

**Reduced Referral Fee**

This incentive helps by lowering the referral fee for items that are priced at or below Walmart’s suggested target price. If the price exceeds the target or the item is unenrolled, the standard referral fee will apply instead.

Here’s how it works:

1. Keep your item’s price at or below the target price.
2. Walmart applies a reduced referral fee.
3. If the price goes above the target, the standard referral fee will apply.

  - ***Example****:*

    - If the target price for an item is $30 and you price it at $28, you’ll qualify for the reduced referral fee. However, if the price goes up to $32, the standard fee will be applied.

**Item Enrollment**

To take advantage of the Reduced Referral Fee, you must enroll each eligible item. The enrollment will have a specific expiration date, after which the standard referral fee will apply to the item. For more details, see the [**Reduced Referral Fee incentives: Manage item enrollment**](https://marketplacelearn.walmart.com/guides/Growth%20opportunities/Price%20Incentives/reduced-referral-fee-incentives-manage-item-enrollment) guide.

FAQs

**What’s the Incentives Report?**

- The **Incentives Report** gives you a comprehensive view of your active and eligible price incentives, including both Walmart-funded adjustments and Reduced Referral Fee recommendations. You can easily generate and download an Excel report to see all your current incentives in one place.

**Does turning off Walmart-funded incentives also opt me out of Referral Fee incentives?**

- No, disabling Walmart-funded incentives won’t affect your participation in Reduced Referral Fee incentives. You can manage both types independently, so you’re free to choose which ones you want to use.

**Can I enroll and unenroll from price incentives using APIs?**

- Yes, you can manage your participation in the Price Incentives Program through APIs, making it easy to automate the process.

**What happens if I add an incentivized item to a Repricer strategy?**

- If you add an item that’s receiving price incentives to a **Repricer strategy**, the price incentives will stop applying. The Repricer will adjust the item’s price according to the rules you’ve set, and the item will automatically be unenrolled from the price incentive program.

**Why is the Price Report taking so long to generate?**

- The Price Report can take up to four hours to generate, though it typically appears much sooner. If you find it's taking longer than expected, you can refresh the page to check on its status.

**Why is the Referral Fee Incentives file taking so long to generate?**

- Referral Fee Incentives files can take up to 15 minutes to generate. If it’s taking longer, feel free to refresh the page to check if it’s ready for download.

**If I apply promotions or discounts to an item receiving incentives, will the item be unenrolled from the program?**

- As long as the promotional price is at or below the target price, you’ll still qualify for the incentives. If the price exceeds the target, the item will be unenrolled from the program.
