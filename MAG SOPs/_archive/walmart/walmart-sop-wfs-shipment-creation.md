# Walmart SOP: WFS Shipment Creation

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/walmart/page/walmart-sop-wfs-shipment-creation

## **WFS Shipment Creation**

**Who is this for?**

The responsibility for Walmart's shipment of inventory creation lies within its supply chain management and logistics teams.

**Objective:**

The primary objective is to fulfill customer demand by creating accurate and complete shipment orders. This includes capturing the right products, quantities, and any special handling instructions to meet the specific needs of each store or destination.

> Important Update – Alternative Fulfillment via Amazon MCF
>
> Walmart is officially acknowledging the MCF partnership with Amazon and is allowing UNBRANDED fulfillments through MCF using non-Amazon carriers. Additionally, the 5% surcharge for using non-Amazon carriers is waived through January 14, 2026.

In this SOP, you will learn how to create an Inbound Order, Prepare the Shipment, and Generate the Carrier and Receiving Labels.

**How to Create an Inbound Order**

1. Log in to Walmart seller central account. Go to [https://seller.walmart.com/](https://seller.walmart.com/).

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-wfs-chipment-creation-image-1(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/ZZOimage.png)

1. Look for WFS from the menu and click on Inventory.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-12-10%20033329.png)

1. Fill out the Ship from address, Total units, Master case setup, Unit per master Case, and Pack and Label.

- **Ship from Address** - Confirm with the client. Add the Ship from address.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-wfs-chipment-creation-image-5(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/uEIimage.png)

- **Expected Delivery Date** - Provide an expected date of delivery

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/walmart-sop-wfs-chipment-creation-image-6(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/IXJimage.png)

- **Total units** - Total sellable units for the item across all master cases.
- **Master case setup** - How you’re packing the outermost box. Each box may have a single sellable unit, or it may have an inner case pack with multiple sellable units packed together.
- **Units per master case** - Number of sellable units inside the outermost box. For individual packaging, it’s automatically 1. For this example, we will send 10 cases with 11 items each.

FAQs

1. What should I do if the "Ship From Address" is incorrect or missing?

- If the "Ship From Address" is incorrect or missing, contact the relevant team or client to confirm the correct address before proceeding. If there’s a delay in obtaining the correct address, it could affect shipment timelines and delivery schedules, so it's best to verify this information early.

2. How do I handle multiple variations or different packaging formats for the same product in the Master Case Setup?

- If you have multiple variations or packaging formats for the same product, be sure to select the appropriate Master Case setup option for each variation. If you’re unsure, refer to the product’s packaging guidelines or consult your logistics team to determine how to pack them properly in master cases. If there’s confusion about what counts as a “Master Case,” consider providing clear documentation to avoid discrepancies.

3. How do I decide between using WFS or other carriers?

- The decision to use WFS or other carriers depends on several factors, including shipping speed, cost, and product type. WFS generally offers competitive rates and faster processing times for Walmart Marketplace sellers, while other carriers might be preferable for specific needs, such as specialized delivery services or lower cost options. You may want to compare shipping costs from both options to determine the best solution for your business.

4. How should I handle special shipping instructions (e.g., fragile or temperature-sensitive products)?

- Special handling instructions can be added during the shipment creation process. For fragile or temperature-sensitive items, ensure you specify these requirements in the "Ship From" or "Shipment Details" section. If needed, consult with your logistics provider or WFS to ensure the right precautions are taken.

5. How do I update the shipment quantity in case of discrepancies or shortages?

- If there’s a discrepancy or shortage in shipment quantity, you can update the shipment quantity before finalizing the order. If you’ve already submitted the order and discovered discrepancies later, you can adjust quantities by contacting Walmart Seller Support or through the "Set up Shipment" page. Always keep track of inventory and communicate with your team to prevent issues.

6. Can I revise or cancel an inbound order once it's created?

- Once an inbound order is created, you may have limited ability to revise or cancel it. If you need to make changes, go to the "Manage Inbound Orders" section and check if the option to cancel or revise is available. For any urgent changes, you can reach out to Walmart Seller Support for assistance.

7. How should I handle returns or adjustments after the shipment has been processed?

- Returns or adjustments can be managed by logging into your Walmart Seller Central account and navigating to the Returns section. Follow the prompts to issue returns or adjustments as needed. If the issue arises after shipment processing, you may need to contact Walmart Seller Support to initiate the return or adjustment process.

8. How can I track the shipment after it has been dispatched?

- After the shipment is dispatched, go to the "Activity Feed" in your Walmart Seller Central account to view the tracking number associated with the shipment. You can then use the carrier's website to monitor the progress of the shipment. Alternatively, you can also check the "Shipment Details" section for up-to-date information on the status of your shipment.
