## **Ticketing Walmart Support for Content and Image Update (Single Listing)**

**Who is this for?** For various teams or individuals responsible for updating the content on the Walmart platforms.

**Objective:**To evaluate and improve the visibility and performance of a product listing on Walmart. To Identify any areas for improvement and implement changes if necessary.

To file a ticket to Walmart for updating the Title, Description, Key Features, Images, etc. First, prepare any proof of UPC.

- Link to Manufacturer's website for a specific item
- Image of the product with the product’s title and UPC in clear view
- Receipt from GS-1 and screenshot from GS-1 showing that you are the owner of the GTIN
- Another reputable retail website that shows matching UPC or Model Number
- Most recent feed ID.

FAQs

1. Why do I need to provide proof of UPC for content and image updates?

Providing proof of UPC is necessary to validate the authenticity of the product and verify the correctness of the product data. It ensures that the product you are selling is linked to the correct catalog entry, avoiding duplicate or incorrect listings. Proof of UPC can include:

- A manufacturer's website showing the UPC.
- An image of the product with the UPC clearly visible.
- GS1 receipt or screenshot confirming ownership of the GTIN.

2. What are some common issues that might cause content and image update errors?

Some common content and image update errors include:

- Invalid UPCs or GTINs: Ensure that the UPC is accurate and corresponds to the correct product.
- Low-resolution images: Walmart requires images to meet a minimum size (1000x1000 pixels) for proper zooming and clarity.
- Missing key product features: If you’ve not included the correct details in the product’s description or bullet points, Walmart may flag your content.
- Category mismatches: Ensure the product is in the correct category, as incorrect categorization can lead to errors.

3. What does "incorrect content" refer to when submitting a case?

"Incorrect content" refers to any data or details that are wrong, incomplete, or do not match the product. This could include:

- Incorrect product title (e.g., missing key attributes like size or color).
- Missing key product features (e.g., missing a key selling point like "durable material").
- Incorrect image or missing image (e.g., low resolution or an unapproved image). Ensure that the content errors are clearly identified before submitting the case.

4. How do I monitor the updates I requested?

After submitting a case, follow these steps:

1. Go to the Activity Feed under the Product Catalog.
2. Search using the Feed ID to check if your content update has been successfully processed.
3. If there are still issues, download the error report and fix any remaining issues.
4. To monitor the status of your case directly, click on the Question Mark icon in the top-right corner of Walmart Seller Center, then select Your Cases to see the Case ID and Walmart’s reply.

5. How can I confirm that my content and image updates have been successfully applied?

After submitting your update case:

1. Wait 24-48 hours for the updates to be applied.
2. Check the product detail page (PDP) to verify if the updates have been reflected.
3. Use the Activity Feed to track the status of your case, ensuring the Feed ID shows as processed successfully.
4. If the updates are still not visible, follow up with Walmart support.

6. When should I escalate the case?

If the issue persists or is not resolved after multiple follow-ups (typically 3-5 business days), it may be time to escalate the case. Here’s what you can do:

1. Submit a new case with more specific details about the issue.
2. If necessary, mention that the issue has not been resolved and request escalation to a higher support level.
3. Always keep the Case ID for future references.

7. How long will it take for my content and image updates to be processed?

Content and image updates typically take 24-48 hours to process and appear on your Walmart listing. If updates are not visible within this timeframe, check the Activity Feed for processing issues. If there’s no progress, reach out to Walmart support for assistance.

8. How often should I follow up on an unresolved issue?

You should follow up on an unresolved issue after 3-5 business days if no response has been received. If you still do not see the update on the product listing or in the Activity Feed, it's important to escalate the issue by submitting a new support case.
