Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/walmart/page/walmart-sop-wfs-inventory-report

## **WFS Inventory Report**

**Who is this for :** Walmart specialists or the seller themselves are responsible for generating the WFS restock report.

**Objective :** To generate a comprehensive restock inventory report that provides the necessary information to make informed decisions about replenishing inventory levels.

Instructions:

1. Log in to Walmart Seller Central account. Go to https://seller.walmart.com/

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1694636643373.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/tNgimage.png)

2. Access the WFS Inventory section and click the Download tab.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1694636741860.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/XUHimage.png)

3. Choose All items and click the Download button.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1694636788853.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Xocimage.png)

FAQs

1. What types of inventory reports are available in Walmart Seller Central?

- The WFS restock report is typically used to monitor and manage inventory levels. However, you may also encounter other reports depending on your account and needs, such as inventory performance reports and sales reports. For comprehensive restocking, ensure you are downloading the restock inventory report specifically.

2. Can I filter the WFS inventory report to show specific products or categories?

- Yes, you can filter the data within the WFS inventory report. Depending on your needs, you can narrow the report by selecting specific product categories, timeframes, or SKU types to focus on particular products or inventory levels.

3. How do I interpret the key data points in the WFS inventory report?

- Here’s a breakdown of the key data columns:

  - Daily Sales: Shows the average units sold daily. It helps to gauge how quickly your products are selling.
  - Daily Units Sold: Displays the number of units sold on a given day.
  - Available to Sell: The amount of inventory you currently have available for customers to purchase.
  - Reservations: Represents the units of inventory that have been reserved (typically by customers or for shipping).
  - Inbound Units: The number of units currently on their way to the warehouse from suppliers.
  - Days of Supply: How many days your current stock will last at the current sales rate.
  - Out-of-Stock Date: The projected date when the item is expected to go out of stock based on current sales trends.
  - Suggested Units to Send: A recommendation for the number of units you should send to the warehouse to maintain healthy stock levels.

4. What should I do if the WFS inventory report fails to download or shows incomplete data?

- If you experience issues such as failed downloads or incomplete data:

  - Check your internet connection and try downloading the report again.
  - Clear your browser cache or try accessing the report using a different browser.
  - If problems persist, contact Walmart support to investigate any technical issues with the report or discrepancies in the data.

5. What do I do if the WFS inventory report shows discrepancies between the numbers and actual stock?

- If the report does not match your physical stock levels, take the following steps:

  - Verify the data: Compare the "Available to Sell" and "Reservations" data against your actual inventory.
  - Check for shipping delays: If there are Inbound Units that have not been processed, it might explain discrepancies.
  - Reconcile with other reports: Cross-reference the WFS inventory report with other inventory or sales reports to confirm accuracy.
  - Contact Walmart support: If discrepancies persist, reach out to Walmart’s support team for clarification.

6. How can I use the "Suggested Units to Send" to plan my inventory restocks?

- The Suggested Units to Send metric provides a recommendation based on your sales trends and current inventory levels. To effectively use this:

  - Review your Daily Sales and Days of Supply to understand how quickly you’re selling and how much inventory you need to keep in stock.
  - Use the Suggested Units to Send as a guideline for restocking before you run low on stock and experience out-of-stock issues.

7. What does "Days of Supply" mean and how can I use it to manage my inventory?

- Days of Supply is the number of days your current stock will last based on your sales velocity. For example:

  - If your Daily Sales is 100 units and you have 500 units available to sell, your Days of Supply would be 5 days (500 units / 100 units per day).
  - Use this metric to forecast when to replenish your stock. If your Days of Supply is low, it may be time to reorder more inventory.

8. How often should I generate the WFS inventory report to monitor stock levels?

- Generate the WFS inventory report at least weekly to keep track of inventory levels and make timely restock decisions. For faster-moving items, it may be beneficial to check the report more frequently, such as bi-weekly or after large sales periods.

9. What should I do if the GMV (Gross Merchandise Value) is significantly lower than expected?

- If GMV is lower than expected, consider investigating the Sales Data to identify any trends or issues:

  - Check if the Reservations are higher than available units, indicating your stock may be reserved but not yet available for sale.
  - Review the Out-of-Stock Date to see if the product is at risk of running out of stock soon.
  - Ensure that your Ad Spend is aligned with product visibility efforts if you are also running ads.

10. Can I download the WFS inventory report for multiple dates or is it restricted to one date range?

- You can customize the date range for the WFS inventory report, but it typically works best for custom date ranges rather than multiple preset options. To analyze trends, download the report regularly and compare data from different weeks or months.
