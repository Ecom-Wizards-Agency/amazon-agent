## **Walmart Keyword Research**

Who is this for? For various teams or individuals who are responsible for optimizing ad campaigns to improve their performance, which includes identifying new keywords to target.

Objective: To expand the reach of your ads by identifying and incorporating new search terms that are relevant to your products.

FAQs

1. How do I prioritize which keywords to target for my campaigns?

When prioritizing keywords, consider the following:

- Search Volume: Target keywords with higher traffic for broader reach, but ensure relevance to your product.
- Relevancy: Keywords should closely align with the product you are advertising. High relevancy ensures the keywords accurately match search intent.
- Conversion Rates: Focus on keywords that lead to actual sales rather than just impressions. Use historical data to guide this decision.

2. What do the metrics in the Keyword Analytics Dashboard mean?

- Normalized Query: This is the search term used by customers that is most relevant to your product. It's normalized to account for varying search patterns across different time periods.
- Item Keyword Frequency: This indicates how often your product is shown for a particular keyword. A high frequency means it’s relevant to that search term.
- Traffic Keyword Frequency: This represents how frequently a specific keyword appears in customer searches. High traffic indicates high demand for that term.

3. How do I interpret the ‘Traffic Keyword Frequency’ and ‘Item Keyword Frequency’ metrics?

- Traffic Keyword Frequency helps identify high-demand keywords but doesn’t necessarily mean they’ll convert. Consider both search volume and relevancy.
- Item Keyword Frequency shows how often your product is being shown for a keyword. The higher the frequency, the better your keyword fits your product. Focus on improving this frequency for relevant keywords.

4. How do I monitor the performance of keywords after adding them to my campaign?

After adding keywords, track their performance regularly by checking:

- Impressions and Click-Through Rates (CTR) to gauge visibility and interest.
- Conversion rates to measure how well keywords turn clicks into sales.
- A/B testing to compare the performance of different keywords within the same ad group.

5. What is the best way to adjust Ad Groups when adding new keywords?

When adjusting Ad Group Names and adding new keywords:

- Keep the ad group focused by adding only relevant keywords.
- Organize keywords based on themes (e.g., product features, customer needs).
- Monitor performance regularly to ensure the ad group is targeting the right audience and delivering results.

6. How does "Units Sold (M2)" correlate with keyword performance?

Filtering by Units Sold (M2) helps identify products that are performing well in terms of sales volume. This is useful when you want to prioritize keywords for items that have demonstrated strong sales, ensuring you’re targeting high-performing products. Higher units sold typically indicate higher demand and successful campaigns.

7. What should I do if the data exported from Walmart is incomplete or erroneous?

If there’s an error in the data or missing information, try the following:

- Re-export the report after verifying the date range and settings.
- Check for filtering issues (e.g., incorrectly grouped by daily vs. cumulative).
- If the issue persists, contact Walmart support for clarification or assistance in resolving the error.

8. How do I evaluate the effectiveness of newly added keywords in my campaigns?

To evaluate new keywords:

- Track the keyword’s performance via Walmart’s reporting tools (click-through rates, impressions, conversions).
- Compare before and after metrics (e.g., sales volume, search rankings).
- Adjust bids or remove poorly performing keywords and experiment with new ones.

9. Why is it important to download the ‘Item Performance Report’ and group by Cumulative?

Downloading the Item Performance Report and grouping by Cumulative ensures that you capture all data over the selected attribution window. This approach provides a complete view of performance across all interactions, unlike daily grouping, which could be more volatile and less comprehensive.

10. How often should I check the Activity Feed for updated Feed IDs after uploading keywords?

Check the Activity Feed at least every 24 hours to monitor keyword uploads and ensure the process is complete. Feed IDs will help you track the status of each keyword upload, and if there are errors, the report will provide actionable insights to resolve them.
