# Walmart SOP: Walmart Sales Report

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/walmart/page/walmart-sop-walmart-sales-report

## **Walmart Sales Report Creation**

Who is this for?: Individuals who are responsible for creating and analyzing Walmart sales reports.

Objective: To analyze and summarize the company's sales performance over a given period. The report would provide valuable insights into the company's revenue, profit, customer trends, and other key metrics that would help evaluate the effectiveness of the company's sales strategies and identify areas for improvement.

**Data needed are:**

Revenue (GMV)

Commission

GMV after Commission

Ad Spend

Ad Revenue

Ad ACOS

True ACOS

FAQs

1. How do I access the Walmart Connect Ad Center for Ad Spend and Ad Revenue data?

To access the Walmart Connect Ad Center:

- Log in to your Walmart Seller Central account.
- Navigate to Advertising in the main menu and select Sponsored Search.
- Scroll down to the bottom of the page, and under More Resources, click Walmart Connect Ad Center or visit [Walmart Connect Ad Center.](https://advertising.walmart.com/view/home)
- From there, you can select your desired date range and view your Ad Spend and Ad Revenue under the All Campaigns Performance Summary dashboard.

2. How can I adjust the report timeframe for the sales data in Walmart Seller Central?

To adjust the report timeframe for sales data:

- From the Item Sales Summary section, select Custom Range from the Date filter.
- Pick the desired date range for your sales report (e.g., weekly, monthly, or any custom period).
- Keep in mind that sales reports are typically generated weekly, so ensure the correct custom date range is selected for accurate data.

3. Can I apply advanced filters to segment my report further?

Yes, you can use advanced filters to segment your report further:

- Under the Item Sales Summary, you can filter by Programs, Superdepartments, and Brands.
- To access these filters, click Filters under the Item Sales Summary section and select your preferred options.
- You can further refine your report by selecting specific ad groups, campaign types, or product categories if necessary for in-depth analysis.

4. How do I analyze the key metrics like GMV, GMV after Commission, and True ACOS?

To analyze key metrics:

- GMV (Gross Merchandise Value): This shows the total sales value before any commission deductions.
- GMV - Commission: This represents the total sales value after the commission is subtracted. The difference between GMV and GMV - Commission is the commission.
- Ad ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sale): This metric indicates how much was spent on ads relative to the revenue generated by ads. Lower ACOS means more efficient ad spending.
- True ACOS: This metric includes total revenue (GMV) and represents how much of the GMV was spent on ads. It gives a broader view of ad efficiency across your entire sales performance.

5. How do I format my report for stakeholders or clients?

To present your report effectively:

- Structure the Report: Create clear sections for each key metric: GMV, Commission, Ad Spend, Ad Revenue, Ad ACOS, and True ACOS.
- Highlight Key Insights: Use graphs or tables to visualize trends over time, showing how ad spend correlates with revenue and profit.
- Provide Context: Include a brief summary at the top of the report to explain the findings and any anomalies.
- Use Clean Formatting: Use bold and clear headings, and organize the data in an easy-to-read manner (e.g., use Excel pivot tables or charts).

6. What should I do if I notice discrepancies or errors in the sales report?

If discrepancies or errors appear:

- Check Filters and Data Segmentation: Ensure that the date range and filters are set correctly in your report.
- Compare with Previous Reports: Look at previous reports to identify any unusual patterns or data discrepancies.
- Verify Ad Spend and Revenue Data: Cross-check the data in the Walmart Connect Ad Center to make sure it's accurate.
- Seek Help from Support: If the discrepancies persist, contact Walmart Seller Support for clarification or to resolve any data issues.

7. How can I ensure that the GMV and GMV - Commission columns are displayed in the report?

To display GMV and GMV - Commission columns:

- Go to the Item Sales Report page.
- Click Configure to customize the columns shown in the report.
- Select GMV and GMV - Commission from the list of available columns.
- Save the configuration to ensure these columns are included in your report.

8. How do I calculate the GMV after Commission and Commission from the report data?

To calculate:

- GMV: This is the total revenue before commission is applied.
- GMV after Commission: This is the total revenue after the commission has been deducted.
- Commission: Subtract GMV after Commission from GMV. Example:

  - GMV = $1,141.78
  - GMV after Commission = $966.85
  - Commission = GMV - GMV after Commission = $1,141.78 - $966.85 = $174.93

9. How do I analyze the effectiveness of my ad spend using Ad ACOS and True ACOS?

To evaluate your ad spend:

- Ad ACOS tells you how much you spent on ads for the revenue directly generated by those ads. A lower ACOS is preferable, indicating more efficient ad spending.
- True ACOS reflects the total sales (GMV) and shows the overall effectiveness of your ad spend, not just the sales driven by the ads themselves. It’s a more comprehensive metric that includes all sales.
