## **Ticketing Walmart Support for Content and Image Update (Multiple Listings)**

**Who is this for :** For various teams or individuals responsible for updating the content on the Walmart platforms.

**Objective :** To evaluate and improve the visibility and performance of a product listing on Walmart. To Identify any areas for improvement and implement changes for multiple listings if necessary.

**To file a ticket to Walmart for updating the Title, Description, Key Features, Images, etc.**

**First, prepare any proof of UPC.**

- Link to Manufacturer's website for a specific item
- Image of the product with the product’s title and UPC in clear view
- Receipt from GS-1 and screenshot from GS-1 showing that you are the owner of the GTIN
- Another reputable retail website that shows matching UPC or Model Number
- Most recent feed ID.

FAQs

1. When should I request a content update for my Walmart listings?

Request a content update if:

- The title, description, or key features contain incorrect information.
- The images are outdated, incorrect, or low quality.
- The UPC or GTIN is not displaying correctly or needs correction.
- The listing violates Walmart’s content guidelines and needs optimization.

Minor optimizations (e.g., keyword improvements) should be tested with a full item spec sheet update instead of submitting a ticket.

2. What are Walmart’s content update guidelines?

- Title: Maximum 75 characters, avoid promotional words (e.g., "Best," "Sale").
- Images: Must be at least 2000x2000 pixels, no watermarks or logos.
- Key Features: Bullet points should be concise and highlight key selling points.
- Descriptions: Should be clear, keyword-rich, and free of promotional language.

Always follow Walmart’s Listing Quality Guidelines to prevent rejections.

3. How do I update content for a single item instead of multiple listings?

1. Go to "Items & Inventory" > "Manage Items".
2. Find the product and click Edit Item.
3. If the fields are editable, update them and save changes.
4. If not editable, submit a single-item content update request through Partner Support.

4. What should I do if Walmart rejects my content update request?

- Verify the proof of UPC or manufacturer details before resubmitting.
- Ensure the new content follows Walmart’s guidelines to prevent rejection.
- Provide additional supporting documentation, such as competitor listings with the correct content.
- If rejected multiple times, escalate the case to Walmart Partner Support.

5. How do I retrieve the most recent feed ID?

1. Log in to Walmart Seller Center.
2. Go to "Activity Feed" under "Product Catalog".
3. Locate the most recent successful feed upload.
4. Copy the Feed ID and include it in your update request.

6. How long does Walmart take to process content updates?

- Most updates are processed within 24-72 hours, but complex cases may take up to 7 business days.
- Bulk content updates for multiple SKUs may take longer.

7. Can I update listing content without opening a support case?

Yes! You can update content using a Full Item Spec Sheet:

1. Download the Full Item Spec Report from Walmart Seller Center.
2. Edit the necessary fields (title, description, images, etc.).
3. Re-upload the updated file under "Update Items in Bulk".

This method is faster than submitting a support ticket.

8. How do I confirm that my content update was successful?

- Check the product page on Walmart.com to see if changes are visible.
- Go to "Manage Items" and verify the updated content.
- Download an Item Report from Seller Center and check the updated fields.

9. What should I do if Walmart support does not resolve my case?

- Reply to the case with additional proof (e.g., manufacturer website screenshots).
- If there is no response within 5 business days, escalate the case via Walmart Partner Support Chat or Phone.
- If the case is still unresolved, submit a new case with higher priority and reference the previous case ID.

10. What are the best practices for optimizing Walmart listing content?

- Use high-quality images with white backgrounds and clear product details.
- Write keyword-rich titles that include brand, product type, and key attributes.
- Use clear, structured bullet points in key features.
- Ensure the description is detailed but free from exaggerated claims or promotional language.

Following these best practices improves search visibility and conversions on Walmart.
