### Generating a Search Query Report for Walmart PPC Optimization

**Who is this for?**

This SOP is intended for **PPC Specialists** working on **Walmart accounts**. The purpose of this guide is to help PPC specialists identify high-performing search queries to improve sponsored search strategies and optimize product listings.

**Objective**:

This SOP provides step-by-step instructions for generating and analyzing the **Search Query Report** within Walmart’s Seller Center. This report helps identify growth opportunities by highlighting the search terms that customers use to find products similar to yours, allowing you to optimize PPC campaigns and product listings.

**Overview**

The **Search Query Report** in Walmart’s **Search Insights** provides valuable metrics to help you assess how well your products are performing for specific customer search queries. By analyzing the data, you can adjust PPC campaigns, optimize product listings, and increase overall sales performance.

**Search Query Report Metrics**

The report will provide valuable metrics to help you optimize your Walmart PPC campaigns and product visibility. Key metrics include:

1. **Added to Cart Share**:

  - Percentage of added-to-cart actions your product received, compared to all other items shown in search results for that query.
2. **Click Share**:

  - Percentage of total clicks your product received, compared to all other products shown in search results for the query.
3. **Frequency Rank**:

  - Indicates the popularity of the query. For example, a frequency rank of 500 means the query is the 500th most popular in the selected time range.
4. **Impressions Share**:

  - Percentage of impressions your product received, compared to all other items shown for that query.
5. **Number of Competing Products**:

  - The number of products displayed in search results for the same query, helping you understand competition levels.
6. **Number One Clicked Product**:

  - The product that received the highest number of clicks after the query was searched.
7. **Query Score**:

  - This score ranks how well your product performs based on traffic and sales for that query. A lower score indicates higher traffic share for your product.

FAQs

1. **How is the Search Query Score Assigned?**

  - The Search Query Score is calculated based on your item’s performance for a particular query, measuring the traffic and sales generated compared to other products for that search.
2. **What Are the Benefits of Using the Search Query Report for PPC Campaigns?**

  - **Identify High-Value Queries**:

    - Use the report to find which search queries drive the most traffic and sales, helping to prioritize the best keywords for your campaigns.
  - **Optimize Campaign Targeting**:

    - Adjust your **Sponsored Search** targeting based on insights from top-performing queries.
  - **Improve Product Listing Visibility**:

    1. Optimize your product listings based on the keywords that drive the most impressions, clicks, and sales.
3. **Why Is There No Data for My Report Despite Having Sales?**

  - The report only includes data from search results on [Walmart.com](http://walmart.com) and the Walmart mobile app. It does not cover traffic from other product discovery experiences or external traffic sources.
4. **Why Is the Data Different from My Item Sales Performance?**

  - The Search Query Report focuses solely on search result data and excludes sales from other channels like direct traffic or personalized product experiences.
5. **Why Do I Have a High Search Query Score but Low Impression, Click, and ATC Share?**

  - The Search Query Score reflects the traffic and sales your product receives for a query, but it doesn’t account for the competition. The impression, click, and ATC share metrics show how well your product is performing compared to others.
