**Placement Inclusion**

The Placement Inclusion section will provide you with the option to promote your items on Search Carousel, Item Buy Box, and Item Carousel ad placements for the keywords you’re bidding on, in addition to the Search in-grid.

By default, the Search Carousel placement is toggled on for all newly created campaigns. The Search In-grid placement cannot be toggled off.

**Note:** The inclusion of additional placements is optional but is highly recommended to expand reach.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/LG5image.png)

**Placement Types**

Search In-grid

This is the default placement for Manual campaigns. When a shopper searches for a query and you are bidding on that keyword/match type, your item may be eligible to serve on the Sponsored Product In-grid placements within the search results

Search Carousel

When a shopper searches for a query and you are bidding on that keyword/match type, your item may be eligible to serve on the Sponsored Product Carousel placement below the search results.

Note: This placement is toggled on by default for all newly created campaigns, but can be toggled off at any time.

Item Buy Box & Item Carousel

When a shopper lands on an item page and you are bidding on a keyword/match type of one of the anchor item entities, your item may be eligible to serve on the Sponsored Products Buy Box and/or Carousel placement on the item page.

An anchor item is an item page/product detail page where you want your ads to appear. The following are considered anchor item entities:

- Item Title
- Item Brand
- Item Taxonomy

**Match-type definitions for Item Page placements**

- Exact Match - all the words in the Bidded keywords (or their normalized version) should be present in the same order and should be continuous (except for stop words or functional words). There cannot be any additional words in the entity.

- Age of Sage
- Home Decor
- Age of Sage White Sage Smudge Sticks 4" Long
- Candles & Home Fragrance
- Incense

- Phrase Match - all the words in the Bidded keywords (or their normalized version) can be in any order, however, all the words should be in one entity and should be continuous. There can be any additional words in the entity before and after the bidding keywords.

- Age of Sage Smudge Sticks
- White Sage Smudge Sticks Candles & Home Fragrance
- Candles Incense
- 4 Inches Long Incense

- Broad Match - all the words in the Bidded keywords (or their normalized version) can be present in any order and do not need to be continuous.

- Home Decoration
- Home Fragrance
- Household Cleansing
- Candles
- Incense

**How to use keywords and match types to target item placements**

**Example 1** - If you want to target shampoo item pages but are unsure of which item(s) to specifically target:

- Start by targeting item pages based on their taxonomy using broad or phase match. It could be any level of the hierarchy such as "beauty", "hair care", or "beauty hair care"
- Use the On-demand Keyword Performance Report to identify the item pages your ads appeared on and performed well. Narrow match-types and/or target specific item entities to specifically target those pages.

**Example 2** - If you know which item pages you want to target

- Use the item title of the targeted item page as the Bidded keyword with an exact match, such as "Age of Sage Everyday Clean Dandruff Shampoo, 33.8 fl oz."
- Alternatively, use "Equate Everyday" as the Bidded keyword with a phrase or broad match to target any/all items that contain the keyword in their item title such as

  - Age of Sage Everyday Clean Dandruff Shampoo, 23.7 fl oz.
  - Age of Sage Everyday Clean 2 in 1 Dandruff Shampoo & Conditioner, 13.5 fl oz.
  - Age of Sage 14.2 Fl. Oz. Everyday Clean Dandruff Shampoo
  - Age of Sage 2N1 Everyday Clean Dandruff Shampoo & Conditioner, 32.1fl oz.

**Example 3** - Target your item pages to ensure competitor ads are not appearing

- Use the item brand of your items as the Bidded keyword to target all the items pages under your brand, such as "equate" with exact match

- Your bidding strategy is determined at the Campaign level. For example, a Manual campaign will only have Keyword Bidding Ad Groups
- You will be able to enable/disable an Ad Group after the campaign has started

**Recommendations to set up your Ad Group(s) for success**

- Automatic campaign:

  - Cluster similar products together to enable you to track performance accordingly.
- Manual campaign:

  - Create Ad Groups for products with similar attributes and related keywords.
  - Include no more than 50-100 products per Ad Group
  - Organize groups by related keywords, brands, product categories, and top sellers, so all products can be found via search easily.
- Choose a naming convention that is easily relatable to items in the Ad Group
- Continuously check Ad Group performance reports to optimize campaigns

**Adding items to your Ad Group**

You can add items to your Ad Group in three different ways:

1. Upload a set of items in a CSV file
2. Search for items by item ID
3. Search for items by product name, keywords, or brand name

**Note:** Sponsored Products only allow you to advertise base item IDs. During the item setup process, one item is classified as a base item and any variations to that item are considered variants. If you don’t know what the base item ID is for an item(s), please reach out to us.

1. Upload a set of items in a CSV file

Before uploading your CSV file, we encourage you to download the CSV template by clicking on the Download CSV Template button.

Once you have downloaded the template, you will need to fill out the required fields below:

- Item ID - Input your base item ID
- CPC - Enter the CPC amount you wish to bid on the item
- Status - To enable an item, you can either enter "Enabled" or leave the field blank. If you wish to disable an item, enter "Disabled."

FAQs

1. How do I track the performance of my campaigns after they go live?

You can track the performance of your campaigns by:

- Monitoring Click-Through Rate (CTR): It helps measure the number of clicks your ad is receiving in relation to impressions. Higher CTR typically means your ads are relevant to the audience.
- Conversion Rate: Track how many clicks convert into actual sales. High conversion rates indicate your ads and keywords are effectively reaching potential buyers.
- Cost Per Click (CPC): Review your bid adjustments for each keyword. Make sure your CPC is competitive but still within your budget. For ongoing monitoring, use the Walmart reporting tools under the Campaign Performance section to evaluate impressions, clicks, conversions, and other key metrics.

2. What are the best practices for selecting keywords for my Walmart campaigns?

To effectively select keywords for your Walmart campaigns, consider:

- Relevance: Ensure that your keywords match the product's title, description, and category.
- Search Volume: Prioritize keywords with high search volume to increase visibility. You can use the Keyword Performance report to find popular search terms related to your products.
- Long-Tail Keywords: Incorporate specific long-tail keywords (e.g., “organic cotton baby blanket”) to target a more precise audience and reduce competition.
- Competitor Analysis: Look at competitors' top-performing keywords to identify untapped opportunities.

3. How can I optimize my Walmart ad campaigns after they are live?

Once your campaigns are live, follow these strategies to optimize:

- Adjust Bids: Continuously monitor the performance of your keywords. Increase bids for high-performing keywords and lower bids for underperforming ones.
- A/B Testing: Run experiments by testing different keywords, ad copy, or targeting strategies to determine what resonates best with your audience.
- Reallocate Budget: Shift your budget towards higher-performing campaigns or keywords. Use the Walmart analytics tools to identify which campaigns provide the best ROI.
- Pause Low-Performing Keywords: Regularly evaluate your campaigns and pause keywords that aren't driving traffic or conversions.

4. What should I do if I encounter errors during the campaign setup process?

If you face errors during setup:

- CSV Upload Errors: Double-check the file for missing columns, incorrect formatting, or invalid data. You can download an error report from the Activity Feed section for more details on the issue.
- Incorrect CPC Values: If your bids are not being accepted, ensure they follow Walmart's CPC limits (less than $20 and with up to two decimal places).
- Missing Items or Keywords: If items or keywords are not showing up correctly, verify that the Item ID is correct and corresponds to the base item. Re-upload the file after making necessary corrections.

5. How do I organize Ad Groups to ensure campaign success?

Best practices for Ad Group organization:

- Cluster Similar Products: Group products with similar attributes or keywords in the same ad group for better performance tracking.
- Limit Items per Ad Group: Keep the number of items in an Ad Group manageable. Ideally, include 50-100 products per group to avoid overcrowding and improve relevance.
- Use Descriptive Naming Conventions: Name your Ad Groups based on relevant product attributes (e.g., brand, category, or product feature) to easily track performance and make adjustments.
- Separate Manual and Automatic Campaigns: Create separate Ad Groups for manual and automatic campaigns to better manage bidding strategies and control over keyword targeting.

6. How do I manage keyword bids in manual campaigns?

In manual campaigns:

- Set Bids Based on Keyword Relevance: Adjust your CPC for highly relevant keywords to maximize visibility.
- Use Exact and Phrase Match: For greater control, use Exact Match for highly specific keywords and Phrase Match for broader targeting. This ensures your ads are shown to a highly relevant audience.
- Monitor Suggested Bids: Walmart will suggest bids based on market trends, but you can adjust your bid slightly higher to improve your chances of winning the auction, especially for high-priority keywords.

7. How can I ensure that my ads are shown on the right placements?

To ensure your ads show in the optimal placements:

- Enable Search Carousel and Buy Box Placements: These placements can drive high visibility for your product. The Search Carousel is toggled on by default for newly created campaigns, and it's recommended to keep it enabled.
- Match Keywords to Specific Items: Use exact match keywords for targeting specific items, or use broader match types for more general categories (e.g., targeting all shampoo products).
- Regularly Review Performance: Monitor the performance of your Search In-Grid, Search Carousel, and Item Buy Box placements to ensure you’re maximizing exposure in relevant sections of Walmart’s website.

8. What is the difference between Automatic and Manual campaigns in Walmart?

- Automatic Campaigns: Walmart automatically selects keywords for you based on your product listing and bidding strategy. This is ideal for newcomers who want a simpler setup but have less control over keyword targeting.
- Manual Campaigns: You choose the specific keywords and bidding strategies. Manual campaigns offer more control, allowing you to directly manage keywords, bids, and placements for better targeting and performance optimization.

9. How can I optimize my manual campaigns using placements?

To optimize your manual campaigns using placements:

- Use Additional Placements: Enable placements such as the Search Carousel and Item Buy Box for higher visibility.
- Monitor Performance by Placement: Regularly check which placements yield the highest click-through and conversion rates. Adjust bids accordingly for placements that drive the best results.
