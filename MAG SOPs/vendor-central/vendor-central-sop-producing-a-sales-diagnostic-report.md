## Producing a Sales Diagnostic Report

**Who is this for :** This is for vendors on Amazon who want to generate a Sales Diagnostic Report using the Retail Analytics Dashboard.

**Objective :** To empower vendors to analyze their sales data, identify top-performing ASINs, understand sales trends, and make informed decisions regarding inventory management, pricing strategies, and profitability.

**To access your Amazon Retail Analytics Dashboard, follow these steps:**

1. Log in to your Vendor Central Dashboard and click on the "Reports" tab.
2. Select "Retail Analytics" from the Drop Down Menu to view the overview of your Retail Analytics Dashboard.

3. From the left side menu, click on "Sales" to access your Sales Diagnostic Reports.

5. The Total View provides grand totals of the Ordered Revenue, Ordered Units, Shipped Revenue, Shipped COGS, Shipped Units, and Customer Returns for the selected period.

FAQs

**1. How often does the Sales Diagnostic Report update?**

- The Sales Diagnostic Report updates daily, but some metrics (such as returns and shipped revenue) may have a 24-48 hour delay. Check back periodically for the most accurate data.

**2. How far back can I access historical sales data?**

- Amazon allows vendors to retrieve sales data for up to two years. If you need older reports, you may need to download and store them periodically.

**3. What’s the difference between Ordered Revenue and Shipped Revenue?**

- Ordered Revenue reflects the total value of purchase orders Amazon placed for your products.
- Shipped Revenue represents the total value of products Amazon actually received and processed for sale.
- Discrepancies between the two may indicate order cancellations, delayed shipments, or fulfillment issues.

**4. What should I do if my report data is missing or incorrect?**

- If your Sales Diagnostic Report has missing or incorrect data:

  - Ensure the correct date range and filters are applied.
  - Check whether there’s a reporting delay (some data updates overnight).
  - Compare with other Vendor Central reports (e.g., Operational Performance Dashboard) to spot discrepancies.

    - If issues persist, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**5. How are customer returns factored into the Sales Diagnostic Report?**

- Customer returns are listed separately in the report and do not automatically deduct from Ordered or Shipped Revenue. However, you can analyze the Customer Returns metric alongside sales figures to identify potential product quality or fulfillment issues.

**6. How can I use the report for forecasting and strategic planning?**

- The report helps you:

  - Identify seasonal trends to adjust inventory levels.
  - Analyze top-selling ASINs to focus on high-performing products.
  - Track return rates to spot potential product quality or customer satisfaction issues.
  - Evaluate revenue per unit to refine pricing strategies.

**7. Can I automate or schedule report downloads?**

- Yes, you can set up scheduled report exports within Amazon Vendor Central. Go to Reports > Retail Analytics > Sales, customize your settings, and enable automatic downloads to receive reports at regular intervals.
