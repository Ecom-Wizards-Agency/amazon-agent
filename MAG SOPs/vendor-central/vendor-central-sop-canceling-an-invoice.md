# Vendor Central SOP: Canceling an Invoice

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-canceling-an-invoice

## Canceling an Invoice

**Who is this for:**

- This is intended for companies or individuals who use Vendor Central on Amazon.
- This SOP is relevant to users who are responsible for managing the Purchase Order cycle, submitting invoices, and ensuring accurate financial records on Vendor Central.

**Objective:**

- To guide users in canceling an invoice on Vendor Central when a duplicate invoice or shortage claim is identified.
- To provide step-by-step instructions on how to cancel an invoice through the "Invoices" page, accessible from the "Payments" tab on the top menu of the Vendor Central Dashboard.

- Sometimes, companies may accidentally create duplicate invoices on Vendor Central, either because multiple people are managing the Purchase Order cycle or because someone forgets they have already submitted an invoice for the same Purchase Order. If you find a shortage claim due to a duplicate invoice, you can cancel the invoice.

1. Access the “Invoices” page from the “Payments” tab on the top menu of your Vendor Central Dashboard.

FAQs

**1. What are the reasons I may need to cancel an invoice on Vendor Central?**

- In addition to duplicate invoices and shortage claims, there are other situations where you might need to cancel an invoice. These can include:

  - Incorrect amounts (e.g., invoicing for the wrong quantity or price)
  - Inaccurate shipping details (e.g., incorrect address or shipping method)
  - Product returns (if items are returned and you need to reverse an invoice)
  - Invoice errors (e.g., clerical mistakes such as misentered data or wrong PO numbers)
  - Customer disputes (if there is a dispute over an invoice that needs to be canceled)

**2. What happens after I cancel an invoice on Vendor Central?**

- After you cancel an invoice, Amazon will update the status in Vendor Central, and the invoice will be removed from your open invoices. If the cancellation is related to a duplicate invoice, you may need to resubmit the corrected invoice to reflect accurate financial data. If it's related to a shortage claim, Amazon may initiate a credit memo or provide further instructions. It's important to follow up with the payments or reconciliation process to ensure everything is reflected correctly in your financial records.

**3. What should I do if the 'Cancel Invoice' option is not available or I encounter an issue during the process?**

- If the “Cancel Invoice” option is not available or you're unable to cancel the invoice, there may be a few reasons for this:

  - The invoice may be in an approved or pending status and cannot be canceled.
  - The invoice might be part of an ongoing dispute or financial adjustment, and additional steps may be required.
  - In these cases, you can try reaching out to Amazon Vendor Support for assistance. Provide the invoice number and details of the issue, and they should help you through the cancellation process.

**4. How do I handle invoices that are still pending or in-process? Can I cancel them?**

- If an invoice is in a pending or in-process status, it may not yet be fully processed by Amazon. Generally, invoices in these statuses cannot be canceled directly through the “Invoices” page. However, you may need to contact Amazon Vendor Support to stop the processing or to make adjustments. Be sure to provide all relevant details, including the PO number, invoice number, and reason for cancellation, so they can assist you effectively.

**5. How can I avoid submitting duplicate invoices in the future?**

- To prevent duplicate invoices, consider implementing the following best practices:

  - Establish clear procedures for submitting invoices, especially if multiple team members are involved in the Purchase Order cycle.
  - Double-check your invoices before submitting to ensure the PO number, amount, and quantities are accurate and haven’t been invoiced already.
  - Use internal tracking systems to monitor submitted invoices to avoid overlap.
  - Utilize the ‘Invoice History’ page in Vendor Central to review previously submitted invoices for the same PO number before creating a new one.

**6. When should I submit a credit memo instead of canceling an invoice during a shortage claim?**

- A credit memo should be submitted when there is a need to reverse or adjust the amount invoiced due to a shortage claim or other discrepancies. This is especially useful when:

  - A product is missing from the shipment or undelivered.
  - There’s a price discrepancy or quantity issue that requires financial adjustment.
  - In contrast, an invoice cancellation removes the entire invoice, while a credit memo adjusts the amounts within the original invoice to account for the changes. You should use a credit memo when you want to preserve the record of the original invoice but correct the financial details.

**7. Can I cancel an invoice if it has already been paid or processed?**

- If an invoice has already been paid or processed by Amazon, you typically cannot cancel it directly through the “Invoices” page. However, you may need to submit a credit memo or initiate a dispute with Amazon if there was an error. For invoices that have been fully processed, it’s crucial to reach out to Amazon Vendor Support to determine the best course of action, whether it involves a correction or further steps to ensure accurate records.
