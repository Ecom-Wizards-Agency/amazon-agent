# Vendor Central SOP: Managing Product Availability

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-managing-product-availability

## Managing Product Availability

**Who is this for**:

- This is intended for vendors and sellers who list their products on the Amazon website.
- It is relevant for individuals or teams responsible for managing product listings and availability.

**Objective**

- To provide guidance on managing product availability on the Amazon website.
- To outline the process of assigning codes to listed products and explains how to update the availability status of products.

This SOP describes how Amazon assigns codes to listed products and how to manage the availability status of products on the Amazon website.

1. Amazon uses internal codes for each item listed to determine whether it can be ordered.

2. If a product has an incorrect code, Amazon will not order it from the vendor. The product will also be unavailable on the Amazon website, which results in lower visibility, clicks, demand, and sales.

3. Amazon has revised its extensive list of codes to make it less confusing. The following codes are currently used:

- Greenlight - product is available from the supplier
- In Stock - product is readily available for Amazon to reorder
- Temporarily unavailable - product is not currently being ordered by Amazon
- Permanently unavailable - product can no longer be ordered

4. As a vendor, it is best to maintain product listings with an "In Stock" replenishment code unless you are discontinuing a product.

5. To update the availability of a product in your catalog. Click Items and choose Catalog from the selections.

FAQs

- **Q1: What is the importance of correct codes for product availability on Amazon?**

  - **A1:** Correct codes are crucial because if a product has an incorrect code, Amazon will not order it from the vendor, and it will be unavailable on the Amazon website, leading to lower visibility, clicks, demand, and sales.
- **Q2: What are the different availability codes used by Amazon?**

  - **A2:** The available codes are:

    - **Greenlight**: Product is available from the supplier.
    - **In Stock**: Product is readily available for Amazon to reorder.
    - **Temporarily Unavailable**: Product is not currently being ordered by Amazon.
    - **Permanently Unavailable**: Product can no longer be ordered.
- **Q3: What availability status should I use for products that are still available for reorder?**

  - **A3:** For products that are still available for reorder, it is best to maintain the "In Stock" replenishment code unless you are discontinuing a product.
- **Q4: How do I update the availability status of a product in my catalog?**

  - **A4:**To update the availability, click on "Items" and select "Catalog," then click the "Update Availability" link in the top left corner.
- **Q5: How can I search for products to update their availability status?**

  - **A5:** You can search for products by entering the ASINs that need availability updates. You can enter up to 1000 ASINs at once.
- **Q6: How do I select the products I want to update?**

  - **A6:** Once the search results appear, select the desired products by ticking the checkboxes next to them, then click the "Update availability" button.
- **Q7: What options do I have for setting a product's availability status?**

  - **A7:**You can choose from the following options:

    - **Available**
    - **Temporarily Unavailable** (requires a date when it will be available)
    - **Permanently Unavailable**
- **Q8: What happens after I submit the updated availability status?**

  - **A8:** Once you submit the updated availability status, the product's status will be updated immediately on the Amazon website.
- **Q9: Do I need to provide a specific date for temporarily unavailable products?**

  - **A9:** Yes, if you select "Temporarily Unavailable," you must provide a date when the product will be available again.
