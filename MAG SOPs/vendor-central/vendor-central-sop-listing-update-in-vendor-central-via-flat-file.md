# Vendor Central SOP: Listing Update in Vendor Central via Flat File

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-listing-update-in-vendor-central-via-flat-file

## **Listing Update in Vendor Central via Flat File**

**Who is this for :** Can be performed by vendors or sellers themselves who are managing their products in Vendor Central.

**Objective :** To ensure that the product information on Amazon is accurate, complete, and up-to-date.

**Instructions:**

1. Access the account by going to [https://vendorcentral.amazon.com/](https://vendorcentral.amazon.com/).

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.00.50%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Rppimage.png)

2. Once logged in, confirm if you are on the correct account, if not click the account name at the left top then click See All to see the complete list of accounts.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.00.59%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/ZzZimage.png)

3. Choose the account that you need to work on.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.01.08%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/w2simage.png)

4. After choosing the correct account go to Items > Catalog

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.01.16%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/8fYimage.png)

5. Search for the ASIN that you need to update (you can search ASINs in bulk) tick the upper box to select all the listings including the dupe SKUs, then click the “Download spreadsheet”.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.01.32%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/QU0image.png)

6. You will be redirected to the Bulk product submissions page https://vendorcentral.amazon.com/listing/download-status, find your spreadsheet in “Spreadsheet download history” (check for the most recent file) and download the generated spreadsheet.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.01.42%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/T5eimage.png)

7. Open the spreadsheet.

- You will see several template tabs in the downloaded file if you are updating listings in bulk with different categories.
- The sample you are trying to update is the Product Name. Find the “Item Name” column and change its current data.
- If you see columns that are highlighted in red it means that these attributes are required to fill out to be able to upload the spreadsheet successfully. You can check the listing in the Amazon Seller Central account (if the client has an SC account as well) to see if the details for these required attributes are available in the SC account. If so, you can copy the data from there, if not kindly inform POC of the required attributes.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.01.52%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/kAYimage.png)

8. Once all required data are filled out. Save the spreadsheet and upload the file by going back to Bulk product submissions https://vendorcentral.amazon.com/listing/upload. Click the “Upload your spreadsheet” tab.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.02.08%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/p8wimage.png)

9. Drag or browse the spreadsheet file on your computer and click Upload file.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.05.49%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/oxCimage.png)

10. Monitor the upload status by going to “Spreadsheet upload status”.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-29%20at%203.02.25%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/37rimage.png)

- The status should be marked as “Done” to confirm that the upload was successful.
- If the status shows “Action required”, download the processing summary to check and fix the error.

11. Once successfully uploaded, monitor the changes in the backend and detail page after 15 minutes to check if the updates were successfully pushed through. Sometimes it takes 24 hours for the system to absorb the updates.

FAQs

**1. What is the purpose of updating listings in Vendor Central via flat file?**

- Updating listings via flat file ensures that product information on Amazon is accurate, complete, and aligned with customer expectations. This process allows bulk updates to attributes like titles, descriptions, images, and other critical product details.

**2. How can I confirm that I’m working on the correct account in Vendor Central?**

- After logging in:

  - Check the account name in the upper-left corner.
  - Click See All to view the complete list of accounts.
  - Select the account you need to update before proceeding.

**3. What should I do if I cannot find the ASIN in the catalog?**

- If an ASIN isn’t appearing:

  - Double-check the ASIN for accuracy.
  - Verify that the ASIN is associated with the selected account.
  - Contact the account POC or Vendor Central support if the issue persists.

**4. What do the red-highlighted columns in the spreadsheet mean?**

- Columns highlighted in red indicate required fields that must be completed for the spreadsheet upload to be successful. Check the following:

  - Seller Central Account (if available): Copy required attributes from the SC account.
  - POC Communication: Inform the POC if you cannot locate the necessary data.

**5. Can I update multiple ASINs from different categories in a single spreadsheet?**

- Yes, the downloaded spreadsheet will include tabs for each category being updated. Ensure you input the correct data for each ASIN under its respective category tab.

**6. What should I do if the required attribute information is unavailable?**

- If required data isn’t available:

  - Verify the missing information in Seller Central (if the client has an account).
  - If unavailable in SC, escalate the issue to the POC for further instructions or clarification.

**7. How do I know if my spreadsheet upload was successful?**

- After uploading:

  - Go to the Spreadsheet upload status section in Vendor Central.
  - Look for the status marked as Done.
  - If the status shows Action required, download the processing summary to identify and resolve errors.

**8. What should I do if the upload status is “Action required”?**

- If the upload fails:

  - Download the processing summary to review errors.
  - Address the identified issues in the spreadsheet.
  - Re-upload the corrected file.

**9. How long does it take for changes to reflect on the product detail page?**

- Updates may reflect in the backend or on the product detail page within 15 minutes, but it can take up to 24 hours for changes to fully propagate.

**10. Can I update non-mandatory fields in the spreadsheet?**

- Yes, non-mandatory fields can be updated, but they won’t impact the upload success. Ensure that any fields you modify adhere to Amazon’s formatting guidelines.

**11. What should I include in the “Item Name” column?**

- The Item Name should clearly describe the product and follow Amazon’s title guidelines, typically including:

  - Brand name
  - Product type
  - Key features (e.g., size, color, material).

**12. Can I make partial updates to listings without filling out all the attributes?**

- Yes, you can update specific fields while leaving others blank, as long as all required fields are filled. Fields left blank will retain their current values.

**13. What file format is required for uploading the spreadsheet?**

- The spreadsheet must remain in its original Excel (.xlsx) format when uploading to Vendor Central. Do not convert it to other formats like CSV.

**14. How do I check if updates were successfully pushed to the product detail page?**

- After upload:

  - Check the Spreadsheet upload status for confirmation.
  - Monitor changes in the product’s backend and detail page after 15 minutes. If no changes appear after 24 hours, escalate the issue to Vendor Central support.

**15. Can I revert changes made through a spreadsheet upload?**

- No, once changes are successfully pushed, they cannot be reverted. You will need to create and upload a new spreadsheet with the previous values to restore them.

**16. What should I do if the spreadsheet download is taking too long?**

- If the spreadsheet doesn’t generate promptly:

  - Refresh the Bulk product submissions page.
  - Check your internet connection.
  - Retry the download after a few minutes.
  - If the issue persists, contact Vendor Central support.

**17. Can I update duplicate SKUs in the same spreadsheet?**

- Yes, ensure all duplicate SKUs associated with an ASIN are included in the spreadsheet. This ensures consistency across all related listings.

**18. Why is it necessary to save the spreadsheet before uploading?**

- Saving the spreadsheet ensures that all changes are properly stored and formatted. Always save the file in its original format before uploading to avoid errors.

**19. What are common errors to look for in the processing summary?**

- Common errors include:

  - Missing required attributes.
  - Incorrect or unsupported values (e.g., invalid characters or formats).
  - Mismatched ASIN or SKU details.

**20. Can I monitor multiple spreadsheet uploads simultaneously?**

- Yes, the Spreadsheet upload status section displays all uploads. Use this to track progress for multiple files and resolve any errors efficiently.
