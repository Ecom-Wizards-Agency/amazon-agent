## Producing an Inventory Health Report

**Who is this for?:** This is for vendors and sellers on Amazon who want to generate an inventory health report using the Amazon Retail Analytics Dashboard.

**Objective:** To help users access the necessary information about their inventory, including net received value, sell-through rate, open purchase order quantity, sellable and unsellable on-hand inventory, aged 90+ days sellable inventory, and replenishment category.

**To access your Amazon Retail Analytics Dashboard in Vendor Central, follow these steps**

1. Click on the "Reports" tab and select "Retail Analytics" from the drop-down menu to see an overview of your dashboard.

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/RUrimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/RUrimage.png)

2. On the top menu, click on "Inventory" to access your inventory health report.

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/RSwimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/RSwimage.png)

3. You can customize your report using the drop-down menus at the top of the right side of the page. After selecting your data criteria, click "Apply" to update the report.

4. Download the report by clicking on the "Download" button in the top right-hand corner of the page.

The report provides important information per ASIN, including net received value and units, sell-through rate, open purchase order quantity, sellable and unsellable on-hand inventory, aged 90+ days sellable inventory, and replenishment category.

This information can help you make informed decisions on each ASIN individually, such as recognizing ASINs with poor sell-through rates and deciding whether to discontinue them or investigate marketing campaigns to boost sales. You can also use the report to factor in the number of sellable on-hand units into your forecasting and planning models, manage unsellable inventory, and assess the performance of aged ASINs.

By leveraging the information available in the Amazon Retail Analytics Dashboard, you can enhance your product catalog and sell only products that perform well on Amazon.

FAQs

**1. How often does the Inventory Health Report update?**

- The report updates daily, but some metrics (e.g., aged inventory and sell-through rate) may have a 24-48 hour delay.

**2. How far back can I access historical inventory data?**

- Amazon allows vendors to retrieve inventory data for up to 24 months. If you need older reports, consider downloading them periodically for long-term tracking.

**3. What do the key metrics in the Inventory Health Report mean?**

- Net Received Value – The total value of units Amazon has received from your purchase orders.
- Sell-Through Rate – The percentage of inventory sold within a specific period, indicating product demand.
- Replenishment Category – Amazon’s recommendation on whether to restock or reduce inventory levels based on sales trends.
- Aged 90+ Days Sellable Inventory – Units that have been in Amazon’s fulfillment centers for over 90 days and may be at risk of long-term storage fees.

**4. What should I do if data is missing or incorrect?**

- If your report has missing or incorrect data:

  - Ensure you have selected the correct reporting range and filters.
  - Check whether the report is still updating (some data may refresh overnight).
  - Compare with other reports (e.g., Sales Diagnostic Report) to spot discrepancies.
  - If issues persist, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**5. How can I use the report beyond forecasting?**

- You can leverage the Inventory Health Report to:

  - Identify slow-moving ASINs and decide whether to promote, discontinue, or bundle them.
  - Prevent overstocking by adjusting purchase order quantities based on demand trends.
  - Manage unsellable inventory to reduce storage fees and avoid unnecessary restocking.

**6. Can I automate or schedule the Inventory Health Report?**

- Currently, Amazon does not offer automated scheduling for this report. However, you can manually download it as needed to track inventory performance over time.

**7. What qualifies as "unsellable inventory" in the report?**

- Unsellable inventory includes:

  - Customer returns deemed non-resalable.
  - Damaged goods from warehouse handling.
  - Expired products (for perishable items).
  - To resolve unsellable inventory, you can create a removal order in Vendor Central or contact Amazon for disposal options.
