## Submitting Certifications for Your Products

**Who is this for :** This is for vendors and sellers on Amazon who need to submit certifications for their products.

**Objective:**

- To help vendors effectively submit their certifications and exemption sheets to Amazon, enabling smooth processing and review by Amazon's internal teams.
- The ultimate goal is to ensure the safety of products and maintain regulatory compliance within Amazon's marketplace.

1. To identify and manage any products that may be considered dangerous, download the ["Dangerous Goods (Hazmat) Identification and Management Guide"](https://d437nepi28pn7.cloudfront.net/Resource%20Center/vendor-central/US/en-US/Dangerous%20Goods%20(Hazmat)%20Identification%20and%20Management%20Guide.pdf) from the ["Dangerous goods (hazmat)"](https://vendorcentral.amazon.com/hz/vendor/members/support/help/node/G202112380) Support content.

Additionally, if your product requires it, complete an [Exemption Sheet](https://d437nepi28pn7.cloudfront.net/Resource+Center/vendor-central/GLOBAL_Selling/Exemption+Sheet+for+Battery+and+Battery-powered+Products.xlsx) by downloading the relevant form from the ["Requirements for Lithium Batteries or Products that Contain Lithium Batteries"](https://vendorcentral.amazon.com/hz/vendor/members/support/help/node/G202037370) support content in the Resource Center on Vendor Central.

3. Send it to Amazon via the "Contact Us" button in the Support Center.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1687886994517.png)

4. Ensure that you have all the necessary certifications and exemption sheets before submitting them to Amazon.
5. Select your business group and then "SDS and Exemption Sheets submissions" from the "Product Compliance" section on the next page, and submit your documents.

6. Amazon's internal teams will notify you if all is in order for your relevant products.
7. If they require further information, they will request it from you.

FAQs

**1. What types of certifications do I need to submit?**

- The required certifications depend on the product type. For example:

  - Electronics: FCC certification
  - Children’s Products: CPSIA compliance
  - Food & Supplements: FDA approvals
  - Hazardous Materials: SDS (Safety Data Sheet)

    - Check Vendor Central’s Product Compliance section for specific certification requirements.

**2. What file formats and sizes are accepted for certification uploads?**

- Amazon accepts PDF, JPEG, and PNG files for certification uploads. File size should not exceed 10MB per document.

**3. How long does it take for Amazon to review my certifications?**

- Amazon’s review process typically takes 5–10 business days, but it may take longer during peak times. You will receive a notification in Vendor Central once the review is complete.

**4. What should I do if my certification is rejected?**

- If your certification is rejected, check Vendor Central for the rejection reason. Common issues include missing information or outdated documents. Correct the issue and resubmit the certification through Product Compliance > SDS and Exemption Sheets Submissions.

**5. How can I track the status of my submitted certifications?**

- Go to Vendor Central > Product Compliance and look under Pending Your Action to track submissions. You can also check your case log under Help > Case Log for updates.

**6. What happens if I don’t submit the required certifications?**

- Failure to submit necessary certifications may lead to product delisting, purchase order delays, or account suspension. Amazon enforces strict compliance to meet regulatory and safety standards.

**7. Can I submit multiple certifications at once?**

- Yes, Amazon allows bulk submission of certifications if multiple ASINs require the same document. However, each certification must be correctly linked to the relevant ASINs.

**8. Where can I get help if I’m unsure about certification requirements?**

- If you are unsure which certifications are required for your product, visit Vendor Central > Resource Center > Product Compliance or contact Amazon support via Help > Contact Us > Product Compliance Inquiries.
