# Vendor Central SOP: Managing Purchase Order Outages

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-managing-purchase-order-outages

## Managing Purchase Order Outages

**Who is this for**:

- This is intended for vendors who have access to Vendor Central and are responsible for managing purchase orders.
- It is relevant for vendors who need to handle situations where orders cannot be generated or shipped due to holidays or other significant dates.

**Objective**: To provide clear instructions on how to manage purchase order outages in Vendor Central.

To manage purchase orders that won't be generated or shipped due to national holidays or other significant dates for your company, you can add those dates to your Vendor Central account.

1. Go to the "Settings" tab on the top menu of Vendor Central, and select "Purchase Order Outages" from the Drop-Down Menu.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/dEWimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/dEWimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712175481744.png)

2. On the next page, click the "Add outage days" button.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/0xBimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/0xBimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712175485386.png)

3. In the pop-up window, choose the Vendor Code(s) the date applies to, and select the start and end dates. Then click "Submit" to add the date.![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712175489669.png)

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What are Purchase Order Outages?**

  - **A1:** Purchase Order Outages are periods when vendors notify Amazon that they are unable to generate or ship purchase orders due to holidays, operational closures, or other significant dates.
- **Q2: Who is responsible for managing Purchase Order Outages?**

  - **A2:** Vendors with access to Vendor Central who manage purchase orders and shipments are responsible for handling outages.

Steps and Navigation

- **Q3: Where can I manage Purchase Order Outages in Vendor Central?**

  - **A3:** Go to the "Settings" tab in the top menu of Vendor Central and select "Purchase Order Outages" from the dropdown menu.
- **Q4: Can I set specific dates for Purchase Order Outages?**

  - **A4:** Yes, you can specify the exact dates for the outage to inform Amazon of the unavailability for generating or shipping purchase orders.

Setting and Updating Outages

- **Q5: What information is required to set an outage?**

  - **A5:** You need to provide the start and end dates of the outage and ensure it aligns with your operational schedule.
- **Q6: Can I update or cancel a previously set Purchase Order Outage?**

  - **A6:** Yes, you can update or cancel the outage by revisiting the "Purchase Order Outages" section in Vendor Central and modifying the details as needed.

Notifications and Impacts

- **Q7: Will Amazon send reminders about upcoming Purchase Order Outages?**

  - **A7:** Amazon does not typically send reminders. It is the vendor's responsibility to manage and communicate outages in advance.
- **Q8: How does setting a Purchase Order Outage impact my account?**

  - **A8:** During the outage period, Amazon will not generate or send purchase orders. This helps prevent penalties or logistical issues due to non-fulfillment.

Planning for Outages

- **Q9: When should I notify Amazon of an upcoming outage?**

  - **A9:** Notify Amazon as soon as possible, ideally several weeks in advance, to ensure smooth adjustments in purchase order generation.
- **Q10: Can I set recurring outages for annual events like holidays?**

  - **A10:** No, outages need to be set manually each time. However, you can proactively plan and schedule them in advance for predictable events.

Troubleshooting and Compliance

- **Q11: What happens if I fail to set an outage and cannot fulfill purchase orders?**

  - **A11:** Failure to notify Amazon can result in missed shipments, penalties, and potential impacts on your vendor performance metrics.
- **Q12: Is there a limit to how long a Purchase Order Outage can last?**

  - **A12:** Amazon allows flexibility, but long or frequent outages may require additional communication with Amazon Vendor Support to ensure compliance with your vendor agreement.
