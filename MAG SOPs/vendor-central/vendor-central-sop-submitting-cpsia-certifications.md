# Vendor Central SOP: Submitting CPSIA Certifications

## Submitting CPSIA Certifications

**Who is this for :** This is for vendors on Amazon who intend to sell products that are intended for children or general consumers.

**Objective :** To meet the regulatory requirements set by the Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC), mitigate any potential risks associated with non-compliance, and ensure the safety and satisfaction of customers who purchase the products on Amazon's platform.

1. If you intend to sell products that are intended for children or general consumers, you will need to obtain a Customized Customer Product Safety Improvement Act (CPSIA) certification, which can either be a Children's Product Certification (CPC) or a General Certificate of Conformity (GCC), depending on your target market.

2. As the responsible party, Amazon is held liable by the CPSC for any imported products. To ensure compliance, Amazon has a strict compliance program that includes the following:

3. Identifying which products are subject to CPSIA regulations.

4. Requesting for the appropriate General Certificate of Conformity (GCC) and the corresponding test reports.

5. Not booking the PO until the necessary documents have been verified.

6. To access the certification templates listed below, go to the Operations section of the Vendor Central Resource Center.

7. For Children's certifications (CPC):

- AMAZON CPSIA CPC - Children's Products and Toys (Domestic)
- AMAZON CPSIA CPC - Durable Infant Toddler Products (Domestic)
- AMAZON CPSIA CPC - Children's apparel (Domestic)
- AMAZON CPSIA CPC - Children's Products and Toys (Import)
- AMAZON CPSIA CPC - Durable Infant Toddler Products (Import)
- AMAZON CPSIA CPC - Children's apparel (Import)

8. For non-children's products certifications (GCC):

- AMAZON CPSIA GCC - GENERIC template (Domestic)
- AMAZON CPSIA GCC - GENERIC template (Import)

9. Submit the certifications as described in [SOP: Submitting Certifications for Your Products](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-submitting-certifications-for-your-products) and [SOP: Managing Your Vendor Compliance Certificates](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-managing-your-vendor-compliance-certificates).

FAQs

**1. How do I know if my product requires a CPSIA certification?**

- Products intended for children (ages 12 and under) require a Children’s Product Certificate (CPC), while general-use products that fall under CPSC regulations require a General Certificate of Conformity (GCC). Categories such as toys, children's apparel, and infant products almost always require CPSIA certification.

**2. How do I obtain a CPSIA certification for my product?**

- You must have your product tested by a CPSC-accepted laboratory. After testing, the lab will issue the appropriate certification (CPC or GCC). You can find a list of accepted labs on the CPSC website.

**3. Where do I submit my CPSIA certification in Vendor Central?**

- CPSIA certifications should be submitted under Vendor Central > Resource Center > Operations Section. Follow the instructions outlined in the SOPs Submitting Certifications for Your Products and Managing Your Vendor Compliance Certificates.

**4. How long does it take for Amazon to review my CPSIA certification?**

- Amazon typically reviews CPSIA certifications within 5–10 business days, but this may vary based on submission volume.

**5. What should I do if my CPSIA certification is rejected?**

- If Amazon rejects your certification, check the rejection reason in Vendor Central. Common issues include missing information, incorrect formatting, or outdated test reports. Update your documentation and resubmit it. If needed, contact Amazon Vendor Support for clarification.

**6. Do CPSIA certifications expire?**

- Yes. CPSIA certifications must be updated whenever there is a change in product design, manufacturing process, or safety standards. Some products require annual testing, while others may have longer validity periods. Check CPSC guidelines for your product category.

**7. What happens if I fail to submit a required CPSIA certification?**

- Failure to submit CPSIA certifications can result in delayed purchase orders, product delisting, or account suspension. Amazon enforces strict compliance to meet regulatory requirements.

**8. Can I submit one CPSIA certification for multiple products?**

- No, each product requires its own certification unless the products are identical in composition and manufacturing process. If you have multiple variations (e.g., different colors or sizes), check with Amazon to see if a single certification is acceptable.
