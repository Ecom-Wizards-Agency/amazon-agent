# Vendor Central SOP: Cancelling a Shortage Claim

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-cancelling-a-shortage-claim

## **Cancelling a Shortage Claim**

**Who is this for :** This is intended for vendors who have received a verified shortage claim from Amazon and need to cancel the claim by issuing a credit.

**Objective :** To guide vendors in canceling a shortage claim by issuing a credit on Vendor Central. To establish a systematic process for canceling a shortage claim. This SOP aims to ensure that all relevant parties involved in the claim cancellation process follow consistent guidelines and procedures, resulting in an efficient and accurate resolution of shortage claims.

Once you have verified the shortage claim by Amazon, issue a credit for the shortage by first creating a credit on your own in-house system to generate a credit memo number to use on the Vendor Central Credit Memo.

1. Navigate to the “Invoices” page from the Drop-Down Menu of your “Payments” tab on the Top Menu of your Vendor Central Dashboard.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1688686198029.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/B0timage.png)

2. Under “Available Actions” on the left-hand side of the screen, click on the “Submit Proof of Delivery” button.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1688686253485.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/x3oimage.png)

3. A table of all shortage claims will be shown on the next page. Click the “Action” button to the right of the invoice you investigated. The shortage claim invoice generated by Amazon will be your original invoice followed by the letters “SCR” - this will help you track this invoice and its pending credit on your remittances at a later stage. Select “Submit Credit Memo” from the “Action” drop-down menu.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1688778759931.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/4t9image.png)

4. On the next page, provide all the necessary information regarding your credit memo and make sure that the Vendor Central Credit Memo details and total value match the credit memo you generated on your own system. Review all the details and click “Submit” if everything is correct.

FAQs

**1. Do I always need to issue a credit memo to cancel a shortage claim?**

- Yes, issuing a credit memo is the only way to acknowledge and resolve a verified shortage claim in Vendor Central. If you dispute the claim instead, you will need to submit proof of delivery rather than a credit memo.

**2. Do I need to upload a physical credit memo file, or just enter the details?**

- Amazon does not require you to upload a credit memo document. Instead, you must enter the credit memo details manually in Vendor Central under Payments > Invoices when selecting “Submit Credit Memo.”

**3. How long do I have to submit a credit memo after verifying a shortage claim?**

- You should issue a credit memo as soon as possible after verifying the claim. While Amazon does not specify an exact deadline, delays may impact your account standing or payment reconciliation.

**4. How can I track the status of a submitted credit memo?**

- To check if your credit memo has been processed:

  - Go to Payments > Invoices in Vendor Central.
  - Search for the original invoice with “SCR” in the invoice number.
  - Look for a "Credited" or "Completed" status.

**5. Can I edit or cancel a credit memo after submitting it?**

- No, once a credit memo is submitted, it cannot be edited or reversed in Vendor Central. If you made an error, contact Amazon Vendor Support to discuss possible corrections.

**6. Can I issue credit memos in bulk for multiple shortage claims?**

- No, each shortage claim must be processed individually in Vendor Central. There is currently no bulk credit memo submission option.

**7. What happens if I don’t issue a credit memo for a shortage claim?**

- If you fail to submit a credit memo, Amazon may automatically deduct the shortage amount from your future remittances, impacting your cash flow and account reconciliation.

**8. What should I do if I believe the shortage claim is incorrect?**

- If you believe Amazon’s shortage claim is invalid, you should dispute it instead of issuing a credit memo by submitting proof of delivery through Payments > Invoices > Submit Proof of Delivery.

**9. Who do I contact if I have issues with a shortage claim or credit memo submission?**

- If you encounter any issues, reach out to Amazon Vendor Support through:

  - Vendor Central > Help > Contact Us > Payments & Invoices > Shortage Claims
  - Open a case and provide relevant details (PO number, invoice number, and supporting documents).
