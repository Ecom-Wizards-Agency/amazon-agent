# Vendor Central SOP: Creating Case Packs

## Creating Case Packs

**Who is this for :** It is intended for warehouse supervisors, inventory coordinators, shipping and receiving personnel, or any other relevant stakeholders responsible for organizing and packaging products into case packs. The procedure assumes a basic understanding of warehouse operations and familiarity with packaging materials and equipment.

**Objective :** To establish a standardized method for packaging and grouping products into cases for efficient handling, transportation, and distribution. This process aims to optimize inventory management, streamline logistics operations, and improve overall operational efficiency within a supply chain.

**Here are the steps to create a case pack of your products.**

1. Go to the "Items" tab on the top menu of Vendor Central and select "Manage Case Packs" from the drop-down menu.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%204.11.05%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/oGUimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/oGUimage.png)

2. Click the "Create Case Pack Template" button on the next page.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%204.11.13%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/HABimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/HABimage.png)

3. Enter a template name, select your Vendor Code, product type, and sizing or other variation from the provided drop-down menus, and select the variations you want for your template.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%204.11.21%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/VZ1image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/VZ1image.png)

4. Click "Submit" to create the template. A pop-up window will confirm that your case pack template has been created. Click "OK" to be directed to your template overview page.

5. Return to the case pack home page by clicking the "Case Pack Home Page" link at the top right of the page.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%204.11.33%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/qoGimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/qoGimage.png)

6. Your Case Pack Template will be listed beneath the "Your Existing Case Pack Template" heading.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%204.11.41%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/bEwimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/bEwimage.png)

7. Type the EAN, UPC, or ASIN you wish to specify for this case pack next to Step 2 and click "Search.".

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%204.11.49%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/dZvimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/dZvimage.png)

8. On the next page, select the colors of your chosen ASIN that you want in the case pack on the left side of the page and click "Add" alongside the template under the heading "Case Pack Associations for Style" on the right side of the page.

9. Once you have added all the necessary ASINs to your template, Amazon will be able to order the case pack from you.

10. To delete a case pack template, click on the template's name on the Case Pack Home Page. Click the "Delete Template" button on the Case Pack Template Configuration template. Confirm that you want to delete the template in the pop-up window.

FAQs

**1. What is a case pack, and why should I create one?**

- A case pack is a pre-packaged group of the same product that Amazon can order as a single unit instead of individual items. Case packs:

  - Simplify fulfillment by reducing warehouse handling.
  - Improve shipping efficiency with consistent packaging
  - Help meet Amazon’s inventory storage requirements by sending standard quantities.

**2. Does Amazon have specific requirements for case packs?**

- Yes. Amazon may have size, weight, and quantity limits for case packs depending on the product category. To ensure compliance, check Amazon’s Packaging and Prep Requirements in Vendor Central > Help & Support.

**3. What happens after I create a case pack template?**

- Once a case pack template is created, Amazon can begin ordering case packs instead of individual units. However, you may need to update your catalog settings or coordinate with your Amazon contact to ensure orders are placed correctly.

**4. Can I modify an existing case pack template?**

- No, Amazon does not allow direct modifications to an existing case pack template. If changes are needed:

  - Delete the current template and create a new one.
  - Ensure all ASINs and case pack details are correct before submission.

**5. What should I do if I can’t create a case pack template?**

- If you encounter issues when creating a case pack:

  - Verify that the ASIN is eligible for case packs.
  - Ensure your Vendor Code and variations are correctly selected.
  - Clear your browser cache or try a different browser.
  - If the issue persists, contact Amazon Vendor Support for assistance.

**6. Will Amazon order case packs by default once I create a template?**

- Not necessarily. Amazon’s ordering system considers demand, storage limits, and inventory needs before switching to case pack purchases. Monitor your purchase orders to see if Amazon is ordering in case packs.

**7. How does creating a case pack affect fulfillment and shipping costs?**

- Case packs can reduce per-unit shipping costs by consolidating multiple units into a single package. However, oversized or improperly configured case packs may lead to higher fulfillment fees or non-compliance penalties. Always review Amazon’s packaging guidelines before creating a case pack.
