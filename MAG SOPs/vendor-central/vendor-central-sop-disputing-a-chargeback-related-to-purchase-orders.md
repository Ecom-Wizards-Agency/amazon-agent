## Disputing a Chargeback Related to Purchase Orders

**Who is this for**:

- This is intended for vendors who have access to Vendor Central and need to dispute a chargeback specifically related to purchase orders.
- It is relevant for vendors who have received chargebacks for non-compliance related to Unconfirmed PO Units, Overage PO Units, PO On-Time Accuracy, and Rejected PO Rate and wish to challenge their validity by providing evidence and documentation.

**Objective**: To provide clear instructions on how to dispute a chargeback related to purchase orders in Vendor Central.

1. Navigate to Vendor Central's "Reports" tab and select "Operational Performance" from the drop-down menu.![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712333656023.png)

2. On the top page, click on the "View All" link to go to the chargeback in detail page.

5. Enter all relevant information in the "Dispute Summary" text box, including an explanation for why you are disputing the chargeback and disagreeing with Amazon's assessment of non-compliance.

6. Upload the required documentation for the chargeback you are disputing by clicking "Add Attachments" and selecting each relevant document.

7. Click "Submit Dispute" at the top right of the page to submit your dispute for these types of chargebacks.![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712333673191.png)

**Note the following:**

- You can only dispute a chargeback twice. After two rejections, the chargeback is no longer eligible for dispute.
- You have 30 days from the date the chargeback was raised in Vendor Central to dispute it.

**To dispute chargebacks related to purchase orders, you will need to provide specific documentation. The required documentation includes:**

1. For Unconfirmed PO Units Chargeback: A screenshot of Vendor Central that shows the ASIN was confirmed on the PO in question.
2. For Overage PO Units Chargeback: A screenshot of Vendor Central that shows the ASIN was confirmed on the PO in question.
3. For PO On-Time Accuracy Chargeback:

**For not on-time units:**

- Prepaid TL/LTL shipments: Proof of the Carrier Central appointment request indicating the requested delivery date within the delivery window.
- Prepaid small parcel shipments: Proof of delivery that includes a tracking number, PO, and a delivery date within the assigned delivery window.
- Collect shipments: A copy of the routing request email from Amazon showing it was submitted on the business day prior to the last date of the shipping window, and that the Freight Ready Date was within the shipping window, with the matching Amazon Reference Number.

**For not filled units:**

**If units were never received:**

- Proof of delivery that includes a tracking number, PO, and a delivery date before the order cancellation date.
- The invoice ID number related to the PO and the ASIN mentioned in the Chargeback.

**If units arrive after the order was canceled:**

- Proof of delivery that includes a tracking number, PO, and a delivery date before the order cancellation date.
- Prepaid TL/LTL shipments: A Carrier Central appointment screenshot showing a Carrier Requested Delivery Date at least three days prior to the auto-cancel date.
- Collect shipments: Routing request email from Amazon that includes a Freight ready date at least seven days before the auto-cancel date and a matching Amazon Reference Number.

**For down-confirmed units:** A Vendor Central screenshot showing the PO, ASIN, and

confirmed units. Also include the reason why the units were adjusted down, as well as

(such as floods, fires, extreme weather, or carriers refusing to collect freight).

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What is the purpose of disputing a chargeback related to purchase orders?**

  - **A1:** Disputing a chargeback related to purchase orders allows vendors to challenge chargebacks for non-compliance issues like Unconfirmed PO Units, Overage PO Units, PO On-Time Accuracy, and Rejected PO Rate by providing relevant evidence and documentation.
- **Q2: How do I initiate the dispute for a chargeback related to a purchase order?**

  - **A2:** Navigate to the "Reports" tab in Vendor Central, select "Operational Performance," then click "View All" to see the chargebacks. Select the applicable chargeback(s) and click the "Dispute" button.

Steps and Navigation

- **Q3: How do I find chargebacks related to specific purchase order issues?**

  - **A3:** Scroll through the transaction types on the chargeback page, including Unconfirmed PO Units, Overage PO Units, PO On-Time Accuracy, and Rejected PO Rate.
- **Q4: What should I do if there are multiple chargebacks for the same PO or ASIN?**

  - **A4:** You can select multiple chargebacks related to the same PO or ASIN and dispute them at once.

Dispute Process

- **Q5: What do I need to include in the "Dispute Summary" when disputing a chargeback?**

  - **A5:** Provide an explanation of why you disagree with the chargeback, along with any relevant evidence that supports your dispute.
- **Q6: How do I submit supporting documents when disputing a chargeback?**

  - **A6:** Use the "Add Attachments" button to upload relevant documentation that supports your dispute.
- **Q7: Is there a time limit to dispute a chargeback related to purchase orders?**

  - **A7:** Yes, you have 30 days from the date the chargeback was raised to submit your dispute.

Supporting Documentation

- **Q8: What documentation is required for an Unconfirmed PO Units chargeback?**

  - **A8:** You need to provide a screenshot of Vendor Central showing that the ASIN was confirmed on the PO in question.
- **Q9: What documentation is needed for an Overage PO Units chargeback?**

  - **A9:** A screenshot of Vendor Central showing the ASIN was confirmed on the PO in question.
- **Q10: What documentation is required for PO On-Time Accuracy chargebacks?**

  - **A10:** The required documents depend on the issue:

    - For Prepaid TL/LTL shipments: Proof of the Carrier Central appointment with the requested delivery date.
    - For Prepaid small parcel shipments: Proof of delivery with tracking number, PO, and delivery date.
    - For Collect shipments: A copy of the routing request email from Amazon with the correct dates and matching reference number.
- **Q11: What documentation should be submitted for Rejected PO Rate chargebacks?**

  - **A11:** You need to calculate and provide your percentage of confirmed units per line on the PO and for the 13 weeks before the chargeback.

Submission and Review

- **Q12: How do I submit the dispute after entering the necessary information?**

  - **A12:** Once all relevant information and documentation are entered, click "Submit Dispute" at the top right of the page to finalize your dispute submission.
- **Q13: Can I dispute a chargeback more than twice?**

  - **A13:** No, you can only dispute the same chargeback twice. If both disputes are rejected, the chargeback cannot be disputed further.

Best Practices

- **Q14: What can I do to improve the chances of my dispute being accepted?**

  - **A14:** Ensure timely submission, provide clear and accurate documentation, and make sure your explanation aligns with Amazon's requirements for the chargeback type you're disputing.
- **Q15: What if my dispute is rejected twice?**

  - **A15:** If your dispute is rejected twice, you can no longer dispute the chargeback. You should carefully review the rejection details and take corrective actions to avoid future chargebacks.
