## Producing a Cancelled Purchase Order Report

**Who is this for:** This is intended for users of the Vendor Central Dashboard who are responsible for monitoring and analyzing cancelled purchase orders.

**Objective:**

- To guide users in producing a Cancelled Purchase Order Report using the Vendor Central Dashboard.
- To provide instructions on how to access and utilize the Cancelled Purchase Order (PO) Items Report to gather information on cancelled items and identify trends related to inventory management and supplier performance.

The Cancelled Purchase Order (PO) Items Report provides information on Items on which Purchase Orders were cancelled either by you or Amazon. It is crucial to monitor this report as if Amazon cancels PO items that you have already shipped, you will be responsible for an overage chargeback.

Similarly, if you cancel PO items due to them being out of stock or defective, you must ensure not to ship them, or you will be liable for an overage chargeback. You can filter the cancelled PO items based on a specified duration such as the last day, week, month, or 1-2 months. It is advisable to download this report for at least a month to keep accurate records of cancelled items.

1. To access the Cancelled PO Items Report, go to the “Orders” tab on your Vendor Central Dashboard's top menu and select “Purchase Orders” from the drop-down menu.

The Cancelled PO Items Report is useful in analyzing the number of items that you did not ship to Amazon and why. You can identify trends where your suppliers or manufacturers do not deliver your inventory promptly and use this to enhance your inventory planning models.

The report also provides insight into the potential revenue you could have earned if you had been able to ship these items. If a particular product consistently gets short-shipped to Amazon, you may want to re-evaluate whether to keep that ASIN in your Amazon catalog, discontinue it, or increase your forecast quantities for that ASIN.

FAQs

**1. How often is the Cancelled Purchase Order Items Report updated?**

- The report updates periodically but may not reflect real-time data. Amazon typically processes PO cancellations within 24-48 hours, so check back regularly for the latest updates.

**2. Why does Amazon cancel purchase orders?**

- Amazon may cancel POs due to demand fluctuations, supply chain adjustments, warehouse capacity constraints, or vendor performance issues. If cancellations become frequent, review your fulfillment rates and discuss potential solutions with your Amazon contact.

**3. What should I do if Amazon cancels a PO after I’ve already shipped the items?**

- If Amazon cancels a PO after you’ve shipped the products, you may face an overage chargeback. Contact Amazon Vendor Support immediately to discuss potential resolutions. If possible, coordinate a return or reallocation of inventory.

**4. How can I use the Cancelled PO Items Report beyond tracking supplier performance?**

- Besides monitoring supplier reliability, you can use the report for demand forecasting, identifying seasonal trends, and evaluating which products consistently face supply chain issues.

**5. How can I reduce the frequency of purchase order cancellations?**

- Maintain accurate inventory levels, ensure timely fulfillment, and improve communication with suppliers. If specific ASINs frequently get canceled, reassess their viability in your catalog or adjust forecasting.

**6. How long does Amazon keep Cancelled PO Items Reports available for download?**

- Amazon retains report data for a limited period. It is recommended to download reports monthly and store them for long-term reference. If you need historical data beyond Amazon’s retention period, keep your own records.

**7. How do chargebacks work for Amazon-canceled purchase orders?**

- If Amazon cancels a PO after you've already shipped the product, you may receive an overage chargeback. This means you won’t be reimbursed for the excess inventory, and additional penalties may apply. Always verify POs before shipping to avoid this risk.
