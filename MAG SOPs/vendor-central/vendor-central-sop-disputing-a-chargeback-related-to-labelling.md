# Vendor Central SOP: Disputing a Chargeback Related to Labelling

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-disputing-a-chargeback-related-to-labelling

## Disputing a Chargeback Related to Labelling

**Who is this for:**

- This is intended for vendors who have access to Vendor Central and need to dispute chargebacks specifically related to labeling.
- It is relevant for vendors who have received chargebacks for non-compliance related to Non-Compliant Barcode, No PO Label on Carton, and No Carton Content Label.

**Objective:**

- To provide vendors with a clear process for disputing chargebacks related to labeling in Vendor Central.
- To inform vendors about the specific documentation requirements for each type of chargeback related to labeling, ensuring they provide the appropriate evidence to support their dispute.

**To dispute chargebacks related to labeling, follow these steps:**

1. Go to Vendor Central and select "Operational Performance" from the "Reports" tab on the top menu.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-08-09%20at%2010.43.38%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/457image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/457image.png)

2. On the top right of the page, click on the "View All" link to go to the chargeback in detail page.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/bY2image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/bY2image.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712239033904(1).png)

3. Choose the individual non-compliance you want to dispute from the Transaction Type list. The non-compliances related to labeling are Non-Compliant Barcode, No PO Label on Carton, and No Carton Content Label.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712239038951(1).png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/ACiimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/ACiimage.png)

4. On the next page, select the chargeback(s) you want to dispute and click on "Dispute" button.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/a0zimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/a0zimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712852476699(1).png)

5. Fill out the "Dispute Summary" text box, explaining why you disagree with Amazon's assessment that this is non-compliance. 6. Upload any relevant documentation to support your dispute by clicking the "Add Attachments" button beneath the "Dispute Summary" text box and selecting each relevant document. 7. Click the "Submit Dispute" button at the top right side of the page to submit your dispute.

**Note:**

- You may only dispute the same chargeback twice. After that, it is no longer eligible for dispute.
- You have 30 days from the date the chargeback was raised in Vendor Central to dispute it.

**For Barcode Chargeback:**

- Provide image(s) of the label and the ASIN barcode that shows the product was sent with a valid barcode and that the barcode could be correctly scanned when it left your facility.

**For No PO Label on Carton Chargeback:**

- Provide image(s) of a valid label on a carton that shows the carton had the PO information.

**For No Carton Content Label Chargeback:**

- The documentation you need to provide will depend on the notes associated with the chargeback:
- If Amazon couldn’t recognize any carton content labels, provide an example of the type of carton content label you use. It does not have to be the exact label from the chargeback, but it must be representative of what you use on a regular basis.
- If the carton level information was missing from the ASN, provide evidence of where the ASN contained the LP barcode information (either the SSCC or the AMZNCC).
- If the GTIN-14 barcode had unusable mapping, supply the GTIN-14 Gold List that contains the mapping between the GTIN-14, the ASIN, and the quantity. You must also provide the email confirmation that you received from your contact at Amazon stating that the GTIN-14 Gold List was approved.

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What is the purpose of disputing a chargeback related to labeling?**

  - **A1:** Disputing a chargeback related to labeling allows vendors to challenge chargebacks for non-compliance related to labeling issues such as Non-Compliant Barcode, No PO Label on Carton, and No Carton Content Label by providing the required documentation and evidence.
- **Q2: How do I initiate the dispute for a chargeback related to labeling?**

  - **A2:** Navigate to the "Reports" tab in Vendor Central, select "Operational Performance," and click "View All" to see the chargebacks. Select the applicable labeling chargeback and click the "Dispute" button.

Steps and Navigation

- **Q3: How do I locate a specific chargeback related to labeling?**

  - **A3:** On the chargeback detail page, look for the specific non-compliance related to labeling from the Transaction Type list. The available options are Non-Compliant Barcode, No PO Label on Carton, and No Carton Content Label.
- **Q4:** **Can I dispute multiple chargebacks at once?**

  - **A4:** Yes, you can select multiple chargebacks that are related to labeling and dispute them together.

Dispute Process

- **Q5: What do I need to include in the "Dispute Summary" when disputing a chargeback?**

  - **A5:** Provide an explanation of why you disagree with Amazon’s assessment of non-compliance. Make sure to clearly state why the labeling was compliant and provide supporting evidence.
- **Q6: How do I submit supporting documents for a labeling chargeback dispute?**

  - **A6:** Click the "Add Attachments" button below the "Dispute Summary" text box to upload relevant images or documentation that supports your claim.
- **Q7: How do I submit the dispute after filling out the necessary information?**

  - **A7:** After entering your dispute explanation and uploading the supporting documentation, click "Submit Dispute" at the top right of the page to finalize your submission.

Supporting Documentation

- **Q8: What documentation is required for a Non-Compliant Barcode chargeback?**

  - **A8:** Provide an image of the label and ASIN barcode showing that the product was sent with a valid barcode, and that the barcode could be correctly scanned when it left your facility.
- **Q9: What documentation is needed for a No PO Label on Carton chargeback?**

  - **A9:** Provide an image of a carton label showing the PO information was included.
- **Q10: What documentation should be submitted for a No Carton Content Label chargeback?**

  - **A10:** The documentation required depends on the issue:

    - If Amazon couldn’t recognize any carton content labels: Provide an example of the type of carton content label you typically use.
    - If carton level information was missing from the ASN: Provide evidence showing that the ASN included the LP barcode information (either SSCC or AMZNCC).
    - If the GTIN-14 barcode had unusable mapping: Provide the GTIN-14 Gold List with mapping details between the GTIN-14, ASIN, and quantity, along with the email confirmation from Amazon approving the GTIN-14 Gold List.

Submission and Review

- **Q11: Is there a time limit for disputing a labeling chargeback?**

  - **A11:** Yes, you have 30 days from the date the chargeback was raised in Vendor Central to submit your dispute.
- **Q12: Can I dispute a chargeback more than twice?**

  - **A12:** No, you can only dispute the same chargeback twice. After two rejections, the chargeback cannot be disputed further.

Best Practices

- **Q13: What can I do to improve the chances of my dispute being accepted?**

  - **A13:** Ensure that you provide clear, accurate, and timely documentation. Double-check that the evidence you provide is relevant to the chargeback and supports your explanation.
- **Q14: What should I do if my dispute is rejected twice?**

  - **A14:** If your dispute is rejected twice, the chargeback is no longer eligible for dispute. Carefully review the details of the rejection to understand why the dispute was unsuccessful and take corrective actions to prevent future chargebacks.
