## Producing a Forecast and Inventory Planning Report

**Who is this for :**Intended for individuals who are responsible for inventory management, demand forecasting, and supply chain planning within the organization. It is designed to guide them through the systematic process of generating a Forecast and Inventory Planning Report, ensuring consistency, accuracy, and informed decision-making across the organization's supply chain.

**Objective :**To outline the process for producing a comprehensive Forecast and Inventory Planning Report. This report aims to provide accurate and reliable forecasts for demand, as well as optimal inventory planning recommendations, to support effective decision-making and efficient supply chain management.

Utilizing Amazon's Forecasting and Inventory Planning Model can greatly assist you in forecasting and planning your inventory to supply Amazon and reduce the risk of being unable to supply certain ASINs. This report shows you what ASINs were ordered in the past and provides you with an overview of trends. You can then use this information to adjust your forecasting and planning model accordingly.

The Mean Forecast is Amazon's best estimate of customer demand for each ASIN, and the P70, P80, and P90 forecasts provide estimates of the probability that the customer demand will be at or below these values. Understanding this information can help you better plan your inventory for Amazon sales.

Amazon's Forecasting and Inventory Planning Model provides valuable insights into how consumers engage with your brand and individual ASINs. By utilizing this information, you can better plan your inventory holding for Amazon sales and avoid shortages or unfulfilled units at Amazon's Fulfillment Centers.

FAQs

**1. How often is Amazon’s forecasting data updated?**

- Amazon updates its forecasts regularly, but the exact timing may vary. Generally, the data refreshes weekly based on customer demand trends, order history, and inventory levels. It’s recommended to check the report frequently to stay up to date.

**2. How should I interpret P70, P80, and P90 forecasts in my inventory planning?**

- These probability-based forecasts help assess demand uncertainty:

  - P70 Forecast – 70% probability demand will be at or below this level (moderate risk).
  - P80 Forecast – 80% probability demand will be at or below this level (conservative estimate).
  - P90 Forecast – 90% probability demand will be at or below this level (low risk, high inventory buffer).

    - Use these to balance supply levels—P90 ensures you rarely run out of stock, but P70 may prevent overstocking.

**3. What should I do if my internal sales forecast differs from Amazon’s forecast?**

- If your internal projections suggest significantly different demand than Amazon’s forecast, consider:

  - Reviewing past discrepancies to identify patterns.
  - Factoring in promotions, seasonality, or external sales data Amazon may not account for.
  - Adjusting supply cautiously while monitoring Amazon’s demand changes over time.

**4. What should I do if an ASIN consistently shows low demand in the forecast?**

- If a product repeatedly appears with low predicted demand, evaluate:

  - Whether to discontinue the ASIN or lower stock levels.
  - Running targeted marketing campaigns or Amazon promotions to boost visibility.
  - Checking customer reviews and competitor pricing for possible demand shifts.

**5. How can I use this report to adjust my purchase orders?**

- Use the Mean Forecast and probability models to determine how much stock you should send to Amazon. If demand is increasing, prepare to increase order quantities. If demand is declining, avoid overstocking to prevent unnecessary storage fees.

**6. Does Amazon’s forecasting model consider seasonality and product launches?**

- Amazon’s model accounts for historical demand patterns, including seasonal trends. However, new product launches and sudden market shifts (e.g., viral trends) may not be fully reflected. Vendors should supplement Amazon’s data with their own insights.

**7. What should I do if my report is missing ASINs or data appears incomplete?**

- First, check that you’ve selected the correct reporting date range and filters. If data is still missing:

  - Verify that the ASIN has had sales activity in the past 13 weeks.
  - Ensure the ASIN is still active in your catalog.
  - Contact Amazon Vendor Support if the issue persists.
