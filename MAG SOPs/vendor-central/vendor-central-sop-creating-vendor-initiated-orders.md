# Vendor Central SOP: Creating Vendor Initiated Orders

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders

## Creating Vendor Initiated Orders

**Who is this for**:

- This is intended for vendors who have access to Vendor Central and have programs like Born To Run included in their accounts.
- It is relevant for vendors who wish to create their own orders or offers within Vendor Central and have control over the process.

**Objective**: To provide clear instructions on how to create Vendor-Initiated Orders or Offers using the Vendor Central platform.

*If your Vendor Central account includes programs like Born To Run, you can create your own Vendor-Initiated Orders or Offers.*

**Instructions:**

1. To create an offer, go to the "Orders" tab on the top menu of Vendor Central, select "Vendor-Initiated Orders" from the drop-down menu, and choose which program you want to create an offer for.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/9Ypimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/9Ypimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders-image1.png)

2. Click the "Go to the Program" link to go to the program page.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/7Hpimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/7Hpimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders-image2.png)

3. Click "Create a new offer."

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/77aimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/77aimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders-image3.png)
4. Enter the offer name and select your Vendor Code from the drop-down menu. The click the "Continue to next" button.
![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders-image4.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/75Vimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/75Vimage.png)

5. Choose what to do with your product if it doesn't sell out within the next ten weeks: either return it with shipping and handling fees charged or let Amazon keep the unsold inventory and charge a retention fee. Then click the "Continue to Next" button.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders-image5.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/azjimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/azjimage.png)

6. On the next page, enter the up to 10 ASINs separated by commas or spaces in the text box and click "Add Products".![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders-image6.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/vdXimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/vdXimage.png)

7. Type in the quantity for each ASIN in the relevant "Quantity Offered" text box. You can also remove any accidentally added ASINs by clicking the "Remove" button to the right of each one. Click the "Continue to Next" button when you're satisfied with your selection of ASINs and quantities.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/uvzimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/uvzimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders-image7.png)
8. Review your offer on the following page, select the box indicating that you accept the offer terms, and click the "Submit Offer" button.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/jZVimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/jZVimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-creating-vendor-initiated-orders-image8.png)

9. Your submitted offer will appear under the "Under Review" tab on the Program Page.

10. Once your offer is accepted, it will appear under the "Active Offers" tab.

11. When your offer finishes, either because it sold out or the ten days have expired, it will appear under the "Completed Offers" tab on the program page.

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What are Vendor-Initiated Orders?**

  - **A1:** Vendor-Initiated Orders are orders or offers created by vendors in Vendor Central to introduce or manage product inventory, often under programs like Born To Run.
- **Q2: Who is eligible to create Vendor-Initiated Orders?**

  - **A2:** Vendors with access to Vendor Central and specific programs like Born To Run can create Vendor-Initiated Orders.

Steps and Navigation

- **Q3: Where can I access the Vendor-Initiated Orders option?**

  - **A3:** Go to the "Orders" tab in the top menu of Vendor Central, select "Vendor-Initiated Orders" from the dropdown menu, and choose the program for which you want to create an offer.
- **Q4: How do I create a new offer?**

  - **A4:** Click "Create a new offer" on the program page, enter the offer name, select your Vendor Code, and follow the step-by-step instructions for adding ASINs and quantities.

Product Selection

- **Q5: How many ASINs can I add to a single offer?**

  - **A5:** You can add up to 10 ASINs to a single offer, separated by commas or spaces.
- **Q6: What happens if I accidentally add an incorrect ASIN?**

  - **A6:** You can remove incorrect ASINs by clicking the "Remove" button next to the ASIN on the product selection page.

Unsold Inventory

- **Q7: What are the options for unsold inventory after the offer ends?**

  - **A7:** You can either:

    - Return the unsold inventory with shipping and handling fees charged, or
    - Allow Amazon to retain the unsold inventory for a retention fee.
- **Q8: Can I change the option for unsold inventory after submitting the offer?**

  - **A8:** No, the decision made during offer creation (return or retain unsold inventory) cannot be changed after submission.

Offer Review and Status

- **Q9: How can I track the status of my offer?**

  - **A9:** You can track the status under the following tabs on the program page:

    - **Under Review:** For submitted offers awaiting approval.
    - **Active Offers:** For approved offers currently running.
    - **Completed Offers:** For offers that have finished, either due to sell-out or the ten-week timeframe ending.
- **Q10: Can I edit an offer after submission?**

  - **A10:** No, once an offer is submitted, it cannot be edited. You would need to cancel the current offer and create a new one.

Additional Considerations

- **Q11: What is the "Born To Run" program?**

  - **A11:** Born To Run is a program within Vendor Central that allows vendors to introduce new products or increase inventory levels with controlled risk by offering flexible terms for unsold inventory.
- **Q12: How long does an offer last?**

  - **A12:** Offers typically run for ten weeks, during which the products can sell out.
- **Q13: Can I create multiple offers at the same time?**

  - **A13:** Yes, you can create multiple offers as long as they adhere to the guidelines of the respective program and your Vendor Central account permissions.
