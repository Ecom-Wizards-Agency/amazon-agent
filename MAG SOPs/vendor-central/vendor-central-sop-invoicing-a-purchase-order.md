# Vendor Central SOP: Invoicing a Purchase Order

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-invoicing-a-purchase-order

## Invoicing a Purchase Order

**Who is this for :** Intended for individuals who are responsible for handling the invoicing process within an organization. It provides detailed instructions and guidelines for generating and submitting invoices for purchase orders, ensuring adherence to company policies and accounting standards.

**Objective :** To establish a standardized process for invoicing a purchase order, ensuring accuracy, timeliness, and consistency in the invoicing procedures. This SOP aims to facilitate prompt payment processing, minimize errors, and maintain a transparent record-keeping system for all purchase orders.

**Steps for Invoicing a Purchase Order:**

1. To invoice a Purchase Order, navigate to the “Invoices” option in the drop-down menu of the “Payments” item on your top menu within Vendor Central. Please see image below for your reference.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%201.28.06%E2%80%AFAM.png)

2. Click on the “Create Invoice” button on the following page.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%201.28.14%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/WEWimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/WEWimage.png)

3. Select the Purchase Order you wish to invoice, then go to the bottom of the table and click “Create Invoice”.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%201.28.22%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/A5Timage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/A5Timage.png)

4. On this page, enter the invoice number and date that aligns with your own system-generated invoice. Ensure that all items appearing on your invoice and your packing list for this Purchase Order are visible on this invoice in Vendor Central.

5. Verify that the applicable tracking number/PRO number appears in each line item, and that all unit costs match the unit cost from the original PO and your own system-generated invoice. Double-check that the total of the invoice you are about to submit matches the total of your system-generated invoice.

6. If any miscellaneous charges apply to this invoice, include them from the Drop-Down menu. After ensuring all details are correct, tick the acknowledgement box and click the “Submit” button.

FAQs

**1. How long does it take for an invoice to be processed and paid?**

- Amazon typically processes invoices within 30-45 days, depending on your payment terms. You can check the payment status in Vendor Central under Payments > Invoices.

**2. What should I do if my invoice is rejected or disputed?**

- If your invoice is rejected, review the error message in Vendor Central > Payments > Invoices. Common issues include:

  - Unit cost mismatch with the purchase order.
  - Incorrect tracking number or missing PRO number.
  - Invoice amount exceeding PO total.
- To resolve a dispute, correct the issue and resubmit the invoice. If you need assistance, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**3. Can I modify or cancel an invoice after submitting it?**

- No, once an invoice has been submitted, it cannot be modified or canceled in Vendor Central. If you made an error, you must submit a credit memo to adjust the incorrect invoice and create a new one with the correct details.

**4. Can I create multiple invoices for the same purchase order?**

- Amazon allows partial invoicing if the purchase order is split into multiple shipments. However, you cannot submit multiple invoices for the same shipped items. Each invoice must correspond to an actual shipment.

**5. What are the most common reasons an invoice gets rejected?**

- Unit prices don’t match the purchase order.
- Missing tracking or PRO number in the invoice.
- Over-invoicing (charging Amazon more than the agreed PO amount).
- Duplicate invoice submission for the same shipment.

**6. How can I check the status of my submitted invoice?**

- Go to Vendor Central > Payments > Invoices to check your invoice status. The most common statuses include:

  - Pending – Amazon is reviewing the invoice.
  - Approved – The invoice has been accepted for payment.
  - Paid – The payment has been processed.
  - Rejected – The invoice has been denied, and you need to correct errors.

**7. What should I do if my invoice is missing required details (tracking, unit costs, etc.)?**

- If key details are missing, do not submit the invoice until you gather the correct information. If you've already submitted it, wait for a rejection notice and then correct and resubmit.
