# Vendor Central SOP: Disputing Returns via the Dispute Management Page

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-disputing-returns-via-the-dispute-management-page

## Disputing Returns via the Dispute Management Page

**Who is this for:**

- This is intended for vendors or sellers who use Vendor Central on Amazon.
- This is relevant to users who believe that their items were wrongly returned by a Fulfillment Center and need to initiate a dispute to resolve the issue.

**Objective:** To guide users in effectively disputing returns through the Dispute Management feature on Vendor Central.

If you believe that your items were wrongly returned by a Fulfillment Center, you can dispute it through the Dispute Management feature.

1. Go to Vendor Central's top menu, select the "Payments" tab, and choose "Dispute Management" from the drop-down menu.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/x3nimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/x3nimage.png)

2. Click the "Create New Dispute" button on the next page.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1686273297019.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/IIgimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/IIgimage.png)

3. In the Pop-Up Window, select "Vendor Return" from the provided drop-down menu, enter the return ID(s) that you want to dispute, and click "Next."

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1686273784061.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wsaimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/wsaimage.png)

4. On the "Create New Dispute" page, fill out the following information:

- If you want to dispute a return from the ASIN level, choose "Dispute by ASIN." Enter the necessary information for the ASIN(s) you want to dispute and click "Save and continue."
- To edit any ASIN information, click "Update ASINs." To select the same dispute reason for multiple rows, choose the rows you want to edit, click "Bulk Edit," and choose the "Dispute reason."
- Enter a disputed title.
- Provide a dispute summary describing your reason for disputing.
- If desired, enter email addresses for other people in your organization who should be notified and included in correspondence regarding the dispute.
- Click "Browse" to upload any relevant documentation to support your dispute.
- Click "Submit" to submit your dispute, or "Save as draft" to save the dispute and complete it later.

5. Your Dispute ID will appear once you've submitted your dispute. Use this ID to search for your dispute. To ensure that Amazon can efficiently manage your returns and avoid any delays or potential deductions, wait until you receive a return authorization before attempting to return items to a fulfillment center.

FAQs

**1. Who can dispute a return on Vendor Central?**

- Only vendors using Vendor Central can dispute returns. If you're a third-party seller using Seller Central, you will need to follow a different dispute process.

**2. What types of returns can be disputed?**

- You can dispute returns that you believe were wrongly processed by an Amazon Fulfillment Center. This includes cases where items were returned in an unacceptable condition, the return reason is incorrect, or you were charged deductions unfairly.

**3. Where can I find the return ID for my dispute?**

- The Return ID can be found in Vendor Central under the Returns section or in your return-related transaction details.

**4. What documentation should I upload to support my dispute?**

- Amazon recommends including invoices, shipping records, product images, return reason explanations, and any correspondence that supports your claim. Ensure your documentation is clear and relevant to avoid delays.

**5. Can I dispute multiple ASINs in one request?**

- Yes, you can dispute multiple ASINs in a single dispute by selecting "Dispute by ASIN" and using the Bulk Edit option to apply the same dispute reason to multiple rows.

**6. What happens after I submit my dispute?**

- Once submitted, Amazon will generate a Dispute ID for tracking. You can monitor the dispute status in the Dispute Management section and will receive updates via email if additional information is required.

**7. How long does it take for Amazon to process a return dispute?**

- Processing times vary, but Amazon typically reviews disputes within a few weeks. If you haven't received a response, you can follow up using your Dispute ID in Vendor Central.

**8. What should I do if my dispute is rejected?**

- If your dispute is denied, review the rejection reason in Vendor Central. If you believe the decision was incorrect, you may submit additional documentation or contact Amazon support for clarification.

**9. Can I edit a dispute after submission?**

- No, once submitted, a dispute cannot be edited. However, you can withdraw the dispute and resubmit it with updated information if needed.

**10. What happens if I don’t receive a return authorization before sending items back?**

- Returning items without prior return authorization may result in additional fees, delays, or rejection of the return. Always wait for authorization before returning items to a fulfillment center.
