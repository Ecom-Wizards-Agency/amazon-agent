## Disputing a Chargeback Related to Packaging

**Who is this for**:

- This is intended for vendors who have access to Vendor Central and need to dispute a chargeback specifically related to packaging.
- It is relevant for vendors who have received chargebacks for non-compliance related to Bagging, Suffocation Warning, Bubble Wrap, Set Creation, Boxing, Taping, Opaque Covering, Apparel - Remove Hanger, Ready-to-Ship Packaging Chargeback, Dunnage Chargeback, Expired Product Chargeback, Overweight Carton Chargeback, and Oversized Carton Chargeback.

**Objective**: To provide vendors with clear instructions on how to dispute a chargeback related to packaging in Vendor Central.

**Additional Compliance Note:**

French Language Compliance for Quebec — Bill 96 Update (Effective June 1, 2025)

What are the new requirements?

- **Signage:**
  All signage (outside and inside) that shows business names or trademarks must be **exclusively or predominantly in French**.
- **Packaging:**
  Product packaging sold in Quebec must include **French translations of all generic terms and product characteristics**, even if these terms are part of a registered or pending trademark.
- **Trademark Status:**
  A trademark with a formal application at the Canadian Intellectual Property Office (CIPO) is treated as registered. If unregistered, French translations are mandatory.
- **Compliance Deadline:**
  Products manufactured before June 1, 2025, may be sold until June 1, 2027. After this, only packaging meeting French language requirements can be sold.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

The Office québécois de la langue française (OQLF) enforces these rules and can issue fines from **$3,000 up to $90,000 per day for repeated offenses**.

What You Need to Do

1. Review all your Quebec-targeted product packaging and signage for French language compliance.
2. Add French translations for all descriptive terms and product information on packaging.
3. Verify trademark registration/application status and translate if necessary.
4. Plan inventory and packaging updates to meet the June 1, 2027 deadline.
5. Consult with your legal or compliance team if you have questions.

**Note:**

- A chargeback can only be disputed twice. After two disputes, it is no longer eligible for dispute.
- Dispute a chargeback within 30 days of the date it was raised in Vendor Central.

**If you wish to dispute a chargeback related to packaging, please provide the following documentation:**

- Bagging Chargeback: *Provide one or more images showing the PO/Shipping label, the product, and evidence that the product was packaged and barcoded properly, so it did not require extra prep work upon arrival at Amazon’s fulfillment center.*
- Suffocation Warning Chargeback:*Provide one or more images showing the PO label, the product, and evidence that the product was packaged and barcoded properly, so it did not require extra prep work upon arrival at Amazon’s fulfillment center.*
- Bubble Wrap Chargeback: *Provide one or more images showing the PO/Shipping label, the product, and evidence that the product was packaged and barcoded properly, so it did not require extra prep work upon arrival at Amazon’s fulfillment center.*
- Set Creation Chargeback: *Provide one or more images showing the correct labels and that the product was shipped in the set quantity requested.*
- Boxing Chargeback:*Provide one or more images showing the PO label, the product, and evidence that the product was packaged and barcoded properly, so it did not require extra prep work upon arrival at Amazon’s fulfillment center.*
- Taping Chargeback: *Provide one or more images showing the PO/Shipping label, the product, and evidence that the product was packaged and barcoded properly, so it did not require extra prep work upon arrival at Amazon’s fulfillment center.*
- Opaque Covering Chargeback: *Provide one or more images showing the PO/Shipping label, the product, and evidence that the product was packaged and barcoded properly, so it did not require extra prep work upon arrival at Amazon’s fulfillment center.*
- Remove Hanger Chargeback:*Provide one or more images showing the purchase order and the product and evidence that the product was not placed on a hanger before shipping to Amazon.*
- Ready-to-Ship Packaging Chargeback: *Provide the following documentation: Completed ISTA6 Packaging Test Report and Completed Amazon ASIN Enrollment Document.*
- Dunnage Chargeback: *Provide image(s) of the PO label and dunnage (packaging materials) with one ASIN from the shipment showing that the box was shipped with Amazon-compliant packaging material.*
- Expired Product Chargeback: *Provide image(s) of the PO label and expiration date on the ASIN showing that the product was shipped with more than 90 days until the expiration date.*
- Overweight Carton Chargebacks: *Provide a summary of the contents of the carton(s) you shipped to Amazon along with the weight of each individual item as proof that the total carton weight is less than 50.0 pounds; or proof that the carton(s) you shipped to Amazon contained one sellable unit. We recommend that each carton does not weigh more than 45 pounds in total for safety purposes.*
- Oversized Carton Chargeback:*Provide a summary of the contents of the carton(s) you shipped to Amazon along with the dimensions of each individual item as proof that all of the items can fit in a carton not measuring more than 25 inches on any side; or proof that the sellable unit(s) within the carton(s) measured over 25 inches, which made an oversized carton necessary.*

FAQs

- **Q1: How do I initiate a dispute for a chargeback related to packaging?**

  - **A1:**To initiate a dispute, go to the "Reports" tab in Vendor Central, select "Operational Performance," and click on "View All." From there, scroll to the relevant chargeback related to packaging, select the chargeback(s) you want to dispute, and click the "Dispute" button.
- **Q2: Can I dispute any chargeback related to packaging?**

  - **A2:** No, only chargebacks related to specific packaging issues such as Bagging, Suffocation Warning, Bubble Wrap, Set Creation, Boxing, Taping, Opaque Covering, Apparel - Remove Hanger, Ready-to-Ship Packaging, Dunnage, Expired Product, Overweight Carton, and Oversized Carton can be disputed.
- **Q3: What documentation is needed to dispute a chargeback for packaging?**

  - **A3:**The required documentation depends on the specific chargeback type. For example, for Bagging Chargebacks, you need images showing the PO/Shipping label, the product, and evidence of proper packaging and barcoding.
- **Q4: Is there a deadline for disputing a chargeback?**

  - **A4:** Yes, chargebacks must be disputed within 30 days from the date it was raised in Vendor Central.
- **Q5: How many times can I dispute a chargeback?**

  - **A5:** A chargeback can only be disputed twice. After two disputes, it is no longer eligible for further dispute.
- **Q6: What happens if my dispute is rejected?**

  - **A6:** If your dispute is rejected, you will need to accept the chargeback. You may request additional support from Amazon if you believe the rejection was in error, but it is no longer eligible for further dispute.
- **Q7: How do I upload supporting documentation for my dispute?**

  - **A7:** To upload supporting documents, click on the "Add Attachments" button under the "Dispute Summary" text box and select the relevant files from your computer.
- **Q8: Can I dispute a chargeback if I already submitted the required documentation?**

  - **A8:**Yes, as long as the dispute is within the 30-day window and follows the correct process, you can dispute the chargeback even if you've already submitted documentation.
- **Q9: What if I do not have the required documentation for the dispute?**

  - **A9:** If you do not have the required documentation, you may not be able to successfully dispute the chargeback. Ensure you gather the necessary evidence before submitting your dispute.
