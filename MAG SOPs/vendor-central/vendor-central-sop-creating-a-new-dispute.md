# Vendor Central SOP: Creating a New Dispute

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-creating-a-new-dispute

## Creating a New Dispute

**Who is this for :** This is intended for users of the Vendor Central Dashboard who need to create a new dispute regarding Co-Op, Vendor Returns, or Shortage Invoice issues.

**Objectives:**

- To guide users through the process of creating a new dispute on the Vendor Central Dashboard.
- To provide step-by-step instructions on how to navigate the platform and submit the necessary information for a dispute.

**Steps for Creating a New Dispute**

1. To create a new dispute on Vendor Central Dashboard, navigate to the “Payments” tab on the top menu and select “Dispute Management” from the Drop-Down menu.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%2010.20.18%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/F93image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/F93image.png)

2. Click on the yellow “Create new dispute” button located at the top left side of the page under the heading “Need to Dispute something”.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%2010.20.26%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/E94image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/E94image.png)

3. A pop-up window will appear with a drop-down menu at the top, allowing you to select the issue you want to dispute: Co-Op, Vendor Returns, or Shortage Invoice.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%2010.20.33%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/tmaimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/tmaimage.png)

4. Enter all the relevant information pertaining to the issue you are disputing. For Co-Op Issues, enter the Co-Op invoice numbers you are querying; for Vendor Return Issues, enter the Return ID and the Return Invoice Number; and for Shortage Invoice Issues, enter the Invoice Numbers. 5. Once you have entered the required information, click the yellow “Next” button located at the bottom left of the pop-up window. Fill out all the relevant information on the next page, including uploading any supporting documentation. Finally, click “Submit” at the bottom left of the page.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%2010.20.43%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wVnimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/wVnimage.png)

6. It is crucial to ensure that you have sufficient documented evidence to dispute your claim, such as labels, calculations on spreadsheets, Bills of Lading, images of the products packed in their cartons, images of the cartons and their relevant labels, packing lists, scanned packing lists from your own systems, etc., before creating a dispute on Vendor Central. The more factual information you provide to support your dispute, the higher the chances of success.

FAQs

**1. Who can create a dispute on Vendor Central?**

- Disputes can be created by authorized Vendor Central users with the necessary account permissions. If you do not see the option to create a dispute, check with your account administrator to ensure you have the correct access.

**2. How long does it take for Amazon to respond to a dispute?**

- Amazon typically reviews disputes within 7–14 business days, but response times may vary depending on the complexity of the issue. You can track the status in the Dispute Management section.

**3. What happens after I submit a dispute?**

- Once submitted, your dispute will be assigned a case ID. You can monitor updates in the Dispute Management section under "Payments." If Amazon needs additional information, they may reach out via email or Vendor Central messages.

**4. Can I edit or cancel a dispute after submission?**

- No, disputes cannot be edited once submitted. If you need to make changes, you must wait for Amazon’s response or submit a new dispute with the correct details.

**5. What are common reasons disputes get rejected?**

- Disputes may be rejected due to missing documentation, incorrect invoice details, or failure to meet Amazon’s dispute guidelines. Ensure all required fields are filled out correctly and provide clear supporting documents.

**6. What types of documents should I submit, and in what format?**

- Accepted documents include Bills of Lading, packing lists, return invoices, images of packed products, and scanned spreadsheets with calculations. Files should be in PDF, JPEG, PNG, or CSV format and should not exceed 10MB per file.

**7. Can I submit multiple disputes at once?**

- No, each dispute must be submitted individually. However, you can track multiple disputes simultaneously in the Dispute Management section.

**8. What should I do if I don’t receive a response from Amazon?**

- If your dispute is not updated within 14 business days, you can follow up by responding within the dispute case or contacting Amazon support through Vendor Central > Help > Contact Us.

**9. How do I escalate a dispute if I disagree with Amazon’s resolution?**

- If you believe your dispute was unfairly denied, you can request a review by replying directly within the dispute case. Ensure you provide additional evidence to strengthen your claim.
