# Vendor Central SOP: Avoiding Provisions

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-avoiding-provisions

## Avoiding Provisions

**Who is this for :** This SOP is applicable to vendors and sellers who want to prevent financial provisions and ensure accurate accounting and billing practices within their interactions with Amazon.

**Objective :** To outline the steps vendors and sellers should take to avoid provisions for missing actuals and receivables in their interactions with Amazon.

1. To prevent Provisions for Missing Actuals, promptly submit the actual amount for returns in your Vendor Central Account. Go to the "Orders" tab on the top menu and select "Returns" from the drop-down menu. Authorize returns and enter their actual quantities and values.

2. Do this as soon as you receive notification of a return from Amazon.
3. Alternatively, check your Returns for Authorizations daily on Vendor Central.
4. Also, regularly monitor your Required Actions section on the Vendor Central Home Page or Invoices, particularly for "Credit Memo’s."

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-08-08%20at%202.55.55%E2%80%AFAM.png)

5. To avoid Provisions for Receivables, shorten your billing cycle with Amazon for your Agreement Deductions.
6. If you have a quarterly or annual agreement, reduce the cycle to monthly to avoid defaulting on a large payment.
7. Contact your Buyer or Vendor Manager to renegotiate the billing cycle for your standard Annual Agreements.
8. Remember, a Provision for Receivables only arises if you risk a negative balance, where what you owe Amazon exceeds what Amazon owes you. Be mindful of this situation and prevent it.

FAQs

**1. What does "Provisions for Missing Actuals" mean?**

- This refers to financial adjustments Amazon makes when a vendor does not submit the actual return amounts on time. If the actual amounts are missing, Amazon may apply estimated deductions, which could negatively impact your balance.

**2. How will I know when to authorize a return?**

- Amazon sends notifications via email and Vendor Central alerts when a return needs authorization. You can also check the "Returns" section under the "Orders" tab in Vendor Central daily.

**3. How long do I have to submit the actual return amounts before a provision is applied?**

- There is no fixed deadline, but it is best to enter actual amounts as soon as you receive a return notification. Delays can lead to Amazon applying estimated deductions, which may not match the actual return value.

**4. How can I track returns that need authorization if I miss the notification?**

- Go to Vendor Central > Orders > Returns and check the "Pending Action" section. You can also monitor your Required Actions on the Vendor Central home page for updates on returns and credit memos.

**5. What happens if a provision is applied to my account?**

- A provision may lead to a temporary negative balance, affecting your cash flow. If left unresolved, Amazon may offset future payments or take additional financial actions.

**6. Can I reverse or dispute a provision once it has been applied?**

- Yes, if you believe a provision was incorrectly applied, you can dispute it by submitting supporting documentation through Vendor Central > Help > Contact Us > Payments & Invoices.

**7. How can I request a shorter billing cycle for agreement deductions?**

- You need to contact your Buyer or Vendor Manager and formally request to switch from quarterly/annual billing to monthly. Approval depends on your contract terms with Amazon.

**8. Can I process multiple return authorizations or credit memos at once?**

- Yes, Amazon allows bulk processing of return authorizations and credit memos in Vendor Central. Look for bulk action options in the "Returns" and "Invoices" sections to save time.
