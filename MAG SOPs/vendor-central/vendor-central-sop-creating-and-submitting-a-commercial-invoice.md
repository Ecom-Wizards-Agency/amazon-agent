# Vendor Central SOP: Creating and Submitting a Commercial Invoice

## Creating and Submitting a Commercial Invoice

**Who is this for?:**

- This is intended for individuals responsible for managing international shipments and complying with customs requirements on the Vendor Central platform.
- This is crucial for individuals involved in shipping operations, including logistics or supply chain teams, who need to accurately generate commercial invoices for international shipments and ensure compliance with customs regulations.
- Relevant for individuals handling payment processing or those in contact with the **vc-commercialinvoice@amazon.com** email address for assistance related to commercial invoices.

**Objectives:**

- To provide instructions on creating and submitting a commercial invoice for shipping purposes.
- To ensure accurate and complete creation and submission of commercial invoices for international shipments.

**Instructions :**

Note that Commercial invoices are exclusively for shipping purposes and cannot be used to process payments. For payment processing, generate standard invoices on Vendor Central. For shipments to or from other countries, a commercial invoice must be created during the advance shipment notification (ASN) submission.

If you ship your products to or from another country, you must create a commercial invoice during the advance shipment notification (ASN) submission.

Once you have submitted your ASN via Orders > Shipments, you will be taken directly to the Commercial Invoice page.

To submit your commercial invoice:

1. Some details are pre-populated from the ASN that you just created. Review the details and if anything is incorrect, click ASN details to update the ASN.
2. Enter any required information that was not populated by the ASN.
3. Select Yes or No for Is this a Small Parcel shipment?
4. Select the Country of Manufacture (or Country of Origin). This is the country where the goods were manufactured. If you did not enter the Country of Origin when you originally added the product in Vendor Central, you will need to enter it manually on the commercial invoice each time you ship this product. To permanently update the country of manufacture, go to Items > Edit Products and ensure that you enter the correct Country of Origin.
5. Scroll down to the Shipper Certification section.
6. If your shipment contains wood packing materials, select the clarification statement that applies.
7. When finished, scroll to the top of the page and click Submit to submit the commercial invoice.
8. Click Print.
9. Sign the printed Shipper Certification and commercial invoice where noted and provide it to the driver when the shipment is picked up.

Note: If your PO terms are Collect, you are shipping internationally, and you cannot access the Commercial Invoice feature, email: **vc-commercialinvoice@amazon.com.**

After submission, print and sign the Shipper Certification and commercial invoice and give them to the driver when the shipment is picked up. If unable to access the Commercial Invoice feature and if PO terms are Collect and shipping internationally, email vc-commercialinvoice@amazon.com. It is advisable to send the Commercial Invoice to Amazon as a backup and avoid any chargebacks.

Update the commercial invoice every time you update the corresponding ASN for shipments to or from other countries.

1. To edit the commercial invoice, go to Shipments and click on one of the links beginning with "View shipments created in the last X days". 2. In "Completed shipments", find the shipment you need to edit. 3. Click the yellow "Actions" button to the right of the shipment and select "Edit commercial invoice". 4. Edit the information directly in the ASN if it's included in both the ASN and commercial invoice. 5. After editing the ASN, return to the Commercial Invoice page.

If the shipment has not been sent, print and sign the Shipper Certification and commercial invoice document and give it to the driver. Alternatively, email it to **vc-commercialinvoice@amazon.com.**
