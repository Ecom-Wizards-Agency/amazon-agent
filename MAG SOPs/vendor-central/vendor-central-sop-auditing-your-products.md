# Vendor Central SOP: Auditing Your Products

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-auditing-your-products

## Auditing Your Products

**Who is this for:** This is designed for vendors or sellers who have a business relationship with Amazon and want to conduct an audit of their product catalog to maximize their sales potential.

**Objective:** To provide step-by-step instructions on accessing the catalog page, selecting products for the audit, downloading the catalog listing, initiating the audit process, and uploading the saved file for analysis.

**Instructions:**

- Amazon has introduced a new feature on Vendor Central that allows you to audit your product catalog to increase your sales potential. The Selection Assistant tool compares a list of product identifiers you provide with your current offers on Amazon and provides recommendations to enhance your sales.
- Amazon's team of catalog experts will analyze your provided list and suggest specific actions to take. Regular and active management of your catalog is crucial for increasing your revenue on Amazon. The Selection Assistant simplifies the task of optimizing your product listings by breaking it down into smaller steps, allowing you to take effective action even with limited time.

1. Before starting the audit process, know the products you need to audit, you can visit your catalog page on Vendor Central by selecting "Catalog" from the "Items" to get the ASIN or product ID of listings that need to audit.

- The file must contain one of the following product IDs: EAN, UPC, GTIN, ASIN, or JAN and the Item Name
- Products to add: Include both new products and products that you added before.

FAQs

**1. How often should I audit my product catalog?**

- It’s recommended to audit your catalog at least once per quarter to ensure product listings are optimized, pricing is competitive, and inventory levels are updated. You can also conduct audits before major sales events like Prime Day or the holiday season.

**2. What kind of recommendations does Amazon provide after the audit?**

- Amazon’s Selection Assistant may suggest:

  - Adding missing attributes (e.g., product descriptions, images, keywords).
  - Adjusting pricing to stay competitive.
  - Improving discoverability by optimizing titles and bullet points.
  - Adding variations (e.g., different colors or sizes) to increase sales.

**3. What should I do if an ASIN is missing from the audit results?**

- If an ASIN does not appear in the audit results:

  - Ensure it was correctly entered in the template (check spelling and format).
  - Confirm that the ASIN is active and available in Vendor Central.
  - Try resubmitting the ASIN or contacting Amazon Vendor Support for assistance.

**4. How long does it take for Amazon to analyze my product list?**

- Amazon typically processes product audit requests within 5-7 business days, but processing times may vary depending on volume and demand.

**5. What should I do if I cannot upload the audit file?**

- If you experience issues uploading your audit file:

  - Ensure the file format matches Amazon’s template requirements (CSV or XLS).
  - Check for missing or incorrect product IDs (EAN, UPC, GTIN, ASIN, or JAN).
  - Clear your browser cache or try using a different browser.
  - If the issue persists, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**6. Can I resubmit a product if Amazon doesn’t provide recommendations?**

- Yes, you can resubmit products that didn’t receive recommendations. Amazon’s database updates regularly, so new insights may be available over time.

**7. Should I use the audit tool alongside other optimization tools?**

- Yes! For the best results, combine the Selection Assistant audit with:

  - A+ Content and Brand Story for enhanced visuals and descriptions.
  - SEO optimization to improve discoverability.
  - Advertising campaigns to increase visibility and sales.
