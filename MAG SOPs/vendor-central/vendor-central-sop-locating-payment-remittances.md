# Vendor Central SOP: Locating Payment Remittances

## Locating Payment Remittances

**Who is this for :** For individuals who receive payments and need to track and reconcile the corresponding remittances. This SOP is intended to be used by employees responsible for payment processing, remittance retrieval, and financial reconciliation tasks.

**Objective :** To establish a standardized process for efficiently and accurately identifying and retrieving payment remittances within a given system or database. The SOP provides a step-by-step guide to ensure consistent and streamlined procedures for locating payment remittances, facilitating timely and accurate financial reporting. Remittances are typically sent to you via email, but you can also access them in your payment report.

**Steps for Locating Payment Remittances:**

1. From Amazon Vendor Central homepage, go to “Payments” tab and select “Remittance” from the drop down menu.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%202.07.17%E2%80%AFAM.png)

2. You will be directed to a page displaying a table of your latest payments from Amazon. To view the line items on a specific Remittance, click on the “Payment Number” associated with that Remittance.

FAQs

**1. How often does Amazon generate payment remittances?**

- Amazon issues remittances based on your payment terms, which may be weekly, biweekly, or monthly. Each remittance reflects payments processed during that period.

**2. How long does Amazon retain remittance records?**

- Amazon typically keeps remittance records for 24 months. If you need older records, it’s best to download reports periodically or request archived data from Amazon Vendor Support.

**3. What should I do if a remittance is missing?**

- If a remittance isn’t available:

  - Check Vendor Central > Payments > Remittance to see if it has been generated.
  - Verify that your invoice was successfully processed and not rejected.
  - Review your email inbox, as Amazon may have sent the remittance via email.
  - If the remittance is still missing, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**4. What should I do if I can’t access or download remittance reports?**

- If you experience issues:

  - Clear your browser cache and refresh Vendor Central.
  - Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge).
  - Ensure pop-ups are enabled if the download button isn’t working.
  - Download a smaller date range if the file size is too large.
  - If the issue persists, submit a ticket to Amazon Vendor Support.

**5. How do I match a remittance to a bank deposit?**

- To verify a payment:

  - Locate the Payment Number (Remittance Number) in Vendor Central.
  - Compare the deposit amount in your bank account.
  - Check for deductions (e.g., shortages, chargebacks) that might affect the final payment amount.
- If discrepancies exist, check the remittance breakdown in Vendor Central.

**6. Why is my remittance amount lower than expected?**

- Amazon may apply deductions before issuing payments. Common deductions include:

  - Shortage claims (SC) – Items Amazon claims were missing from your shipment.
  - Chargeback deductions (VCBSINV) – Penalties for non-compliance with Amazon’s policies.
  - Price protection adjustments (IPAPUS codes) – Adjustments due to pricing agreements.

    - To dispute incorrect deductions, submit a case through Vendor Central > Payments > Disputes & Chargebacks.

**7. Can a single remittance cover multiple invoices?**

- Yes, Amazon often consolidates multiple invoices into one remittance. You can check the line items within the remittance to see which invoices were included in that payment.
