## Registering Your Products for Amazon Packaging Programs

**Who is this for :**Intended for individuals who sell products on Amazon's platform and are required to register their products for Amazon Packaging Programs. It is specifically designed for sellers who want to ensure their packaging complies with Amazon's guidelines and standards to optimize customer experience and mitigate any potential issues related to packaging.

**Objective :** To provide detailed guidelines on registering your products for Amazon Packaging Programs. This SOP aims to ensure that the packaging of your products meets Amazon's requirements and standards, thereby enhancing customer satisfaction, reducing product damage during shipping, and complying with environmental regulations.

- Please note that packaging certification programs are optional, but if you wish to enroll in one or if your products require shipping in larger packaging, follow the steps below. To enroll in Amazon's Frustration-Free Packaging Program, Vendors must have their products tested by an ISTA-certified third-party lab and obtain an ISTA6 Packaging Test Report. Vendors must complete the Amazon Vendor Enrollment Template and submit it along with the ISTA6 Packaging Test Report for review and approval by Amazon.
- The ISTA website or Amazon Packaging Support and Supplier Network (APASS) can provide a list of labs for testing. For each primary ASIN, vendors must submit an ISTA6 Packaging Test Report using the approved Amazon template and a completed Amazon Vendor Enrollment Template.
- If products are similar, vendors may send only one ASIN for testing. Certain ASIN classifications are currently unaffected, including ASINs with any Hazmat classifications, ASINs in Prime Pantry, Amazon Fresh, or media, ASINs below minimum dimensions required for certification, ASINs at least eight months old and shipped fewer than 250 units in the last twelve months, and ASINs packaged for shipment to Amazon in an efficient orientation.

**Additional Compliance Note:**

French Language Compliance for Quebec — Bill 96 Update (Effective June 1, 2025)

What are the new requirements?

- **Signage:**
  All signage (outside and inside) that shows business names or trademarks must be **exclusively or predominantly in French**.
- **Packaging:**
  Product packaging sold in Quebec must include **French translations of all generic terms and product characteristics**, even if these terms are part of a registered or pending trademark.
- **Trademark Status:**
  A trademark with a formal application at the Canadian Intellectual Property Office (CIPO) is treated as registered. If unregistered, French translations are mandatory.
- **Compliance Deadline:**
  Products manufactured before June 1, 2025, may be sold until June 1, 2027. After this, only packaging meeting French language requirements can be sold.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

The Office québécois de la langue française (OQLF) enforces these rules and can issue fines from **$3,000 up to $90,000 per day for repeated offenses**.

What You Need to Do

1. Review all your Quebec-targeted product packaging and signage for French language compliance.
2. Add French translations for all descriptive terms and product information on packaging.
3. Verify trademark registration/application status and translate if necessary.
4. Plan inventory and packaging updates to meet the June 1, 2027 deadline.
5. Consult with your legal or compliance team if you have questions.

FAQs

**1. Is enrollment in Amazon’s packaging programs mandatory for all vendors?**

- No, packaging certification programs are optional for most sellers. However, some product categories, such as heavy or oversized items, may require compliance with specific packaging standards to reduce damage rates and meet Amazon’s fulfillment guidelines.

**2. How long does the packaging certification process take?**

- The timeline varies, but it generally takes 2-4 weeks for Amazon to review and approve packaging certification requests. If additional testing or revisions are needed, the process may take longer.

**3. What happens if my product fails the ISTA6 Packaging Test?**

- If your product does not pass the test, you will need to redesign your packaging to meet Amazon’s requirements and re-submit it for testing. Amazon may provide recommendations for necessary improvements.

**4. Do I need to re-certify ASINs that were previously approved?**

- Amazon does not require re-certification unless:

  - The packaging has changed significantly.
  - Amazon updates its packaging standards.
  - The ASIN is flagged for packaging-related customer complaints or high return rates.

**5. What are the consequences of not complying with Amazon’s packaging standards?**

- Failure to comply may result in:

  - Increased shipping costs due to inefficient packaging.
  - Higher damage rates, leading to negative customer reviews and potential account warnings.
  - Restrictions on certain fulfillment programs, such as Frustration-Free Packaging (FFP) or Ships in Own Container (SIOC).

**6. Does the same certification apply across different fulfillment methods (FBA, SFP, Direct Fulfillment)?**

- No, certification requirements may differ depending on fulfillment method. FBA shipments require packaging that minimizes warehouse handling damage, while Direct Fulfillment may have different packaging considerations. Always verify Amazon’s requirements for each fulfillment type.

**7. What should I do if I can’t find the enrollment section in Vendor Central?**

- Try clearing your browser cache or using a different browser. If the issue persists, file a support ticket in Vendor Central under “Amazon Packaging Certification” to request assistance.
