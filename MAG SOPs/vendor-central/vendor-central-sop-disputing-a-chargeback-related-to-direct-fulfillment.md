## Disputing a Chargeback Related to Direct Fulfillment

**Who is this for**:

- This is intended for sellers or vendors using Amazon's Vendor Central platform who need to dispute chargebacks related to direct fulfillment.
- It is relevant for individuals responsible for managing operational performance and chargeback disputes on Vendor Central.

**Objective**:

- To provide step-by-step guidance on how to dispute a chargeback related to direct fulfillment on Vendor Central.
- Ensure that all necessary information and documentation are provided to dispute the chargeback effectively.

**INSTRUCTIONS:**

1. Go to the "Reports" tab on the top Menu of Vendor Central and select "Operational Performance" from the Drop-Down Menu

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/6N5image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/6N5image.png)

2. Look for the Direct Fulfillment non-compliance you want to dispute on the Scrollable bar on your Dashboard, which includes:

- Cancellation Rate;
- Ship Method Mismatch

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/78yimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/78yimage.png)

3. Click on the non-compliance total penalty fees to the right of the graph, which is a clickable link on the chargeback fees themselves under the title "Chargebacks".

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vc-sop-disputing-a-chargeback-related-to-direct-fulfillment-image-3.png)

4. On the next page, select the Chargeback or Chargebacks you want to dispute, and click "Dispute Chargeback".

**NOTE to the following:**

- You can only submit a dispute for the same Chargeback twice. If a Chargeback has been disputed and rejected twice, it is no longer eligible for dispute.
- You have 30 days from the date the Chargeback was raised in Vendor Central to dispute it.

**For Cancellation Rate Chargebacks, the following documentation is required:**

- Provide justification for rejecting the customer's order, along with supporting documents such as a case number.

**For Ship Method Mismatch Chargebacks, the following documentation is required:**

- Provide justification for the change of shipping method, along with supporting documents such as the ASIN's actual dimension and weight or product restrictions.

FAQs

- **Q1: What is a chargeback related to direct fulfillment?**

  - **A1:** A chargeback related to direct fulfillment is a penalty imposed by Amazon for non-compliance with operational performance standards. Common types include cancellation rate and ship method mismatch chargebacks.
- **Q2: Where can I find details about a specific chargeback in Vendor Central?**

  - **A2:** Go to the "Reports" tab in Vendor Central, select "Operational Performance," and locate the chargeback under the appropriate non-compliance category (e.g., cancellation rate or ship method mismatch).
- **Q3: What should I include in the "Dispute Summary" when disputing a chargeback?**

  - **A3:** Clearly explain why you disagree with Amazon’s assessment of the chargeback. Provide detailed reasoning and refer to supporting documentation to justify your dispute.
- **Q4: What kind of documentation is required for disputing a cancellation rate chargeback?**

  - **A4:** You need to provide justification for rejecting the customer's order, along with supporting documents such as a case number or any other relevant evidence.
- **Q5: What kind of documentation is required for disputing a ship method mismatch chargeback?**

  - **A5:**You must provide justification for the shipping method change. Include supporting documents such as the ASIN's actual dimensions, weight, or product restrictions.
- **Q6: How many times can I dispute the same chargeback?**

  - **A6:** You can dispute the same chargeback twice. If it is rejected both times, it becomes ineligible for further dispute.
- **Q7: What is the deadline to dispute a chargeback?**

  - **A7:**You have 30 days from the date the chargeback was raised in Vendor Central to submit a dispute.
- **Q8: What happens if I miss the 30-day dispute window?**

  - **A8:** If the dispute is not submitted within 30 days, the chargeback is considered final, and no further action can be taken to dispute it.
- **Q9: Where can I upload supporting documents for a chargeback dispute?**

  - **A9:**Upload the documents in the "Add Attachments" section below the "Dispute Summary" text box during the dispute submission process.
- **Q10: What happens after I submit a chargeback dispute?**

  - **A10:** Once submitted, Amazon will review your dispute and supporting documentation. You will receive a decision on whether the chargeback is upheld or reversed.
