## Producing a Chargebacks Report

**Who is this for :** Intended for individuals who are responsible for generating chargebacks reports within an organization. It can be used by financial analysts, accounting personnel, or individuals in similar roles who are tasked with tracking, analyzing, and reporting chargeback activities.

**Objective :** To outline a systematic and efficient process for generating a comprehensive report that analyzes chargebacks within a specified timeframe. This SOP aims to provide clear guidelines on data collection, analysis, and reporting, ensuring accuracy and consistency in chargeback reporting.

6. To make the most of the report after downloading, follow these steps:

- Review the chargebacks issued and the reasons behind them
- Identify which Purchase Orders are affected
- Determine the penalty amount
- Understand the Defect or Non-Compliance

7. Armed with this information, investigate the chargeback, gather relevant information, and return to the page to submit a dispute. Manage your reconciliations by using the report to track chargebacks deducted from your Amazon payments, even if disputes are pending review.

8. Monitor the success and reversal of your disputes. This report is a valuable tool for tracking all non-compliances, whether charged or waived, disputed or not, successful or rejected, and whether penalties have been deducted and subsequently reversed.

FAQs

**1. How often does Amazon update chargeback reports?**

- Chargeback reports typically update daily, but some deductions may take up to 30 days to appear. If you don’t see a recent chargeback, check back later or confirm with Amazon Vendor Support.

**2. How long does Amazon retain chargeback data?**

- Amazon usually keeps chargeback records for 24 months. If you need older data, download reports regularly or request archived records from Vendor Support.

**3. What are the most common reasons for chargebacks?**

- Chargebacks usually occur due to:

  - Late shipments – Orders shipped after Amazon’s expected fulfillment window.
  - Labeling errors – Missing or incorrect barcodes or carton labels.
  - Missing or incorrect ASNs (Advance Shipment Notifications) – Shipments that arrive without a matching ASN.
  - Packaging issues – Improperly packed shipments that don’t meet Amazon’s packaging guidelines.

**4. How can I prevent chargebacks in the future?**

- To reduce chargebacks:

  - Follow Amazon’s shipping and labeling guidelines.
  - Ensure ASNs are submitted correctly and on time.
  - Double-check packing requirements before shipment.
  - Use Amazon’s Compliance Reports to track and fix recurring issues.

**5. How long does Amazon take to review chargeback disputes?**

- Chargeback disputes typically take 30-60 days to process. You can monitor the dispute’s status in Vendor Central > Payments > Disputes & Chargebacks.

**6. What should I do if I can’t access or export chargeback reports?**

- If you’re experiencing issues:

  - Clear your browser cache and refresh the page.
  - Try using a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge).
  - Ensure pop-ups are enabled if the download button isn’t working.
  - Try downloading a smaller date range to reduce file size.
  - If the issue persists, submit a support ticket to Amazon Vendor Support.

**7. How do disputed chargebacks appear in future reports?**

- Successful dispute reversals will be reflected in the "Waived" or "Reversed" columns of your chargeback report. Pending disputes may still show as active deductions until fully processed.
