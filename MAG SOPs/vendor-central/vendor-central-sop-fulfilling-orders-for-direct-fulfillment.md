## Fulfilling Orders for Direct Fulfillment

**Who is this for:**

- This is intended for vendors or sellers who participate in Amazon's Direct Fulfillment program.
- Relevant to vendors who receive customer orders for products designated for Direct Fulfillment.

**Objective:** To guide vendors in correctly fulfilling orders through the Direct Fulfillment program to ensure smooth and efficient order processing.

Once an Amazon customer orders a product that you've designated for Direct Fulfillment, you'll receive a notification from Amazon and a prepaid shipping label to ship the item directly to the customer. It is vital to follow the correct procedure to avoid any Chargebacks associated with fulfilling Direct Fulfillment Orders. If you're unable to fulfill the entire order, cancel the order, as partially filled orders are not allowed.

1. To fulfill a Direct Fulfillment order, select **"Direct Fulfillment Orders"** from the menu under the **"Orders"** tab in the navigation section of Vendor Central.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-2QZSTRK9.png)

2. Select the order on the next page, and print the packing slip and/or gift message (if applicable).

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-2Z0RTAOK.png)

3. Package your product appropriately for shipment, in line with the Direct Fulfillment Program Policies, to ensure it arrives safely to the Amazon customer.

4. You can adjust the number of packages assigned to you by Amazon. Go to the Order details > Order summary > Shipment configuration section by clicking Edit. Depending on the size of your product, you may want to ship in multiple packages or combine multiple products into a single package.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-8HLPD7XF.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/IQ5image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/IQ5image.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712241140817.png)

5. Print the shipping label and attach it to the shipment. If Amazon provides the shipping label, click “Print shipping label,” but if you use your shipping labels, provide Amazon with the tracking ID. Contact the carrier to arrange for the package to be picked up. After the carrier picks up the order, click “Confirm shipment.”

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712241144355.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/YHgimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/YHgimage.png)

6. Confirming shipment before the carrier picks up the order can negatively impact your Direct Fulfillment metrics, so wait until the carrier has left your warehouse with the shipment before confirming the shipment.

FAQs

**1. What should I do if an item is out of stock after I receive the order?**

- If you cannot fulfill the order in full, you must cancel it immediately through Vendor Central. Partially fulfilled orders are not allowed. Repeated cancellations may impact your Direct Fulfillment metrics and eligibility.

**2. What should I do if I can’t print the shipping label or the label is incorrect?**

- First, ensure your printer is functioning correctly. If the issue persists, try downloading the label again. If the label is incorrect (wrong address, missing information, etc.), contact Amazon Vendor Support immediately. Do not ship the order until you receive the correct label.

**3. Are there specific packaging requirements for Direct Fulfillment orders?**

- Yes, all orders must comply with Amazon’s Direct Fulfillment Packaging Guidelines. Use sturdy, damage-resistant packaging, avoid excessive void fill, and ensure that fragile items are adequately cushioned. Do not include any promotional material, price tags, or invoices inside the package.

**4. What happens if the package is lost or damaged during transit?**

- If the carrier loses or damages the package, submit a claim with the carrier and notify Amazon immediately through Vendor Central. You may be required to provide proof of shipment and other documentation. Failure to address lost or damaged shipments can result in chargebacks.

**5. What are the penalties for incorrect fulfillment or early shipment confirmation?**

- If you confirm shipment before the carrier picks up the order, it can negatively impact your metrics and lead to chargebacks. Similarly, incorrect tracking information or non-compliance with Amazon’s Direct Fulfillment policies may result in account warnings, suspension, or financial penalties.

**6. What should I do if the carrier does not pick up my package?**

- Contact the assigned carrier to reschedule the pickup. If delays persist, update the tracking information accordingly and notify Amazon. Do not confirm shipment until the package has been collected.

**7. Can I request an extension if I need more time to fulfill an order?**

- Amazon expects vendors to fulfill orders within the designated timeframe. If you anticipate delays, contact Amazon Vendor Support as soon as possible to discuss possible solutions. Repeated delays may affect your Direct Fulfillment eligibility.

**8. When and how should I update tracking information?**

- If you are using Amazon’s prepaid shipping label, tracking updates are automatic. If using your own carrier, update the tracking ID in Vendor Central immediately after the package is picked up. Ensure tracking details are correct to avoid chargebacks.
