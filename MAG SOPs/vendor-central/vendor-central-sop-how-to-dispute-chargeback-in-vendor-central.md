# Vendor Central SOP: How to Dispute Chargeback in Vendor Central

## How to Dispute Chargeback in Vendor Central

**Who is this for**:

- This is intended for vendors who have access to Vendor Central and need to dispute a chargeback.
- It is relevant for vendors who have received a chargeback for non-compliance and want to challenge its validity by providing evidence and documentation.

**Objective**:

- To provide clear instructions on how to dispute a chargeback in Vendor Central.
- To enable vendors to effectively navigate the process of disputing chargebacks by accessing the necessary reports, identifying the relevant chargebacks, providing required information and evidence, and submitting a dispute for review and resolution.

1. Go to Vendor Central and click on the "Reports" tab from the top menu. From the drop-down menu, select "Operational Performance."

To locate the Chargeback you wish to dispute, sort and filter the Chargebacks as needed using the options available on the left side of the page.![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-how-to-dispute-chargeback-in-vendor-central-image3.png)

4. Select the Chargeback or Chargebacks if there are multiples of the same Non-Compliance for the same ASIN, and then click on "Dispute" button.![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/vendor-central-sop-how-to-dispute-chargeback-in-vendor-central-image4.png)

5. Provide all necessary information and upload evidence in the form of images or documentation. Once you have completed everything, click on the "Submit Dispute" button.

**Note:**

- You can only dispute the same Chargeback twice. If your dispute has been rejected twice, you can no longer dispute it.
- It is important to carefully read the details of the Non-compliance to ensure that you submit the appropriate documentation and evidence to refute the claim.
- A successful dispute depends on timely submission, factual evidence, and supporting documents or images.

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What is a chargeback in Vendor Central?**

  - **A1:** A chargeback is a penalty issued by Amazon for non-compliance with operational or logistical requirements, such as shipping or labeling errors.
- **Q2: Who can dispute a chargeback?**

  - **A2:** Vendors with access to Vendor Central who receive chargebacks for non-compliance are eligible to dispute them if they believe the chargeback is invalid.

Steps and Navigation

- **Q3: Where can I find the chargeback section in Vendor Central?**

  - **A3:** Go to the "Reports" tab in the top menu, select "Operational Performance," and then click "Total Chargebacks."
- **Q4: How can I locate a specific chargeback to dispute?**

  - **A4:** Use the sort and filter options on the left side of the "Total Chargebacks" page to locate the chargeback(s) you wish to dispute.

Dispute Process

- **Q5: What information is required to submit a chargeback dispute?**

  - **A5:** You need to provide details about the chargeback, upload evidence such as documents or images, and include any relevant information to refute the claim.
- **Q6: Can I dispute multiple chargebacks at the same time?**

  - **A6:** Yes, you can select multiple chargebacks for the same type of non-compliance and dispute them together if applicable.

Submission and Review

- **Q7: How many times can I dispute a chargeback?**

  - **A7:** You can dispute the same chargeback twice. If both disputes are rejected, further disputes for the same chargeback will not be allowed.
- **Q8: What happens after I submit a dispute?**

  - **A8:** Amazon reviews the dispute along with the evidence provided. If the dispute is validated, the chargeback will be reversed. If not, the chargeback will remain, and a reason for rejection will be provided.

Supporting Documentation

- **Q9: What types of evidence should I provide when disputing a chargeback?**

  - **A9:** Evidence may include shipment tracking details, compliance documentation, images of labels, or any other supporting material that demonstrates compliance.
- **Q10: Can I submit a dispute without documentation?**

  - **A10:** No, disputes must include sufficient evidence and documentation to support your claim. Without this, the dispute is likely to be rejected.

Best Practices

- **Q11: How can I ensure my dispute is successful?**

  - **A11:** Ensure that your submission includes:

    - Timely submission of the dispute.
    - Factual and detailed evidence.
    - Supporting documents or images relevant to the non-compliance.
- **Q12: What should I do if my dispute is rejected twice?**

  - **A12:** If your dispute is rejected twice, you cannot challenge the chargeback further. Review the rejection reasons and ensure compliance to avoid future chargebacks.

Timeliness and Limits

- **Q13: Is there a time limit to dispute a chargeback?**

  - **A13:** Yes, disputes must be submitted within the timeframe specified in the chargeback details. Late submissions are not accepted.
- **Q14: Can I request a review of a rejected dispute outside of the dispute system?**

  - **A14:** No, Amazon’s system only allows two disputes per chargeback. Additional reviews are not possible outside the designated dispute process.
