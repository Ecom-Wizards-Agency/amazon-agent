## **Submitting Catalog Content Through Your Catalog Feed**

**Who is this for :** Submitting Amazon catalog content through a catalog feed can be done by the seller or the seller's designated team. As an Amazon seller, you have the responsibility to manage and update your product listings.

**Objective :** This process refers to uploading and synchronizing product information and data to an online platform or marketplace using a catalog feed. The objective is to ensure accurate and up-to-date product information is available to potential customers and to maintain consistency across various channels.

**Instructions:**

Provide your new or updated product content to the CSP. The CSP's software will consume your data, transform it into AFS format, and validate it. In case of validation failure, collaborate with the CSP to correct and resubmit the data. Once validation is successful, you can publish the content to Amazon using CSP's software.

When the processing is complete, your CSP will receive a notification that includes both success and failure details. If the processing is successful, your data will be available on Amazon. If there is a processing error, check the error details and suggested the next steps in the error notification.

You can also access the same information under "Submission Status" on the "Manage Catalog Feeds" page.

FAQs

**1. Do I have to use a Catalog Service Provider (CSP) to submit catalog content?**

- No, using a CSP is optional. You can submit catalog content directly through Vendor Central > Manage Catalog Feeds, but CSPs can help automate and manage large-scale submissions.

**2. What file formats and sizes does Amazon accept for catalog content feeds?**

- Amazon accepts CSV, XML, and JSON formats. The maximum file size for feed uploads varies, but it's recommended to keep files under 50MB for smooth processing.

**3. How long does it take for Amazon to process catalog feed submissions?**

- Processing times vary but typically range from a few hours to 48 hours. You can check the status under Manage Catalog Feeds > Track Submitted Feeds.

**4. What should I do if my catalog feed submission fails?**

- Go to Manage Catalog Feeds > Submission Status and review the error details.
- Correct any formatting issues, missing attributes, or validation failures.
- Resubmit the corrected file or work with your CSP to troubleshoot.

**5. If my feed has errors, will it update some products and reject others?**

- Yes, Amazon processes valid data while rejecting incorrect entries. Any errors will be flagged, and only the failed items will need correction and resubmission.

**6. What should I do if my feed submission remains in processing for too long?**

- If your feed is stuck in processing for more than 48 hours, contact Amazon Vendor Support through Help > Contact Us > Catalog Feeds & Product Data Issues and provide your submission ID.

**7. Can I update multiple products in a single feed submission?**

- Yes, you can submit bulk updates for multiple products in a single feed file, as long as they follow Amazon’s Amazon Feed Specification (AFS) format.

**8. Will my catalog feed updates override manual changes made in Vendor Central?**

- Yes, a new catalog feed submission will overwrite any manually updated content in Vendor Central. If you need to retain manual changes, do not resubmit those specific attributes.
