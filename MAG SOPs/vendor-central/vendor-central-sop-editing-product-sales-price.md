# Vendor Central SOP: Editing Product Sales Price

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-editing-product-sales-price

## Editing Product Sales Price

**Who is this for :** This is designed for individuals or teams responsible for managing product listings and pricing on Amazon Vendor Central.

**Objectives :**

- To guide users through the process of editing the MSRP or List Price of products on Amazon Vendor Central.
- To ensure accuracy and efficiency in updating product pricing on the Amazon platform.

Manufacturer Suggested Retail Price refers to the price a brand believes one of its products should sell for. It aims to coordinate sale prices across online and offline retailers.

Before editing product sales price, it is important to communicate, and get client’s approval first. Include the approval proof (screenshot or fireflies call link) as Task Details or as a Comment.

**Instructions:**

1. To edit the MSRP or List Price of your products on Amazon, access your Vendor Central Dashboard and go to the “Items” menu. And select “Catalog” from the Drop-Down Menu.

FAQs

**1. How long does it take for a price update to reflect on Amazon?**

- Price updates typically go live within 24-48 hours, but in some cases, it may take up to 72 hours. You can check the status under Catalog > Product Details.

**2. Can Amazon override or reject my price update?**

- Yes, Amazon has an algorithm that adjusts prices based on competitive market conditions. Even if you update the MSRP/List Price, Amazon may offer the product at a lower or higher price.

**3. What should I do if my bulk price update fails?**

- If your spreadsheet upload fails:

  - Check the error message in the "Spreadsheet Upload Status" tab.
  - Ensure the file format follows Amazon's bulk update template.
  - Verify that all required fields (ASIN, SKU, new price) are correctly filled.
  - If the issue persists, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**4. How can I verify that my price change was successfully applied?**

- To confirm your price update:

  - Go to Catalog > Manage Products and check the product details.
  - Search for your product on Amazon’s marketplace to see the listed price.
  - If the update is missing, allow 24-48 hours or check the upload status for errors.

**5. Will price changes affect existing purchase orders?**

- No, purchase orders are not retroactively adjusted to the new price. The price at the time Amazon placed the order remains in effect. Future orders will reflect the updated price.

**6. What’s the difference between MSRP/List Price and the actual selling price?**

- MSRP (Manufacturer Suggested Retail Price) – The price you recommend to Amazon.
- Amazon’s Selling Price – Determined by Amazon based on competition, demand, and other market conditions.
- Even if you set an MSRP, Amazon may choose to sell the product at a different price.

**7. Can I access historical pricing data to track changes?**

- No, Amazon does not provide historical pricing reports within Vendor Central. However, you can track pricing changes manually by exporting your catalog regularly.
