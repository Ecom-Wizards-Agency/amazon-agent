## Reading and Reconciling Payment Remittances

**Who is this for?:** Intended for individuals who are responsible for managing payment remittances within an organization. It provides a step-by-step guide for receiving, reviewing, and reconciling payment remittances against outstanding invoices or accounts.

**Objective:** To establish a standardized process for reading and reconciling payment remittances received from customers or clients. The SOP aims to ensure accurate and efficient handling of payment information, identification of discrepancies, and timely resolution of any payment-related issues.

5. In order to help you read and reconcile your payment remittance, take note of the following invoice numbers and their corresponding links, which you can download and review:

  1. Invoice Number with "SC" after it: a shortage claim on this invoice and is deducted from your invoice.
  2. Invoice Number with "SCR" after it: a shortage claim reversal on this invoice and is a credit to you.
  3. Invoice Number with "SCRSC" after it: a revised shortage claim on this invoice and is deducted from your invoice.
  4. Number with "VCBSINV" appended to it: a Chargeback debit note and is deducted from your account.
  5. XXXX-XXXXXXXX Invoice Number: an Agreement Invoice which you can locate on your "Agreements" page under the "Settings" tab on the top menu of Vendor Central.
  6. XXXXXX-PROVISION FOR RECEIVABLES: a provision for receivables (see SOP: Avoiding Provisions).
  7. XXXXXX-PROVISION FOR RECEIVABLESR: a reversal of the above provision.
  8. IPAPUS followed by 7 numbers: a debit note for a Price Protection Agreement.

FAQs

**1. How often does Amazon issue payment remittances?**

- Amazon typically issues payment remittances based on your payment terms, which can be weekly, biweekly, or monthly. You can check your payment schedule under Vendor Central > Payments > Remittance.

**2. What should I do if a payment remittance is missing?**

- First, verify that your invoice was successfully submitted and processed. If a remittance is missing, check:

  - Vendor Central > Payments > Remittance to see if it was generated.
  - Your email inbox (Amazon may send remittances via email).
  - Your bank account to confirm if payment was received without a remittance.
  - If you still can’t locate it, contact Amazon Vendor Support for assistance.

**3. How do I resolve discrepancies between my invoice and the payment remittance?**

- If your invoice amount doesn’t match the payment received, check for:

  - Shortage claims (SC) or chargeback deductions (VCBSINV).
  - Price protection adjustments (IPAPUS codes).
  - Provision adjustments Amazon may have applied.
  - To dispute a discrepancy, gather supporting documents and submit a case through Vendor Central > Support > Payments & Invoices.

**4. How long are remittance records available for download?**

- Amazon retains remittance records for at least 24 months. If you need older records, download them periodically or request archived data through Vendor Support.

**5. Can I dispute a chargeback or shortage claim deduction in my remittance?**

- Yes. If you believe a deduction is incorrect:

  - Review the claim details under Payments > Remittance.
  - Gather supporting documentation (e.g., proof of delivery, invoices).
  - Submit a dispute through Vendor Central > Support > Disputes & Chargebacks.
  - Amazon typically reviews disputes within 30-60 days.

**6. What should I do if I can’t download remittance reports?**

- If you encounter issues downloading remittances:

  - Clear your browser cache and try again.
  - Switch to another browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge).
  - Ensure pop-ups are enabled.
  - Try downloading a smaller date range to reduce file size.
  - If the issue persists, contact Vendor Support for technical assistance.

**7. How do I match a payment remittance to my bank deposits?**

- Compare the payment number (Remittance Number) in Vendor Central with your bank statement. If amounts don’t match, check for:

  - Currency conversions (if applicable).
  - Bank processing fees.
  - Partial payments due to deductions.
