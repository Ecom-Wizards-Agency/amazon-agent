# Vendor Central SOP: Locating Agreements

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-locating-agreements

## Locating Agreements

**Who is this for :** Amazon Vendor Central Agreements are primarily for vendors who want to sell their products directly to Amazon. Vendors are typically manufacturers, wholesalers, or distributors who have a supply of products that they want to sell to Amazon as a first-party seller.

**Objective :** The objective of Amazon Vendor Central Agreements is to establish a clear and mutually beneficial relationship between Amazon and the vendor, ensuring transparency, accountability, and adherence to the agreed-upon terms and conditions for selling products through the platform.

**Instructions:**

You will receive your initial agreements via email. After reviewing the terms, you will need to accept them on Vendor Central. To find your agreements on Vendor Central,

1. Go to the "Settings" tab on the top menu of your Vendor Central dashboard and select "Agreements".

7. It's important to understand that entering into agreements with Amazon means that you are selling your product as a wholesaler to Amazon, who becomes the distributor of your product.

8. As per the terms and conditions of trade with Amazon and your agreements with them as the vendor, Amazon becomes the legal owner of the product they order from you.

FAQs

**1. Do Vendor Central agreements expire or require renewal?**

- Yes, some agreements, such as co-op agreements and purchasing terms, may have renewal periods or need updates over time. Check the "Pending" tab periodically for any required renewals.

**2. How will I know if I have a new agreement to review?**

- Amazon typically sends an email notification when a new agreement requires acceptance. You can also check Vendor Central > Settings > Agreements to see if any agreements are listed under the "Pending" tab.

**3. Can I modify an existing agreement?**

- No, once an agreement is accepted, you cannot directly modify it in Vendor Central. To negotiate changes (e.g., payment terms, freight options), you must contact your Amazon Vendor Manager or submit a case through Vendor Support.

**4. What should I do if I can’t find an agreement that I’m expecting?**

- If an expected agreement isn’t listed under "Pending" or "Accepted/Rejected", try the following:

  - Ensure you are logged into the correct Vendor Central account.
  - Check with your Amazon Vendor Manager if the agreement is still being processed.
  - If it was accepted but isn’t showing, contact Amazon Vendor Support for assistance.

**5. How long can I access past agreements in Vendor Central?**

Amazon retains agreements indefinitely, but vendors should download and save copies for their records. If an older agreement is missing, reach out to Amazon Vendor Support for retrieval.

6. What happens if I reject an agreement?

Rejecting an agreement may affect your ability to sell on Amazon, depending on the type of agreement. Some agreements (e.g., co-op terms) may be negotiable, while rejecting purchasing terms could impact your vendor relationship. Always review agreements carefully before rejecting them.

7. Who do I contact if I have questions or disputes about an agreement?

For general agreement questions, reach out to Amazon Vendor Support via Vendor Central > Support. If you need to renegotiate terms, contact your Amazon Vendor Manager or buyer.
