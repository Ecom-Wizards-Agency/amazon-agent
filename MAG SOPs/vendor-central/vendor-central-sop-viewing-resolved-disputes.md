# Vendor Central SOP: Viewing Resolved Disputes

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-viewing-resolved-disputes

## Viewing Resolved Disputes

**Who is this for :** This SOP is relevant to vendors who want to monitor the status and outcomes of their dispute cases, track payment resolutions, and maintain accurate records of past disputes.

**Objectives:**

- To guide vendors in effectively accessing and reviewing information related to their resolved disputes on Amazon.
- To provide vendors with a clear process for viewing the status and details of resolved disputes, including insights into Amazon's resolution process and timelines for payment remittance.

**Steps to View Your Resolved Disputes on Amazon:**

1. Log in to your Vendor Central Dashboard and select the "Payments" tab. From there, click on "Dispute Management" to go to the Dispute Management Page.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%2010.34.04%E2%80%AFPM.png)

2. Under the "Disputes Summary" heading, click on "Resolved" to see a table of all your resolved disputes. Note that the "Action" column to the right of each resolved dispute will indicate "None - Closed".

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%2010.34.11%E2%80%AFPM.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%2010.34.20%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/GvHimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/GvHimage.png)

3. If you click on the actionable link for each "None - Closed" action, a pop-up window will appear with information about Amazon's process for resolving disputes. Amazon searches for receipt of goods for 35 days after the invoice due date. If receipt of goods is found, Amazon will match against the shortage ASINs and remit payment within a few days. If receipt of goods cannot be found, the dispute process is used to gather additional information to identify receipt of goods. Once there is a final resolution, disputes cannot be reopened.

4. You can also view your resolved disputes by selecting "Dispute Status" from the drop-down menu under the "Search Disputes" heading. In the cell to the right of the "Dispute Status" cell, select "Resolved" from the drop-down menu and click the "Search" button.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-04%20at%2010.34.28%E2%80%AFPM.png)

5. You can download a list of your resolved disputes by clicking the "Download" link at the top of the table on the right-hand side.

FAQs

**1. What does "Resolved" mean in Dispute Management?**

- "Resolved" means that Amazon has completed its review of the dispute. However, this could mean the dispute was approved (with a payment issued), partially approved, or denied. Check the dispute details for the specific outcome.

**2. How long does it take to receive payment after a dispute is marked as resolved?**

- If a dispute is approved, payments are usually processed within 5–7 business days. You can track remittance details in the Payments section of Vendor Central.

**3. Where can I find details about why my dispute was approved or denied?**

- Click on the dispute in the Dispute Management section. A pop-up will provide details on Amazon's decision, including any supporting notes or references to policies used in the resolution.

**4. What can I do if I disagree with a dispute resolution?**

- Once a dispute is marked as "resolved," it cannot be reopened. However, if you believe an error was made, you can contact Amazon Vendor Support with supporting documents for further review.

**5. What should I do if my dispute is resolved, but I haven't received payment?**

- Check the Payments tab to verify if the payment has been processed. If more than 7 business days have passed and payment is not reflected, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**6. How long are resolved disputes available in Vendor Central?**

- Resolved disputes typically remain accessible in Vendor Central for 12–24 months, but availability may vary. Download records periodically for long-term tracking.

**7. Is there a limit to how many disputes I can download at once?**

- Yes, Amazon may limit bulk downloads based on the number of disputes or file size. If you encounter issues, try filtering disputes by date range and downloading in smaller batches.

**8. How can I get further assistance with a resolved dispute?**

- For additional questions, contact Amazon Vendor Support through Vendor Central under Help > Contact Us and provide your dispute ID for reference.
