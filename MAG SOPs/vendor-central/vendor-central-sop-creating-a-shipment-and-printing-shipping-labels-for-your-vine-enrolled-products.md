# Vendor Central SOP: Creating a Shipment and Printing Shipping Labels for your Vine-Enrolled Products

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/vendor-central/page/vendor-central-sop-creating-a-shipment-and-printing-shipping-labels-for-your-vine-enrolled-products

### Creating a Shipment and Printing Shipping Labels for your Vine-Enrolled Products

**Who is this for**?

For employees or individuals responsible for managing Vine-enrolled products on Vendor Central. It is also relevant for those responsible for shipping and handling products on behalf of the company.

**Objective**:

To guide users through the process of creating a shipment and printing shipping labels for Vine-enrolled products on Vendor Central. This SOP is aimed at employees or individuals responsible for managing Vine-enrolled products on Vendor Central.

To create a Vine Program shipment and print labels for an enrolled product, follow these steps on Vendor Central:

1. Select “Amazon Vine” from the “Merchandising” Tab in the Top Menu and click on the “Scheduled” link to view enrolled Vine products and their quantities at the fulfillment center.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-12-07%20011807(3).png)

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/BMLimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/BMLimage.png)
2. To create a shipment and print labels for an unshipped Vine product, choose “Print Shipping Label” from the “Actions” Column Drop-Down Menu.
3. On the Vine Print Labels page, select the ASINs you want to include in your shipment.
4. It is important to note that Vine products should not be combined with your regular Amazon inventory.
5. Click “Create Shipment” to generate the labels, then click “Print Shipping Labels” and “Print Item Labels” to print the relevant labels.
6. The labels will contain an Amazon Reference ID, which is considered a purchase order (PO) for shipping purposes.
7. For each Vine product, add an item label that covers the original barcode or label completely, is readable and scannable, and matches the correct unit.
8. Add the Vine shipment label to the shipment, ensuring that it is placed at least 1.25 inches from any natural edge, is not covered with tape, and is not combined with other shipments in a box.
9. Select a transportation carrier and delivery speed for your shipment. You can use any carrier and speed when shipping to Amazon.
10. Note the special requirements for large shipments, including the acceptable pallet size and type, maximum pallet height and weight, and stretching wrapping.
11. To send Vine large shipments, download the Vine & FBA Booking Form from the “Operations” section of the “Resource Center”, complete it, and email it to your carrier.
12. Ensure that your carrier adheres to Amazon's safety standards and delivery requirements, such as scheduling a delivery appointment at least 24 hours in advance, communicating box and pallet counts, and providing delivery confirmation if using UPS.
13. Save a copy of your tracking numbers in case of any issues with receiving your products.

FAQs

General Process

- **Q1: What is the purpose of the Amazon Reference ID on the labels?**

  - **A1:** The Amazon Reference ID acts as a purchase order (PO) for shipping purposes and is used to track the shipment.
- **Q2: Can Vine products be shipped together with regular Amazon inventory?**

  - **A2:** No, Vine products should not be combined with regular Amazon inventory to avoid processing errors.
- **Q3: What should I do if I cannot find the Vine Print Labels page?**

  - **A3:** Ensure you are selecting “Print Shipping Label” from the “Actions” column drop-down menu for the relevant unshipped Vine product.

Labeling

- **Q4: How should the item label be applied to Vine products?**

  - **A4:** The item label should fully cover the original barcode or label, be readable and scannable, and match the correct unit.
- **Q5: Where should the Vine shipment label be placed on the box?**

  - **A5:** The Vine shipment label should be at least 1.25 inches from any natural edge, not covered with tape, and not combined with labels for other shipments.

Shipping Requirements

- **Q6: What are the requirements for shipping large Vine shipments?**

  - **A6:** Large shipments must adhere to Amazon's guidelines, including acceptable pallet size and type, maximum pallet height (72 inches) and weight (1,500 lbs.), and use of stretch wrapping.
- **Q7: Can I use any transportation carrier for shipping Vine products to Amazon?**

  - **A7:** Yes, you can use any carrier and delivery speed as long as they comply with Amazon's safety standards and delivery requirements.

Carrier Communication

- **Q8: What information must I provide to the carrier when scheduling a delivery?**

  - **A8:** You need to provide the box and pallet counts, schedule a delivery appointment at least 24 hours in advance, and ensure delivery confirmation if using UPS.
- **Q9: What should I do if the carrier does not meet Amazon's safety or delivery standards?**

  - **A9:** You should contact the carrier to resolve the issue or consider using a different carrier that meets Amazon's requirements to avoid shipment delays.

Documentation and Tracking

- **Q10: Why is it important to save tracking numbers?**

  - **A10:** Tracking numbers are crucial in case of issues with Amazon receiving your products, as they help trace the shipment's progress and resolve any discrepancies.
- **Q11: Where can I find the Vine & FBA Booking Form for large shipments?**

  - **A11:** You can download the Vine & FBA Booking Form from the “Operations” section of the “Resource Center” on Vendor Central.
