## Producing a Product Catalog Report

**Who is this for :** This is intended for Amazon vendors who want to access their Amazon Retail Analytics Dashboard and generate a comprehensive report on their product catalog. It provides step-by-step instructions on how to navigate the Vendor Central platform and customize the report to obtain valuable information about listed products.

**Objective :** To guide Amazon vendors in producing a Product Catalog Report through Vendor Central. By following the outlined steps, vendors will be able to access their Amazon Retail Analytics Dashboard, customize their view of the product catalog, and generate a report that includes the ASIN and product title. This report can be used to verify the status of ASINs and ensure alignment with internal systems. Additionally, vendors will learn how to access more detailed information, such as product descriptions and prices, if needed.

**Steps to Access Your Amazon Retail Analytics Dashboard:**

1. Go to your Vendor Central Dashboard and click on the "Reports" tab in the top menu. Select "Retail Analytics" from the Drop Down Menu to get an overview of your dashboard.

FAQs

**1. How often does the Product Catalog Report update?**

- The report updates daily, but some attributes (like Replenishment Category) may take 24-48 hours to reflect changes.

**2. How far back can I access historical catalog data?**

- Amazon does not store historical Product Catalog Reports, so it’s recommended to download and archive reports regularly for comparison.

**3. What do the key attributes in the Product Catalog Report mean?**

- Replenishment Category – Indicates if a product is reorderable or discontinued.
- Prep Instructions Required – Notes any preparation requirements before shipping to Amazon.
- Prep Instructions Vendor State – Identifies if prep responsibilities fall on the vendor or Amazon.
- Parent ASIN – Groups variations (e.g., size, color) under a main ASIN.

**4. What should I do if data is missing or incorrect in the report?**

- If an ASIN is missing or has incorrect details:

  - Ensure the correct filters and date range are applied.
  - Check whether the ASIN has been deactivated or suppressed by Amazon.
  - Compare the report with other catalog reports in Vendor Central.
  - If the issue persists, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**5. How can I use the report beyond aligning internal systems?**

- The report is useful for:

  - Inventory planning – Identifying slow-moving or discontinued ASINs.
  - Compliance checks – Ensuring ASIN details meet Amazon’s requirements.
  - Performance tracking – Monitoring product data consistency over time.

**6. Can I automate or schedule Product Catalog Report downloads?**

- No, Amazon does not currently offer automated scheduling for this report. However, vendors can manually download it periodically for regular tracking.

**7. What should I do if I see discontinued or inactive ASINs in the report?**

- If an ASIN appears in the report but should no longer be listed:

  - Check its Replenishment Category to confirm its status.
  - Remove the ASIN from your catalog if it’s no longer in use.
  - If the ASIN is still active but should be discontinued, submit a request to Amazon to update its status.
