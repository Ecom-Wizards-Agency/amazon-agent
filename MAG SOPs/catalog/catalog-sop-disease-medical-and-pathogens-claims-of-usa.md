## **Disease, Medical & Pathogens Claims of the United States of America (USA)**

**Who is this for :** This is intended for sellers operating in the USA Amazon marketplace who offer products that make disease, medical, or pathogens claims. This includes but is not limited to, manufacturers, distributors, and resellers of dietary supplements, herbal remedies, and other products marketed for health or medical purposes.

**Objective :** To provide guidance for sellers operating in the USA Amazon marketplace regarding the restrictions and limitations on disease, medical, and pathogens claims. This aims to ensure that all product claims are compliant with federal laws and Amazon's policies, particularly those related to the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act of 1994.

The USA Amazon marketplace has stricter policies than other marketplaces when it comes to disease, medical & pathogens claims because of its federal laws. Section 6 of the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act of 1994, titled Statements of Nutritional Support, states that “A statement under this subparagraph may not claim to diagnose, mitigate, treat, cure, or prevent a specific disease or class of diseases.”

In compliance with this law, Amazon’s legal team put restrictions on any products that have such claims.

**Documents You May Need**

1. **Real-world product packaging images (Often asked by Amazon)**. Amazon may need to review the product packaging if it’s compliant and does not have disease, medical, and pathogens claims.
2. **Certificate of Analysis (CoA) (Rarely asked by Amazon)**. If the violation states that the product contains an ingredient or drug for the treatment, cure, mitigation, or remedy of a disease, then, you’ll need a CoA.
3. **Certificate of Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) (Rarely asked by Amazon)**. In a very rare case if the ASIN needs selling application approval and at the same time received a disease, medical, and pathogens violation, then, you’ll need GMP along with the 2 documents above to clear it from the selling application.
4. **Manufacturer Link of the Product (Rarely asked by Amazon)**. Amazon may specify that there are claims present from the manufacturer's website link of the product. If that’s the case, ask the seller to edit their website and remove the prohibited claims.
5. **Test Reports (Rarely asked by Amazon)**. If the response from Amazon states that the product isn’t fit for human consumption or poses safety concerns, then you’ll need a test report from the manufacturer.

**Restricted Product Policy Violations Details**

**The violation details are located under “Reason” in Restricted Product Policy Violations in Account Health Performance.**

**Diabetes**

- This product has been identified as claiming to be a treatment, cure, or remedy for Diabetes or Diabetic Neuropathy symptoms. Products marketed as Diabetes treatments, remedies, cures, or similar Diabetes related products are prohibited from listing or selling on Amazon unless they have been approved by the FDA. For more information, please see our [Help Pages](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/help/external/help.html?itemID=200164490&language=en-US&ref=efph_200164490_cont_200164330)
- For reinstatement, please remove the prohibited disease claim from the detail page and appeal the restriction. **Please note that if the disease claim is on the product labeling, there may not be a path to reinstatement.** For additional guidance, please see [HERE](https://www.fda.gov/ForConsumers/ConsumerUpdates/ucm361487.htm)

**Pathogens**

- This product has been identified as a dietary supplement that makes prohibited disease claims about pathogens such as bacteria, fungi, viruses, yeast, protozoa, or microbes. Dietary supplements that make disease claims are prohibited from sale and listing on Amazon. For additional guidance, please see:

  - [Resource 1](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/help/help.html?itemID=201829010&language=en_US&ref=su_201829010_cont_200164330)
  - [Resource 2](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/small-entity-compliance-guide-structurefunction-claims)
- To be considered for reinstatement, you must

  - Remove the prohibited disease claims from the detail page before appealing the restriction,
  - Upload a picture of the product's ingredient list to the detail page (such as the "Drug Facts" or "Supplement Facts" table).
- **Please note that if the disease claim is on the product labeling, there may not be a path to reinstatement.**

**Pathogens Claims (not limited to):**

- Microbe
- Bacteria/Bacterial
- Virus/Viral
- Toxin
- Candida
- Fungi/Fungal
- Any form/type of bacteria
- Any form/type of fungi

**Other Claims (not limited to):**

- Any disease with the ‘itis’ suffix (eg. tonsillitis, gingivitis, appendicitis, etc)
- Any description that may mitigate illnesses (eg. relief, relieve)

**Remember:**

1. The 4 violations above are the most common yank reasons for some dietary supplements. There is more disease, medical and pathogens violations in the US Amazon marketplace. If ever you run into other violations, please search in Google about the disease, medical, and pathogens claims of that particular “condition”.
2. If the medical and disease claims are present on the product packaging, ask the seller to create a new label. If the seller wanted the listing to get reinstated immediately, ask a designer to remove the claims from the packaging.

**How to Fix?**

**Document the changes/actions you will take.**

1. Check the product images and remove the prohibited claims from infographics.
2. Download a category listing report (CLR) and remove all prohibited claims from the following attributes.

  1. Title
  2. Description
  3. Bullet points
  4. Search terms
  5. Intended Use
  6. Other Attributes
3. Upload the updated CLR and take the batch ID.
4. Go to A+ contents and look for the ASIN. Remove the prohibited claims from the description, images, and alt-text keywords.
5. If you’re confident that everything is set, go to Performance > Account Health > Restricted Product Policy Violations > Look for the ASIN > Appeal > Check the 2 boxes and Submit. Wait 24 hours before the listing gets reinstated.

FAQs

**1. How do I appeal a disease claim violation on Amazon after taking corrective actions?**

**Answer:**After you have removed the prohibited disease claims from your product’s detail page and updated all necessary documentation (like images, descriptions, and the Category Listing Report), you can submit an appeal through Amazon Seller Central. To do this:

- Go to Performance > Account Health.
- Look for the Restricted Product Policy Violations section.
- Find the specific ASIN and click on Appeal.
- Check the two boxes (confirming the issue has been addressed and that you've removed the claims) and submit your appeal.
- Typically, Amazon responds within 24-48 hours. If your appeal is rejected, you will need to reassess the situation and ensure that all prohibited claims have been completely removed from your product listing, including on the product packaging. You can also reach out to Amazon support if you're unsure about why your appeal was denied.

**2. What if the prohibited disease claims are on my product’s packaging? Can the listing ever be reinstated?**

**Answer:**If the prohibited disease claims are on your product packaging, it may not be possible to reinstate the listing without making changes to the product label. In most cases, Amazon will require a new label with the prohibited claims removed. Here are your options:

- Remove the prohibited claims from the packaging: You’ll need to update the packaging before submitting the appeal. Once updated, take clear images of the new packaging and submit them along with your appeal.
- Create new packaging: In some cases, you might need to create entirely new packaging that complies with Amazon’s policies. Consider consulting a designer to ensure compliance.
- Proof of compliance: If the seller insists the packaging is compliant after making the necessary changes, Amazon will typically ask for images of the updated packaging to review.

**3. How can I distinguish between a general health claim and a prohibited disease claim?**

**Answer:**The line between general health claims and prohibited disease claims can sometimes be blurry, but here are some guidelines:

- General health claims (like "supports immune health," "promotes joint comfort," or "helps with digestion") typically do not violate Amazon’s disease claim policies as long as they avoid directly stating or implying treatment, cure, or prevention of a specific disease.
- Prohibited disease claims involve terms that directly imply a product will diagnose, treat, cure, mitigate, or prevent a disease (e.g., "treats diabetes," "cures cancer," "prevents the flu").
- If you’re uncertain whether a claim might be crossing the line, err on the side of caution and consider rewording or removing the claim to avoid potential issues. You can also contact Amazon Seller Support for clarification on specific claims.

**4. What should I do if I’m unsure why my product’s claim was flagged as a violation?**

**Answer:**If your product has been flagged for a disease claim violation and you’re unsure why, follow these steps:

- Review the violation message: Often, the details provided in the violation message will give you a hint about the specific claim that caused the issue (e.g., a reference to "arthritis" or "diabetes").
- Search for the specific claim: Look at your product title, description, images, and any A+ content for wording that might trigger Amazon’s restricted product policies.
- Review Amazon’s policy pages: Amazon provides links to relevant policy pages in their violation notices. These will outline what types of claims are prohibited.
- Reach out to Amazon Seller Support: If you still don’t understand why the claim was flagged, contact Amazon’s support team for a more detailed explanation. They may be able to guide you on the specifics of the violation.

**5. What if my product has multiple disease claims across different listings? How can I prevent future violations?**

**Answer:**If you have multiple products with similar disease claims, it’s important to review your entire catalog to ensure compliance. Here are some steps to minimize future violations:

- **Conduct a thorough review of all claims:** Go through each listing carefully and remove any language that could be construed as a disease treatment or prevention claim. Ensure that all claims comply with Amazon’s guidelines.
- **Update your product labeling and descriptions:** If any labels or descriptions on your products mention treatment for specific diseases, these need to be changed to more general health benefits. Be especially cautious with health supplements.
- **Implement a compliance check system:** Before submitting new listings, create an internal checklist to ensure that no prohibited claims are included in the title, description, bullet points, or images. Consider using a compliance expert or a legal advisor if necessary.
- **Educate your team:** Make sure everyone in your team, from the listing creation team to marketing, understands the guidelines for disease, medical, and pathogens claims. This will help avoid accidental violations in the future.

**6. Can I get my product reinstated if the disease claim was on the packaging, and I can’t change it right away?**

**Answer:**If the prohibited disease claim is on the packaging and you’re unable to change it immediately, you may not be able to get your listing reinstated until the packaging is updated. However, there are a few things you can do:

- Start the packaging update process: Begin the process of revising your packaging to comply with Amazon’s policies.
- Appeal with a timeline: If you can’t immediately update the packaging, submit an appeal with a clear timeline indicating when you expect to have the updated packaging available. Amazon may provide guidance on next steps while you make the necessary changes.
