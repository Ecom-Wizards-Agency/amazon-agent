# Catalog SOP: How to fix Medical Devices and Accessories Yanks

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-how-to-fix-medical-devices-and-accessories-yanks

## **How to fix Medical Devices and Accessories Yanks**

**Who is this for :**This SOP is for Amazon Sellers who are responsible for reinstating medical devices and accessories listings that have been yanked due to non-compliance with FDA regulations.

**Objective :** The objective of this SOP is to provide clear guidelines and instructions on how to reinstate medical devices and accessories listings on Amazon that have been removed due to non-compliance with FDA regulations. The document will help ensure that all listings comply with FDA regulations and that the products offered for sale on Amazon are safe and effective for human use.

Medical devices are regulated by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA), which is the U.S. federal agency that is responsible for ensuring that medical devices intended for human use are safe and effective. A medical device is an instrument, apparatus, machine, or related object used to diagnose, cure, treat, or prevent diseases in people or animals. Medical devices can also be used to change the structure or function of the body, such as, but not limited to, stimulating hair growth.

To get more information about Medical Devices, see this [article](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/G200164650).

**Restricted Product Policy Violation Warning/Details:**

The violation details are located under “Reason” in Restricted Product Policy Violations in Account Health Performance. The details are very specific for each Yank reason. The following are just 2 out of many detail-page-removed reasons for Medical Devices and Accessories yanks:

- This product has been identified as a microneedling skin roller or similar product that is considered a medical device due to either :

  - The product’s technical characteristics, as the product’s needle length is greater than 0.3 mm,
  - The inclusion of medical marketing claims in the product details and/or images, or (
  - A combination of both technical characteristics and marketing claims.
- This product has been identified as a pulse oximeter, which is a professional-use-only medical device. Per Amazon policy, professional-use-only medical devices may only be sold to appropriately licensed healthcare customers who have Amazon Business accounts. If you believe this determination was made in error and your product is not a professional-use-only medical device, please contact Seller or Vendor Support and

  - Upload photos of all sides of the product packaging to the product detail page,
  - Upload photos of the instructions for use included with the product to the product detail page, and
  - Provide the 510(k) number issued by the U.S. Food & Drug Administration (FDA) for this product.
- If the name of the product provided on the product packaging, instructions for use, or ASIN detail page does not match the device name provided on the 510(k), please also provide, with the information noted above,

  - The device manufacturer’s FDA establishment registration number OR owner/operator number, T
  - The device name as it appears in the manufacturer’s device listing, and
  - A purchase order, invoice OR letter (written in English) from the manufacturer confirming that the device was purchased from the manufacturer.
- For more information, please see our [Medical Devices and Accessories Help Page](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/help/external/help.html?itemID=200164650&language=en-US&ref=efph_200164650_cont_200164330) and the following FDA guidance:

**How to Fix?**

**Document the changes/actions you will take.**

The best way to fix a medical device Yank is by providing counterarguments against the Yank reason. You need to prove that Amazon claims aren’t visible from the product’s (a) description, (b) instruction, and (c) label or appeal that the product is eligible to use medical device claims.

**Needful Things:**

1. Photos of all sides of the product packaging (just the packaging).
2. Instructions manual for use included with the product (PDF format).
3. Screenshot of the FDA Establishment Registration & Device Listing and its link, if the product is FDA registered.
4. Manufacturer Invoice, if the product is a registered FDA medical device.
5. 510(k) number issued by the U.S. Food & Drug Administration (FDA) if the seller is participating in the Professional Health Care Program and if the yanked product is an FDA Medical Device 3.

**Actions:**

1. Identify your product - Check your product's classification:

  - Non-medical device - Go to step 2.
  - FDA medical devices 1 and 2 - Get a screenshot of the FDA Establishment Registration & Device Listing link. You can obtain the information from your manufacturer.
  - FDA medical device 3 - Get a screenshot of the FDA registration of your product, its link, and the 510(k) certificate or number. You can obtain the information from your manufacturer.
2. Check the product description

  - Non-medical device - Remove any prohibited claims present from the product description (Title, Bullet Points, and Description), images, search terms keywords, and A+ content (description, images, and alt text keywords).

    - Let's use the microneedling issue as an example. The reason states that the only allowed needle length without a certificate is less than 0.3 mm. Thus, make sure that there is no mention of 0.3 mm or higher length in the product description, images, search terms, and A+ content. Put the correct length of the needles as proof that the length is shorter than 0.3 mm, thus, a good counterargument against the yank reason. The same principle applies to all. It’s best to do the changes through a feed file to obtain the batch ID. Please follow the [Download Category Listings Report SOP](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-download-category-listings-report).
  - FDA medical devices 1, 2, and 3 - Ensure that the product description, images, search terms, and A+ content are in harmony with the description in the FDA registration information. It’s best to do the changes through a feed file to obtain the batch ID. Please follow the [Download Category Listings Report SOP](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-download-category-listings-report).
3. Product Documents

  - Non-medical device

    - Product packaging images - You may need to take product packaging images (all sides). Amazon may ask for the images for them to further check the label if it's compliant with the Medical Devices and Accessories policy.
    - Product instructions manual - You may need to provide the instructions manual if Amazon asks for it. If your product has no instructions manual, tell Amazon and focus on the product label's content.
  - FDA medical devices 1 and 2

    - FDA Establishment Registration & Device Listing and its link - Take a screenshot of your product information from the FDA website. You can get the FDA information from your manufacturer. You can look up the Establishment Registration & Device Listing [here](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cdrh/cfdocs/cfRL/rl.cfm).
    - Product packaging images - You'll need to provide the product packaging images for Amazon to see if it has complete information about the product. Take product packaging images (all sides).
    - Product instructions manual - You'll need to provide the product instructions manual (if any) for Amazon to see if it's in the harmony with the registered product information under FDA.
    - Manufacturer Invoice - You'll need to provide the manufacturer invoice of your product. The manufacturer invoice and the FDA Registered Establishment Name information should match. It's also proof that you're an eligible seller of the product.
  - FDA medical device 3

    - The same documents showed above plus;
    - 510(k) Certificate - 510(k) is a premarket submission made to FDA to demonstrate that the device to be marketed is as safe and effective, that is, substantially equivalent, to a legally marketed device. You can obtain this certificate from your manufacturer
4. Appeal - If you’re confident that everything is set, go to Performance > Account Health > Restricted Product Policy Violations > Look for the ASIN > Appeal > Check the 2 boxes and Submit. Wait 24 hours before the listing gets reinstated.

FAQs

**1. How do I determine if my medical device is FDA registered or needs a 510(k) certificate?**

**Answer:**To check whether your medical device is FDA registered or requires a 510(k) certificate, you need to understand your product’s classification:

- **FDA Medical Devices 1 and 2:**These products may require FDA Establishment Registration & Device Listing (a confirmation from the FDA that the device is compliant with regulations). To verify, you can ask your manufacturer for a screenshot or documentation of the FDA Establishment Registration & Device Listing.
- **FDA Medical Device 3:** These devices will need additional clearance via the 510(k) certificate (which demonstrates the device is safe and effective for its intended use). You can obtain the 510(k) number and certificate from the manufacturer.
- Check your product’s details against the FDA’s Device Classification Database or directly reach out to your manufacturer for the proper documentation.

**2. What if I can't find my FDA Establishment Registration or 510(k) certificate?**

**Answer:**If you cannot locate your FDA Establishment Registration or 510(k) certificate, here are a few steps to take:

- **Contact the manufacturer:**The manufacturer should have these records, as they are responsible for ensuring the device is FDA compliant.
- **FDA Database:** You can search the FDA's website to check whether the device is listed or has an active 510(k) approval. If you're unsure how to navigate this, consult with your manufacturer for the most direct route to finding this information.
- If you still cannot obtain the documentation, consider reaching out to Amazon’s Seller Support for guidance on what additional steps you should take.

**3. What other non-medical devices might be wrongly flagged as medical devices on Amazon?**

**Answer:**Several common products can mistakenly be flagged as medical devices due to their characteristics or claims. Some examples include:

- Beauty products (like facial rollers or skincare tools) that could be mistakenly classified as microneedling devices.
- Health supplements with ambiguous claims that could be interpreted as medical or therapeutic.
- Personal care devices like hair growth stimulators, which could be misidentified as medical devices due to their intended use in altering body function.
- Massage tools that claim to “relieve pain” or “treat” muscular conditions might be considered medical devices.
- If you suspect that your product has been wrongly classified, ensure the product description, images, and claims are correctly worded to avoid confusion with regulated medical devices. You can also appeal with clear evidence that your product does not make medical claims.

**4. How do I prepare and upload the necessary documents for my medical device appeal?**

**Answer:**When submitting an appeal for a medical device yank, it’s crucial to prepare your documents in a way that meets Amazon's specifications:

- Product Packaging Photos: Take clear, high-quality photos of all sides of the product packaging. Ensure that the images show the necessary details such as device specifications and claims. Use JPEG or PNG formats.
- Instructions Manual: If applicable, upload a PDF of the instructions manual that clearly aligns with the FDA listing information.
- FDA Documents: For FDA registered devices, include screenshots of the FDA Establishment Registration & Device Listing or your 510(k) certificate. Ensure these screenshots are clear and the URL is visible.
- To upload, go to Inventory > Manage Product Documents and select the appropriate document type (e.g., User Manual or Application Guide).

**5. What should I do if my medical device listing is still yanked after 24 hours?**

**Answer:**If your listing remains yanked after submitting an appeal and waiting 24 hours, follow these steps:

- Check Appeal Status: Go to Performance > Account Health > Restricted Product Policy Violations to check the status of your appeal.
- Review Documentation: Double-check all the documents you've uploaded to ensure they align with Amazon’s requirements. Ensure all required fields and images are included, such as FDA registration, product photos, and instruction manuals.
- Contact Seller Support: If your appeal is still pending or has been rejected, contact Amazon Seller Support directly. You can send a ticket for a more detailed explanation or further guidance. When submitting the ticket, make sure to reference the ASIN and reason for appeal in the subject line.

**6. How can I prevent future yanks of my medical devices?**

**Answer:**To prevent future yanks, you should:

- Ensure Compliance: Always make sure that your products and listings comply with FDA regulations. This includes accurate labeling, appropriate claims, and documentation such as the FDA Establishment Registration, 510(k) certificates, and instructions manuals.
- Update Product Listings: Regularly review and update your product listings, making sure that descriptions, titles, and images don’t imply your product is a medical device unless it truly is.
- Educate your Team: If you have multiple team members handling listings, educate them about FDA regulations and Amazon’s restricted product policies to avoid accidental violations.
- Proactive Documentation: Always have your FDA documents, instructions manual, and product images ready and up-to-date. It will speed up the reinstatement process if a violation occurs.

**7. What happens if my product is flagged due to marketing claims in the images or description?**

**Answer:**If your product is flagged due to marketing claims (like health claims, treatment suggestions, or other medical statements) in the images or description, follow these steps:

- Review Claims: Go through your product's description, title, images, and A+ content to ensure no claims suggest medical or therapeutic benefits unless the product is FDA-approved.
- Remove Prohibited Claims: Modify or remove any prohibited claims such as “cures,” “treats,” or “prevents diseases” from the product description, images, and search terms.
- Upload Correct Documentation: If your product does have FDA approval and you believe it was wrongly flagged, submit the necessary documentation (e.g., FDA Establishment Registration, 510(k) certificate) with a clear counter-argument to show that the product does not violate Amazon’s policies.
