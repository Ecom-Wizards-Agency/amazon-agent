## **How to Generate a Reimbursements Report**

**Who is this for :** This SOP is designed for sellers or teams who have access to Amazon Seller Central and are responsible for managing reimbursements. It is suitable for users who need to generate a Reimbursements Report to track and review reimbursement transactions on the platform.

**Objective :** The objective of this standard operating procedure (SOP) is to guide sellers or teams responsible for managing reimbursements on Amazon Seller Central in generating a Reimbursements Report. The SOP aims to provide clear instructions on how to navigate to the report section, select the desired date range, and download the report in either Excel or text format. By following this SOP, users will be able to access comprehensive reimbursement information for tracking, reconciling financial records, and gaining insights into the reimbursement process.

**Introduction:**

The Reimbursements Report is a valuable resource for sellers on Amazon Seller Central to effectively manage reimbursement transactions. When you have requested reimbursements, refer to the case ID in the report to view the associated details. Similarly, for reimbursements linked to customer refunds, use the Amazon order ID in the report to access the relevant information. It is important to note that there may be a delay of up to five days for reimbursements to appear in the report after they have been approved. Additionally, if multiple reimbursements are processed on the same day for the same issue, they may be combined into a single transaction. By following this SOP, sellers can efficiently track and analyze their reimbursements, ensuring accurate financial management and operational decision-making on the Amazon platform.

1. To generate a Reimbursements Report, navigate to the "Reports" tab on the top menu of Seller Central and select "Fulfillment" from the drop-down menu.

FAQs

1. How long does it take for the Reimbursements Report to generate after requesting it?

The report generation time varies, but it usually takes a few minutes. The "Report Status" column will show "In Progress" while the report is being prepared. Once it is ready, the status will change to "Download," and you can retrieve the file.

2. What information is included in the Reimbursements Report?

The report contains details about approved reimbursements, including case IDs, reimbursement reasons, amounts credited, associated order IDs, and SKUs. It helps sellers track and reconcile their reimbursement transactions.

3. How can I interpret the data in my Reimbursements Report?

Each row in the report represents a reimbursement transaction. Key columns include the reimbursement ID, order ID, SKU, amount refunded, and reason for reimbursement. Sellers can use this data to match reimbursements with their cases or reconcile financial records.

4. What should I do after downloading the Reimbursements Report?

After downloading the report, review it to verify that all expected reimbursements are listed. Compare the data with your case records and financial statements. If you notice missing reimbursements or discrepancies, you may need to open a case with Amazon Seller Support.

5. Do all sellers have access to the Reimbursements Report?

No, only sellers with access to Amazon’s Seller Central reports can generate the Reimbursements Report. If you are part of a team, your user permissions must allow access to "Fulfillment Reports."

6. What should I do if my reimbursement doesn’t appear in the report?

If a reimbursement is missing, wait at least five days after approval, as there may be a delay in processing. If it still does not appear, check if it was combined with another reimbursement on the same day. If you suspect an error, contact Amazon Seller Support for further assistance.

7. Why do some reimbursements get combined into a single transaction?

Amazon may group multiple reimbursements for the same issue into one transaction if they were processed on the same day. This can happen if multiple units of the same SKU were reimbursed at once.

8. What should I do if I receive an error when trying to download the report?

If you encounter an error, try refreshing the page and requesting the download again. Ensure that your internet connection is stable and that pop-up blockers are not preventing the download. If the issue persists, clear your browser cache or try a different browser.

9. Can I generate a report for specific SKUs or orders?

Yes, you can filter the Reimbursements Report by entering a Merchant SKU, Fulfillment Network SKU, Reimburse ID, or Amazon Order ID in the search field before downloading the report.

10. Where can I find more information about reimbursement policies and processes?

For detailed guidelines on Amazon’s reimbursement policies, visit the [Amazon Seller Central Help Page](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/help).
