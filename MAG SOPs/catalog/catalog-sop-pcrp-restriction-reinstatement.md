## **PCRP Restriction Reinstatement**

**Who is this for :** This SOP is intended for individuals or teams responsible for managing product listings on Amazon and who are dealing with ASINs that have been restricted due to PCRP policies.

**Objective :** This aims to guide the process of reinstating a PCRP (Pain, Cough, Respiratory, and Cold) restricted ASIN (Amazon Standard Identification Number) by providing the necessary documentation.

1. Download the Batch File for the category of the affected ASIN

2. Input the Legal_compliance_certification attribute that you have available (NPN, DIN, or DIN-HM #), and legal_compliance_certification_value (specify which certification it is)

3. Include the “Certification Date of Issue”

5. Use the following template and send a case to Amazon.

The feed file is attached with the NPN information. Please see the following information regarding this case.

Website Proof.

Batch ID Processing Report (See attached).

Image of product bottle with NPH (see attached).

Please reinstate this listing as we have provided all of the necessary documentation.

6. Attach the completed Batch ID report, an Image of the product showing the compliance number, and a screenshot of the compliance information on the batch file.

FAQs

1. Why do ASINs get restricted under PCRP policies?

Amazon restricts certain health-related products, including pain relief, cough, respiratory, and cold medications, to ensure compliance with regulatory guidelines. If an ASIN lacks the necessary legal certifications, it may be removed from the marketplace until compliance is verified.

2. What certifications are required for reinstating a PCRP-restricted ASIN?

Amazon requires one of the following certifications, depending on the product and country of sale:

- NPN (Natural Product Number) – Issued by Health Canada for natural health products.
- DIN (Drug Identification Number) – Required for drugs approved by Health Canada.
- DIN-HM (Homeopathic Medicine Number) – Used for homeopathic remedies approved in Canada.

3. Where can I find the necessary compliance documentation?

You can obtain compliance documentation from the product manufacturer, regulatory agencies (such as Health Canada), or the product packaging. If the required certification is missing, you may need to work with the manufacturer to obtain it.

4. How long does it take Amazon to review a PCRP reinstatement request?

Amazon typically reviews reinstatement requests within 5–7 business days, but this can vary based on case volume and the completeness of the documentation provided.

5. What should I do if my reinstatement request is rejected?

If Amazon denies your request:

- Review the rejection message for missing or incorrect information.
- Ensure all certifications, images, and batch reports match Amazon’s requirements.
- Submit a new case with a revised explanation and any additional supporting documents.

6. How can I check the status of my reinstatement request?

You can track the status of your case in Seller Central > Help > Case Log. Amazon will notify you via email once a decision has been made.

7. What happens if my batch file upload fails?

If your batch file does not process correctly:

- Check the Processing Report in Seller Central to identify errors.
- Ensure the file is formatted correctly and includes all required attributes.
- Re-upload the corrected file and verify successful processing before submitting the case.

8. How can I prevent future PCRP restrictions on my listings?

- Ensure all PCRP products have valid compliance certifications before listing them.
- Regularly audit your product catalog for compliance and update any missing certifications.
- Work directly with manufacturers to obtain proper regulatory approvals before launching new products.

9. Can I sell PCRP-restricted products without certification?

No, Amazon requires sellers to provide proper documentation for all PCRP-restricted products. Selling without certification may result in listing removal, account warnings, or permanent restrictions.
