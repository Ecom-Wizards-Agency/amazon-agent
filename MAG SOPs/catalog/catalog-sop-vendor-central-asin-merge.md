## Vendor Central ASIN Merge

**Who is this for :** Individuals who wanted to merge identical listings.

**Objective:** The purpose of this document is to present a step-by-step guide to merging the ASINs in Vendor Central.

**Instructions:**

1. Access the account by going to [https://vendorcentral.amazon.com](https://vendorcentral.amazon.com)/.

9. Open the Merge Form and fill it out. Enter the vendor’s original ASIN and the vendor’s product title. Next, navigate to the duplicate ASIN(s) on Amazon and enter the ASIN and the title. You can enter more than one duplicate ASIN here to match against the vendor’s ASIN. Save the file once completed or filled out.

FAQs

1. **Why should I merge ASINs?**

- Merging ASINs eliminates duplicate listings, which:

  - Prevents customer confusion.
  - Consolidates product reviews into a single ASIN.
  - Improves search rankings and visibility.
  - Ensures inventory and sales data remain in one place.

1. **How do I know if two ASINs qualify for merging?**

- Amazon allows ASIN merging only if:

  - The products are 100% identical (same brand, model, size, color, features).
  - The listings have minor differences in titles or descriptions but refer to the same product.
  - Both ASINs are not part of an active variation relationship.

  **Important:** Products with different packaging, branding, or versions cannot be merged.

1. **What happens to the duplicate ASIN(s) after the merge?**

- Once the merge is approved:

  - The duplicate ASIN(s) will be redirected to the primary ASIN.
  - All product details will reflect those of the primary ASIN.
  - Customer reviews may be consolidated, but not always guaranteed.

1. **How long does it take for Amazon to process an ASIN merge?**

- Merging typically takes 7–14 business days, but it can take longer depending on the complexity of the case.

1. **What should I do if Amazon refuses my merge request?**

- If Amazon denies the merge:

  - Check the reason for rejection (Amazon may claim the ASINs are not identical).
  - Provide additional proof such as manufacturer links, UPC/EAN data, or product images.
  - Request an escalation if you are sure the ASINs should be merged.

1. **Will merging ASINs affect product rankings or reviews?**

- Yes, merging ASINs can impact:

  - Search rankings: The primary ASIN may gain improved ranking due to consolidated sales history.
  - Reviews: Some reviews from duplicate ASINs may transfer, but Amazon does not guarantee all reviews will merge.

1. **Does this process apply to Seller Central as well?**

- No. The Vendor Central ASIN merge process is separate from Seller Central. If you need to merge ASINs in Seller Central, you must open a case through Seller Support and request a catalog correction.

1. **How do I escalate my request if Amazon keeps rejecting it?**

- Reopen the case and ask for a detailed explanation.
- Provide additional evidence (UPC codes, manufacturer specs, proof of duplication).
- Request escalation to the Amazon Catalog Team for manual review.
- If necessary, contact your Vendor Manager to push the request forward.
