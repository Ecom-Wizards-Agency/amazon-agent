# Logistics SOP: How to Switch from Amazon Barcodes to Manufacturer Barcodes for Virtual Tracking

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/logistics-sop-how-to-switch-from-amazon-barcodes-to-manufacturer-barcodes-for-virtual-tracking

## **How to Switch from Amazon Barcodes to Manufacturer Barcodes for Virtual Tracking**

**Who is this for?**

Amazon Specialists handling FBA listings, SKU setup, and barcode preference updates for virtual tracking.

**Objective:**

This SOP guides you through switching your barcode preference from Amazon barcodes to **manufacturer barcodes** to enable **virtual tracking** for eligible FBA products. Virtual tracking allows you to send inventory using only the product’s manufacturer barcode, simplifying your logistics process.

Upcoming Update Effective March 2026

##### **Upcoming update effective March 2026**

Amazon Announcement: [https://sellercentral.amazon.com/seller-news/articles/QVRWUERLSUtYMERFUiNHSktDTEM5N0s5MkRBWFAy?ref=nswg_scgw_GJKCLC97K92DAXP2_en_US_1_nslp&communicationDeliveryId=5a9bba4b-d567-4f22-a967-3331b7e4eb03](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/seller-news/articles/QVRWUERLSUtYMERFUiNHSktDTEM5N0s5MkRBWFAy?ref=nswg_scgw_GJKCLC97K92DAXP2_en_US_1_nslp&communicationDeliveryId=5a9bba4b-d567-4f22-a967-3331b7e4eb03)

In March, Amazon will end commingling practices across the supply chain and update eligibility criteria for using manufacturer barcodes.

The following requirements will go into effect for inventory shipped on or after March 31, 2026:

- Brand owners

  with the Brand Representative selling role in

  Amazon Brand Registry

  will no longer need to apply Amazon barcode stickers to prevent commingling for products that already have manufacturer barcodes (such as UPC, ISBN). This means more flexibility in how you manage inventory because you will not need to pre-allocate units to Amazon or other channels. For more information, go to

  How to switch to manufacturer barcodes

  .
- Resellers

  (not enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry as a Brand Representative selling role) will now be required to use Amazon barcode stickers for products even if they have a manufacturer barcode. If you are already using these stickers, you can continue to follow your existing process.
- For products that

  do not have a manufacturer barcode

  , both brand owners and resellers will need to use Amazon Barcode stickers.

**Before You Begin**

1. **Check Eligibility**

  - Not all products qualify for virtual tracking.

    - Visit[**Using manufacturer barcodes with FBA virtual tracking**](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/) to confirm your product’s eligibility.
2. **Inventory Restrictions**

  - You **cannot** switch barcode preferences for inventory already in fulfillment centers.
  - For SKUs with existing inventory using Amazon barcodes, you’ll need to:

    - **Sell through** that inventory **or**
    - **Submit a removal order**
  - Barcode preference changes apply to **future shipments only**.

**Step-by-Step Instructions**

**OPTION 1: Bulk Conversion (Best for multiple listings)**

1. Go to [**Manufacturer barcode: Convert eligible offers**](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/c2c/feeds).
2. Under Step 1, click **Generate download file**.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/fQpimage.png)

1. Once available, click **Download File**.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/WhEimage.png)![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/logistics-sop-how-to-switch-from-amazon-barcodes-to-manufacturer-barcodes-for-virtual-tracking-image-nsl9lop4.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Enhimage.png)

1. Open the file and navigate to the **Manufacturer Barcode** worksheet.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/oEaimage.png)

1. For each product you want to convert:

  - Enter a **new SKU** in the SKU: Manufacturer barcode column.

    ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/l8Pimage.png)

- Type "**Yes**" in the Enable manufacturer barcode tracking column.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/xS7image.png)

1. Save and upload the file following Step 2 on the same page.

⚠️ Some listings may **require a new SKU** due to open shipments or other restrictions.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/rsVimage.png)

**OPTION 2: Send to Amazon Workflow (Best for individual SKUs)**

1. Go to **Manage All** **Inventory**.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/p2oimage.png)

1. Select your product, click **Edit listing** → **Send/Replenish Inventory** or click **Replenish inventory**under the “**out of stock**” status

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/UgKimage.png)

1. On Step 1 of the **Send to Amazon** workflow, look for the **Save using manufacturer barcode** link under “**Information/Action**.”

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/PTIimage.png)

1. Click the link and follow the steps to complete the switch.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/r7Limage.png)

If the link isn’t visible, the product is likely **ineligible** for virtual tracking.

**OPTION 3: Create a New SKU (Best if current SKU is tied to active inventory)**

1. Go to **Manage All Inventory**.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wx0image.png)

1. Click **Edit** next to the ASIN → select **Add another condition**.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/corAjkimage.png)

1. Enter a new **Seller SKU**, fill out required fields, scroll down and click **Save and finish**.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/DUzimage.png)

1. On the **List as FBA** page:

  - Choose **Manufacturer barcode** from the barcode type dropdown.
  - Click **Confirm and List as FBA**.

For ineligible ASINs, you may apply for an exemption via **Amazon Brand Registry**.

After Switching to Manufacturer Barcodes

**What Changes & What to Do:**

| Affected Area | What Happens | What You Should Do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Inventory recommendations | New SKU has its own data | Close old SKU & submit removal order |
| Pricing recommendations | Not available right away | Use previous SKU's pricing history |
| Advertising suggestions | Reset for new SKU | Reapply ads & promotions manually |
| Business pricing & discounts | Not carried over | Re-add to the new SKU |
| Inventory planning tools | Separate entries | Track both SKUs carefully |
| Sponsored ads | Don’t auto-carry | Launch new or update existing campaigns |
| Deals/Coupons/Promos | Not auto-applied | Recreate for the new SKU |
| Subscribe & Save | Subscriptions don’t carry | Submit a transfer request |
| Virtual Bundles | Not linked | Create new bundles with the new SKU |
| Seller Fulfilled Prime | Eligibility may change | Recheck requirements and re-enroll |
| Global Selling | SKU applies to one store | Repeat the setup per marketplace |

**Notes & Tips**

- **Data isn’t carried over** between old and new SKUs—treat them like separate products.
- You’ll need to **manually reapply** most settings (ads, pricing, promotions, etc.).
- **Global settings are not inherited**—barcode preferences must be updated per marketplace.
- If ineligible, consider applying for **Brand Registry** to request an exemption.
