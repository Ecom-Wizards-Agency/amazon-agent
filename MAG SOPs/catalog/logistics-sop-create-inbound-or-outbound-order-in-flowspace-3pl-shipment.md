## **Create Inbound or Outbound Order in Flowspace (3PL Shipment)**

**Who is this for:** Intended for individuals who are involved in or responsible for managing the shipment process

**Objective:** To help us learn on how to create inbound and outbound orders in Flowspace, which is a third-party logistics (3PL) platform used for managing shipments.

**CREATE INBOUND ORDER IN FLOWSPACE**

**Inbound orders**inform the warehouse that your products are in transit to be received. To create inbound order, click **Inbound → Create Inbound Order**

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Wvbimage.png)

**Step 1: Order Details**

- In the first step, you will need to enter basic information about your inbound order, including the:

  - **PO Number:**
  - **From Address:**
  - **To warehouse:**
  - **Open Ended Order?:** NO (This is for removing items from FBA)

Once completed, click **Continue** to proceed.

**Step 2: Line Items**

- enter the line items for the products that will be coming into the warehouse. Then click Continue to proceed.
- SKU and Description: Provide details about the product SKU.
- Quantity: (How many eaches/cases to send)

Note: **EACHES** - This means the number of units to send. | **CASES** - This means the number of boxes to send.

**Step 3: Transportation Details**

- You need to enter the transportation details as follows. This informs the warehouse on how will it arrive. Provide details such as:

  - **Product Arrives:** Palletized
  - **Total Pallets | Total Weight**: You can get these details from the client
  - **Pallet Dimension:** You can get these details from the client
  - **Carrier Details***

    - Will Flowspace book the transportation?: NO
    - Carrier: TBD (to be discussed)
    - Arrival at the warehouse: (Allow 2 business days in Flowspace)
- Click Continue to proceed.

**Step 4: Confirm Order Details**

- The last step is the order summary where you can verify all the details before creating the order.

**CREATE OUTBOUND ORDER IN FLOWSPACE**

- **Outbound orders** should be created if the product is being transported out of the warehouse. To create an outbound order, click **Outbound → Create Outbound Order**

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1680299281567.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/ypCimage.png)

**Step 1: Order Details**

- enter basic information about your outbound order, including the:

  - PO Number:
  - Transportation Mode: Parcel
  - From Warehouse: - To Address: Amazon multiple destinations, CA 45645
- Once completed, click **Continue** to proceed.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1680299429018.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/KyAimage.png)

**Step 2: Order Contents**

- You will enter the order contents for the products that will be leaving the warehouse. Provide details about the Product SKU and Quantity shipped.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1683758194961.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/6sAimage.png)

**Note:** Inform POC if the **Available Quantity** shows 0 when creating an outbound.

Before clicking the **CONTINUE** button, create a shipment in **SELLER CENTRAL** for those ASINs listed in the outbound order. Allow 3 business days for the ship date of your shipment.

Print the **BOX LABELS** after shipment creation, then you can click **CONTINUE** in Flowspace.

**Step 3: Shipping Details**

- enter the transportation details for this order. This informs the warehouse on how it should be departing and contains details such as:

  - Will Flowspace produce a shipping label?: No, I will provide my own shipping label.
  - Preferred Departure Date: Allow 2 business days in Flowspace preferred departure date but on seller central make sure to allow 3 business days ship date.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/create-inbound_outbound-order-in-flowspace-3pl-shipment-image-jsh6viiy.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/9DKimage.png)

Once all details have been added, click Continue to proceed.

**Step 4: Confirm Order Details**

- the last step is the order summary, which allows you to verify that all details are correct before the order is created.

**Step 5: Click Create Outbound Order to finish creating the outbound order**

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1680299718602.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/PCGimage.png)

- After clicking the Create Order button, click **DISCUSSION**. Attach the **BOX LABELS** you downloaded in seller central in the**ADD ATTACHMENT** section.
- Provide the Flowspace SKU and the shipment id in the message box and **Send**.

  - SAMPLE: SKU: H8-BBB-0000- Shipment ID: FBA000723HS

FAQs

1. Do I need special access or permissions to create inbound and outbound orders in Flowspace?

Yes, you must have the appropriate user role and access rights to create and manage shipments in Flowspace. If you’re unable to create an order, check with your administrator for access permissions.

2. How can I track my inbound or outbound orders after creation?

After creating an order, go to Inbound Orders or Outbound Orders in Flowspace and use the search function to track status updates. You can also check with the warehouse team if there are delays.

3. What should I do if Flowspace does not allow me to create an order?

If you encounter issues, verify the following:

- Ensure all required fields are completed correctly.
- Check if the SKU exists in the Flowspace system.
- Make sure you have the necessary permissions.
- If the issue persists, contact Flowspace support or your internal POC.

4. What should I do if the available quantity shows 0 when creating an outbound order?

- Confirm the inventory levels in Flowspace.
- Check if there’s a pending inbound order that hasn’t been processed.
- If the SKU should have stock but doesn’t, contact the POC for inventory reconciliation.

5. What format and size should the box labels be when uploading them to Flowspace?

Box labels should be in PDF format, ideally in 4x6 inches to meet Amazon’s shipping requirements. Ensure that labels are clear and scannable before uploading.

6. Why do we need to create an FBA shipment before finalizing an outbound order?

Creating an FBA shipment in Seller Central ensures that Amazon is expecting the inventory. This step prevents delays and helps avoid rejection of shipments at Amazon fulfillment centers.

7. What should I do if the shipment ID is missing after creating an FBA shipment?

If the shipment ID is not generated, try the following:

- Refresh Seller Central and check under Manage FBA Shipments.
- Confirm that all shipment details were entered correctly.
- If still missing, retry creating the shipment or contact Amazon support.

8. What happens if the transportation details for an inbound order change after creation?

If transportation details (e.g., carrier, arrival date) change after creating an inbound order, notify the warehouse team and update the information in Flowspace under the Discussion section.

9. How long does it take for Flowspace to process an inbound or outbound order?

- Inbound Orders: Allow 2 business days for warehouse processing after arrival.
- Outbound Orders: Allow 2 business days for Flowspace processing, but ensure Amazon’s ship date allows 3 business days for shipment.

10. What should I include when sending a discussion message after creating an outbound order?

Your discussion message should include:

- Attached box labels from Seller Central.
- Flowspace SKU of the product.
- Shipment ID (e.g., FBA000723HS).
  This ensures that the warehouse has all necessary details for processing.
