## Uploading Cost of Goods for Amazon Reimbursement Policy Update

***(Effective March 10th, 2025)***

**Who is this for?**

This SOP applies to all sellers who need to update the cost of goods for FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) products as part of Amazon's updated reimbursement process.

**Objective:**

To guide sellers on how to upload the cost of goods for Amazon's automatic reimbursement policy, which is effective starting March 10th, 2025. The SOP covers how to update your product's sourcing cost and submit supporting documentation for reimbursement.

  3. Click on **Inventory Defect and Reimbursement** to access the reimbursement dashboard.
  4. Review any reimbursement information for eligible products.
- **Managing Sourcing Costs for Reimbursement:**

  1. **Navigate to the Manage Sourcing Cost Page:**

    1. In Seller Central, go to **Manage Sourcing Cost**
    2. If this feature is available to your account, you’ll be able to update the sourcing costs.
  2. **Review Estimated Costs Provided by Amazon:**

    1. Amazon will provide an estimated cost of goods. If the estimated cost is close to your actual cost, you may proceed to update it.
    2. **Example**: If Amazon lists the cost as $34, but your actual cost is $35, you can update it accordingly.
  3. **Updating the Cost of Goods:**

    1. Click the **Update**button next to the product and enter your actual sourcing cost (e.g., $35 instead of $34).
    2. If the update is within a reasonable range, **Amazon will auto-approve** the change within minutes.
- **Providing Documentation for Large Updates:**

  If the sourcing cost update is substantial (e.g., an increase of $40 or more), Amazon will require **documentation**to support the change.

  1. **Required Documentation:**

    1. **Manufacturing Invoice** or **Wholesaler Invoice**: Proof of purchase from your supplier.
    2. **Invoice Number**: Include the invoice number for tracking.
    3. **Invoice Date**: Ensure that the invoice date aligns with the purchase date.
    4. **Buyer Entity Name**: The name of your Amazon business entity.
    5. **Issuer Entity**: The name of the manufacturer or wholesaler from whom the product was purchased.
    6. **Product Details**: Include SKU and a brief description of the product. This will help differentiate similar products, like different color variations.
    7. **Manufacturing Cost/Purchase Price**: Clearly indicate the cost of the product.
- **Uploading the Documentation:**

  1. When making a large update, Amazon will prompt you to upload proof of purchase.
  2. After submitting the documents, Amazon will review the information.

    1. If all details match, your cost update will be approved.
    2. If there is any discrepancy, Amazon may request further information or reject the submission.
- Important Notes:

  - **Exclusion of Certain Costs**: Amazon will only reimburse based on the manufacturer cost. This means that shipping costs, handling fees, and Amazon fees are excluded from reimbursement calculations.
  - **Estimation Accuracy**: In most cases, Amazon’s estimations for product costs are close to actual costs. However, if your actual costs are higher, you may benefit from updating them as per Amazon's process.
  - **Reimbursement Process**: If you’re paying below industry norms, you may not see significant changes in your reimbursements. However, if your product costs are above average, Amazon may offer higher reimbursements than they have in the past.

**Conclusion:**

This SOP outlines the steps to upload your cost of goods for Amazon’s updated reimbursement policy, which begins March 10th, 2025. Follow the instructions carefully to ensure accurate reimbursement, and always provide the required supporting documentation when necessary.
