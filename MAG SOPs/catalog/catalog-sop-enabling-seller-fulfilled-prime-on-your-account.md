## **Enabling Seller Fulfilled Prime on Your account**

**Who is this for :** This is for sellers who want to take advantage of the benefits of the Seller Fulfilled Prime program, including increased visibility for their products and the ability to offer Prime shipping to customers without using Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA).

**Objective :** To provide a step-by-step guide for sellers who want to enable Seller Fulfilled Prime on their accounts.

1. To activate Seller Fulfilled Prime, start by registering for the trial phase on the ["Seller Fulfilled Prime"](https://sell.amazon.com/programs/seller-fulfilled-prime) main page.
2. Next, enable Prime shipping in your trial shipping template.

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/d7Vimage.png)
4. Assign SKUs to your Prime shipping template to enroll items in Seller Fulfilled Prime

6. To fulfill Prime trial orders, identify them by the Prime badge displayed on the "Manage Orders" page in Seller Central and the "is prime" identifier in "Order Reports."

FAQs

1. What is Seller Fulfilled Prime?

Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP) allows you to offer Prime shipping to customers without using Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA). This program gives your products increased visibility and ensures they qualify for Prime benefits, helping improve customer satisfaction and sales.

2. How do I register for the Seller Fulfilled Prime trial?

You can register for the trial phase by visiting the "Seller Fulfilled Prime" main page in Seller Central. Once there, follow the prompts to enroll in the trial.

3. Do I need to enable Prime shipping in my shipping template?

Yes, after registering for the trial, you must enable Prime shipping in your trial shipping template. This is necessary for assigning SKUs and fulfilling orders through Seller Fulfilled Prime.

4. How do I assign SKUs to my Prime shipping template?

To assign SKUs to your Prime shipping template, go to the shipping settings and select the SKUs you want to enroll. Ensure that these SKUs meet Amazon's Seller Fulfilled Prime requirements.

5. How will I know if an order is a Prime trial order?

Prime trial orders will be marked with a Prime badge on the "Manage Orders" page in Seller Central. Additionally, they will have an "is prime" identifier in the "Order Reports."

6. What are the performance requirements during the trial period?

During the trial, all SKUs assigned to your Prime shipping template will count as Prime trial orders, regardless of the buyer's location. You will need to meet the performance requirements as outlined by Amazon to successfully complete the trial.

7. How do I fulfill Prime trial orders?

Prime trial orders should be fulfilled according to the instructions provided in the "Fulfilling Seller Fulfilled Prime via Email Notification" and "via Seller Central" guidelines. Make sure to follow these steps carefully to ensure the orders qualify for Prime shipping.

8. Can I still use my existing shipping template for other orders?

Yes, you can use your regular shipping template for non-Prime orders while using the Seller Fulfilled Prime template for Prime trial orders. Just ensure you assign the correct SKUs to the Prime template.

9. What happens after the trial period?

Once the trial period ends, Amazon will review your performance. If you meet the necessary requirements, you will be approved for full Seller Fulfilled Prime eligibility, and your products will continue to qualify for Prime shipping.

10. Can I make changes to my shipping template during the trial?

Yes, you can make changes to your shipping template during the trial period. Just ensure that any adjustments comply with Amazon's Seller Fulfilled Prime requirements.
