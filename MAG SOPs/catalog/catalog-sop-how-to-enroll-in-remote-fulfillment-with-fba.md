# Catalog SOP: How to enroll in Remote Fulfillment with FBA

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-how-to-enroll-in-remote-fulfillment-with-fba

## **Enroll in Remote Fulfillment with FBA**

**Who is this for :** Everyone in MAG

**Objective :** To help us understand the process of remote fulfillment and to help sellers expand their businesses in order to increase sales through FBA Remote Fulfillment.

With FBA Remote Fulfillment, sellers in the United States can sell to customers in Canada and Mexico without shipping stocks to any of those countries. When you sign up for the program and create offers on Amazon.ca and Amazon.com.mx, FBA will use your inventory in US fulfillment centers to fulfill orders directly to customers from said countries.

**UPDATE: Products made of wood or similar materials require authorization from SAGARPA (Secretaría de Agricultura, Ganadería, Desarrollo Rural, Pesca y Alimentación) and PROFEPA (Procuraduría Federal de Protección al Ambiente) to be imported into Mexico. This requirement applies to all Cross Border programs, including NARF, Global Store, and Core Exports. Currently, there is no available pathway to enable wood tray products for the Mexico marketplace through Remote Fulfillment with FBA.**

**Requirements for account eligibility:**

1. Professional Selling Account registered for FBA in the US and the destination marketplaces
2. North America Unified Account
3. Enabled FBA Export Settings

**Note:** With NARF you do get prime but the delivery time will be longer and an import charge will be added to the customer so it will lower the conversion rate.

In the enrollment workflow, and click Enroll.

• Amazon will evaluate your inventory and automatically add all eligible items to the program.

- any new eligible item added to your US inventory
- any items that are currently ineligible but later become eligible

• If you haven’t enabled automatic listing creation in your setting preferences, create offers for Canada and Mexico so you can sell your US inventory in those marketplaces.

4. Once the account is enrolled in the remote fulfillment, generate and download your ASIN Status report from the [Remote Fulfillment page](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/rafn/inventory) to:

- See which of your ASINs have been added to the program
- Find out if you have to take action on any ASINs so they can be added to the program
- Remove ASINs from the program that you don’t want to sell in a particular marketplace

**Managing your offers**

You can add or remove ASINs for Canada and Mexico in the ASIN Status Report. After making changes, upload the file to the [Remote Fulfillment page](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/rafn/inventory). Eligible ASINs for which you have created offers should be active on Amazon.ca and Amazon.com.mx within 24 hours.

A.) Download the report. Enter No for ASINs to be removed and Yes next to ASINs to be enabled for each Amazon marketplace in question.

Check the ASIN Status report from the [Remote Fulfillment page](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/rafn/inventory) to do the following:

- See which of your ASINs have been added to the program.
- Find out if it’s necessary to take additional action to fully activate the offers on your eligible items.
- Remove ASINs from the program that you don’t want to sell in a particular store.

**Note:** After you enroll in Remote Fulfillment with FBA, it may take up to 24 hours to determine whether your ASIN is eligible.

In some cases, an ASIN is not eligible for Remote Fulfillment because of import or export restrictions.

We can verify this by downloading the report on this [page](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/reportcentral/EXPORTABLE_INVENTORY/1) or go to**Reports > Fulfillment > Inventory > Exportable Inventory (US) > Request Download**

If the ASIN is exportable, **create a case** on a specific marketplace (Amazon.ca or Amazon.mx) depending on where you want to activate the listing for this program.

Explain your issue and let them know that the listing is exportable by providing the Exportable Inventory report as proof to your case.

You may also provide documentation that the product is compliant with country-specific regulations, has complete required import documentation, aren't transportation risks, or a combination of these factors.

FAQs

**1. What is Remote Fulfillment with FBA, and how does it work?**

- Remote Fulfillment with FBA (NARF) enables U.S.-based sellers to sell products to customers in Canada and Mexico without storing inventory in those countries. Amazon fulfills orders using inventory stored in U.S. fulfillment centers and ships the items directly to customers.

**2. What are the eligibility requirements for enrolling in Remote Fulfillment with FBA?**

- To enroll, you must:

  - Have a Professional Selling Account registered for FBA in the U.S. and the destination marketplaces (Canada and Mexico).
  - Enable FBA Export Settings.
  - Have a North America Unified Account.

**3. Can I opt-out of selling certain ASINs in Canada or Mexico?**

- Yes, you can exclude specific ASINs by updating the ASIN Status Report. Enter “No” for ASINs you don’t want to sell in a specific marketplace and re-upload the report to the Remote Fulfillment page.

**4. How do I determine why an ASIN is ineligible for Remote Fulfillment?**

- Download the Exportable Inventory Report from Reports > Fulfillment > Inventory > Exportable Inventory (US). If the ASIN is exportable but still marked as ineligible, create a case with Seller Support. Provide the Exportable Inventory Report as evidence and any required documentation (e.g., compliance certifications).

**5. What should I do if the listing remains inactive after enabling Remote Fulfillment?**

- If a listing is inactive despite meeting requirements:

  - Verify that the ASIN is marked “Yes” in the ASIN Status Report for the relevant marketplace.
  - Ensure compliance with country-specific import/export regulations.
  - Contact Amazon Seller Support if the issue persists, providing necessary reports and documentation.

**6. How does Amazon handle import/export restrictions for Remote Fulfillment?**

- Some ASINs may be restricted due to import/export regulations or classification as hazardous materials. To resolve this:

  - Provide documentation showing compliance with regulations.
  - Include import permits or safety certifications, if required.
  - Open a case with Seller Support for further investigation.

**7. How long does it take to activate offers in Canada or Mexico after enrollment?**

- Eligible ASINs for which you’ve created offers typically become active on [Amazon.ca](http://Amazon.ca) and [Amazon.mx](http://Amazon.mx) within 24 hours of uploading the updated ASIN Status Report.

**8. Can customers in Canada and Mexico enjoy Prime benefits through Remote Fulfillment?**

- Yes, customers can enjoy Prime benefits; however, delivery times will be longer compared to local fulfillment, and additional import charges may apply. These factors could impact the conversion rate.

**9. What happens if new inventory is added after enrollment?**

- Amazon will automatically evaluate and add eligible items to the program. You can review newly added ASINs in the ASIN Status Report to ensure they meet your selling preferences for Canada and Mexico.

**10. What types of products are not eligible for Remote Fulfillment?**

- Products may be ineligible due to:

  - Import/export restrictions.
  - Transportation risks (e.g., hazardous materials).
  - Incomplete compliance documentation.
  - Specific country regulations or marketplace restrictions.

**11. Can I enable or disable offers for Remote Fulfillment in bulk?**

- Yes, use the ASIN Status Report to enable or disable offers in bulk. Mark ASINs as “Yes” or “No” for each marketplace ([Amazon.ca](http://Amazon.ca) or [Amazon.mx](http://Amazon.mx)), then upload the updated Excel file on the Remote Fulfillment page.

**12. What should I do if Remote Fulfillment fees reduce my profit margins?**

- Consider these strategies:

  - Adjust your pricing to account for higher cross-border fees.
  - Evaluate demand in Canada and Mexico to determine if the program is worthwhile for specific products.
  - Use local fulfillment centers for high-demand items in those regions.

**13. How can I track the performance of my Remote Fulfillment offers?**

- Monitor your sales in Canada and Mexico through the Reports section in Seller Central. Analyze conversion rates, shipping times, and customer feedback to assess performance and optimize pricing or offers as needed.

**14. Can I use Remote Fulfillment alongside local fulfillment in Canada or Mexico?**

- Yes, you can combine Remote Fulfillment with local fulfillment centers in Canada and Mexico. Use Remote Fulfillment for low-demand items and local fulfillment for faster delivery of high-demand products.

**15. How do customer returns work with Remote Fulfillment?**

- Returns for Remote Fulfillment orders are processed through Amazon’s return system. Returned inventory is sent back to the U.S. fulfillment center from which the order was fulfilled. Ensure your return policy complies with Amazon's guidelines for cross-border sales.

**16. How do I verify if a product complies with country-specific regulations?**

- Check Amazon's guidelines for selling in Canada and Mexico. If unclear, consult local regulatory agencies or a compliance expert to ensure your product meets all import/export and safety standards.
