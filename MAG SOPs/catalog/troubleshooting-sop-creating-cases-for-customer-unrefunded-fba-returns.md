# Troubleshooting SOP: Creating Cases for Customer Unrefunded FBA Returns

## **Creating Cases for Customer FBA Returns not Refunded**

**Who is this for :** For anyone who is responsible for managing fba orders.

**Objective :** To develop a systematic approach for creating cases to handle customer returns that were not refunded.

**To create a case for customer returns that haven't been refunded, follow these steps:**

1. Go to the "Help" page by clicking on the "Help" link in the top right corner of your Seller Central Account.

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/iPzimage.png)
2. At the bottom of the "Help" page, click on the "Get Support" button.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.29.10%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/1Qaimage.png)
3. Select "Selling on Amazon" then click on "Or, browse for your issue in the menu

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.29.17%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/IPPimage.png)
4. From the drop-down menu, choose "Disbursements and Payments" under the "Your Account" heading.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.29.25%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/sZuimage.png)
5. Click on the "When will I be paid for my Order?" link.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.29.33%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/ozzimage.png)
6. Enter your Order ID and click the "Investigate Order or Next" button.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.29.41%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Qo5image.png)
7. Click on the "View Payments Report" button to see the total refund amount and when you can expect reimbursement.
8. If you do not have a pending payment report, you can search and open a case for the missing reimbursement by returning to the "Help" page, selecting "Selling on Amazon" as the issue, and choosing "Orders" from the "Fulfillment by Amazon" section.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.29.47%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/qVTimage.png)
9. Select "Request Order Reimbursement" and enter your Order IDs one at a time. Then click on the "Check status" button.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.29.55%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/YgIimage.png)

NOTE: If the returned item is in a sellable condition and Amazon has not paid you for it yet, they will not reimburse you. The customer will be refunded instead. If the item is unsellable, Amazon will determine its disposition and reimburse you if the fault wasn't yours.

1. Once you have filled in all the necessary details, click the "Submit" or "Send" button at the bottom of the page.

FAQs

1. What qualifies a return for reimbursement?

A return qualifies for reimbursement if the item is unsellable and the issue was not caused by the seller. If the item is in sellable condition, Amazon will refund the customer instead of reimbursing the seller.

2. How long does Amazon take to process a reimbursement request?

Amazon typically processes reimbursement requests within 7 to 10 business days. However, complex cases may take longer.

3. What if my case is denied?

If your reimbursement case is denied, review the response from Amazon for specific reasons. You can reply to the case with additional supporting documentation, such as the original order details or proof of return status.

4. Can I submit multiple Order IDs at once for reimbursement?

No, you must enter Order IDs one at a time when requesting order reimbursements. Each Order ID requires a separate case investigation.

5. Where can I find the refund status before submitting a case?

You can check the refund status by clicking on "View Payments Report" in Seller Central. This report will show the total refund amount and expected reimbursement date.

6. What happens if Amazon determines the item is sellable, but I have not received payment?

If Amazon classifies the item as sellable but has not reimbursed you, the customer likely received a refund instead. In this case, reimbursement is not eligible.

7. Is there a deadline for submitting a reimbursement request?

Yes, sellers typically have 18 months from the return date to request reimbursement. Check Amazon’s current policy for any updates.

8. What should I include when opening a reimbursement case?

Ensure you provide:

- Order ID
- Refund details from the Payments Report
- Reason for reimbursement request
- Any supporting documents (if applicable)

9. Can I track my reimbursement request after submitting a case?

Yes, go to "Help" > "Your Cases" in Seller Central to track the status of your reimbursement request.

10. What if I accidentally submit the wrong Order ID?

If you submit an incorrect Order ID, respond to the case as soon as possible with the correct details. If the case is closed, you will need to open a new one with the correct Order ID.
