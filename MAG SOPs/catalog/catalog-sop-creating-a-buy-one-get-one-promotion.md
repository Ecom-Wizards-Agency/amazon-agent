# Catalog SOP: Creating a Buy One Get One Promotion

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-creating-a-buy-one-get-one-promotion

## Creating a Buy One Get One Promotion

**Who is this for :**This is for all individuals, sellers, and those who want to know how to create a buy one get one promotion.

O**bjective:**

- Before implementing the proposed Buy One Get One Promotion, it is important to inform, brief details, and get client’s approval. Include the approval proof (screenshot or fireflies call link) as Task Details or as a Comment. Do the Same when there is an edit to be made on running promotions except if the need to edit is critical.
- This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) will help you understand step-by-step how to create a buy-one-get-one promotion.

**To Create a Buy One Get One Promotion on Seller Central, follow these steps:**

1. Click on "Advertising" on the top menu and select "Promotions" from the drop-down menu.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-WIVKCX42.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/XmOimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/XmOimage.png)

1. Click on "Create" under the "Buy One Get One Promotion" tab.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1684330886605.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/sWyimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/sWyimage.png)

- Under "Step 1: Conditions," choose whether the customer needs to buy a certain quantity or every item purchased to qualify for the promotion.
- Select the product for the promotion from the "Product Selection" drop-down menu.
- If necessary, create a new product selection for the promotion by clicking the link and following the instructions.
- Select "Free Items" for the "Buyer Gets" field and "Purchased Items" for the "Applies to" field.
- Enter the number of items the customer needs to purchase to qualify for the promotion in the "Buyer Benefit Applies to a Quantity Of" field.
- Alternatively, select "Additional Item" and choose which ASIN the customer needs to purchase along with the product selection.
- Under "Advanced Options," exclude any product selections from the promotion if desired.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1684330947481.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/D5jimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/D5jimage.png)

- Under "Step 2: Scheduling," choose the start and end dates and times for the promotion.
- Enter an "Internal Description" to help identify the promotion.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1684331069035.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/UUtimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/UUtimage.png)

- Under "Step 3: Additional Options," choose the "Claim Code" option and select whether the promotion is limited to one redemption per customer.
- Combine any number of unrestricted claim codes, but do not include more than one preferential claim code or combine an exclusive claim code with any other claim code.
- For "Single-Use" or "Group" options, enter the claim code and choose whether to allow "Preferential" or "Unrestricted" Claim Code Combinability.

**Note that the "Group" option generates a claim code automatically.**

- Customize messaging by adding text to the "Checkout Display Text," "Purchased Items Display Text," and "Terms and Conditions" fields.
- Choose whether to display the promotion on the detail page and whether to use standardized or customized text.
- Select the Display Precedence number.
- Click "Review" and check the promotion details.
- Click "Submit" to create the promotion, or go back to make changes if necessary.

FAQs

1. Who needs to approve the Buy One Get One promotion before implementation?

Approval should come from the account owner, brand manager, or relevant decision-maker within the company. Proof of approval (e.g., screenshot of email, Fireflies call link) must be attached as a comment in the Task Details before proceeding.

2. Are there any restrictions on which products can be included in a Buy One Get One promotion?

Yes, not all products are eligible for BOGO promotions. Amazon may restrict certain categories, including:

- Digital products, subscriptions, and gift cards
- Some restricted categories (e.g., alcohol, tobacco, prescription drugs)
- Products enrolled in other promotions or discounts that may conflict with the BOGO offer

3. How does a Buy One Get One promotion impact my seller fees?

The standard Amazon referral fee still applies to the purchased item, and fulfillment fees will be charged for both the purchased and free item. Running a BOGO promotion may reduce overall profit margins, so sellers should analyze their costs before launching.

4. Can I modify or cancel a Buy One Get One promotion after it has started?

Yes, you can edit or cancel an active BOGO promotion by:

- Navigating to Advertising > Promotions in Seller Central
- Finding the active promotion under "Manage Promotions"
- Selecting "Edit" to modify or "Cancel" to stop the promotion
  However, once a customer has redeemed the offer, changes may not apply retroactively.

5. Are there any limitations on scheduling a Buy One Get One promotion?

Yes, Amazon may impose restrictions such as:

- A maximum duration for promotions (e.g., 365 days)
- Delayed start times (some promotions may take a few hours to go live)
- Claim code restrictions (single-use vs. group claim codes)

6. How can I track the performance of my Buy One Get One promotion?

You can track the promotion's performance by:

- Going to Advertising > Promotions > Manage Promotions
- Viewing redemption data, sales impact, and claim code usage
- Using the Reports > Business Reports section to analyze total sales and units sold during the promotion period
