# Catalog SOP: How to Check Hidden Suppression

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-how-to-check-hidden-suppression

## How to Check Hidden Suppression

**Who is this for :** This is for all individuals and sellers who have listings with Hidden Suppression.

**Objective :** This aims to provide an Overview of the hidden suppression, how to check it, and a step-by-step guide on how to remove this hidden suppression from your listings.

**What is meant by Hidden Suppression?**

Active listings that are not searchable in the Amazon catalog (amazon.com), hence the term "hidden". When you enter your ASIN in the search bar on the Amazon.com page, it shows no results, then the listing could possibly have hidden suppression.

Please note that hidden suppressions are different from the suppressed list that is found in Manage All Inventory > Search Suppressed.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/uEsimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/uEsimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1720039064962.png)

**Steps:**

1. Log in to seller central using the login email and password.

2. Once logged in to seller central, under the Inventory tab click “Inventory Reports” and download the Category Listing Report (CLR).![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-hidden-suppression-check-image-59pj994h.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/JOGimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/JOGimage.png)

3. If in any case that there is no Category Listing Report (CLR) option on the Inventory Reports, create a ticket to Amazon requesting to enable the Category Listing Report (CLR).

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-hidden-suppression-check-image-y444d3v5.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/6JWimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/6JWimage.png)

4. Once Category Listing Report is downloaded, open the downloaded file then go to the “Review” section and click “Unprotect sheet”.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-how-to-identify--solve-active-listings-with-no-sales-rank-image-erkret0r(2).png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/4JOimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/4JOimage.png)

5. On your keyboard, press CTRL+SHIFT+RIGHT ARROW. Next, go to “Sort & Filter” then click “Filter”. Go to the “item_type” section and you will see a dropdown button then click the dropdown button and select blanks. Lastly, go to the “external_product (Product ID Type)” section you will see a dropdown button then click the dropdown button and select the color yellow.

6. Once done with the steps provided above, the sheet will then be updated and you will see all the remaining ASINs on the sheet. You can now check these ASINs one by one on the Amazon.com page.

7. Go back to seller central and open a new tab and type in Amazon.com. ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-hidden-suppression-check-image-ozyfcr4w(2).png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/0Eoimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/0Eoimage.png)

8. On the search bar on the Amazon.com page, paste the ASIN from the CLR sheet and click search.

9. If the ASIN is showing on the Amazon.com page then the listing is fine.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-hidden-suppression-check-image-ua7yg92v(2)(2)(1).png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Gfaimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/Gfaimage.png)

10. If the ASIN is not showing on the Amazon.com page as it shows no results, then the listing could possibly have hidden suppression.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-hidden-suppression-check-image-rthgx9y9.jpg)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/D5Wimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/D5Wimage.png)

11. Go back to the CLR sheet and highlight the ASIN that shows no results on the Amazon.com page.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-hidden-suppression-check-image-3yr64549%20(1)(1).jpg)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Ss5image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/Ss5image.png)

Only highlight ASIN on the CLR sheet if you found that the listing is not showing on the Amazon.com page. Refer to the attached sample photo above for ASINs highlighted in yellow.

1. Check one by one on the manage inventory all the highlighted ASINs found on the CLR sheet if there is hidden suppression.
(You will only check for ASINs that are showing active on the manage inventory page. Disregard if the status shows Inactive (out of stock), Inactive (closed), etc.)

2. If found that ASIN is showing active on manage inventory but not showing in the Amazon detail page and has no category, then change the category in the “HELP” section on the seller central page.

3. If ASIN is showing active on manage inventory and not showing in the Amazon detail page but has a category, cut a ticket to Amazon requesting to remove the suppression since the listing is not an adult category product.

**Note: In order to check and confirm whether the listing has a category or not, open a new tab and type in “Amazon.com/dp/ASIN” It should be like this “Amazon.com/dp/B0XXXXXXX” (change it to its respective ASIN).**

FAQs

1. What causes hidden suppression on Amazon listings?

Hidden suppression can happen for several reasons, including:

- Missing or incorrect product details (e.g., missing category, product type, or key attributes)
- Listing flagged as an adult product incorrectly
- Restricted keywords or prohibited content in the title, bullet points, or description
- Amazon catalog errors or system glitches
- ASIN merge issues or incorrect product classification

2. How long does it take for Amazon to enable the Category Listing Report (CLR) after requesting it?

Amazon typically enables the CLR within 15 to 60 minutes, but in some cases, it can take up to 24 hours. If it doesn’t appear after 24 hours, follow up with Seller Support.

3. Are there alternative ways to check for hidden suppression without using the CLR report?

Yes, you can also check for hidden suppression by:

- Using the Listing Quality Dashboard under the "Inventory" tab in Seller Central
- Running a search in the Amazon catalog using the ASIN or SKU
- Checking the “Suppressed” and “Inactive” listings section in Manage Inventory

4. What other listing statuses might appear in Manage Inventory, and what do they mean?

- Active (Buyable): The listing is live and visible.
- Active (Not Buyable): The listing is live but suppressed due to pricing or missing details.
- Inactive (Out of Stock): The listing is deactivated due to zero inventory.
- Inactive (Closed): The listing is closed but can be reactivated.
- Inactive (Blocked): The listing is flagged for policy violations.

5. What should I do if Amazon refuses to remove hidden suppression?

If Amazon does not approve your request to remove suppression:

- Double-check your listing details and ensure all required fields are filled out correctly.
- Submit a follow-up case and request clarification on why the listing remains suppressed.
- Escalate the case by asking for a supervisor if your request is repeatedly denied.
- Check Seller Forums or Contact Account Health Support for additional guidance.

6. How long does it take for Amazon to resolve a hidden suppression issue?

Once a case is submitted, Amazon typically resolves suppression issues within 24 to 72 hours. However, if further investigation is needed, it can take longer. You can follow up after 48 hours if no update is provided.
