## **REQUEST A BIN CHECK TO AMAZON**

**Who is this for :** Individuals who are responsible for managing Inventory.

**Objective :** To help us learn the process of requesting a bin check from Amazon. Understand the data required, as well as get information from the request about whether the products in the seller's bin or shelf are accurate, undamaged, and complete.

- Bin checks are internal reviews by Amazon. Amazon is going to look through the bin that belongs to you as a seller, or the shelf that you own. They will check if the products on hand match the product detail page, have damages/defective, wrong items or items have missing parts, etc.

Here are some scenarios when you can request a bin check from Amazon.

- Customer complaints about items received are incorrect or have missing parts.
- Units marked as Defective by Amazon warehouse (not from any FBA return order)
- Units tagged as Expired
- Mislabeled units from shipment
- Overage units from shipment
- Any other FBA order-related concerns

Note: When requesting a bin check, provide at least 3 affected FBA orders to Amazon and detailed requests. (Applicable only for customer complaints for returned fba orders)

FAQs

**1. What is the purpose of a bin check?**

- A bin check ensures that the inventory stored in Amazon’s fulfillment centers matches the product details. It helps identify issues like damaged, defective, incorrect, expired, or mislabeled items and resolves discrepancies between reported and actual inventory.

**2. What happens during a bin check?**

- Amazon’s team reviews the specific bin or shelf assigned to your inventory. They confirm whether the units are correctly labeled, undamaged, complete, and match your product listing details.

**3. When should I request a bin check?**

- You should request a bin check in the following situations:

  - Customers report receiving incorrect, damaged, or incomplete items.
  - Units are tagged as defective or expired by Amazon’s warehouse.
  - Mislabeled or overage units appear in shipments.
  - Any FBA order-related discrepancies or concerns arise.

**4. What information do I need to include in a bin check request?**

- When requesting a bin check, provide:

  - The ASIN(s) of the affected product(s).
  - At least 3 FBA order IDs related to customer complaints (if applicable).
  - A detailed explanation of the issue, such as incorrect items or damaged units.
  - A request for clear images of the items, barcodes, and packaging.

**5. Can I request a bin check for all my inventory at once?**

- No, bin checks are typically limited to specific ASINs or affected units. If you suspect widespread issues, you may need to submit multiple requests for different ASINs or cases.

**6. How long does it take for Amazon to complete a bin check?**

- The time frame for a bin check to be completed varies but generally takes 2–5 business days. Amazon will notify you once the process is complete or if further action is required.

**7. Can I request a bin check for returned FBA orders?**

- Yes, but this only applies to specific cases such as customer complaints about returned items being incorrect or missing parts. Provide at least 3 FBA order IDs related to the issue when submitting your request.

**8. What should I do if Amazon’s bin check results do not resolve the issue?**

- If the results are incomplete or inaccurate, open a follow-up case with Amazon Seller Support. Provide additional evidence, such as customer complaints, photos, or shipment records, to support your claim.

**9. Can I dispute defective or expired unit designations after a bin check?**

- Yes, if you believe the defective or expired designation is incorrect, use the bin check findings as evidence and appeal through Amazon Seller Support. Provide any additional documentation to substantiate your claim.

**10. Are there any restrictions on requesting a bin check?**

- Bin checks are only conducted for FBA inventory-related issues. They cannot be used for inventory managed through FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant) or issues unrelated to Amazon’s fulfillment centers.

**11. What happens if I don’t provide enough information in my bin check request?**

- Incomplete requests may delay the process or result in rejection. Ensure you include all required details, such as ASIN(s), order IDs, and a clear explanation of the issue, to avoid unnecessary back-and-forth with Seller Support.

**12. Can I request a bin check via phone or chat instead of email?**

- Yes, Amazon allows you to choose your preferred contact method (phone, chat, or email) when submitting the bin check request. Be prepared to provide the same detailed information during the conversation.

**13. How can I prevent the need for frequent bin checks?**

- To reduce inventory issues:

  - Ensure shipments are accurately labeled and packed.
  - Regularly audit your listings and inventory.
  - Use a trusted carrier and maintain documentation for all shipments.
  - Respond promptly to customer complaints to address issues before escalation.

**14. Will Amazon provide images for all bin check requests?**

- While Amazon may provide images of the items, barcodes, and packaging, this depends on the nature of the request and availability. If images are not provided, you can request additional follow-up from Seller Support.
