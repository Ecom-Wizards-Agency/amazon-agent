## **How To Create Virtual Bundles**

**Who is this for :**This is intended for All individuals who will be creating virtual bundles.

**Objective :** To equip all with the knowledge and resources necessary to effectively create virtual bundles in order to increase customer engagement and drive sales.

Before implementing, it is important to communicate, and get client’s approval. Include the approval proof (screenshot or fireflies call link) as Task Details or as a Comment.

**Requirements for Virtual Bundling**

- You must have an official brand registry for the products you’re offering.
- The ASINs included in the bundle must be sold ‘New’ and be FBA.
- A bundle must contain 2-5 ASINs.
- Each ASIN in the bundle must be available for individual purchase.
- We recommend bundling products that make sense coming together (ex: spiral-bound notebooks, mechanical pencils, and an eraser).
- Currently Virtual bundle is only available in US Marketplace.

Examples of eligible and ineligible product bundles:

- Red pen (2) – This cannot be a bundle because there is only 1 ASIN.
- Red pen (1) + blue pen (1) – This can be a bundle.
- Red pen (4) + blue pen (7) – This can be a bundle because there are two unique ASINs.

**Part I. Preparing Copy and Bundle Details**

Before starting, make sure that your ASIN bundles are ready. Once ASIN Bundles are ready and their corresponding prices are approved, prepare the images for the bundles.

**Step 4: Create Your Title and Bullets**

Merge the titles and bullets in the best way possible. Make sure to follow the best practices in creating bullets by following this SOP.

**Suggestions:**

- Title - Combine the 2 Titles in the best way possible.
- Bullet 1 - What is the key product feature of ASIN 1
- Bullet 2 - What is the key product feature of ASIN 2
- Bullet 3 - What are the products in this bundle used for?
- Bullet 4 - Why is this bundle a great deal?
- Bullet 5 - Why should you buy from this brand?
- Product Description - Put an introduction, feature ASIN 1, feature ASIN 2, and finally, add a CTA at the very end.

**Part II. Uploading the Virtual Bundles**

**Step 5: Create the Bundle**

- Login to the correct Seller Central account and click this [link](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/bundles/create)
- Then, follow this navigation path to access the Virtual Bundles creation tool:
- Seller Central > Brands > Virtual Bundles

**Step 6: Copy the product information**

Input all the information needed to create the bundle.

Note: the tracker you have create is for reference only and will not be uploaded as Amazon does not have the option to do so. It will be shared via the ASANA task.

FAQs

**1. What is a virtual bundle?**

- A virtual bundle is a grouping of 2–5 ASINs sold together as one listing, designed to increase customer engagement and drive sales. Each ASIN in the bundle must also be available for individual purchase.

**2. Can I create a virtual bundle if my products are not FBA?**

- No, all ASINs included in a virtual bundle must be FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon). If an ASIN is not FBA, it cannot be included in the bundle.

**3. Can I bundle multiple quantities of the same ASIN?**

- No, virtual bundles require at least two unique ASINs. For example, bundling four units of the same pen would not qualify. However, bundling a red pen and a blue pen is eligible.

**4. Are virtual bundles available in all marketplaces?**

- Currently, virtual bundles are only available in the US Marketplace. They cannot be created in other marketplaces at this time.

**5. Do I need brand registry to create virtual bundles?**

- Yes, only products with an official brand registry are eligible for virtual bundles. Ensure that your brand is registered before attempting to create bundles.

**6. How should I price a virtual bundle?**

- The price should reflect the combined value of the ASINs in the bundle while providing a perceived value to the customer. Always seek client approval for bundle prices before proceeding.

**7. What should I do if the bundle image does not reflect immediately?**

- It may take at least 10 minutes or more for the images to appear on the bundle listing. If it takes longer, check the image specifications or contact Seller Support for assistance.

**8. How should I create titles and bullets for virtual bundles?**

- Follow these guidelines:

  - Title: Combine the titles of the included ASINs in a cohesive and clear manner.
  - Bullets:

    - Highlight the key feature of ASIN 1.
    - Highlight the key feature of ASIN 2.
    - Explain the bundle's use case or purpose.
    - Emphasize why the bundle is a great deal.
    - State why the customer should purchase from your brand.

**9. What should I include in the tracker for bundle preparation?**

- The tracker should include:

  - ASINs for bundling
  - Bundle title
  - Bundle price
  - Five bullet points
  - Image link
- This tracker serves as a reference during the bundle creation process.

**10. Can I use the tracker for uploading bundles to Amazon?**

- No, the tracker is only for internal reference. Amazon does not support uploading bundles directly from a spreadsheet. Input the information manually when creating the bundle in Seller Central.

**11. What happens if I create a bundle with ineligible ASINs?**

- If any ASIN in the bundle does not meet the requirements (e.g., not FBA or not brand-registered), Amazon may reject the bundle listing, or it may fail to publish.

**12. How do I ensure client approval for the bundle?**

- Before proceeding, communicate with the client and obtain explicit approval for the bundle. Save the approval as proof (e.g., screenshot or Fireflies call link) and attach it to the Asana task or as a comment.

**13. How should I handle errors during the bundle creation process?**

- If you encounter issues, double-check the following:

  - All ASINs meet the requirements (e.g., FBA, new condition).
  - Titles and bullets are optimized and follow Amazon guidelines.
  - Images are correctly formatted and uploaded.
  - If errors persist, contact Seller Support for further assistance.

**14. Can I update a bundle after it’s published?**

- Yes, you can edit the bundle title, bullets, or price after publishing. However, any significant changes may require re-approval from the client.

**15. Are there any specific product combinations that perform better as bundles?**

- Products that complement each other or are often used together (e.g., notebooks with pens) tend to perform better. Avoid bundling unrelated items, as this may confuse customers or result in poor sales performance.
