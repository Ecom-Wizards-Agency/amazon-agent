## **Purchase Quantity Variance Shortage Claims**

**Who is this for :** This Amazon Catalog SOP: Purchase Quantity Variance Shortage Claims is for anyone who is responsible for managing inventory or logistics and is involved in the process of sending invoices to Amazon for a number of items.

**Objective:**To provide a clear and concise process for disputing a shortage claim resulting from a Purchase Quantity Variance (PQV).

- A shortage occurs when you send an invoice to Amazon for a number of items that are higher than the quantity received. The discrepancy between the quantity invoiced and the quantity received results in what Amazon calls a Purchase Quantity Variance (PQV), which results in a shortage claim.

**Shortages are due to the following causes:**

- Catalog setup
- Advanced Shipment Notification
- Invoicing
- Labeling
- Vendor Lead Time
- Delivery Compliance
- Inbound Receive
- Loss Prevention

**To dispute a shortage claim, you’ll need to provide the quantities, barcodes as well as shipment date of each item. Please follow the process below:**

1. In Vendor Central Select 'Payments' and click 'Invoice'. Please see the image below for your reference.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Gznimage.png)

3. When you access this tool, a list of invoices affected by a shortage claim is displayed. To start the dispute, click on the Action button, and in the pop-up window, click Dispute shortage by ASIN.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-28%20at%206.47.09%E2%80%AFAM.png)

4. The shortage claim dispute process requires that you provide additional information for each item/ASIN which was deducted from the payment of your invoice. Select the individual lines you want to dispute or click Select All.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-28%20at%206.47.18%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/ymlimage.png)

5. Fill in the data in the Dispute Quantity, Shipped Product and Shipped Date columns.

6. Add Comments and enter any additional information to help Amazon in its research and click Save. (We suggest that you provide as much information as possible to confirm your claims. Information such as the ASNs for the shipment and images of the carton level label can expedite the process.)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-28%20at%206.47.31%E2%80%AFAM.png)

7. Click Continue.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-28%20at%206.47.38%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-28%20at%206.47.45%E2%80%AFAM.png)

FAQs

**1. Can all vendors dispute a Purchase Quantity Variance (PQV) shortage claim?**

- Most vendors can dispute PQV shortages, but Amazon may limit dispute eligibility for certain vendor agreements. If the dispute option is missing, contact Amazon Vendor Support.

**2. How long do I have to dispute a shortage claim?**

- Amazon typically allows vendors to dispute shortages within 30–60 days from the invoice date, but this timeframe may vary based on your vendor agreement.

**3. Where can I check the status of my dispute?**

- Go to Vendor Central > Payments > Invoice Disputes.
- Search using the Dispute ID, PO number, or Invoice number.
- The status will show as Pending, Approved, or Denied.

**4. What should I do if my dispute is rejected?**

- Review Amazon’s rejection reason under Vendor Central > Payments > Invoice Disputes.
- If additional proof is needed, submit a new dispute with clearer supporting documents.
- If you believe Amazon made a mistake, contact Amazon Vendor Support with the dispute details.

**5. What if Amazon lost or misplaced the inventory after receiving it?**

- If Amazon has received the shipment but claims a shortage due to misplaced inventory, provide:

  - Proof of Delivery (POD) from the carrier.

    - Bill of Lading (BOL) showing full shipment details.
- Carton labels and packing slips to prove items were shipped correctly.

**6. What supporting documents should I provide for a successful dispute?**

- To improve your chances of approval, include:

  - ASN (Advanced Shipment Notification) details.
  - Packing List & Carton Labels (clearly showing barcodes and quantities).
  - Bill of Lading (BOL) or Proof of Delivery (POD).
  - Images of pallet/carton configurations if available.

**7. Can disputing shortage claims affect my future invoices?**

- Repeated shortage disputes do not directly affect invoices, but a high rate of disputed shortages may impact your vendor performance metrics, potentially leading to stricter compliance requirements.
