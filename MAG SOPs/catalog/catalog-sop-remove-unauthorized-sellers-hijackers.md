## **Remove Unauthorized Sellers (Hijackers)**

**Who is this for :** This Catalog SOP for removing unauthorized sellers (hijackers) is designed for brand owners or their authorized representatives who have registered their brand with Amazon's Brand Registry and are responsible for protecting their brand's intellectual property rights on the platform.

**Objective :** To effectively identify and report unauthorized sellers (hijackers) of a specific product on Amazon, in order to protect the brand's intellectual property rights and ensure customer trust.

**Please note the following important information:**

- You must provide the exact ASIN and seller name to report.
- All report hijackers need to be done through your main Seller Central account. DO NOT USE OTHER ACCESS TO REPORT HIJACKERS.
- The retraction process has been very complicated on Amazon recently. Please note that you might not be able to retract a complaint after it is submitted.

> **Before Submitting a case, Make sure :**
>
> - You have only selected the seller who should not be in the listing.
> - You are not selecting your OWN listing/account to be removed
> - You have NOT chosen the “Remove all sellers” option.
> - The request is not being submitted on a Friday or the day before a holiday after 12 PM EST

FAQs

**1. Can I remove hijackers if I am not in Amazon Brand Registry?**

- No. Amazon only allows brand-registered sellers to report hijackers through the Brand Registry tool. If you are not brand-registered, consider:

  - Filing a Cease & Desist letter through Seller Messaging.
  - Using price-matching strategies to make it unprofitable for hijackers.
  - Registering your brand through Amazon Brand Registry for better control.

**2. How can I confirm if a seller is truly a hijacker?**

- Check for the following signs:

  - Different seller name than your authorized distributors.
  - Lower-than-normal pricing, suggesting liquidation or counterfeit products.
  - Customer complaints about product authenticity.
  - Lack of invoices or documentation proving legal sourcing.

    - If unsure, purchase the product from the suspected hijacker and verify if it’s counterfeit.

**3. What should I do if Amazon refuses to remove a hijacker?**

- If Amazon denies your complaint:

  - Submit another request, making sure you provide clear evidence.
  - If the hijacker is selling counterfeits, buy the product, document proof, and submit a counterfeit claim.
  - Escalate to Amazon Seller Support leadership through a support ticket.

**4. What if the hijacker is selling my real product through unauthorized distribution?**

- If the hijacker is selling genuine but unauthorized products:

  - File a Cease & Desist letter through the seller's Amazon storefront contact.
  - Implement MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) policies with authorized distributors to discourage hijackers.
  - Use Amazon’s Transparency Program to authenticate genuine units.

**5. How can I prevent hijackers from coming back?**

- Enroll in Amazon's Transparency Program to require a unique code on every unit.
- Use Amazon’s Project Zero to automatically remove counterfeits.
- Monitor your listings regularly and report hijackers immediately.
- Use a private label or custom packaging to make unauthorized reselling harder.

**6. Can hijackers affect my Buy Box eligibility?**

- Yes. If a hijacker sells at a lower price, Amazon may give them the Buy Box, reducing your sales and visibility. Removing them restores Buy Box control and pricing stability.
