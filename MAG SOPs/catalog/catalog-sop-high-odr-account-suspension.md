# Catalog SOP: High ODR Account Suspension

## **High ODR Account Suspension**

**Who is this for?** This SOP is for Amazon sellers who have been suspended due to High ODR (Order Defect Rate) and are looking to appeal their account suspension.

**Objective:** To provide a clear and effective appeal process for Amazon sellers who have been suspended due to High ODR (Order Defect Rate). This SOP outlines the best practices for reducing ODR, identifying the root causes of the issue, and submitting a well-structured appeal.

High ODR (Order Defect Rate) suspensions are caused by a high number of negative feedback, A-to-z guarantee claims, and chargeback claims. The order defect rate is supposed to be under 1%. The best ways to reduce the order defect rate are appealing negative feedback through the Feedback Manager page, appealing claims, and increasing the number of total orders (in order to reduce the percentage of defective orders).

After identifying the root causes, review the following template and update it accordingly. Then submit the appeal on the account health page.

Below there are 3 different templates you can choose from. Review them and decide the best option for the client.

**Template 1 for the Submission**

Cause of the Problem:

1. We received three negative feedbacks on our seller account, which caused our ODR to go over 1%.

2. These feedbacks mentioned our shipping time, service, and return policies. We shipped items with an inappropriate shipping service which caused the order to be delivered outside of Amazon's shipping time frame.

3. This negative feedback led to our account suspension.

What have we done to solve the issue?

1. We have shipped all outstanding orders on our Amazon account with verifiable tracking.

2. We have responded to all customer messages and have assisted the customer through a refund or return where applicable.

3. We have audited our inventory to ensure that all listings are accurate and have proper shipping times and methods.

What will we do to prevent future issues?

1. We will ship promptly with verifiable tracking information, using a shipping carrier that will deliver all orders in accordance with the promised delivery date set by Amazon. We have also reset our handling time to ensure that customer expectations are in line with our ability to ship.

2. We have trained our staff to ensure that ALL orders are verified to go out on time and that the shipping method used will get orders delivered on time.

3. We will abide by the Amazon return policy and allow for returns with a FULL refund or replacement for all eligible orders, to ensure customer satisfaction is always achieved.

Amazon - our account has complied with the above issues. We are a large seller and have a valid business. We have learned from this event and will ensure that our account and operations abide by Amazon policies to prevent further negative feedback and to keep the customer happy. Please review our appeal and reactivate our account as we have met Amazon's policy

FAQs

1. How does Amazon calculate Order Defect Rate (ODR)?

Amazon calculates ODR based on three key factors:

- Negative Feedback Rate – The percentage of orders with negative seller feedback.
- A-to-Z Guarantee Claim Rate – The percentage of orders where buyers filed an A-to-Z claim.
- Chargeback Rate – The percentage of orders that resulted in chargebacks.
  All these factors are combined into an overall percentage. If ODR exceeds 1%, the account is at risk of suspension.

2. When does Amazon take action against high ODR?

Amazon may issue warnings before suspending an account. However, if ODR remains above 1% for an extended period or spikes suddenly, Amazon may immediately suspend the account without warning.

3. How often does Amazon review ODR?

Amazon reviews ODR on a rolling 60-day basis. However, recent spikes in negative feedback, A-to-Z claims, or chargebacks can lead to immediate actions.

4. How long does a suspension for high ODR last?

The length of a suspension depends on Amazon’s review process. If a well-structured appeal is submitted, reinstatement can take a few days to several weeks. If the appeal is denied, the suspension can become permanent.

5. Can I appeal a suspension more than once?

Yes, if your initial appeal is rejected, you can submit a revised Plan of Action (POA) addressing Amazon’s concerns. However, multiple rejections reduce the chances of reinstatement.

6. Can I continue selling on another Amazon marketplace if my account is suspended?

If your global Amazon account is linked, a suspension may apply across all regions (e.g., US, UK, Canada). However, if you operate separate accounts, a suspension in one marketplace may not affect the others.

7. What steps can I take to prevent high ODR before suspension?

- Monitor your Order Defect Rate (ODR) dashboard daily.
- Address negative feedback by contacting customers and resolving issues.
- Offer fast and reliable shipping to prevent A-to-Z claims.
- Use signature confirmation and verifiable tracking to prevent lost shipments.
- Ensure accurate product descriptions to reduce returns and chargebacks.

8. Does high ODR affect only specific ASINs or my entire account?

While high ODR may be linked to certain ASINs, Amazon evaluates your entire account when considering suspension. However, individual ASINs with excessive complaints can be delisted or restricted separately.

9. How long does Amazon take to review my appeal?

Amazon typically takes 24–72 hours to respond, but in some cases, it can take up to two weeks. If there is no response, follow up with Account Health Support.

10. Can I recover my account after multiple ODR suspensions?

Repeated ODR suspensions can result in permanent account closure. In rare cases, sellers can appeal for reconsideration after several months, but approval is not guaranteed.
