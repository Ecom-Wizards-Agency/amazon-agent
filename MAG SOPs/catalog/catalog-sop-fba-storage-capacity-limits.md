## FBA Storage Capacity Limits

**Who is this for?**

Individuals who are responsible for managing FBA storage capacity limits.

**Objective:**

To provide an overview of the current storage limits and potential solutions that can be implemented to improve storage capacity.

Amazon's FBA Capacity limits are determined based on storage per cubic feet and are in place to manage unexpected surges in demand and supply chain disruptions, allowing for efficient fulfillment and faster delivery of products to customers. These limits apply to various storage types, including standard-size, oversize, extra-large, apparel, footwear, flammable, and aerosol.

**HOW CAPACITY LIMITS ARE SET**

Each storage type has one capacity limit per month, with individual selling accounts having a fixed limit of 15 cubic feet. New professional selling accounts with less than 39 weeks active in FBA do not have FBA capacity limits to establish their sales record without restrictions. Professional selling accounts with more than 39 weeks active in FBA receive capacity limits based on their IPI score and sales performance. Higher IPI scores result in higher capacity limits, adjusted for sales volume and available capacity. FBA capacity limits consider inventory on hand and shipments on the way to fulfillment centers.

To learn more about FBA Capacity Limits, go to this [link](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/GAFNWEYTJUV2GBFC).

> IMPORTANT NOTE: Accounts with no limit on capacity
>
> - The script will base the recommended units on the available capacity as well since we want to ensure we have enough space to actually ship the recommended number of units. Hence, it says 'Capacity Limit' since there is '0.00' on the total capacity, the script thinks you don't have enough space.
> - 'No Limit' on the capacity may mean that the client has just started using FBA and Amazon is still measuring your needed space by monitoring the FBA sales. Eventually, it will show a limit, normally 2-3 months after selling continuously with FBA.

FAQS

**1. What are FBA storage capacity limits, and why are they implemented?**

- FBA storage capacity limits are restrictions set by Amazon to allocate space efficiently across fulfillment centers, manage demand surges, and ensure faster deliveries. These limits are based on storage type (e.g., standard-size, oversize, apparel) and measured in cubic feet.

**2. How are storage limits determined for sellers?**

- Limits are determined based on:

  - Inventory Performance Index (IPI) score: Higher scores result in higher limits.
  - Sales performance: Consistent sales may increase limits.
  - Account tenure: New professional sellers with fewer than 39 weeks active in FBA are exempt from limits, allowing them to establish sales performance.

**3. What is the Inventory Performance Index (IPI) score, and how does it impact storage limits?**

- The IPI score measures inventory efficiency, including factors like excess inventory, sell-through rates, stranded inventory, and in-stock rates. A higher IPI score typically allows for larger storage limits.

**4. How can I check my current FBA storage capacity limits?**

- Navigate to Inventory → Shipments → Capacity Monitor in Seller Central. Here, you can view your current storage usage, remaining capacity, and limits for each storage type.

**5. What happens if I exceed my storage capacity limit?**

- Exceeding your limit may result in overage fees. These are charged per cubic foot of excess inventory stored and vary based on storage type and time of year (e.g., higher fees during peak seasons).

**6. Can I increase my storage capacity limit temporarily?**

- Yes, you can submit a capacity increase request via the Capacity Manager tool. Specify the storage type, requested volume, and maximum reservation fee you're willing to pay, and provide an expiration date for your request.

**7. What is the maximum reservation fee, and how is it determined?**

- The maximum reservation fee is the amount you’re willing to pay for additional storage. It’s calculated based on historical fees for similar requests and fluctuates depending on demand. Use the Reservation Fee History and Calculator Tool in Seller Central to set an appropriate fee.

**8. How does Amazon provide performance credits for increased capacity?**

- Performance credits are awarded based on sales generated from the additional inventory stored under your increased capacity. These credits can offset the reservation fee and make additional storage more cost-effective.

**9. Can I request capacity increases for multiple storage types simultaneously?**

- Yes, you can submit separate capacity increase requests for different storage types (e.g., standard-size, oversize). Each request must include details specific to the storage type.

**10. What should I do if my capacity increase request is denied?**

- If your request is denied:

  - Review the feedback from Amazon in the Capacity Manager tool.
  - Adjust your maximum reservation fee or requested volume based on competition for space.
  - Resubmit the request with updated details.

**11. How often should I review my FBA storage capacity usage?**

- Regularly monitor your capacity usage, especially during peak sales periods or promotional campaigns. Check the Capacity Monitor in Seller Central to stay updated on your remaining storage and adjust inventory shipments accordingly.

**12. Are there storage limits for seasonal products?**

- Seasonal products are subject to the same storage limits as other items. However, demand for storage space increases during peak seasons, which may result in higher competition and reservation fees for capacity increases.

**13. Can I reduce my storage capacity if I no longer need additional space?**

- No, storage capacity cannot be reduced once a reservation is approved. Plan your requests carefully to avoid unnecessary fees for unused space.

**14. How can I optimize my inventory to avoid exceeding storage limits?**

- To optimize inventory:

  - Focus on high-demand products with fast sell-through rates.
  - Remove stranded or excess inventory through Amazon’s Removal Order process.
  - Regularly review IPI metrics to improve your performance score.

**15. Are there restrictions on which products can be stored under increased capacity?**

- Yes, some products may be restricted due to storage type, hazmat classifications (e.g., flammable or aerosol), or compliance issues. Verify product eligibility before requesting additional capacity.

**16. How long does it take for a capacity increase request to be approved?**

- Approval times vary based on demand and competition for space. Typically, requests are reviewed within a few business days, but peak periods may take longer.

**17. What happens to unsold inventory stored under an increased capacity reservation?**

- Unsold inventory remains subject to storage fees. If it exceeds your standard capacity limits after the reservation expires, overage fees may apply. Plan inventory levels carefully to minimize costs.

**18. Are overage fees higher during peak seasons?**

- Yes, Amazon charges higher overage fees during Q4 (October–December) due to increased demand for storage space. Monitor your capacity usage to avoid these additional fees.

**19. How can I improve my IPI score to increase storage limits?**

- Strategies to improve your IPI score include:

  - Reducing excess inventory by running promotions or creating removal orders.
  - Improving sell-through rates with competitive pricing.
  - Fixing stranded inventory to ensure all items are available for purchase.
  - Maintaining a high in-stock rate for popular products.

**20. What happens if I don't use the additional capacity I requested?**

- You are still responsible for paying the reservation fee for unused space. Carefully calculate your inventory needs before submitting a request to avoid unnecessary costs.
