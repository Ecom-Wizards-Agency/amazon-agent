## How to File a Ticket in Seller Central

**Who is this for :** This is for Amazon Sellers and other employees who need to get in touch with Amazon Seller Associates to escalate an issue within the Seller Central account.

Objective : To provide a step-by-step process on how to file a ticket to troubleshoot or report an issue in Seller Central.

**Instructions:**

1. Click “Help” on the upper right corner of the Seller Central dashboard.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-06-05%20at%209.10.59%E2%80%AFPM.png)

2. Click “Get help and resources”.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-06-05%20at%209.11.59%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/iy6image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/iy6image.png)

3. On the Get Support page, Choose the issue from the menu or click “My issue is not listed” if you can’t find it in the menu.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-06-05%20at%209.12.54%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/vWRimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/vWRimage.png)

4. If your issue is not listed, Provide a short and concise description of the problem. If you updated a listing manually and via flat file, mention these here and add the Batch ID as well. You can also attach screenshots showing the actual error or any screenshots to prove your point. Note: Ideally, we keep the maximum number of SKUs attached in one case to 5. If you need to ticket for more than 5 SKUs, you may file separate tickets.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-06-05%20at%209.14.01%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/aGtimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/aGtimage.png)

5. Click on a category that relates to the reason you're filing the case. For product information-related issues, choose "Products, Listings, or Inventory"

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/r50image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/r50image.png)

6. Make sure you update the short description and choose "email" as your contact method.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202024-06-05%20at%209.14.49%E2%80%AFPM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Ol9image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/Ol9image.png)

7. Enter your email address to receive email notifications from Seller Support, including any updates or requests for additional information needed to resolve the case. They may request proof of product ownership, such as a link to the product on the client's website displaying the UPC or a GS1 certificate.

8. Carefully review the information above and then submit it.

Note: The exact steps may vary based on the type of case you're creating and the reason for the case.

FAQs

1. What is a ticket in Seller Central?

A ticket in Seller Central is a formal request or inquiry submitted to Amazon Seller Support to resolve issues related to your Seller Central account. It’s used to troubleshoot problems, report issues, or escalate matters that require Amazon’s assistance.

2. When should I file a ticket in Seller Central?

You should file a ticket when:

- You encounter technical issues, errors, or glitches in your Seller Central account.
- You need to escalate a concern that can’t be resolved through the usual support channels.
- You have an issue with a product listing, inventory, or any other account-related concerns.

3. How do I file a ticket in Seller Central?

To file a ticket:

1. Go to the “Help” menu on the top-right corner of the Seller Central dashboard.
2. Select “Get help and resources”.
3. Choose the issue from the menu or click “My issue is not listed” to provide a detailed description of the issue.
4. Attach any relevant screenshots or documents, such as error messages or batch IDs.
5. Select the relevant category and update the short description.
6. Choose “email” as the contact method and enter your email address.
7. Review and submit the ticket.

4. Can I file a ticket for multiple SKUs?

It’s recommended to file a separate ticket for more than 5 SKUs to avoid complications. For fewer than 5 SKUs, you can include them in one ticket. Always ensure you provide accurate details for each SKU to help Amazon assist you efficiently.

5. How do I provide additional documentation with my ticket?

When filing your ticket, you can attach relevant screenshots or files (e.g., error screenshots, proof of product ownership). This documentation will help clarify the issue and speed up the resolution process.

6. How will I know if Amazon needs more information?

Once you submit your ticket, Amazon Seller Support will send you updates via email. If they require additional information, they will notify you and provide instructions on how to proceed.

7. What if my issue is not listed in the available categories?

If your issue is not listed, select “My issue is not listed” and provide a brief, clear description of the problem. You can also explain if you’ve made updates manually or through flat files, and provide batch IDs and screenshots as proof of the issue.

8. How do I contact Seller Support if I need urgent assistance?

If you need urgent assistance, follow the ticket submission process and indicate the urgency in your description. In critical cases, you can escalate the issue directly to Seller Executive Relations via email or by using the appeal options available in your Account Health dashboard.

9. What type of issues can I file a ticket for?

Common issues that may require ticket submission include:

- Product listing errors.
- Shipping or inventory-related issues.
- Account or policy violations.
- Technical glitches in Seller Central.

10. How long does it take for Amazon to resolve my ticket?

Resolution times may vary depending on the complexity of the issue. Typically, Amazon Seller Support will provide an initial response within 24 to 48 hours. If the issue is complex, it may take longer. Keep checking your email for updates.
