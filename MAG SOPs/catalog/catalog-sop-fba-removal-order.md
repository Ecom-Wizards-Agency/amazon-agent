## FBA Removal Order

**Who is this for?** Intended for Amazon sellers who want to remove their inventory from Amazon's fulfillment centers.

**Objective:** To help understand the removal order process for Amazon, including the steps involved in creating and fulfilling a removal order.

Video: How to Create a Removal Order on Amazon and What to Do About Overstocked Inventory in 2020

A Removal Order is an order created by an Amazon seller to have Amazon remove inventory from its fulfillment centers. This order is typically used when a seller needs to return or dispose of excess or unwanted inventory. Per-item removal fees apply. For more information, go to the help pages for [*FBA removal order fees*](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/help/200685050)*and*[*FBA disposal order fees*](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/help/5FKTA8LXU4TZPD5)*.*

**REMOVE INVENTORY AUTOMATICALLY**

You can set up automatic removals for your inventory at Amazon fulfillment centers. You can enable removals for unsellable inventory only, for inventory subject to long-term storage fees only, or for both types of inventory.

**To enable automatic removal of inventory:** **1.** Go to Setting ➜ Fulfillment By Amazon **2.**Click Edit ➜ Enable **3.** Select whether you want to return or dispose of the units **4.**Set your schedule **5.**Input all the other required information **6.**Finally, click Update.

**FBA REMOVAL ORDER CLAIMS**

You can file reimbursement claims for removal orders for the following reasons:

| Shipment issue | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Received incorrect units: | You received different items than originally ordered |
| Missing units from shipment: | You received the incorrect number of items |
| Incorrectly removed as "expired:" | The items you received are labeled as expired when they are not |
| Entire shipment not delivered: | The entire shipment is missing |
| Damaged units/packaging: | The items or their original packaging that you received are damaged |
| Missing packaging/parts from units: | The items you received were missing original components, accessories, or packaging |

**NOTE:** All claims must be filed within 18 months of the date the item was removed from the Amazon fulfillment network.

**When filing a claims, Amazon needed the following information and documents:**

- Amazon Removal Order ID
- Shipment ID and active tracking ID or proof of delivery, which the carrier can confirm from the packing slip
- ASINs/FNSKUs and quantities affected
- LPN number if available
- Images of the affected units
- Images of the affected units' product packaging
- Images of the shipping box

FAQS

1.**Can I cancel a removal order after it’s created?**

- Yes, you can cancel a removal order as long as it hasn’t been processed. To cancel:

  - Navigate to Reports > Removal Order Detail Report, locate the removal order, and click Cancel Order if the option is still available.

**2. How long does it take for a removal order to be processed?**

- Removal orders can take up to 90 days or more to process and for the inventory to leave Amazon’s fulfillment centers. Factors like peak seasons or high volumes may extend processing times.

**3. Are there limits to how much inventory I can remove at once?**

- There are no specific limits for the quantity of inventory in a removal order, but large removal orders may take longer to process due to logistical constraints. Monitor your inventory and create multiple smaller orders if needed.

**4. What happens to inventory marked as "Total inventory not available for removal"?**

- Inventory not available for removal may include restricted products, stranded inventory, or items pending fulfillment. Use the legend under "Total inventory not available for removal" to identify specific reasons and resolve issues before creating a removal order.

**5. What are the fees for removal orders?**

- Removal fees depend on the item size and weight. For detailed pricing, refer to Amazon’s FBA Removal Order Fees and FBA Disposal Order Fees help pages.

**6. Can I automate removal orders for unsellable or long-term storage inventory?**

- Yes, you can set up automatic removal for:

  - Unsellable inventory (e.g., customer returns or damaged goods).
  - Inventory subject to long-term storage fees.
  - To enable automation: Go to Settings > Fulfillment by Amazon > Automatic Removals, edit your preferences, and update the schedule.

**7. What are my options for the method of removal?**

- You can choose one of the following:

  - Return to Address: Ship the inventory to your warehouse, manufacturer, or designated address.
  - Dispose: Have Amazon dispose of the inventory at their fulfillment centers.
  - Liquidation: Sell inventory through Amazon’s liquidation program to recover some value.

**8. What should I do if my removal order includes damaged or missing items?**

- If items arrive damaged or are missing, file a reimbursement claim within 18 months of the removal date. Provide the following documents:

  - Removal Order ID.
  - ASINs/FNSKUs and quantities affected.
  - Images of damaged units, packaging, or shipping boxes.
  - Proof of delivery, such as a packing slip or tracking ID.

**9. How can I track the status of a removal order?**

- Track the status of removal orders by navigating to Reports > Removal Order Detail Report. You can see order details, processing progress, and expected completion dates.

1**0. Can I combine multiple SKUs or ASINs in a single removal order?**

- Yes, you can include multiple SKUs or ASINs in one removal order by searching for items under "Search and add items" on the Create Removal Order page and adding them to your list.

**11. What happens to liquidated inventory?**

- Liquidated inventory is sold through Amazon’s liquidation partners, and you’ll receive a portion of the proceeds based on the product’s recoverable value. This option is useful for slow-moving or overstocked items.

**12. Can I choose a specific carrier for returned inventory?**

- No, Amazon determines the carrier used for removal orders. However, you can track the shipment using the tracking ID provided once the order is processed.

**13. What happens if a removal order is delayed beyond 90 days?**

- If the removal order exceeds 90 days, contact Amazon Seller Support with the Removal Order ID for an update. Delays may occur during peak seasons or for inventory requiring special handling.
