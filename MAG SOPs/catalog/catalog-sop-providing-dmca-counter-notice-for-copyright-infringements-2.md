## Providing DMCA Counter Notice for Copyright Infringements

**Who is this for :** Intended for sellers whose ASINs have been taken down due to a copyright infringement claim.

**Objective:** The SOP provides a guide on how to submit a DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act) Counter Notice to address instances of copyright infringement. It outlines the necessary steps and instructions for filing the counter notice, ensuring compliance with legal requirements and helping to protect against wrongful takedowns of content.

> **Before Submitting a case, Make sure :**
>
> - You have only selected the seller who should not be in the listing.
> - You are not selecting your **OWN listing/accoun**t to be removed
> - You have **NOT chosen** the “Remove all sellers” option.
> - The request is not being submitted on a Friday or the day before a holiday after 12 PM EST

#### Refer to the appeal and instructions on how to submit the documents to Amazon:

Dear Amazon Seller Performance Team,

This letter is a formal response to a claim of copyright infringement against ASIN: insert ASIN here received on insert date here at insert time here hours, that were uploaded and published on Amazon.com at this URL location: insert product listing link here.

I believe the claims of copyright infringement made by insert email address here are inaccurate and should be rejected because:

- The material in question is not copyrighted, or the copyright has expired in the United States of America. It is therefore in the public domain and may be reproduced by anyone. The complainant has provided no copyright registration information or other tangible evidence that the material in question is in fact copyrighted, and I have a good faith belief that it is not. The allegation of copyright violation is therefore in dispute, and at present unsupported.
- The complainant does not hold the copyright to the material in question, is not the designated representative of the copyright holder, and therefore lacks standing to assert that my use of the material is a violation of any of the owner’s rights.
- My use of the material is legally protected because it falls within the “fair use” provision of the copyright regulations, as defined in 17 USC 107. If the complainant disagrees that this is fair use, they must work directly with me, through legally viable channels, to resolve the dispute.

Amazon.com and its employees are under no obligation to settle this dispute or to take any action to restrict my speech at the behest of this complainant. The complaint does not follow the prescribed form for notification of an alleged copyright violation as set forth in the Digital Millennium Copyright Act, 17 USC 512©(3). Specifically, the complainant has failed to:

- Provide a complaint in written form. [17 USC 512©(3)(A)]
- Include a physical or electronic signature of the complainant. [17 USC 512©(3)(A)(i)]
- Identify the specific copyrighted work claimed to be infringed, or, if multiple copyrighted works are covered by a single complaint, provide a representative list of such works. [17 USC 512©(3)(A)(ii)]
- Provide sufficient information to identify the complainant, including full name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address. [17 USC 512©(3)(A)(iv)]
- Include a written statement that the complainant has a good faith belief that the use of the disputed material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law. [17 USC 512©(3)(A)(v)]
- Include a written statement that the information in the notification is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that the complainant is authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed. [17 USC 512©(3)(A)(vi)]

This communication to you is a DMCA counter-notification letter as defined in 17 USC 512(g)(3):

I declare, under penalty of perjury, that I have a good faith belief that the complaint of copyright violation is based on mistaken information, misidentification of the material in question, or deliberate misreading of the law.

I ask that Amazon.com, upon receipt of this counter-notification, restore the material in dispute, unless the complainant files suit against me within ten (10) days, pursuant to 17 USC 512(g)(2)(B).

My name, address, and telephone number are:

Insert Name Here

Insert Address Here

Insert Phone Number Here

I hereby consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which I reside (or, if my address is outside the United States, any judicial district in which you may be found). I agree to accept the service of process from the complainant.

Insert Signature Here

Insert Name Here

Date: Insert Date Here

FAQs

#### 1. What is a DMCA Counter Notice?

A DMCA Counter Notice is a formal legal document that a seller can file when their product listing has been taken down due to a claim of copyright infringement. It serves as a response to the claim, disputing the infringement and requesting the reinstatement of the listing.

#### 2. When should I submit a DMCA Counter Notice?

You should submit a DMCA Counter Notice if your ASIN has been taken down due to a copyright infringement claim that you believe is incorrect or unsupported. The notice will dispute the claim and request the reinstatement of your product listing.

#### 3. How do I submit a DMCA Counter Notice to Amazon?

To submit a DMCA Counter Notice:

1. Prepare the Counter Notice: Use the template provided to draft your counter-notice, ensuring you include relevant details such as the ASIN, product listing URL, and reasons why the copyright claim is inaccurate.
2. Submit the Counter Notice: Go to Performance > Account Health in Seller Central, find the ASIN under the "Product Compliance" section, and click on the "Appeal" button next to the listing.
3. Follow the instructions to upload and submit the DMCA Counter Notice to Amazon.

#### 4. What should I include in the DMCA Counter Notice?

Your DMCA Counter Notice should include the following:

- A statement of good faith belief that the copyright claim is inaccurate.
- Explanation of why the material in question is not copyrighted or falls under fair use.
- Specific reasons why the complainant does not hold the copyright or is not authorized to file the claim.
- Confirmation that you understand the legal implications of submitting a false counter-notice.

Refer to the provided template for detailed language to use in your counter-notice.

#### 5. How long does it take for Amazon to process the Counter Notice?

Once you submit your DMCA Counter Notice, Amazon will typically take up to 10 business days to review the counter-notice. If the complainant does not file a lawsuit within 10 days, Amazon will restore the listing.

#### 6. What happens if the complainant doesn't respond within 10 days?

If the complainant does not file a lawsuit within 10 days of receiving your counter-notice, Amazon will typically restore your listing to the product detail page.

#### 7. Can I file a DMCA Counter Notice if the copyright claim is incorrect?

Yes, if you believe the copyright claim is mistaken, misidentified, or misinterpreted, you can submit a DMCA Counter Notice to dispute the claim and request reinstatement of your listing.

#### 8. What if the copyright complaint is legitimate?

If the copyright claim is legitimate and the material is actually copyrighted, your counter-notice may be rejected. In such cases, it’s important to work directly with the complainant to resolve the issue, or you may have to remove the infringing material from your listing.

#### 9. How do I know if my appeal is successful?

If your DMCA Counter Notice is successful, Amazon will notify you that your listing has been reinstated. You can also check the status of the appeal in Account Health to see if the suspension has been lifted.

#### 10. What happens if I continue to have copyright infringement issues?

If you continue to have issues with copyright infringement claims, it’s crucial to:

- Review your listings to ensure they comply with copyright laws.
- Consult with a legal professional if necessary.
- Ensure your content does not infringe on any copyrights and remove any disputed material to avoid further issues with Amazon.
