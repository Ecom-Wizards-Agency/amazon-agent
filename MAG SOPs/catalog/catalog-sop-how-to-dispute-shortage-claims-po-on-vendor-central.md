# Catalog SOP: How to Dispute Shortage Claims PO on Vendor Central

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-how-to-dispute-shortage-claims-po-on-vendor-central

## **How to Dispute Shortage Claims PO on Vendor Central**

**Who is this for :** For individuals who receive shortage claims from Amazon Vendor Central

**Objective :** To help us learn the process of disputing shortage claims on Vendor Central, which helps ensure that vendors are not held responsible for any losses or damages they did not cause and that they are fairly compensated for any legitimate claims.

Vendor Central Shortage Claims imply that Amazon is accusing the Vendor of not supplying as many units to Amazon as they had agreed to during the Purchase Order process. This is not usually true, and we can dispute this with Amazon Vendor Central. We will need to file a shortage claim dispute ticket and attach the PO Number, Invoice Number, SKU List, and BOL, all of which we can gather through the following steps.

Vendor Central sends a shortage claim notification via email, which will be provided to us by the client or account POC. It will contain the information below, the SKU list, and the Bill of Lading (BOL), which we will need upon filing the Shortage Claim Dispute Ticket.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712156370692.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/fUHimage.png)

1. Locate the PO Number by logging in to the [Vendor Central account](https://vendorcentral.amazon.com/), clicking Shipments under the Orders tab, typing in the PO number, and hitting Search.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712156374863.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/oOLimage.png)

2. Grab the carrier tracking numbers for that PO by clicking on View Details under the Action tab and choosing Events and Tracking.

We're now ready to file the ticket.

3. Click Help, found on the upper right-hand side of the screen.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/1.1.1.jpg)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/irHimage.png)

4. Scroll all your way to the bottom of the page and click Contact Us>Payments>Shortage Claim (SC) / Pay-on-Receipt (POR)>Still need help>Send an email

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712156438720.png)

![image-1712156438720.gif](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/image-1712156438720.gif)

5. Fill out the Subject Field, the Shortage Dispute ID (Invoice Number+SCR), and your contact number (you may use any agency-owned Google voice numbers found on the credential sheet).

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712156451855.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/G2Ximage.png)

6. Indicate that the items have been shipped and delivered, provide all the tracking numbers (PRO Numbers), attach the SKUs in question and the BOL, and submit.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712156456986.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/RGvimage.png)

FAQs

**1. What happens after I submit a shortage dispute ticket in Vendor Central?**

**Answer:**After submitting the dispute ticket, Amazon will review your case and typically respond within 24-48 hours. You will receive a confirmation email with a reference number and an update on the status of your dispute. If Amazon requires additional information, they may reach out to you. You can track the status of your dispute ticket through the "Help" section in Vendor Central, where you'll find updates on its resolution. Make sure to monitor the email inbox linked to your Vendor Central account for any communication.

**2. What if the shortage claim is partially legitimate (e.g., only some items were missing)?**

**Answer:**If you believe the shortage claim is partially legitimate, you should still dispute it and provide the necessary supporting evidence. When submitting the dispute ticket, indicate the items that were shipped and delivered and include tracking information for the items that were missing. If there’s a discrepancy, Amazon may ask for clarification or additional documentation to assess the situation. If the missing items were indeed shipped but lost or damaged, make sure to provide proof (e.g., photos or shipment reports) to help Amazon understand the context.

**3. How do I prepare and submit the required documentation (PO Number, Invoice Number, SKU List, BOL)?**

**Answer:**When filing a shortage claim dispute, ensure all required documents are complete and accurate. Here’s how to prepare them:

- PO Number: You can locate the PO Number by logging into your Vendor Central account and searching under the "Shipments" section.
- Invoice Number: This can be found on the corresponding invoice for the shipment. Ensure that the invoice number matches the details in your PO and shipment records.
- SKU List: This should include all SKUs associated with the disputed shipment. You can find this by checking the shipping manifest or your inventory records.
- Bill of Lading (BOL): The BOL, issued by the carrier, is proof that the goods were shipped. You’ll need to attach the BOL to the dispute ticket. Ensure all documents are in PDF or JPEG format when attaching them to the dispute ticket to avoid any upload issues.

**4. How should I handle a shortage claim if the products were damaged or lost in transit?**

**Answer:**If you believe the shortage claim is due to damaged or lost goods during shipping, you should include this information in your dispute. In addition to the usual documentation (PO, Invoice, SKU List, and BOL), provide supporting evidence, such as:

- Photos of the damaged goods (if available).
- Carrier reports or shipment claims (if a claim was filed with the carrier).
- Any correspondence with the carrier regarding the damaged or lost items. Make sure to specify the tracking numbers for the items that were damaged or lost, as this can help Amazon assess whether the shortage was caused by transit issues.

**5. What if there are discrepancies between the BOL, invoice, and shipment records?**

**Answer:**If you notice discrepancies between your BOL, invoice, and shipment records, investigate the issue thoroughly before filing the dispute. It could be that the wrong information was entered or there were issues during the shipping process. To resolve the discrepancy:

- Double-check that the BOL and invoice match the shipment data.
- If there are errors, contact your shipping carrier or warehouse team to resolve them.
- If you’ve found that the discrepancy is due to an error on Amazon’s side, provide corrected documentation when filing your dispute ticket. If the issue is ongoing, consider creating a more robust system to track and verify shipment details to prevent future discrepancies.

**6. What should I do if I receive multiple shortage claims for different shipments?**

**Answer:**If you face multiple shortage claims over time, it's important to review your shipping processes and identify any patterns or recurring issues. Here’s how you can address the problem:

- Identify common factors: Look at whether certain products, warehouses, or carriers are involved in most of the claims.
- Review shipping practices: Ensure that all items are correctly documented and tracked before shipment. Consider working with your fulfillment team to improve packaging and inventory accuracy.
- Communicate with Amazon: If you’ve addressed the internal issues but continue to face claims, reach out to Amazon Vendor Support to discuss potential systemic issues. They may offer suggestions or escalate the issue if it persists.
- Track disputes closely: After each dispute is resolved, ensure all claims are fully closed in Vendor Central. This will help prevent confusion in the future.

**7. Can I get reimbursed for the claimed shortages if I win the dispute?**

**Answer:**Yes, if Amazon accepts your dispute and determines that the shortage claim was incorrect, you should receive reimbursement for the missing goods. Amazon will adjust the payment and ensure you are compensated for any legitimate claims. Keep in mind that the process can take some time, so it’s important to track the resolution and follow up if necessary.

**8. How long does it take for Amazon to resolve a shortage dispute?**

**Answer:**Typically, Amazon will review and respond to your dispute within 24-48 hours. However, if the dispute is more complex or additional documentation is required, the process may take longer. You can track the status of your dispute through the Vendor Central Help section and expect regular updates via email.
