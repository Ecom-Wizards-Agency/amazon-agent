# Catalog SOP: Viewing Lightning Deal Costs and Performance

## Viewing Lightning Deal Costs and Performance

**Who is this for:**This is designed for sellers on the Seller Central platform who want to track and analyze the costs and performance of their Lightning Deals.

**Objective:** To guide sellers in understanding the costs and performance of their Lightning Deals on Amazon. To enable sellers to assess the financial impact and effectiveness of their Lightning Deals, helping them make informed decisions and optimize their promotional strategies on Amazon.

Each successfully run Lightning Deal will incur a charge. The Deal Fee will not be affected by changes to variations, quantity, or price. However, it will vary depending on the country and week in which the Deal is run. Weeks with higher fees coincide with higher customer traffic on Amazon, such as holidays or Black Friday. If your Deal is scheduled during a high-traffic period, the fee will be higher and visible on the Lightning Deal page and Deals page. You will be charged after the scheduled date and time of the Lightning Deal.

**To check the fees for your lightning deal:**

1. Go to the menu on the Seller Central homepage, select "Advertising," and then choose "Deals."

FAQs

1. How long does it take for the Lightning Deal fee to appear in my Payments report?

The Lightning Deal fee typically appears in your Payments report within 24 to 48 hours after the deal ends. However, in some cases, it may take until the next payment cycle for the transaction to be visible.

2. Can all Seller Central users view Lightning Deal costs and performance?

No, only users with the appropriate advertising and financial permissions can access Lightning Deal costs and performance. If you cannot see the "Deals" or "Payments" report sections, check with your account administrator to request access.

3. What factors can improve the performance of a Lightning Deal?

While Lightning Deals do not guarantee sales, several factors can improve their effectiveness:

- Competitive Pricing: Lower prices tend to attract more buyers.
- Strong Reviews & Ratings: Products with positive feedback perform better.
- Well-Timed Scheduling: Running a deal during high-traffic periods (such as Prime Day) can increase visibility.
- Sufficient Inventory: Ensuring enough stock is available helps avoid early deal termination.
- Attractive Product Images & Titles: A well-optimized product listing increases customer engagement.

4. What are the key performance metrics to track for a Lightning Deal?

When reviewing your Lightning Deal performance, focus on:

- Total Sales Revenue – The total earnings from the deal.
- Units Sold – The number of products purchased during the deal.
- Conversion Rate – The percentage of deal page visitors who made a purchase.
- Deal Fee Percentage – The fee relative to total sales.
- Impressions & Click-Through Rate (CTR) – Indicators of customer interest in the deal.

5. When are Lightning Deal fees highest?

Fees are highest during major shopping events and peak shopping seasons. Common high-fee periods include:

- Black Friday & Cyber Monday
- Prime Day
- Back-to-School Season
- Holiday Shopping (November-December)
- Special Sales Events (e.g., Valentine's Day, Mother’s Day, etc.)

6. What happens if I cancel a Lightning Deal after it has started?

If you cancel a Lightning Deal while it is running, you will still be charged the full Deal Fee. However, if you cancel before the scheduled start time, you won’t be charged. If Amazon cancels the deal for any reason, you will not be charged.
