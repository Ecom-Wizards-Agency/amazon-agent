# Catalog SOP: How to Set Up a Coupon

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-how-to-set-up-a-coupon

## **How to Set Up a Coupon**

**Who is this for?** Individuals who wish to effectively utilize Amazon coupons.

**Objective:**To increase customer engagement and boost sales by creating and promoting effective Amazon coupon campaigns that incentivize shoppers to purchase products at a discounted price, while also building brand loyalty and increasing product visibility on the Amazon platform.

Amazon Coupons are a way for online shoppers to save money on the products they're interested in, just like physical coupons you find in your Sunday newspaper. Amazon Coupons show up as a button under the product listing and are redeemable with a simple click on the "Clip Coupon" button.

**Setting up Amazon Coupons**

Go to the **Advertising menu**, select **Coupons.**

There are 2 ways to create a coupon, in bulk or single entry/manual.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/1fzimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Catalog%20SOP-%20How%20to%20Set%20Up%20a%20Coupon%20Image%203(2).png)**Setting up Amazon Coupons Manually**

- Click the **Create a new coupon** button.![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Catalog%20SOP-%20How%20to%20Set%20Up%20a%20Coupon%20Image%204(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/9Zvimage.png)

**Choose Coupon Type**

When creating a new coupon (manually or via bulk), you will now be prompted to choose the coupon type:

- Standard Coupon: General discount coupon.
- Subscribe & Save Coupon: Targeted to repeat purchase customers.
- Reorder Coupon: Targeted at customers who’ve purchased before.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/vWKimage.png)

- You can follow the **Amazon-recommended products**, just tick the box beside the listing. Once ticked, it will be added to the list of **Participating products**.
- ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/2akimage.png)

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/catalog-sop-how-to-set-up-a-coupon-image-5.png)
- Or you can manually search for a particular ASIN just tick the **small box**. Then you will notice that there will be added quantity in the **Participating products**. Click the **Continue button to** continue.

  - Up to 200 ASINs can be added manually to the coupon.

- Fill out all the necessary information:

  - **Schedule** - Set the Start and End dates. Duration is from 1 up to 90 days only.
  - **Discount**- Discount can be a Percentage or Money off

    - **Percentage**- Minimum of 5% up to a maximum of 80%
    - **Money**- The minimum and maximum amount should also be within the minimum and maximum percentages when computed (min 5% to max 80%).
  - **Limit**- We can set the coupon redemption to one per customer.
  - **Budget**- Minimum of $100 up to a maximum of $1,000,000

    - The budget will be shared among the following 2 costs:

      - USD equivalent of the discount you are offering
      - Redemption fees ($0.60 for each redemption)
  - **Title**- Choose a definition that accurately describes the product group added to the coupon.

    - Depending on the percentage or money off selected, this will form the initial title of the coupon:

      - Save 15% on ____________
      - Save $15 on _____________
    - Limit to a maximum of 100 characters only.
  - **Targeting**- You may choose to limit the audience of your coupon to one of the customers.

    - Customer to target:

      - All customer
      - Amazon Prime members
      - Amazon Student members
      - Amazon Family members
- Once all information is filled out, you will notice the **Continue**button will turn to the color yellow. Click the button to continue.

**REMINDER: The coupon will be deactivated automatically when it reached 80% budget utilization.**

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-how-to-set-up-a-coupon-image-tpbd4xcl.jpg)

FAQs

**1. What are Amazon Coupons, and why should I use them?**

- Amazon Coupons allow sellers to offer discounts to customers directly on product detail pages. These coupons help attract attention, increase sales, build brand loyalty, and improve product visibility by incentivizing shoppers with savings.

**2. What’s the difference between creating a coupon manually and in bulk?**

- Manual Creation: Best for creating coupons for a smaller number of products (up to 200 ASINs).
- Bulk Creation: Suitable for managing large campaigns or creating coupons for many ASINs simultaneously using a downloadable spreadsheet template.

**3. How many ASINs can I include in a single coupon?**

- You can add up to 200 ASINs manually per coupon. For bulk creation, there’s no explicit limit, but your spreadsheet size and Amazon’s processing ability may dictate feasibility.

**4. Can I set up a coupon for an out-of-stock or suppressed ASIN?**

- No, coupons cannot be applied to out-of-stock or suppressed ASINs. If these issues occur after the coupon is submitted, the affected ASINs will appear under Needs Action in the coupon dashboard.

**5. How does the budget for a coupon work?**

- The budget includes:

  - Discount Costs: The total amount saved by customers using the coupon.
  - Redemption Fees: Amazon charges $0.60 per coupon redeemed.

    - For example, if your budget is $500 and your redemption fees total $60, $440 remains for discounts.

**6. Can I limit the number of redemptions per customer?**

- Yes, you can set the coupon to one redemption per customer, preventing repeat usage and distributing savings to more unique customers.

**7. What happens if my coupon reaches 80% of its budget?**

- Amazon will automatically deactivate the coupon once it reaches 80% budget utilization to prevent overspending. Plan your budget carefully to avoid abrupt deactivation.

**8. What targeting options are available for Amazon Coupons?**

- You can target:

  - All customers
  - Amazon Prime members
  - Amazon Student members
  - Amazon Family members
  - This allows you to focus discounts on specific customer groups based on your marketing strategy.

**9. What should I do if my bulk coupon setup fails?**

- If your bulk setup fails:

  - Download the Status File from the coupon page.
  - Review the file to identify and fix errors (e.g., formatting issues or incorrect ASINs).
  - Correct the spreadsheet and re-upload it.

**10. How do I handle errors related to manual coupon creation?**

- If errors occur (e.g., suppressed or out-of-stock ASINs), these will appear under Needs Action in the coupon dashboard. Resolve the issue (e.g., restocking the item or unsuppressing the ASIN), and the coupon should activate.

**11. How long can a coupon run?**

- Coupons can run for a minimum of 1 day and a maximum of 90 days. Be sure to set the appropriate start and end dates during setup.

**12. Can I edit a coupon after it’s been submitted?**

- No, coupons cannot be edited once submitted. If changes are needed, deactivate the current coupon and create a new one with the desired adjustments.

**13. How do I check if my coupon is active?**

- After submission:

  - Check the Running section on the coupon dashboard for active coupons.
  - If the coupon is not listed, verify it hasn’t moved to the Needs Action section due to ASIN issues.

**14. What happens if I select a discount amount that doesn’t meet Amazon’s minimum threshold?**

- Amazon requires a minimum 5% discount (either percentage or dollar amount). If the discount is below this threshold, the coupon setup will fail.

**15. Can coupons run alongside other promotions like Lightning Deals?**

- Yes, Amazon Coupons can be combined with other promotions, such as Lightning Deals, for additional savings. Keep in mind that this may lead to higher discounts than initially planned.

**16. What should I include in the coupon title?**

- The coupon title should clearly describe the product or group of products covered. For example:

  - Save 15% on Organic Coffee Beans
  - Save $10 on Home Cleaning Supplies
  - Titles are limited to 100 characters.

**17. How will customers see the coupon on the product detail page?**

- Customers will see a Clip Coupon button under the product listing. Once clicked, the discount will be applied automatically at checkout.

**18. Can I track the performance of my coupons?**

- Yes, you can monitor:

  - Redemption counts (number of times the coupon was used).
  - Budget utilization (percentage of the budget spent).
  - These metrics can be found on the coupon dashboard.

**19. Are there any restrictions on the types of products that can have coupons?**

- Yes, certain restricted products (e.g., alcohol, weapons, and prohibited items) cannot have coupons. Additionally, all ASINs must meet Amazon’s eligibility criteria to be included in a coupon.

**20. How long does it take for a coupon to start after submission?**

- Coupons typically begin running immediately on the specified start date, provided all information is correct and there are no issues with the ASINs.

**21. Can I cancel a coupon after it’s been submitted?**

- Yes, you can cancel a coupon at any time from the coupon dashboard. However, any redemptions made before cancellation will still count toward your budget.
