# Logistics SOP: How to Download Restock Inventory Report in Seller Central

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/logistics-sop-how-to-download-restock-inventory-report-in-seller-central

## **How to Download Restock Inventory Report in Seller Central**

**Who is this for?**Amazon sellers who want to manage their inventory more effectively by monitoring restock inventory levels.

**Objective**: The objective of this guide is to provide step-by-step instructions on how to download a restock inventory report in Amazon Seller Central.

**Instructions :**

1. Go to the "Inventory" tab on the top menu of Seller Central and select "FBA Inventory".

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.44.02%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Tu3image.png)
2. From this page, you can select the "Restock Inventory" from the Inventory drop down.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.44.12%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/xrJimage.png)

  This will display a table with your SKUs, sales summary from the last 30 days, current consumer price on Amazon, days' worth of supply, inbound inventory, number of units on hand, and stock status. If Amazon is satisfied with the available units, there will be no considerations beneath the number of units in their Fulfillment Centers.
3. The "Days of Supply" column provides the "Recommended Replenishment Quantity" and "Recommended Ship Date" columns.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.44.21%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/nJAimage.png)
4. Download the report as an Excel sheet by clicking the "Download Report" button in the top right corner of the page.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.44.29%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/OZjimage.png)
5. Click the "Generate Report" button on the next page. Once the "Report Status" says "Download", click the link to download your report.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%2012.44.37%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/fZeimage.png)
6. Sort your report by "Recommended Replenishment Quantity" and "Recommended Ship Date" to determine what to ship and when.

FAQs

1. How often is the Restock Inventory Report updated?

The report is updated daily, but changes in sales trends, inventory levels, or Amazon's restock recommendations may take 24–48 hours to reflect in the report.

2. Can I download the Restock Inventory Report for a specific date range?

No, Amazon does not allow users to select a specific date range. The report provides real-time restock recommendations based on Amazon’s forecasting and inventory levels.

3. Why don’t I see restock recommendations for certain SKUs?

If an SKU does not have a restock recommendation, it could be because:

- Amazon considers the current inventory level sufficient.
- The product is not eligible for restocking due to restrictions or fulfillment issues.
- Sales velocity for the SKU is too low for Amazon to generate a forecast.

4. What should I do if my report download fails?

If you cannot download the report, try the following:

- Refresh the page and request the report again.
- Clear your browser cache and cookies.
- Use a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge).
- Ensure you have the correct Seller Central permissions.

5. Can I automate the Restock Inventory Report download?

Yes, you can automate report downloads using Amazon’s Reports API or by setting up scheduled reports in Amazon Selling Partner API (SP-API).

6. What does the "Days of Supply" column mean?

The Days of Supply column estimates how long your current inventory will last based on recent sales trends. Amazon calculates this by dividing the available stock by the average daily sales rate.

7. Why is my recommended replenishment quantity different from my usual restock amount?

Amazon's recommendations are based on algorithms considering sales trends, seasonality, inbound shipments, and fulfillment center storage limits. If your usual restock quantity differs, compare it with historical demand and business needs before making final restocking decisions.

8. Can I filter the report by fulfillment center or marketplace?

No, the report does not allow filtering by fulfillment center or marketplace. However, you can manually filter the data in Excel after downloading the report.

9. Does the report account for pending inbound shipments?

Yes, the report includes inbound inventory in its calculations, meaning any shipments in transit to Amazon fulfillment centers are factored into restock recommendations.

10. How do I interpret the "Recommended Ship Date"?

The Recommended Ship Date suggests when you should send inventory to maintain stock levels. If a date appears, it means Amazon anticipates a potential stockout based on current sales trends.
