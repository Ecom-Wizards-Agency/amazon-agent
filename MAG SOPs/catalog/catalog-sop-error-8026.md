# Catalog SOP: Error 8026

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-error-8026

## **Error 8026**

**Who is this for :** All Individuals who are encountering Error 8026.

**Objective :** To be able to list products that require approval, to be able to list new products in categories that require approval, and enable the successful upload of the flat file.

This error occurs when you submit the product data for a category in which you are not authorized to list. For certain products, brands, categories, and sub-categories, Amazon requires sellers to obtain approval before listing products.

**Instructions:**

- Applying to list new products in categories that require approval

An 8026 will be received in the flat file if you perform listing the product via upload using a category flat file that requires approval. Most listings are pushed through in the Manage Inventory but with Inactive status.

1. Click the Inactive link to see or to fix issues.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image9.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/7Gximage.png)

2. Click the Request Approval button to begin the application process.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image10.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/QGyimage.png)

Please note: Amazon may request documents such as performance checks, images of the product, and other qualifications to process the application. If so, please get the necessary documents from the client.

- Applying to list existing products that require approval

3. Hover your mouse over the Catalog menu from the Seller Central Home page and select Add a Product.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/error-8026-image-mr90l61t.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/5Usimage.png)

4. Search for the existing listing in the search box (ASIN, UPC). In the search results,

- Choose a condition from the dropdown
- Click the Show Limitations link next to the item to see restrictions.
- Click the Apply to Sell button to begin the application process.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image8(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Ztzimage.png)

Note: Apply to Sell button is only available if Amazon is currently accepting applications to sell this product. If the button is grayed or not clickable then a listing can’t be created in your catalog.

- Tracking of Application Status

5. Go to the Catalog tab and choose View Selling Applications.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/error-8026-image-hh9i5ng7.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/gceimage.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Ukjimage.png)

The application status will be listed as Approved, Declined, and Draft.

**Approved**

If your request is approved, you will be able to list the product in your selling account.

**Declined**

If the status is declined, you can “Reapply”. Ensure that when asked to upload documents such as purchase invoices, pictures of your physical product, or compliance documents, the document you provide must meet all requirements listed on the Selling Application page. If any information is missing or a requirement is not met, your request will be declined.

**Draft**

A draft is automatically created on your Selling Applications dashboard. From the dashboard, you can open your draft to review requirements and submit your request, view the status of your submitted applications, or re-apply.

For a comprehensive list of product categories that you can sell, see [Overview of Categories](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/G200332540).

FAQs

**1. What is Error 8026, and why does it occur?**

- Error 8026 occurs when you attempt to list a product in a category, brand, or sub-category requiring prior approval from Amazon. Without this approval, the product cannot be listed or made active.

**2. How do I know if a category or product requires approval?**

- You can identify restricted categories or products by:

  - Receiving Error 8026 during a flat file upload.
  - Seeing an Inactive status in Manage Inventory with a link to fix the issue.
  - Clicking Show Limitations when attempting to list the product manually in Seller Central.

**3. What is the difference between listing new products and existing products that require approval?**

- New Products: You need to request approval using a category flat file or via the Request Approval button after the upload results in an Inactive listing.
- Existing Products: Use the Add a Product tool to search for the item, check restrictions, and apply to sell.

**4. What documents might Amazon request during the approval process?**

- Amazon may request the following documents:

  - Performance Checks: Metrics to ensure seller compliance.
  - Product Images: Clear images showing the item and packaging.
  - Invoices: Recent purchase invoices from a credible supplier or manufacturer.
  - Compliance Documents: Certificates of authenticity or regulatory compliance, if applicable.

**5. How do I apply for approval to sell restricted products?**

- Go to Catalog > Add a Product.
- Search for the ASIN or UPC.
- Click Show Limitations.
- Click Apply to Sell to begin the application process.

**6. What does it mean if the “Apply to Sell” button is grayed out?**

- If the button is grayed out, Amazon is not currently accepting applications to sell that product. In this case, you cannot list the item in your catalog.

**7. How can I track the status of my application?**

- You can track your application under Catalog > View Selling Applications. The statuses include:

  - Approved: You can list the product.
  - Declined: Review the reasons and reapply if applicable.
  - Draft: Incomplete applications saved for later submission.

**8. What should I do if my application is declined?**

- Review the reason for the rejection on the Selling Applications page.
- Ensure all required documents are accurate and complete.
- Correct any issues (e.g., missing information or invalid documents).
- Reapply with updated documents.

**9. What happens if my product remains inactive after approval?**

- If your product is still inactive:

  - Verify the status in Manage Inventory.
  - Confirm that the required documents are fully processed.
  - Contact Amazon Seller Support if the issue persists.

**10. How long does it take for Amazon to review a selling application?**

- The review process can take anywhere from 24 hours to several weeks, depending on the complexity of the request and the category.

**11. Can I submit multiple applications for different products or categories simultaneously?**

- Yes, you can submit multiple applications, each specific to the product or category requiring approval. Track their statuses under View Selling Applications.

**12. What is the "Draft" status in the Selling Applications dashboard?**

- The Draft status indicates an incomplete application. You can return to the draft to:

  - Review the requirements.
  - Upload missing documents.
  - Submit the application.

**13. Can I request category approval for multiple products at once?**

- Yes, you can request approval for multiple products in the same category by:

  - Including them in a flat file.
  - Following the category-specific approval process outlined in Seller Central.

**14. What should I include in my purchase invoices for approval?**

- Ensure your invoices include:

  - Supplier’s name and contact information.
  - Your name or business name.
  - Date of purchase.
  - Product details (e.g., name, quantity, and price).
  - Invoices must be recent and from a credible supplier.

**15. Are there specific categories where Error 8026 is more common?**

- Yes, Error 8026 is more common in high-regulation categories like:

  - Electronics
  - Health & Personal Care
  - Beauty
  - Grocery & Gourmet Foods
  - Baby Products

**16. Can I sell restricted products if I don’t meet the requirements?**

- No, you must meet all Amazon’s requirements for the specific category or product. If you don’t, your application will be declined, and the product will remain restricted.

**17. How can I access a comprehensive list of restricted categories?**

- Visit the Overview of Categories page in Seller Central for an up-to-date list of restricted and approval-required categories.

**18. What should I do if I cannot obtain the required documents for approval?**

- If you cannot provide the requested documents:

  - Contact the supplier or manufacturer for proper documentation.
  - Consult with your client or brand manager to acquire necessary records.
  - If unavailable, the product cannot be listed.

**19. Will Error 8026 affect my seller performance metrics?**

- No, Error 8026 itself does not impact your metrics. However, failure to address it could result in missed sales opportunities or delays in listing products.

**20. Who can I contact if I encounter issues during the approval process?**

- For unresolved issues:

  - Contact Amazon Seller Support via the Help section in Seller Central.
  - Provide relevant details, including the ASIN, error code, and supporting documents.
