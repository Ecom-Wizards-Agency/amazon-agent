# Catalog SOP: 360 Images

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-360-images

## **360 Images**

**Who is this for :** All individuals who want to upload images in 360.

**Objective :** To be able to successfully upload images in 360.

**Amazon Requirements:**

First of all, in order to enjoy this feature in your Amazon product listing, you have to be an Amazon Vendor and not a seller.

What you need to comply with:

Next, you still need to comply with Amazon Product Image Requirements to use 360 Spin. Non-compliance with the said requirements would mean your listing gets hidden, not show up on Amazon product search results. To avoid that, you need to know and follow certain guidelines. Technically, your image should:

- be in TIFF (.tif/.tiff), JPEG (.jpeg/.jpg), GIF (.gif), and PNG (.png) format;
- have preferred dimensions of at least 1,500×1,500 pixels or larger;
- be in CMYK or RGB color mode; and,
- The ASIN item should fill at least 85% of the frame (1,275px for 1500px by 1500px) for either the length or width for at least 1 of the 72 images. This means the ASIN should reach 85% of either the length or width of the frame for at least 1 image out of the set.

Additionally, Amazon requires the following site standards for primary product images:

- The image must be professionally photographed. Drawings or illustrations of the product are not allowed.
- No gratuitous or confusing additional objects are added to the image.
- The image should be sharp, professionally lit, and photographed; or scanned, with realistic color and smooth edges.
- Jewel cases, promotional stickers, and cellophane in the images are not allowed.
- Products should fill 85% or more of the image frame.
- The full product must be in the frame.
- Use a pure white background.
- Images should not contain text, graphics, or watermarks.
- Pornographic and offensive materials are not allowed.

Before uploading any images, it is important to communicate, and get client’s approval first. Include the approval proof (screenshot or fireflies call link) as Task Details or as a Comment.

**Instructions:**

Uploading 360 images is the same process as uploading bulk images. You can check the SOP for Bulk Image upload [here](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-sc-bulk-image-upload).

- Once there, you will be asked to log in. Log in using the client's Amazon login. (Can be found on the Credentials Spreadsheet).
- Once you are logged in, the landing page should look something like this:

4. Rename the images to be uploaded:

zip file name:

- The files should be packaged into a .zip file, with
- Ex = ASIN B00E4O05XU would be ‘B00E4O05XU.zip’

.zip file contents:

- The .zip file should contain a 360 folder named <ASIN#>_360
- Ex = B00E4O05XU_360

360° Folder Contents

- The image format must be either TIFF, GIF, PNG, or JPEG.
- The image filename extension must be ".png"
- The file name format = <ASIN#>_360_####_web.png (where #### is the number of the file in the sequence, starting at 0000).
- File sequence Ex = B00E4O05XU_360_0000_web.png, B00E4O05XU_360_0001_web.png, … B00E4O05XU_360_0071_web.png
- Image ASIN rotation should rotate counterclockwise as the image # progresses (0000-00NN), and be at the correct angle based on your total set of images.
- Correct angle ex = 72 images = 5 deg rotation (360 degrees total)
- Rotation Ex = for a 72 image set, 1st = B00E4O05XU_360_0000_web.png = 0 degrees, 2nd = B00E4O05XU_360_0001_web.png = 5 degrees counterclockwise, etc
- The first image in the sequence (<ASIN#>_360_0000_web.png) should contain an ASIN angled at 0 degrees (front-facing item).

5. Drag the created .zip file to the Bulk Image Uploader. It will show a progress bar while the image is being uploaded.

FAQs

1. Who is eligible to upload 360 images on Amazon?

Only Amazon Vendors can use the 360 Spin feature. This feature is not available for third-party sellers (Amazon Seller Central users).

2. What are the Amazon requirements for 360 Spin images?

To use 360 images, you must follow Amazon’s product image requirements, including:

- Image format: TIFF (.tif/.tiff), JPEG (.jpeg/.jpg), GIF (.gif), PNG (.png)
- Minimum dimensions: 1,500 × 1,500 pixels
- Color mode: CMYK or RGB
- The product should fill at least 85% of the frame in at least one of the 72 images
- A pure white background (RGB 255,255,255) is required
- No text, graphics, watermarks, or promotional stickers

3. What are Amazon’s primary image standards for 360 images?

- The product must be professionally photographed (no drawings or illustrations).
- No extra objects in the image that could confuse buyers.
- Images must be sharp, well-lit, and realistic in color.
- The product must be fully visible in the frame.
- Jewel cases, promotional stickers, or cellophane are not allowed.
- No pornographic or offensive content.

4. What should I do before uploading 360 images?

- Get client approval before uploading.
- Include proof of approval (screenshot or call log) in the task details or comments.
- Follow Amazon’s bulk image upload process.

5. How do I format and rename 360 images for upload?

- Zip file name: <ASIN>.zip (e.g., B00E4O05XU.zip)
- Inside the .zip file: A folder named <ASIN#>_360 (e.g., B00E4O05XU_360)
- File format: .png
- Filename format: <ASIN#>_360_####_web.png (#### = image sequence, starting at 0000)
- Example sequence:

  - B00E4O05XU_360_0000_web.png (0° front-facing)
  - B00E4O05XU_360_0001_web.png (5° counterclockwise)
  - Continue rotating 5° for a total of 72 images (360° spin)

6. How do I upload 360 images to Amazon?

1. Go to Seller Central and log in.
2. Navigate to Catalog > Upload Images.
3. Select "Bulk Image Upload".
4. Drag and drop the .zip file into the uploader.
5. Click "Submit Images" after uploading.

7. How long does it take for 360 images to appear on my listing?

Processing time varies, but it can take 24–72 hours for the 360 images to go live.

8. What if Amazon rejects my 360 images?

- Check if the file naming, dimensions, or formats meet Amazon’s requirements.
- Ensure the product fills 85% of the frame in at least one image.
- Verify that no text, watermarks, or non-compliant elements are present.
- If the issue persists, contact Amazon Seller Support and provide the pre-formatted assets via Dropbox or another file-sharing service.
