# Catalog SOP: Managing Suppressed Lightning Deals

## Managing Suppressed Lightning Deals

**Who is this for :** For individuals who are involved in managing and resolving suppressed lightning deals on an e-commerce platform. It can be used by the platform's administrators, customer service representatives, and other individuals responsible for ensuring the smooth functioning of lightning deals.

**Objective :** To manage suppressed lightning deals is to establish a clear and structured process for identifying, evaluating, and resolving issues related to suppressed lightning deals on an e-commerce platform. The SOP aims to ensure that suppressed lightning deals are promptly addressed, allowing for effective resolution and improved customer experience.

There are several reasons why a Lightning Deal might be suppressed. These include:

1. The product in the Deal is no longer eligible or violates eligibility criteria.
2. If the product is fulfilled by Amazon (FBA), Amazon has run out of inventory.
3. The Deal price exceeds the Maximum Deal Price, which considers the lowest price from any seller with an offer on the product in New condition.
4. The Deal quantity falls below the Minimum Deal Quantity specified during setup.

**To ensure your upcoming Deals remain eligible, you can monitor them from the Deals page leading up to the start date. If any of your Deals become suppressed, you'll see a warning at the top of the Deals page. Click the "View these Deals" link to locate the Deals that require attention.**

Note that errors must be resolved 25 hours before the scheduled start time of the Deal to avoid cancellation. Here are some steps you can take to resolve suppression issues:

- For ineligible or violating products, make them eligible or remove them from the Deal.
- For FBA products with out-of-stock issues, ship outstanding units to Amazon.
- For Deals with prices exceeding the Maximum Deal Price, reduce the Deal price to meet or be lower than the maximum.
- For Deals with quantities below the Minimum Deal Quantity, increase the quantity to meet or exceed the minimum.

For more information, see Troubleshooting Suppressed and Active Deals Help page https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/GKN9A84DGTYWYHWY

FAQs

1. What factors determine product eligibility for a Lightning Deal?

A product must meet Amazon’s Lightning Deal eligibility criteria, which typically include:

- A strong sales history and positive customer ratings
- Compliance with Amazon’s category and listing policies
- No policy violations or restricted keywords in the listing
- Meeting the minimum inventory and pricing requirements
- Not being part of a restricted product category (e.g., alcohol, certain electronics)

2. How can I prevent my Lightning Deal from being suppressed before it starts?

To reduce the risk of suppression:

- Check eligibility in advance – Review product and pricing requirements before submitting the deal.
- Ensure sufficient inventory – Have enough stock in Amazon’s fulfillment centers to meet the minimum deal quantity.
- Monitor pricing changes – Keep the deal price within the required range and check for competing offers.
- Review deal status regularly – Check the Deals page leading up to the launch date for alerts.

3. How does Amazon notify sellers about suppressed Lightning Deals?

Sellers are alerted about suppressed deals in the following ways:

- A warning banner appears on the Deals page in Seller Central.
- A "View these Deals" link directs sellers to suppressed deals that need attention.
- Some sellers may receive email notifications from Amazon if their deal is at risk.

4. How long does it take for inventory restocking to affect a Lightning Deal?

Restocking times vary based on shipping method:

- FBA Standard Restock: Typically 2–3 weeks, depending on warehouse processing.
- FBA Expedited Restock: May take 3–5 business days if using Amazon’s partnered carrier programs.
- Self-Fulfilled (FBM) Inventory Updates: Changes are reflected as soon as stock is updated in Seller Central.
  If inventory does not arrive before the 25-hour deadline, the deal may be canceled.

5. What happens if I don’t resolve a suppression issue before the 25-hour deadline?

If the issue is not fixed at least 25 hours before the deal’s scheduled start time:

- The deal will automatically be canceled by Amazon.
- You will not be charged a fee, but you may lose the opportunity to run the promotion.
- You may need to resubmit the deal for approval and schedule it for a later date.

6. How can I confirm that a suppressed Lightning Deal has been reinstated?

After making necessary changes, check the deal’s status by:

- Going to the Deals page in Seller Central and ensuring the warning banner is gone.
- Clicking "View" on the deal to check if the suppression issue is still highlighted.
- Waiting for Amazon’s system to update—status changes may take up to an hour to reflect.
