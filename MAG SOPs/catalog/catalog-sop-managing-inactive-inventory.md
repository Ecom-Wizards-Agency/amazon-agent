# Catalog SOP: Managing Inactive Inventory

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-managing-inactive-inventory

## **Managing Inactive Inventory**

**Who is this for :** This is for all individuals and sellers who want to know how to manage and take action for their Inactive Inventory.

**Objective :** This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) will help you understand and fix your Inactive Inventory in your Seller Central Account.

**To manage your inactive inventory, which can be caused by various factors like stock shortages or pricing issues, follow these steps:**

1. Go to the "Manage Inventory" page in Seller Central by selecting it from the drop-down menu under the "Inventory" tab.

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/vjtimage.png)
2. Click on the "Search Suppressed and Inactive Listings" link at the top of the next page.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%202.56.19%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/M69image.png)
3. You'll see a table with all your inactive SKUs and their respective reasons.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%202.56.27%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/PoSimage.png)

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%202.56.27%E2%80%AFAM.png)
4. Choose the options on the left to group items with the same reason and take bulk actions by clicking the "Action" button at the top left of the table.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%202.56.36%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/MESimage.png)
5. Filter by fulfillment type to Fulfilled By Amazon or Merchant Fulfilled. Each line item displays the inactive reason, issue description, and fix issues.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%202.56.43%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/iscimage.png)
6. To reactivate an inactive item, click on the "Fix Issue" button to the right of each line and make the necessary amendments.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-15%20at%202.56.53%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/4IUimage.png)
7. Some reasons for the inactive stock include being out of stock, having a future start date, pricing issues, being paused or blocked, no longer selling the item, requiring approval, or being blocked unless FBA.
8. Click on the "Fix Issue" button to learn how to resolve the issue and reactivate the SKU.

FAQs

1. What causes inventory to become inactive on Amazon?

Inventory can become inactive due to various reasons, such as:

- Stock shortages (out of stock).
- Pricing issues, such as incorrect pricing or pricing violations.
- Future start dates (if a listing is scheduled to go live at a future time).
- Items being paused or blocked, often due to Amazon's policies or seller account issues.
- Approval required for selling specific products.
- Products no longer being sold or removed from the catalog.

2. How do I find my inactive inventory?

To find inactive inventory, go to the Manage Inventory page in Seller Central under the Inventory tab. Then, click on the "Search Suppressed and Inactive Listings" link at the top of the page to view all inactive SKUs and their reasons for being inactive.

3. What should I do if I see inactive items due to out-of-stock issues?

If an item is inactive due to being out of stock, restock the item as soon as possible. Once it is available in your inventory again, the SKU should automatically become active. You can also manually update the quantity in Seller Central if needed.

4. How can I group inactive inventory by reason for easier management?

On the inactive listings page, you can use the options on the left to group items by their inactivity reason. This allows you to take bulk actions, making it easier to manage and address multiple issues at once.

5. Can I filter inactive inventory by fulfillment type?

Yes, you can filter inactive inventory by Fulfilled by Amazon (FBA) or Merchant Fulfilled (MFN) to help narrow down the issue and apply fixes more effectively.

6. How do I reactivate an inactive SKU?

To reactivate an inactive SKU, click the "Fix Issue" button next to the item. Follow the prompts to resolve the specific issue (e.g., correcting the price, updating inventory, or resolving approval requirements). Once the issue is fixed, the SKU will be reactivated.

7. What happens if my item is inactive due to a blocked status?

If your item is blocked, you may need to contact Amazon Seller Support for clarification on why it is blocked and what actions you can take. Blocks are often due to policy violations or restrictions related to the product category.

8. What should I do if an inactive SKU requires approval to be sold?

If the item requires approval to be sold, you’ll need to apply for approval through the Seller Central interface. You may need to provide additional documentation or meet Amazon's criteria for that category before the SKU can be reactivated.

9. How can I identify the specific issue that caused my inventory to be inactive?

Each inactive SKU displays the inactive reason and an issue description. You can view these details to understand what caused the SKU to become inactive and take the necessary corrective actions.

10. How long does it take for changes to reflect after reactivating an inactive SKU?

Once you’ve resolved the issue and clicked "Fix Issue", changes typically take up to 24 hours to reflect. However, it may take longer depending on the complexity of the issue or if the SKU needs further review by Amazon.
