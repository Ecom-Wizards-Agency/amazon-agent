# Catalog SOP: Breaking a Phantom Parentage

## Breaking a Phantom Parentage

**Who is this for :** This SOP for Breaking a Phantom Parentage is intended for those who have encountered phantom parentage issues and need guidance on resolving them.

**Objective :** The objective of this SOP is to provide a clear and efficient process for identifying and resolving phantom parentage issues on Amazon.

**Here are some steps that you can take to identify the issue:**

Phantom parentage refers to a situation on Amazon where another seller has created a listing or variation for your ASIN, causing issues in making proper changes. It occurs when historical data from another seller remains in the system, leading to the creation of a duplicate listing, even if you have Brand Registry or are the sole seller of that product. This problem can also arise when the Amazon catalog team has locked the listing, which is more common on Vendor Central.

Check the variation wizard and if you come across an ASIN or variation that is not supposed to be in the listing, it indicates a 'phantom parentage' problem.

**How to Ticket Amazon:**

1. Create separate tickets for both Seller Central and the Brand Registry Portal.

- Create Ticket from Seller Central and Brand Registry

  - [Catalog SOP: How to Submit a Ticket to Brand Registry](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/brand-registry-sop-how-to-submit-a-ticket-to-brand-registry)
  - [Catalog SOP: How to File a Ticket in Seller Central](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-how-to-file-a-ticket-in-seller-central)
- Use the ADMIN Seller Central email when creating a ticket
- Finally, call Amazon from the created ticket (request for the captive team) and ask them to action it.

2. Use the template below as a starting point, and adjust it to fit your specific scenario:

Dear Amazon Seller Support,

We are having issues with phantom parentages on our listings which prevent us from making proper changes on our listings.

Entity ID: (Enter this)

Merchant ID: (Enter this)

We are the administrator for the brand: (Enter brand name)

For the Following ASINs:(the child ASINs we want to remove)

We would like these ASINs removed from the PARENT ASIN (Enter parent ASIN from wizard) See screenshot from the Variation Wizard: (Take a screenshot and show Amazon)

Please action this request and let us know when done.

Thanks,

FAQs

**1. Why does phantom parentage happen?**

- Phantom parentage usually occurs due to Amazon's catalog merging errors, historical data from another seller, or incorrect parent-child relationships being auto-generated. Even if you have Brand Registry or are the sole seller, Amazon may still link your ASIN to an unrelated variation.

**2. How does phantom parentage affect my listing?**

- Prevents you from making listing changes (title, images, bullet points, etc.)
- Incorrect variations may appear under your ASIN
- Buy Box eligibility may be impacted if another seller’s variation is incorrectly linked
- Confuses customers who see unrelated products grouped together

**3. How can I prevent phantom parentage from happening again?**

- Regularly check your variation relationships using the Variation Wizard
- Submit an accurate flat file update for your ASINs with correct variation details
- Monitor changes in your catalog history and report unauthorized edits immediately
- Use the Brand Registry tool to lock your listing attributes

**4. How long does it take for Amazon to resolve phantom parentage issues?**

- Resolution time varies. On average, it can take 7–14 days, but some cases require multiple escalations. If you don’t see progress after a week, follow up daily and request escalation.

**5. What should I do if Amazon doesn’t fix the issue after submitting a case?**

- Reopen the case and insist on escalation to the Catalog or Captive Team
- Call Amazon Support instead of relying on email responses
- Submit a case under both Seller Central and Brand Registry
- Provide screenshots and explain how the incorrect parentage is affecting your listing

**6. What if the phantom ASIN is locked by Amazon’s catalog team?**

- If the ASIN is locked:

  - Request a manual override from the Catalog Team
  - Provide proof of brand ownership through Brand Registry
  - Ask Amazon to remove the variation tree and unlink your ASIN manually

**7. Can I fix phantom parentage by deleting and relisting my ASIN?**

- No, deleting and relisting won’t fix phantom parentage because the issue is at the catalog level. Amazon still retains listing history, which means the phantom parent will likely remain. The correct approach is filing a ticket and escalating the case.

**8. What’s the best way to get Amazon’s attention on this issue?**

- Submit separate tickets via both Seller Central and Brand Registry
- Use the "Call Me" option in Seller Support to explain the urgency
- Request escalation to the Captive Team immediately
- Reiterate that your brand ownership is being affected and the incorrect parentage is damaging the listing
