## **Disease, Medical & Pathogens Claims of Canada (CA)**

**Who is this for:**

- This is for Amazon Canada sellers who are interested in selling dietary supplements with disease treatment claims licensed as Natural Health Products (NHP) in Health Canada.
- It is intended for those who need guidance on how to ensure their product listings are compliant with Health Canada regulations and Amazon Canada policies.

**Objective:**

To provide instructions for Amazon Canada sellers on how to ensure compliance with Health Canada regulations when selling dietary supplements with disease treatment claims licensed as Natural Health Products (NHP) on Amazon Canada.

Unlike the USA, Canada allows the sale of dietary supplements with disease treatment claims as long as they are licensed as Natural Health Products **(NHP)** in [Health Canada](https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada.html). Licensed NHPs will obtain an **eight-digit Natural Product Number** **(NPN)**, and an NPN link and webpage where the Product Information is listed. The two most important parts of it are **“Recommended Use or Purpose”** and **“List of Medicinal Ingredients”**.

**Check your NPN here**: [Licensed Natural Health Products Database](https://health-products.canada.ca/lnhpd-bdpsnh/index-eng.jsp)

**Additional Compliance Note:**

French Language Compliance for Quebec — Bill 96 Update (Effective June 1, 2025)

### French Language Compliance for Quebec — Bill 96 Update (Effective June 1, 2025)

Who is impacted?

All sellers and businesses selling goods or services in Quebec, including those listing on Amazon Canada.

What are the new requirements?

- **Signage:**
  All signage (outside and inside) that shows business names or trademarks must be **exclusively or predominantly in French**.
- **Packaging:**
  Product packaging sold in Quebec must include **French translations of all generic terms and product characteristics**, even if these terms are part of a registered or pending trademark.
- **Trademark Status:**
  A trademark with a formal application at the Canadian Intellectual Property Office (CIPO) is treated as registered. If unregistered, French translations are mandatory.
- **Compliance Deadline:**
  Products manufactured before June 1, 2025, may be sold until June 1, 2027. After this, only packaging meeting French language requirements can be sold.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

The Office québécois de la langue française (OQLF) enforces these rules and can issue fines from **$3,000 up to $90,000 per day for repeated offenses**.

What You Need to Do

1. Review all your Quebec-targeted product packaging and signage for French language compliance.
2. Add French translations for all descriptive terms and product information on packaging.
3. Verify trademark registration/application status and translate if necessary.
4. Plan inventory and packaging updates to meet the June 1, 2027 deadline.
5. Consult with your legal or compliance team if you have questions.

**Recommended Use or Purpose**

Whatever medical, disease, prevention, or treatment claims present in the product description and product label should be present or within the interpretation of recommended use or purpose in the Licensed Natural Health Products Database (LNHPD) or the NPN website. Adding claims, misinterpreting, or misleading the purpose will put restrictions on the products in the Amazon Canada marketplace.

**List of Medicinal Ingredients**

There are regulated ingredients that need to get approval from Health Canada. Ingredients shown on the product label should also be present in the Product Information. If Amazon Canada claims that you aren’t allowed to use or sell the product’s ingredients, you can point out that the ingredients in question are present in the List of Medicinal Ingredients.

For more information about the “Compliance Checklist of NHPs” please see this [article](https://sellercentral.amazon.ca/help/hub/reference/G200164490).

**Compliance List for NHP Products**

- Ensure the Natural Product Number (NPN), the Drug Identification Number (DIN), or the Homeopathic Medicine Number (DIN-HM) is clearly visible on the product packaging. If not please upload images that clearly display the applicable license number for your product.
- Ensure the ingredient list is clearly visible on the product packaging. If not, please upload images that clearly display the ingredient list for your product.
- Ensure the NPN, DIN, or DIN-HM is uploaded to the license information section in Seller Central.
- Ensure all claims on the detail page and images comply with the terms of the product's market authorization. Product description should be accurate according to the product information from Health Canada
- Ensure the product packaging clearly shows English and French. If not, please upload images that clearly show an English and French label.

**Policy Violation Details**

The violation details are located under “Reason” in Restricted Product Policy Violations in Account Health Performance. The violation details of unlicensed NHP vary depending on the yank reason. There are 3 types of it but not limited to:

**General**

- This product has been identified as Natural Health Product (NHP) or drug product, and it is subject to the provisions of the Food and Drugs Act (FDA) and its regulations. All NHPs and drug products must be licensed by Health Canada, meet labeling requirements, and comply with the terms of their market authorization. Amazon policy prohibits the sale or listing of unlicensed NHPs and drug products. Before submitting an appeal please ensure the required labels, including the DIN or NPN and the ingredient list, are clearly visible and uploaded to the product detail page and that all claims comply with the terms of the product’s market authorization. Please also review [Amazon's policy for Drugs and NHPs](https://sellercentral.amazon.ca/gp/help/external/help.html?itemID=200164490&language=en_CA&ref=efph_200164490_relt_200164650) and/or [Amazon's policy for Cosmetics](https://sellercentral.amazon.ca/gp/help/external/help.html?itemID=200164470&language=en_CA&ref=efph_200164470_relt_200164490)

**Medical/Disease Claims**

- This product has been identified as an unlicensed drug or Natural Health Product (NHP) that makes prohibited disease claims regarding diabetes. Amazon policy prohibits the sale or listing of prescription-only or pharmacy-only products. If you believe that this determination has been made in error, please appeal with your Drug Identification Number from Health Canada. For more information click [HERE](https://sellercentral.amazon.ca/gp/help/external/G200164490).

**Prohibited Ingredients**

- This product has been identified as a prescription-only or pharmacy-only product containing the active ingredient Vitamin A. Amazon policy prohibits the sale or listing of prescription-only or pharmacy-only products. If you believe that this determination has been made in error, please appeal with your Drug Identification Number from Health Canada. For more information Click [Here](https://sellercentral.amazon.ca/gp/help/external/G200164490).

**How to Fix?**

- Document the changes/actions you will take

**IMPORTANT : Require written approval by Manager before accepting the product policy violation.**

**NHP Registered**

1. Obtain the NPN number and the NPN link from the seller or the point of contact (POC).
2. Go to Inventory > Manage Inventory > Look for the yanked ASIN > Edit > Compliance > Put the **NPN** in Compliance Certification Metadata > Fill out the Certification Date Of Issue > Compliance Certification Value = Natural Product Number > Save and Finish

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712151865350.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/h5Nimage.png)

1. Compare the Product Information from the NPN link and the Product Description (including A+ content) from seller central (SC). If they’re in harmony, then keep things as is. If not, adjust the information from SC.
2. If you’re confident that everything is set, go to Performance > Account Health > Restricted Product Policy Violations > Look for the ASIN > Appeal > Check the 2 boxes and Submit. Wait 24 hours before the listing gets reinstated.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/n7Rimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712151869370.png)**Non-NHP Registered**

1. Check the product images and remove the prohibited claims from infographics.
2. Download a category listing report (CLR) and remove all prohibited claims from the following attributes.

  1. Title
  2. Description
  3. Bullet points
  4. Search terms
  5. Intended Use
  6. Other Attributes
3. Upload the updated CLR and take the batch ID.
4. Go to A+ contents and look for the ASIN. Remove the prohibited claims from the description, images, and alt-text keywords.
5. Go to Account Health under Restricted Products and click “Appeal”. Check the 2 boxes and submit. Wait for 24 hours before the listing gets reinstated.

**Remember:**

- If the product isn’t NHP registered and if the prohibited claims are present on the label, then there’s no path of reinstatement.

FAQs

**1. What should I do if my product images or documents are not correctly uploaded to Seller Central?**

**Answer:**If the required images, such as the NPN number or ingredient list, are not clearly visible or properly uploaded, you will need to update the product listing in Seller Central. Go to the Compliance Certification Metadata section and upload clear images of the product packaging. Ensure the images comply with Health Canada's regulations, showing both the NPN and ingredient list. If issues persist, double-check your images for legibility and clarity or contact Seller Support for further assistance.

**2. What exactly are “prohibited disease claims,” and how can I avoid them?**

**Answer:**Prohibited disease claims refer to statements that suggest a product can diagnose, treat, or cure a specific disease or medical condition without proper licensing. For example, claims related to diabetes, cancer, or other major illnesses fall under this category if they are not supported by proper NHP registration. To avoid this, ensure that any claims made on your product detail page, including titles, descriptions, and images, align with the Recommended Use or Purpose listed in the Licensed Natural Health Products Database (LNHPD) or the product’s NPN. Always refer to Health Canada's guidelines on approved claims for your product.

**3. If my product is not NHP registered yet, but I am flagged for a policy violation, can I still reinstate my listing?**

**Answer:**If your product is not registered as a Natural Health Product (NHP) and has been flagged for prohibited claims, you cannot reinstate the listing until the product is properly licensed by Health Canada. Ensure that you apply for NHP registration and obtain a valid NPN before attempting to relist. If your product is in the process of being registered but is still under review, contact Seller Support for guidance on how to proceed while awaiting approval.

**4. How can I address a policy violation if my appeal is rejected after 24 hours?**

**Answer:**If your appeal is rejected, you can take a few steps to resolve the issue:

- Recheck the compliance of your product: Ensure that all documentation, images, and claims fully comply with Health Canada's regulations. You may need to adjust the product description or provide additional supporting evidence.
- Revise the appeal: Submit a revised appeal with clearer evidence or explanations. If necessary, contact Amazon Seller Support for additional guidance on improving your case.
- Contact Health Canada: If you believe the product is compliant but Amazon has made an error, contact Health Canada for confirmation and include their response in your appeal.

**5. Are there any exceptions to the policy on disease claims or restricted ingredients?**

**Answer:**While most claims must adhere strictly to Health Canada’s guidelines, certain exceptions may apply based on the specific classification of your product. For example, some natural health products may have approval for specific therapeutic claims, provided they are listed in the NPN or Health Canada’s Licensed Natural Health Products Database. However, any exceptions would need to be confirmed with Health Canada or an Amazon policy specialist. If you're unsure whether your product qualifies for any exceptions, it’s always a good idea to consult with Health Canada or Amazon Seller Support directly.

**6. What happens if my product is flagged for containing prohibited ingredients, like Vitamin A?**

**Answer:**If your product contains a prohibited ingredient, such as Vitamin A (in specific doses or forms), and has been flagged by Amazon, you will need to prove that it complies with Health Canada's regulations for safe use. You should check the product’s NPN or DIN and ensure the ingredient is listed in the Licensed Natural Health Products Database. If the product is compliant, appeal the decision by submitting the correct documentation. If it's not compliant, you'll need to either remove the ingredient or relabel the product accordingly.

**7. How do I ensure compliance with both Health Canada regulations and Amazon’s policies?**

**Answer:**To ensure full compliance, follow these steps:

- Verify the NPN or DIN: Ensure your product has the correct registration with Health Canada and the corresponding NPN/DIN is visible on both the product and product images in Seller Central.
- Align product descriptions: Ensure all claims made on the product detail page align with those approved by Health Canada. The product’s recommended use, medicinal ingredients, and claims must all match the information listed in the Licensed Natural Health Products Database.
- Follow labeling requirements: Product packaging should feature both English and French labeling, as required by Canadian regulations.
- Regularly review Amazon’s Restricted Product Policy and Health Canada's updates to stay aligned with any changes to regulations.
