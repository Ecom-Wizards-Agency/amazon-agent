## How to Use the Repeat Purchase Behavior Report in Brand Analytics

**Who is this for?** This SOP is for Amazon Sellers who are enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry and have access to Brand Analytics in Seller Central. It covers the process of navigating to the Repeat Purchase Behavior report, understanding its features, and utilizing the insights to improve brand performance.

**Objective**: This SOP provides detailed instructions on how to access, interpret, and use the **Repeat Purchase Behavior Report** in Amazon Brand Analytics. This report helps brands enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry understand how frequently customers make repeat purchases, assess customer satisfaction, and monitor customer engagement with their products.

**Accessing the Report**

**Access Requirements**

- Sellers must be enrolled in **Amazon Brand Registry**.
- This feature is only available to sellers who have been assigned the **Brand Representative** role in **Brand Registry**.
- The seller must be internal to the brand and responsible for selling the brand on Amazon.
- If you encounter an error message stating, "You do not have permissions to access this page," request that your **Primary Account Administrator** grant you permissions to **Amazon Brand Analytics** in the **Global User Permissions** page.

If you believe you meet these criteria and do not have access, contact **Selling Partner Support** via the **Contact Us** form in **Seller Central**.

**How to Access the Repeat Purchase Behavior Report**

1. **Log in to Seller Central**.
2. **Navigate to the Brand Analytics** tool:

  - Select **Brands** from the main menu.
  - Click on **Brand Analytics** or click **All Brand Benefits** to locate the **Brand Analytics** card.

#### **Report Metrics Overview**

- **Repeat Ordered Product Sales**: Sales generated from customers who made repeat purchases of a product.
- **Repeat Ordered Units**: The total number of units sold to repeat customers.
- **Repeat Customer Count**: The number of customers who made repeat purchases of a product.
- **Repeat Customer Share**: The share of repeat sales, units, or customers within the total for the selected period.

Understanding the Report Metrics

The **Repeat Purchase Behavior Report** provides detailed data about repeat customer purchases across your brand and individual products. Below are the key metrics and what they represent:

1. Repeat Ordered Product Sales

  :

  This metric shows the total sales generated from repeat customers (those who have purchased the same product more than once).

  Example: If a customer buys a soccer ball for $10 and then purchases two more at $8 each, the repeat sales for that item would be $16.
2. Change vs. Prior Period

  :

  This metric shows the difference in repeat sales compared to the previous period (e.g., weekly comparison).

  Example: If your brand had $150 in repeat sales for this week and $75 last week, the change would be $100.
3. Percent Share of Total Sales

  :

  This is the proportion of repeat sales in relation to total sales for the selected period.

  Example: If the repeat sales for your brand are $150 and total sales are $450, the percent share of total sales is 33.33%.
4. Repeat Ordered Units

  :

  This metric shows the total number of units sold via repeat orders.

  Example: If a customer buys one soccer ball and later purchases two more, the repeat ordered units would be two.
5. Repeat Customer Count

  :

  This indicates the number of unique customers who made repeat purchases of your product(s).
6. Repeat Customer Share

  :

  This shows the percentage of repeat customers relative to the total customer base for your brand.

**Using the Data Effectively**

- **Reviewing the Data**:
  Use the report to identify which products are generating repeat business and which are not. This insight can help you target high-engagement products for marketing campaigns or promotions.
- **Customer Retention**:
  A higher percentage of repeat customers generally indicates good product satisfaction and customer loyalty. Use these insights to measure customer retention and adjust your strategies as needed.

  **Marketing Strategy**:
  The data can help assess the effectiveness of past marketing campaigns and guide future efforts by identifying high-performing products and repeat purchase trends.

FAQs

**Q: What information is contained in the Repeat Purchase Behavior dashboard?**The dashboard shows repeat ordered product sales, repeat ordered units, and repeat customers within a selected timeframe. By comparing the number of orders with the number of repeat customers, brand representatives can identify products or brands that have been ordered more than once per customer during the specified timeframe.

**Q: Which products show up in the dashboard?**The report shows all the products associated with brands that the brand owner has successfully registered with the Amazon Brand Registry.

**Q: Which customer orders are counted as a repeat purchase?**Repeat purchases are sales generated from customers who place more than one order for a brand or an ASIN within the selected reporting time range. Only subsequent repeated orders are counted as repeat purchases; the first order is excluded.

**Q: Why don’t my repeat orders match my repeat ordered units?**A single order may include multiple quantities of the same product. This means the number of repeat orders might be higher than the repeat ordered units.

**Q: Are order adjustments, such as cancellations and returns, considered in calculating a repeat purchase?**No, adjustments such as returns or cancellations are not considered in the calculation of a repeat purchase.

**Q: How far back does the data in the repeat purchase dashboard go?**The report retains data for the last **three years**.

**Q: What does "-" mean in the report?**The metric value of "-" means that the data is unavailable for the selected time period.

**Q: Does the dashboard allow searching for a particular product?**Yes, brand representatives can search for a specific product or set of products using the **ASIN search box** at the top of the report. The ASIN search box supports searches for up to **100 ASINs** at a time, with either a space or comma separating each ASIN.
