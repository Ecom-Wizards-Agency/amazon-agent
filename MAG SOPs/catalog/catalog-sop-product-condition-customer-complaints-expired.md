## **Product Condition Customer Complaints**

**Who is this for :** This is for employees in charge of quality control, warehouse management, and shipping departments in a company that sells products on Amazon or other online marketplaces.

**Objective :** To provide a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for addressing customer complaints about product conditions, specifically regarding expired products or products without a printed or labeled expiration date on the packaging.

This issue is caused by customer complaints who received expired products. The complaint will show up on the account health page:

Try to find out the root cause of the problem and update the template below accordingly.

**Worked**

**The root cause of the issue**

1. Improper storage techniques and lack of quality checks being performed before shipping items caused us to ship an expired item to a customer.

2. Our warehouse staff failed to store items in a way that allowed for easy viewing of important details like expiration dates.

3. Lack of proper packing practices allowed an expired item to be shipped out without being noticed by our shipping team.

**The actions you have taken to resolve the issue**

1. We have implemented new shipping procedures and thoroughly educated our staff on the new procedures

2. We have had a warehouse-wide meeting with the warehouse division informing the packagers that there were complaints about expired products being received due to potential defects caused by the shipping process. We reviewed their shipping and packaging processes and reinforced our shipping policies to ensure that these issues will not happen again.

3. We as a company have reviewed all of Amazon’s policies, paying special attention to the item condition policies to ensure our policies match and exceed Amazon’s guidelines.

4. Our catalog listing team has thoroughly examined the product detail pages of not only this ASIN in question but all of our products. Our product detail pages meet Amazon’s guidelines in listing descriptions, are accurately described, and leave no ambiguity in regard to the customer experience.

**The steps you have taken to prevent the issue going forward**

1. Our warehouse team will carefully review all items that are sitting in our warehouse and remove any units that are close to their expiration date.

2. All new products that are coming from our supplier into our warehouse will be verified that all units receiving are in sellable condition with a future expiration date.

3. When a customer purchases our products, our warehouse staff will verify that the unit purchased is not near or past the product's expiration date before it is shipped out to the customer.

4. With our new quality control procedure in place, this will prevent issues going forward.

FAQs

1. What are Product Condition Customer Complaints related to expiration dates?

These complaints occur when customers receive products that:

- Are expired or near expiration
- Do not have a printed or labeled expiration date on the packaging
- Appear damaged, tampered with, or improperly stored

2. How do I check if my listing has been flagged for expired product complaints?

Go to Seller Central > Performance > Account Health > Product Condition Customer Complaints to check flagged ASINs and details of the issue.

3. What are the common reasons for expiration-related complaints?

- Improper inventory management, leading to expired items being shipped
- Lack of quality control, allowing near-expiration products to be sent out
- No expiration date printed on the packaging, causing confusion for customers
- Storage issues, where expiration dates are not easily visible to warehouse staff

4. How do I fix a listing flagged for an expiration date issue?

1. Identify the issue by reviewing customer complaints and the flagged ASIN in Account Health.
2. Inspect warehouse inventory and remove any expired or near-expiration items.
3. Verify supplier shipments to ensure all incoming products have proper expiration labeling.
4. Update the listing with accurate expiration date details (if applicable).
5. Submit an appeal with supporting documentation, including photos of the expiration date.

5. What should be included in my appeal for reinstatement?

Your appeal should include:

- Root cause analysis of why the expired/missing expiration issue occurred
- Corrective actions taken to resolve the issue (e.g., inventory audits, supplier checks)
- Preventive measures to ensure compliance moving forward
- Supporting evidence, such as photos of expiration dates or BIN check requests

6. How can I prevent future complaints about expired products?

- Conduct regular inventory audits and remove near-expiration items.
- Train warehouse staff to check expiration dates before shipping.
- Require suppliers to provide products with clearly printed expiration dates.
- Use First-In, First-Out (FIFO) inventory management to ship older stock first.

7. What if my products don’t have a printed expiration date?

- Work with suppliers to add expiration dates to packaging.
- Label products manually if required by Amazon’s policies.
- Provide clear documentation in the listing details explaining where to find the expiration date.

8. What should I do if a customer falsely claims an item is expired?

- Request a BIN check through Amazon to verify the inventory’s condition.
- Submit an appeal with proof of expiration dates on products.
- Attach photos of actual inventory showing valid expiration dates.

9. Can I relist an item if it was flagged for missing an expiration date?

Yes, but you must ensure:

- The expiration date is clearly printed or labeled on the product.
- The packaging meets Amazon’s expiration date policies.
- No misleading claims about shelf life or expiration are present in the listing.

10. Where can I find Amazon’s policies on expiration dates?

Refer to Amazon’s Item Condition Guidelines and Expiration Date Policy in Seller Central for compliance details.
