# Catalog SOP: A/B Test (Product Title)

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-ab-test-product-title

## A/B Test (Product Title)

**Who is this for :** Individuals who wished to conduct A/B split testing within seller central.

**Objective :** The purpose of this document is to provide a step-by-step guide on how to create A/B split testing to submit alternate content for your products to collect insights such as traffic and conversion. Split testing or AB testing, on Amazon, is an excellent way of optimizing your listings through experiments with product content, images, and pricing. Four crucial elements of the listing affect conversion rate: Image, Title, Product Features, Description, and Price.

**Seller Central A/B Testing**

Access the [Amazon Seller Central](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/) page

- Go to Brands, then select Manage Experiments.

**Product Title A/B Testing**

- If the ASIN is part of a variation, you will need to select first the whole parentage as the reference ASIN.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-30%20at%204.59.35%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/9yfimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/9yfimage.png)

- Enter a descriptive test name, duration, and desired start date. You may also enter an optional hypothesis around what you wish to learn from the test.

  - Experiment Name
  - Hypothesis - Brief description of the experiment and the desired outcome.
  - Duration - Minimum of 4 weeks to a maximum of 10 weeks.

    - The start Date will be automatically set 1 week after you created the experiment, but you can still set it at a later date.
    - The end Date will be automatically set depending on the duration of the experiment.
  - You can tick To significance which automatically ends the experiment when there is enough time data to declare winner.
  - You can also set it to Automatically publish once the experiment is done.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-30%20at%204.59.44%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/y1Mimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/y1Mimage.png)

- Uncheck all other child ASINs that are not part of the experiment.

  - When you run an experiment on a variation family, you can submit an experiment for one or more of the child ASINs, but you always have to submit for the parent ASIN.
- Type in the new product title in the Version B section of the ASIN involved.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-30%20at%204.59.54%E2%80%AFAM.png)

- Click View Details to check the experiment's current status.

FAQs

1. **What should I do after I schedule an A/B test for my product title?**

- After scheduling your A/B test, you can track the progress of the experiment through the "View Details" section on the experiments dashboard. The status will show whether the test is still running or if it's completed. You can also see how much data has been collected and whether the test is reaching statistical significance. Amazon will automatically notify you once the experiment is completed.

1. **How do I know when to end my A/B test or stop it early?**

- You can set your experiment to end automatically when enough data is collected by checking the "To significance" box. This option uses statistical analysis to determine when the data is conclusive enough to declare a winner. If you believe the results are already clear before the automatic end date, you can manually end the experiment by going to the experiment’s settings and selecting "End Experiment." However, we recommend allowing the experiment to run for the full duration (minimum of 4 weeks) to ensure accurate and reliable results.

1. **How do I analyze the results of my A/B test?**

- Once your experiment ends, you can view the results on the "View Results" page. Amazon will display key metrics such as conversion rates, traffic, and sales for both versions of the title (Version A and Version B). To determine the better-performing title, compare the data: the version with higher conversions and sales is typically considered the "winner." Pay attention to statistical significance, which Amazon will indicate to ensure the results are reliable.

1. **What factors should I consider when creating my alternate product title (Version B) for A/B testing?**

- When creating Version B, focus on experimenting with different elements of your product title that could affect performance. You might try variations in:

  - Keywords (e.g., trying different search terms)
  - Brand names or product descriptors (e.g., adding more specific details)
  - Length (e.g., testing a more concise vs. a detailed title)
  - Emotional appeal (e.g., adding urgency or highlighting benefits) The goal is to test changes that you believe could impact search visibility or customer interest, based on your target audience and keyword research.

1. **What if my ASIN is not eligible for A/B testing?**

- If your ASIN is not eligible for A/B testing, it may be due to it not being part of a variation family or other restrictions imposed by Amazon. If your ASIN is not eligible, you won’t see it listed as an option when setting up the experiment. In such cases, consider contacting Seller Support to inquire about eligibility or to explore other ways to optimize your product listing.

1. **Are there any limits on how many A/B tests I can run on a single ASIN?**

- Currently, Amazon allows you to run only one experiment per ASIN at a time. If you wish to test multiple elements (e.g., title, image, and description), you will need to run separate experiments for each. Be mindful of this limitation when planning your testing strategy to ensure that you do not overlap tests on the same ASIN simultaneously.

1. **What should I do if the results of my A/B test are inconclusive?**

- If your A/B test results are inconclusive (i.e., there is no clear winner between Version A and Version B), you can try re-running the experiment with different variations or experiment with other elements of the product listing, such as images or bullet points. It's also a good idea to increase the duration of the test to collect more data, which may help reach more conclusive results.
