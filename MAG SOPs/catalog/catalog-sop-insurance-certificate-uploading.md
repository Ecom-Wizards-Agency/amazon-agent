## Adding a New Business Insurance Policy on Seller Central

**Who is this for?**

All individuals who want to add a new business insurance policy.

**Objective:**

To be able to successfully upload a new business insurance policy on Seller Central.

**Video Reference:**

**Steps on How to Upload the Insurance Certificate**

- Go to Settings >> Account Info >> [Business Insurance](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/mario/v2/az/flow/BusinessInsurance/page/InsuranceEstablishment/global/render) >> Add new policy

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/insurance-certificate-uploading-image-doozpque.png)

- Proceed to Step 2 to Upload Proof of Insurance that satisfies the [Insurance Policy Requirements](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/200386300) set by Amazon.

- Fill out all the information needed, making sure that each entry exactly matches the information on the Certificate of Insurance. The insured name must match the legal entity of the account as well, and the certificate must be signed by an authorized representative.

FAQs

1. **What should I do if my insurance policy does not meet Amazon’s specific requirements?**

- If your policy does not meet Amazon’s requirements, review the "Insurance Policy Requirements set by Amazon" to ensure your certificate complies. You may need to contact your insurance provider to make necessary changes. If you continue to have trouble, reach out to Amazon Seller Support for further assistance.

1. **What other information needs to match exactly between the certificate and Seller Central entries?**

- In addition to the insured name matching your legal entity, make sure that all details on the certificate align exactly with what you input in Seller Central, including the address, policy number, coverage dates, and any other relevant fields. Discrepancies in these details can lead to the rejection of the submission.

1. **What happens after I submit my insurance certificate?**

- After submission, Amazon will review the certificate to ensure it meets the required criteria. The approval process typically takes a few days. You will be notified via email or through the Seller Central dashboard once your policy is approved. If there are any issues, you will be informed and asked to make corrections.

1. **What should I do if my insurance certificate is rejected?**

- If your certificate is rejected, carefully review the feedback provided by Amazon to understand why it was not accepted. Common issues include mismatched information, missing signatures, or incorrect policy details. You may need to upload a corrected certificate that meets all of Amazon’s requirements.

1. **Are there specific file format or size requirements for uploading the insurance certificate?**

- The insurance certificate should be uploaded in a common file format, such as PDF or JPG. While there is no specific file size limit mentioned, it's recommended to use a file that is clear and legible. If your file is too large or in an unsupported format, try reducing the size or converting the file before uploading.

1. **How can I update an existing insurance policy on Seller Central?**

- To update your existing insurance policy, simply go to the same “Business Insurance” section in Seller Central, and follow the same steps as you did when adding your initial policy. You will need to upload the updated certificate and review the information to ensure it matches.

1. **Where can I find the "Insurance Policy Requirements" set by Amazon?**

- The "Insurance Policy Requirements" are available in the Seller Central help section. You can find them by navigating to the Insurance Policy section under "Account Info" or by reviewing Amazon’s official guidelines on the matter. If you cannot locate the requirements, you can reach out to Seller Support for clarification.

1. **What should I do if I don’t have a business insurance policy yet but need to upload one?**

- If you don’t currently have a business insurance policy but need one to meet Amazon’s requirements, contact an insurance provider to purchase a policy that meets Amazon’s specifications. Ensure that the policy you choose covers the necessary liability and aligns with Amazon’s insurance policy criteria.
