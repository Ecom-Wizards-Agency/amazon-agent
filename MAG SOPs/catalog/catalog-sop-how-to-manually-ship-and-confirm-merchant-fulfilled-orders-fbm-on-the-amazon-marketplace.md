## **How to Manually Ship and Confirm Merchant Fulfilled Orders (FBM) On the Amazon Marketplace**

**Who is this for :** Individuals who are responsible for managing the fulfillment of orders for a merchant-fulfilled listing.

**Objective :** This SOP will help us learn how to ship and confirm merchant-fulfilled orders on the Amazon Marketplace through a manual process.

**Instructions:**

**Step 1: Set Notifications**

A. The first thing you need to do is set your notification settings. Hover your mouse over the Settings → Notification Preferences

K. If you already have your shipping service selected, you can Confirm Shipment, Enter in Ship Date, Enter the Tracking Number, and Shipping Method. Then click Confirm Shipment.

FAQs

**1. What does it mean to manually ship a merchant-fulfilled order?**

- Manually shipping an FBM order involves processing, packaging, and shipping products yourself or through your logistics partner, instead of relying on Amazon's fulfillment network (FBA). This also requires you to confirm the shipment details in Seller Central.

**2. Why do I need to set notification preferences for merchant-fulfilled orders?**

- Setting notifications ensures you receive timely alerts about new orders, shipping deadlines, or customer inquiries. This helps you manage orders efficiently and avoid late shipments.

**3. How do I access unshipped orders?**

- Navigate to:

  - Orders > Manage Orders > Unshipped.
  - This section displays all pending orders that need to be processed and shipped.

**4. What is the “Ship By Date,” and why is it important?**

- The Ship By Date is the deadline by which you must ship the order to avoid delays. Meeting this deadline ensures customer satisfaction and helps maintain your seller performance metrics.

**5. What is “Buy Shipping,” and why should I use it?**

- Buy Shipping is an Amazon tool that allows you to purchase shipping labels directly through Seller Central. Using it offers benefits like:

  - Pre-filled customer information.
  - Access to discounted shipping rates.
  - Automatic shipment confirmation.

**6. How do I enter packaging details when using Buy Shipping?**

- When prompted:

  - Enter the weight and dimensions of the package.
  - Choose existing packaging or add new packaging details.
  - Click Save to confirm before proceeding.

**7. Can I use my own shipping service instead of Buy Shipping?**

- Yes, you can use your own shipping service. In this case:

  - Ship the order through your chosen carrier.
  - Enter the tracking number, shipping method, and ship date manually in Seller Central when confirming the shipment.

**8. How do I confirm a shipment after manually shipping the order?**

- To confirm a shipment:

  - Navigate to the order in Manage Orders.
  - Enter the ship date, tracking number, and shipping method.
  - Click Confirm Shipment to finalize the process.

**9. What happens if I forget to confirm the shipment?**

- Failing to confirm the shipment will result in:

  - The order appearing as unshipped in Amazon's system.
  - Potential late shipment penalties, which can impact your seller performance metrics.
  - Always confirm shipments promptly after processing.

**10. How do I print shipping labels through Buy Shipping?**

- After purchasing a shipping label:

  - Click Buy Shipping to process the order.
  - Once completed, select Print Labels to generate and print the label.

**11. What should I do if the declared value of an item exceeds the shipping service’s default coverage?**

- Most shipping services provide limited insurance. If your item’s declared value exceeds this amount, you can purchase additional coverage during the Buy Shipping process by selecting the insurance option.

**12. What information do I need to provide when confirming a shipment?**

- When confirming a shipment, provide:

  - Ship date: The date you shipped the item.
  - Tracking number: Provided by your carrier.
  - Shipping method: The service used (e.g., Standard, Expedited, Two-Day).

**13. How can I handle international shipments?**

- For international orders:

  - Confirm any customs requirements for the destination country.
  - Use Buy Shipping to access discounted international shipping rates.
  - Enter customs information accurately to avoid delays.

**14. What should I do if I encounter an error while confirming a shipment?**

- If you face an issue:

  - Double-check the tracking number and shipping method for accuracy.
  - Ensure you’ve selected the correct order from Manage Orders.
  - Contact Amazon Seller Support if the issue persists.

**15. Can I update tracking information after confirming a shipment?**

- Yes, you can update tracking details if they change. Navigate to the order in Manage Orders, edit the tracking number or shipping method, and save the updates.

**16. How does confirming shipment affect my performance metrics?**

- Timely shipment confirmation helps maintain a high Order Defect Rate (ODR) and ensures good Seller Feedback. Late or missed confirmations may lead to penalties or account suspension.

**17. What should I do if I miss the Ship By Date?**

- If you miss the Ship By Date:

  - Ship the item immediately using the fastest possible shipping method.
  - Inform the customer via Buyer-Seller Messaging.
  - Monitor the impact on your performance metrics.

**18. Can I split a shipment if the order contains multiple items?**

- Yes, but you must:

  - Confirm each shipment separately in Seller Central.
  - Provide the corresponding tracking numbers for each package.

**19. How do I track orders after shipping them?**

- Use the tracking number provided by your carrier or through Amazon’s Manage Orders section. Customers can also track their orders via the tracking number you’ve confirmed.

**20. Are there any fees for using Buy Shipping?**

- Amazon does not charge additional fees for using Buy Shipping. You only pay for the carrier’s shipping rates, which are often discounted through Amazon’s negotiated rates.
