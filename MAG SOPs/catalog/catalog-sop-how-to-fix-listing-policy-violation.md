# Catalog SOP: How to Fix Listing Policy Violation

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-how-to-fix-listing-policy-violation

## How to Fix Listing Policy Violation

**Who is this for :**

- To the copywriting team that creates copies for listings.
- Every member of the Reinstatement Team that will receive Listing Policy Violation reinstatement tasks
- To all members of My Amazon Guy (MAG) that wanted to learn more about Listing Policy Violations
- Sellers that wanted to learn more about Listing Policy Violations.

**Objectives :** This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) will help you understand what to avoid in writing product descriptions, and how to fix Listing Policy Violation and related issues. Amazon sets Listing Policies on the writing styles of the product description to protect customers from false claims, and solicitations from sellers. This helps give customers a clear and consistent buying experience. For more information about the writing style guide, please see the [Product Detail Page Rules article](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/200390640?ref=ss_ahd_pp_list_pol).

**These are the primary prohibited elements in writing product descriptions, but not limited to:**

1. Product Comparison to Another

Amazon doesn’t want the sellers to compare their products to another. Amazon observes a fair marketplace for customers and sellers alike. Also, they want to avoid legal/lawsuits

from other sellers or companies outside or within Amazon. In order to prevent all these possibilities, Amazon only allows the comparison of products within the seller’s catalog.

Here are some examples of prohibited comparisons from the product’s copy:

- Forget cheap imitations
- Our product has more beneficial ingredients than ….
- Our product is more affordable than …

2. Benefits

Sellers wanted to highlight the benefits of their products to the customers to get more attention and a higher chance of selling their listings. Nevertheless, in some cases, the benefits described in the product description are off the chart. This is prevalent in food supplements as they promise health benefits but it also has a fine print of not solving any health issues, thus, contradicting their own product description.

In order to prevent this, Amazon wanted sellers to focus on the Features of their products. Please avoid the Feature Bridge Benefit (FBB) writing style. Do not tell the customers “what’s in it for them” if they buy your product. Just stick to the features of the product.

Here are some examples of prohibited “Benefit” descriptions from the product’s copy:

- so you can easily see and tell what's going on
- so you won’t feel tired and get enough energy
- Any phrases after the products feature and has the conjunction “so”

3. Soliciting Reviews/Feedback

Amazon doesn’t want the sellers to force the customers to provide reviews on the products bought. It’s even worse to promise a reward if the customers give a review as Amazon will see it as buying reviews from customers.

Here are some examples of prohibited product review solicitation from product’s copy:

- We want to improve your experience, let us know where we can improve.
- We wanted to hear from you.
- Get 10% on your next purchase after giving your first review about us.
- tell us how your experience is!

4. Pressuring Language

It’s almost the same as the Benefits section but pressuring language is on another level because this writing style is proactively asking the customer to buy the product mischievously or in a subtle way. Amazon doesn’t want the customers to feel obliged to buy the seller's products.

Here are some examples of pressuring language from product copy:

- Reap the benefits
- Grab your pool cartridge filter now!
- Use our sleep aid pills
- a strong desire to try them right away

5. Accuracy and Precision

Stick to the product’s specifications in its product manual or instruction. Do not promise beyond what the products can do. This applies mainly to devices but may apply to food supplements (dietary supplement intake).

Remember to always check the product website, manual, and packaging of devices before writing the proposed product description.

6. Assurance

Never promise assurances in the product description unless it’s a money-back guarantee. So far, there are no minimal encounters of listing policy violations with the reason of the money-back guarantee as it’s in harmony with the refund policy of Amazon to customers.

Assurance also includes promising the customers that the products are of high quality or proven.

Here are some examples of assurance from the product’s copy:

- rest assured that
- You will get assurance that our supplement can help you…
- high quality, premium
- Best selling

7. Wear and Tear

No product can last forever nor can withstand weather conditions without wear and tear. Therefore, Amazon flags down listings that promise they can last forever without their quality being affected.

Here are some examples of wear and tear from the product’s copy:

- built to last
- without losing the original quality
- Weatherproof

8. Boastful and Informal Marketing

We know that many sellers want to have that personalized rapport with their customers and will make their product descriptions feel like they’re directly talking to them. Nevertheless, most of the time, Amazon doesn’t like this. There are times when the copies are unprofessional and informal which leads to a boastful description of the products.

Paraphrasing informal and boastful sentences or phrases into a more professional tone will help.

Here are some examples of boastful and informal marketing from product copy:

- Forget about getting your hands all sticky and smelling like ointment all-day
- we've been committed to giving you only the gold standard- the purest and most potent supplements available on the market, without false claims and mediocrity.

9. Promoting Irresponsible Intake of Substances

Some Amazon products are associated with prohibited listings (e.g., beer bong, cigar flask, etc). Most of the sellers are tempted to reel in customers that use substances by making them feel great at what they do. Amazon prohibits this.

Here are some examples of promoting Irresponsible Intake of Substances from product’s copy:

- Using our pink leopard beer bong will make your party more relaxed and fun.
- There is no doubt the 420 Flask will become your favorite Marijuana accessories

What if the Listing Remained Yanked After Removing Claims?

1. Call Account Health

Make sure you have removed all of the possible listing policy violation claims and it should be reflecting both on the seller's and Amazon’s end before calling account health. They may tell you what you have missed or tell you to review some articles to understand writing styles.

2. Make Product Description “Bare”

Making the product description simple by describing the product in 5 sentences in the product description and assigning each sentence to the 5 bullet points will likely clear out the listing policy violation.

3. Send a Case to Help

- Make sure that you have documented all of the actions taken in the listing.
- What are the claims removed and where have they been?
- Take the batch ID (if you uploaded the feed file)
- Go to Help > Get Support > Selling on Amazon > Or, browse for your issue in the menu > Products, Listings, or Inventory > Inventory file upload issue

FAQs

1. What is a Listing Policy Violation?

A Listing Policy Violation occurs when a product listing does not comply with Amazon's established rules for product descriptions, titles, images, or other listing content. Violations can arise from the use of prohibited phrases, claims, or improper marketing tactics that can mislead customers or create unfair competition.

2. What are some common causes of Listing Policy Violations?

Common reasons for violations include:

- Product comparisons: Comparing products to others or using language like "better than" or "cheaper than."
- Unsubstantiated benefits: Describing benefits that aren't supported or that exaggerate the product's effects.
- Soliciting reviews: Asking customers to leave reviews or promising rewards in exchange for them.
- Pressuring language: Using persuasive language that pushes customers to buy immediately or misleads them about the necessity of the product.
- Overpromising product performance: Making guarantees about the product that aren't supported by facts or the product's capabilities.
- Inaccurate or boastful claims: Claims like "built to last forever" or "high quality" without sufficient proof.
- Promoting irresponsible intake of substances: Encouraging the use of harmful substances, which violates Amazon’s policies.

3. How do I fix a Listing Policy Violation?

Follow these steps:

1. Review the product listing: Carefully read through the product description and ensure that no prohibited language or claims are present.
2. Remove prohibited claims: Eliminate any comparisons, unsubstantiated benefits, solicitation for reviews, pressuring language, or any inappropriate claims.
3. Make the description "bare": Simplify the product description by providing just the facts—describe the product and its features concisely in a professional tone.
4. Call Account Health: If the listing is still flagged, contact Amazon Seller Support and ask for specific feedback on what may be causing the violation.
5. Resubmit the listing: After removing the violating content, submit the listing again to Amazon for review.

4. What if the listing remains removed after making the changes?

If the listing is still removed, follow these additional steps:

1. Contact Account Health: Ask for further clarification and guidance if the violation is not resolved after removing the claims.
2. Check with Seller Support: If your listing continues to be flagged, contact Amazon support to ensure no other issues are contributing to the violation.
3. Use the "bare" description method: Simplifying the description to focus on just the product features can often resolve lingering issues.
4. Send a case to Amazon Support: If necessary, create a case with Amazon to confirm that the necessary updates have been made and request the listing be reinstated.

5. How do I appeal a Listing Policy Violation?

Follow these steps to appeal:

1. Document your actions: Include details of what was changed and what claims were removed from the listing.
2. Submit an appeal to Seller Support: Go to Help > Get Support > Selling on Amazon > Products, Listings, or Inventory > Inventory file upload issue and submit the appeal.
3. Provide a clear explanation: In the appeal, state that the product complies with Amazon’s policies and provide evidence of the actions taken, such as batch IDs or screenshots.
4. Request for reinstatement: Ask Amazon to review the case and reinstate the listing.

6. What should I include in my appeal?

When submitting an appeal, include:

- The ASIN in question
- A summary of the changes made to the listing
- The Batch ID of the file uploaded
- A statement confirming that the listing now complies with Amazon’s listing policies
- Any relevant documentation or screenshots that show the violation has been corrected.

7. How long does it take for a listing to be reinstated?

The reinstatement process can take several days. Follow up with Seller Support if you don’t hear back within 7-10 days. Keep the case ID and continue to follow Amazon’s instructions to resolve the issue.

8. Can I lose reviews if my listing is removed?

No, deleting and reinstating a listing with the same SKU will not affect the reviews or sales history. However, ensure that all violations are resolved before resubmitting to avoid further issues.

9. How can I prevent Listing Policy Violations in the future?

- Follow Amazon's guidelines: Regularly review Amazon’s Product Detail Page Rules to ensure your listings comply with their policies.
- Stay professional: Use clear, factual language in your product descriptions and avoid overhyping your products.
- Avoid comparison: Never compare your product directly to others.
- Stick to features: Focus on the features of your products, not on benefits or unverified claims.

10. What if I need more help?

If you’re unsure about how to correct the violation or if it persists, consider reaching out to Amazon Seller Support or consult with Amazon account specialists who can guide you through the process.
