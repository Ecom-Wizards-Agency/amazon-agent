# Catalog SOP: Amazon Policy Violations Check

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-amazon-policy-violations-check

## **Amazon Policy Violations Check**

**Who is this for:**This is intended for team members responsible for conducting Policy Compliance Checks in a business or organization, and for those who need to be informed of any compliance issues found during the check.

**Objective:** To establish a standardized procedure for conducting Amazon Policy Violations Checks, identifying and documenting any instances of policy violations, and initiating appropriate actions in compliance with Amazon's policies and guidelines.

1. In Seller Central, Go to the Performance tab, select “Account Health”

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-4W1CZCEH.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/7XZimage.png)

2. Check all Policy Compliance complaints/violations by clicking each one of the categories or by clicking “View All” at the bottom of the section.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-28%20at%204.56.29%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/axWimage.png)

3. Log the issues you found on the Weekly Account Health Check tracker.

**For 15 issues and below: log each issue on the tracker with screenshots (see attached) and on the ASANA task.

**For 16 issues above: Just put the screenshot on the tracker and on ASANA. No need to log each issue on the tracker.

4. Inform the brand manager by tagging them on Asana or by sending them a DM.

FAQs

1. What types of policy violations does Amazon track in Account Health?

Amazon tracks several types of policy violations, including:

- Intellectual Property (IP) Violations – Unauthorized use of trademarks, copyrights, or patents.
- Restricted Product Violations – Listing prohibited or regulated products.
- Listing Policy Violations – Incorrect product detail pages, keyword stuffing, or misleading content.
- Code of Conduct Violations – Manipulating reviews, price-fixing, or dishonest selling practices.
- Product Condition Complaints – Selling used items as new or misrepresenting product conditions.

2. How do I know if a policy violation is critical?

Amazon marks critical violations in Account Health with warnings such as:

- “Action Required” – Immediate correction needed to avoid suspension.
- “Under Review” – Amazon is assessing the violation before taking action.
- “Suspended” – The listing or account is restricted and requires an appeal.

3. How can I appeal a policy violation in Seller Central?

1. Go to Performance > Account Health.
2. Click on the violation under Policy Compliance.
3. Select “Appeal” (if available).
4. Provide a Plan of Action (POA) explaining:

  - The root cause of the violation.
  - The corrective actions taken.
  - The preventative measures to avoid recurrence.
5. Submit the appeal and wait for Amazon’s response.

4. How long does Amazon take to review policy violation appeals?

- Most appeals are reviewed within 24–72 hours, but some cases may take up to 7–14 days.
- If there is no response after 5 business days, sellers should follow up via Seller Support.

5. What happens if I don’t resolve a policy violation?

- Unresolved violations may lead to listing suppression, ASIN deactivation, or account suspension.
- Repeated violations can result in permanent account deactivation.

6. How can I prevent policy violations in the future?

- Regularly review Amazon’s policies (Amazon Seller Policies).
- Use Amazon Brand Registry for trademark protection.
- Monitor listings for compliance using Category Listing Reports (CLR).
- Train your team on Amazon’s compliance requirements.

7. How often should we conduct Amazon policy compliance checks?

- High-risk accounts (frequent violations) – Daily monitoring.
- Mid-level risk accounts – Weekly checks.
- Low-risk accounts – Biweekly or monthly reviews.

8. What should I do if Amazon rejects my policy violation appeal?

1. Review the rejection message to understand Amazon’s reasoning.
2. Revise the appeal with more details or stronger supporting documents.
3. Escalate the case to the Account Health Support Team.
4. If necessary, contact Amazon Legal for complex IP-related cases.

9. How should violations be documented in ASANA?

- Minor Issues (Under 15 Violations) – Log each issue with screenshots in the ASANA tracker.
- Major Issues (16+ Violations) – Upload a summary screenshot only and tag the brand manager.
- Assign follow-up tasks to the compliance team or listing managers.

10. Who should I contact if a violation results in account suspension?

- Amazon Account Health Support via Seller Central > Get Support.
- Escalate to an Amazon Account Manager (if available).
- Consult an Amazon compliance expert or legal advisor for major suspensions.
