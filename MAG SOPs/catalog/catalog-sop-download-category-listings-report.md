## Download Category Listings Report

**Who is this for :** This is intended for Amazon Sellers who want to safeguard their listings against data loss, inaccuracies, and hijackers.

**Objective:** The objective of this SOP is to provide guidance for Amazon Sellers on how to generate and utilize the Category Listings Report to back up their Amazon data. This SOP is intended to help sellers who want to safeguard their listings against data loss, inaccuracies, and hijackers.

The Category Listings Report is an Inventory Report that can back up your Amazon data. It contains copy, keywords, parentage setup, and all other listing information—this aids in spotting inaccurate information, hijackers, and if Amazon modifies data. We may constantly refer to this report in case of a data loss.

FAQS

**1. What is the purpose of the Category Listings Report?**

- The Category Listings Report (CLR) is a comprehensive inventory report that serves as a backup for your Amazon product data. It helps:

  - Safeguard against data loss or inaccuracies.
  - Identify hijackers or unauthorized listing changes.
  - Verify and restore listing information if needed.

**2. Who is eligible to access the Category Listings Report?**

- The CLR is available to sellers with Professional Selling Plans. If it is not visible in your Inventory Reports dropdown menu, you will need to file a support ticket to activate it.

**3. What should I do if the Category Listings Report option is unavailable in my account?**

- If the option is missing:

  - Submit a ticket through Help > Get help and Resources in Seller Central.
  - Select My Issue is Not Listed, and request activation of the Category Listings Report.
  - Provide your seller account information and a brief explanation for your request.

**4. How long does it take for Amazon to activate the Category Listings Report?**

- The activation process can take 1–3 business days. You can monitor the ticket status under Case Log in Seller Central.

**5. How do I ensure the report I download is up-to-date?**

- Always request a new report before downloading to ensure it reflects the most current data. Avoid using previously generated reports for accurate troubleshooting or restoration.

**6. Why should I rename the downloaded report to include the account name?**

- Renaming the file with the account name ensures easy identification, especially if you manage multiple accounts or need to share the report with others.

**7. Can I use the CLR to restore lost or altered listing data?**

- Yes, the CLR can be used to identify and restore lost or altered data. Compare the report with current listing details and upload corrections via a flat file if necessary.

**8. Does the CLR include suppressed or inactive listings?**

- Yes, the CLR includes data for both active and inactive listings, providing a comprehensive overview of your inventory.

**9. What type of data is included in the Category Listings Report?**

- The CLR contains:

  - Product copy (e.g., title, bullet points, description).
  - Keywords and search terms.
  - Parentage setup (parent-child relationships).
  - Pricing, inventory, and other listing details.

**10. How often should I download the CLR?**

- It is recommended to download the CLR:

  - Regularly (e.g., monthly or quarterly) as a precaution.
  - Immediately before making significant changes to listings.
  - After identifying unauthorized changes or data discrepancies.

**11. Can the CLR detect listing hijackers?**

- Yes, by comparing the CLR with your intended listing information, you can identify unauthorized changes or additions made by hijackers.

**12. What should I do if the downloaded CLR contains incorrect data?**

- If you notice inaccuracies in the report:

  - Verify if the changes were made intentionally or by another party.
  - Correct the data using a flat file or directly in Seller Central.
  - Monitor your listings regularly to prevent recurring issues.

**13. Can I share the CLR with external teams or partners?**

- Yes, but ensure sensitive account data is handled securely. Use shared folders with restricted access, such as Google Drive or a secure file-sharing platform.

**14. What should I include in the support ticket when requesting activation of the CLR?**

- When submitting the ticket, include:

  - Your seller account name.
  - A brief explanation of why you need the CLR (e.g., data backup, listing restoration).
  - Your contact email address.

**15. How do I verify that the CLR was successfully enabled?**

- Once Amazon processes your request, the Category Listings Report will appear as an option under Reports > Inventory Reports in Seller Central.

**16. Can I use the CLR for bulk updates or optimizations?**

- Yes, you can extract data from the CLR to prepare flat files for bulk updates or listing optimizations.

**17. Is the CLR different for each Amazon marketplace?**

- Yes, the CLR is marketplace-specific. Ensure you generate and download the report for the correct marketplace where your listings are active.

**18. How do I follow up on a case if Amazon hasn’t responded?**

- Go to Help > Case Log in Seller Central.
- Locate the relevant case ID.
- Submit a follow-up message or escalate the case if needed.

**19. Can I generate the CLR for specific categories only?**

- No, the CLR includes data for all listings in your account. To focus on specific categories, filter the data after downloading the report.

**20. What are the limitations of the Category Listings Report?**

- The CLR doesn’t include historical changes to listings, only current data at the time of generation.
- It cannot directly restore listings; corrections must be re-uploaded manually or via flat file.
- It may take time to generate, depending on the size of your inventory.
