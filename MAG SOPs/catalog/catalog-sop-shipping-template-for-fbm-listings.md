# Catalog SOP: Shipping Template for FBM Listings

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-shipping-template-for-fbm-listings

## **Shipping Templates for FBM Listings. (Manual and Bulk)**

**Who is this for :** Everyone needs to update and customize their shipping preferences in the account settings.

**Objective:** To demonstrate the process of creating and modifying shipping templates and applying them to inventory items either manually or in bulk.

> **IMPORTANT REMINDER:**
>
> **(Effective July 30, 2025):** Amazon now offers **Premium Shipping** for 1-day and 2-day regional deliveries.
>
> To enable it, sellers must update their shipping template and meet specific performance thresholds.
>
> See Premium Shipping program policy for full details.

Shipping templates are pre-made shipping settings that you can use to update and customize your shipping preferences. This streamlines the fulfillment process and makes it easier for sellers to accurately and efficiently ship their products to customers. You can use them for various items and set the following:

- Shipping cost and how you charge it (by weight, order, or sales price)
- Transit time, excluding handling time
- Regions you ship
- Address type (Street or PO Box)

**Instructions:**

**To Create a New Shipping Template:**

1. Click the gear button on the upper right-hand side of the page and choose "Shipping Settings."

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-25%20at%2012.57.07%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/IxBimage.png)

2. You can create a brand new template from scratch or duplicate a pre-existing one and adjust specific settings according to your needs.

In this example, we will use the latter.

On the Shipping Templates tab, click Create New Shipping Template and choose an option that matches the settings you have in mind under the "No, I want to copy the content from" dropdown. Then click OK.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-25%20at%2012.57.14%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/caMimage.png)

3. Enter the template name (for example, "Free Shipping").

4. Choose the [rate model](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/help/201841310) for your shipping rates: Per item/weight-based, or Price banded

5. Enable or disable Prime delivery as required.

6. Select or deselect Same day for Handling Time Settings.

7. Edit Shipping Options, Regions, and Rates as applicable. Then click OK.

- Enable Domestic or International shipping options (such as Expedited) by selecting the checkbox next to the shipping option name.
- Select the regions by clicking either Edit or Add New Region.
- Within the Select Deliverable Regions pop-up window, select or deselect the region(s) you want to use for the same transit time and shipping cost.

8. Select the Address Type for the region(s)

9. Set your Shipping Time (in business days) for the region(s).

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-25%20at%2012.57.24%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/GL6image.png)

10. [Set your shipping rates](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/help/201841310). Click Save.

How to apply a shipping template to an item in the inventory

There are two ways to apply a shipping template to an FBM listing. You can manually update the shipping template through the Manage Inventory page or upload a partial update feed file if you're updating multiple listings.

**MANUAL UPDATE:**

1. Go to Inventory → Manage All Inventory

2. Search the **ASIN/SKU** you want to update. **Note:**Make sure the filters are set to “**All”** for the Status and **Fulfilled by**.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-25%20at%2012.57.32%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wCNimage.png)

3. Click the drop-down arrow beside the **“Edit”** button and select **“Change shipping template”** from the options. Sometimes, this button is on the far right side.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-25%20at%2012.57.38%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/02bimage.png)

4. On the landing page, you will find a **“Change shipping template”** button on the upper right side of the page. Click it and select what shipping template to use.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-25%20at%2012.57.45%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/AvOimage.png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-25%20at%2012.57.54%E2%80%AFAM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/p5limage.png)

5. Click the **“Yes, continue”** yellow button.

Sometimes, the updates take 15 minutes or less, or even hours, to reflect on the product detail page. Just monitor this and report once complete.

**FEED FILE/BULK UPDATE:**

1. Download a fresh flat-file template specific to the types of products you need to update. On the feed file, fill out the SKU column, press **Ctrl “F”** on your keyboard, and search for **“Shipping-Template.”**
2. Click the cell below **“merchant_shipping_group_name”** on row 4 and then click on the drop-down option, which will give you what shipping template to use.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-25%20at%2012.58.02%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/lm7image.png)
3. Locate the "update_delete" column, input **"Partial Update"** into each row with an SKU filled in, and save the file.
4. Refresh Seller Central twice to ensure that you're on the correct account, then upload your saved file to the [Add Products via the Upload page](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/listing/upload?ref_=xx_upload_tnav_status).
5. Download and open the processing report. Check if the upload was successful, and troubleshoot as needed.

FAQs

**1. What is a shipping template, and why should I use it?**

- A shipping template is a pre-configured set of shipping preferences (e.g., rates, regions, and delivery times) that you can assign to your FBM listings. Using templates helps standardize shipping settings across multiple listings, saving time and ensuring consistency.

**2. Can I create multiple shipping templates for different product categories?**

- Yes, you can create multiple shipping templates tailored to different products or categories. For example, one template can be for free domestic shipping, while another can focus on expedited international shipping.

**3. How do I know which rate model to choose for my shipping template?**

- Choose a rate model based on your business needs:

  - Per item/weight-based: Best for items with varying sizes and weights.
  - Price banded: Ideal if you want to offer free shipping over a certain order value or tiered rates based on purchase amount.

**4. What regions can I select for domestic and international shipping?**

- Regions vary based on the marketplace you’re operating in. Domestic regions include states or provinces, while international regions may include specific countries or continents. Use the "Select Deliverable Regions" tool in the shipping template settings to enable or disable specific areas.

**5. How does enabling or disabling Prime delivery affect my shipping template?**

- Enabling Prime delivery allows you to cater to customers looking for faster shipping options. However, Prime delivery eligibility depends on your shipping performance and fulfillment capabilities.

**6. Can I offer different handling times for different shipping options?**

- No, handling time applies to all shipping options within a template. You can enable or disable "Same Day" handling for faster order processing if required.

**7. How long does it take for shipping template changes to reflect on listings?**

- Changes can take anywhere from 15 minutes to several hours to reflect on the product detail page. Monitor updates and check back periodically.

**8. What happens if I don’t assign a shipping template to an FBM listing?**

- If no shipping template is assigned, Amazon may use default settings, which might not align with your preferences. This can lead to mismatched shipping rates, longer transit times, or regions you don’t intend to serve.

**9. Can I bulk-apply a shipping template to multiple listings without a feed file?**

- No, bulk updates without a feed file are not supported. To apply a shipping template to multiple SKUs, you must use the flat-file upload method.

**10. What should I do if my feed file upload fails?**

- If your feed file upload fails:

  - Check for errors in the file format, such as missing or incorrect data in required columns (e.g., SKU, shipping group name, update/delete).
  - Download the processing report to identify the issue and fix it.
  - Re-upload the corrected file and verify the status.

**11. Are there restrictions on using PO Box addresses in shipping templates?**

- Yes, certain carriers or shipping methods may not deliver to PO Box addresses. Be sure to verify with your carrier before enabling this address type in your shipping template.

**12. Can I apply a shipping template to inactive or suppressed listings?**

- Yes, you can assign a shipping template to inactive or suppressed listings. Ensure the listing is active or compliant before expecting it to reflect on the product detail page.

**13. How do I troubleshoot if my shipping template settings aren’t applying correctly?**

- If the settings don’t apply as expected:

  - Confirm that the correct template is selected for the listing.
  - Double-check that all required fields in the feed file are filled out properly.
  - Refresh Seller Central and ensure the update has been processed successfully.

**14. Can I edit an existing shipping template instead of creating a new one?**

- Yes, you can edit an existing shipping template directly. Navigate to Settings > Shipping Settings, select the template you wish to modify, and update the fields as needed.

**15. How can I test my shipping rates and regions after creating a template?**

- Use Amazon’s Shipping Settings Preview Tool to simulate shipping rates and delivery times for specific regions and ensure they align with your configured template.

**16. What is the difference between manual updates and bulk updates for shipping templates?**

- Manual updates: Update shipping templates for individual listings through the Manage Inventory page.
- Bulk updates: Use a flat-file upload to assign templates to multiple SKUs at once, ideal for large inventories.

**17. What happens if I select “Partial Update” in the feed file?**

- Choosing "Partial Update" ensures only the specified fields (e.g., shipping template) are updated for the listings, while all other details remain unchanged. This is helpful to avoid overwriting unintended information.

**18. Can I delete a shipping template after it’s been created?**

- Yes, but only if the template is not currently assigned to any active listings. Remove the template from all SKUs before deleting it to avoid system errors.

**19. Are there additional fees for enabling expedited or international shipping?**

- Yes, enabling these options may increase your fulfillment costs, which you’ll need to account for when setting shipping rates. Verify with your carrier and adjust rates accordingly to avoid losses.
