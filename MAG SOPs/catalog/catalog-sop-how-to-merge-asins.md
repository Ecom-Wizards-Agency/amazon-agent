# Catalog SOP: How to Merge ASINs

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-how-to-merge-asins

## **Seller Central ASINs Merge**

**Who is this for?** Individuals who wanted to merge identical listings.

**Objective:** The purpose of this document is to present a step-by-step guide to merging the ASINs.

**Overview:**

- ASIN merging is a valuable strategy for Amazon sellers who want to protect their brand against unauthorized sellers or clean up their catalog listings.
- There are 2 ways to merge duplicate ASINs, but the simplest and most effective method is through the **Merge Duplicate Product Page** and contacting Amazon’s Catalog Team to do the merge for you via Seller Support.

**NOTE :** Amazon will only merge products when it is clear that they are one and the same and have the same identifying attributes such as **categories, brand names, titles, images, and all other attributes except UPC in most cases**.

**Definition of terms**

**TARGET ASIN**

- Definition : The ASIN that remains active and retains all its details after the merge
- Purpose : This is typically the most accurate, complete, or primary listing that will represent the merged product on Amazon.
- Outcome

  - It absorbs the product data, reviews, sales rank, and inventory from the source ASIN
  - Customers are redirected to the target ASIN when searching for the source ASIN.

**SOURCE ASIN**

- Definition : The listing being merged into the target ASIN
- Purpose : This listing is often a duplicate, outdated, or secondary version of the product.
- Outcome :

  - It is deactivated or suppressed, and its product data (e.g., reviews, inventory, etc.) is transferred to the target ASIN
  - The source ASIN's page will typically redirect customers to the target ASIN

**Instances for when to merge ASIN’s**

- If there is a change in UPC
- Mistakenly created a duplicate listing or we want to merge the reviews.

**Instructions:**

1. Make sure that all attributes are the same before submitting a merge request.

2. Download the CLR and compare if the ASINs have the same attributes.

3. Submit an ASIN merge request, follow these steps:

***Note :****We Usually retain the Old ASIN since it has all the sales history and reviews*

FAQs

**1. What is an ASIN merge, and why is it important?**

- An ASIN merge combines two identical listings into one, consolidating product data, reviews, sales rank, and inventory. This helps protect your brand from unauthorized sellers, clean up catalog duplicates, and improve customer experience by reducing confusion.

**2. What is the difference between a Target ASIN and a Source ASIN?**

- Target ASIN: The primary ASIN that remains active and retains all details after the merge. It absorbs data like reviews and sales history from the Source ASIN.
- Source ASIN: The duplicate or secondary ASIN that is deactivated after the merge. Its page redirects customers to the Target ASIN.

**3. When should I merge ASINs?**

- Merge ASINs when:

  - A duplicate listing has been mistakenly created.
  - You want to consolidate reviews for identical products.
  - There is a UPC change, but the products remain identical in all other attributes.

**4. How can I verify if two ASINs are eligible for merging?**

- Download the Category Listings Report (CLR) for both ASINs and compare the attributes. Ensure the ASINs are identical in:

  - Brand name
  - Title
  - Images
  - Product category
  - Product details (dimensions, features, etc.).
  - If they differ significantly, Amazon will reject the merge request.

**5. Why does Amazon prioritize keeping the older ASIN during a merge?**

- Older ASINs often have more sales history, reviews, and better rankings, making them more valuable for customer retention and search visibility. Retaining the older ASIN ensures minimal disruption to the listing’s performance.

**6. How do I submit an ASIN merge request?**

- Search for Merge duplicate product page in the Seller Central search bar to access the Merge ASIN tool.
- Enter the Source ASIN (to merge) and the Target ASIN (to keep).
- Submit the request. If Amazon rejects the request, proceed with a manual case.

**7. What should I include in a manual case for merging ASINs?**

- Provide:

  - The Source ASIN (to merge).
  - The Target ASIN (to keep).
  - A brief explanation of why the ASINs need to be merged (e.g., identical products with the same brand and images).
  - Use the sample email provided in the SOP as a guide.

**8. What happens if Amazon rejects my ASIN merge request?**

- Amazon may reject a request due to:

  - Mismatched attributes between ASINs.
  - Issues with Amazon Catalog Team control.
  - Ambiguity in the request.
  - If rejected, review the ASIN details for accuracy and resubmit the request with additional clarity. Don’t hesitate to open a new case if you’re confident the merge is valid.

**9. Can I merge ASINs with different UPCs?**

- Yes, Amazon allows merges for ASINs with different UPCs, provided all other attributes are identical (e.g., title, brand, images, category).

**10. How long does it take for Amazon to process an ASIN merge request?**

- Processing times vary, but requests are typically reviewed within 3–5 business days. Monitor your Seller Central case for updates.

**11. What happens after a successful ASIN merge?**

- The Source ASIN is deactivated or suppressed.
- Customers searching for the Source ASIN are redirected to the Target ASIN.
- Data such as reviews, inventory, and sales history from the Source ASIN is transferred to the Target ASIN.

**12. Can I undo an ASIN merge after it’s completed?**

- Yes, it is possible to unmerge ASINs, but the process may require a request through the appropriate support channels. Ensure all details are accurate before proceeding with a merge.

**13. What should I do if the Source ASIN isn’t redirecting to the Target ASIN after the merge?**

- If the redirection isn’t functioning correctly:

  - Wait 24–48 hours for the changes to propagate.
  - If the issue persists, open a case with Amazon Seller Support and provide the merge details for troubleshooting.

**14. What should I do if the ASIN merge involves a listing under Amazon Catalog Team control?**

- If Amazon Catalog Team controls the listing, mention this in your manual case. Amazon will review the request and handle the merge if appropriate.

**15. Are there risks associated with merging ASINs?**

- Yes, risks include:

  - Losing sales history or reviews if attributes are mismatched.
  - Incorrectly merging non-identical ASINs, which could confuse customers.
  - Always verify that ASINs are identical before requesting a merge.

**16. Can I merge ASINs for products in different categories?**

- No, ASINs in different categories cannot be merged. Both ASINs must belong to the same product category.

**17. How can I track the status of my merge request?**

- Monitor the case status in Seller Central under Case Log. Amazon will provide updates, including acceptance, rejection, or additional inquiries.

**18. Why is my merge request repeatedly rejected?**

- Common reasons include:

  - Discrepancies between Source and Target ASIN attributes.
  - Insufficient explanation provided in the request.
  - The ASINs do not meet Amazon’s merge criteria.
  - Review the attributes, provide clear reasoning, and resubmit if necessary.

**19. Can I merge ASINs created by unauthorized sellers?**

- Yes, if the unauthorized ASIN is a duplicate of your product, you can merge it into your legitimate Target ASIN. Provide evidence (e.g., identical images, titles, or brand ownership) when submitting your request.

**20. What happens to the reviews of the Source ASIN after the merge?**

- Reviews from the Source ASIN are transferred to the Target ASIN, ensuring all feedback consolidates into one listing.
