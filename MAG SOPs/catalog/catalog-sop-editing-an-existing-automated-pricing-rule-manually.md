## Editing an Existing Automated Pricing Rule Manually

**Who is this for :** For Amazon sellers who are interested in understanding the process of manually editing automated pricing rules to enhance their knowledge and skills in pricing optimization.

**Objective :** To ensure accurate and optimized pricing strategies that align with the business goals, market dynamics, and customer preferences. By manually editing the automated pricing rule, the aim is to fine-tune and customize the pricing algorithm, taking into account specific factors and considerations that automated algorithms may overlook. The goal is to strike a balance between automation and human intervention to achieve the desired pricing outcomes.

Prior any planned changes for auto pricing rule, it is important to inform, brief details and get client’s approval. Include the approval proof (screenshot or fireflies call link) as Task Details or as a Comment.

**To manually update an existing automated pricing:**

1. In Seller Central page, go to "Pricing" and then select "Automate Pricing". FAQs

1. Who needs to approve changes to an automated pricing rule?

Approval should come from the business owner, account manager, or another designated decision-maker responsible for pricing strategies. Proof of approval (e.g., email screenshot or Fireflies call link) must be attached in Task Details or as a Comment.

2. What happens when I manually change an automated pricing rule?

Manually editing an automated pricing rule can immediately affect:

- The product’s competitive position in the marketplace
- Pricing adjustments based on the new rule settings
- Sales performance, depending on how pricing aligns with competitors’ offers

3. How long does it take for manual pricing changes to take effect?

Price updates typically reflect within 15 minutes but may take longer due to system delays. If changes do not update after 30 minutes, refresh Seller Central or check for conflicting rules.

4. How can I verify that my pricing rule changes were applied correctly?

To confirm that updates were successful:

- Go to Pricing > Automate Pricing and check if the ASIN is assigned to the correct rule.
- Visit the Manage Inventory page to see the updated pricing.
- Check your product listing on Amazon to verify the price display.

5. Are there any limitations on how often automated pricing rules can be changed?

Yes, pricing rules can be changed at any time, but frequent adjustments may cause pricing inconsistencies. Additionally:

- Automated pricing does not override promotional discounts or coupons.
- Some pricing updates may be delayed if Amazon's system is processing multiple rule changes.

6. How do I disable automated pricing for an ASIN?

To turn off automated pricing for a specific ASIN:

1. Go to Pricing > Automate Pricing in Seller Central.
2. Locate the ASIN under the assigned rule.
3. Click "Take Action" and select "Remove from Rule" to stop automatic adjustments.
4. The ASIN will now require manual pricing updates.
