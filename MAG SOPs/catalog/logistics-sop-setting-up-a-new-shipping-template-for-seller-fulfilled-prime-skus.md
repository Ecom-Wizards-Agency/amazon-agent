# Logistics SOP: Setting up a New Shipping Template for Seller Fulfilled Prime SKUs

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/logistics-sop-setting-up-a-new-shipping-template-for-seller-fulfilled-prime-skus

## **Setting up a New Shipping Template for Seller-Fulfilled Prime SKUs**

**Who is this for :** Anyone who works in the catalog management team, specifically those who are responsible for setting up new shipping templates for Seller Fulfilled Prime SKUs.

**Objective :** To provide step-by-step instructions on how to set up a new shipping template for Seller Fulfilled Prime SKUs to ensure efficient and accurate shipping to customers.

> **IMPORTANT REMINDER:**
>
> **(Effective July 30, 2025):** Amazon now offers **Premium Shipping** for 1-day and 2-day regional deliveries.
>
> To enable it, sellers must update their shipping template and meet specific performance thresholds.
>
> See Premium Shipping program policy for full details.

**To create a new shipping template on your Seller Central Dashboard, follow these steps:**

1. Go to the "Settings" tab located at the top right-hand side of your dashboard and select "Shipping Settings" from the dropdown menu.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1682440057506.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/KSmimage.png)
2. Click "Create New Shipping Template" or choose a template from the dropdown menu. If it's your first time creating a shipping template, select "Create a New Shipping Template" and click "Ok".

  **Note:** If your Merchant Fulfilled SKU weighs less than 16oz, select Template A. If it weighs more than 16oz, select Template B. Keep in mind that the templates were designed for packages that are no more than 12 inches in any direction and weigh less than 70 lbs.
3. Enter the relevant information for each section, including the Shipping Template Name (we recommend "Prime" or "Seller Fulfilled Prime"), Rate Model, Shipping Region Automation, Prime Settings, and Shipping Options, Regions, and Rates.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1682441501390.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Hvfimage.png)

  4. Customize your shipping rates, transit times, and address types for Standard Shipping, Expedited Shipping, Two-Day Delivery, and One-Day Shipping, and add regions or states to the rule if needed. You can also set International Shipping Options.

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wnvimage.png)

  1. Click the "Save" button at the bottom of the page to create and save your new shipping template.

FAQs

1. Do I need to be enrolled in Seller Fulfilled Prime before creating a shipping template?

Yes, you must be enrolled in Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP) before setting up a shipping template for SFP SKUs. If you’re not enrolled, your SKUs won’t be eligible for Prime benefits.

2. What are the account requirements for setting up a Seller Fulfilled Prime shipping template?

To create an SFP shipping template, your account must:

- Be actively enrolled in Seller Fulfilled Prime.
- Maintain high fulfillment performance standards, including on-time shipping and low cancellation rates.
- Offer premium shipping options like Two-Day and One-Day Delivery.

3. Which rate model should I choose when setting up a shipping template?

- Per Item/Weight-Based Pricing: Best for variable-sized products.
- Price Band Pricing: Useful if you want to offer different rates based on the total cart value.
- Free Shipping: If you want to absorb the shipping cost for customers.
- Automated Shipping Settings: Amazon sets rates dynamically based on location.

4. How do I ensure my shipping template is correctly applied to my SKUs?

After creating the template, go to Inventory > Manage Inventory, select your SFP SKUs, click Edit, and assign the correct shipping template under the Offer tab.

5. What should I do if I can’t see the “Seller Fulfilled Prime” option in my shipping settings?

If the SFP option is missing, try the following:

- Confirm that your account is enrolled in Seller Fulfilled Prime.
- Check if you meet Amazon’s performance criteria.
- Contact Amazon Seller Support to verify eligibility.

6. How do I test whether my new shipping template is working?

- Create a test order using your SKU to see if Prime shipping options appear at checkout.
- Use Amazon’s Preview Shipping Template feature to check available rates.
- Monitor any customer orders to ensure expected shipping speeds are applied.

7. What are common errors when setting up an SFP shipping template?

- Error: Missing Prime Shipping Options – Ensure Two-Day and One-Day shipping are enabled.
- Error: SKU Not Assigned to Template – Check that the SKU is mapped to the correct shipping template.
- Error: Shipping Rates Not Displaying – Verify that the correct shipping regions are selected.

8. Can I edit my Seller Fulfilled Prime shipping template after saving it?

Yes, you can edit the template at any time by going to Settings > Shipping Settings, selecting the template, and clicking Edit. Changes will apply to SKUs assigned to that template.

9. Is there a way to set different shipping rates for specific regions?

Yes, you can customize rates for different states or regions under Shipping Options, Regions, and Rates. You can also exclude specific areas if needed.

10. Where can I find a tutorial or training on setting up Seller Fulfilled Prime shipping templates?

Amazon provides official training through Seller University. You can also check the Learning Video section on your Shipping Settings page for guided instructions.
