## Maximizing Efficiency with Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD)

[Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD)](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/asdn/about) is an efficient, cost-effective solution for bulk inventory storage and distribution, designed to help sellers optimize their operations. Whether you're a seasoned seller with large quantities of products or a new seller looking to streamline your logistics, AWD provides significant benefits in reducing costs and simplifying processes.

**What is AWD?**

[Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD)](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/asdn/about) is a service that allows sellers to store their inventory in Amazon's warehouses and distribute it not only to Amazon fulfillment centers but also to other sales channels. It is a particularly valuable service for sellers who need to store large quantities of inventory in a cost-efficient way.

**Key Benefits of AWD:**

- Cost-Effective Bulk Storage

  : AWD offers lower per-unit storage fees compared to standard Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) storage options. By sending your inventory in bulk to AWD facilities, you save on storage costs, particularly for large product volumes.
- Automated Replenishment

  : One of the standout features of AWD is its automation. The service automatically replenishes and optimizes your inventory at Amazon Fulfillment Centers (FBA), ensuring you always have stock ready for sale without needing to manage inventory manually.
- Expanded Distribution Channels

  : With AWD, your inventory isn’t just stored in Amazon’s fulfillment centers; it can also be distributed to other sales channels. Whether you sell on Amazon or other marketplaces, AWD can help manage and distribute your stock seamlessly.
- Simplified Logistics

  : AWD takes the logistics burden off your shoulders by handling inbound placement fees to FBA and other distribution tasks. This makes it a more streamlined and affordable way to manage your inventory, as Amazon takes care of the majority of the logistical operations.

**How to Get Started with AWD:**

1. Access Seller Central

  :

  Start by logging into your Seller Central account. From the inventory dropdown menu, select

  Warehousing and Distribution (AWD)

  .
2. Create a Shipment

  :

  After selecting AWD from the program page, click on

  "Create Shipment"

  . This will allow you to start the process of sending your inventory to Amazon’s AWD facilities.
3. Inventory Management

  :

  Within 2 to 3 days of creating your first shipment, you’ll gain access to the

  Amazon Warehousing and Distribution Inventory

  page, where you can manage and track your shipments.
4. No Fees to Get Started

  :

  There are no fees associated with setting up AWD. You will only be charged when you ship your inventory to Amazon’s AWD facilities, so there are no upfront costs involved. This makes it a low-risk option for sellers looking to optimize their distribution.

**Permissions for Accessing AWD Tools:**

Note that the AWD tools in Seller Central are only accessible to users with appropriate permissions. If you do not see the AWD tools in your account, you’ll need to ask your primary account holder to grant you access via the **User Permissions** page.

**Additional Considerations for AWD:**

Before sending inventory to AWD, ensure your products meet all relevant FBA requirements, including:

- Restricted Product Categories

  : Some categories require pre-approval from Amazon before listing products. Review the

  FBA guidelines

  for restricted categories.
- Stranded Inventory

  : FBA inventory in fulfillment centers that is not available for sale is considered stranded. Resolve any stranded inventory issues before sending products to AWD.
- FBA Barcodes

  : All products must be labeled with FBA barcodes. You can print Amazon barcodes yourself or have Amazon print and apply them for a fee.
- Product Listing Issues

  : Ensure all product listings are valid before sending inventory to AWD. Invalid listings must be corrected before inventory can be shipped.
- Seller Account Performance

  : Any performance issues with your seller account or items being sent to AWD must be resolved before sending inventory.

If your products are incorrectly labeled as dangerous goods or if there are dimension issues preventing you from adding ASINs to the AWD shipment, contact [**Selling Partner Support**](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/cu/contact-us?_encoding=UTF8&ref_=ag_contactus_nmhb_521).

**Summary:**

By using Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD), sellers can streamline inventory management, reduce storage costs, and expand their product distribution capabilities. Whether you're managing large quantities of inventory or looking to optimize your existing supply chain, AWD simplifies the process, saves you time, and helps you reach more customers efficiently. Be sure to review the **AWD Product Eligibility** criteria and other requirements before getting started to ensure the program is right for your business.
