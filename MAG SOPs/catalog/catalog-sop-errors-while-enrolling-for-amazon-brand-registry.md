# Catalog SOP: Errors while Enrolling for Amazon Brand Registry

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-errors-while-enrolling-for-amazon-brand-registry

## **Errors while Enrolling for Amazon Brand Registry**

**Who is this for:**

- For individuals who are enrolling for Amazon Brand Registry and may encounter errors during the enrollment process.
- For individuals who have already enrolled but are experiencing issues with their brand record or need to add their Seller Central or Vendor Central account as a user to their Brand Registry account.

**Objective:** To provide guidance on how to handle errors that may occur while enrolling for Amazon Brand Registry.

**Below are the requirements for enrolling in the Brand Registry program:**

- An active registered trademark for your brand that appears on your products or packaging.
- The ability to verify yourself as the rights owner or authorized agent for the trademark.
- An Amazon account can either be an existing Amazon account (credentials associated with Vendor Central or Seller Central) or a new one created for free.

**There are some errors you may encounter while enrolling in the Brand Registry program:**

- If you receive an error message stating "Due to your current account status, you are not eligible to enroll a new brand in Amazon Brand Registry at this time." or any error related to your account status, your account status is not normal. To fix it, contact the team mentioned in the error message.
- If you receive an error message on the "Tell Us About Your Business" page or "Reset Default View" page during account creation, wait for 15 minutes before trying again. If the error persists, contact the Seller Support team if you are a seller, or contact support through http://www.amazon-brand-registry.com/eng/contact_us if you are not a seller.
- If you receive the Reference 140 Error, it means your account status is not normal or the other accounts linked to your ID are not in normal status. Contact Seller Support to address the issue before reapplying.
- If you receive an error message stating "You indicated you are a new customer, but an account already exists with the email abc@xyz.com", there is an existing account associated with the email address you are using to create a new Brand Registry account. Use your Seller Central or Vendor Central account credentials when creating your Brand Registry account to ensure those accounts are properly connected.
- If you are already enrolled in Brand Registry but receive a disclaimer stating "If you are planning on creating a Seller Central account with the credentials you used to log in, please create that account first...", it means you need to create a Seller or Vendor account first. If you already have one, create a new Brand Registry account while logged in to your Seller Central/Vendor Central account, or use the email you used to create your Brand Registry account to complete your brand enrollment. After enrollment, contact Brand Registry Support to add your Seller Central or Vendor Central account as a user to your Brand Registry account.
- If you are unable to apply for Brand Registry or you are already enrolled but there is no brand record and the system asks you to enroll in a new brand, it is a catch-all error message for any unexpected problem that arose while creating the application. Wait for 15 minutes before trying again or contact the Amazon Brand Registry team to resolve the issue.
- If you receive an error message on the "Intellectual Property" page stating "The trademark registration number you entered is in the incorrect format...", you may be trying to add the serial number of the trademark instead of the registration number. Ensure you are entering the correct trademark registration number and selecting the correct trademark office. Also, if your trademark was recently approved, it may take up to 48 hours for the change to be reflected on the site.

**Rejection Appeal Steps :**

- Go to Brand Applications Dashboard
- Check the rejected application and click on Appeal
- Read carefully the "Appeal Submission Form
- Select the applicable options
- Upload required documents in PDF varying per rejection reason
- Submit the appeal and wait for the Result email.

**Media Learning:**

FAQs

**1. How do I check if my account is in "normal" status before enrolling in Brand Registry?**

- To verify your account status:

  - Log in to Seller Central or Vendor Central and check for any performance warnings or restrictions.
  - Go to Account Health in Seller Central and ensure there are no policy violations or suspensions.
  - If issues exist, resolve them first before attempting to enroll in Brand Registry.

**2. How long does it take for Amazon to fix an enrollment error?**

- Most Brand Registry errors are resolved within 5–7 business days, but more complex issues may take up to 30 days.
- If you don’t receive an update within a week, follow up with Brand Registry Support.

**3. What should I do if Seller Support or Brand Registry Support isn’t resolving my issue?**

- If your issue remains unresolved:

  - Reopen the case and request escalation to a senior representative.
  - Provide detailed evidence, such as screenshots of error messages, trademark documentation, and previous support case numbers.
  - If necessary, contact Amazon Brand Registry through their official website and request manual intervention.

**4. My brand application has been in review for weeks. What should I do?**

- Check your email for any additional documentation requests from Amazon.
- Ensure your trademark is fully registered, not just pending.
- Contact Brand Registry Support if your application has been under review for more than 30 days.

**5. Will changing my Seller Central or Vendor Central account details affect my Brand Registry enrollment?**

- Yes, if you change the email, legal business name, or country of registration associated with your Seller or Vendor Central account, it may cause issues with Brand Registry access. If changes are necessary:

  - Update your Brand Registry account details accordingly.
  - Contact Brand Registry Support to confirm continued access.

**6. My trademark information is correct, but Amazon says it doesn't match their records. What should I do?**

- Double-check if you are entering the exact registration number, not the serial number.
- Confirm that the trademark office selected matches the country of registration.
- If your trademark was recently approved, wait up to 48 hours for Amazon’s database to update.
- If the issue persists, contact Amazon Brand Registry Support with official trademark documentation.

**7. How do I add users to my Brand Registry account after enrollment?**

- Log in to Amazon Brand Registry.
- Go to "User Permissions" and select "Add New User".
- Enter the email associated with their Seller Central or Vendor Central account.
- Assign the correct role (Admin, Rights Owner, or Registered Agent).
- If the system does not recognize the email, contact Brand Registry Support for assistance.

**8. What if my appeal for a rejected Brand Registry application is denied?**

- Review the reason for rejection carefully in your Brand Applications Dashboard.
- Gather additional supporting documents (e.g., trademark registration certificates, invoices, brand website screenshots).
- If the rejection was due to an incorrect trademark match, update and resubmit the correct trademark details.
- If denied again, escalate the appeal through Brand Registry Support.
