## Update FBA Weight and Dimension (Cubiscan)

**Who is this for :** Individuals who are responsible for managing inventory

**Objective :** To ensure accurate and up-to-date product measurements and prevent clients from being overcharged for shipping due to incorrect dimensions and weights.

Item package weight and dimensions refer to the weight and dimensions of the individual unit that you’re listing under an FNSKU. The weight and dimensions include the unit’s packaging, such as the individual box or polybag. The item, item package, and case can all have distinct weights and dimensions. FBA fees are based on item package weight and dimensions.

**Update as of 12/9/2025** - Amazon has updated the maximum remeasurement requests from 20 to 30 per month.

1.) You can request a remeasurement through the Remeasure FBA products and confirm fees widget. Locate the widget by typing “remeasure” into the search bar.

5.) Once the request is submitted, Amazon will provide the current weight and dimension as well as the FBA fees. Amazon mostly responds to re-measurement requests within five business days however, some cases may take longer.

What are other reasons why a remeasurement request may not be processed?

Other reasons we may not be able to process re-measurement requests include the following:

- There is no inventory in our fulfillment centers, or inventory is not in a location that is accessible to associates.
- You have already submitted two remeasurement requests for the same FNSKU within the last 60 days. This cap is independent of account-level remeasurement limits. Even if an account is still able to measure additional FNSKUs in a given month, each FNSKU can only be re-measured twice within the preceding 60 days.

Please Note: We only get 30 remeasure requests per account, per month.

FAQS

**1. What is the purpose of requesting a re-measurement?**

- Re-measurements help ensure that the dimensions and weight of your product are accurate in Amazon’s system. This prevents overcharges for FBA fees, which are based on item package weight and dimensions.

**2. Can I request a remeasurement for a product with no inventory in Amazon's fulfillment centers?**

- No, re-measurement requests cannot be processed if there is no inventory available in Amazon’s fulfillment centers or if the inventory is inaccessible to associates.

**3. How often can I request a re-measurement for the same FNSKU?**

- You can request up to two re-measurements per FNSKU within 60 days. If both requests are used, you must wait for the 60-day period to reset before submitting another request.

**4. Is there a limit to how many re-measurement requests I can submit per month?**

- Yes, you can submit up to 20 re-measurement requests per account per month. This limit applies at the account level, regardless of the number of FNSKUs or ASINs you manage.

**5. What happens if Amazon’s remeasurement does not resolve the issue?**

- If the re-measurement does not resolve the discrepancy, you can:

  - Open a case with Amazon Seller Support.
  - Provide documentation such as shipping invoices, manufacturer specifications, or images showing the accurate dimensions and weight.

**6. How long does it take for Amazon to complete a re-measurement request?**

- Amazon typically responds to re-measurement requests within five business days, though some cases may take longer depending on the circumstances.

**7. Can I request reimbursement if the incorrect dimensions resulted in overcharges?**

- Yes, after the remeasurement confirms the correct dimensions and weight, you can request a reimbursement for any overcharges incurred due to incorrect FBA fees. This can be done by opening a case with Amazon Seller Support.

**8. What should I check before submitting a re-measurement request?**

- Before submitting a re-measurement request, verify the following:

  - The FNSKU or ASIN entered is accurate.
  - The package weight and dimensions listed in Amazon’s system differ from the actual measurements.
  - You have not exceeded the monthly or FNSKU-specific request limits.

**9. How do I ensure my measurements are correct before submitting to Amazon?**

- Use tools such as a Cubiscan machine or a measuring tape to accurately determine the package weight and dimensions. Include all packaging (e.g., polybags, boxes) in your measurements.

**10. Can re-measurement requests affect my IPI score?**

- No, submitting a re-measurement request does not directly impact your Inventory Performance Index (IPI) score. However, ensuring accurate product dimensions can help optimize your inventory storage and fees, indirectly supporting a better IPI score.

**11. What happens if my re-measurement request is rejected?**

- If your request is rejected, review the reason provided by Amazon. Common issues include:

  - Submitting more than two requests for the same FNSKU within 60 days.
  - No inventory being available in fulfillment centers.
  - Errors in the submitted FNSKU or ASIN.
  - After resolving the issue, you can re-submit the request if applicable.

**12. Are all products eligible for re-measurement?**

- Most products are eligible, but items without inventory in Amazon's fulfillment centers or in restricted storage locations cannot be remeasured. Additionally, hazardous materials and certain restricted categories may have limitations.

**13. How do I track the status of a re-measurement request?**

- You can track the status of your request in the Remeasure FBA products and confirm fees widget. Amazon will notify you of the outcome once the request is processed.

**14. Are there fees associated with submitting a re-measurement request?**

- No, submitting a re-measurement request is free. However, it’s important to stay within the monthly and FNSKU-specific limits to avoid delays.

**15. Can I appeal if Amazon’s measurements still seem incorrect after a re-measurement?**

- Yes, if the re-measurement results are inaccurate, you can appeal by opening a case with Amazon Seller Support. Provide detailed evidence, such as images of the product on a scale or with measurement tools, to support your claim.
