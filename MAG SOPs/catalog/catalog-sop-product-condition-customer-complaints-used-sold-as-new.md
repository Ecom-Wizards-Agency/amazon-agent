## **Product Condition Customer Complaints**

**Who is this for :** This is for the warehouse staff, packagers, and management team who are responsible for ensuring that the products are packaged and shipped in good condition, and to prevent customer complaints related to product condition.

**Objective :** To prevent customer complaints related to the product condition caused by packaging and shipping issues.

This issue is caused by customer complaints who received used products. The complaint will show up on the account health page:

**WORKED SEVERAL TIMES**

1. We have not checked all products we received from the manufacturer. Some products arrive with bad packaging that seems like it had been previously opened or repackaged or appeared used because of the lack of proper protective shipping equipment.

2. They did not package each item properly as we shipped it to customers causing damages to its outer packaging during shipment to the customers.

3. Lack of proper packaging and protective shipping material caused items to arrive with scratches, scuffs, or other markings such as fingerprints or smudges.

4. This lack of quality control process and care led to the products arriving in opened or used packaging, or with signs of use such as scratches, dirt, or scuffs.

**Preventive actions we have taken to prevent future complaints:**

1. We responded to all customer complaints and issued a full refund or free replacement.

2. We audited our catalog to ensure all listings are solid and in the proper condition and all of them have proper packaging.

3. We have had a warehouse-wide meeting with the warehouse division informing the packagers that there were complaints about products being received as used items due to potential defects caused by the shipping process while being shipped from our warehouse to the buyer.

4. We reviewed their shipping and packaging processes and reinforced our shipping policies to ensure that these issues will not happen again. We restocked protective materials such as bubble wrap, and scratch-resistant papers which go over our products before shipping them to customers.

5. We as a company have reviewed all of Amazon’s policies, paying special attention to the item condition policies to ensure our policies match and exceed Amazon’s guidelines.

6. We have taken the extra step to order fragile stickers to put on our packages to ensure our postal carrier does not damage the products, and we have communicated this to our carrier representative as well. This will ensure the products will not be damaged during the shipment to warehouses or customers.

7. Management will routinely check and audit the shipment process to ensure all in-house rules and Amazon rules and regulations are being followed.

8. We take all responsibility for this incident and we have learned from this issue. We confirm this will never happen again in the future.

FAQs

1. What are Product Condition Customer Complaints on Amazon?

Product Condition Customer Complaints occur when customers receive products that appear used, repackaged, damaged, or missing essential packaging. These complaints impact Account Health and may lead to listing removal if not resolved.

2. How can I check if my listing has been flagged for a Product Condition Complaint?

Go to Performance > Account Health > Product Condition Customer Complaints to see flagged ASINs and the reason for the issue.

3. What are the common reasons for receiving product condition complaints?

- Products arriving with scratches, scuffs, fingerprints, or dirt
- Packaging appearing previously opened, repackaged, or damaged
- Missing or broken seals or protective covers
- Items being shipped without adequate protective materials
- Lack of quality control in checking inventory before shipping

4. How can I fix a listing flagged for a product condition complaint?

- Identify the issue by reviewing customer complaints and Account Health notifications.
- Inspect inventory to ensure proper packaging, seals, and product condition.
- Update product listings by removing any misleading condition descriptions.
- Improve packaging with protective materials like bubble wrap, scratch-resistant paper, and outer cartons.
- Appeal the restriction by submitting an updated Plan of Action via Seller Central.

5. What should I include in my appeal for reinstatement?

- A clear acknowledgment of the issue
- Steps taken to fix existing inventory
- Preventive actions implemented to avoid future complaints
- Proof of compliance with Amazon’s item condition policies

6. How do I handle complaints about missing or broken seals?

- Check all incoming inventory for seal defects before shipping.
- Use tamper-proof packaging to prevent seal damage during transit.
- Update FBA shipments with protective wrapping to avoid damage.

7. How can I prevent future product condition complaints?

- Implement strict quality control checks for inbound inventory.
- Train warehouse staff on proper packaging procedures.
- Use protective materials like bubble wrap, outer cartons, and scratch-resistant paper.
- Apply fragile stickers to signal careful handling during transit.
- Conduct regular audits to ensure compliance with Amazon’s policies.

8. What should I do if my product keeps getting flagged despite improvements?

- Open a BIN CHECK request with Amazon to inspect FBA inventory.
- Request damaged items be returned and repackage them correctly.
- Review shipping carriers and warehouse handling to identify weak points.

9. Can I relist an item if it was flagged for being used or repackaged?

Yes, but only if:

- The listing is updated to reflect accurate condition.
- The packaging and product appearance meet Amazon’s item condition policies.
- No prohibited claims or misleading descriptions remain.

10. Where can I find Amazon’s guidelines on product condition policies?

Refer to Amazon’s Item Condition Guidelines in Seller Central for details on acceptable product conditions and packaging requirements.
