# Catalog SOP: Creating Seller Fulfilled Prime SKUs for Your ASINs

## **Creating Seller-Fulfilled Prime SKUs for Your ASINs**

**Who is this for?** This is for Amazon sellers who want to offer Prime-eligible listings for their products while fulfilling the orders from their own warehouses or third-party logistics providers. It is also relevant for sellers who want to expand their customer base beyond the areas served by Amazon's fulfillment centers.

**Objective:** To offer Prime-eligible listings to Amazon customers while maintaining control over the fulfillment process. With SFP, you are able to fulfill Prime orders from your own warehouse or any other third-party logistics provider.

**Instructions:**

1. Go to the "Inventory" on the top menu of Seller Central and select "Manage Inventory" from the options.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-UB1598G0.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/bSiimage.png)

2. Copy the ASIN that you need to re-list or need an FBM SKU creation.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712241860115.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/K2cimage.png)

3. Go to the "Catalog" on the top menu of Seller Central and select "Add Products" from the options.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Creating%20Seller-Fulfilled%20Prime%20SKUs%20for%20Your%20ASINs-IMAGE3.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/QLNimage.png)

4. Paste the ASIN in the product search box and click enter.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712241870480.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/wvyimage.png)

5. Your existing listing will show from the result.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712241876553.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/o6pimage.png)

6. To the left of the product, select "**New**" from the dropdown menu for condition.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712241883624.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/goqimage.png)

7. Click the "Sell this product" button.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712241889845.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/D33image.png)

8. On the "Offer" tab of the next page, set the SKU to be different from the current FBA SKU, such as by adding FBM" before or after the standard SKU to indicate it's Merchant or Seller Fulfilled.

9. Enter your price in the relevant text field for the "Standard Price".

10. Set the item quantity to at least 1.

11. Leave the condition as "New".

12. Choose "I will ship this item myself (Merchant Fulfilled)" under the "Fulfillment Channel" section.

13. Click the "Save and Finish" button at the bottom of the page.

**Sample of filled-out Offer Tab**

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Creating%20Seller-Fulfilled%20Prime%20SKUs%20for%20Your%20ASINs-IMAGE8.png)![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/UoZimage.png)

14. You will receive a notice that your request is being processed.

15. Once your new listing is processed, it should appear at the top of the table of SKUs on the "Manage Inventory" page.

FAQs

**1. What does "FBM SKU Creation" mean, and how does it relate to Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP)?**

**Answer:**"FBM SKU Creation" refers to creating a new Merchant Fulfilled SKU (FBM = Fulfilled by Merchant), which is necessary for fulfilling orders from your own warehouse or a third-party logistics provider (3PL). When you create an FBM SKU for a product, you are taking over the fulfillment process from Amazon’s warehouses (FBA), and you will be responsible for shipping the product directly to the customer.

For Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP), you need to create a separate FBM SKU for your ASIN to indicate that you will fulfill the order from your warehouse while still offering Prime-eligible shipping. This separate SKU allows you to maintain control over fulfillment while adhering to Amazon’s Prime requirements.

**2. Do I need Amazon's approval before my ASIN can be eligible for Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP)?**

**Answer:**Yes, before your ASIN can become eligible for Seller Fulfilled Prime, Amazon must approve your seller account for participation in the Seller Fulfilled Prime program. This involves meeting certain performance standards, such as on-time shipment rates and delivery expectations.

After you create your new FBM SKU, you will need to ensure that you’ve been granted SFP eligibility. If your account is approved, the newly created FBM SKU will then be eligible for Prime shipping as part of Seller Fulfilled Prime. Amazon will notify you if any additional actions are required to activate Prime shipping for your listings.

**3. Will creating a new FBM SKU affect my inventory tracking or cause conflicts with my FBA SKU?**

**Answer:**Creating a new FBM SKU for Seller Fulfilled Prime will not affect your FBA inventory or create conflicts with your existing FBA SKU. The FBM SKU is a separate listing that you will manage independently of your FBA inventory. It’s important to ensure that the SKU names are distinct to avoid confusion.

For example, adding "FBM" to the SKU name (e.g., "ProductName-FBM") helps you differentiate between your FBA and FBM versions of the same product. This allows you to maintain separate inventory tracking for each fulfillment method while selling the same product on Amazon.

**4. Are there any specific product eligibility requirements for Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP)?**

**Answer:**Yes, not all products are eligible for Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP). To be eligible, your product must:

- Be able to be shipped using Prime-eligible carriers with guaranteed delivery times.
- Meet Amazon’s SFP performance standards, including on-time shipment and delivery rates.
- Be eligible for Prime delivery in your area, which includes offering Two-Day Delivery and, in some cases, One-Day or Same-Day Delivery.
- To verify if your product can be offered through Seller Fulfilled Prime, check the eligibility of your product in your Seller Central account under the SFP section. Some categories or products may have additional restrictions or requirements.

**5. What are the shipping and handling requirements for Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP) orders?**

**Answer:**When fulfilling orders through Seller Fulfilled Prime, you must meet Amazon’s strict shipping and handling requirements, which include:

- On-time shipment: Your orders must be shipped within the promised time frame, typically within 24 hours or less.
- Tracking updates: You must provide accurate tracking information for each order and update it promptly.
- Prime Delivery: You must offer Free Two-Day Shipping to nearly all U.S. addresses (including P.O. Boxes and certain U.S. territories) and may also need to offer One-Day or Same-Day Delivery depending on the location.
- Failure to meet these requirements could result in suspension from the program, so it’s crucial to maintain reliable and fast shipping practices.

**6. What should I do if my new Seller Fulfilled Prime SKU doesn’t show up after processing?**

**Answer:**If your new Seller Fulfilled Prime SKU doesn’t show up on the Manage Inventory page after processing, here are the steps you should take:

- Check for listing errors: Sometimes there may be issues with how the SKU was created (e.g., incorrect product details or incomplete fields). Go back to the "Manage Inventory" section to see if any issues are flagged.
- Wait for processing time: It can take up to 24 hours for your new FBM SKU to appear on the Manage Inventory page. Try refreshing the page or checking back later.
- Contact Seller Support: If the SKU still doesn’t appear after 24 hours, reach out to Amazon Seller Support for assistance. They can help troubleshoot and resolve any issues with your listing.
