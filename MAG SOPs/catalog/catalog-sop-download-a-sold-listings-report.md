# Catalog SOP: Download a Sold Listings Report

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-download-a-sold-listings-report

## **Download a Sold Listings Report**

**Who is this for :** An Amazon seller may request a Sold Listings Report in order to gather insights into the performance of their products on the platform.

**Objective :** To obtain detailed information about the products that have sold, including the quantity sold, the selling price, and the date and time of each sale.

**Instructions:**

1. Access the “Report” tab from the top menu of Seller Central and select “Inventory Reports” from the selection Menu.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/NSAimage.png)

2. On the next page, select “Sold Listings Report” from the “Select Report Type” Drop-Down Menu.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/VIAimage.png)

3. From the same menu, choose the number of days for your report: 7, 15, 30, or 60 days.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/ALRimage.png)

4. Click the “Request Report” button.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/k35image.png)

5. The report will appear in the table below, with a “Request Submitted” status. It usually takes only a minute or two to have the report ready.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/jb9image.png)

6. Once the report is ready for download, the status will change to “Ready,” and a “Download” link will appear. Click the “Download” link to download the report.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Ypnimage.png)

7. The report will be available in text format.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/PCCimage.png)

8. It is advisable to open the report via Excel for easy audit. Sample of Sold Listings Report below is in Excel format.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/0ibimage.png)

FAQs

1. How do I access the "Sold Listings Report" in Seller Central?

To access the report, navigate to the "Report" tab at the top of Seller Central, then select "Inventory Reports" from the dropdown menu.

2. What do I need to select to generate the Sold Listings Report?

After selecting "Inventory Reports," choose "Sold Listings Report" from the "Select Report Type" dropdown menu. Then, choose the number of days for your report, such as 7, 15, 30, or 60 days.

3. How long does it take to generate the Sold Listings Report?

The report will typically take a minute or two to generate. Once it’s ready, the status will change to "Ready," and a "Download" link will appear.

4. What file format is the Sold Listings Report downloaded in?

The report will be available in a text format, but it is recommended to open it via Excel for easier auditing and analysis.

5. Where can I find the "Download" link for the report?

Once the report is ready, the status will change to "Ready" in the table, and the "Download" link will appear. Click the link to download the report.

6. Can I filter the data for specific dates or products within the report?

The report can be generated for different time ranges (7, 15, 30, or 60 days), but you cannot apply additional filters for specific products directly through the report generation process. However, once downloaded, you can use Excel to filter the data further.

7. Can I download the Sold Listings Report for a custom date range?

Currently, the report only offers preset timeframes (7, 15, 30, or 60 days). To download a custom range, you would need to manually combine multiple reports or adjust your analysis accordingly.

8. What kind of data will the Sold Listings Report provide?

The Sold Listings Report includes details such as the product's quantity sold, the selling price, and the date and time of each sale.

9. Is there any benefit to downloading the report in Excel?

Yes, downloading the report in Excel format allows for easier sorting, filtering, and auditing of the data, which can be helpful for performance analysis and inventory management.
