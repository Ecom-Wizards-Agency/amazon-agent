## **How to Break a Parentage**

**Who is this for :** This is intended for the Account Specialists who need to break the parentage.

**Objective :** This is to ensure that all the Account Specialists know how to break the parentages properly and have a clear understanding of the steps involved in breaking the parentage and the impact it will have on the variation family.

**Overview:**Breaking the parentage is the initial step in either transferring your Child's ASINs to a different variation or constructing a new variation.

[Video Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GkOO0M-p3K4)

3. **Break Parentage by deleting the parent listing:**

- The quickest and simplest method for breaking the parentage is to delete the parent listing. This results in the complete breaking of the whole parentage, causing the child listings to become individual listings.

**NOTE:**Although the parent SKU can still be utilized after deletion, take note a new ASIN will be generated.

FAQs

**1. Why is breaking parentage necessary?**

- Breaking parentage is essential when transferring Child ASINs to a new variation family or creating individual listings from existing variations. This allows for better management of products and flexibility in variations.

**2. What information is required to request breaking parentage through Seller Support?**

- Ensure you provide the following:

  - ASIN or SKU of the parent listing.
  - A clear request to break parentage.
  - Any specific reason for breaking the parentage.
  - Providing detailed and accurate information will expedite the process.

**3. Can I recover a deleted parent listing?**

- Once a parent listing is deleted, the child listings become individual ASINs, and the original parent ASIN cannot be reused. A new parent ASIN must be created if required.

**4. How do I back up data before breaking parentage?**

- To back up data:

  - Download the Category Listings Report (CLR) from Reports > Inventory Reports.
  - Save the report in a secure location or upload it to Asana for reference.
  - This ensures you have a record of all SKUs and their details.

**5. What is the difference between manually deleting a parent listing and using a flat file?**

- Manual Deletion: Performed directly through the Inventory Manager. Suitable for quick, one-time deletions.
- Flat File Deletion: Recommended for bulk operations or when working with multiple SKUs. Requires editing the Category Listings Report and uploading the updated file.

**6. How long does it take for Amazon to process a parent listing deletion?**

- Deletion through a flat file or manual process typically takes 5–15 minutes to reflect in the backend. Always confirm in the Inventory Manager afterward.

**7. What happens to the child ASINs when a parent listing is deleted?**

- When the parent listing is deleted, the child ASINs automatically become standalone listings with no variation relationship.

**8. Can I create a new parent listing after breaking the original parentage?**

- Yes, you can create a new parent listing. However, it will have a new ASIN, and you’ll need to reassign the child ASINs manually or via a flat file.

**9. How do I ensure I’m working on the correct Amazon account before uploading a flat file?**

- Before uploading the flat file:
- Double-check that you are logged into the correct Amazon account.
- Refresh the page to ensure your changes are reflected in the correct account.

**10. What is the impact of breaking parentage on product rankings and reviews?**

- Breaking parentage does not affect individual child ASIN rankings or reviews. However, the parent ASIN and its cumulative reviews will no longer be associated with the child listings.

**11. How do I handle errors when uploading a flat file for parentage deletion?**

- If you encounter errors:

  - Check the flat file for proper formatting and field completion (e.g., SKU and "update_delete" column).
  - Verify that no unnecessary rows are included in the file.
  - Re-upload the corrected file.
  - If issues persist, contact Seller Support.

**12. Can I restore a parent-child relationship after breaking parentage?**

- Yes, but you must manually create a new parent listing and assign child ASINs to it. This will involve re-uploading a flat file or using the "Variation Wizard" in Seller Central.
