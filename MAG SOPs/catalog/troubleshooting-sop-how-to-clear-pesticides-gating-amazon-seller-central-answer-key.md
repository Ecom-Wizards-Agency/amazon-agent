## How to Clear Pesticides Gating Amazon Seller Central (Answer Key)

**Who is this for :** This is for sellers of pesticides and/or products that have pesticidal claims on Amazon.com. Objective: This is a required Training for Selling Pesticides and/or Products with Pesticidal Claims – U.S. Domestic Sellers Only. This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) will help you to find the answers to the questions to pass this Gating Test as quickly as possible.

**SUMMARY:** Pesticide gating can be a major hurdle to leap on Amazon and is responsible for many random listing Yanks. Amazon will sometimes pull a listing to make it pull a pesticide test. This Flow will help save time by helping you pass the gating test right off the bat.

**Why was the Product Gated?**

We're not sure how Amazon selects listings to force through pesticide gating. Unfortunately, there doesn't seem to be any common correlations or reasons for the gate, with most products being gated and not being remotely pesticide-related.

As such, this SOP is designed to help bypass this as quickly as possible. All you need to do is pass the gating test (more on that later in this Flow).

FAQS

1. Why was my product gated under pesticide regulations?

Amazon may automatically gate products that have pesticidal claims, even if the product isn't a pesticide. The gating can happen without clear patterns or correlations, but it is often triggered when a listing contains claims related to pest control or similar terms.

2. How do I pass the pesticide gating test?

To pass the pesticide gating test, you need to select the correct answers based on the information provided in this SOP. The answers to the questions in the gating test are provided in the answer key above. Be sure to read each question carefully and select the right options to bypass the gate.

3. What do I need to pass the pesticide gating test?

You need to provide accurate answers to the test based on the questions in the answer key provided in this SOP. Make sure to answer each question based on Amazon's requirements, especially ensuring that any product related to pest control has the necessary EPA registration.

4. What should I do if my product is wrongly gated?

If your product is gated even though it isn't a pesticide, follow the pesticide gating test and appeal process. Remove any pest-related claims from your product listing and ensure that it complies with Amazon’s policies.

5. What is the EPA Registration Number, and do I need it for my product?

An EPA Registration Number is required for products that are considered pesticides by the EPA. If your product is a pesticide or makes pesticidal claims, ensure it is registered with the EPA and include the registration number in your product listing to meet Amazon's requirements.

6. Can I still sell my product if it doesn't have an EPA registration?

If your product is not a pesticide and does not make pesticidal claims, it should not be gated. However, if it’s incorrectly flagged, you can follow the steps in the gating process and appeal the decision.

7. How often does Amazon change the pesticide gating process?

Amazon occasionally updates its pesticide gating process, so it’s important to stay informed. The answers provided here are based on the most recent test as of 9/10/2020. Check for updates regularly to ensure you have the most accurate information.

8. Can I reapply for approval if my pesticide gated product remains blocked?

Yes, if your product remains gated, follow the necessary appeal process within Amazon Seller Central. Ensure all necessary product attributes are accurate, and submit a request for approval once you’ve corrected any issues.
