# Troubleshooting SOP: How to Fix Federal Communications Commission (FCC) Yanks

## **How to Fix Federal Communications Commission (FCC) Yanks**

**Who is this for :** This is for all individuals and sellers that will receive the FCC Yanked listings and wanted to learn more about the FCC Yank reinstatement procedure.

**Objective :** This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) will help you understand and fix FCC-related issues within RF devices or products mistaken as RF devices.

**SUMMARY:**

FCC regulates all devices that emit radio frequencies (RF). These devices have the potential to interfere with authorized radio communications and must be authorized under the appropriate FCC procedure. So far, this policy only applies to the United States (US).

**Examples of devices that require FCC authorization include, but are not limited to:**

- Wi-Fi devices
- Bluetooth devices
- Mobile phones
- Electronic tablets and other devices with cellular connectivity
- Two-way radios and walkie talkies
- Automatic identification system (AIS) devices
- Broadcast transmitters
- Signal boosters

For more information, please visit the [Radio Frequency Devices article.](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/HXJGN6T5K8HGGWW)

**Yank Reason Message:**

- This product has been identified as a radio frequency (RF) device product that does not have an FCC ID entry in the fcc_radio_frequency_emission_compliance attribute.

**How to Fix?**

- **Devices that Emit RF**
- **Has FCC ID**

  - Go to Inventory > Manage Inventory > Look for the Yank ASIN > Edit > Compliance > Update Radio Frequency Emission & Authorization Status to has_fcc_id > Key in the FCC ID under FCC ID.
- **Has no FCC ID**

  - Ask the seller to enroll the device to FCC to get an ID.

**Follow the following steps:**

If the Listing Still Remained Yanked.

- Download a category listing report (CLR) and removed all RF claims from the product description (Title, description, and bullet points), and search terms/keywords. Upload the updated CLR. Remember: Take note of all the removed RF claims and get the feed file batch ID for proof that you took action.
- Get the product manual/instructions images and/or product packaging images. This is to show that the product does not emit RF.
- Go to the same case ID and respond to the following:

Greetings Amazon,

We’re sorry that you retained the violation/restriction on ASIN B0xx.

Please send this to the internal team for further review and reinstatement.

FAQs

**1. How do I enroll my product to receive an FCC ID?**

- To enroll your product for an FCC ID, you’ll need to have it tested by a certified laboratory that specializes in radio frequency emissions. The lab will provide the test results necessary for obtaining an FCC ID. Once you have the ID, you can update your product’s compliance information in Seller Central by entering the FCC ID in the Compliance section under the appropriate ASIN. For a step-by-step guide on finding an accredited lab, refer to the FCC’s official website or contact Amazon Seller Support for additional guidance.

**2. What should I do if I’m unsure whether my product is emitting RF energy?**

- If you're uncertain whether your product emits RF energy, consider the function of your product. Devices such as Wi-Fi routers, Bluetooth-enabled items, and mobile phones emit RF energy. If your product uses wireless communication or signal transmission, it is likely an RF-emitting device. If you still have doubts, you can consult an FCC-certified laboratory for a proper assessment. If your product does not emit RF energy, you can update its compliance status to not_capable_emitting_rf_energy.

**3. How long does it take for Amazon to process the reinstatement request?**

- The reinstatement process can take anywhere from 3 to 14 business days depending on the complexity of the case and whether Amazon needs additional documentation. Once you submit your appeal, you’ll receive updates through the Case ID. If you don’t hear back within the expected timeframe, it’s important to follow up with Amazon Seller Support, providing the Case ID for reference. Be patient, but proactive in ensuring your issue is resolved.

**4. What if my device doesn't qualify for an FCC ID or I can't get one?**

- If your product does not qualify for an FCC ID (e.g., it is not an RF-emitting device), you will need to update the product's compliance status to not_capable_emitting_rf_energy. However, if Amazon continues to flag the product, you should reach out to Seller Support with further clarification and provide documentation that proves the product is non-RF-emitting, such as product manuals or expert statements. If the issue persists, you may need to escalate the case.

**5. What should I do if I encounter technical issues while updating compliance information?**

- If you face technical difficulties during the update process—whether it’s uploading compliance documents, updating FCC ID details, or editing the product listing—try the following steps:

  - Refresh your browser and clear your cache to resolve minor glitches.
  - Check file sizes and formats to ensure they comply with Amazon’s upload requirements.
  - Use a different browser to eliminate browser-specific issues.
  - If the issue persists, contact Amazon Seller Support for assistance, providing details of the error message or technical problem.

**6. What exactly qualifies as an "RF claim" that I need to remove from my listing?**

- "RF claims" refer to any statements or references in your product’s title, description, bullet points, or search terms that imply the product emits or uses RF energy. These claims can include phrases such as:

  - "Wi-Fi-enabled"
  - "Bluetooth"
  - "wireless"
  - "RF communication"
  - "4G/5G cellular"
  - "signals transmitted"
- Be sure to thoroughly review your product listing and remove any reference to RF capabilities unless it’s explicitly required for the product's functionality. After removing these claims, update your inventory and include the batch ID from your feed file upload to show you’ve made the necessary corrections.

**7. What should I do if the violation or restriction on my product is not lifted after I’ve removed all RF claims?**

- If the violation remains even after removing all RF claims from your listing, gather additional proof of the product’s non-RF capabilities. This can include:

  - A product manual or instructions showing that it does not emit RF.
  - Images of the product packaging or product itself, clearly indicating that it does not contain any RF-emitting components.
  - Submit this documentation along with the batch ID and a detailed explanation of the changes you’ve made. If the issue still persists, you may need to escalate the case with Amazon Seller Support by providing the Case ID and all supporting materials.
