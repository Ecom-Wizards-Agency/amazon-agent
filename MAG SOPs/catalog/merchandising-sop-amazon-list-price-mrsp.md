## **Amazon List Price MRSP**

**Who is this for?** This is intended for Amazon Sellers who need to configure the Amazon list price on a listing.

**Objective:** To provide step-by-step instructions for setting up the Amazon list price for a listing, ensuring that they have the necessary knowledge and skills to perform the task accurately and efficiently.

**OVERVIEW**

**What is a List Price?**

List Price, MSRP, or Suggested Retail Price is completely fabricated price data and has no bearing on the product. The manufacturer sets it up to give the illusion that the product is on sale when it's actually not, to make consumers think there’s additional value.

**Instructions: HOW TO SETUP LIST PRICE MANUALLY**

1: Hover your mouse to Inventory > Manage All Inventory

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/xz4image.png)

2: Go to the Product Edit Page as shown below:

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/zJ7image.png)

3: Go to the Offer Tab then place your List Price

**IMPORTANT REMINDERS:**

- If we place the List Price, it’s good to put it 20% to 35% higher than the actual price of the item, or the previous highest price that the item has sold for at a price that we previously sold at. This increases the chance of the List Price displaying on the page.
- Make sure that you will not set your actual price higher than your list price to avoid future buy-box losses.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/mz3image.png)

Additional details on about List Price: Provide the list price for the product not including tax. The List Price is the suggested retail price of a product as provided by a manufacturer, supplier, or seller. This is not the offering or cost price. 4: Click Save and Finish once you confirmed the details.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/FpRimage.png)

5: Click Done and wait for 15 minutes after each change before checking again to give the system plenty of time to apply the changes.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/i10image.png)

**FAQ**

**What if it did not show up at the price you set it up at?**

- Sometimes strikethrough does not display on PDP if there has been no sale in the last 30 days.
- Lower it down a dollar for each adjustment until it shows up.
- Make sure you are giving it time to properly load on the system before you make any new changes.
- Wait 15 minutes after each change before checking again to give it plenty of time to apply the changes.

**Tips on how to find the List Price if not shown under the Offer Tab:**

- Some categories may show List Price under the More Details section
- Do a CTRL+F (Windows) / CMD+F (Apple)
- You can search for MSRP / Suggested / List

FAQs

1. What is the List Price (MSRP), and why is it used?

The **List Price (MSRP)** is a suggested retail price set by the manufacturer or supplier. It is often used to create the illusion of a discount on Amazon's product detail page (PDP), even if the product is not actually on sale. This price can help attract customers by showcasing potential savings.

2. How do I ensure my List Price appears on the product detail page?

To increase the chance of your List Price appearing with a strikethrough, set the price **20-35% higher** than your actual selling price or previous highest price. However, make sure that your actual price remains lower than the List Price to avoid losing the Buy Box.

3. Why doesn't the List Price show up on my product detail page (PDP)?

The List Price may not appear if:

- There hasn't been a sale in the last **30 days**.
- The List Price is set too close to your current selling price.
- The system hasn't had enough time to process the change (wait **15 minutes** after changes).
  If it still doesn't appear, try lowering your List Price by **$1** and check again.

4. How long does it take for the List Price to appear on the PDP?

It typically takes up to **15 minutes** for changes to reflect on your listing. After that, check to see if the List Price has appeared.

5. Can I use the List Price if it's not shown in the Offer Tab?

If the List Price is not visible under the **Offer Tab**, try looking in the **More Details** section on the product page. You can also use **CTRL+F (Windows)** or **CMD+F (Mac)** to search for terms like "MSRP," "Suggested," or "List Price."

6. How do I set up List Price in bulk?

To set List Price in bulk:

1. Go to **Catalog > Add Products via Upload**.
2. Download the **Inventory file** template.
3. Fill in the necessary fields, including **List Price** for each SKU.
4. Upload the updated file to **Seller Central** under **Add Products via Upload**.
5. Review your **processing report** to confirm successful updates.

7. Can I set a different List Price for different marketplaces?

Yes, you can set a unique List Price for each marketplace. Make sure to select the **correct marketplace** when uploading your inventory file, as prices can vary by region.

8. Why is my List Price not being applied in the bulk upload?

If your List Price is not showing up after uploading the bulk file, ensure:

- You’re using the **correct category path** and attributes.
- The file has been uploaded properly and **refresh the page** before checking.
  If the issue persists, check the **processing report** for errors and fix them accordingly.

9. How can I verify that my List Price is showing up after I upload the file?

Once you've uploaded the file and the changes have been processed, go to the product listing on the **Amazon detail page (PDP)**. You should see the **List Price** displayed along with the product details.

10. What should I do if my List Price shows a strikethrough but the product isn't actually on sale?

If your List Price shows a strikethrough but you’re not offering a sale, ensure that the **actual selling price** is **lower** than the List Price and remains competitive. Amazon may automatically display the **discounted price** if it sees a significant difference between the List Price and your selling price.

11. **What if it did not show up at the price you set it up at?**

- Lower it down a dollar for each adjustment until it shows up.
- Make sure you are giving it time to properly load on the system before you make any new changes.
- Wait 15 minutes after each change before checking again to give it plenty of time to apply the changes.
