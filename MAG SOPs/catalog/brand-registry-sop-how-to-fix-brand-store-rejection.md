# Brand Registry SOP: How to Fix Brand Store Rejection

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/brand-registry-sop-how-to-fix-brand-store-rejection

## How to Fix Brand Store Rejection

**Who is this for :**

- For creatives that get brand store rejections.
- To all Amazon Specialists (AS) and Brand Managers (BM) that wanted to learn how to handle brand store rejections and how to clear them.

**Objective:**This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) will help you understand how to check the rejection, and how to correct the rejection reasons.

All brand stores need to comply with[Store's Content Guidelines and Acceptance Policies](https://advertising.amazon.com/en-us/resources/ad-policy/stores#claims) to get approved or else submissions will get rejected.

Prerequisites:

- The brand should be properly registered and set in the Brand Registry.
- All of the items in a brand store should be under the same brand name.
- All of the ASINs should comply with all Amazon policies and remain healthy/active

What’s the difference between A+ Content and Brand Store rejection messages?

The A+ rejection message will tell you to know what words or images to remove while the brand store rejection reasons don’t give the exact word or exact reason for content and/or image rejection most of the time.

How to Fix: Troubleshooting Steps

I. Identifying the Issue

1. Go to Stores > Manage Stores in the Seller Central categories found on the top left corner and select the brand store that received the rejection message by clicking Edit Store.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-11%20at%205.37.52%E2%80%AFAM.png)

2. Check the rejection reason box on the top middle part of the Edit Store page. Click the Edit link and it’ll redirect you to the page and/or section that has the policy violation.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-02-11%20at%205.38.00%E2%80%AFAM.png)

3. Once you’re in the correct section, you’ll see another rejection reason box. To understand what’s causing the issue, click the See Policy link.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/WRBimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/WRBimage.png)

4. The policy link will direct you to the brand store policy/guideline that was violated. Read and comprehend the inclusion of the compliance section. If you agree that there are violations in the content or in the image, please continue the troubleshooting steps. If you don’t agree with the decision, jump to How to File a Case for Brand Store Rejection.

II. Corrective Actions

1. If the rejection reason is present in the meta description, remove all of the prohibited claims or replace them.
2. If the rejection reason is present in the crawlable text or on the image, ask the creative team to remove the prohibited claims and/or edit the image for it to be compliant with the policy.
3. Resubmit the brand store for another review. If it gets approved, congratulations! If the brand store gets another rejection reason, loop back to I. Identifying the Issue. FAQS

1. Why was my Brand Store rejected?

Your Brand Store may be rejected due to non-compliance with Amazon’s Stores Content Guidelines and Acceptance Policies. Some common reasons include use of prohibited claims in text or images, Brand Store ASINs not matching the registered brand, presence of inactive or non-compliant ASINs, or violation of Amazon's image or content policies.

2. How can I check why my Brand Store was rejected?

Go to Stores > Manage Stores in Seller Central. Click Edit Store for the rejected store. Look at the Rejection Reason Box at the top of the page. Click See Policy to read the specific guideline that was violated.

3. How is a Brand Store rejection different from an A+ Content rejection?

A+ Content rejection provides specific words or images to remove. Brand Store rejection usually does not specify exact content issues. Instead, it provides a general policy link, requiring manual review.

4. How do I fix a rejection related to meta descriptions?

Remove any prohibited claims in the meta description. Ensure that descriptions follow Amazon’s compliance policies and do not contain misleading or promotional content.

5. What should I do if an image in my Brand Store is rejected?

Ask the creative team to remove prohibited claims or modify the image to be compliant. Ensure that the image resolution meets Amazon’s guidelines. Avoid using logos, trademarks, or elements that violate Amazon policies.

6. Can I appeal a Brand Store rejection?

Yes! If you believe your Brand Store complies with Amazon’s policies, follow these steps: Click the question mark icon in the Edit Store page. Go to Support Center > Stores > Store rejected > Email. Submit an appeal with the Brand Store name, rejection reason, and supporting details.

7. How do I monitor my Brand Store appeal?

After submitting an appeal, collect the Case ID and its link. Check for updates in Seller Central > Case Log. If Amazon responds with further concerns, make the necessary edits and resubmit the store.

8. What if my Brand Store is still rejected after resubmitting?

If your Brand Store remains rejected despite compliance, double-check the rejection policy details. If you’re confident there is no violation, escalate the case by replying in the case log. Submit a new ticket if necessary, reinforcing that the store meets all policy requirements.

9. How long does Amazon take to approve a Brand Store after resubmission?

Typically, Amazon reviews Brand Store submissions within 3-7 business days. However, high review volumes may cause delays.

10. Can I prevent Brand Store rejections in the future?

Yes! Follow Amazon’s Brand Store content guidelines closely. Ensure all ASINs belong to the registered brand. Use high-quality images and avoid policy-violating content. Review rejection reasons from past submissions to avoid similar issues.
