## **Children's Product Safety Request Or Children’s Product Certificate (CPC)**

**Who is this for :** This is for all individuals and sellers that sell consumer products designed or intended primarily for children 12 years of age or younger.

**Objective :** This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) will help you understand and fix Children's product safety requests in order to approve your products to sell on Amazon.

Amazon will ask the sellers to provide supporting documents that their products are safe to play with if they identify their products as “toys” and for “kids” or “children”. Another instance is if the listing identifies its age group as **12 years of age or younger.**

**SUMMARY:** Certification means the issuance of a written Children's Product Certificate (CPC) in which the manufacturer or importer certifies that its children's product complies with all applicable children's product safety rules (or similar rules, bans, standards, or regulations under any law enforced by the Commission for that product)

Children's products are subject to a set of federal safety rules, called children's product safety rules. Determining how your product is classified enables you to correctly identify the set of children’s product safety rules applicable to your product. The law defines a "children's product" as a consumer product designed or intended primarily for children **12 years of age or younger.**

**Why do we need this?** Amazon will ask the sellers to provide supporting documents that their products are safe to play with if they identify their products as “toys” and for “kids” or “children”. Another instance is if the listing identifies its age group as 12 years of age or younger.

**What if the Listing is not a Toy?**

Actions needed:

- Investigate the description of the listing and remove all indications of “kids”, “toys”, “children”, and age group of “12 years old or younger”
- You can also add in the description “for 12 years old and up”.
- Go to Performance > Account Health > Manage your compliance > Look for the ASIN > Appeal Request

FAQs

**1. What products require a Children's Product Certificate (CPC)?**

- Any product designed or marketed for children 12 years of age or younger requires a Children’s Product Certificate (CPC). This includes toys, apparel, furniture, accessories, and school supplies marketed toward children.

**2. What if my product is misclassified as a children's product?**

- If your product is not intended for children 12 and under but was flagged by Amazon, you should:

  - Review your listing and remove terms like “kids,” “children,” “toys,” or any age references.
  - Submit an appeal via Performance > Account Health > Manage Compliance > Appeal Request and explain that the product is for ages 12 and up.
  - Update the CPSIA Warning Attribute to reflect "NoWarningApplicable" if applicable.

**3. Where can I find a CPSC-accepted testing lab?**

- You can find a list of CPSC-accepted labs on the Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) website:

  - CPSC-Accepted Labs

**4. How long does it take Amazon to review compliance documents?**

- Amazon typically reviews CPC and test reports within 5–10 business days, but it may take longer during peak times. You can track your submission status by checking the case log under Performance > Account Health.

**5. Why was my CPC or test report rejected?**

- Common reasons for rejection include:

  - Missing required details (e.g., test lab info, product description, or applicable safety standards).
  - Test reports not issued by a CPSC-accepted lab.
  - Old or expired test results (test reports must be current).
  - If rejected, review the feedback, correct the issue, and resubmit your documents.

**6. Do I need a separate CPC for every product variation?**

- Not always. If your variations only differ in color, design, or size but are made of the same materials and tested under the same conditions, one CPC may cover all variations. However, if different variations require separate safety testing, each will need its own CPC.

**7. What is the CPSIA Warning Attribute, and why do I need to update it?**

- The CPSIA Warning Attribute informs buyers about potential safety hazards (e.g., small parts, choking hazards). Keeping this updated is crucial because:

  - Incorrect or missing warnings can cause listing suppression.
  - It ensures compliance with Amazon’s safety requirements.
  - If you can’t update it via Manage Inventory, use a feed file update.

**8. Can I sell my product while waiting for CPC approval?**

- No, if Amazon has flagged your listing for CPC compliance, your product may be suppressed or restricted until the CPC and test reports are approved. To prevent interruptions:

  - Submit your documents as soon as possible.
  - Monitor your case log for updates.
  - If you believe your product does not require a CPC, submit an appeal request.
