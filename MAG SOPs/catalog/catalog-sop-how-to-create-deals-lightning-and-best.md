## **How to Create Deals (Lightning and Best)**

**Who is this for :** This is for All individuals who want to create Deals (Lightning and Best).

**Objective :**To ensure that all are able to create Deals (Lightning and Best).

A Lightning Deal is a time-bound, promotional offer where an item is featured for a limited number of hours, usually 4 to 12 hours (as determined by Amazon), on the Amazon Deals page. Featuring an item as a Lightning Deal may help increase sales and reduce your inventory.

A Best Deal (formerly known as "7-day deal") is a time-bound, promotional offer where an item is featured for a limited number of days on the Amazon Deals page.

To be eligible for deals, you must be a professional seller with an overall rating of at least 4.0 stars.

**Guidelines for Creating Amazon Deals**

- Have a sales history in Amazon stores and at least a 4-star rating.
- Include as many variations as possible.
- Not be a restricted product or offensive, embarrassing or inappropriate product.
- Be Prime eligible in all regions.
- New condition.
- Be compliant with customer product review policies.
- Compliant with pricing policies and have a valid reference price.
- Compliant with deal frequency requirement. To learn more, go to Create a Deal.

**Before implementing proposed deals, it is important to communicate, and get client’s approval. Include the approval proof (screenshot or fireflies call link) as Task Details or as a Comment.**

FAQs

**1. How does Amazon calculate the 4-star rating requirement for eligibility?**

- Amazon uses the seller's overall rating, which is based on customer feedback, order defect rate, and performance metrics. Individual product ratings do not necessarily affect eligibility, but consistently low-rated products may not qualify for deals.

**2. Do all product variations need to meet the eligibility criteria?**

- No, but variations that do not meet the pricing policy, Prime eligibility, or review rating requirements may be excluded from the deal. Including all eligible variations helps maximize deal exposure.

**3. How can I check the deal fee before submitting a deal?**

- The deal fee is automatically determined by Amazon based on product category, pricing history, and estimated traffic. You can view the fee:

  - During the deal creation process before submission.
  - By navigating to Advertising > Deals and selecting an eligible product.

**4. How do I ensure my deal meets Amazon's pricing policies?**

- Amazon requires that:

  - The deal price is lower than the recent lowest price.
  - The reference price is valid, meaning it has been offered for a reasonable period before the deal.
  - The price is not higher than the maximum allowed price set by Amazon.
  - To verify compliance, compare your deal price with the last 30-day average selling price before submission.

**5. What should I do if my deal is rejected?**

- If Amazon rejects your deal:

  - Check the rejection reason in the Deal Dashboard.
  - Adjust pricing, variations, or inventory levels accordingly.
  - Ensure the product is Prime-eligible and follows all review policies.
  - Resubmit the deal after making corrections.

**6. Can I modify a deal after submission?**

- No, once a deal is submitted, you cannot make changes to pricing, inventory, or scheduling. If necessary, you must cancel the deal and submit a new one.

**7. How can I cancel a deal after submission?**

- To cancel a deal:

  - Go to Advertising > Deals in Seller Central.
  - Locate the active or scheduled deal.
  - Click Cancel Deal before it goes live.
  - Once the deal is live, it cannot be canceled, and committed inventory must be honored.

**8. Are there different requirements for Prime Day, Black Friday, or Cyber Monday deals?**

- Yes, Amazon may have additional criteria for high-traffic events, such as:

  - Stricter pricing policies to ensure deep discounts.
  - Higher minimum committed inventory.
  - A higher deal fee due to increased visibility.
  - Amazon will notify you if your product is eligible for these events during deal creation.
