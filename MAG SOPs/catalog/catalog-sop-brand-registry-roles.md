## **Brand Registry Roles**

**Who is this for :** For brand owners, administrators, or authorized employees who are enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry and want to assign or remove roles for a user account associated with their brand.

**Objective :** To provide instructions on the roles available in Amazon Brand Registry and how to assign or remove roles for a user account associated with a brand.

Amazon Brand Registry assigns the roles of Rights Owner and Administrator to a brand once it is enrolled. These roles allow users to manage the brand and assign different levels of access to Brand Registry tools.

**There are three roles that can be assigned to user accounts associated with a brand:**

- **Rights Owner**

  - This role is given to an individual who is the trademark owner or an authorized employee. Users with this role have access to the Report a Violation tool and listing benefits.
- **Registered Agent**

  - This role is given to a third party who is authorized by the Rights Owner to report violations using Brand Registry tools.
- **Administrator**

  - This role is given to an individual who has permission to assign roles to user accounts.

Only the Rights Owner and Administrator can assign roles. The person who submitted the brand application is automatically assigned these roles.

To assign a new role, the individual must create a Brand Registry user account and accept the terms. If the user already has a Seller or Vendor account with Amazon, they can use the same credentials to create a Brand Registry user account. The Administrator for the brand can then access the Contact Us page within Brand Registry and select Update your brand profile, then Update role for a user account. The required information to assign a new role is the email address login of the Brand Registry user account, the brand that the request pertains to, and the specific role or roles to assign.

An individual can have multiple roles and be associated with multiple brands in their Brand Registry user account. There is no limit to how many user accounts can be associated with a brand.

To check the roles assigned to a user account and view who is associated with a brand, individuals can submit a case through the Contact Us page and a Brand Registry Support Associate will provide the necessary information.

FAQs

1. Who can assign roles in Amazon Brand Registry?

Only the Rights Owner and Administrator can assign roles to user accounts associated with a brand.

2. How do I assign a new role to a user in Brand Registry?

- Ensure the user has created a Brand Registry user account.
- The Administrator logs into Brand Registry and navigates to Contact Us.
- Select Update your brand profile → Update role for a user account.
- Provide the user’s email address, the brand name, and the role(s) to be assigned.

3. Can a user have multiple roles?

Yes, a user can have multiple roles and be associated with multiple brands in their Brand Registry account.

4. What are the differences between the Rights Owner, Registered Agent, and Administrator roles?

- Rights Owner: Trademark owner or authorized employee with access to Brand Registry tools.
- Registered Agent: A third party authorized by the Rights Owner to report violations.
- Administrator: Manages user roles and brand settings.

5. How do I remove a user from my brand?

- The Administrator logs into Brand Registry.
- Go to Contact Us → Update your brand profile → Remove a user from your brand.
- Provide the necessary details to remove the user.

6. What happens when a user is removed from a brand?

All previously assigned roles for that brand will be removed from the user’s Brand Registry account.

7. Can I use my existing Seller or Vendor Central account for Brand Registry?

Yes, you can use the same login credentials to create a Brand Registry user account.

8. How can I check the roles assigned to my Brand Registry account?

Submit a case through Contact Us in Brand Registry, and a Brand Registry Support Associate will provide the role details.

9. Is there a limit to how many users can be associated with a brand?

No, there is no limit to the number of user accounts that can be associated with a brand.
