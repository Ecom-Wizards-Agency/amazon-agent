# Troubleshooting SOP: Stranded Inventory (Inventory Error)

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/troubleshooting-sop-stranded-inventory-inventory-error

## Stranded Inventory (Inventory Error)

**Who is this for :** This SOP is for those who are experiencing issues with stranded inventory within their FBA inventory. It is intended for those who need to troubleshoot and resolve an inventory error to ensure all products are active and available for purchase by customers.

**Objective :** The objective of this SOP is to locate and resolve stranded inventory errors by identifying the reason for the issue, relisting the inventory, and uploading an updated category listings report to fix the ASIN connection. The ultimate goal is to ensure all inventory is active and available for sale, with any stranded inventory issues resolved in a timely and efficient manner.

> **Inventory Overview Column Update**
>
> **What's changing:**
>
> •The "Available" category has been replaced by "On-hand", which includes both Available and FC transfer units. These units are considered customer-purchasable.
>
> •As a result, FC transfer units have been removed from the "Reserved" category. Reserved now reflects only FC processing and Customer order units.
>
> These changes apply to all FBA sellers in the US store. No action is required on your part.
>
> **Please note:**
>
> •These updates will be fully reflected in FBA reports by mid-2026.
>
> •Until then, inventory reports may differ from the new Inventory Overview column, as they will continue to classify FC transfer units as Reserved.
>
> We understand this temporary discrepancy may cause some confusion and appreciate your patience as the transition is completed across all systems.
>
> **For more details on managing your FBA inventory, please visit the Inventory Performance page.** [**https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/G202174810**](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/G202174810)

1. From the Seller Central home page, click the 3 line bar on the left side and select "Inventory." Then, proceed to FBA Inventory.

1. Hover your mouse over "Inventory" and then select "Stranded Inventory."

1. Identify the Stranded Reason.

- On the Stranded Inventory page, you'll find a list of your stranded inventory items along with the reasons Amazon has flagged them. Hover over the specific issue to get a detailed explanation of the problem. Identify which listings have an inactive offer and prepare to address the issue.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-31%20at%2012.15.32%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/fiQimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/fiQimage.png)

1. Resolve Amazon listing error

- If your product isn't connected to any ASIN, it's considered a listing error. This can occur if your listing was deleted or never created. To relist your inventory, go to the Stranded Inventory tab, locate the last column of the problematic listing, and select the option "Refresh stranded reason". Wait for a few minutes and repeat this process multiple times.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-31%20at%2012.15.38%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/CCximage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/CCximage.png)
- If the listing is fixed, it will be removed from the Stranded Inventory, and the ASIN should show as "Active" in your inventory.
- If the issue persists, download the Category Listings Report and conduct a partial update of the ASIN. If the partial update does not work, seek approval from the BM and proceed with a full update.

1. Download the Category Listings Report

- Go to 'Reports' and then select 'Inventory Reports' from the menu.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-31%20at%2012.15.45%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Tm9image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/Tm9image.png)
- To generate the necessary report, click on the "Select Report type" dropdown menu and select the Category Listing Report. After selecting the desired report type, click on "Request report" to generate the report. Once the report has been generated, click on "Download" to save a copy of the report to your computer.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-31%20at%2012.15.52%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/80Simage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/80Simage.png)
- Open the downloaded file and search for the problematic listing (SKU) in the 'Seller SKU' column. Note: Ensure that you only update the problematic listing(s).
- Review all details for accuracy before proceeding with the upload.

1. Upload the update file

- Save the corrected file and upload it to Amazon by navigating to 'Catalog' and then select 'Add Products via Upload'.

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/DVLimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/DVLimage.png)

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-31%20at%2012.15.59%E2%80%AFAM.png)
- From the 'Upload your spreadsheet' tab, click on "Drag and drop file or Browse files." Select the file you want to update and then click on the "Upload file" button.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-31%20at%2012.16.06%E2%80%AFAM.png)

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/mK0image.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/mK0image.png)

- Wait for at least 15 minutes for the inventory file to be processed.
- Ensure the file is uploaded successfully and record the BATCH ID.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-31%20at%2012.16.14%E2%80%AFAM.png)

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/lKRimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/lKRimage.png)
- Return to the 'Stranded Inventory' page to confirm whether the issue is resolved.
- Try to “Refresh Stranded Reason” again. Wait another 15 minutes – if it wouldn’t disappear in stranded inventory and the listing wouldn’t change to Active on its own, contact Seller Support and ask them to Reactivate this offer and provide the batch ID.

FAQs

**1. Why does stranded inventory happen?**

- Stranded inventory occurs when products in Amazon’s fulfillment centers do not have an active, buyable listing. This can be caused by:

  - Deleted or deactivated listings
  - Suppressed listings due to policy violations
  - Category restrictions or missing approvals
  - Pricing errors that trigger the minimum or maximum price threshold
  - Amazon system errors

**2. How long does it take for stranded inventory to be resolved?**

- If the issue is fixed by refreshing the stranded reason, it may resolve within minutes to a few hours.
- If a partial update is needed via the Category Listings Report, it can take up to 24 hours.
- If Amazon intervention is required (e.g., contacting Seller Support), resolution may take several days depending on the complexity of the issue.

**3. What should I do if my SKU keeps becoming stranded repeatedly?**

- If the same SKU continues to become stranded:

  - Check for policy violations or brand restrictions that may be triggering the issue.
  - Verify if pricing rules are causing the listing to deactivate.
  - Monitor Amazon notifications for updates related to the ASIN.
  - Open a Seller Support case to investigate further.

**4. What if "Refresh Stranded Reason" doesn’t work?**

- If refreshing does not resolve the issue:

  - Check for suppressed listing issues under Manage Inventory > Fix Issues.
  - Ensure the ASIN is not flagged for intellectual property or restricted content violations.
  - Download the Category Listings Report, perform a partial update, and re-upload it.
  - If all else fails, submit a case to Amazon Seller Support with the Batch ID and request a manual reactivation.

**5. Does stranded inventory affect my IPI (Inventory Performance Index) score?**

- Yes. Stranded inventory negatively impacts your IPI score by lowering sell-through rates and increasing excess stock. Resolving stranded inventory quickly helps maintain a healthy storage utilization score.

**6. What happens if stranded inventory is not fixed?**

- If inventory remains stranded for an extended period:
- Amazon may automatically remove or dispose of it based on your FBA removal settings.
- You may be charged storage fees for unsellable units.
- It may lead to delisting of your ASIN if it violates Amazon’s policies.

**7. What should I do if stranded inventory is due to listing suppression or policy violations?**

- Check the Performance Notifications tab in Seller Central for policy violation alerts.
- If the ASIN is flagged, file an appeal through Account Health > Policy Compliance.
- If the issue is due to missing category approval, apply for approval before relisting.

**8. What if Seller Support is not helping resolve the stranded inventory issue?**

- If Amazon Support is not resolving your case:

  - Ask for escalation to the Captive Team (they have higher permissions).
  - Submit a Brand Registry ticket if the issue affects a registered brand ASIN.
  - Post in Amazon Seller Forums for additional troubleshooting insights.
  - Call Seller Support directly instead of relying on email responses.
