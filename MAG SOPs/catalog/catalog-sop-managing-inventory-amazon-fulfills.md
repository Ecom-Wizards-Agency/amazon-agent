## **Managing Inventory Amazon Fulfills**

**Who is this for :** It's important to note that while Amazon handles the fulfillment logistics, as the seller, you are responsible for managing your inventory, ensuring accuracy, and making strategic decisions to optimize your inventory levels and sales performance.

**Objective :** To leverage Amazon's fulfillment infrastructure, improve customer service through faster and more reliable order fulfillment, and streamline your operations for scalable growth.

> **Inventory Overview Column Update**
>
> **What's changing:**
>
> •The "Available" category has been replaced by "On-hand", which includes both Available and FC transfer units. These units are considered customer-purchasable.
>
> •As a result, FC transfer units have been removed from the "Reserved" category. Reserved now reflects only FC processing and Customer order units.
>
> These changes apply to all FBA sellers in the US store. No action is required on your part.
>
> **Please note:**
>
> •These updates will be fully reflected in FBA reports by mid-2026.
>
> •Until then, inventory reports may differ from the new Inventory Overview column, as they will continue to classify FC transfer units as Reserved.
>
> We understand this temporary discrepancy may cause some confusion and appreciate your patience as the transition is completed across all systems.
>

FAQs

1. What is the role of Amazon FBA in managing inventory?

Amazon FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) handles the storage, packing, and shipping of your products. However, as the seller, you are responsible for inventory management, including pricing, inventory levels, and ensuring your listings are active and available.

2. How can I access my "Inventory Amazon Fulfills" page?

To access the Inventory Amazon Fulfills page:

1. Go to Inventory > Manage All Inventory in Seller Central.
2. Click on the "Inventory Amazon Fulfills" link at the top of the page to view your FBA inventory.

3. What information can I view and adjust on the "Inventory Amazon Fulfills" page?

On this page, you can:

- Adjust pricing for your FBA items.
- Check inventory levels and availability.
- View fulfillment fees for your FBA items.
- Edit product listing information, such as resolving issues that may cause items to be unavailable, inactive, or suppressed.

4. How do I check the status of my FBA inventory?

To check the complete status of your FBA inventory, click on the total number of available inventory. A detailed table will show the current inventory status, including the quantities and fulfillment status for each SKU.

5. Can I perform bulk audits of my FBA inventory?

Yes, you can perform a bulk audit by downloading the Manage FBA Inventory Report. This report provides a comprehensive overview of your inventory, allowing you to review and manage your FBA stock efficiently.

6. How do I download the Manage FBA Inventory Report?

Follow the instructions provided in the SOP to download the Manage FBA Inventory Report. This will allow you to export a detailed report of your FBA inventory, which you can use for auditing or inventory management purposes.

7. What should I do if my FBA inventory is unavailable or suppressed?

If your FBA inventory is unavailable or suppressed, review the listing information for any issues that might be causing the problem, such as missing details, pricing discrepancies, or policy violations. You can also check if the inventory is out of stock and make any necessary adjustments in Seller Central.

8. How do I track the fulfillment fees for my FBA inventory?

You can view the fulfillment fees associated with your FBA inventory by accessing the "Inventory Amazon Fulfills" page. Fees will be listed alongside your inventory, helping you understand the cost of Amazon fulfilling each item.

9. How often should I audit my FBA inventory?

It’s advisable to regularly audit your FBA inventory, especially if you're managing large quantities or high-volume SKUs. A good practice is to conduct monthly or quarterly audits to ensure accurate stock levels and avoid issues with listings.

10. How do I address inventory issues like stockouts or delays in FBA?

If you experience stockouts or fulfillment delays, take action by restocking your inventory as soon as possible. You can also update your listings to reflect any changes in availability, and proactively manage your inventory levels to prevent future issues.
