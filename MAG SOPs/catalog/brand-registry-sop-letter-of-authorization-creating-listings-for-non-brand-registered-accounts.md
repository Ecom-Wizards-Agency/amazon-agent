# Brand Registry SOP: Letter of Authorization - Creating Listings for Non-Brand Registered Accounts

## Letter of Authorization - Creating Listings for Non-Brand Registered Accounts

**Who is this for :** Intended for Amazon sellers that are non-brand registered.

**Objective:** This document serves as a guide on how to submit a Letter of Authorization and successfully list a product without a registered brand account.

**Overview:** When creating listings with a brand name that the client is not brand registered for, the client needs to secure a Letter of Authorization from the brand owner. This letter should state that our client has been granted permission to sell these products.

**Instructions:**

Reminder: Sellers should personally do this since the application to sell under someone else’s brand should come from the admin email of the Seller Central account.

Log in to [Seller Central](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/) and go to [User Permissions](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/gp/account-manager/home.html/ref=xx_userperms_dnav_xx) to see which email account has admin privileges. The admin is the user who does not have a "Delete" button next to "Manage Permissions."

FAQS

**1. What specific details should the Letter of Authorization include?**

- The Letter of Authorization should include:

  - The brand owner's name, address, and contact information.
  - The seller's name, address, and Amazon Seller Central account details.
  - A clear statement granting permission to sell the brand’s products.
  - A list of ASINs, SKUs, or product categories covered under the authorization.
  - The brand owner's signature and company stamp (if applicable).
  - The date of issuance.

**2. Does the Letter of Authorization need to be on company letterhead?**

- Yes, it is recommended to have the letter on the brand owner’s official company letterhead to verify its authenticity. Amazon often requires professional documentation to approve such requests.

**3. Who should submit the Letter of Authorization?**

- The application must be submitted by the admin email of the seller’s Amazon account. Verify admin privileges in Settings > User Permissions before starting the process.

**4. How long does it take for Amazon to approve the application?**

- Approval times can vary but typically take 2–7 business days. Delays may occur if the documentation is incomplete, unclear, or requires additional verification.

**5. What happens if the application is denied?**

- If denied:

  - Review Amazon’s rejection message for specific reasons.
  - Double-check that the Letter of Authorization includes all required details.
  - Contact the brand owner to clarify or update the document.
  - Reapply with the corrected document or escalate the case through Seller Support.

**6. Can I use one Letter of Authorization for multiple products?**

- Yes, as long as the letter explicitly lists all the ASINs, SKUs, or product categories you are authorized to sell. Ensure the brand owner includes this information to avoid the need for multiple submissions.

**7. What should I do if the brand owner is unresponsive or unwilling to provide a Letter of Authorization?**

- Without a Letter of Authorization, you cannot create listings under the brand name. Consider:

  - Building a relationship with the brand owner to gain their trust.
  - Exploring generic or unbranded product listings (if applicable).
  - Selling other products that do not require authorization.

**8. Can I use the Letter of Authorization for restricted categories?**

- No, the Letter of Authorization is specific to the brand name. Selling in restricted categories (e.g., health, beauty) requires additional category-specific approvals from Amazon.

**9. Does the Letter of Authorization expire?**

- Amazon does not specify an expiration date, but the letter may need to be updated periodically, especially if the brand owner has included a validity period. It’s best to confirm with the brand owner if renewal is required.

**10. Can I submit a scanned copy of the Letter of Authorization?**

- Yes, a scanned copy is acceptable as long as it is clear and legible. Ensure the file format complies with Amazon’s requirements (e.g., PDF, JPEG).

**11. What should I do if the brand owner issues a blanket authorization to multiple sellers?**

- If the brand owner provides a blanket authorization, ensure the letter includes your specific seller account information to prevent potential disputes or application rejections.

**12. What steps should I take after my application is approved?**

- Once approved:

  - Create the product listing using the approved brand name.
  - Verify that the listing is active and visible on the product detail page.
  - Monitor your account to ensure compliance with Amazon’s policies and avoid potential delisting.

**13. Can I create listings for private-label products without a Letter of Authorization?**

- Yes, private-label products do not require a Letter of Authorization. However, you must list them under a unique, unregistered brand name or apply for brand registration to secure the brand name.

**14. What happens if Amazon requests additional documentation after the Letter of Authorization is submitted?**

- Provide the requested documents promptly. This may include invoices, proof of purchase, or additional communication with the brand owner to confirm the authenticity of the Letter of Authorization.

**15. Can I use the same Letter of Authorization across different Amazon marketplaces?**

- This depends on the letter. If it specifies authorization for multiple regions (e.g., U.S., Canada, Europe), it can be used across marketplaces. If the letter is region-specific, you will need separate documentation for each marketplace.

**16. What should I do if I encounter technical issues during the application process?**

- If you face technical issues, clear your browser cache, try a different browser, or refresh your session. If the problem persists, contact Amazon Seller Support for assistance.
