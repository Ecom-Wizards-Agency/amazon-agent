## **How to Create FBA Shipment**

**Who is this for :** Intended for Specialist or any individuals who are involved in or responsible for managing the shipment process.

**Objective :** To help understand the new process workflow of shipment creation and learn more about setting up shipments for case packs or individuals, the difference between LTL and SPD shipments, as well as the shipping labels and costs incurred during shipment creation.

Send to Amazon or STA - is the shipment creation streamlined workflow to save time and send your products to Fulfillment by Amazon or FBA.

It allows Amazon to store, pack, and ship the items to customers on the seller's behalf. The seller is responsible for creating the shipment and providing the necessary information. Occasionally, our sellers will ask us to create an FBA shipment for them. This SOP will walk you through the steps in creating FBA shipments. This new streamlined shipment creation workflow requires fewer steps to replenish your FBA inventory which is more straightforward than the old version.

**Note :** Before we create the shipment, the seller must provide the information about the inventory they want to send. Create a separate copy of the [FBA Plan Template.xlsx](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/attachments/25) and ask the seller to fill it out. You may proceed with the following steps after they complete the template.

FAQs

1. What is the difference between Small Parcel Delivery (SPD) and Less Than Truckload (LTL) shipments?

- SPD (Small Parcel Delivery) is for smaller shipments where each box is individually labeled for delivery. These are usually sent via carriers like DHL, UPS, or FedEx.
- LTL (Less Than Truckload) is for larger freight that doesn’t require a full trailer. It’s typically used for bulk shipments where items are packed on pallets, and multiple SKUs may be combined in one shipment.

2. Can I choose a different carrier than the Amazon-partnered carriers for my shipments?

Yes, you can select a non-Amazon partnered carrier if you prefer, but you won’t be able to take advantage of Amazon’s discounted shipping rates or automated tracking.

3. What happens if my shipment doesn’t meet the weight or size requirements?

If your shipment exceeds the weight or size limits, you may face additional charges or your shipment might be rejected. For LTL shipments, ensure that you adhere to pallet size, weight, and labeling requirements. For SPD, box size limits must be respected, and larger items may need special handling.

4. How do I track my shipment?

If you’re using an Amazon-partnered carrier, tracking information will be automatically generated. If using a non-partnered carrier, you’ll need to manually input the tracking IDs in Seller Central under the Tracking Details step of the shipment workflow.

5. What are the pallet requirements for LTL shipments?

For LTL shipments, ensure:

- Boxes containing multiple Standard-Size units don’t exceed 25 inches on any side.
- Individual Oversize units exceeding 50 lbs must be labeled with “Team Lift” or “Mech Lift” depending on weight.
- Boxes with Jewelry or Watches should not exceed 40 lbs.

6. Do I need to manually enter dimensions for my boxes or pallets?

Yes, you’ll need to accurately weigh and measure your boxes and pallets to ensure that the correct information is entered for shipping. This is especially important if using an Amazon-partnered carrier.

7. Can I mix SKUs from different pack groups in the same box?

No, you should not mix SKUs from different pack groups. Each pack group must be packed separately, and this should be reflected in your packing details.

8. What should I do if my shipment contains hazardous materials?

If your shipment contains dangerous goods or hazardous materials, you must ensure compliance with Amazon’s policies. In such cases, UPS (Amazon-partnered carrier) cannot be used, and specific labeling or additional documentation may be required.

9. Can I edit the packing details after confirming the shipment?

Once you’ve confirmed and saved your packing details, changes cannot be made to the shipment unless you cancel and recreate the shipment plan. Always double-check the details before confirming.

10. What happens if I select the wrong shipping method or carrier?

Once you confirm the shipping method and carrier, this selection cannot be undone. Ensure that you review all shipping charges, prep fees, and carrier information before finalizing your selection.
