# Catalog SOP: Mental Health Disorder and Sleep Disorder Claims

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-mental-health-disorder-and-sleep-disorder-claims

## **Mental Health and Sleep Disorder Claims**

**Who is this for :**

- This is for Amazon sellers who are selling dietary supplements or cosmetics and are dealing with restricted product policy violations related to mental health and sleep disorder claims.
- It is also for sellers who want to avoid such violations and ensure compliance with Amazon's policies.

**Objective :**

- To provide guidelines to Amazon sellers who are dealing with restricted product policy violations related to mental health and sleep disorder claims.
- This SOP aims to help the sellers take the necessary actions to fix the listing and appeal for reinstatement.

Few people are aware of the connection between nutrition and depression while they easily understand the connection between nutritional deficiencies and physical illness. Depression is more typically thought of as strictly biochemical-based or emotionally rooted. On the contrary, nutrition can play a vital role in the onset as well as severity and duration of depression.

This is the reason why many Amazon sellers took advantage of providing dietary supplements that claim to cure, treat, mitigate, or prevent depression and other mental health disorders. Sellers can put mental health disorder symptoms and treatments because they are not part of the disease types/claims in the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act of 1994.

Nevertheless, Amazon’s legal team took action and put restrictions on dietary supplements that claim to cure, treat, mitigate, or prevent depression and other mental health disorders after 10 companies received warning letters from FDA for illegally selling such dietary supplements in February 2021.

It’s fairly new and there are no current or updated Amazon articles that disclose mental health disorders. If ever you receive a violation notification, Amazon will direct you to the [Dietary Supplements article](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/G201829010).

**Remember:**

- Canada has different ways of natural health products (NHP) regulations under Health Canada’s supervision.

**Restricted Product Policy Violations Details:**

The violation details are located under “Reason” in Restricted Product Policy Violations in Account Health Performance.

- This product has been identified as a dietary supplement or cosmetic that claims to treat, cure, or prevent a disease related to a mental health disorder and will not be considered for reinstatement unless the seller removes all prohibited disease claims from the product detail page and product images. Please note that if the disease claim is on the product labeling, there may not be a path to reinstatement.

**How to Fix?**

**Document the changes/actions you will take.**

1. Check the product images and remove the prohibited claims from infographics.
2. Download a category listing report (CLR) and remove all prohibited claims from the following attributes.

  1. Title
  2. Description
  3. Bullet points
  4. Search terms
  5. Intended Use
  6. Other Attributes
3. Upload the updated CLR and take the batch ID.
4. Go to A+ contents and look for the ASIN. Remove the prohibited claims from the description, images, and alt-text keywords.
5. If you’re confident that everything is set, go to Performance > Account Health > Restricted Product Policy Violations > Look for the ASIN > Appeal > Check the 2 boxes and Submit. Wait 24 hours before the listing gets reinstated.

Sleep Disorder

### Sleep Disorder

As it turns out, there’s quite a bit of truth behind this colloquial saying. Sleep is closely connected to mental and emotional health and has demonstrated links to depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, and other conditions.[4]

Americans are notoriously sleep deprived, but those with psychiatric conditions are even more likely to be yawning or groggy during the day. Chronic sleep problems affect 50% to 80% of patients in a typical psychiatric practice, compared with 10% to 18% of adults in the general U.S. population. Sleep problems are particularly common in patients with anxiety, depression, bipolar disorder, and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).[5]

FAQs

1. What are Amazon’s restrictions on mental health and sleep disorder claims?

Amazon prohibits dietary supplements and cosmetics from claiming to treat, cure, mitigate, or prevent mental health disorders or sleep disorders. Listings containing terms like "depression," "anxiety," "insomnia," or "mood boosting" may receive policy violation notices and be removed.

2. How do I check if my listing has been flagged for a mental health or sleep disorder claim?

Go to Performance > Account Health > Restricted Product Policy Violations and locate the flagged ASIN. The reason for the violation will be listed under "Reason," indicating if the product was identified as making a prohibited claim.

3. Can I use indirect mental health-related claims like "supports relaxation" or "promotes a calm mind"?

Even indirect claims can lead to violations if Amazon determines they imply a treatment effect. Avoid wording that suggests treating, curing, or mitigating conditions like anxiety, depression, or insomnia.

4. What are some alternative phrases to avoid mental health claim violations?

Instead of prohibited terms like "reduces stress" or "fights insomnia," consider using compliant alternatives such as:

- "Promotes a sense of well-being" instead of "Relieves anxiety"
- "Supports a restful night" instead of "Treats insomnia"
- "Encourages relaxation" instead of "Calms the mind"

5. What steps should I take to fix my listing after a mental health disorder claim violation?

1. Review the violation notice in Account Health.
2. Remove all prohibited claims from the title, bullet points, description, and images.
3. Download a Category Listing Report (CLR) and update the necessary fields.
4. Check A+ Content and remove any restricted terms from text, images, and alt-text keywords.
5. Re-upload the updated CLR and keep the batch ID for reference.
6. Submit an appeal under Performance > Account Health > Restricted Product Policy Violations > Appeal and check the required boxes before submitting.

6. My listing was still removed after editing. What should I do?

If your listing remains inactive, submit a ticket via Help > Get Support > Selling on Amazon > Products, Listings, or Inventory > Inventory file upload issue. Include the ASIN, details of the corrections, and the batch ID of the updated listing.

7. Can I reinstate a listing if the prohibited claim appears on my product label?

No. If the claim is physically printed on the product label or packaging, Amazon may deny reinstatement. The only possible solution is to update the packaging and resubmit the product.

8. How often does Amazon update its list of restricted terms for mental health and sleep disorder claims?

Amazon does not publicly disclose updates, but new restrictions can be enforced at any time. Sellers should regularly review Amazon’s Dietary Supplement Policy and stay informed about FDA warnings related to supplement claims.

9. Are products containing psychoactive or hallucinogenic ingredients allowed on Amazon?

No. Amazon prohibits products containing psychoactive or hallucinogenic compounds, even if they claim to support mental health. Identified restricted ingredients include Tetrahydroharmine (THH) and Kanna, but the list may expand.

10. Where can I find official guidelines on restricted supplement claims?

For compliance, refer to:

- Amazon’s Dietary Supplement Policy
- FDA Warning Letters ([source](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-warns-10-companies-illegally-selling-dietary-supplements-claiming-treat-depression-and-other))
- Health Canada’s NHP Regulations
- NIH’s DSHEA Guidelines ([source](https://ods.od.nih.gov/About/DSHEA_Wording.aspx))
