# Catalog SOP: Brand Registry Ticket Cases & Updates

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-brand-registry-ticket-cases-updates

## Brand Registry Ticket Cases & Updates

**Who is this for :** Sellers and other individuals who wish to contact brand registry support.

**Objective :** To guide on how to appropriately file a brand registry case

Instructions:

1. Log into Amazon’s Brand Registry dashboard

  - Click sign in when on the [Brand Registry Dashboard](https://brandregistry.amazon.com/) and select your marketplace

    ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-30%20at%204.27.36%E2%80%AFAM.png)
2. Once logged in, hover over the Support tab and click on Contact Brand Support. (Use Brand Support Case log if you want to go see the cases you have previously filed)

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-30%20at%204.27.42%E2%80%AFAM.png)
3. Select the category closest to your issue but most of the time, we would use the Technical Issue Tab
4. Contact Reason - should be renamed into something easy that you can go back and with a quick glance, you’ll know what the ticket case is about
5. Please describe your issue - you will explain to Amazon the issue that you are trying to fix, explain the actions that you have taken, and what actions you are looking for Amazon to help with.
6. Additional information - using files and screenshots will sometimes do better than the explanations, it helps Seller Support with the specific issues you are facing
7. Check if it’s the correct email address that you want the cases to be sent to and click send when ready

Action to Take = File a ticket through Amazon Brand Registry explaining the following below:

1. The issue you are trying to fix
2. Explain the actions you have taken
3. What actions are you looking for Amazon to help with?

FAQs

1. **What should I do if I'm unsure which category to select when filing a case?**

- If you're unsure which category to select, start by choosing the "Technical Issue Tab," as it's often the most applicable for many issues. If your issue doesn’t seem to fit in any of the predefined categories, try selecting the option that seems most closely related, and then clearly explain your issue in the description. If in doubt, you can always ask for further clarification after submitting the case.

1. **What happens after I submit a ticket through Amazon Brand Registry?**

- Once you submit your ticket, Amazon Brand Registry Support will review the details and respond via email or through the Brand Registry dashboard. Response times can vary, but typically you can expect a response within 24-48 hours. Make sure your email is up-to-date so you can receive updates promptly.

1. **How do I escalate a case if I’m not getting a satisfactory resolution?**

- If you're not happy with the response or if the issue isn’t resolved after a reasonable amount of time, you can escalate the case by following up through the Brand Registry dashboard. In your follow-up, be sure to express that the issue needs urgent attention and that prior attempts to resolve it were unsuccessful. You may also request to speak to a senior representative.

1. **How do I follow up on an open case?**

- To follow up on an open case, go to the “Brand Support Case log” section in the Brand Registry dashboard. Here, you can check the status of your case and add additional information, such as files or updated details. You can also send a direct follow-up message to the support team if you haven’t received a response within the expected timeframe.

1. **What should I do if my ticket is closed without a resolution?**

- If your ticket is closed without a resolution, reopen the case by submitting a follow-up ticket or contacting support again through the "Brand Support Case log." Be sure to mention that the issue wasn’t resolved and provide additional details or screenshots if necessary. If the issue persists, consider escalating the case to ensure it gets the necessary attention.

1. **How can I track the status of my open ticket case?**

- You can track the status of your open ticket by visiting the “Brand Support Case log” in the Brand Registry dashboard. Here, you’ll see all your active and closed cases, as well as any updates or responses from Amazon Brand Registry Support.

1. **What specific information should I include in my ticket to ensure the best outcome?**

- To ensure a quick and effective resolution, include the following information in your ticket:

  - A clear description of the issue you're facing
  - The specific actions you’ve already taken to try and resolve it
  - Any relevant details such as ASINs, order numbers, or other identifiers
  - Screenshots, error messages, or files that can help support the issue
  - A clear request for the action you're seeking from Amazon Support

1. **What should I do if there is no clear category that fits my issue?**

- If you can't find a category that fits your issue, select the closest option available, such as "Technical Issue Tab," and explain your issue in detail in the ticket description. You can also mention that no category matched your issue to ensure that the support team fully understands your case.
