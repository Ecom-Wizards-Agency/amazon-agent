# Catalog SOP: How to Request GTIN Exemption

## How to Request GTIN Exemption

**Who is this for?**This is for Account Specialists preparing to list a product without a product ID.

**Objective:**To ensure that the Account Specialists know how to request a GTIN (Global Trade Item Number) exemption and proceed with listing creation.

**PRE-REQUISITE BEFORE YOU APPLY FOR GTIN EXEMPTION:**

Product name and a minimum of two (and maximum of nine) images showing all sides of your product and the packaging of the product. Ensure the guidelines below are met:

- The brand name entered during the application process should be an exact match to the brand name available on the product or packaging.
- Images should be real-world photos of the products and packaging, meaning the product and packaging need to be held or placed on a table while taking the photo.
- Branding is permanently affixed on the packaging or product.
- Products should not have a GS1-approved barcode available on the product or packaging of the product. If a GS1-approved barcode is available, you can use it to list products instead of applying for a GTIN exemption.

**Instructions:**

1. Go to**Catalog > Add products**, and select [I'm adding a product not sold on Amazon](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/productclassify?ref_=xx_catadd_dnav_xx).

FAQs

#### 1. What is GTIN Exemption?

GTIN Exemption allows sellers to list products on Amazon without a GTIN (Global Trade Item Number), such as UPC, EAN, or ISBN, if the product is unbranded or falls into specific categories where a GTIN is not available.

#### 2. How do I know if I need a GTIN Exemption?

You need to request a GTIN exemption if your product does not have a GTIN (such as a custom or unbranded product) or if the product packaging does not include a GS1-approved barcode. You will be asked to apply for the exemption when you cannot use a product ID during the listing process.

#### 3. What do I need to apply for a GTIN Exemption?

Before applying for a GTIN exemption, you need the following:

- Product name.
- A minimum of two (and a maximum of nine) images showing all sides of the product and the packaging.
- Ensure that the brand name matches the one on the product or packaging.

#### 4. What kind of images are required for the GTIN exemption application?

The images should be real-world photos of the product and its packaging. These images should clearly show the product with branding permanently affixed to the product or packaging. The product should be placed on a table or held up to showcase the packaging.

#### 5. Can I list a product without a GTIN if it has a GS1 barcode?

No. If the product has a GS1-approved barcode on the product or packaging, you should use that barcode to list the product instead of requesting a GTIN exemption.

#### 6. How do I apply for a GTIN Exemption on Amazon?

- Go to Catalog > Add products and select "I'm adding a product not sold on Amazon."
- Enter the product name and select the category.
- In the Brand name field, input the brand name. If the product is unbranded, select the checkbox stating that the product doesn’t have a brand name.
- Select I don’t have a product ID and click Next.
- If an exemption is required, click Apply to sell and fill out the application form.
- Click Submit once the form is completed.

#### 7. How long will it take to get a response after submitting the GTIN exemption request?

You will receive an email within 48 hours about the approval status of your request. You can also check the status in your case log.

#### 8. What should I do if my GTIN exemption request is rejected?

If your request is rejected, ensure that the product and brand details match the requirements. Double-check the images and make sure they meet Amazon's guidelines. If you believe there was an error, you can appeal or contact Seller Support for assistance.

#### 9. What do the error codes 5665, 5461, and Match Only Error mean during the application process?

These errors may occur if the product information doesn’t meet Amazon's listing criteria or if the exemption is not available for your specific product. Refer to the "Catalog SOP: Feed Processing Error Code - 5665" article for further guidance.
