# Catalog SOP: Amazon Damaged and Lost Inventory Reimbursements

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-amazon-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursements

## **Amazon Damaged and Lost Inventory Reimbursements**

**Who is this for:** Intended for individuals or businesses who sell products through Amazon and have experienced lost or damaged inventory.

**Objective:** Provide guidance on the process of tracking and investigating lost and damaged inventory, as well as the process for requesting reimbursement from Amazon. By following the procedures outlined in this SOP, individuals will be better equipped to navigate the reimbursement process with Amazon.

**Claim Window :**

| Eligibility window start date | Eligibility claim window | Eligibility window end date |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Day item is reported as lost or damaged | 60 days | 60 days from the day the item was reported lost or damaged in Amazon’s fulfillment center |

**LOST ITEMS**

1. Go into your Amazon seller account. Then hover over Reports and click on Fulfillment.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-NMFCK4BJ.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/o7yimage.png)

2. From the Inventory header, select Inventory Ledger.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/catalog-sop-amazon-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursements-image-2.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/HGKimage.png)

3. Click the "Download" link from the Inventory Ledger page.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/catalog-sop-amazon-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursements-image-3.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/S8Mimage.png)

4. Make sure to select the following:

- Type of Report: Detailed View
- Event Type: Adjustments
- Date Range: Exact Dates (go back as far as you can and select the present day)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/mZfimage.png)

5. Click on Request csv or txt Download button. Open the file, and filter the "Reason" column to "M" and "5". Then, filter the "Unreconciled Quantity" column to 1 or more. Make sure to delete duplicates under the data "FNSKU" column. See sample file below.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/catalog-sop-amazon-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursements-image-5(2).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/7XAimage.png)

6. Once you have all the transaction/reference id, request reimbursement for lost inventory by going to this [link](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/GGEV4254LJJ9BAEG)

7. Scroll to the bottom of the page and look for **"Investigate an Item Lost in Warehouse"**

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/catalog-sop-amazon-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursements-image-6.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/tK4image.png)

8. Enter the FNSKU (one by one) and click "Continue"

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop---fba-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursement-charles-ro-image-68iup0hi.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/yBLimage.png)

9. Amazon will come back with their findings:

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/EShimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/EShimage.png)

10. Click on the “Create a Case” button.

11. If successful, a case will be created. It will either be approved to reimburse the account or a “further investigation is needed” one. It will look like this:

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop---fba-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursement-charles-ro-image-5yg8rsti.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/kY4g1Uimage.png)

OR

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop---fba-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursement-charles-ro-image-h0tl9xcf.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/vgrimage.png)

12. This will most often be instant reimbursement to your account.

**DAMAGED ITEMS**

Reimbursement for damaged inventory follows a similar process. You can use the exact same list you generated above.

1. Simply filter the **"Reason"** column to include values 6, 7, E, H, K, U, and filter the **"Unreconciled Quantity"** column to 1 or more. Also, ensure to delete duplicates under the "FNSKU" column in the data. See sample file below.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/catalog-sop-amazon-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursements-image-7.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/W5mimage.png)

2. Go to this [link](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/GGEV4254LJJ9BAEG) again

3. Copy each Transaction/Reference ID and paste them in the box under **“check warehouse damaged reimbursement status”**, and request reimbursement.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/msuimage.png)

4. Amazon will let you know if the transaction id is eligible for reimbursement:

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop---fba-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursement-charles-ro-image-jh6mxvw9.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/7yMimage.png)

5. Click on “Continue”.

This only works for FBA SKUs so if you get a drop-down menu to choose an SKU, pick the FBA SKU. If successful, a case will be created for the claim. You can review it in the case log. The case will look like this:

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop---fba-damaged-and-lost-inventory-reimbursement-charles-ro-image-mhak82v3.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/naximage.png)

FAQs

- **What is the difference between "lost" and "damaged" inventory?**

  - Lost inventory refers to items that have gone missing within Amazon's fulfillment centers. Damaged inventory includes items that have been broken, rendered unusable, or tampered with while in Amazon's fulfillment centers.

- **What should I do if my eligibility claim window has expired?**

  - Unfortunately, claims must be filed within Amazon’s 60-day eligibility window. For expired claims, you won’t be able to request reimbursement. Moving forward, make it a practice to regularly monitor and review your inventory adjustment reports to avoid missing deadlines.

- **Can I request reimbursement for inventory damaged or lost during inbound shipments to Amazon?**

  - Yes, but this process differs from the fulfillment center reimbursement. If inventory was lost or damaged while being shipped to an Amazon fulfillment center, you’ll need to file a claim through the "Reconcile" tab within the Shipping Queue of your seller account.

- **How can I track the status of my reimbursement claim?**

  - You can review the status of your reimbursement claim by navigating to your Case Log in your Amazon Seller Central account. From there, locate the specific case and review the latest updates or resolution provided by Amazon.

- **What do the different "Reason" column values mean in the inventory ledger?**

  - The "Reason" column in your downloaded inventory ledger specifies why the adjustment was made:

    - M and 5: Typically indicate missing or lost items.
    - 6, 7, E, H, K, U: Correspond to various damage reasons (e.g., defective, misplaced, or destroyed). Ensure you filter for these values when creating your claim lists.

- **What happens if Amazon rejects my reimbursement claim?**

  - If Amazon rejects your claim, review the case notes for specific reasons. Common issues include incorrect FNSKU, ineligible transaction IDs, or filing claims outside the eligibility window. If you believe the rejection is in error, you can open a follow-up case with additional evidence, such as shipment tracking records or proof of inventory sent.

- **How often should I check for lost or damaged inventory?**

  - To stay proactive and avoid missing the eligibility window, it’s best to check for adjustments in your inventory ledger every two weeks. Setting a reminder or automating reports can help streamline this process.

- **Are reimbursements instant once the claim is approved?**

  - Reimbursements are typically processed immediately upon approval, but it may take up to five business days for the funds to reflect in your account. If further investigation is required, resolution times may vary.

- **Can I file claims for items sold via FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant)?**

  - No, this reimbursement process only applies to FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) inventory. Items sold via FBM are not eligible for reimbursement under these guidelines.

- **What should I do if my reimbursement claim contains duplicates?**

  - Before filing claims, ensure you remove duplicates from the FNSKU and Transaction ID columns in your inventory ledger. Filing duplicate claims may delay processing or result in rejection.

- **Are there any fees or penalties for filing reimbursement claims?**

  - Amazon does not charge fees or penalties for reimbursement claims. However, it’s essential to ensure that all claims are legitimate and accurate to avoid potential account issues.

- **Can I delegate this process to a team member or external service?**

  - Yes, you can delegate the task. Ensure they have access to your Seller Central account with appropriate permissions. Alternatively, some third-party services specialize in handling lost or damaged inventory claims on behalf of sellers.

- **How do I identify if an item is lost or damaged during order fulfillment (to customers)?**

  - The SOP does not cover customer-related lost/damaged claims. For such cases, review the shipment and order details under the "Manage Orders" tab in Seller Central, then file a claim through Amazon’s customer service or seller support.

- **What documentation should I keep for claim audits?**

  - Maintain detailed records of your inventory shipments, receipts, and adjustment reports. Keep all communication with Amazon regarding claims for at least 12 months in case of an audit.
