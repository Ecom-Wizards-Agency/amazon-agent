## How to Print Amazon Barcodes

**Who is this for :** Intended for everyone who needs guidance or instructions on how to print the barcodes

**Objective :** To learn how to print labels and understand the importance of barcodes in identifying and tracking inventory for the fulfillment process. Fulfillment by Amazon uses barcodes to identify and track inventory throughout the fulfillment process. Every item sent to Amazon through FBA requires a barcode.

Upcoming Update Effective March 2026

##### **Upcoming update effective March 2026**

Amazon [Announcement](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/seller-news/articles/QVRWUERLSUtYMERFUiNHSktDTEM5N0s5MkRBWFAy?ref=nswg_scgw_GJKCLC97K92DAXP2_en_US_1_nslp&communicationDeliveryId=5a9bba4b-d567-4f22-a967-3331b7e4eb03):

In March, Amazon will end commingling practices across the supply chain and update eligibility criteria for using manufacturer barcodes.

The following requirements will go into effect for inventory shipped on or after March 31, 2026:

- Brand owners

  with the Brand Representative selling role in

  Amazon Brand Registry

  will no longer need to apply Amazon barcode stickers to prevent commingling for products that already have manufacturer barcodes (such as UPC, ISBN). This means more flexibility in how you manage inventory because you will not need to pre-allocate units to Amazon or other channels. For more information, go to

  How to switch to manufacturer barcodes

  .
- Resellers

  (not enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry as a Brand Representative selling role) will now be required to use Amazon barcode stickers for products even if they have a manufacturer barcode. If you are already using these stickers, you can continue to follow your existing process.
- For products that

  do not have a manufacturer barcode

  , both brand owners and resellers will need to use Amazon Barcode stickers.

There are three kinds of barcodes for identifying products:

- Manufacturer barcodes (eligible barcodes include UPC, EAN, JAN, and ISBN)
- Amazon barcodes (such as FNSKU)
- [Transparency](https://brandservices.amazon.com/transparency) authenticity code (brand owner only, may be required to help prevent counterfeit)

To Print Amazon barcodes:

1. Go to Inventory → Manage All Inventory
2. Scan your list of products and find the one you want to print labels for.
3. Click Edit on the right of the selected product
4. In the dropdown menu, click Print item labels

5. Enter how many labels you want to print in the "Number of labels to print" box
6. Select the drop-down beside the Paper/Sticker Type and choose your preference
7. When you’re finished, click Print Item Labels

FAQs

**1. What types of barcodes are used for identifying products in Amazon's fulfillment process?**

- Amazon uses three types of barcodes:

  - Manufacturer Barcodes: Includes UPC, EAN, JAN, and ISBN.
  - Amazon Barcodes: Such as FNSKU, which are specific to items sent through FBA.
  - Transparency Authenticity Codes: For brand owners to prevent counterfeit products.

**2. How do I print Amazon barcodes for a specific product?**

- To print barcodes for a product:

  - Go to Inventory → Manage All Inventory.
  - Find the product you want to print labels for and click Edit on the right side.
  - From the dropdown menu, select Print item labels.
  - Enter the number of labels to print in the Number of labels to print box.
  - Choose your preferred Paper/Sticker Type from the dropdown menu.
  - Click Print Item Labels to complete the process.

**3. Can I print labels during the shipment creation process?**

- Yes, labels can be printed during the shipment process:

  - Go to Inventory → Shipments.
  - Select Send to Amazon from the Shipment dropdown menu.
  - If there’s an existing workflow, click Start New to begin a new workflow.
  - Search for the ASIN/SKU or select it from the list.
  - Click Print SKU labels.
  - Enter the number of labels in the Print label box and click Print.

**4. What should I do if I cannot find the option to print item labels?**

- Ensure you are in the Manage All Inventory section and have selected the product by clicking Edit. Confirm the product is FBA-eligible and has an FNSKU assigned. If issues persist, contact Amazon Seller Support.

**5. Can I select different paper or sticker types for printing labels?**

- Yes. When printing, use the dropdown next to Paper/Sticker Type to select the appropriate label format based on your printer and shipping requirements.

**6. How many barcodes can I print at a time?**

- You can specify the quantity in the Number of labels to print box. There is no maximum limit, but ensure your printer and label sheets match your needs.

**7. What is the purpose of Transparency authenticity codes?**

- Transparency authenticity codes are used by brand owners to prevent counterfeit products. These codes ensure that items meet the standards of Amazon’s Transparency program.

**8. What should I do if my printed barcodes are unreadable or incorrect?**

- If barcodes are unclear or incorrect:

  - Double-check your selected product and label type.
  - Ensure your printer settings are optimized, and your printer is properly maintained.
  - Reprint the labels after troubleshooting the issue.

**9. Do I need barcodes for all items sent to Amazon FBA?**

- Yes. All items sent through Amazon FBA require a barcode (Manufacturer Barcode, Amazon Barcode, or Transparency Code) for proper identification and tracking.

**10. Where can I find more information about Amazon barcodes?**

- Visit the FBA Product Barcode Requirements and Print Amazon Barcodes sections in Amazon Seller Central for detailed instructions and guidelines.
