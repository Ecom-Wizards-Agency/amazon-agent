## **How to Enroll a Brand and Products in the Amazon Transparency Program**

**Who is this for:** This SOP is for brand owners and their designated team members responsible for enrolling in Amazon's Transparency program to protect their products from counterfeiting.

**Objective:** This guide walks brand owners through the process of enrolling in Amazon's Transparency program, ensuring product authenticity, and managing transparency codes for enrolled ASINs.

**Prerequisites:**

Before enrolling in the Amazon Transparency program, ensure the following requirements are met:

1. **Brand Registry Enrollment:** Your brand must be enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry.
2. **Registered Trademark:** You must have a government-registered trademark for your brand. If you need assistance obtaining a registered trademark,[click here](https://sell.amazon.com/programs/ip-accelerator).
3. **Global Trade Item Number (GTIN):** You must have a GTIN for your products, such as a UPC, ISBN, or EAN.
4. **Rights Owner Status:** You must be listed as the Rights Owner in Amazon Brand Registry.

  ***Note****:* You can create a [Transparency account](http://transparency.amazon.com/v2/tpncyaccount/signup?ref=SDNATRREGLP24) using your Brand Registry credentials.

**Overview:**

The Transparency program is designed to protect your products by providing each unit with a unique code, allowing both you and your customers to verify the authenticity of the product, no matter where it's sold or shipped.

1. Once your ASINs are enrolled, go to the **Code Requests** tab in the Transparency portal.

  [![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/DCAimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/DCAimage.png)

1. Find your ASIN and enter the number of codes you need (you can request up to 100,000 codes per ASIN).
2. You can also provide additional details to display when customers scan the Transparency code.
3. Click **Request Codes**, and you’ll receive an email within about 30 minutes to let you know when your codes are ready.

**Important Considerations:**

- Keep your enrolled and unenrolled inventory separate to prevent any mix-ups.
- Remember that each unit of an enrolled product must have a unique Transparency code.
- Transparency codes are valid globally, across all Amazon fulfillment centers, and in multiple countries.

FAQs

1. What is the Amazon Transparency Program?

- The Amazon Transparency program is designed to protect your products from counterfeiting. It provides each product with a unique Transparency code, which customers and Amazon can scan to verify the product’s authenticity, no matter where it's sold or fulfilled.

2. What are the prerequisites to enroll in the Transparency program? Before enrolling, ensure that:

- Your brand is enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry.
- You have a government-registered trademark for your brand.
- Your products have a Global Trade Item Number (GTIN) like a UPC, ISBN, or EAN.
- You are listed as the Rights Owner in Brand Registry. You can create a Transparency account using your Brand Registry credentials.

3.Can I request multiple codes for the same ASIN?

- Yes, you can request up to 100,000 codes per ASIN. Each unit of the product requires its own unique Transparency code.

4.Are Transparency codes valid worldwide?

- Yes, Transparency codes are valid globally, across all Amazon fulfillment centers and in multiple countries.

5.What should I do to prevent issues with enrolled and unenrolled inventory?

- It's important to keep your enrolled and unenrolled inventory separate to avoid mix-ups and ensure that each unit of an enrolled product has a unique Transparency code.

6.How do I know if my enrollment was successful?

- After you submit your product information, you’ll receive an email confirmation when your enrollment is complete. You can also check the Job History section in the Transparency portal for updates on the status of your ASINs.
