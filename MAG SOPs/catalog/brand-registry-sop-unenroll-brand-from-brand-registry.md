# Brand Registry SOP: Unenroll Brand from Brand Registry

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/brand-registry-sop-unenroll-brand-from-brand-registry

## **Unenroll Brand from Brand Registry (Remove Brand From Amazon Brand Registry Program Completely)**

**Who is this for :** This process is for individuals with admin permissions for a specific brand name in the Amazon Brand Registry Program, who need to completely unenroll and remove the brand.

**Objective:** To efficiently and effectively unenroll a brand from the Amazon Brand Registry Program, ensuring that all associated user permissions are removed and the brand is no longer visible in the Store Tab.

**Note:**You need to have admin permissions for that specific brand name first of all.

**Here are some steps that you can take to unenroll Brand from Brand Registry**

1. Go to brand registry user permissions: [https://brandregistry.amazon.com/brg/user-permissions](https://brandregistry.amazon.com/brg/user-permissions)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1681898872546.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/njqimage.png)

2. Filter out to the specific brand name you are working on:

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screen%20Shot%202024-10-10%20at%209.34.04%20PM(1).png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/8L0image.png)

3. Remove all other user permissions for that specific brand name except your own:

4. Click Manage next to the email address:

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screen%20Shot%202024-10-10%20at%209.36.37%20PM(1).png)

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-unenroll-brand-from-brand-registry-image-no1el6d4.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/9dqimage.png)

5. Remove all checks for that specific brand name:

6. Scroll down and click Update:

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/sop_-unenroll-brand-from-brand-registry-image-v49wqxov.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/lOWimage.png)

7. Once you remove other users from the brand name, click Manage Permissions next to our email address. Remove the brand name from our email address as well. You will receive an error message at the top of the page.

8. Click “Click Here” (If you don’t want to grant permissions to a new Admin)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/R02image.png)

9. Click “Confirm Changes”

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1681252858400.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/4Vsimage.png)

10. Once you have removed all users, the brand will be partially enrolled in Amazon. Create a case in the SC account.

**Use the template below as a starting point, and adjust it to fit your specific scenario:**

"Greetings,

We have partially removed the brand (Enter brand name) from the brand registry by removing all users including our permission. Hence, there are no expected brand users of “Brand Name” at the moment.

This trademark will be discontinued soon therefore, we have to remove it from the brand registry.

Please completely unenroll the brand from your system. "

**Note:** If you receive confirmation that they have removed/unenrolled the brand, review the store tab. The brand should no longer appear in the Store Tab if they did remove the brand from BR.

FAQs

**1. Can all brands be removed from Amazon Brand Registry?**

- Not all brands can be removed. If the brand:

  - Has an active registered trademark, it may require proof that the trademark is no longer in use.
  - Is involved in any pending disputes, IP claims, or enforcement actions, Amazon may delay or deny the request.

**2. What happens to my product listings after removing the brand from Brand Registry?**

- Your ASINs will remain active, but you lose brand protections, such as:

  - Control over product detail page edits.
  - Access to A+ Content and Brand Stores.
  - The ability to remove unauthorized sellers.
  - Hijackers and unauthorized resellers may take over the listings.

**3. How long does it take for Amazon to fully remove a brand from Brand Registry?**

- Amazon typically processes requests within 7–14 business days, but this may vary.
- If no response is received within 14 days, open a follow-up case with Amazon Brand Registry Support.

**4. What should I do if Amazon refuses to remove my brand from Brand Registry?**

- If Amazon denies the request:

  - Ask for a specific reason and request further clarification.
  - If the brand has an active trademark, you may need to cancel it with the USPTO or relevant trademark office before Amazon processes the removal.
  - If Amazon does not respond, escalate the request through Amazon Seller Support leadership.

**5. How can I confirm that the brand has been fully removed?**

- Check Store Tab to confirm that the brand no longer appears.
- Go to Brand Registry User Permissions and verify that the brand is no longer listed.
- Try to edit an ASIN that was previously brand-registered—if you no longer have access, the brand has likely been removed.

**6. Can I re-enroll my brand after removing it from Amazon Brand Registry?**

- Yes, you can reapply for Brand Registry if the trademark is still active. However, you must go through the full re-enrollment process, including verifying trademark ownership again.

**7. What alternative actions can I take if Amazon refuses to remove the brand?**

- If the trademark is no longer in use, you may formally abandon or cancel it with the trademark office and provide proof to Amazon.
- Consider contacting Amazon Legal Support if the issue involves a disputed brand ownership claim.
