## **How to Reconcile FBA Shipments - Missing and Overage Units**

**Who is this for :** Individuals who are involved in managing inventory and tracking shipments.

**Objective :**This SOP explains how to submit an investigation for missing or overage units from inbound shipments and to know how to use the Shipment Reconciliation tool, which can be found in the shipping workflow.

To navigate to the Shipment Reconciliation tool:

1. On the Inventory tab, click Shipments.
2. Find your shipments and then click Track Shipment.
3. On your shipping workflow, click the Contents tab.

The Contents tab on the Shipment summary page will display the status of the units as they are received. If there are any differences between what you have sent in the shipment and what is received, use the tools within the Contents tab to:

- Get detailed information about discrepancies between your shipping plan and what the fulfillment center received
- Provide more information about the shipment contents
- Request an investigation for missing or overage items

**Things to check before submitting a request to reconcile shipment:**

1. Shipment must be eligible for investigation.
2. Proof of inventory ownership (Invoice or packing slip).
3. Proof of delivery (POD): For LTL or FTL shipments, a bill of lading showing the number of boxes in the shipment and the total weight when it was picked up by the carrier. The document must be stamped by Amazon confirming that the shipment was received and signed for at the fulfillment center. Your carrier should have a copy of this document. This information helps verify that the entire shipment was picked up and shipped as expected.

Products in the shipment that are eligible for additional research will have “Action required” options for you to select from in the Status column. Every product with a status of “Action required” must have an option selected before you can preview and submit the research request.

**Submit and Upload documents**

- Use the Choose File button to select all documents and the Upload button to attach documents to the research request.

  - Follow the document best practices below to avoid delays in your request:

    - Resolution should be 2,000 x 1,500 pixels or higher
    - The document should be properly oriented (not sideways or upside down)
    - Only one document per submission, uploaded in a single file
    - Submit only .jpg, .jpeg, .png, .tif, .tiff, or .pdf file formats

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/Screenshot%202025-01-24%20at%2011.22.24%E2%80%AFPM.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/6sDimage.png)

**Preview and submit your request**

After you have classified the discrepancies remaining on your shipment, click Preview your request, review the information, and, if everything is correct, click Submit Request. The submission will create a case with Selling Partner Support for the products that require research.

After submitting the request, you will be provided with a case number, which will also be shown in the Shipment Reconciliation tool when you next visit the page. You can watch for status updates and communications by clicking on the case number or going directly to your Case Log.

FAQs

- **How do I know if a shipment is eligible for investigation?**

  - Eligibility depends on the status of the shipment and whether it falls within Amazon's investigation window (typically 30–90 days after the shipment is marked as closed). Items eligible for research will have an "Action required" status in the "Contents" tab of the Shipment Summary page.

- **What should I do if I miss the eligibility window for reconciliation?**

  - Once the eligibility window closes, you cannot request an investigation. To avoid this, regularly monitor your shipment statuses and reconcile discrepancies as soon as possible after the shipment is marked as received or closed.

- **What qualifies as proof of inventory ownership?**

  - Proof of inventory ownership includes invoices or packing slips that list the shipped products and quantities. Ensure these documents match your shipping plan to prevent delays in processing reconciliation requests.

- **What should I do if my shipment lacks a stamped proof of delivery (POD)?**

  - If your shipment lacks a stamped POD, contact your carrier to provide the necessary documentation. Without a valid POD, Amazon may deny your reconciliation request.

- **How do I handle discrepancies for products that don’t have an “Action required” status?**

  - Items without an "Action required" status are not eligible for reconciliation. Double-check your shipping plan and ensure you accurately account for all items during the shipment creation process.

- **Can I submit multiple documents for a single research request?**

  - No, you can only upload one document per research request. If you need to submit multiple documents, combine them into a single file in one of the accepted formats (e.g., PDF, JPEG).

- **How can I track the progress of my reconciliation request?**

  - Once submitted, you’ll receive a case number. Track the progress of your request by visiting the Shipment Reconciliation tool or checking your Case Log in Seller Central for updates and communication.

- **What should I do if my reconciliation request is rejected?**

  - If rejected, review the notes in the case details to understand why. Common issues include missing documentation, submitting outside the eligibility window, or incorrect information. Correct any issues and resubmit your request, or contact Selling Partner Support for assistance.

- **How long does it take for Amazon to resolve a reconciliation request?**

  - Reconciliation requests are typically resolved within 7–14 business days. The timeframe may vary based on the complexity of the case and the volume of requests Amazon is handling.

- **What happens if I forget to classify all discrepancies before submitting the request?**

  - The system will not allow you to submit your request until all discrepancies with an “Action required” status have been addressed. Ensure that you classify every product discrepancy before clicking "Submit Request."

- **Can I make changes to a request after submission?**

  - No, you cannot edit a request once it has been submitted. If there’s an error, wait for Amazon to respond to the case and provide additional information or documentation if requested.

- **Can overage units be returned to me instead of being reconciled?**

  - In most cases, overage units are reconciled within Amazon’s system. If they are determined to belong to you, Amazon may add them to your inventory. To have them physically returned, you can create a removal order.

- **What should I do to prevent discrepancies in future shipments?**

  - Follow these best practices:

    - Double-check your shipping plan for accuracy before sending shipments.
    - Use secure packaging and proper labeling.
    - Work with reliable carriers who provide detailed tracking and proof of delivery.
    - Keep thorough documentation, including invoices, packing slips, and proof of delivery.
