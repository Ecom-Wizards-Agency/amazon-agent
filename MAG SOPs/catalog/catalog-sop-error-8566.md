# Catalog SOP: Error 8566

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-error-8566

## **Catalog SOP: Error 8566**

**Who is this for?**

This procedure applies to all Amazon specialists and team members handling product uploads and managing Amazon Seller accounts.

**Objective:**

To provide a clear and concise process for identifying and resolving Amazon error code 8566, ensuring that the issue is addressed efficiently and effectively.

**Error Code Description:**

Error Code 8566 is part of the 8,000 series of Amazon error codes and may arise due to various reasons related to product upload limitations or data inconsistencies.

**Video Reference:**

**Overview**

**1. Identify the Error Code**

- This error typically occurs during product upload processes and indicates issues related to sales history or data mismatches.

**2. Determine the Cause**

Error Code 8566 can be triggered by the following issues:

**a. Insufficient Sales History**

- **Description**: Amazon restricts the number of products a new seller can upload based on their sales history. New sellers with limited sales may encounter this error when attempting to upload a large number of products.
- **Solution**:

**i. Increase Sales:** Focus on driving more sales to build a strong sales history on Amazon.

**ii. Monitor Weekly:** Amazon evaluates sales history weekly, so monitor your progress and try uploading additional products after a week or two.

**iii. Upload Incrementally:** Begin with a smaller number of products to avoid limitations on the number of items you can upload at once.

**b. Incorrect Product Data**

- **Description**: The error may also occur if the feed file contains incorrect product IDs or mismatched brand names. This includes having incorrect UPCs or brand names that do not match the existing Amazon catalog.
- **Solution**:

**i. Verify UPCs**: Ensure that all UPCs are GS1 certified and accurately reflect the correct product.

**ii. Check Brand Names:** Confirm that the brand names in your feed file match the registered brand names associated with the UPCs.

**iii. Update Flat File:** Correct any discrepancies in your flat file or feed file before re-uploading.

**Steps to Resolve Error Code 8566**

1. **Review Error Details:**

- Check the specific details of the error in the Amazon Seller Central account or error report.

1. **Address Insufficient Sales History:**

- Increase sales on the platform to build credibility.
- After a week or two, retry uploading products, starting with a limited number.

1. **Correct Product Data Issues:**

- Verify that the UPCs are GS1 certified and correctly associated with the product.
- Ensure the brand names in the feed file match the registered brand names.
- Correct any errors in the flat file and re-upload it.

1. **Test Uploads:**

- After making corrections, upload a small batch of products to test if the issue is resolved.

1. **Monitor Progress:**

- Track the status of the upload and verify if the error is resolved.

1. **Seek Further Assistance:**

- If the issue persists despite following the above steps, contact Amazon Seller Support for additional help.

FAQs

1. When should I escalate Error 8566 to Amazon Seller Support?

If you've followed all troubleshooting steps—such as increasing sales, verifying UPCs, correcting brand names, and re-uploading the feed—but the error persists for more than two weeks, it's best to contact Amazon Seller Support. Provide detailed information, including error reports, proof of GS1 UPC certification, and screenshots of your brand registry.

2. Does Error 8566 affect existing listings, or only new uploads?

This error primarily affects new product uploads, but in some cases, Amazon may restrict updates to existing listings if the brand name or UPC details do not match Amazon’s records. If your existing products are impacted, check your brand registry and UPC details before making changes.

3. What factors does Amazon consider when increasing a seller’s product upload limit?

Amazon evaluates a seller’s ability to upload products based on:

- Sales Performance: Higher sales volume leads to increased upload limits.
- Account Health: Good standing with no policy violations improves approval chances.
- Fulfillment Metrics: Consistent order fulfillment and low defect rates boost credibility.
- Time on Platform: Older accounts with stable performance are more likely to receive higher limits.

4. Are there alternative ways to list products if I can't use bulk uploads due to this error?

Yes, sellers can try manual product listing via Seller Central by navigating to Catalog > Add a Product and entering details one at a time. If listing restrictions persist, sellers may need to focus on resolving sales history issues before scaling uploads.

5. How can I check if my UPCs are GS1-certified?

To verify UPC authenticity:

- Visit the GS1 Database (https://gepir.gs1.org) and enter your UPC or company name.
- Check the Certificate: If you purchased UPCs from GS1, you should have an official certificate.
- Avoid Third-Party UPCs: Many resold UPCs are invalid and will trigger listing errors.

6. What should I do if the error is due to a brand name mismatch, but my brand name is correct?

If Amazon reports a brand name mismatch, try these steps:

1. Verify Brand Registry: Ensure your brand is registered with Amazon Brand Registry.
2. Check Amazon’s Catalog: Search for similar products and see if Amazon has a different brand name for them.
3. Submit a Case: If your brand is correct, open a case with Amazon Seller Support, providing proof of brand ownership (trademark registration, manufacturer invoices, etc.).

7. How long does it take for Amazon to process changes after correcting errors?

Amazon typically updates product listing changes within 24–48 hours after a successful re-upload. However, brand name corrections and GS1 verification may take up to a week. Sellers should monitor their case logs for updates.

8. How is Error 8566 different from other common Amazon listing errors?

Error 8566 is specifically related to sales history restrictions and product data mismatches. In contrast:

- Error 5461 (Brand Approval Required): Occurs when a seller lists a restricted brand without prior approval.
- Error 8572 (Missing Attributes): Occurs when the provided product ID (ASIN, UPC, EAN, ISBN, or JAN) does not match with the brand or product.
- Error 8058 (UPC Already Exists): Means the UPC is already linked to another product in Amazon’s catalog.
