## **SDS Upload/HAZMAT Issues**

**Who is this for :**This information is intended for Amazon sellers who list products classified as dangerous goods or containing potentially hazardous chemicals, materials, or components. It is crucial for these sellers to comply with Amazon's product safety regulations by accurately uploading Safety Data Sheets (SDS) and exemption sheets on the Manage dangerous goods classification page.

**Objective :**To ensure compliance with Amazon's product safety regulations by accurately uploading and maintaining Safety Data Sheets (SDS) and exemption sheets for products classified as dangerous goods or containing potentially hazardous chemicals, materials, or components, in order to facilitate proper storage, handling, and disposal measures.

**WHAT IS SDS (SAFETY DATA SHEET)?**

A Safety Data Sheet (SDS) is a standardized product safety document, that provides detailed instructions on how to safely handle, label, and store certain products.

**AN SDS ENABLES AMAZON TO:**

- Determine whether your products are regulated as dangerous goods.
- Correctly classify your products as either dangerous goods or non-dangerous goods (the classification determines how products are stored and transported)
- Determine the correct handling and disposal measures if a product spills or leaks at a fulfillment center
- Confirm your products as non-regulated goods

**TO BE COMPLIANT, AN SDS MUST:**

1. Be created or updated within the past five years

2. Include new GHS/CLP hazard identification information

3. Match your product listing

- The listing title (the product name, as it appears on the detail page) is the same as the product name shown on the SDS
- The manufacturer or brand name of your product on the SDS is the same as what appears on the detail page

4. Include all 16 standard SDS sections

**Which products require an SDS?**

Products that contain potentially hazardous chemicals, materials, or components are often classified as dangerous goods. Note that this is not limited to harmful chemicals and heavy metals. Here are a few examples (Not only limited to these):

- Batteries
- Power banks
- Other products containing batteries
- Household cleaning products
- Auto parts
- Skincare products
- Cosmetics
- Essential oils
- Haircare products
- Pet care products
- Certain types of children’s products

As you can see in the list above, most products in contact with human skin or battery-powered devices require an SDS. Further, the product as a whole may be classified as dangerous goods if the device contains certain substances or materials (e.g. essential oils).

**How do I get a Safety Data Sheet (SDS)?**

- In theory, you should receive the SDS from your manufacturer. Many contract manufacturers in China and other countries in Asia don’t know how to issue an SDS. Even if they do, they may not use the correct format and provide an accurate English language translation. Simply because OEM manufacturers are not compliance experts.
- In case your supplier cannot provide an SDS, your only option is to use a third-party Safety Datasheet (SDS) Authoring Service to do it for you. (your supplier expects you to pay for it)

**SDS FORM COMMON MISTAKES CAUSING REJECTION:**

1. The product name or brand on the SDS does not exactly match the product name or brand on the detail page.
2. Information in the hazard identification section and the transportation section conflicts. For example, the hazard identification section states the product is flammable but the transportation section does not.
3. The SDS does not contain all 16 mandatory sections.
4. The product’s flash point is below 140 degrees Fahrenheit but the product is declared as not regulated, instead of flammable, in the transportation section.
5. The SDS is either not dated or is over 5 years old.
6. Uploaded an invalid document instead of an SDS, such as a test report or certification sheet.

**Mandatory 16 Sections of Safety Data Sheet for Amazon FBA:**

**1. The Identification**

- Product Identifier (UPC, EAN, GTIN, etc.)
- Manufacturer or Distributor Name
- Address of Supplier
- Phone/Contact Number of Supplier

**2. Hazard Identification**

- All hazards regarding the product and required label elements
- Hazard Classifications
- Pictograms
- Signal Word
- Hazard Statement

**3. Composition / Information on Ingredients**

- Information on chemical Ingredients and trade secret claims i.e. CAS Number.

**4. First Aid Measures**

- Whether first aid treatment might be required upon exposure to a chemical and the symptoms that indicate exposure.

**5. Fire Fighting Measures**

- The techniques and equipment recommended for extinguishing a fire involve the chemical and hazards that may be created during combustion.

**6. Accidental Release Measures**

- Steps to take in the event of a spill or release involving the chemical. Includes
- Emergency Procedure
- Protective Equipment
- The proper method for containment and clean-up

**7. Handling and Storage**

- Precautions for safe handling and storage, including incompatibilities.

**8. Exposure Control / Personal Protection**

- OSHA’s permissible exposure limit (PELs)
- Threshold limit values (TLVs)
- Appropriate engineering controls (AECs)
- Personal Protective Equipment (PPEs)

**9. Physical and Chemical Properties**

- The mention of chemical & physical characteristics i.e. Color, Odor, Matter, melting or boiling points of the substance, etc.

**10. Stability and Reactivity**

- Chemical Stability and possible hazardous reactions.

**11. Toxicological Information**

- Routes of exposure (Inhalation, Ingestion, or absorption contact)
- Symptoms
- Acute and Chronic effects
- Numerical measures of toxicity

**12. Ecological Information**

- How the chemical might affect the environment and the duration of the effect.

**13. Disposal Considerations**

- Have to describe the safe handling of wastes and methods of disposal, including the disposal of any contaminated packaging.

**14. Transportation Information**

- Packing
- Marking
- Labeling requirement for hazardous chemical shipments.

**15. Regulatory Information**

- Indicates regulations that apply to chemicals.

**16. Other Information**

- Includes the date of the revision or last revision and other information.

**EXEMPTION SHEETS:**

For products that don’t require an SDS, an exemption sheet is acceptable for these two categories only:

- Battery and battery-powered products
- Products without harmful chemicals

**Battery and battery-powered products:**Review the product packaging, the product manual, or the battery itself to find specific battery information such as watt-hours and voltage.

**Products without harmful chemicals:** Review the product packaging or the product itself to find specific information such as ingredients, pictograms, or warning phrases.

**NOTE:**If you submit an exemption sheet for products not in these two categories, it will be rejected. Be sure to fill in your exemption sheet completely and correctly to ensure that it’s accepted.

**Battery and Dangerous Goods Information at Listing Creation**

When listing a new product for FBA or converting an existing ASIN, Amazon requires complete battery and dangerous goods classification to prevent delays or blocked listings.

You must complete this section when prompted in Seller Central (via **“Add dangerous goods information”**):

**⚠️** **Required Fields:**

| Field Name | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Are batteries required? | Select Yes if the product requires or includes batteries. |
| Are batteries included? | Select Yes if batteries are in the product, packaged with it, or sold alongside it. |
| Is the battery a button cell? | Select Yes if the battery meets the button/coin cell definition (e.g., LR44, CR2032). |
| Has multiple battery-powered components? | Select Yes if more than one component has batteries (e.g., toy and remote). |
| Battery Cell Composition | Choose from Lithium-Ion, Alkaline, NiMH, etc. |
| Battery Packaging | Indicate if the battery is Contained in equipment, Packed with, or Standalone. |
| Battery Weight & Unit | Enter battery net weight and specify the unit (g, kg, oz, lb). |
| Number of Batteries & Type | Enter the quantity and battery type. Include spare batteries. |
| Is battery non-spillable? | Select Yes only if certified under transportation regulations. |
| Battery contains free unabsorbed liquid? | Select Yes if applicable (typically NO for most sealed batteries). |
| Lithium Battery Energy Content (Wh) | Required for lithium batteries. Use: Wh = Voltage × Ah |
| Non-Lithium Battery Energy Content | Required if not lithium. Use manufacturer specs. |
| Number of Lithium-ion or Metal Cells | Enter number of cells if not encased in a battery. |
| Has <30% charge | Optional. If shipping by air, must answer Yes to be eligible. |
| Is OEM sourced? | Select Yes if the product is from its original manufacturer. |
| Is battery replaceable? | Select Yes if end user can replace the battery. |
| Battery IEC Code | Optional, but enter if available (e.g., CR2032, LR44). |

FAQs

**1. How do I know if my product requires an SDS?**

**Answer:**Products that contain hazardous chemicals, materials, or components are typically classified as dangerous goods and require an SDS. Here are some general categories:

- Batteries and battery-powered products (including power banks)
- Household cleaning products (e.g., cleaners with toxic chemicals or solvents)
- Personal care items (like skincare, cosmetics, and essential oils that contain chemicals)
- Auto parts (that may contain hazardous materials or components)
- Pet care products (that contain chemicals)
- Certain children’s products (if they contain hazardous materials)

If your product contains chemicals, heavy metals, flammable substances, or other hazardous ingredients, it will likely need an SDS. Always verify with your manufacturer or check the product’s safety regulations.

**2. What happens if I upload an incorrect or incomplete SDS?**

**Answer:**Uploading an incorrect or incomplete SDS can result in several issues, including:

- Rejection of your product listing: If Amazon detects discrepancies between the SDS and the product listing (e.g., mismatched product names, outdated information), your product could be delisted.
- Account health penalties: Multiple SDS rejections can negatively impact your account’s health, potentially leading to a suspension of your listings or your seller account.
- Delayed processing: Incorrect SDS submissions can slow down the approval process, which could delay product shipping or cause issues during fulfillment.
- To avoid these problems, double-check your SDS for accuracy before uploading and ensure all required sections are included.

**3. What format should my SDS be in when I upload it?**

**Answer:**Amazon generally accepts SDS documents in PDF format. This is the most common format and ensures the document is readable and secure. However, you may encounter situations where your SDS is in a different format (e.g., DOCX, DOC), and you will need to convert it to PDF before uploading it.

Additionally, always ensure that the SDS is in English, as Amazon requires it to be in the language of the marketplace you're selling in. If your SDS is only available in another language, consider using a certified translation service or request a translated copy from your supplier.

**4. What should I do if my manufacturer cannot provide an SDS?**

**Answer:**If your manufacturer cannot provide an SDS, here’s what you can do:

- Contact a third-party SDS authoring service: There are many companies that specialize in creating SDS documents. These services typically cost between $100 to $300 per SDS, depending on the complexity of your product. Look for reputable companies by researching online or asking for recommendations from industry groups.
- Work with your supplier: If your supplier cannot provide the SDS, they may have alternative solutions or can guide you on how to acquire one.
- Ensure Compliance: If you have to create an SDS through a third-party service, make sure all 16 sections are included, and the information matches your product and listing accurately. This will prevent rejection by Amazon.
- Once the SDS is created, upload it according to the guidelines in your Seller Central account.

**5. How do I fill out an exemption sheet, and what information should it include?**

**Answer:**Exemption sheets are used for products that don’t require an SDS. They are typically accepted for two categories:

- Battery and battery-powered products: This includes information such as the battery's watt-hour (Wh) rating, voltage, and whether the battery is rechargeable.
- Products without harmful chemicals: These products should not contain hazardous materials and should be free of chemical substances that require regulatory handling.
- Make sure your exemption sheet is completed correctly and includes:
- Product Name and ASIN.
- Product Details: Provide any relevant information that proves the product does not contain harmful chemicals (e.g., ingredients, packaging information).
- Certification Statement: This should state that the product does not fall under hazardous goods and does not require an SDS.
- If Amazon rejects your exemption sheet, double-check the information you’ve included. If the product falls outside of the exemption categories, you may need to provide an SDS instead.

**6. What should I do if my SDS or exemption sheet is rejected?**

**Answer:**If your SDS or exemption sheet is rejected by Amazon, follow these steps:

- Review the Rejection Message: Carefully read the rejection notice from Amazon to identify why your document was rejected. Common reasons include mismatched product names, missing sections, outdated SDS, or incorrect product classification.
- Correct the Issue: Once you identify the reason for rejection, take the necessary steps to resolve the issue. This might involve updating your SDS, correcting the product name, or providing a new exemption sheet.
- Re-upload the Correct Document: After making corrections, upload the updated SDS or exemption sheet. If you're unsure about the accuracy, consult a third-party SDS authoring service or your supplier.
- Appeal If Necessary: If you believe the rejection was a mistake, you can appeal through Amazon Seller Support. Provide all necessary documentation and a detailed explanation of why your submission should be accepted.
- Be proactive in ensuring that your SDS meets Amazon's requirements to avoid repeat rejections.

**7. Can I use an SDS from a previous product listing, or do I need a new one each time?**

**Answer:**If you are re-listing the same product, you can use an existing SDS, but it must meet Amazon's requirements:

- The SDS must be current (created or updated within the last five years).
- The product details on the SDS must match the information in your listing, including the product name and manufacturer.
- If the product has undergone changes (e.g., new ingredients, packaging), you will need to update the SDS to reflect those changes.
- If the SDS is outdated or doesn't match the product details, Amazon will likely reject it.

**8. How do I track the status of my SDS submission?**

**Answer:**To track the status of your SDS or exemption sheet, follow these steps:

- Go to Seller Central and search for SDS in the search bar.
- Click on Upload and track the status of your safety data sheets (SDS) and exemption sheets.
- Here, you will see the status of your uploaded documents. If they are approved, you will receive confirmation. If rejected, the system will provide details on why it was rejected.
- Monitor this page to ensure that all your products meet Amazon's safety regulations and that your SDS is up-to-date.
