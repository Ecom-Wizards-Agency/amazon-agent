## **A/B Test (Product Description)**

**Who is this for :** Individuals who wished to conduct A/B split testing within seller central.

**Objective :** The purpose of this document is to provide a step-by-step guide on how to create A/B split testing to submit alternate content for your products to collect insights such as traffic and conversion.

Split testing or AB testing, on Amazon, is an excellent way of optimizing your listings through experiments with product content, images, and pricing. Four crucial elements of the listing affect conversion rate: Image, Title, Product Features, Description, and Price.

**Seller Central A/B Testing**

Access the Amazon Seller Central page by clicking the [URL](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/):

- Go to Brands, then select Manage Experiments.

- On the experiments dashboard, click Create a New Experiment.

  - You can run an experiment for A+ Content, Bullet Points, Main Image, Product Description, and Product Title.

**Product Description A/B Testing**

- If the ASIN is part of a variation, you will need to select first the whole parentage as the reference ASIN.

  ![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/ab-test-product-description-image-3.png)

  ![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/whKimage.png)

Enter a descriptive test name, duration, and desired start date. You may also enter an optional hypothesis around what you wish to learn from the test.

- Experiment Name
- Hypothesis - Brief description of the experiment and the desired outcome.
- Duration - Minimum of 4 weeks to a maximum of 10 weeks.

  - Start Date will be automatically set 1 week after you created the experiment, but you can still set it at a later date.
  - End Date will be automatically set depending on the duration of the experiment.
- You can tick To significance which automatically ends the experiment when there is enough time data to declare winner.
- You can also set it to Automatically publish once the experiment is done.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/ab-test-product-description-image-4.jpg)

- Uncheck all other child ASINs that are not part of the experiment.

  - When you run an experiment on a variation family, you can submit an experiment for one or more of the child ASINs, but you always have to submit for the parent ASIN.
- Type in the new product description in the Version B section of the ASIN involved.
- Once all necessary fields are filled up, click Schedule Experiment.
- Click View Details to check the experiment's current status.

FAQs

**1. What is an A/B Split Test (Experiment) in Amazon Seller Central?**

- An A/B Split Test, or experiment, in Amazon Seller Central allows you to test different versions of product content (such as images, titles, descriptions, etc.) to determine which one performs better in terms of conversion rate, traffic, and sales. The goal is to optimize your product listings by comparing the performance of alternate content or strategies.

**2. What is the step-by-step process for creating an A/B test experiment in Seller Central?**

- Here’s a quick breakdown:

  - Go to Manage Experiments under the Brands section in Seller Central.
  - Click Create a New Experiment on the experiments dashboard.
  - Select what you wish to test (e.g., Product Title, Description, Main Image, etc.).
  - Fill in the experiment name, hypothesis, duration, and start date.
  - If testing a product description, input the new content in Version B.
  - Select which ASIN(s) will be included in the test (be sure to choose the parent ASIN if testing a variation family).
  - Click Schedule Experiment to launch it.

**3. Do I need special permissions to create an A/B test in Seller Central?**

- Yes, you need to have the appropriate permissions in Amazon Seller Central to create and manage experiments. Typically, this would be available to account owners, administrators, or users with roles that allow access to the “Manage Experiments” feature.

**4. How long should I run an A/B test?**

- The ideal duration for an A/B test is generally between 4 to 10 weeks. However, the length can depend on your product's sales volume and the category it belongs to. If your product has low traffic, you may need a longer test duration to gather sufficient data for meaningful insights.

**5. What is the advantage of using the “Automatically Publish” feature after an A/B test?**

- The "Automatically Publish" feature publishes the winning variation of your experiment once a statistically significant result is reached. This feature is useful if you want to quickly implement the best-performing option without manually reviewing and applying the results yourself. However, if you prefer more control over the decision, you can opt not to use this feature and review the results first before making any changes.

**6. Will my product listing be visible to customers during the A/B test?**

- Yes, both the original version (Version A) and the alternate content (Version B) are visible to different segments of customers during the test. Amazon will rotate between these versions to ensure that each is shown to a sufficient number of customers to gather reliable data. Customers may not notice the difference, as the variations are typically subtle, like changes to images, titles, or descriptions.

**7. How do I handle variations (child ASINs) in an A/B test?**

- If your product is part of a variation family (i.e., it has multiple child ASINs), you must always select the parent ASIN as the reference for your experiment. You can choose to test one or more child ASINs, but the parent ASIN must always be included in the experiment to ensure consistency across variations.

**8. How do I analyze the results of my A/B test?**

- Once the experiment is complete, you can view the results in the Experiments Dashboard in Seller Central. Look for key metrics like conversion rate, click-through rate, and overall sales to determine which version of the listing performed better. The winning variant should ideally show a statistically significant improvement in your desired metric(s).

**9. What should I do if my A/B test doesn’t show clear results?**

- If the experiment results are inconclusive or show no significant difference, consider extending the test duration or modifying the variations you're testing. Sometimes, factors like seasonal trends, marketing campaigns, or external events can affect the data, so it may require further experimentation.

**10. What happens if I don’t choose the “To Significance” option for the experiment?**

- If you do not select the “To Significance” option, the experiment will run for the duration you specify, regardless of whether enough data has been collected to declare a winner. Enabling “To Significance” ensures that the test will automatically conclude as soon as a statistically significant result is reached, saving you time and effort.

**11. Can I make changes to my product listings during the A/B test?**

- It’s generally advised not to make other changes to your product listings (e.g., pricing or images) during the test. Introducing additional variables could distort the results and make it harder to attribute any performance changes to the A/B test itself.
