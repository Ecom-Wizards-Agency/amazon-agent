# Catalog SOP: Unenroll from the Remote Fulfillment Program (NARF)

Source: https://sop.myamazonguy.com/books/catalog/page/catalog-sop-unenroll-from-the-remote-fulfillment-program-narf

## **Unenroll from the Remote Fulfillment Program (NARF)**

**Who is this for :** Intended for Amazon sellers who want to opt-out with the Remote Fulfillment program or NARF.

**Objective :** This SOP will show you the step-by-step process of eliminating remote fulfillment on certain marketplaces or ASINs. Unenroll a marketplace to remote fulfillment.

**UPDATE: Products made of wood or similar materials require authorization from SAGARPA (Secretaría de Agricultura, Ganadería, Desarrollo Rural, Pesca y Alimentación) and PROFEPA (Procuraduría Federal de Protección al Ambiente) to be imported into Mexico. This requirement applies to all Cross Border programs, including NARF, Global Store, and Core Exports. Currently, there is no available pathway to enable wood tray products for the Mexico marketplace through Remote Fulfillment with FBA.**

1. Sign in to Seller Central.

2. Go to Manage your Remote Fulfillment by clicking this [link](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/rafn/inventory). The landing page will look like this below.

3. Click **“Marketplace Enrollment”** in the upper left-hand portion of the screen.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-1712332049033.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/Vb5image.png)

4. Clear the check mark of the marketplace you wish to opt-out from then hit Update.

![image](https://files.document360.io/d9e9b7a2-6758-4c75-97d3-36a6877048cf/Images/Documentation/image-N5HYYTK7.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/CdNimage.png)

5. Wait for 24 hours to fully update account settings.

**How to unenroll ASIN to NARF (option 1)**

1. Refer to step 2 of **unenrolling to NARF by Marketplace**. Click the Generate Report button and then Download Report

Note: Depending on the account listings this may take up to 24 hours

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/yySimage.png)

2. After downloading the NARF report, you may disable ELIGIBLE ASINs by filling in “NO” under the target marketplace you wish to opt-out.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/4Flimage.png)

3. Save the NARF Report → Choose File → Upload

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/vZwimage.png)

**The uploaded NARF Report will show the following:**

- Date uploaded
- File name
- Upload status - if still processing
- Results - if it's successful or with errors encountered

Note: Catalog may take up to 24 hours to update

**How to unenroll ASIN to NARF (option 2)**

(If the account’s NARF was set up through BIL or Build International Listing)

1. Go to Inventory and then select "Sell Globally". This may also appear on your account's home page if bookmarked.

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/GjEimage.png)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/TlWimage.png)

2. On the "Build International Listings" page, you will find the product exclusions section on the right side of the page.

3. Click View and Edit all Exclusions

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/s4aimage.png)

4. Make sure you are on the right marketplace → On the text box, fill in the SKU you wish to opt-out from the target marketplace → Exclude Offers

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/JNSimage.png)

5. If the Exclusion of offers is successful, SKU will be inactive on the target marketplace and will appear as OOS (Out of Stock)

![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/iYKimage.png)

FAQs

**1. What is the Remote Fulfillment Program (NARF)?**

- The Remote Fulfillment Program allows sellers to list FBA products in Amazon's international marketplaces without maintaining inventory in those locations. It uses your domestic inventory to fulfill international orders.

**2. Why would a seller unenroll from NARF?**

- Sellers may choose to unenroll due to:

  - Ineligibility of certain ASINs for international fulfillment.
  - High shipping costs reducing profitability.
  - Low sales volume in specific marketplaces.
  - Changes in inventory or marketplace strategy.

**3. What happens when I unenroll from a marketplace in NARF?**

- Unenrolling from a marketplace disables remote fulfillment for that region. Products will no longer appear available in the target marketplace and will not be fulfilled internationally from your domestic inventory.

**4. How long does it take for changes to reflect after unenrolling from NARF?**

- It may take up to 24 hours for account settings and catalog updates to reflect after unenrolling.

**5. Can I unenroll only specific ASINs from NARF without removing an entire marketplace?**

- Yes, you can unenroll specific ASINs by:

  - Downloading the NARF report.
  - Marking “NO” under the target marketplace for the ASINs you wish to opt-out.
  - Uploading the updated NARF report.

**6. What should I do if the NARF report takes longer than expected to generate?**

- If the report doesn’t generate within the usual time frame:

  - Refresh the Manage Your Remote Fulfillment page.
  - Check your internet connection.
  - Contact Amazon Seller Support if the issue persists.

**7. What is the difference between marketplace unenrollment and ASIN-specific unenrollment?**

- Marketplace Unenrollment: Disables remote fulfillment for all ASINs in a particular marketplace.
- ASIN-Specific Unenrollment: Removes only selected ASINs from being fulfilled in specific marketplaces.

**8. Can I re-enroll in NARF after unenrolling?**

- Yes, you can re-enroll by going to the Marketplace Enrollment section, selecting the desired marketplace, and updating your settings.

**9. How do I know if my NARF report upload was successful?**

- After uploading the NARF report, check the Upload Status and Results fields in the upload history. If errors are encountered, download the error report, correct the issues, and re-upload the file.

**10. How do I confirm that an ASIN has been successfully unenrolled?**

- Once the ASIN is unenrolled:

  - It will no longer appear as available in the target marketplace.
  - It may show as Out of Stock (OOS) in the excluded marketplace.

**11. What should I do if the ASIN is still active after unenrollment?**

- If the ASIN remains active after 24 hours:

  - Verify that the NARF report was uploaded successfully.
  - Double-check the report to ensure the ASIN was marked as “NO” for the correct marketplace.
  - Contact Amazon Seller Support if the issue persists.

**12. How does Build International Listings (BIL) affect NARF unenrollment?**

- If NARF was set up using BIL, unenrollment must be managed through the Sell Globally section in Vendor Central. You can exclude offers by SKU for specific marketplaces in this section.

**13. What happens if I unenroll an ASIN from NARF but forget to exclude it in Build International Listings (BIL)?**

- If BIL exclusions are not updated, the ASIN may still appear active in the target marketplace. Always ensure SKUs are excluded in both the NARF report and BIL settings.

**14. Can I unenroll multiple ASINs at once?**

- Yes, use the NARF report to bulk update multiple ASINs. Mark the target marketplaces as “NO” for all ASINs you wish to unenroll, save the file, and upload it.

**15. Are there any restrictions on which ASINs can be unenrolled from NARF?**

- Generally, all ASINs can be unenrolled unless restricted by Amazon policies or if they are already inactive in the target marketplace.

**16. How can I avoid errors when uploading the NARF report?**

- To minimize errors:

  - Ensure the file is saved in the correct format (e.g., .xlsx).
  - Double-check that the changes are correctly marked under the appropriate columns.
  - Address any required attributes highlighted in the report.

**17. Will unenrolling from NARF affect my domestic listings?**

- No, unenrolling from NARF only affects remote fulfillment in international marketplaces. Domestic listings and fulfillment remain unaffected.

**18. Can I unenroll from all NARF marketplaces at once?**

- Yes, you can unenroll from all marketplaces by clearing the check marks for all regions in the Marketplace Enrollment section.

**19. How do I verify if an account’s NARF setup uses BIL?**

- To check if BIL is used:

  - Navigate to Inventory > Sell Globally.
  - Look for the Build International Listings section.
  - If BIL is active, exclusions must be managed through this interface.

**20. What support is available if I encounter issues with NARF unenrollment?**

- If you encounter issues:

  - Review the NARF report or BIL settings for errors.
  - Refer to the error messages in the upload status.
  - Contact Amazon Seller Support for assistance with unresolved problems.
