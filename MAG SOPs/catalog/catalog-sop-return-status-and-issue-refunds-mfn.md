## Return Status and Issue Refunds (MFN)

**Who is this for?** This is for customers who have questions or concerns related to refunds and returns of products or services they have purchased.

**Objective:** To efficiently and accurately handle return requests and issue refunds to ensure customer satisfaction and maintain a positive brand reputation.

**Instructions:**

1. Log in to [Seller Central Account](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/) and go to Orders> Manage Returns

2. Paste the Order ID and Search, Note that you can modify the Date range, and Sort By.

[![image.png](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/scaled-1680-/3xQimage.png)](https://sop.myamazonguy.com/uploads/images/gallery/2025-08/3xQimage.png)

3. Check the return Details, Return Status and Available Action
Note: You must respond to return requests that require manual authorization within 24 hours. You are required to issue a refund within two business days of receipt of a return. If you do not take action regarding the refund, Amazon may refund the buyer on your behalf and charge the amount to your seller account. In addition, if you refuse to accept the return of an item and Amazon determines that it is materially different, you might be held responsible for any A-to-Z Guarantee claim or chargeback dispute that is filed.

**Return Auto-Authorization:** Amazon automatically authorizes US return requests that fall within Amazon’s return policy, including items with seller-filed SKU exemptions from the Prepaid returns for seller-fulfilled orders. Return requests that are out-of-policy or category-exempt will be sent for manual authorization. For more information, go to Authorize a return request.

**Prepaid return labels:** Amazon automatically enrolls US sellers in the Prepaid returns for seller-fulfilled orders. In this program, we provide buyers with prepaid return shipping labels on your behalf through Use Buy Shipping services.

R**eturn labels for exempt items:** For returns of seller-fulfilled SKUs that are exempt from the Prepaid Returns Label program, you will have the option to upload a merchant-paid label. We recommend that these merchant-paid labels include tracking. Go to Return Settings in Seller Central to select the return label you want to provide for exempt items.

**Wrong item returned by buyers**: Sometimes buyers mistakenly return a different item than the one they purchased from you. If this happens, contact the buyer to do the following:

- Let them know that they may have accidentally returned the wrong item.
- Ask them if they would like you to return the item to them.
- Inform them that they can return the correct item for a refund.

If the buyer indicates that they would like the wrong item back, consider sending it back to them. If the buyer indicates that they received the wrong item initially, consider giving them the benefit of the doubt.

Please make sure that the item was returned to the warehouse or to your client before processing any refunds. Once the return has been confirmed by the client, advise them to check the condition of the item so that they can decide whether they process a full or partial refund.

FAQs

**1. How do I check the status of a return request?**

- Log in to Seller Central, navigate to Orders > Manage Returns, and enter the Order ID in the search bar. Adjust the date range and sort by your preference to locate the request. Review the return details, status, and available actions.

**2. What is the time frame for responding to return requests?**

- You must respond to return requests requiring manual authorization within 24 hours. Failure to do so may result in Amazon refunding the buyer on your behalf and charging your seller account.

**3. When should I issue a refund?**

- Refunds must be processed within two business days of receiving a return. Ensure the item is confirmed returned and inspected by the warehouse or client before issuing a refund.

**4. What happens if the wrong item is returned by the buyer?**

- Step 1: Contact the buyer to inform them of the mistake.
- Step 2: Ask if they want the incorrect item returned to them.
- Step 3: Allow them to return the correct item for a refund.

  - If the buyer claims they initially received the wrong item, consider giving them the benefit of the doubt when deciding how to proceed.

**5. Can I charge a restocking fee for returns?**

- Yes, you may charge a 10% restocking fee in the following cases:

  - The buyer states the item was "purchased by mistake" or "no longer needed."
  - The packaging was altered or changed.
  - For damaged items, missing parts, or items returned outside the return period, partial refunds may apply.

**6. How are prepaid return labels handled?**

- Amazon automatically provides buyers with prepaid return labels for US sellers enrolled in the Prepaid Returns Program. For exempt SKUs, you can upload a merchant-paid label via Return Settings in Seller Central.

**7. What should I do if the returned item is damaged or incomplete?**

- Inspect the returned item once received. If it is damaged, incomplete, or does not meet the return policy conditions, you can issue a partial refund or charge a restocking fee. Ensure accurate documentation for any deductions.

**8. How do I issue a refund in Seller Central?**

- Step 1: Confirm the item was returned using tracking information.
- Step 2: Navigate to the return request, click Issue Refund, and select the appropriate refund reason.
- Step 3: Click Submit Refund to finalize the process.

**9. What happens if I don’t process a refund on time?**

- If you do not issue a refund within two business days of receiving the return, Amazon may refund the buyer on your behalf and charge the amount to your seller account.

**10. Can I deny a return request?**

- You can deny a return request if it falls outside of Amazon’s return policy. However, ensure proper communication with the buyer and provide valid reasons for the denial. Keep in mind that denying a valid return could lead to an A-to-Z Guarantee claim or chargeback dispute.

**11. How do I handle return requests for items exempt from the Prepaid Returns Label program?**

- For exempt items, upload a merchant-paid return label in Return Settings. Ensure the label includes tracking information to verify the return.

**12. What should I do if a buyer claims they didn’t receive a prepaid return label?**

- Confirm whether the return is eligible for the Prepaid Returns Program. If eligible, advise the buyer to check their email or Amazon account for the label. For exempt items, provide a merchant-paid return label.
