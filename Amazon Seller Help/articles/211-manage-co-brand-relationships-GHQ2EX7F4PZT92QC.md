# Manage co-brand relationships


Amazon Brand Registry provides a co-brand relationships feature that enables brand Administrators to record and manage their co-brand relationships. A co-brand relationship exists when a single product displays multiple brand identities, combining the names, logos, or other brand elements of different companies. By maintaining co-brand relationship information, Brand Registry helps protect brand integrity and builds customer trust in legitimately licensed products.

Note: Co-brand relationships and selling roles serve different purposes. Learn more about these differences in the FAQ section below.

The use of the co-brand relationships feature provides:

Brand protection: Products with multiple legitimate brands (like "LEGO Star Wars") remain active and accurately represented, while unauthorized usage is prevented.
License authentication: The system validates authorized manufacturers and confirms the legitimacy of licensed merchandise.
Enhanced shopping experience: Brand partnership data makes it easier for customers to discover relevant products.
Co-brand relationships features

Record new or missing co-brand relationships when licensees report impacted Amazon Standard Identification Numbers (ASINs) View all co-brand relationships and their history in a comprehensive dashboard
Select multiple brands under Your Brand and multiple Target Brands in a single co-brand relationship submission
Add context notes before submitting relationship requests using the Additional Information field
Process multiple incoming co-brand relationships simultaneously using bulk actions on the Incoming Relationships tab
Manage relationships with dedicated controls
Create new co-brand relationships
Review, accept, or reject incoming relationship requests
Withdraw outgoing requests before confirmation
Terminate co-brand relationships when necessary

How to use the Co-brand Relationships feature

In Brand Registry, navigate to Manage > Manage brand relationships > Co-brand relationships

The landing page will display a high-level overview of existing co-brand relationships for all brands for which the user is a brand Administrator.
Click Record co-brand relationship and start filling out the following fields:

Required

Your brand(s): As brand Administrator, you will choose your brand registered in Brand Registry from the drop-down. You can select multiple brands from the Your Brand dropdown. A count of selected brands will appear at the top of the dropdown. To remove a brand, you can click the x icon next to its name
Your classification in this co-brand relationship: This selection determines your classification in the co-brand relationship. As the brand Administrator, you must specify whether you are granting permission to another brand to use your brand’s intellectual property (IP), or if you are requesting authorization from another brand to use their brand IP. This choice defines which direction the authorization flows in this co-brand relationship.
Target brands: Enter the name of the brand you want to establish a relationship within the search box. As you type, matching brands will appear in a drop-down list. Click “Select” next to the correct brand name when you see it. You can add multiple target brands by performing multiple searches (up to 50 brands). Each selected brand is added to your list, and the total count of selected target brands will be displayed. To remove a target brand, click the x icon next to its name.
Stores: Select from the drop-down menu the stores where this co-brand relationship applies.

Optional

Target brand contact for licensing: Provide the email address of the point of contact with knowledge of this relationship from the target brand. A request for validation of this relationship will be sent to the brand Administrators of the target brand, and by entering this brand contact you’ll ensure they can validate the relationship within their organization.
Validity dates: These dates define the official time period during which the co-brand relationship is recorded.
Additional Information: Provide additional details about your licensing arrangement that will help the target brand understand and evaluate this co-brand relationship request.
Once all required fields are filled out, click “Record” to submit the co-brand relationship request.
A confirmation screen will inform you that your co-brand relationship request has been sent to the target brand Administrator for review.

How to use the Recommended tab

The Recommended tab displays potential co-brand relationships that Amazon has identified based on your Amazon activity.

To review recommended relationships:

From the landing page, click a brand to access the brand profile
Click the "Recommended" tab
Review the potential relationships Amazon has identified
Choose either “Review and record” or “Dismiss” for each recommendation

For “Review and record”

You will be directed to the Record co-brand relationship page
Follow the instructions provided in How to use the Co-brand Relationships feature section, steps 2-4

For “Dismiss”

The brand will be removed from your Recommended tab
To add the brand later, follow the process in How to use the Co-brand Relationships feature section

Important Notes:

Recommended relationships appear on both potential target and potential source brands' lists
If one brand dismisses a recommendation, it will disappear from on both (target and source) brands' lists
If one brand records the co-brand relationship, it will appear in the other brand's "Incoming requests" tab for review
Recommendations will remain in the list until you take action (either "Review and record" or "Dismiss")

Next steps after submitting the relationship record

Once the co-brand relationship request is submitted, the Administrators on the target brand will receive a consolidated email summarizing co-brand relationship activities, including your request.

Within the request email, the brand Administrator needs to review the request by following the steps provided in the email.

Sign in to Brand Registry
Navigate to Manage > Manage brand relationships > Co-brand relationships
Locate this request in the “Incoming requests” tab
Review the incoming request list and select one or multiple requests (up to 50 requests) to accept or reject the request
Click "Accept" or "Reject" to take action on the selected co-brand relationship requests

Important Notes:

Licensing relationships are not active until a target brand Administrator accepts
Requests can be withdrawn if status is "Pending Confirmation"
Co-brand relationship requests expire in 30 days and will be automatically closed if no action is taken
Once accepted, relationships can only be terminated, not withdrawn
Audit history is available to review existing relationships

How to terminate or withdraw a co-brand relationship

There are two ways to end a co-brand relationship depending on the relationship status:

Terminating an active relationship

A relationship can only be terminated when it is in an “Active” status.

To terminate an active relationship:

Navigate to Brand Registry > Manage > Brand relationships > Manage co-brand relationships > Co-brand relationships tab
Locate the relationship with "Active" status
Select one or multiple relationships (up to 50) with "Active" status to terminate
Two options to terminate:

Option 1 (Single selection):

Click “View Details”
Click “Terminate co-brand relationship”

Option 2 (Single or bulk selection):

Select all active relationships you would like to terminate (up to 50)
Click "Terminate relationship" at the bottom of the page

Once completed, the status will change from "Active" to "Terminated"

Withdrawing a pending relationship

Withdrawal is only available for relationships with "Pending target brand confirmation" status.

To withdraw a pending relationship:

Go to Brand Registry > Manage > Brand Relationships > Manage co-brand licensing > Co-brand relationships tab
Select all pending relationships you would like to withdraw (up to 50)

Click "Withdraw request" in the action bar that appears at the bottom of the page

FAQ
What is the difference between co-branding and selling roles?

Co-brand relationships occur when a single product features multiple brand identities—combining the names, logos, or other brand elements of different companies (for example, LEGO × Star Wars collaborations). The Co-brand Relationships feature within Brand Registry allows brand Administrators to record, withdraw, accept, and terminate active co-brand relationships. This helps protect legitimate co-branded products from incorrect suppressions and promotes a more accurate, transparent brand experience across Amazon stores.

In contrast, selling roles define the relationship between a selling partner and a brand. A selling role can be assigned to a selling partner account through a Merchant Token or Account ID, establishing that account’s official relationship with the brand. Once assigned, the selling role determines eligibility for additional brand-specific benefits and listing privileges.

In summary, co-branding and selling roles serve different purposes:

Co-branding manages partnerships between multiple brands featured on a single product ( for example, LEGO × Star Wars).
Selling roles manage a selling partner’s relationship with a single brand, governing their privileges and access within Amazon.

For more information on selling roles, go to Brand Registry selling roles.

How can I view my licensing relationship?

You can view all co-brand relationships associated with your brand in the co-brand relationships table in Manage Co-brand relationships within Brand Registry. Here, you can see your classification as either Licensor or Licensee, check validity start and end dates, and monitor the status of relationships (active, withdrawn, pending, terminated, expired, or rejected).

To view your licensing relationships:

Sign in to Brand Registry and navigate to Manage > Brand relationships > Co-brand relationships.
On the landing page, you'll find the Co-brand relationships tab where you can search for your brand to view the relationships.

How do I view the audit log for a brand relationship?

To view audit information for an individual relationship:

In the co-brand relationships tab, locate the relationship you want to review
In the Action column, select “View Details”
A side panel window will appear displaying the licensing brand information, your classification, your brand, validity date, status, and action history

What communication will I receive about my co-brand relationship status?

Brand Registry will send the brand Administrator for both parties an email communication in these cases:

Status changes:

When a pending request is accepted or rejected
When a pending request is withdrawn or expires
When an active relationship expires or is terminated

What happens if I make a mistake in my relationship request?

If you make a mistake in your co-brand relationship request, you have two options depending on the current status of the request:

For pending requests: You can withdraw the request and create a new one with the correct information.
For active relationships: You'll need to terminate the existing relationship and create a new one with the correct details.

What happens if I am unable to find the target brand? OR What happens if the target brand is not a registered brand?

If you cannot locate your target brand in Brand Registry, there are two possible scenarios:

The brand name may be spelled or formatted differently in Brand Registry than what you're searching for. Double-check the spelling, try different versions of the brand name in the search field, and make sure you're using the official brand name rather than abbreviations.
The target brand may not be registered in Amazon Brand Registry. In this case:
The brand will need to enroll in Brand Registry before you can establish a co-brand relationship
To complete the registration, refer to the "Amazon Brand Registry Application Guide" brochure: Resource available in English (US), English (UK), English (IN), Spanish, French, German, Italian, Japanese, and Chinese.
Once they complete their registration, you can proceed with recording the co-brand relationship

What happens if I do not know the email contact?

A valid email contact for the target brand is required to complete the co-brand relationship application. We recommend reaching out to the brand directly to identify the appropriate point of contact before beginning your application. The email field is mandatory and cannot be skipped in the submission process.

Can I select multiple brands when recording a co-brand relationship?

Yes. You can select multiple brands under Your Brand and add multiple Target Brands in a single submission. A count of selected brands will be displayed, and you can remove any brand by clicking the x icon next to its name.

What is the Additional Information field and when should I use it?

The Additional Information field is an optional free text field that allows you to add context or relevant information about the co-brand relationship before submitting. This note is visible to the target brand's Administrator during review.

How does bulk processing work on the Incoming Relationships tab?

You can filter relationships by status and process multiple relationships at once using the available bulk actions — Accept, Reject, or Terminate. Terminated relationships are greyed out and excluded from bulk selection. After a bulk action is completed, the tab automatically refreshes to reflect the updated relationship statuses.
