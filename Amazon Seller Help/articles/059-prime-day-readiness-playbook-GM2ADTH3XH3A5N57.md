Ahead of Prime Day 2026, our experts shared their tips and tool recommendations to help you prepare. Use this playbook to find important information about timelines, deal-sourcing best practices, inventory management, shipping, and storage.
Section 1: Understand the key dates

Here are the dates you’ll want to know to plan ahead for Prime Day.

For more information, see the Prepare for Prime Day guide.

Schedule deals and promotions

March 24, 2026: Prime-Exclusive Best Deal and Lightning Deal submissions open for Prime Day. You'll receive $50 off the upfront fee per deal if you schedule your Best Deal or Lightning Deal by April 30, 2026.
April 6, 2026: Prime-Exclusive Price Discounts submissions open for Prime Day, and will close six hours before the end of the event. Go to Price discounts and events.
May 26, 2026: Deal scheduling for Prime-Exclusive Best Deals and Lightning Deals for Prime Day closes. You may continue to submit Prime-Exclusive Price Discounts until 12-hours before the event end time and Coupons through the end of the event.

Send your inventory

To ensure your products are Prime badge-ready by Prime Day, make sure your inventory arrives at our facilities by the following dates. While we'll continue to receive inventory after these dates, late arrivals may not be processed in time for Prime eligibility.

May 27, 2026: Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD) shipments
May 27, 2026: FBA shipments with “minimal shipment splits” option selected
June 5, 2026: FBA shipments with “Amazon-optimized shipment splits” option selected

When creating a shipping plan, you can select from a number of inventory inbound placement options. These options allow you to select between shipping your inventory to the minimal number of inbound locations or to multiple inbound locations for a reduced fee or no fee. To learn more, go to Send to Amazon: Confirm shipping.

Section 2: Send your shipments to Amazon

To help meet customer demand and drive sales during Prime Day, we aim to store inventory closer to customers so that your products can be delivered faster. You can help facilitate this process when you send shipments in early and provide tracking information so we can prepare to receive your inventory.

Send your shipments in early

To allow time for transportation to our fulfillment centers, we recommend that you schedule your delivery appointment at least 7 days before the dates listed above. Once your delivery dates are finalized, be sure to update the delivery window for your shipments to minimize receiving delays.
If you use the Amazon Partnered Carrier program, schedule your carrier pickup 14 days before the dates listed above.
When planning shipments, book your carrier service well ahead of time to account for potential supply chain disruptions like supplier delays, carrier issues, or customs delays. Big events like port closures can also cause issues.
Use Send to Amazon to save time when you create shipments to replenish your FBA inventory. Be mindful of issues with your shipments that can slow down how quickly we receive your products or cause delays in order fulfillment. Check the Shipment Performance dashboard to keep track of how well your shipments to us are performing. To learn more, see the Intro to Send to Amazon video:

Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD) is a great option to send your shipments in early. When you store bulk inventory with AWD, we’ll automatically replenish your products to fulfillment centers as needed to help you maintain the right inventory levels. To learn more, see the Introduction to Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD) video:

Simplify your supply chain by using Amazon Global Logistics (AGL) and Seller Export and Delivery (SEND) to transport your inventory directly from China to Amazon fulfillment centers in the US, UK, and Europe—all in one integrated service. To get started, see our US onboarding guide, EU and UK onboarding guide, and US booking guide.

Avoid shipment delays

To help reduce receive times and ensure your products are available for customer sales and fast delivery before Prime Day, we recommend the following actions:

Ensure your delivery windows and tracking IDs are accurate when you schedule your inbound shipments. Accurate tracking information and adherence to scheduled delivery windows helps reduce wait times, prevents unexpected congestion at our fulfillment centers, and enables faster processing of your inventory. Shipments arriving within their scheduled window will receive priority processing, while shipments arriving outside of their scheduled delivery windows may face appointment and receiving delays.
Use the Amazon Partnered Carrier Program and Amazon Freight for small-parcel, less-than-truckload, and full-truckload shipments, or use eligible ShipTrack carriers for shipments from China. We can work directly with the partner carrier to redirect freight after destinations are assigned to help you avoid longer receive times.
If you ship with carriers that aren’t partnered with Amazon, make sure to provide an accurate estimated delivery window in the tracking details step via Create shipments with Send to Amazon. This helps us receive and route your products to the right fulfillment centers faster. If you expect delays, you can update the delivery window before it starts.
Consolidate your deal inventory in dedicated boxes, separate from non-deal inventory. When deal inventory is packed together rather than randomized with regular inventory, we can process these high-priority items more efficiently and ensure they're available for your promotional events.
Get the most out of your deal events by allowing sufficient lead time between your inventory shipments and deal start dates. This helps ensure your promotional inventory is fully processed and available when customers are actively shopping for deals. For tips on specific deal types for the event, go to the Create a promotion section.
Make sure to give carriers the right destination address for your shipment. We start to prepare to receive your inventory when you create a shipping plan, and when shipments are sent to the wrong place, or sent as incomplete shipments, it can cause delays. See our policies for details on Deleted, misrouted, and incomplete shipments and learn more about Inbound defect fees.
Provide tracking information like carrier name and shipping method in the tracking details section of your Create shipments with Send to Amazon workflow. This helps us plan and receive your inventory faster, which minimizes delays and makes in-transit inventory available sooner for sale to customers.
Select night or weekend deliveries to increase your chances of securing earlier appointments and spread out demand. If you’re shipping through a non-partnered carrier, contact your carrier to find out if earlier appointments are available in Carrier Central. Book inbound appointments during off-peak hours and weekends when fulfillment centers have lower demand. This reduces wait times and helps ensure faster processing.
Provide accurate box content information, including detailed product identifiers and quantities for each box, to avoid manual processing fees and delays.
If you have FBA inventory covering more than 4-6 months of sales, leverage Amazon Warehousing & Distribution (AWD) to store any additional inventory and avoid potential storage issues, while keeping your products available and in-stock on Amazon.

Follow shipment guidelines

When you create FBA shipments, double check that all information—such as number of units in each box, total number of boxes, and total weight—is accurate to avoid receiving delays.
Provide information on what’s inside each box during shipment creation, either through Create shipments with Send to Amazon or Fulfillment By Amazon (FBA) Inbound v2024 API. If you omit this information, it can disqualify your shipment from auto-receiving and incur an FBA manual processing fee.
For heavy items, attach a “Team Lift” or “Mechanical Lift” label to the boxes to help ensure safe handling and receiving in our fulfillment center. For more information, go to Shipping and routing requirements.
Provide tracking information like carrier name and shipping method in the tracking details section of your Create shipments with Send to Amazon workflow. This helps us plan and receive your inventory faster, which minimizes delays and makes in-transit inventory available for sale sooner.

Track your shipments

The Shipment events tab on the Shipment summary page provides detailed updates about your shipments to Amazon, from creation to closing, and shows the status of your inventory.
If you use a non-partnered carrier, proactively communicate with your carrier to get progress reports and delivery confirmation. For shipments from China, you can use Amazon Global Logistics, SEND, or ShipTrack carriers to get integrated tracking information on Seller Central.
If you use Amazon freight and carriers that partner with Amazon, we can help locate your shipment if needed. Submit a Carrier missed pickup request to us for additional assistance.
Use the Inventory Ledger report to monitor your end-to-end inventory movements to and from our fulfillment centers. This report shows your starting inventory balance, received inventory, customer orders and returns, adjustments, removals, and ending balance.
We use tracking information to identify when your shipment was delivered and without it, we can’t provide a delivered status update on your shipment. Providing carrier tracking information helps you avoid receiving delays. For faster receive times, make sure that the tracking IDs for parcel shipments correspond to the correct FBA box-ID label on each box. To learn more about managing and tracking your inventory, see the Manage your Send to Amazon shipments guide.

Section 3: Create a promotion

Deals are time-bound, promotional offers that receive special merchandising on the product detail page, in search and in the cart. Prime Day deals are subject to various seller and product-level quality requirements, which differ by promotion type.

Tip: Submit deals early! Receive $50 off the upfront fee per deal if you schedule your Best Deal or Lightning Deal by April 30, 2026.

To learn more about the process, see the How to create a deal video:

What deal types are available for Prime Day?
Prime-Exclusive Best Deals

Prime-Exclusive Best Deals offer great products at a high-quality discount, and may perform well with best sellers in your catalog. These deals run the entire event duration. Customers can see these offers during Prime Day from the deal badging and strike-through pricing displayed on the detail page, in search, and in the cart. Eligible offers may also appear on Amazon deals during the event.

Eligibility criteria: To learn more about eligibility requirements for Prime-Exclusive Best Deals, go to Deals for events.
Benefits: Prime-Exclusive Best Deals allow you to promote your products to over 200M+ Prime members, and can be scheduled to run for the duration of Prime Day. When you participate in a large sales event, like Prime Day, it can help increase sales by making your products more visible to customers.
Prime Day fee: $100 per Best Deal plus 1.5% of sales (capped at $5,000)

How to submit:

From Seller Central, go to Advertising, and then navigate to Deals.
Click Create a new deal and then Show eligible products for Prime-Exclusive Best Deals.
Select any ASIN that shows a Best Deal available to schedule during Prime Day week.

Prime-Exclusive Lightning Deals

Lightning Deals allow you to offer a steep discount for a limited time to create a sense of urgency for customers. They can run for up to 12 hours (or until sold out), as scheduled by us. Offers that qualify may also be featured on Amazon deals.

Note: Lightning Deals are subject to Amazon selection and scheduling processes. Not all Lightning Deals submitted for event week consideration are guaranteed to be approved for participation, and even if approved, may not run on Prime Day dates.

Eligibility criteria: To learn more about eligibility requirements for Prime-Exclusive Lightning Deals, go to Deals for events.
Benefits: Lightning Deals can be a great way to drive conversion, attract new customers, and move excess inventory on recommended products. Make your inventory stand out and promote your brand when you offer an exclusive discount for Prime shoppers.
Prime Day fee: $100 per Lightning Deal plus 1.5% of sales (capped at $5,000)

How to submit:

From Seller Central, go to Advertising and then navigate to Deals.
Select Create a new deal and then select any ASIN that shows a Lightning Deal available for scheduling during Prime Day week.

What other promotion types are available?

You can also submit Prime-Exclusive Price Discounts starting on April 6, 2026, as well as Prime Member Coupons once the Prime Day dates are announced. For more information, see the Which Amazon promotion is right for me? guide.

To learn more, go to Amazon promotions and Deals for events.

Prime-Exclusive Price Discount

A Prime-Exclusive Price Discount is a price discount offered to Prime members only, including seller-fulfilled items with free shipping that meet other high-quality criteria. Each participating campaign can support up to 500 SKUs.

Eligibility criteria: To learn more about eligibility requirements for Prime-Exclusive Price Discounts, go to Price discounts and events.
Benefit: Promote your products to over 200M Prime members by offering an exclusive discount as incentive to drive conversion. Eligible products will appear with event badging in search, on the detail page, and in cart. However, unlike deals, they will not be featured on the main event page.
Prime Day fee: $100 per campaign plus 1.5% of sales (capped at $5,000)

For instructions and best practices, see the Create a Price Discount guide.

How to submit and receive event badging for a fee:

From Seller Central, go to Advertising and then navigate to Price Discounts and select Create discount.
Select Prime Day from the Events drop-down menu and complete the form.
Click Step 2: Add products, and complete the workflow.

Prime Member Coupons

Prime Member Coupons are point-of-sale promotions oﬀered exclusively to Prime members on a single product or a set of products with automatic placement on the Coupons page, shopping results, and product detail pages. For more information, go to Coupons and review the Create and manage coupons guide.

Eligibility criteria: To learn more about eligibility requirements for coupons, go to Coupon eligibility criteria.
Benefit: Coupons offer flexibility so you can run them on a single ASIN or set of ASINs, for a dollar amount or percentage off. They can differentiate you from your competitors and give customers a chance to try your product at a discount.
Prime Day fee: $5 per coupon plus 2.5% of sales (capped at $2,000)

Coupon submissions will open after Prime Day dates have been announced. You’ll be able to submit them from Seller Central by following these steps:

Go to Advertising.
Navigate to Coupon.
Select Create a new coupon.

What makes a successful promotion?

Successful promotions motivate customers to buy your product. To run an effective promotion, consider the following guidelines:

The customer must know about it. Consider social media campaigns, advertising coverage and e-mail marketing to drive traffic to your promotion.
It is time-sensitive. Customers respond to limited-time offers when they face the possibility of missing the offer. Experiment with duration to drive urgency, depending on your product.
It offers compelling savings to influence a purchase. Track current sales and gather feedback to figure out what offers your potential customers find more attractive, then refine your promotions accordingly.

Best practices

Use recommended products. Products that appear in your Deals dashboard have strong sales potential compared to others in the same product category and price band. Recommendations update weekly, so don’t wait to take action if you see a product recommended for Prime Day, as an eligible deal may disappear the following week.
Spotlight your top-selling items. Running a promotion on our store is your opportunity to attract Prime customers during high-traffic periods. Focus on top products and deep discounts to maximize your growth opportunities during the event.
Use high-quality content. Make sure your product imagery doesn’t include any text, logos, or watermarks that aren’t part of the product itself. If a promotion doesn’t follow our product image requirements or isn’t representative of the products within the promotion, the promotion may be canceled.
Improve product detail pages. Inspire customers to purchase products. Use bullet points, images, and A+ Content to share your brand message and unique product beneﬁts.
Manage pricing and suppressions. To ensure customers discover the most exciting offers on Prime Day, actively monitor your upcoming deals on the Deals Dashboard leading up to the event. For any deals appearing under the Needs action section, you must make the appropriate updates to meet eligibility requirements. Go to Troubleshooting suppressed and live deals for more information.
Consider Sponsored Ads. Help your Prime Day deals get noticed by running ad campaigns on top of your promotions. Sponsored Products and Sponsored Brands can help you grow visibility, reach new shoppers, and build lasting brand recognition in the Amazon store and beyond. To get started with Sponsored Ads, go to Amazon Ads.

Remember: consider the potential impact of promotions on your historical or reference pricing benchmarks leading up to Prime Day, as these factors may influence suppressions for your Deals and Price Discounts. For more information, see Amazon policy on reference prices and Troubleshooting suppressed and active Deals.

Section 4: Plan and manage your inventory

We recommend that you start early on your Prime Day inventory strategy with these tools that can be used separately or together, to create a customized storage plan.

Capacity Monitor: Use the Capacity Monitor to stay up-to-date on your capacity limits and plan ahead. The Capacity Monitor helps you keep track of how much inventory you can send and store in our fulfillment network. It shows your capacity limits for the upcoming month for each storage type, as well as estimated limits for the following two months.
Capacity Manager: The Capacity Manager allows you to request additional capacity at fulfillment centers for your products that sell quickly, typically those that sell out in under four weeks. You can submit a bid based on a reservation fee you specify. No upfront payment is required. Requests are granted objectively starting with the highest reservation fee in descending order until all available capacity has been allocated. You’ll earn performance credits from sales created with the extra capacity, which can offset up to 100% of the reservation fee. To learn more about Capacity Manager requests, set reservation fees, and estimate performance credits, see the FBA Capacity Manager guide.

For more information, see the Increase FBA capacity limits with Capacity Manager video:

Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD): Save cost with AWD during peak season. There are no additional fees for AWD during peak times, so send in your excess weeks of cover to AWD to avoid fees and FBA inbound placement fees. Inventory that is auto-replenished from AWD to FBA isn’t subject to 'FBA capacity limits'. To get started, go to our AWD shipment creation guide. For more information, go to Send shipments to Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD).
Section 5: Maintain stock

Proper inventory tracking during events helps ensure you have enough items in stock to capitalize on faster delivery speeds.

Go to FBA Inventory to check the recommended minimum levels for your ASINs. Navigate to the Inventory health status and Recommendation columns. Download the FBA Inventory report for detailed information on your recommended action.
The Demand forecast tool predicts your products' future demand for up to 40 weeks based on your previous orders, active deals, and seasonal patterns.
For new ASINs or products without recommendations, aim to keep at least 28 days of inventory based on historical demand.
Review any Minimum Inventory Level (MIL) Potential Sales Lift estimates to understand potential incremental sales opportunities available by increasing inventory levels to meet recommended MIL targets.
To avoid the low-inventory level fee, make sure you have 4-6 weeks of inventory available before your deal event. After the event, we recommend that you maintain at least 28 days of inventory for your post-deal sales.
Consider using AWD auto-replenishment to FBA for easier stock management.
To maintain optimal inventory levels and avoid product level restrictions, we recommend that you regularly review your inventory health status, understand hazardous materials limitations, stay informed about product classification processes, and explore alternative storage solutions such as Amazon Warehousing and Distribution.

Section 6: Tools and services

Tools

Capacity Monitor: Keep track of how much FBA inventory you can send to and store in our fulfillment centers.
Capacity Manager: Request extra FBA capacity for fast-selling products you need to keep in stock—generally those that sell in less than four weeks.
Send to Amazon: Use this streamlined workflow for simplified FBA shipment creation.
Shipment Performance dashboard: Oversee and manage your FBA shipments.
FBA Inventory report: View your current inventory levels and the recommended minimum amount to keep in stock.
Track your shipments: Shipping Queue and shipment summary: Get step-by-step updates about your shipments to Amazon and visibility into the status of your inventory.
Inventory Ledger: Monitor and analyze your inventory movements to and from our fulfillment centers.
FBA dashboard: View a summary of your FBA business, including sales, shipments, inventory, and FBA opportunities.
FBA Inventory: Monitor critical factors that affect your sales performance, assess fees, optimize prices, and maintain a healthy Inventory Performance Index (IPI) score by using your capacity at fulfillment centers efficiently.

Services

Amazon Warehousing and Distribution (AWD): Store bulk inventory with AWD and we’ll automatically replenish your products to fulfillment centers based on demand signals to help ensure you maintain the right FBA inventory levels.
Amazon Global Logistics: Ship ocean cargo from China directly to our fulfillment network in the US, UK, and EU.
