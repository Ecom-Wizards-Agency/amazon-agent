Two types of roles can be assigned to users in Amazon Brand Registry: protection roles and selling roles.

Selling roles, which include Brand Representative and Reseller, determine which brand selling benefits users can access. Protection roles, which include Administrator, Rights Owner, and Registered Agent, are assigned separately and determine which tools users can access in Brand Registry.

Both selling roles and protection roles can only be assigned or removed by the Administrator.

Note: Selling roles do not grant access to brand protection benefits, such as the Report a Violation tool. Brand protection benefit access is controlled by protection roles. You can learn more about protection roles by going to Brand Registry protection roles.

Available selling roles

There are two selling roles that an Administrator can assign:

Brand Representative: A selling partner who is directly employed by your brand
Reseller: An external, third-party selling partner who sells your brand’s products on Amazon

Note: Amazon remains an open marketplace. Users without an assigned Brand Registry selling role will maintain the ability to sell products on Amazon, provided they adhere to our selling policies.

Assign selling roles to selling partner accounts

Note: Only the Administrator can assign roles.

To assign a selling role to a Seller or Vendor Central account, the brand Administrator must follow these steps in Brand Registry:

Click Manage at the top of the page, and then select Manage selling roles.
Click Connect a selling partner account.
Choose between Seller Central accounts or Vendor Central accounts.
Choose the type of selling role that you want to assign, either Brand Representative or Reseller.
Select one of your accounts or Other account from the drop-down menu.
If you select Other account, provide either a merchant token for the seller or an account ID for the vendor that you want to invite to your brand. Sellers can access their merchant token from their Seller Central account by clicking the gear icon at the top of the page, then going to Account info, and then selecting Business information. Vendors can access their account ID from their Vendor Central account by clicking the gear icon at the top of the page, selecting Global accounts, and then selecting the Accounts tab.
Choose one of your registered brands from the drop-down list.
Click Connect selling account.

The selling partner will receive an email with instructions to accept the invitation through Seller Central or Vendor Central. After they accept, the selling role will show as active on the Connected tab of the Manage selling roles page.

You can invite and connect as many selling partner accounts to your brand as you would like.

Note: If the seller or vendor has a problem accepting your invitation, go to the Brand Registry Support page and select Technical issue. In the box labeled Please describe your issue, include "Error code 6789."

Merchant token

A merchant token is a unique identifier assigned to merchants and individual sellers that enables secure access to third-party applications within Amazon. This token serves as a critical authentication credential.

Access the merchant token

Sellers can access their merchant token from their Seller Central account by clicking the gear icon at the top of the page, selecting Account info, and then selecting Business information.

Account ID

An account ID is a unified business identifier that represents a vendor company across Amazon’s global marketplaces. Unlike previous vendor codes that were marketplace-specific, an account ID consolidates all your business data from different regions into a single, comprehensive profile.

Why Brand Registry uses partner account IDs to assign selling roles

Brand Registry uses account IDs for selling role assignments because they provide a single, unified identifier that works across all Amazon stores, programs, and regions, which eliminates the complexity of managing multiple vendor codes for a single selling-role relation.

Access the account ID

Vendors can access their account ID from their Vendor Central account by clicking the gear icon at the top of the page, selecting Global accounts, and then selecting the Accounts tab.

Note: Account IDs always starts with “amzn1.pa.o”.

Manage selling roles

Note: Only the Administrator can manage roles.

To update or change selling roles that have already been assigned, the Administrator must follow these steps in Brand Registry:

Click Manage at the top of the page, and then select Manage selling roles.
Choose between Seller Central accounts or Vendor Central accounts.
Click Connected Seller Central accounts or Connected Vendor Central accounts.
Find the seller merchant token or vendor account ID that you want to edit, and click Manage access.
Add or remove selling roles by checking or unchecking the boxes for each brand.
Click Update, and then Confirm changes.

Brand Benefit Eligibility (only on Seller Central)

Note: Only sellers can request a selling role assignment from a Seller Central account. Vendors must connect directly with the brand Administrator to request a selling role.

If you’re an Amazon seller that sells the products of a registered brand, you can use the Brand Benefit Eligibility tool to review existing selling role relations and request a selling role from an Administrator. This selling role defines your relationship with the brand, either as a Brand Representative or Reseller. These roles grant different access and privileges. You can learn the distinctions between these roles in the Available selling roles section above.

Request selling roles

To request a selling role, follow these steps on Seller Central:

Select Brands in the drop-down menu, and then select Build your brand.
Click View registered brands.
Click the Eligible brands tab to review the brands for which you can request a selling role.
Note: Brand Registry automatically detects the brand with which you had a previous selling relationship and enables a selling role request for them.
Click Request selling role.
Depending on your relationship with the brand, choose between Brand Representative or Reseller.
Click Submit request.

You will receive a notification regarding the decision of the brand Administrator on your selling role assignment. If your request is approved, you’ll become eligible to access related benefits within 48 hours.

If you don’t have access to the Brand Benefit Eligibility page or see your brand on Eligible brands, you can request a selling role by contacting the registered brand’s Administrator directly.

Note: We do not share Administrator contact information.

For more information, watch Request access to brand selling benefits (English only).

Note: If you're currently assigned a selling role and you no longer want to be associated with the brand, you must contact the Administrator directly and ask to be removed. If you're unable to contact the Administrator, you can contact Selling Partner Support via Seller Central or Vendor Central or use Amazon's Seller Assistant. In Seller Central, choose My issue is not listed and then provide the brand’s name along with your merchant token and explain that you want to remove your account from the brand. In Vendor Central, select Other assistance & feedback, then Amazon Brand Registry, and then Still need help? Provide the brand’s name along with your account ID and explain that you want to remove your account from the brand.
Review existing selling role assignments

To review the brands that you already have a selling role assigned, follow these steps in Seller Central:

Select Brands from the drop-down menu, and then select Build your brand.
Click View registered brands.
Click the Connected brands tab to review the brands that have a selling role assigned.

To request a Brand Representative role for brands in which you’re Reseller, follow these steps in Seller Central.

Select Brands in the drop-down menu, and then select Build your brand.
Click View registered brands.
Click the Connected brands tab to review the brands that have a selling role assigned.
Click Request a Brand Representative selling role.

You will receive a notification regarding the decision of the brand Administrator on your selling role assignment. If your request is approved, you'll become eligible to access related benefits within 48 hours.

Manage selling role requests
Note: Only the Administrator can manage selling role requests. They have 30 days to make a decision on a selling role request before it expires.

Once a seller submits a request for brand selling benefits, their request will appear on the Administrator’s Manage selling roles page. The Administrator will then be able to assign permissions, at their discretion, to the user.

To approve or decline selling role requests, the Administrator must follow these steps in Brand Registry:

Click Manage at the top of the page, and then select Manage selling roles.
Select Seller Central accounts, and then click Access requests.
Find the merchant token of the requests that you want to review.
Accept or reject as needed.

For more information, watch Manage selling benefits for your brand with roles (English only).
