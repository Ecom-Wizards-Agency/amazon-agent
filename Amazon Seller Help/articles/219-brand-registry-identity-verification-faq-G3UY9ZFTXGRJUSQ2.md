Amazon Brand Registry requires identity verification to activate certain benefits for your account. For a step-by-step guide, go to Brand Registry identity verification process.

What if I want to withdraw my consent for identity verification using biometrics?

Identity verification using facial recognition technology (biometrics) is optional. If you have already consented to have your facial data captured but you have not yet uploaded documents, you can exit out of the verification experience before any biometric data is created. Next, you’ll need to contact us or use Amazon's Seller Assistant to enable identity verification with a video call.

How can I appeal Amazon’s decision to decline my application?

You will have the option to appeal Amazon’s decision after your first decline of identity verification. However, post-appeal verification decisions are final and are based on the information and documentation that we have received from you.

What if I don't agree with the identity verification decision? How do I appeal it?

While we expect this scenario to be rare, if the verification process results in an inconclusive or failed outcome, you may choose to raise an appeal. To do so, go to the Identity verification page and click Next steps. Then, in the pop-up window, click Submit appeal.

You will then be taken to the Appeal Submission form. Once you read through the details on the form, click Submit. You will see a confirmation message stating that your appeal has been submitted. To see the status of your appeal, go to Support and then select Brand support case log click.

How long will it take to verify my information?

Once we receive all of the required information, we will proceed with the verification of your account. Verification could take up to 15 minutes.

As part of manual verification, why can’t I edit my information?

Usually, you cannot edit your information once you have submitted it. You also cannot update any information or upload documents while this information is being verified.

How is the primary contact person defined?

The primary contact person is the person who has access to the Amazon Brand Registry account, provides the registration information on behalf of the account holder, and performs activities such as using Notice of Infringement services. Actions taken by the primary contact person are deemed to be taken by the account holder.

Why is my identity document not being accepted?

If your identity document is not accepted, usually the reason is that the document that you provided does not meet the document requirements or is not an acceptable proof of identity. However, we recommend that you refer to the Brand Registry identity verification process help page to learn more about possible errors that can lead to rejection. Specifically, refer to the sections titled “Document requirements” and “Step-by-step submission guide."

How do I complete identity verification if I experience technical issues with the process?

If you are experiencing difficulty taking your photo for identity verification, you can exit the process by closing the window. Then, you can restart the verification process and choose to confirm your identity using the live video call option.

What can I do if I don't want my facial or biometric data to be captured?

During the verification process, you will be offered a choice to complete identity verification using the automated method or by attending a live video call with one of our associates.

We will never collect your facial data without your consent. For more information, go to About Brand Registry facial data verification.
