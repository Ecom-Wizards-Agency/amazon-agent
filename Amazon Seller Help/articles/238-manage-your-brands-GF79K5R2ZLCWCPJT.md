Manage your brands helps you easily manage your brand portfolio:

View and manage your existing brand portfolio.
Use the Brand Selection Panel to search for and add new brands to your portfolio.
Track brand applications that are under review.

Brand Portfolio

Brand Portfolio shows you all the brands that you are currently authorized to list in Amazon’s stores.

You can view the brand name approved in Amazon Brand Registry, brand aliases or representations that you have used to list the brand. Additionally, you can view brand identifiers, such as logos, product images, or brand descriptions.

If a brand is incorrectly associated with your selling account, or if you are no longer selling a brand that’s shown in the list, click Remove brand to remove your association with that brand. You won’t be able to list products with the same brand name if you remove it from your portfolio.

Go to Brands and select Manage your brands. Brand portfolio is displayed by default
Review the list of brands and metadata associated with your portfolio.
To remove a brand, click Remove brand next to the brand you want to remove.
Confirm the removal when prompted. Note that you cannot list products with the same brand name after you remove it from your portfolio.
To re-add a brand that you removed, click Add brand.

Brand Selection Panel

Use the Brand selection panel to find and add new brands to your portfolio. Once you obtain brand authorization, you can add brands to your portfolio that are already enrolled in Brand Registry.

You can also add new brands to your portfolio by enrolling them in Brand Registry or Brand Name Approval process.

On the Manage your brands, click Add brand to open the Brand selection panel.
Enter the brand name that you want to list.
Review the list of potential matches from brands enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry. Each match will display brand identifiers such as brand logo, product image, or brand description.

If you find the brand name that you want to list, select it and click Connect this brand.

To protect our customers and maintain their trust, we place selling restrictions on certain products and brands. To learn more, go to Categories and products that require approval.

Based on your brand selection, you may see one of the following prompts:

If you are not authorized to list new ASINs for the brand, you may be required to complete a selling application with the manufacturer information and with photos of your product’s packaging for review.
If you are not authorized to sell products from the brand, you may be asked to provide documents such as invoices or photos of your product’s packaging for review.
If you can't find the brand that you want to list, check the spelling and spacing of the brand name and search again.
If the brand you want to list still isn’t found, select Can't find my brand to enroll your brand. If you are the intellectual property owner with a registered or pending trademark, you can use the Brand Registry application process. If you are a reseller or don’t own a trademark for your brand, you can use the Brand name approval process.
Brands to connect

The Brands to connect tab displays brand names that you're already using in your product listings (SKUs) but haven't yet connected to Amazon Brand Registry.

On the Manage your brands page, select the Brands to connect tab.
Review the list of brands identified from your existing listings that are not yet connected to a brand in Amazon Brand Registry.
Click Connect Brand next to the brand you want to connect. You can also use the search box to search for the brand you want to connect.
Follow the steps in the Brand selection panel to complete the connection process.

Once added successfully, the connected brand name will appear on the Brand portfolio tab.

Note: Based on your brand selection, you may be required to submit a selling application if you don't have existing authorization. Once the selling application is approved, the brand will automatically appear in your Brand Portfolio tab.
Brand Applications Under Review

The Brand applications under review table lets you track your Brand Registry, Brand name approval, and Selling applications that have been submitted and that are currently being reviewed.

On Manage your brands, select the Brand applications under review tab.
Review the list of your current applications. Each entry will show the brand name along with a link to the applications dashboard.
If you are an intellectual property owner who submitted a Brand Registry application, you’ll be directed to Manage applications in Brand Registry.
If you are a reseller who submitted a selling application, you’ll be directed to the Selling applications summary, where you’ll find additional information, such as the case link.
If an application is approved, the brand will be added to your Brand portfolio automatically.
If an application is rejected, you'll receive information about the reason for rejection in the case log.
FAQ
What are the requirements to enroll a brand in Brand Registry?

To be eligible for Brand Registry, your brand must have one of the following:

An active registered trademark or pending trademark in each country where you would like to enroll
A pending trademark application that is filed through Amazon IP Accelerator

To enroll one or more brands in Brand Registry, sign into the Brand Registry site, click Manage, and select Enroll a brand.

During the enrollment process, you’ll be prompted to enter the following information:

Your brand name that has an active registered trademark. We also accept brand names with a trademark that is pending registration in a subset of trademark offices.
The government-registered trademark number. If you are enrolling with a trademark that is pending registration, you may provide the application number issued by the IP office.

A list of product categories in which your brand must be listed, for example apparel, sporting goods, or electronics.

Note: Brand Registry applications must be submitted by an applicant who is internally affiliated with the brand. Brand Registry applications submitted by external parties, such as resellers or distributors will be rejected.

To learn more about enrolling in Brand Registry, go to Brand Registry application process.

I am an intellectual property owner who applied to Brand Registry. How can I track the status of my application?

You can view Brand Registry applications that are under review on Manage your brands. The enrollment status will Processing application to enroll new brand via Brand Registry. If you click on the brand enrollment status, you’ll be taken to Manage applications in Brand Registry where you can search for applications by brand name, trademark name, or application status. You can also view the status of your applications and case links for applications that require more information.

I am an intellectual property owner, but I don’t have a trademark for my brand. Can I add the brand to my portfolio and create new listings with the brand?

Yes. While you can create new listings with the brand, we must approve all new brand names prior to listing. Review the Amazon Brand Name policy before you create a new listing.

If you don’t have a trademark for your brand, submit a brand name approval application with the following information:

The brand name of your product. The brand name must be spelled exactly as you want it to appear on the detail page.
Product identifier information, such as UPC, EAN, or JAN.
Images of the product and packaging that clearly show the brand name permanently affixed to the product or packaging.
The branding on the packaging must be real. It must not be digitally added to the product or packaging in photo post-processing, or be completely computer generated.
The branding must be distinguishable from similar products that do not belong to the brand.
The branding cannot be an attempt to obfuscate a different brand name.

What are the benefits of enrolling my brand in Brand Registry instead of Brand Name Approval?

While Brand Name Approval allows you to list products under a specific brand name, Brand Registry provides additional benefits, such as enhanced brand protection, greater control over product detail page, and access to brand building benefits such as A+ Content and brand stores.

I am a reseller who wants to create new listings with a brand that isn’t sold on Amazon. Can I still add the brand to my portfolio and create new listings with the brand?

Yes. You can seek approval to create new listings for your brand by providing the following information:

The brand name of your product. The brand name must be spelled exactly as you want it to appear on the detail page.
Product identifier information, such as UPC, EAN, or JAN.
Images of the product and packaging that clearly show the brand name permanently affixed to the product or packaging.
The branding on the packaging must be real. It must not be digitally added to the product or packaging in photo post-processing, or be completely computer generated.
The branding must be distinguishable from similar products that do not belong to the brand.
The branding cannot be an attempt to obfuscate a different brand name.

Note: Create new listings only when the product doesn’t exist in Amazon’s catalog. Creating a new listing when the product already exists in Amazon's catalog is prohibited, and can result in your listing creation or selling privileges being temporarily suspended or permanently removed. For more information, go to ASIN creation policy.

I am a reseller whose selling application for a branded product was approved. How do I receive selling benefits, such as A+ content and Brand stores, for my brands?

Contact the brand Administrator in Brand Registry to assign a selling role to your account. For more information, go to Brand Registry selling roles.

I am a reseller who is no longer selling a brand displayed in my brand portfolio. How can I remove the brand association?

On the manage your brands page, click Remove to remove the brand from your portfolio.

If your account is associated with a selling role in Brand Registry, you cannot remove the brand from the Manage your brands page. Contact the brand Administrator to remove the selling role that’s connected to your account in Brand Registry. For more information, go to Brand Registry selling roles.

The current Administrator has left the company or is no longer associated with the brand. How can I update the Administrator?

If there are no active Administrators for the brand, there are a couple of ways to request access to the brand.

Option 1: If you have at least one other brand that is already enrolled in Brand Registry and you can access the Brand Registry site, then you can submit your request via Brand Registry Support. Select Update brand ownership as the topic. Your request will be reviewed, and you will receive a response accordingly.

Option 2: You can attempt to apply for Brand Registry with the same trademark number as a currently enrolled brand. Once you enter the already registered trademark number, a warning will appear, offering a link to a form for sharing your contact information. Click the link to access the Share Contact Information form. At the bottom of the form, there’s a link available only for eligible users, leading to an Appeal Submission form. If your appeal succeeds, you'll be added to the brand with the Administrator and the Rights Owner roles.

I see error code 100550 when I try to create a new listing. What does this mean and how do I resolve it?

Error code 100550 indicates that you have to connect your brand with your account before you can create new ASINs with that brand. This connection helps Amazon accurately identify your brands and ensures proper brand attribution for your products.

To fix error 100550:

Go to Manage your brands.
Click Add Brand to open the Brand selection panel.
In the search bar, enter the exact brand name you want to use in your listing.
Review the list of potential matches. Each match will display brand identifiers such as the brand logo, product image, or brand description.
Select the appropriate brand and click Connect this brand.
If you are not authorized to sell products from the brand, you may be required to complete a selling application.
Once your brand is successfully connected to your account, you’ll see it in your Brand portfolio.
Resubmit your listing to resolve the error.

Why are brands appearing in the Brands to connect tab?

Amazon automatically scans the brand names in your active product listings and compares them against the brands connected in your Brand Portfolio. Any brand names found in your listings that aren't yet connected will appear in this tab. Connecting brands ensures uninterrupted listing capabilities and unlocks brand protection benefits.

What happens to existing listings during and after connection in Brands to connect tab?

Your existing listings remain active throughout the connection process, and there is no disruption to your current inventory. Once connected, your existing SKUs are automatically associated with Amazon Brand Registry. No manual updates are required for your existing listings.

When should I use Brands to connect vs Add Brand?

Use the Brands to connect tab when you see brand names that you're already using in your existing product listings. The brand name is pre-filled based on what Amazon has detected in your inventory. This option is useful when you want to connect multiple brands from your current listings at once.

Use Add Brand in the Brand Portfolio tab when you want to add a completely new brand that doesn't currently appear in your inventory.

Why can’t I see my brand in my Brand portfolio even though I have authorization to sell?

The Brand portfolio on Manage your brands only shows brands for which you are currently authorized to list new ASINs.

If you received a temporary authorization for a brand, which is typically valid for 96 hours after approval, that brand will no longer appear in your Brand portfolio once the authorization period expires. If you want to create new ASINs for a brand that's no longer in your Brand portfolio because the temporary authorization expired, you’ll have to add the brand again through the Brand selection panel, and complete the required approval process.

I am selling generic products. Do I need approval to sell the product?

No, generic products are unbranded products that don’t belong to any identifiable brand. An unbranded product doesn’t have a distinctive name or logo. For example, a mobile phone case that doesn’t belong to a brand wouldn’t have any branding, so “generic” should be entered in the brand name field when a new listing is created. For more information, go to Amazon Generic Product policy.
