Product Opportunity Explorer is a tool to explore estimated customer demand for new product ideas. The tool allows you to understand Amazon customer discovery and purchasing behavior to evaluate whether there’s unmet customer demand and a potential opportunity for you to meet that demand through new products. You can explore up-to-date and accurate data on customer needs and the current selection in the Amazon store.

Note: Product Opportunity Explorer is currently available to Professional sellers only. For details on Professional selling account fees, go to Monthly subscription fee.

Important: This tool is intended to provide you with general information to help you make selection decisions. The tool is provided as a guide only and should not be a substitute for your own judgment about demand for your products. Many factors can influence selection decisions, including price fluctuations, consumer demand shifts, and changes in competing offers for those products. Amazon does not guarantee any particular outcome or sales by providing this tool. You remain responsible for independently determining your own selection.
Get started

To access the tool, go to Product Opportunity Explorer or, from the Seller Central drop-down menu, select Growth and then select Product Opportunity Explorer.

To search for data in a particular store, use the Find opportunities in drop-down menu in the top right corner. The page will default to the US store on your first visit and then will reflect the last store that you accessed in Seller Central.

Additionally, you can browse by category, search keywords, or ASINs, or use example “niches” provided on the landing page.

You can discover broad customer needs by searching for niches, such as “dog bed.” You also can search directly for ASINs and get detailed information on the product that you’re looking for.

About niches

A niche is a collection of customer search terms and products that represent a specific customer need. Customers tell us what they want when they enter specific terms to find their next buy (for example, stainless-steel water bottle, BPA-free water bottle, insulated water bottle), and we combine these terms around specific shopping needs based on customer demand. These terms are grouped to identify a unique customer intent, and the top search term becomes the niche title, such as “water bottle.”

You can find niches by entering a keyword in the tool’s search field or browsing by category or subcategory. Example niches are also provided on your initial page based on categories in which you currently sell. If you don't see example niches, it's because you don't have any products yet. If that’s the case, start with a search or browse niches.

A niche includes top products that customers bought and that got the most clicks from those search terms. We focus on top products because they’re considered the most in demand based on the customer intent.

We create niches by grouping search terms based on the products that customers view or buy after searching. A cluster is created with search terms that typically have high ASIN overlap in purchases and clicks. The search term that is high in search volume in that cluster is determined as the niche. The number of search terms displayed for a niche is capped based on the search terms with the greatest search volume.

For more details on data definitions, go to the Metric glossary on the top right side of the Product Opportunity Explorer page.

Tabs on the niche details page

Products tab

If you click the niche name, you’ll land on the Products tab. Use this tab to review products in the niche that generally got the most clicks and have been bought after a customer search. You can review metrics such as launch date, click share, and other pertinent data to help you understand what a customer is looking for and identify overall competition within the niche.

If you click the link for the product, you’ll be taken to the product detail page, where you can zoom in on a product to dive deep and compare it with existing Amazon ASINs. Data provided on this page includes price and offer data, average Amazon fees, and product clicks and trends. For more information, go to the “Search for ASINs” section below.

Search terms tab

This section can help you learn about customer intent and review product attributes or features that are potentially important to customers. On this page, you can evaluate data such as search volume, click share, search conversion, and the top three clicked products.

Insights tab

Use the Insights section to conduct a deeper analysis into the niche by reviewing 20 different data points across three time frames: today, 90 days, and 360 days. You can review the number of products and top-clicked products to understand saturation in the niche, or low percentage of Prime products metric to see opportunities for adding a product with a Prime offer.

Trends tab

The Trends tab is composed of two parts: Metrics picker and Chart. The metrics picker allows you to select up to two metrics to plot in the chart. You can have up to five total metrics present in the picker to toggle quickly between selections. If you have fewer than five metrics in the metrics picker, click Add metric. If you don’t see the Add metric icon, click x on any metric to remove it from your picker and select other metrics.

The chart represents the time-series data for each of the metrics selected in the metrics picker. The color of the plot lines will correspond to the highlight colors in the metrics picker. You can analyze the chart by hovering your mouse over the plot lines. Each data point in the chart represents the data that was aggregated in the seven days before the date reflected in the data point.

Customer review insights tab

This section provides you with insights into customer reviews, aggregated by themes that are further divided into more specific subtopics and corresponding snippets. You can toggle between positive and negative reviews to understand customer sentiment.

The bar chart illustrates how a topic affected the average star rating of the top 25% of products in the niche and all products in the niche for the past six months, differentiated by a dark blue bar and a light blue bar. The line chart illustrates the percentage of times the review topics have been mentioned in products within the niche.

Purchase drivers tab

This section provides you with the most relevant features that positively or negatively affect the number of units sold for the products in the niche. The impact is calculated by comparing the estimated sales of the products with that feature against the average units sold for all products in the niche. We present up to 10 of the most relevant positive or negative impact features.

The features are extracted from product name, description, and bullet points and are specific to the products in the niche. Whenever there’s an apparent theme that applies to the features, they’ll be named with a tag for reference (such as color: blue for a T-shirt niche, where color is the theme tag and blue is the feature).

Returns tab

This section provides you with the most common buyer reasons for returning a product in the niche and corresponding frequency of mentions expressed as a percentage compared with all returns for products in this niche. The line graph illustrates the trends for each topic over time. The reasons displayed in this tab are extracted from buyer feedback.

Search for ASINs

You can also use Product Opportunity Explorer to search for a specific ASIN or for ASINs that are related to a given search term. To look for a specific ASIN, enter the alpha-numeric identifier. The result will include the target ASIN, identified by a header at the top of the table, and other relevant products, identified as similar ASINs, right below the target ASIN. The key metrics will help you to identify how relevant the products are.

If you want to search for ASINs that are related to a given search term, enter that search term, select the ASIN view tab and then click Search. This will lead you to the list of related ASINs and corresponding key metrics.

For both cases, you can find a full Metric glossary on the top right side of the page.

Customer review insights tab on the product details page

Like the Customer review insights tab for the niches, this section provides you with insights into customer reviews that are specific to the product, aggregated by themes that are further divided into specific subtopics and corresponding snippets. You can toggle between positive and negative review snippets to understand customer sentiment.

The chart illustrates how buyer feedback affected the average star rating of that ASIN, the parent ASIN, and the other ASINs in the product’s category for the past six months, differentiated by a dark blue bar, a light blue bar, and an orange bar. The line chart illustrates the percentage of times the review topics have been mentioned for that ASIN, the parent ASIN, and the other ASINs in the category with a dark blue line, light blue line, and orange line respectively.

Navigate from ASIN view to Niche view

If you want to go to a niche from a specific product that you’ve identified by either searching for the ASIN itself or going through the results of a keyword search in the ASIN view tab, click the product and get into the product details page. On this page, you can see different metrics related to that product and, at the bottom, you’ll find the section indicating all the niches in which the product is included.

To go from a niche to specific products, click the niche name to enter the niche details, and the first tab containing all the products in that niche. Then select the product name to go to the product details page.
