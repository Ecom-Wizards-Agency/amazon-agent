Project Zero is an anti-counterfeit program which gives brands the ability to immediately remove counterfeit listings of their products.

Where is Project Zero currently available?

Project Zero is currently available to eligible brands in the U.S., UK, Ireland, France, Italy, Spain, Germany, Japan, India, Mexico, Canada, Australia, Brazil, the United Arab Emirates, Türkiye, Singapore, the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, Sweden, the Netherlands, Belgium, Poland, Egypt, and South Africa.

How can I apply for Project Zero?

Project Zero is an invite-only program. Brands enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry that meet the eligibility criteria will automatically receive an invite. We identify and invite qualified brands. You cannot directly request an invitation for Project Zero.

We monitor enrolled brands weekly to assess their eligibility against program criteria. When your brand meets these requirements, you’ll automatically receive an invitation via email, and you’ll see a notification on your Brand Registry homepage.

Once invited, brands are required to complete a training in order to begin using Project Zero.

You cannot submit a request or application for Project Zero.

Who is eligible for Project Zero?

You must meet the following minimum requirements to be considered for Project Zero enrollment:

You must have a Brand Registry account with a registered trademark and a Rights Owners role.
You must have submitted reports of potential infringements through the Report a Violation tool with an acceptance rate of at least 90% over the last six months. The acceptance rate can be found on Submission history on Brand Registry.

To learn more about eligibility, go to Project Zero: Guidelines and training.

How can I check my eligibility?

A banner on the Project Zero page will let you know if you’re eligible for the program:

If you are eligible, you'll see a banner that says, "Enroll your brands in Project Zero."
If you are not eligible, you'll see a banner that states, "You are not eligible to access Project Zero." You can continue to use the Report a Violation tool.

How can I request an invitation for Project Zero?

You cannot directly request an invitation for Project Zero. Amazon automatically monitors enrolled brands weekly to assess their eligibility against program criteria. Once your brand meets these requirements, you’ll automatically receive an invitation via email, and you’ll see a notification on your Brand Registry homepage. There is no need to submit any request or application.

How can I check the acceptance rate for my submissions to Report a Violation?

To view your acceptance rate, go to Submission history on Brand Registry. This page shows your submission summary and a detailed history of your removal requests. We include reports for the last 12 months of your submissions and their outcomes.

I believe that I am eligible for Project Zero enrollment, but the banner still shows that I am not eligible to access Project Zero. Why is that?

We have the right to evaluate additional factors when determining your eligibility. You may continue to submit reports of potential infringement through the Report a Violation tool . We’ll automatically invite you to enroll once you meet our criteria.

I just joined Brand Registry, and have not yet used the Report a Violation tool. Am I eligible for Project Zero?

No. We encourage you to first submit reports of potential infringements through the Report a Violation tool. We’ll automatically invite you to enroll once you meet our criteria.

Are Registered Agents eligible for Project Zero?

No. Only the Rights Ownerof the trademark for the brand enrolled in Brand Registry is eligible to access Project Zero. To learn more about roles, go to Brand Registry protection roles.

Is the Project Zero training required in order to enroll in the program?

Yes. You’re required to complete a15-minute training as part of your enrollment. This training covers our counterfeit policy and how to identify and remove counterfeit listings using the Self-Service Counterfeit Removal tool. The training includes one-to-two pages of informational content and a quiz at the end.

After you complete the required training, you’ll gain immediate access to the Self-Service Counterfeit Removal tool, which allows you to instantly remove counterfeit listings of your products from our stores. Your removal of counterfeit listings will take effect within a few minutes.

What happens after I remove a counterfeit listing?

A notice of complaint is sent to each seller associated with the removal. They have the option to appeal the removal action.

Can a selling partner appeal a listing removed through the Self-Service Counterfeit Removal tool?

Yes. Selling partners are able to dispute a listing removal if they believe it was made in error.

How does Amazon ensure that brands are making accurate decisions when they use the Self-Service Counterfeit Removal tool?

We provide brands with an unprecedented level of responsibility. We do so because we believe that our combined strengths can eliminate counterfeits. Brands must maintain an accuracy of at least 99% in order to maintain their Project Zero privileges. We have several processes in place to promote accuracy, including required training and continuous monitoring to prevent misuse of our tools.

If my brand is enrolled in Project Zero in one country, am I automatically enrolled in the other countries?

Yes. Once a brand is enrolled in Project Zero in one country, that brand is automatically enrolled in other countries. However, removals can only be submitted with registered trademarks that are both connected to your Brand Registry account and enforceable in the jurisdiction of the selected store, such as USPTO for amazon.com.

Do brands need to serialize their products to enroll in Project Zero?

No, but brands that serialize their products may see better results as this process helps detect and prevent counterfeits from reaching customers. You can also choose which products you want to serialize. The product serialization service is currently available in the U.S, Canada, Italy, France, Germany, Spain, UK, India, Australia, and Japan. We plan to expand the service to more countries.

Is Project Zero free?

Yes. It is free to enroll in Project Zero, get access to the automated protections, and use the Self-Service Counterfeit Removal tool. Brands that choose to use the product serialization service incur a cost per unit, based on volume.
