# Seller Lending program


The Seller Lending program is a program offered by Amazon Lending. Under this program, you may be eligible to apply for loans provided by local third-party lenders or lending service providers to grow your business on Amazon. While we strive to serve as many sellers as possible, this program is available only to sellers who meet the criteria determined by the lenders or lending service providers.

Frequently asked questions
Where can I find the finance products available to me if my business meets the program’s criteria?

Sign in to your Seller Central account and go to the Amazon Lending page to see if your business is eligible to apply for a loan. This page is available only to sellers who meet the criteria determined by the lenders or lending service providers.

What are the eligibility criteria?

The eligibility criteria are determined by the lender or lending service provider and vary accordingly. The lenders or lending service providers will review sellers’ applications and make loan decisions.

I was eligible to apply for a loan in the past but don’t see any available loans now. Why?

Your business’s sales and performance metrics might have changed, or the criteria determined by the lenders or lending service providers might have changed. These criteria can change at any time. We recommend that you check your eligibility periodically.

Who will be the parties to a loan agreement under the program?

Any loan agreement will be an independent arrangement between you and the lender or lending service provider. Amazon will not be a party to such agreement.

Will you share my business’s data on Amazon with lenders or lending service providers? If so, what data?

With your consent, we’ll share your business’s sales and performance data that’s on file with Amazon, on your behalf, directly and securely with the lender to which you’re applying for a loan. For more information, go to our data sharing FAQ.

Why do I have to provide more information on the lender's or lending service provider’s website?

The loan will be offered to you by the lender or lending service provider, and you’ll be contracting directly with that entity. If you apply for a loan, you’ll be redirected to an application page hosted by the lender or lending service provider, where you’ll be asked for more information so they can process your application. Such information may also be transmitted to lenders through lending service providers.

Would lenders or lending service providers share my personal information from the loan application with Amazon?

We receive data from the lenders about the progress of your application and the loan characteristics. This data may include the product type (for example, business line of credit) and information about your loan, such as the amount and interest rate. This information helps us monitor and improve the Seller Lending program. For more information, go to our data sharing FAQ.

Will my Amazon seller account be blocked or suspended if I miss a loan payment?

No. If you get a loan from a third-party lender, you’ll sign the loan agreement with that lender and will be subject to the terms of that agreement. Your participation in the Seller Lending program and your loan repayment status won’t affect the status of your Amazon seller account.

Can I apply for more than one loan?

You can apply for as many loans as you would like, but you can choose to accept only one through our Seller Central offerings. After confirming one loan to be funded, you won’t be able to proceed with the other loan opportunities or with any outstanding applications made through the Seller Lending program.

Can Individual sellers apply for loans under the program?

Yes, both Individual and Professional sellers can apply for loans. Ultimately, the lender or lending service provider makes the decision on loan underwriting and fund disbursement. For questions about underwriting or funding, such as whether a personal guarantor is required or which bank account is to be funded, contact the lender or lending service provider directly, using the contact information provided at the end of this page.

Will my seller account be under review if I change my disbursement account through the account locking window in Seller Central?

Changing the bank account through the account locking window won’t trigger review of your seller account, and we won’t remove the bank account details from your selling account. For questions about account locking, China-based sellers can contact daikuan@amazon.com, and Vietnam- or Singapore-based sellers can contact apac-lending@amazon.com. We’ll respond within 24 hours during working days.

What does it mean to have a lending service provider-designated disbursement account?

When you take out a loan through the Seller Lending program, the lender or lending service provider requires that we lock your disbursement account. That means that you can’t change the account into which you receive your disbursements for as long as you have an active loan with that lender or lending service provider.

In some jurisdictions, the lenders or lending service providers have direct relationships with payment service providers and will require that your disbursement account be held with an approved provider before making a loan. If your disbursement account isn’t acceptable, the lender or lending service provider will notify you that you must change it and will provide a list of approved payment service providers. The list that they provide might be different from Amazon's list of acceptable bank accounts and payment service providers.

Which third-party lenders or lending service providers will offer loan opportunities?

In the Seller Lending program in China, there are currently four lenders or lending service providers: Dowsure, Ju Kai Feng (TSF), Mybooster, and HSBC Express Finance. In Vietnam and Singapore, Dowsure is currently the only lending service provider under the program.
