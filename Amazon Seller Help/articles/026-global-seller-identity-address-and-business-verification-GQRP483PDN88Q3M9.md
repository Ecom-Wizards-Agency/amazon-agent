To maintain a trusted store for buyers and sellers, we need to verify the information that you provided during registration. Here you can find information on the global seller identity verification process and the documents that you have to provide to create and verify your selling on Amazon account.

Document submission

Before you submit relevant documentation to activate your seller account, you must sign in to Seller Central and complete the registration process:

Update the business information
Seller information
Billing

Verification

You must confirm your details under Identity, Address verification, Beneficial owner, and Business verification and upload the required documents as per the instructions on this page. The verification page will display customized options based on your business type and the country, state, or region in which your business is located or where you reside.

Document requirements

We must verify the identity of the legal representative or beneficial owner registering the account, and the legitimacy of the business and address information.

The person registering the account, also known as the primary contact person, must provide the following documents:

Identity document
Business verification documents
Proof of address

Identity document

We require a government-issued photo identity document that is used to confirm one’s identity, such as a passport or national ID. This document must match the proof-of-identity information that you selected under Identity.

Business verification documents

We require government-issued documents that display information about your business. These documents must match the information about your business that you entered during registration. We require the following documents that prove your business is valid:

Certificate of good standing or an equivalent explicitly stating that the business is active and compliant as of a recent date. Different countries have various names for such certificates, for example, “certificate of existence,” “attestation de situation,” and “Aktif Belge.”
Recently issued or renewed business license
Other official documents:
Tax clearance certificate or VAT registration with a recent issue date
Official registry extract from a government website indicating that the business is active
Chamber of commerce statements (if regionally accepted)
The point of contact (Primary contact) or business owner must upload their business association or relationship documents as described below, based on the country that you selected as your business location:
All countries except Japan and Türkiye: A letter of authorization, articles of association, or a business license
Japan: A letter of authorization, a recent pay slip or payroll statement, articles of association, a corporate bank or credit card statement, an official company letter confirming role (employment certification), or a business license
Türkiye: A letter of authorization, articles of association, signature circular, or tax registration certificate

The letter of authorization must be signed by the legal representative director, beneficial owner, or trustee of the business and include the date of issuance.

To create a new letter of authorization, use the following template:

Make sure that your business documents meet the document requirements and criteria below and is issued within the past 180 days. A valid business document is issued by a government-authorized agency. A business document must contain the following information:

Name or logo of the provider or issuing authority
Name of the business or entity
Business license or commercial registration number
Date of issue. If the document has validity or expiration dates, make sure that the document is not expired.
Dates of validity or expiration (if applicable)

Proof of address

We require the following documents proving that your address is valid, based on the type of your seller account.

Individual sellers:
Bank statement for a checking, savings or loan account
Credit card statement or bill, or account statement provided by an e-commerce payment service provider such as Payoneer, Hyperwallet, World First, or Alipay
Certified bank letter
Utility bill such as gas, water, electricity, TV, Internet, mobile phone, or landline phone
Rent or lease agreement documents
Building society or credit union statement
Mortgage statement from a bank or other financial institution
Tenancy bill from a local council or reputable lending agency
Recent letter from a benefits agency that confirms the right to benefits, such as work-related, pension, or veteran benefits
Tax identity document containing address
Professional sellers:
Bank statement for a checking, savings or loan account
Credit card statement or bill, or account statement provided by an e-commerce payment service provider such as Payoneer, Hyperwallet, World First, or Alipay
Utility bill such as gas, water, electricity, TV, Internet, mobile phone, or landline phone
Rent or lease agreement documents
Building society or credit union statement
Mortgage statement from a bank or other financial institution
Tenancy bill from a local council or reputable lending agency
Tax identity document containing address
Business license or business extract (an electronic copy is also accepted)

Make sure that your document meets the document requirements and the following criteria. A valid proof of address is a document that is provided by a reliable and independent provider. A valid proof of address cannot be handwritten, and it must contain the following information:

Name or logo of the provider or issuing authority
Name of the customer
Professional sellers: Must contain the name of the business or entity
Individual sellers: Must contain the first and last name of an individual seller
Seller’s complete address
Date of issue. If the document has validity or expiration dates, make sure the document is not expired.
Certified bank letter only:
Must show both bank name and bank logo
Must be signed or stamped by the bank

Additional documents for legal and compliance obligations

Based on your business' physical location or the store or stores in which you operate, we may request additional documents and information about your business.

To avoid rejection of your documents, make sure that they meet the following requirements:

Be valid (not expired, revoked, inactive, or closed)
Be high-resolution and unobstructed (clear, readable, visible, and in focus)
Be complete and not cut off from any sides (not angled or cropped)
Be a scanned image or a photo of the original document taken from your mobile device's camera
Display the full document (front and back, if applicable)
Be less than 50MB in size
Be in one of these formats: TIF, TIFF, PNG, JPG, PDF, or JPEG. Do not include special characters in the file name (examples: $, &, or #).
Be authentic and unaltered
Must not be password-protected
Be in one of the following languages: Arabic, Simplified Chinese, Dutch, English, French, German, Hindi, Italian, Japanese, Polish, Portuguese, Spanish, Swedish, Tamil, Thai, Turkish, or Vietnamese. If your documents are not in one of those languages, you must submit a notarized translation in an accepted language along with the original document.

Identity document

We accept documents such as passports, passport cards, national identity cards, driving licenses, enhanced driving licenses, citizenship cards, permanent residency cards, health cards, or military identification cards.

Make sure that your identity document meets the document requirements and the following criteria:

Must be a government-issued photo identity document that is recognized and issued by the country where you are a citizen or resident.
Must show first and last name. The name must match the name that is entered as the primary contact person in your seller account.
Must include date of birth. The ID holder must be 18 or older.
Must show the unique ID number.
Have an expiration date (if applicable).
Note: The date of birth, unique ID number, and expiration date that you enter must be an exact match with the document. If your document doesn’t have an expiration date, you can enter a dummy date of three months from the date of registration.
Have the bearer's signature (if applicable). Certain passports have signatures on the first or last page.
Must include both sides of the ID if there’s any vital information on the back, such as signature, expiration date, birth date, or barcode.
Note: If the upload page doesn’t ask for separate front and back image uploads, merge the images into one file to upload.
Must be a color copy.

Beneficiaries, directors, or trustees

If you are a business seller, you are required to provide information about the beneficial owner, director, or trustee of the company. A beneficial owner, director, or trustee is a natural person who directly or indirectly owns more than 25 percent of the shares or voting rights of the business, or who owns the business via other means. If no individual qualifies under the criteria mentioned, then any individual who holds the position of senior manager is a beneficial owner, director, or trustee.

The following questions can help you determine the person who controls the company:

Are there individuals who de facto control the company, directly or indirectly?
Are there individuals who have the power to appoint or remove members of administrative, management or supervisory bodies, directly or indirectly?
Are there individuals who are responsible for key managerial decisions, directly or indirectly?
Are there individuals who can use all or part of the assets of the company?
Do not use ordered (numbered) lists in concepts unless your list has a logical order or ranking that cannot change

You can provide up to four beneficial owner names. If there are more than four beneficial owners, we will obtain this additional information from you later.

How do I add beneficial owner, director, or trustee to my account?

If the beneficial owner, director, or trustee is not the same person as the primary contact person, you must provide details about them and each of the beneficial owners, directors, or trustees of the company to meet the above criteria. To update the number of beneficial owners, directors, or trustees, follow the instructions below:

Go to the Identity information section of Seller Central.
Click +Add beneficial owner, director, or trustee which is located at the bottom of the screen, in blue font.
Enter the beneficial owner, director, or trustee’s details.
Repeat steps 1-3 for all applicable beneficial owners, directors, or trustees.
Click Submit for verification.

What to expect after you upload your documents

Once you upload all necessary documents and information, Seller Central will display a confirmation message. You can close the tab in your browser. We’ll review your information and might contact you for further clarification, if needed, within two business days. We’ll also keep you informed via an email to the address that you provided during registration.

Important: Do not resubmit documents or submit an appeal while we’re reviewing your information.

If we can’t verify the information you provided and ask that you resubmit your documents, you’ll have 30 days to complete this request. If we don’t get your documents within this time frame, your seller onboarding process will be closed, and we won’t be able to activate your account to sell on Amazon. For security reasons, we can only accept documents uploaded through Seller Central.

Important: If your Amazon seller account is deactivated after passing verification, refer to the help page Seller performance review.
