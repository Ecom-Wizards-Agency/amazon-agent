Global registration allows you to create Amazon selling accounts in multiple Amazon stores worldwide through one registration application. Doing this unifies your selling experience across Amazon stores and can help you manage and scale your business more easily.

By using single sign-on, you’ll have access to a single view of sales, orders, and buyer messages from all the stores in which you sell. You can also access tools that previously were restricted to specific countries and benefit from a discounted monthly fee for the Professional selling plan.

When you register to sell on Amazon via Global Registration, you’ll have merged accounts in the North American stores (the US, Canada, and Mexico), the Brazil store, the European stores (Belgium, France, Germany, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Poland, Spain, Sweden, and the UK), the Japan store, the Singapore store, the Australia store, the UAE store, the Egypt store, and the Saudi Arabia store. You can switch between stores in Seller Central.

Global registration FAQ
Registration
What should I do if I don't have all the information to complete registration in a particular store?

To register, you don't need store-specific information but only general information about your business. During registration, you can save your progress and sign in again later to continue from where you left off. You’ll have 100 days to complete your registration and verification.

After registration is completed, you may be required to provide additional information or documentation based on the stores in which you want to sell. For example, to sell in the US store, you’re required to provide tax information after registration is completed.

Is there a time limit to complete my registration in a particular store?

Yes. Once you start your registration for an Amazon selling account, you’ll have 100 days to complete registration and verification in at least one store before you can access Seller Central. If you don’t complete your registration and verification within 100 days, you’ll be guided to restart your registration.

Stores
Which stores am I registering in?

When you register for an Amazon selling account, we automatically create merged accounts that give you access to the North American stores (the US, Canada, and Mexico), Brazil, the European stores (Belgium, France, Germany, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Poland, Spain, Sweden, and the UK), Japan, Singapore, Australia, the UAE, Egypt, and Saudi Arabia.

Once you've created a selling account, you can start creating product listings in each store, unless that store requires additional information. You can see a list of your merged global accounts on the Your global accounts page.

Do I have to sell in all stores?

No. Even though you will have an account in each of the North American, Brazil, European, Japan, Singapore, Australia, UAE, Egypt, and Saudi Arabia stores, you can select the specific stores where you want to sell for each product that you offer. You aren’t required to list products in all stores. If you want to list a product in a store for which you have a merged account already, you won’t be required to go through the entire registration process again.

I don't want to list in all the stores in Europe. Can I select specific stores?

When you list your products in one of Amazon’s European stores, by default the listing will be available in all European stores to make it easier for you to expand your business in Europe. If you want to remove your listing from a specific store or opt out of this functionality altogether, you can deselect the store or stores in your Seller Central account settings.

I don’t need a selling account in all the stores for which you created merged accounts. How do I un-register myself from a store?

You can choose to downgrade your account from a Professional selling plan to an Individual selling plan. To learn how, go to Switch to an Individual selling plan.

To close an account, follow these steps:

Sign in to the account for the store that you want to close.
From the settings drop-down menu, select Account info and the select Account management.
Select Close account.

You must close each regional account individually: North America, Brazil, Europe, Japan, Singapore, Australia, the UAE, Egypt, and Saudi Arabia. If you downgrade or close one of your accounts but still have buyable ASINs listed in your other merged accounts, you’ll still pay a Professional Selling Plan subscription fee.

If you don’t want to be charged that monthly fee, make sure to downgrade each merged account individually.

I also want to sell in a store for which you don’t automatically create a merged account. What do I need to do?

You must register for that store separately. You can then merge that account with your global registration account. To learn how, go to Merging accounts.
