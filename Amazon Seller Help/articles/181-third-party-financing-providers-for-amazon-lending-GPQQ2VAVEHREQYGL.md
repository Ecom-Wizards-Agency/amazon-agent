# Third-party financing providers for Amazon Lending


We partner with third-party financing providers who may be able to help provide business financing options to sellers who meet specific business requirements, to help these sellers grow their business on Amazon.

Which US third-party financing providers are participating in the program?

In the US, there are currently three active third-party financing providers participating in the program. Marcus by Goldman Sachs has a line of credit product available by invitation, Lendistry has a term loan product available by invitation, and Parafin has a merchant cash advance product available by invitation.

Where are the business financing options displayed?

Sellers with business financing options can view available options when they are signed into their Seller Central account.

Is the program available to all sellers?

This program is available only to sellers who meet the criteria determined by the third-party financing providers.

Who is involved with the financing product agreement?

The seller will sign the financing product agreement directly with the third-party financing provider. Amazon will not be a party to such agreement.

Which products are available through the program?

To help address the financing needs of our sellers, Amazon Lending now provides eligible sellers access to four financing products: a standard term loan from Amazon Lending, and three third-party financing provider options from Marcus by Goldman Sachs, Lendistry, and Parafin. Marcus currently offers a Business Line of Credit to qualifying US-based businesses by invitation, Lendistry currently offers a term loan to qualifying US-based businesses by invitation, and Parafin currently offers a merchant cash advance to qualifying US-based businesses by invitation.

Can a seller apply for multiple financing options?

A seller with multiple financing options can apply for more than one financing option if they are from different financing providers. If approved, the seller can accept a loan from Amazon Lending and a line of credit from Marcus by Goldman Sachs or a merchant cash advance from Parafin at the same time. However, term loans from Lendistry cannot be combined with other financing products on Seller Central. Once the seller accepts a term loan from Lendistry, the seller will not be able to move forward with any in-progress applications from other financial providers.

Why is locking a bank account required? How does it happen?

Locking the seller's bank account on file with Amazon may be a prerequisite from the third-party financing provider. Once the account is locked, the third-party financing provider verifies if it matches what was provided in the financing product’s application. The account on file with Amazon cannot be changed after this step.

In order to lock the account, visit the approved application in Seller Central by referring to the section labeled Applications in progress. Click Lock account and follow the steps that appear onscreen.

Note: This account will remain locked until the financing product is paid in full and the financing product is closed. For any issues during this process, contact amazon-lending@amazon.com.

How does the bank account unlock?

The account will be automatically unlocked once the third-party financing provider confirms that the financing product has been paid in full. If the financing product is closed but a bank account remains locked, send an email to amazon-lending@amazon.com with the request and a copy of the payoff letter stating that the account has been paid from the third-party financing provider.

How can a seller update their Marcus bank account?

If a seller would like to update their preferred bank account for both Amazon and Marcus, they must first update the bank account associated with their business’s Marcus Business Line of Credit. They can contact Marcus at (833) 906-2224 to request the change, which may take one to two business days to be processed.

Once the bank account information has been updated on Marcus.com, the seller will have three days to update the account information on Seller Central. During this period, the seller will not be able to request funds from their business’s Marcus Business Line of Credit. Draw request privileges for the Marcus Business Line of Credit will be reinstated at the end of the three days.

How can a seller update the bank account associated with their business’s loan with Lendistry?

If a seller would like to update their preferred bank account for both Amazon and Lendistry, they must first update the bank account associated with their business’s term loan with Lendistry. They can contact Lendistry at (888) 216-6445 to request the change.

Once the Lendistry account update has been processed, the seller can proceed with updating the bank account on Amazon. The seller will be required to confirm that their Lendistry and Amazon bank accounts match and are re-linked before the update becomes effective.

Why is the third-party financing provider requiring additional information or documentation for the application process?

Since the financing product is extended by the third-party financing provider, they determine what information they need to process an application.

Will any data on Amazon be shared with the third-party financing providers? If yes, what data?

No personal information will be shared. We take your explicit consent before sharing any data. We will share your business’s sales and performance data on Amazon.com with the third-party financing provider after receiving your consent.

Before being redirected to a third-party financing provider's site to apply for a financing product, you will be asked in Seller Central whether you agree to share your business’s sales data with this third-party financing provider.

If you do not want to share your business’s sales data, you may click Cancel. You will not proceed to the application page on the third-party financing provider's site, and no data will be shared. If you agree to share, we will share your business’s sales data on Amazon.com with the third-party financing provider.

Refer to Data Share FAQ for more information on the data we will share with your consent.

Will the third-party financing providers share any personal information used in the application process with Amazon?

Amazon does not ask the third-party financing providers for your personal information, including your personal identity information, biggest shareholder, or financing product guarantor, nor does Amazon ask for your contact information such as phone number, email, and address. To learn more about what information the third-party financing provider will share with Amazon refer to the Data Share FAQ.

Who can answer questions about a current application or financing product?

For questions related to Marcus by Goldman Sachs, call Uncapped at (833) 785-4416, Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Eastern Time

For questions related to Lendistry, call (888) 216-6445. They are available Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 8 p.m. (ET).

For questions related to Parafin, call (855) 558-5005 or email help@parafin.com. They are available Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. (PT).
