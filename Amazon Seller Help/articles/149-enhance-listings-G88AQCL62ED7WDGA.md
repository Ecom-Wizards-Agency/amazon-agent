# Enhance Listings


High-quality product detail page content boosts your product’s discoverability through search and filter inclusion, turns more browsers into shoppers, and makes it easier for customers to evaluate and purchase your products confidently, resulting in fewer returns and positive reviews. The Enhance Listings view in Catalog shows you listings that need improvement, along with their current listing quality and the specific attributes that impact quality. Where available, you’ll also see AI-generated suggestions to help you improve those attributes. The tool suggests improvements to product details such as titles, bullet points, and key attributes to help shoppers better understand your products and make confident purchase decisions.

As you review and apply updates, your Listing Quality automatically refreshes to reflect improvements, showing how close the listing’s product detail page is to Amazon’s quality standards. This helps remove the guesswork out of optimizing your product detail pages.

How to optimize listings

Go to Items and navigate to Catalog.
Click Enhance listings to view listings with improvement opportunities.
Under the Enhance listings tab, click View enhancements for the ASIN you want to improve listing quality.
Navigate to the side panel that shows all the opportunities to improve listing quality.
Note: Where available, you will see the reasons for those opportunities underneath each attribute.
Click Enhance listing for any ASIN you want to improve the quality of.
View the side panel which opens to show all opportunities to improve listing quality, including the factors impacting your listing's quality and where available, AI-generated recommendations for these attributes.
Note: AI capability is not supported when improving listing quality using bulk edits. For more information, go to Improving quality using bulk editsImproving quality using bulk edits.
You can enter your own value, update the AI-provided value, or use the AI-generated suggestions as they are.
Note: It's your responsibility to review and verify all generated content thoroughly and ensure it is accurate and complies with all Intellectual Property and Listing policies. AI-generated content can include features and claims that were not in the listing's original content.
After reviewing all opportunities, click Save and Finish to publish accepted updates.
Note: The listing quality refreshes within 15 minutes of you publishing updates.
Once all opportunities are addressed, a listing is removed from this view.

Alternative way to enhance listings

When editing an existing listing (One ASIN at a time), you may also see the option Enhance listing on the lest side of the listing form. Click View enhancements to view AI-suggested content for your listing. You can review and customize the generated content, then select Add to form. Click Save and Finish on the form to submit all updates.

Note: If your contribution is verified by Amazon, they will show on the product detail page within 48 hours. If, after the allotted period, your contributions do not show on the product detail page, it means that Amazon's system has selected another contributor's information that best describes the product based on the guidelines set by Amazon. The Listing Quality Indicator updates only when the update appears on the product detail page.

Listing Quality Indicator

The Listing Quality Indicator measures how complete and correct your product detail page is, based on two pillars:

Content quality: Evaluates product data such as titles, bullet points, and key attributes for correctness, completeness, and policy compliance. Requirements vary by product type.
Multimedia quality: Reviews image and A+ content quality and completeness.

Based on the quality of these two pillars, your listing is classified into one of the following listing quality categories:

Poor: Listing violates Amazon detail page policies, for example, the title contains special characters (!, $, ?, _, {, }, ^, ¬, ¦.) or the listing is missing key information.
Fair: Meets minimum requirements and all Amazon policies but lacks some key product information. Shoppers may need more details before purchasing.
Good: Listing has reasonable depth of information (high-quality title and images, most primary attributes filled), with minor gaps remaining.
Great: Near-perfect and shopper-ready. Listing is complete and high quality in both content and multimedia, with minimal missing secondary information.
Optimized: Fully optimized listing that meets all current standards. Listing quality evolves over time, and you can check back periodically for new opportunities.

Note: The Listing Quality Indicator measures how your listing’s content and attributes compare to best-in-class listings in similar categories. It evaluates completeness, accuracy, and policy compliance, but does not account for dynamic factors such as offer price, fulfillment, or seasonality. While high-quality listings are generally better positioned to perform, the score should be used as a directional indicator rather than a predictor of visibility or sales.
