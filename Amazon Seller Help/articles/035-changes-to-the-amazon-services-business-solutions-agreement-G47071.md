# Changes to the Amazon Services Business Solutions Agreement


Effective March 4, 2026, we will update the US/CA Amazon Services Business Solution Agreement (BSA) as follows: 

Update opening language, including sentence in French for sellers whose elected country is Canada and statement on binding arbitration agreement and class action waiver. 
Remove all references to Mexico because the Mexico store has a standalone Business Solutions Agreement. View the MX BSA here. 
Update Section 1 to include reference to the Amazon.ca Privacy Notice. 
Update agreement section references in Section 3. 
Revise Section 4 as follows: 
Add new Subsection 4.1 title
Add new Subsection 4.2 prohibition on using Amazon materials to develop or improve AI/machine learning models. Also add restrictions on data mining, reverse engineering, and deriving source code or model components. 
Update Sections 5 and 17, replacing government authority examples with new general “Government Authority” phrase. 
Replace Section 18 reference to the “Developer Site” with the new site title “Solution Provider Portal” and move language from Section 18 on dispute resolution to new standalone Dispute Resolution Section 20.
Add new Section 19, defining and restricting use of automated agents/AI systems. 
Revise Definitions Section as follows: 
Add new definitions for "Agent," "Applicable Government Authority" and “Our Materials,”
Revise “Service” definition,
Revise “Insurance Limits” definition, removing reference to Mexico and adding new bullet for amounts that Amazon may require. 
Update Selling Partner API Terms, Section API-1 with reference to new Solution Provider Portal Agreement.
This information is intended to provide a summary of some of the recent changes. Please be sure to carefully review the terms of the updated agreement here. Your continued use of Selling Services constitutes your acceptance of these changes.
