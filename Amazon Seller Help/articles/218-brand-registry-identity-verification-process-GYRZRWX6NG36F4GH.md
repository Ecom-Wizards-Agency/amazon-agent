Amazon Brand Registry requires identity verification to activate certain benefits for your account. This process helps us to maintain a trusted store for customers and selling partners.

This page includes details about the identity verification process and the documents that you must provide in order to create and verify your Brand Registry account.

Identity document

Amazon will verify the identity of anyone who registers to have access to brand protection benefits in Brand Registry. Each of these users must provide a photograph of at least one valid identity document, along with a selfie.

Identity document: A government-issued photo identity document that is used to confirm one’s identity. We accept passports, national identity cards, driver’s licenses or enhanced driver’s licenses, and visas as identity documents.

Note: Your identity document must match the proof of identity information that you select on the Identity verification page under Identity data.

Document requirements
To ensure that your document is not rejected, confirm that it meets the following requirements:
It must be a government-issued photo identity document that is recognized and issued by the country where you are a citizen or resident.
It must include your full name.
It must include your date of birth.
Note: ID holder must be 18 years or older.
It must show the unique ID number, if applicable.
Note: Per Indian regulation, India Aadhar numbers should not be used and can be hidden by you during image capture.
It must be valid and not expired, revoked, inactive, or closed.
It must have an expiry date, if applicable.
Note: If your document does not have an expiry date, you can enter a dummy date of three months from the date of registration.
It must have the bearer's signature, if applicable.
Note: Certain passports have signatures either on one of the initial pages or one of the last pages.
It must be the original or physical copy of the ID document and not a copy or screen capture.
It must be authentic and unaltered.
It must be in one of these supported languages: Arabic, Bengali, Chinese (simplified or traditional), Dutch, English, French, German, Gujarati, Hindi, Italian, Japanese, Kannada, Korean, Malayalam, Marathi, Polish, Portuguese, Spanish, Swedish, Tamil, Telugu, Thai, Turkish, or Vietnamese.
Image requirements

Once you have your authentic identification document in hand, you must use your mobile device to capture a high-quality image of the document. The following requirements apply:

The image must be a photo of the original document taken from your mobile device's camera.
The image must be high resolution and unobstructed.
The image must show the entire document and not be angled or cropped on any side.
If both sides of your document include any vital information such as a signature, date of expiry, date of birth, or barcode, you must provide images of both the front and back of the document.
Image capture quality

Successful identity document verification depends on the quality of the images that you capture on your mobile device. Low-quality images result in lower acceptance rates and more manual review.

Listed below are key factors for improving your document capture results:

Place ID on a plain, dark surface. ID document verification works best when there is contrast between the ID and the surface on which it is placed. Preferably, use a plain black sheet of paper or another black surface. The ID should not be placed on a newspaper, magazine, keyboard, countertop, or your lap. Holding the document in your hand may result in a failed verification. Ensure that your fingers are not obscuring the document. If any key elements (such as barcodes or text) are obstructed, it may result in a failed verification.
Ensure that all four corners are visible. Align the edges of the ID within the camera screen, and ensure that all four corners of the ID are visible.
Find the right distance between the ID and your device. Hold the mobile device over the ID at a distance where the document is legible and completely inside the camera screen. For passports, ensure that the biographical page (the page with your name, passport number, and photograph) is the only page in view.
Ensure that there is no glare on the document surface. You might have to try capturing the image from different directions or locations if there is a glare on the document.
Avoid dark areas. Capture the image in a well-lit room or area. Natural daylight or a well-lit room is ideal.
Avoid skewing the image. Images should not be skewed or angled within the picture, although two to three degrees off of a right angle is acceptable. The edges of the image should align with the edges of the photo as closely as possible.
Hold your mobile device steady. Try to not to shake the device when you are capturing the image of the ID document. Images that are too blurry may not be verified.
Ensure that the image is high resolution. Modern cameras can take high-resolution images. We encourage you to submit images with a 1080p resolution. The image must be clear enough for the document’s text to be legible.

Step-by-step submission guide

First, sign into your Brand Registry account on a desktop or laptop computer.

Next, follow these steps:

Go to the Identity verification page, and click Begin identity verification.
Select Use automated ID verification.
Note: To opt out of automated ID verification, select Schedule video verification, which will direct you to schedule a live video call with an Amazon representative.
If you confirm that you agree to automated identity verification, you will see a page with a QR code. At this time, you must use the camera feature on your mobile phone to scan the QR code. Tap the URL that appears onscreen when you scan the code.
Once you scan the QR code to your mobile phone, your desktop browser will load a screen with instructions. Keep this tab open on your desktop while you continue the ID verification process on your mobile phone.
On your mobile phone, select whether you will use a driver’s license, a passport, or a different government-issued ID as your identity document. (Refer to the section above titled “Identity document” for more information.)
Complete the required information and grant access to your phone’s camera.
Place your identity document on a plain surface and face the rear camera of your mobile device toward the document to take a photo. (Refer to the sections above titled “Image requirements” and “Image capture quality” for additional guidelines.) If necessary, take a photograph of the front and back of your document.
Next, you will be directed to capture a selfie. You will now use your mobile device’s front-facing camera to capture an image of your face.
You will be shown a summary page of all of the images that you have taken—for example, the front and back of your ID and your selfie. If you are ready to submit these images, select Submit.
The screen will display a confirmation message of your submission.
You can now refer back to your desktop or laptop, where you have the “Identify verification” page open. The page will update to show that your identity verification is in progress and that the status is “Under review.” At this point, you can close the tab on your browser.

What to expect after you complete the submission process

Once you have uploaded all necessary documents and information, the Brand Registry identity verification page will display a confirmation message. Amazon will review your information and confirm your verification results within 15 minutes of your submission confirmation. We will also keep you informed via email to the email address that you provided during account registration.

Note: Do not resubmit documents while we are reviewing your information.

If we are unable to verify the information that you provided and request that you resubmit your documents, you will have 30 days to complete this request. If we do not receive your documents within this timeframe, your identity verification process will be considered closed, and you will not be able to access some or all of the brand protection benefits.

Opt out of automated identity verification process

Automated identity verification using facial recognition technology (biometrics) is optional. As an alternative to the automated identity verification process, you will have the option to opt out and choose manual identity verification. As part of this process, you will be required to submit your identity documents via an opt-out form. You will then be prompted to schedule a video call with an Amazon representative to perform live on-screen identity verification.

Note: Manual identity verification could take longer than automated identity verification to complete because it depends on the availability of scheduling slots for your selected language.
