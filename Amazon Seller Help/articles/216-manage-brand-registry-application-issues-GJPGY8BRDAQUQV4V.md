# Manage Brand Registry application issues


Use the table below to understand why your Brand Registry application was declined and how to manage the issue.

Status	Possible scenarios	How to manage
Submitted	Your account access has been restricted due to one of the following policy violations:
You are listed as the primary owner of one or more selling partner accounts that have not passed the seller verification process.
Your account is connected to another account that has engaged in activities that violate our conduct policies.
	If you believe there has been an error in this decision, you can submit an appeal by visiting Manage application and clicking Appeal next to the brand application marked as ineligible. An approved appeal is necessary to proceed with your Brand Registry application. Once an appeal has been submitted, you will receive an initial response within 3 to 5 days.

For specific information about possible abusive conduct, go to Abusive conduct error messages.

Brand Validation	Trademark and Brand Information issues	

Trademark status:
Only trademarks with "REGISTERED" or "PENDING" status can be accepted.
Other status types are not eligible.
Brand name accuracy:
The brand name in your application must match your trademark text exactly.
Review this information carefully.
Trademark number verification:
Verify that the trademark number corresponds to the correct trademark office.
If you entered incorrect information, submit a new application.
Contact information:
Keep your and your correspondent's contacts details updated with your trademark office.
A pending trademark application is filed through Amazon IP Accelerator.
For specific information regarding the office where you submitted your IP, go to Country-specific trademark information.

Invalid images	
Provide at least one image of the product or packaging that clearly shows your trademark name or trademark design image permanently affixed to the product.
Permanently affixed brand names are typically added during production and can be printed, sewn, laser-etched, or engraved onto items. Stickers, labels, hung tags, or stamps are not considered permanently affixed since they can be easily added or removed after production.
Certain products such as furniture, jewelry, soft toys, wigs, and handmade items might not have permanently affixed brand names. In these cases, the product’s packaging must have a brand name that is permanently affixed. Other products, like phone cases or clothing, can have branding included as part of the products themselves.
Ensure the ASIN or website you are providing shows the trademark name on it.
The product image provided has evidence it was photoshopped or the brand name was digitally added to the product. The images should not be computer generated and must be of the physical product you intend to sell on Amazon.
Providing multiple artifacts (such as website/ASINs) help us validate your brand. Only share artifacts displaying the brand on your application.
For more information about image guidelines for a Brand Registry Application, review the Brand Registry Application Guide.

Owner Verification	Ownership not confirmed	
We've sent an email to your trademark correspondent regarding brand ownership verification.
The correspondent must verify ownership through our portal within 7 days using the provided link.
Ensure your trademark correspondent's contact details are current.
If you need to update the contact information at the trademark office, complete this before applying.

Uniqueness Verification	Your trademark application has been rejected due to its similarity to an existing registered brand in Amazon's store, a measure we take when evaluating applications with trademarks in a pending registration status. If you believe your brand is unique, you may reapply once your trademark achieves active registration status in its trademark office.	The application was rejected because your trademark is currently in "pending" status and Amazon detected similarity to an existing registered brand in their store.
