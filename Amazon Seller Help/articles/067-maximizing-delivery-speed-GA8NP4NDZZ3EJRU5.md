Delivering faster speed to customers drives higher conversion rates and increases customer satisfaction. In this help page, we're sharing key steps you can take to maximize your delivery speeds.

1. Maintain sufficient inventory levels
Action

Maintain inventory levels between 30-60 days of supply in Amazon's storage network, as recommended by our Minimum Inventory Level (MIL) guidelines.

Why it matters

When you hold sufficient inventory, Amazon can distribute your products across our fulfillment network. The closer products are stored to customers, the faster we can deliver them.

Best practices

Check the 'Inventory Health status and recommendations' section on your FBA inventory page for ASIN-specific minimum inventory level (MIL) guidance. MIL guidance is also available in FBA inventory health report.
To get the most accurate MIL recommendations, we recommend checking your specific MIL inputs, such as lead time (transit time from shipment creation to Amazon warehouse arrival) and replenishment frequency. To update your inputs, go to Customize SKU settings page. For more information, go to Product settings.
Access your product demand forecast to view 52-week projections, showing the range between mean and optimistic scenarios to guide your inventory planning decisions. To view your product demand forecast, from the FBA Inventory page, navigate to Restock Inventory and click View forecast in the Sales summary column for your ASIN. For more information, go to Demand forecast.

2. Replenish inventory frequently
Action

Replenish your inventory regularly and frequently.

Why it matters

When you replenish frequently, Amazon can use the latest demand signals in deciding where to distribute and store your products. This brings inventory closer to customers, which increases delivery speeds.

Best practices

Set replenishment alerts for your products based on weeks of cover or fulfillable quantity thresholds on the Manage FBA Inventory page. FBA will send you email notifications when inventory levels need attention.
For high-velocity products (100+ units weekly), replenish at least every three weeks.
Consider using Amazon Warehouse and Distribution (AWD) for automated daily inventory replenishment. Daily stock replenishment enhances your ability to navigate unexpected demand fluctuations.

3. Use Amazon-recommended packing
Action

Follow Amazon's recommended packing guidelines by shipping fewer units per box to optimize your inventory distribution across our network.

Why it matters

By following recommended packing configurations, your inventory reaches more fulfillment centers efficiently, enabling faster delivery promises to your customers.

Best practices

When you send to only one location using minimal shipment split option, it is recommended to send at least 5 single-SKU boxes per product.
When you send to multiple locations using optimized shipment splits option, send single-SKU boxes with no more than 25 units per box.

4. Provide accurate delivery windows
Action

Submit accurate tracking information and precise delivery windows for shipments sent through non-Amazon partnered carriers.

Why it matters

Accurate delivery window information allows fulfillment centers to prepare for your inventory's arrival, resulting in more efficient processing and faster availability to customers.

Best practices

Always update shipment tracking numbers.
Provide precise delivery window date ranges.
You can modify delivery windows up until the moment the delivery window begins.

5. Ship to designated Amazon warehouses
Action

Ship inventory only to the designated Amazon warehouses assigned during shipment creation and verify all shipments contain accurate quantities with complete documentation.

Why it matters

By shipping to designated Amazon warehouses, you enable proper routing of your inventory, leading to faster processing and distribution throughout our fulfillment network.

Best practices

Ensure documentation matches physical shipments.
Include all required paperwork with each shipment.
Adhere strictly to shipment plans for each designated Amazon warehouses.

6. Plan ahead for holiday season and promotional events
Action

Prepare for high-volume periods by sending inventory well in advance.

Why it matters

Amazon's fulfillment network experiences increased congestion during holiday seasons that may cause inbound delays.

Best practices

Follow recommended inbound cut-off dates (typically 30+ days before events).
For faster warehouse delivery during holiday season, schedule delivery appointments during off holiday times: weekends or after 10 p.m., when delivery appointments are more readily available.
Increase inventory levels up to recommended minimum inventory level (MIL) before promotional events to maintain fast delivery speeds.

7. Ensure shipment quality and packaging standards
Action

Ensure all products meet Amazon's FBA packaging requirements for safety and quality standards. Products with non-compliant packaging may be refused, returned, or repackaged at your expense.

Why it matters

Compliant products pass through our inbound network without delays, making them available to customers faster.

Best practices

Keep all package dimensions and weights current in your listings.
Provide detailed box content information for each shipment to avoid manual processing fees and potential delays.

8. Review capacity limits
Action

Review your capacity limits regularly by accessing Capacity Monitor to stay up to date on your capacity utilization.

Why it matters

Our forecasts may not always capture your promotional plans or additional storage needs, submit capacity limit increase requests to maintain sufficient inventory for expected sales growth.

Best practices

Monitor your capacity utilization against both current and upcoming confirmed limit. Limits are refreshed in the fourth week of each month for next three months.
Submit capacity limit increase requests early through Capacity Manager to secure additional space when needed - requests are evaluated multiple times per week.
